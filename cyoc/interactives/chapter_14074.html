I was shown where the feed was siloed and how to open the silo without being buried alive. Then the feed placed in large wheelbarrows was walked out to the standing cattle and scooped into troughs.

Ted was the first to spout telling Frank I worked just fine. John was next as he patted me on the back, then the two of them walked out of the barn leaving me and Frank alone.

There inside the stall was one large bull. Frank said it was of the Shorthorn breed but was still dangerous, it was not a tame pet!

He danced about turning this way and that as the cow turned to keep her rump away from the bull. Groaning the bull tried to mount her from the side and inch his way behind. A quick move sent the bull falling to his four hooves with a thud.

    Mooing in some frantic manner it seemed the cow was looking at me much of the time. Her feelings of fear of the bull were hitting me and making some odd impressions.

    A time or two the bull stopped and turned his face toward mine then making some strange gutteral sound.

    After yet an hour and Frank still gone the cow was sweating herself into a lather. The bull too was getting lathered both physically and mentally as try after try failed.

    Turning to face me the bull lifted his head up as high as possible he could. Then to my astonished ears he spoke out a gutteral trail of words which sounded like begging me for help.

    Laughing made him angry and he charged the steel fence where I stood.

    The cow backed her rump up to the fence near me and stood her ground.

    Then she turned her head to look at me and like the bull dragged out her throat some sounds seemingly as if thanking me for my help in keeping her a virgin.

    Now this all had me rather upset when Frank returned some time later. I told him the two had not coupled and were just fighting constantly. He took out a cattle prod and zapped the bull's behind driving him back into his stall. The cow needed no such prodding and headed out into the pasture and freedom.

    "Duties are over for today!" Frank yelled and walked out the barn leaving me still looking at the bull.

    Pacing back and forth the bull walked then to stand directly infront of me by the steel bar fence.

    Deep sounding voice came out the throat and mouth of the bull. I stood there amazed by what I was seeing and then hearing made me fear this place all the more.

    As if fitting out the words the bull his lips unable to help form words spoke to me in short statements. It sounded first like "Why?" Then "She was mine!" "Needed your help!" "Want me to go to market?"

    Once I contained my fear the thought of asking a question came to mind too. "Who are you?" a simple but direct question knowing that bovine animals can't talk!

    The reply was slow as the bull tried to say what he wanted to say but not having lips and a tongue working in as if human it was of course difficult.

    "Simon Templer assistant state investigator"
