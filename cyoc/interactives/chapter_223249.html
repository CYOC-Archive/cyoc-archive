    As Drake and Alan continued their workout in the gym, they were approached by three freshmen students who had been rowing together earlier.

    The first freshman, Ethan, was a compact guy with a boyish charm. He had a slender frame and wore bright green briefs that contrasted with his tan skin. "Hey, you guys look like you know what you're doing. Mind if we join you for a set or two?" he asked, flashing a friendly smile.

    The second freshman, Jackson, was tall and lean, his sculpted muscles evident in the way he moved. He had chosen sky-blue briefs that emphasized his long, toned legs. "Yeah, we saw you lifting earlier, and it seemed like you really knew your stuff," he added, his voice filled with enthusiasm.

    The third freshman, Ryan, was the smallest of the group, but he radiated confidence. His red briefs hugged his athletic frame, and he had a playful glint in his eye. "We're always looking to learn from the pros," he said with a grin.

    Drake glanced at Alan, who seemed to be enjoying the attention. Then, he noticed the striking size difference between the freshmen and Alan. The contrast was undeniable, and Drake couldn't help but recall how Alan had always been intrigued by the concept of size differences between guys.

    "Sure, guys, you can join us," Alan said, welcoming them with a friendly demeanor. "We're always up for some company and a good workout."

    As they all started working out together, the freshmen asked Alan for advice on their routines, and he happily shared his knowledge. Meanwhile, Drake couldn't shake the feeling that Alan's newfound size, both in terms of muscle and height, was somehow connected to the lamp's wish. He knew he needed to find an opportunity to talk to Alan privately and figure out what had transpired since the lamp had come into their lives.

    As the group continued their workout together, Drake couldn't help but remember that Ryan was more than just a friend to Alan; he was Alan's boyfriend. Jackson and Ethan were also close friends of Alan's. Drake had always found it a bit peculiar that many of his closest friends were gay, including his best friend, Alan. He didn't quite understand it, being straight himself, but he valued their friendships immensely.

    Drake had met Ryan, Jackson, and Ethan through Alan, and over time, they had all become a tight-knit group. He admired their camaraderie and cherished the bond he shared with them, even if he didn't fully comprehend the dynamics of their relationships.

    As they lifted weights and chatted between sets, Drake felt grateful for the acceptance and friendship that Alan and his friends had shown him, despite their differences in sexual orientation. They were more than just workout partners; they were a close-knit group that supported each other in various aspects of life.

    As the workout continued, Drake couldn't help but compare himself to the others. He noticed the lean and slender physiques of Jackson, Ethan, and Ryan and realized that he shared a similar body type with them. His body felt lithe and lightweight, just like theirs.

    But there was something nagging at the back of Drake's mind. A vague memory or dream of having more weight and muscle than he currently possessed. It was like a fleeting image that he couldn't quite grasp, and the harder he tried to recall it, the more it seemed to slip away.

    Drake looked at Alan, who effortlessly lifted heavier weights and carried an imposing presence in the gym. He couldn't help but feel grateful for their friendship. It was unusual for a freshman like him to be roommates and best friends with an upperclassman like Alan. Drake had memories that hinted at him being older, but he dismissed them, knowing that he was indeed a freshman, just like Ethan, Ryan, and Jackson.

    As they wrapped up their workout and headed out of the gym, Drake's thoughts remained clouded by the uncertainty surrounding the lamp and its wishes. He needed answers and clarity, but he also didn't want to jeopardize the bonds he had with Alan and his friends. It was a delicate balance, and Drake was determined to find a way to navigate it.
