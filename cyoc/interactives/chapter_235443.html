    The residence at 12 Rue de l’Abbaye in Paris, built in the early 19th century, is a place steeped in mystery and legend. Once an elegant private mansion, the building was marked by tragic events that left an indelible imprint on its history. Today, the residence serves as a home for young women, but strange tales continue to unsettle its residents.

    In the 1800s, the residence housed a community of witches led by the enigmatic and powerful Armande de la Roche. Armande and her companions were known for their mastery of the occult and their commitment to protecting women from harm.

    One day, a tragic incident occurred: Léon, a young man from a good family, committed a heinous act by raping Élisabeth, a close friend of Armande's. Furious and determined to avenge the injustice done to Élisabeth, Armande cast a curse on the residence. According to the curse, any man who took advantage of a woman in this place would be doomed to transform into a woman himself, irreversibly. This transformation, while not painful, was gradual and delicate, occurring slowly as the man engaged in sexual intercourse with the woman. Additionally, the magic erased all traces of his past male identity and rewrote the memories of those around him, so that everyone would now perceive him as a woman.

    Years passed, and the residence was converted into a student dormitory for young women. The students went about their daily lives, unaware of the heavy secret that lingered within the walls. However, mysterious incidents began to disturb their routine.

    Lucie, a sociology student, was among the first to notice something strange. She remembered how her boyfriend Thomas, whom she had invited to stay with her for a romantic weekend, had inexplicably changed. Thomas had become Thérèse, a woman. Although Lucie was disturbed, she tried to explain the situation to other students, but her revelations were met with skepticism and disbelief. No one seemed to remember Thomas, and the other residents found her explanations absurd.

    Another troubling example occurred with Émilie, another resident. She had invited her boyfriend Marc to spend a few days with her at the residence. When Marc and Émilie had sexual intercourse, the transformation began, and Marc gradually became Marcelle, a gentle and timid young woman. Émilie, shocked by this change, tried to share what had happened with her friends, but once again, her stories were dismissed as nonsense. Magic had rewritten the memories of those around Marc, and no one remembered him as a man.

    The residence's administration and staff, although aware of the rules prohibiting men, had no idea of the real reason behind this restriction. Transformations occurred regularly, but magic ensured that each new resident or staff member accepted these changes as the norm. The environment seemed regular and stable, even though Lucie’s and Émilie’s stories were considered strange anomalies.

    Thus, the residence at Rue de l’Abbaye continues to operate as a student dormitory for young women, its secrets carefully guarded by ancient and powerful magic. Residents enjoy their time in this historic building, unaware of the invisible forces shaping their everyday lives and the truths hidden behind the walls of their Parisian home.
