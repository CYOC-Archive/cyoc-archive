    Damn, I knew I should've tied stuff down, but where am I going to get rope?

    Previous chapter

    added 13 years ago I O

    Dylan was racing down the highway toward the football field when he
    caught a glimpse of yellow, white and blue plastic flash in his rear
    view mirror. The next thing he saw were flashing red and blue lights.
    He pulled over and hopped out of the truck after first sticking the
    wand in his back pocket.

    "Thanks officer, I just realized that my load came loose. I've gotta
    get that stuff," he shouted as he ran pass the park squad car and
    after the deflated bottle of Miller beer blowing in the wind. He
    spotted a purple football further up the road.

    Sheriff Bartlesby got out of his squad car and adjusted his
    sunglasses. He shook his head and started filling out his ticket
    citation. He strolled over to the pick up and glanced in the back of
    the pickup bed. He picked up the inflatable rubber sheep and put it
    back. He looked at all the new football gear, and stuck his pen in
    his mouth. It was a full 10 minutes before a breathless Dylan made it
    back to the officer carrying the two inflatables and Hulk placard that
    had blown out of his pick up.

    "Where you going with all this football gear?" Bartlesby asked
    pointedly.

    "Huh, huh, taking it to the equipment shed at our high school football
    field," panted Dylan.

    "Okay, well, you need to tie it down before you get back on the
    highway. You're just lucky the mannequins or other gear didn't end up
    scattered back there. It's a rough bit of highway."

    "Yes, sir. I'll remember that in the future."

    "Now where did you get this stuff?"

    "Oh, Jeremy was supposed to be raising money for new equipment for the
    team and well, I guess he's ultimately responsible, but-"

    "Fine. Just need it for reference. If it turns out any of this stuff
    is stolen, I'll be talking to you and Jeremy. Now where do you think
    you're going?"

    "To the equipment shed," Dylan answered as he stowed the deflated
    inflatables on the floor behind his seat.

    "Not without that stuff tied down first!" warned the sheriff.
    Bartlesby had only been the sheriff since November and he was rather
    young for his post. He was only 23, but he had a huge groundswell of
    sympathy vote due to the fact that his father had been sheriff before,
    and had been killed by a drunk driver earlier that year while standing
    on the roadside assisting a disabled vehicle. It was only a kid with
    pick up full of football gear, but the new Sheriff Bartlesby had to
    demonstrate his authority.

    Dylan shook his head, "Well do you have some rope I can borrow?"

    "No. You should've thought of that before you started driving this
    stuff!"

    Absentmindedly, Dylan had taken the wand from his back pocket and was
    chewing on it like a pencil. He was nervous and uncertain. He gestured
    with the wand as he spoke, "Listen Sheriff I wish I had netting to
    properly secure the stuff in my truck. But I don't. But you have to
    understand about the gear and my team mates."

    "You have to understand that I wish you had netting too, th-" Sheriff
    said stopping in midword as his body collapsed into a heavy duty mesh
    netting designed perfectly to fit over Dylan's truck bed. The sheriff
    while getting into Dylan's face had pressed his chest into the tip of
    the wand.

    "Oh, no, not again!" exclaimed Dylan."I keep trying to explain to you
    that all this gear are players, and I'm trying how to figure out how
    to turn them and you back. The sheriff no less, this dang wand changed
    the sheriff into netting for my pick up truck. I'd throw it away, but
    I know I'll need it if I'm ever going to turn you guys back. Well,
    Sheriff since you wished yourself this way, I'm going to take it that
    you volunteered to help me secure my load so I could take the gear to
    the equipment shed."

    Dylan carefully secured the netting that used to be the sheriff to the
    top of his pick up truck. He stashed the sheriff's gun, boots and
    clothing under his car seats. It might get dirty, but he was really
    looking forward to changing the sheriff back unless it was really
    necessary.
