His mother had stacked a ton of leftovers in the kitchen recently, but Jack was looking for one thing in particular. Rummaging around, Jack smirked as he finally found it. Reaching in with his hands, Jack soon pulled out a tuna casserole that was on the verge of being bad.

What made this even better for Jack, was that he knew that his victim couldn't stand the smell of fish, even going so far as to vomit sometimes when the lunch ladies served it. Adding something else for good measure, Jack took out a platter of raw tuna, making sure to add that smell as well.

Saving the changes, Jack went back to Edwin, slowly slipping in his feet.

The hoodie was then changed into a dog within a matter of seconds, a Jack Russell terrier at that, and Edwin could tell that Ms. Santos still kept her old mind. Still, with some menacing words from Jack, she made sure to act like a regular dog.

    "Well then, Edwin," Jack said, stepping outside, keeping Ms. Santos on a black leash as Edwin felt his first taste of Jack's feet pressing into his insides. "Let's get going."
