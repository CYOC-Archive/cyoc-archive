    “Hey, we’re both gonna do this, Alan,” Jeff fired back. “Betcha twenty bucks you’re even cuter.”

    They bickered back and forth, not noticing that although the ‘aware’ option had been enabled, another setting was also accidentally enabled.

    The ‘Reality Adjustment’ option.

    “Fine, I’m doing this!” Jeff declared, taking a deep breath before finally clicking ‘CONFIRM’.

    In an instant, the world around them started to swirl and spin. It was as though a hundred invisible hands were grabbing at them, tearing at their clothes and pinching their cheeks. Pushing their chests inward, squishing their arms and legs, pulling at their hair.     “What did you DO!?” Alan shrieked, his high-pitched voice ringing through the entire house. “I said to keep us as boys!”

    “I-I-I…” Jeff stammered, starting in disbelief at his friend.

    They both stood just a hair over four feet tall, a little short for nine-year olds, but that wasn’t the issue here. Alan was wearing a pastel-purple t-shirt covered in sparkling sequins and images of prancing unicorns, over a pair of thigh-high denim shorts decorated with stick-on plastic gems. Jeff wore a fluffy pink sweater a few sizes to large that hung down nearly to his knees, along with a pair of colorful striped tights over skinny legs.

    “We’re girls!?” Alan nearly screamed, his hands immediately going to his crotch, only to frown as he found something unexpected. Or, something he had expected, but which didn’t fit with the rest of his new appearance. “Hold on a sec…”

    Jeff and Alan glanced at their reflections in the mirror and blushed, making their adorable little faces even more unbearably cute.

    “Guess we’ve know. Only the girliest, sissiest boys in the 3rd grade,” Alan sighed dejectedly. “Well, shit. Guess we should have thought about this a little harder…”

    “I-it’s okay, I think I can fix this,” Jeff said, looking sidelong at his friend. “Unless… you’re okay with this?”
