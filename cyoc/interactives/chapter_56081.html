    once the spell is complete, will be among the first to feel absolute freedom from this unfair
    universe.'

    The first one they changed had become the male of the group, the rest that followed had
    become his lionesses, and a couple had become brother and sister, a common pattern to
    the witch's games.

    The leoman family worked without complaint or word, to a point that would have even the
    normally altruistic leomans stunned. Even their two cubs worked with bright eyes and
    smiles on their faces, none showing the slightest frown of boredom. The witches had built
    up their family one by one, not only working as free carpenters to continue the work of
    expanding the house, but also to act as physical body guards in case their magic somehow
    failed them. The witches didn't comprehend how their magic failing them was remotely
    possible, but there was a chance they could be away when another gang of less-than-
    dogs attacked their familiars, and that would be totally unacceptable!

    The leomans did their absolute hardest not to get in the way of the unruly children, and
    serving in other tasks when needed.

    Gold Deer were ideal prey animals, if for no other reason than they had no true mating
    season, and could reproduce year round if need be. Hunting was thus one of the things
    that naturally kept their numbers in check. This meant that there always fawns in some
    form or another among the herds.

    And sometimes they wandered off. This led to an incident where the witches' charges had
    wanted to play with a gold fawn that had also wandered from it's parents. When the buck
    found them however, it thought the worst and decided an example needed to be made. As
    it ran foreword with it's horns lowered, one of the witch's leomans grabbed hold of the
    buck by the horns and twisted it's neck, dead. The fawn cried out and fled in terror. The
    buck's pelt became a nice skin for many of the children including Joella.

    That was all the witches needed to know, to be sure that these leoman they had created,
    with reddish gold fur and crimson manes, were indeed the perfect physical protectors.

    'Princess' mates and kittens had of course moved in with the witches now. Princess' litters
    now acting as the witches eyes and ears, there was little that now happened in Glendale
    that these witches didn't know.

    'Princess'' first mate, a plain brown cat named Cain, was only vaguely aware of the world
    around him anymore than any mundane cat, he knew that these humans however were
    making life easier for him, his mate, and his litters, so why complain?

    Cain did it very odd that he didn't feel like fighting to the death over Herodias/Princess,
    but something about her presence made him shrug off these urges to battle... besides... his
    brown coat had long since turned to gray, and his muscles decayed and his body grew
    chubby. 'Maybe it's better something about her makes us not want to kill each other... I
    don't think I'd last long... anymore...'

    With Herodias' mates and litters as a security network, and their created leomans as
    bodyguards, and the geass around Joella, their familiars now (they wouldn't make that
    mistake again) and themselves, the witches were certain that they were effectively
    invincible.

    Glass Marie said, "For Joella, and the children she shared the house with now, the 'oath'
    was ultimately just a game that Joella had invented on the spot. the witches had nothing to
    do with it.

    But there was one thing if the witches had found out they would have gone mad if they
    were not already lost in their own world. To the many mad children they had inflicted, by
    using them as a massive joint familiar, they had brought ORDER to them! They had given
    the chaos inside them DIRECTION! It had created a sense of unity and form. It wasn't a hive
    mind, not in the least. But it had brought a sense of clarity back to these broken children's
    minds.

    This 'miracle,' the witches barely noticed, after all, this little world was supposed to be
    perfect already. They also didn't realize how it worked in their favor, since the people of
    Glendale stopped fleeing... and slowly began to trickle back to their home town. And new
    ones were drawn back to Glendale as well, either to add to their leomans, their children, or
    become a new family to support their increasingly large household.

    This would leave Joella as the sole inheritor of a surprisingly impressive estate when the
    witches died. But that's getting ahead of the story.

    It was March by the time that the hunters had their disastrous first encounter with the
    witches. When the spell over Glendale was only twenty-five percent complete more or
    less. And it wouldn't be until around ninety-two or ninety-three percent that the spell had
    completely changed Glendale from what it was and began to build up to expand.

    Of course, Marie had no way to know this, only knowing that it was by the end of the year
    that it would be too late to stop the tiny snow ball from becoming an avalanche.

    Sadly, Draco was now in no condition to go anywhere. Kathrine had suffered a concussion
    and several broken bones from impacting the gargoyle from the hammer of water. And
    Vatican, had lost his wings, and had come dangerous close to losing his head, and with no

    blind, deaf, and mute."

    coming from. In no small part due to realizing this would mean realizing how awful her
    foster parents were, which Joella, born from idealized memories, couldn't comprehend

    "My parents saved me and all my friends and their families from a bunch of bad men who
    were going to kill us! They shouldn't be called bad people for that."

    The hunters all slowly nodded. Wondering where this girl truly came from and why she was
    saying all of this to them out of the blue.

    "So please, please," the girl who had not even given her name said, "JUST LEAVE MY FAMILY

    don't want to see any of you hurt!.... I... I just wanted to say that because I don't like seeing
    people hurt and in spite of what you did you're still people... Please. Just stay away if you
    don't want to get along, at least let us be.... I'm going home now, please don't follow me."

    Linus wondered if this was one of the witches in disguise. When the girl began to back up,
    Kathrine stood up and used her good arm to grab the child.

    Draco's magical sense screamed at Kathrine to stop seeing the protective aura surrounding
    the child like a fortress, but it was too late.

    This contact combined with Kathrine's intent trigger Joella's geass, and Kathrine's arm was

    Kathrine screamed, Marie at once, ignoring that it burned her own hands began to roll her

    Everyone worked quickly and silently and when it was done, Kathrine's was shedding
    streams of tears of pain... If not for her time with Armando, she'd be screaming in agony.

    The small felman body on one side was now devoid of fur, she looked half like some
    strange goblin from fairy tales rather than a human suddenly without fur.

    Linus, Marie, and Joella helped her up. Le-Fay just watched all this confused, not knowing
    why the Big But Not Giant Cat had suddenly caught on fire, but glade she seemed to be
    okay.

    "Please," Joella said at last still not having given her name, BEGGING, "Just live your lives
    and let my family and friends be!"

    Joella ran away, no one stopped her this time.

    Joella had unwittingly given the witches another service... efforts to Kathrine's recovery
    would now add yet another month to the hero's mending on top of the months needed for
    Draco and Vatican to recover....

    Joella returned to Glendale, waved at one of the trees, and it looked like it tried hard to
    wave back, it would have it down in a few months.

    Joella wandered the streets for a bit, looking about town. The buildings were all the same.
    The people were all the same. Besides some newborn lupman with odd fur. The spell was
    only thirty three percent complete at this point after all. But things would be truly
