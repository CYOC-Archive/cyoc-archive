    Day thirty on Mermaid Island. You, Alex, and Yasmin sit together on the beach near the sand-dunes. You've already make sure that the coast is clear and there are no other mermaids around. You'd rather they didn't witness your sudden disappearance.

    All day long, you lounge on the beach, holding Yasmin's hand or hugging her tightly to your body. It's as if you're afraid you might simply vanish without warning, leaving your beloved behind, and you won't allow that.

    Your attention is focused on Yasmin, and hers on you, but Alex is just bored. There's really nothing for her to do, and she frequently heads out into the water to swim around for a bit and break up the monotony of waiting. From time to time, you and Yasmin join her, always with your hands firmly clasped together. She's eager, hyped with anticipation, you can tell.

    Only, as evening draws near, all three of you are starting to become nervous. When the sun sets, Alex suggests that maybe you'll be transported when you fall asleep, and you'll wake up back in the shop.

    When morning arrives and the three of you find yourselves still all on the island together, you lamely suggest that maybe Uncle Jack meant a thirty-one day month.

    So you all remain together on the beach for another day, waiting to be whisked away back home. When the midday sun starts sinking low in the sky and afternoon arrives, you and Alex start shouting aloud, "Uncle Jack! Hey, Uncle Jack!" which eventually gives way to "Okay, ha-ha, but this isn't funny anymore! Really, you can bring us home now! Please? Anybody? Can you hear us! UNCLE JAAAAACK!!!!"

    * * * * * * * * *

    The next day, Yasmin is pissed. She thinks that you've lied to her. She thinks that just when she was really starting to accept her fate and actually getting used to the semi-horrific idea of being a mermaid for literal eternity—it only took her thirty-seven years to arrive in that place mentally and emotionally—you had to come along with some story of false hope that escape was possible.

    Of course, you know that you've told the truth, and Alex backs you up. But it regardless devolves into a huge, messy, mean-spirited argument, and in the end, Yasmin shouts that she wants nothing more to do with either of you, and she crawls off the beach, dives into the ocean, and swims away.

    And you, your hope is utterly deflated. Alex just looks defeated. After Yasmin has been gone for a while and the two of you have shared the beach alone together in uneasy silence, Alex turns to you and says, "John, I don't want to be stuck here forever. Not like this."

    You put an arm around Alex's shoulders and pull her into a half-hug. "It'll be okay. It's not really all that bad."

    Alex swats your arm and pushes you away. "No, John, I'm serious. I don't know how much longer I can last like this. If I have to go another month here without any kind of release, I'm just—I'm just gonna freak out, okay? Crap, maybe I'm already freaking out. What could have happened to Uncle Jack? Why would he just leave us here? Tell me!—Why would he abandon us to this!?"

    "Whoa, slow down, Alex. You're right, you *are* freaking out. But maybe there's no reason to worry yet."

    She grits her teeth and angrily hisses, "No. Reason. To. Worry!?" She starts swatting your arm and your tail with half-hearted slaps and cries out, "We're trapped on a freaky magic island in another dimension! We've been turned into fish! Fucking *fish*, John! Fish don't have any sex organs, at least not the fun kind! And, oh, yeah, in five or six more days, all the goddamn mermaids around here are going to start *filling the whole goddamn ocean* with their eggs! Which—if you'll recall—you are not allowed to fertilize even one of, or else the fucking Queen of the Mermaids is going to fucking MURDER YOU!"

    "Okay, yeah, that's bad," you admit. "But you said it yourself. We're in another dimension. Maybe time is just flowing differently here. You know, like a Narnia, wardrobe situation?" You look at Alex hopefully.

    She stares at you and then shouts, "DON'T SAY THINGS LIKE THAT! Do you even remember how that story goes? Those kids GREW UP in Narnia after living their WHOLE LIVES there!" Alex screams out a wordless yell of anger and frustration, and then she spins onto her belly, plops down onto the sand, and buries her head in her folded arms to cry.

    You don't know what to do or say to comfort her. You just pat Alex on the back and stay with her. All you can do now is wait, and hope that you won't be trapped on this island for too very long, or that Uncle Jack is working on a way to rescue the both of you.

    But something that Alex said has you really worried now. The mermaids' egg-time is coming, and it's only a few short days away.

    One wrong move from your randy, scaly ass, and it will be your head on the chopping-block.

    Maybe it's a good thing after all, you think glumly, that Yasmin hates your guts now.
