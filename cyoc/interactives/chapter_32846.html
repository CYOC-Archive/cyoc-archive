There was no beam of light, no sliver blob of nanites that engulfed you, you simply selected something and you changed, no special effects beyond your own transformation.     Her clothes and personal belonging she THOUGHT she would have been using on her vacation had been perfectly well kept. She hadn't met a single anthro in the building since changing back to human. Made you almost wonder if it was all just some grand mass hallucination. But Spike's screams were far too real for Sally.

The questions were simple enough, how much she enjoyed the park, what could be done to improve it, what could entice her to come again, if she was estranged, tempted, or accepting of anthros because of, or before or after visiting the park. And of course, the twice repeated question of if she felt any instinct or gender behavior side effect. For all truths, Sally felt like herself. She was herself. She just wish the time as Sam hadn't had to end on such a awful note, but she needed time away, away from naked furry bodies, antlers, hooves, bucks, does, and instincts that felt as reasonable as human reason when you had them.

    Putting on a conservative dress, she walked out of the giant building that acted as the looking glass between the outside world, and the inner world of TF Park America.

    "See you around Hannah. I hope you find someone. But right now, I just need to be back with my someone."

    Her car had, also, been perfectly kept for her. She thought about Hannah, and how she planned to stay a doe when she returned to the real world. Sally wondered, had any other former humans already come to that decision? And if so, had any of them already left? It wasn't like there was any law against dramatically altering your DNA. Though she was pretty sure the anthros were gonna have to learn how to wear clothes again when they returned to civilization.
    After all, if they wanted all the rights of humans, these hyper evolved animals would have to accept all the rules of humanity as they had when they were human themselves.

    Sally's family was surprised to see her back early, not that her kids complained, nor did her husband, now he didn't have to be the only one to drive the kids to the video arcade. And of course, he wanted to know what the mystery vacation park was all about. Heh. She knew how the kids would react at first, then react again when they learned there weren't any video games inside the park.
    All the same, in all truths and realities, it was very good to be home.
