Despite its age, it worked very well and Julie could hear waltz music playing softly in the background. She reached her father who took one hand and held it out to her right. She then began to reach with her other hand before her mother cut her off.

    "Your other hand goes up on his shoulder," Melissa Stovall said slowly, "He is the one that will reach around you to hold the small of your back..."

    "Or at the waist at the absolute lowest," Martin added, and waited for Julie to raise her forearm up while letting the rest of her arm hang down, "Many dances will have a similar hold as this... some will be very close while others are held at greater distance. We'll start with some of the basic waltz steps... as it's those that your mother and I know best..."

    "This isn't working," Julie sighed weakly.

    She and her father had practiced for a while and they played through a few of the songs on the record. She tried to mimic her father's steps, but didn't have much in the way of success. Occasionally she stepped on Martin's feet, and she was sure that if he hadn't been wearing strong shoes that he would have winced. After a while, she looked down to better track her feet and so not to step on her father's feat, and while that helped allow her to avoid stepping on Martin's feet, she still found that she couldn't fully match her father's steps and she fell out of step when she had to move her feet to avoid stepping on her father's feet.

    "You're trying to lead, dear," Melissa commented, "you don't lead... He leads."

    "Why? Why does it matter if you're with the one you love?" Julie asked.

    "In reality it probably doesn't matter," Melissa answered, "but by tradition... in ballroom dancing, the men lead. So, in that sense... you don't copy your partner's moves, but follow them."

    Julie looked down for a moment.

    "Why don't you flip the record over and let your father and I show you," Melissa offered, "Neither of us will ever be on Dancing with the Stars... but we can at least give you a demonstration..."

    Julie complied and moved over to the record player. It was on a small stand next to the rest of their family music players, which was in a corner of the room. She quietly flipped the record while her mother moved to take her father's hand to dance with him.

    "Now to remember to follow how move," Melissa spoke as Julie set the new side of the record to begin playing.

    Julie watched, and as the music started, parents danced. This then went in a slow circle, and she followed how her mother moved. Her steps were similar to her father's moves but not quite. They moved sort of half-way in order to follow Martin as he moved, rather than copying Martin's movements. She watched the two moved through the steps.

    Eric's first practice went a little bit better. He had followed as his parents danced together though the first song on the CD before they pulled apart. Nate paused the CD as the first song ended and William and Martha slowly pulled apart at the end. As they pulled apart the two smiled to each other, remembering the wedding, which had been nice, and while Martha's womb was pushing forward with her pregnancy with Nate at that time, it had been a good ceremony. Martha even remembered that William had proposed before she even knew she was pregnant. The dance had reminded Martha of her wedding and the reception, which she truly hoped Jennifer Harper would get to enjoy with her fiancee.

    After they pulled apart, William turned to Eric and gave him a soft smile.

    "Now... that that's with one specific type of ballroom dancing," William said slowly, "it's fairly slow... fairly simple and easy to at least get a rudimentary knowledge of... and it will keep you close to your partner."

    Eric nodded.

    "Now... why don't you take your turn," William asked, "do you want to try to lead... or should we reverse roles and let you follow Martha to get used to it?"

    "I think I can try," Eric said slowly and stepped up to Martha. She offered him her hand, and she carefully took the same sort of position and grip that he had seen his father take. Eric found it was a bit different from what he had experienced in his impromptu dance practice with Julie. Martha was taller than he was, partially that he hadn't had a real growth spurt yet and partially that his mother had grown taller when she became a werecat. Even in her human form she had gained some height on him.

    "It's okay," Martha assured her son, as she noticed his look of unease, "this is to help you get better..."

    "Practice makes perfect," Eric said slowly.

    Martha nodded to Eric and then looked over to Nate, "Rewind to track one, please."

    Nate nodded and pressed the rewind button and the CD player flashed a switch backward from two to one on its demonstrated screen. The first song soon began to play again. Eric didn't necessarily get started right away as tried to remember the steps his father had used, but after Martha gave his hand a gentle squeeze he did begin. He did his best, and did manage to repeat his father's steps, though it meant that he fell slightly behind the beat in the song being played, but his mother didn't do anything to rush him as they went through. That went well for him, though much of it for Eric wasn't instinctively. It was as if he was consciously remembering his father's moves to copy rather than truly dancing, but he figured that since he was just starting that was the way it would go. In time, he'd surely get better. He'd do that for Julie.

    +++++++++++++++++++++++++++

    Copying her mother's steps to follow her father worked a bit better and Julie did feel a bit proud of getting through one waltz without either tripping over her father's feet or her own. She still had to look down on occasion to look down to make sure that she didn't step on her father's feet. She didn't feel completely comfortable with things, but she did feel good about being able to get through the waltz without making too tremendous mistakes.

    "That's a good start," Martin smiled down to her.

    Julie smiled at the compliment and Martin couldn't help but smile back. He had some unease with how young Julie was and how fast her romance with Eric Anderson was progressing. But, at the same time he was happy for the fact that Julie had found love and that she had found a very bright and respectful young man. He wished that this might have waited until she was older, and he knew that the way things were progressing, it was a real possibility that Julie and Eric might not be able to wait until they were sixteen... let alone until they were eighteen.

    "I could still be better," Julie commented.

    "We can keep practicing in the evenings until Halloween comes up," Martin assured her, "with some time and practice... you'll get better."

    "Thanks, dad," Julie smiled and hugged him.

    "Why don't you go on and get ready for bed," Melissa said to Julie, "or deal with any other last minute things you have to do. Your father and I have a few things to clean up down here before I'll come up and talk with you."

    Julie nodded and soon went upstairs.
