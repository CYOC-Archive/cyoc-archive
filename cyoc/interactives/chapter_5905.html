    The alarm rand as normal at 5:00a.m. at the bedside of Kevin Rooste. A lawyer by trade and a long time enemy of the Genie's Master and friend. A long time the Genie had offered to arrange Kevin's untimely death or disappearance, but his Master would not hear of it.

    Kevin got out of bed and dressed in his jogging suit and quickly tied the strings of his tennis shoes. Now he was ready for the morning five mile jog.

    Out the front door of the three story English Tudor mansion he owned since he sued the previous owner. Down the long driveway and out along Short Road he jogged. The early morning air was fresh and made him feel as if he were young again. The streets of the city were almost deserted of cars as he plodded along. Down the Stafford Drive hill and on to the south alone Westnedge Avenue. Then soon the climb to Kline Park and a short rest before heading home.

    Huffing and short of breath Kevin sat down on a park bench overlooking the downtown. As he sat leaning back and soaking up as much fresh air as possible a Squirrel suddenly darted from the crotch of an Elm tree. The sudden noise made Kevin jump to alert status and check out what made the noise.

    A casual gaze about spotted a silver sparkle coming from the Elm tree. As Kevin stood on the back of his park bench he reached high as his fingers touched the shiny object. As he grasp it the Squirrel chattered from a nearby tree.

    "What in Heaven's name do you have up in your tree?" said Kevin as he stepped down with his new treasure. As he opened his hand the sight of an ornate silver charm with a red Ruby stone in it's center laid before him. A long and thin silver chain was attached and after some looking upon his find he put it over his head. The bobble laid cold against his skin but he knew it was worth a lot and now it was his to keep.

    The jog toward home met with a sports cars which almost ran Kevin over when the driver ran a stop sign. As kevin stumbled and fell in his attempt to get missed he cursed the fool driver. "Damn idiot I hope he meets a truck the next time he does that again!"

    Kevin stood up and whirled around at the loud crash from behind him. There in a steaming wreck was the young man after a five ton box truck hit him broadside. Kevin ran over to the accident and watched the truck driver help the injured man from his smoking car. Kevin gave him one of his business cards and told him to call his office later.

    The rest of the jog was uneventful. Kevin returned to his home tired but ready to shower and head for the office. He was about to leave home when his Cel phone rang. A Mrs. Kelly was on the line being very demanding and wanting a mid morning appointment. After Kevin finally agreed and was on his way the thought of the old bitch caused him to think the worst for her. "Damn her, I wish that old bitch would get humped by her Great Dane, she could use a good screwing!"

    The office was busy and time flew by quickly. Just before noon a call from the Kelloge Hospital announced that Mrs. Kelly was in the hospital as the victim of a dog attack. Kevin interviewed the caller to see if he had a good suit case against the dog's owner. Instead the office person laughed as she explained how when Mrs. Kelly came out of her shower and bent over to dry her feet the family Great Dane jumped her and humped her. The big dog got his knot all the way in and unloaded into the 79 year old lady. The Hospital was keeping her for a day or two but the dog was sent to the local pound.
