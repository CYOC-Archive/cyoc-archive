    Spike was large, and a series of eight pointed antlers to boot! Jeremy recognized this fact
    even before they faced each other. Spike was fast in spite of his size, and efficient.

    However, Jeremy didn't even quiver. After finally closing Lance's mouth, Jeremy had
    climbed his way through the ranks, and now just two more fights and he.... what? Proved
    he was the most worthy of beauty queen Hannah? That he was alpha buck or whatever you
    called it with deer? He knew that they had started this whole thing just to show off to
    beauty queen Hannah. But it honestly felt like to Jeremy that it had grown beyond that.
    True, there was a sense of dissatisfaction without the does watching, but the fact they had
    continued when they had left for some reason or another, and no one had suggested
    stopping the tournament, suggested there was more to this than just a typical frat macho
    contest. The way he look at Lance now, seeing a subordinate, and the way Lance acted
    towards him, seeing only a superior, while the same time their rational minds (it didn't feel
    right calling it the 'human' part of them) knew about their actual ranking in frat, was proof
    of that.

    Jeremy had always been considered the runt of the litter, someone for the senior members
    of the frat, in particular Lance, to torture. However, those attitudes were changing before
    his very eyes with each buck he brought down. He knew rational, logically, and very much
    inevitably, that once their mass vacation was over, and their ultimately returned to collage,
    Lance would make Jeremy's life Hell for this humiliation. But at this moment in time,
    Jeremy realized he just didn't care.

    "I hope you enjoyed your time as a rising star." Said Spike too calmly. Pulling Jeremy out of
    his introspection. "Because this is where you call in line with the rest. All the same, thank
    you for shutting up that blow hard Lance. Summit now, and you'll save yourself a lot of
    hurt."

    "No way." Jeremy said on reaction alone. "I never thought I'd get this far, I'm going to see
    how far I can get!"

    Spike put one hooved foot in front of another and hunched over lowering himself, not
    trying to use his size to intimidate Jeremy like Jeremy thought Spike would.

    "If that's the way you want it. Then let's begin." Spike lowered his head, as did Jeremy and
    they charged.

    The scarred rival was intense, as the younger buck felt himself being pushed back each
    time their antlers made contact, his heart pounded. His armored fingers and 'toes' left
    deep ruts in the dirt.

    The younger buck realized the scarred rival's pattern was predictable. While the younger
    buck knew he couldn't match him in strength, and the younger buck didn't have the size
    to throw the scarred rival like the no-longer-doe had that rival, each attack was the same.
    And unlike the four leg bucks, the younger buck was two legs, and thus was quite a bit
    better at learning quickly.

    When the scarred rival charged next, the younger buck dropped as low he could, and stuck
    his antlers upwards.

    The younger buck's antlers stabbed past the skin of the larger buck, the force letting him
    stab deeply. However, the scarred rival didn't cry out. Jeremy almost shook his head, had
    he, had he killed him?! Oh no, oh-'

    Jeremy felt two muscular hands with armored finger tips grab him by the shoulders, which
    pulled his antlers away from the bleeding holes in the larger buck's chest, giving him a full
    view of something he'd rather never see close up.

    "Bout time you noticed." He heard Spike whisper, just as Jeremy felt his body being lifted
    off the ground.

    'He, he -let- me stab him, just so he could get close enough to grab me!'

    Spike was dangerous. He must have predicted, or gambled, on how Jeremy would place his
    antlers to intercept! Spike was willing to gamble everything to gain everything, this make
    him incredibly dangerous.

    "How." Jeremy had just enough time to get out.

    "Behold the wonders of nature's invention the rib cage."

    And Jeremy was throw heads over hooves, hearing the slightest crack in one of his antlers
    as he hit the ground, kicking up dirt and grass. Somehow safely landing on his back,
    feeling none too comfortable on his tail, he looked up at the sky, and soon enough was
    staring up into Spike's eyes. "That Kung-Fu?"

    "Kenpo. Get up." Spike said simply.

    Without a second thought, Jeremy obeyed, in a heart beat Lance was his side helping him
    stand and letting the superior buck lean on him as Jeremy got his bearings.

    Jeremy kept looking into Spike's eyes, and finally, just lowered his head. Lance helped him
    over to the group of the bucks who had been beaten by both Jeremy and Spike accepting
    him with open arms. Jeremy knew what he was feeling, as far as the frat, no, the HERD was
    concerned, Spike was his superior.

    Spike looked at Sam. Then Spike looked at his own chest and the holes in his skin left by
    Jeremy. He looked at Sam again. "Looks like you're at an advantage." For some reason,
    Sam didn't believe that. "We shouldn't keep the does waiting. Shall we begin?" Still in that
    cool, calm voice. He truly did act more like a buck on two legs than a human given half a
    deer's DNA.

    This was it, Sam and Spike were now the most highest ranking bucks in the herd. The
    group mentality of the deer combined with the sociological habits of the primate. They
    effective now had an equal amount of respect and loyalty from the rest. But there was
    room for only one at the top.
