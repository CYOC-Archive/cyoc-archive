    More dingo, less Stan

    With his human mind still whirling with the new experience of wielding dingo ears and a tail, Stan didn't notice anything amiss in with his legs and feet until they suddenly weakened and collapsed beneath him. He yelped and flattened his ears at the sudden unbearable pain of his new tail getting body-slammed between his back and the desk chair he had toppled into. Almost reflexively, he grabbed the thing and yanked it out from behind him so it could stick out to the side. But he yanked too hard, yelped again, and had to let go.

    "Ow! ... Well, now I know first-hand why you're not supposed to do that." Stan was reminded of his toddler years, when the family dog used to bite him for pulling on its tail.

    Once the pain subsided, his attention returned again to his feet. He saw they were getting much longer. Fur overtook them and from the toes stretched his claws. Some joints clicked, and his ankles now resembled backwards knees.

    "Ha, ha, I'm dog-legged!"

    He didn't have as much time to admire his new feet before his mouth and knows started swelling. Before he knew it, he could run his hands up and down a long bony ridge from eyes to a small, rough, moist noise. Looking once more at his reflection, he was impressed by the long muzzle that stretched out from him. The short gray fur around his nose and mouth was a pleasant complement to the tawny fur on his other dingo parts. It looked sort of gruff, but also a little distinguished, like an old man's beard. He lowered his jaw, thrilled by how far he could open his mouth. Those formidable teeth were fantastic! He got another surprise when he stuck out his tongue and saw how long the thing was.

    A strangely familiar but seemingly unrecognizable scent seemed to be all around him. He sniffed. He could smell it on the keyboard, he could smell it on his table, and it was really strong when he sniffed his hands. Finally, with a fair amount of concentration, he began to realize it was his own scent. He could smell everything else in the room, too: the plastic of the computer, the wood of the table, the cotton cloths of the mattress. His own scent was mixed with all of these, since he spent so much time here.

    "Wow, this nose is going to be distracting," he thought.

    Deciding to get back up out of his chair again, he braced himself and gently maneuvered his weight onto those graceful new paws--and clumsily stumbled forward, catching himself with his hands on the desk. He adjusted his posture and managed to stand rather stably with his weight on his altered legs. His feet felt oddly cushy, like standing in really comfortable slippers. "Pawpads," he realized with a grin. Gingerly, he took a step forward. Clearly, dingo legs are not for bipedalism: keeping his balance was a challenging task, to say the least. But he made it work, and listened delightedly to the clicking of his claws along the floor.

    Just then, there was another sound; Stan stopped and perked his dingo ears.
