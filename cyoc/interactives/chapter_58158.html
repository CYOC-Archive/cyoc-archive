    Present Day

    Abdul did not like the look of things. Their hike was intended to follow the game trails that the Pack had used to mark the borders of their claimed hunting grounds. And these trails were ones that all of the Pack knew by heart, and the teen wolves would soon be learning those trails as well. Unfortunately, they were somehow off of those trails... and the male Omega couldn't explain it.

    "We're lost," Abdul grumbled as he sniffed around trying to find some familiar scent. No real luck.

    "You know how to get back to the manor, don't you?" Mary asked nervously.

    "Yes," Abdul nodded to the young Leoman, "we aren't THAT lost. It's just that we got turned around somewhere and ended up off of our Pack's game trails..."

    "This a total bloody disaster!" Nane spoke, "all the deer have fled, and we're lost. In a dark, and very expensive forest."

    "Cheer up, Nane, it could be worse," Saba chuckled.

    "Oh, how could it possibly be worse?" Nane chuckled again.

    "There's a clearing up ahead..." Lilly pointed.

    "A road?" Abdul wondered.

    "Let's go see!" Jay volunteered and took off at a jog, forcing the rest to jog after him to make sure he didn't get hurt.

    They ended up moving into a clearing that looked like it had been cleared into a little farm. They could see stalks of corn, wheat, barely, vegetables and tomatoes, even if the weather or time of year wasn't conducive to the growth of some of the plants that were there.

    "It's a farm..."

    "For people who love the color, pink," Nane commented, spotting the farmhouse.

    "Hello," came a semi nervous voice from nearby.

    The whole group turned their heads to see a small purple horse with what looked like a pink and white wheel on her rear. But there were things that marked this horse as unnatural to the Lupmen and Leomen present. It had no noticeable hooves. Its legs were largely four stumps the same color as the rest of it, yet as it slowly approached them, it's feet made the same sound as what a normal horse would have made as it walked. Other then its mane and tail, which were rather stylized for a horse, it didn't seem to have any fur. It it did, it had to be very fine so that it wouldn't be visible. Mary was also certain that no breed of horse, donkey, or mule had a mane or tail that had hair that curled slightly at the end.

    It's head also didn't resemble a normal horse's. It's ears were short triangles, while normal horse's ears were more like a football then a triangle. Its muzzle was more cartoonish in shape then what a normal horse's head was shaped, and its eyes largely faced forward. Normal horses... and most other potential prey animals, had their eyes on the sides of their head to give them a better range of vision, allowing them to potentially see an approaching predator. Forward facing eyes was normally a hallmark of predatory animals and Primates (though Humans, Chimpanzees, and various Baboons were both Primates AND predators) to allow them to gage the distances to their prey, or a branch they intended to swing to.

    "Who are you?" the horse asked in a feminine voice.

    "Omega Whitney/Abdul, Omegas of the Big Bad Wolf Pack."

    "Jay." "Pence." "Cassandra." "Lilly." "Giselle." "Gretchen." "Toni." "Paula." "Laura." "Zulema."

    "Pups of the Big Bad Wolf Pack," Abdul spoke.

    "I am Mary Hardy of the Glendale Pride, and these are my sisters, Moja, Mbili, Tatuli, and Nneli... well half-sisters, but I love them like they were full sisters. And it would be uncivilized to treat them poorly."

    Moja hugged her.

    "I am Tano Rathmensen of the Glendale Pride, and these are my sisters, Sita, Saba, and Nane."

    Jay having the most bravery carefully sniffed in the horse's direction and then asked, "Who are you?"

    "What are you?" Mary asked.

    "My name is Destiny," the horse said, "and I'm a My Little Pony. A real one. This is our farm, Pony Land."

    "Wasn't that a cartoon?" Saba asked.

    "They aren't interesting unless you're eating them."

    "NANE!"

    "What? Uncle Kirk said that to Aunt Penny the other day."

    "Now, now, we do not want to be insulting," Whitney told the red orange cub.

    "It's alright," Destiny answered, "We like to keep Pony Land hidden. So not many know of us."

    Abdul then looked out and saw a good many similar horses engaged in farm work, hoeing, harvesting, and other such activities, with another purple one and a red one playing tag. He wondered how such a farm could be hidden, and how they could use any sort of tool when it didn't look like they had fingers or thumbs, but he decided to let it go.

    "But, I was wondering about what is going on outside our farm," Destiny answered.

    "Don't you get a newspaper?" Abdul asked, "or the radio? Or television?"

    Destiny shook her head.

    "No television?" various pups lamented.

    "Do you have books?" Mary asked.

    Destiny again shook her head, and Mary looked down at that.

    "Things have been rather hectic," Abdul commented, "at least lately. The Game came alive and attacked the world."

    "The game?"

    "Transform or Dare," Abdul answered.

    "What is that?"

    "A curse of the worst degree," Mary shuddered, "it attacked the world... the Pride and the Pack specifically. Everyone went to go fight it and some monster named Kurse. Nearly all of them are back at Wolf Manor recovering from the battle."

    "That doesn't sound like "happily ever after"."

    "That will come when the game is gone and dead," Tano spoke, "a elderly human woman has found a cure for the game. They intend to use it soon, but I don't know precisely when. You might want to invest in a radio or something. I'm sure they'll announce when they're going to bless the town on it."

    "I don't know if Never-Star and Valentines have any money," Destiny said slowly.

    "Then they need to seek out a Madame Serenity," Tano told the horse, "she will know when the game is ended."

    Destiny nodded, "would you like to stay with us?"

    "We can not leave our responsibilities," Abdul and Whitney answered.

    "We can not leave our parents," Gretchen, Giselle, Laura, Zulema, and Cassandra, and Lilly spoke.

    "I don't think I could go for long without something to read, and I can't leave my parents behind," Mary said.

    Her half sisters nodded, though none of them knew that the magic of the My Little Pony's wishes couldn't change the Lupmen or Leomen as that would represent a change in species, even if they wanted to stay in Pony Land.
