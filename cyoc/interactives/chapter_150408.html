    Well with her own transformation into a drak the Empire became the Mecca for the world of Gihon's rather
    downtrodden drak population.

    The problem was that old drak legends in Europe had
    a sort of Doctor Jeckyll,Mister Hyde dual personality to them.
    In the legends a drak was either
    a) a slender river nymph who entired her victims into a
    river with her seductive good looks and then drowned her victims
    or
    b) a huge scaly 14 foot tall river dragon woman-troll
    who simply lurked beneath the surface,plucked her unsuspecting victims out of a passing boat and ate them and left behind their bones in her underwater cave.

    So not surprisingly before Empress Giselda came along the
    usual solution to humans turning into a drak
    ( actually a seven to eleven foot tall anthro fish-lizard gill woman )
    was to just burn the emerging beast at the stake
    lest either folk legend a) or b) come to pass.

    So there weren't too many draks older then a few months
    post-transformation
    to set the record straight on what a drak was
    basically a ill tempered scaly giantess with gills and fins
    and webbing.
    So when Giselda the Drak Empress arriving on the scene
    in 1900 draks around the world made sure to make their
    way to the nearest Emsland submarine or airship
    to get out of a hostile human populace's pitchforks and torches
    *****************************
    Gihon-World War One,the Great Gihon-War
    began with the most unlikely of circumstances in Emsland.
    Giselda had invited Edward,the Crown Prince of
    Gihon Britain over to Hapsburg Castle on the tenth
    anniversary of her transformation into a drak.
    So August 8th 1910.
