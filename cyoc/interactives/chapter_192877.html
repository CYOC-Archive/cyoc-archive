    “Hey bro, you okay?” Todd asked, extending a paw for Jason to grab. Jason graciously took his friend’s paw as the large male hoisted the wolf off the ground and back onto his feet.

    “Thanks Todd,” Jason responded. He was thankful that he had fur on his face, otherwise his friend would have seen his blushing face. “I’m sorry about running into you. I was lost in thought and wasn’t paying full attention to where I was going.”

    “Don’t worry man, this week has been stressful for all of us,” the kangaroo responded. “Thankfully it’s Friday, the school day is over, and we’ve got all next week off! Thank God for Heat Week, am I right?”

    “Heat…Week?” Jason asked.

    “Damn bro, you really are out of it today,” Todd said. “Come on, let’s get outta here before the bell rings. I’ll explain as we walk.”

    Keeping his arm around Jason’s shoulder, the kangaroo led the wolf down the main hallway of the school and out the front door into the cool autumn air.

    “Brrr…it sure is cold out today, huh?” Todd asked, exaggerating a shiver. “Winter is almost here. You’re lucky you’ve got such a coat of fur to keep you warm.”

    Jason chuckled, trying to keep his mind off of how close he was to Todd. “So, remind me what Heat Week is again?”

    “Right, so Heat Week is when a lot of animals go into heat. Because of this, it’s hard for both female students and teachers to focus. While there are some prescriptions to help, they aren’t widely available. So, we get a week off school to let Heat Week pass.”

    “Oh right, how could I forget!” Jason said, lying. “So what are you planning to do with your week off?”

    “Actually, that’s something I wanted to ask you about,” Todd said. “You know how my parents always go out of town on a nice vacation during Heat Week? Well, they left town this morning, and they won’t be back until next Monday, meaning I’ll have the house to myself. So I was wondering: would you want to stay over at my place for the week?”

    Jason’s heart was pounding as he realized what was happening. He knew that he shouldn’t accept. He couldn’t risk his best friend discovering that he was gay. And yet…

    “Sure, I’d love to!” Jason said.

    Todd grinned. “Awesome man! Why don’t you text your parents and let them know? We’ll head back to your place so you can pack a bag.”

    “Yeah, I’ve got enough clothes to last me for the week,” Jason responded.

    Todd smiled and wrapped his arm back around Jason’s shoulder, making the young wolf blush again.

    “We’re gonna have a great week bro,” Todd said, leading Jason in the direction of his house.
