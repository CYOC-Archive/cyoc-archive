    Alice speaks softly as she looks down at you. "You are an awful being, aren't you? Not that nothing can be done against that..." With an almost manic grin she continued, "I'll just have to remove everything that makes you so awful, your masculinity, your personality, your humanity. I'm sure I can find the perfect place here for you in our little family. And don't worry, you won't feel a thing... or at least, you won't remember feeling it."

    It's with these last words that the final dots connect. She's going to wipe your memory, make you like them. That's what she must have done with Doe and maybe even all the others. It makes way too much sense.

    "YOU MONSTER!" you scream at the top of your lungs. "DOE! DOE, IT'S A LIE! YOU'RE NO-"

    WHAP! Pain shoots through your face as Alice gives you a hard slap, her smooth latex skin making the impact even worse. You breathe in to scream again as soon as you've recovered, but Alice covers your mouth. She gets a frustrated look in her eyes and tries to maintain her composure, but you can tell she's furious at you.

    "A clever one, aren't we? But I want you to know one thing, none of the girls here was forced against her will. They all came here and joined our family willingly, even our little Doe. Every girl has been horribly, horribly hurt by the despicable males of the world out there. I just offered them a chance at a new life, unbound by this society and freed of their emotional burdens. And then you came and tried to tempt her, hurt her, almost forced her back into a life that was only pain for her. You deserve only the worst punishment and I already have an idea.

    “I will make you see how wonderful this place is. But just turning you into another of my girls and wiping your memories won't be enough. Parts of your original personality would stay intact and I can't risk you trying to corrupt my dear sisters again.” Alice begins creeping uncomfortably close to you, getting closer with each line. “More drastic measures will be necessary. I will carve out your brain, hollow out your mind, ensure that nothing of ‘you’ will ever return. Then I will re-educate you until you fully appreciate our way of living, as it will be the only one you’ll ever know. And trust me, I’m being generous right now. What I’m about to do is still way too good for you.”

    Your eyes go wide as Alice forcefully grabs your face. In the corners of your vision you can see latex spreading out of her hand, clinging to your head as more and more of it is being covered in the white substance.

    “Do not fear. I’m turning you into something that can fill the hole you ripped into Doe’s heart.” Those are the last words you hear before the latex covers your eyes and deafens you. You see Alice sneering down at you, taking obvious pleasure in what she is doing to you. It is the last you see before the latex also covers your eyes, turning everything dark. Stuck in complete sensory isolation you are left solely to your thoughts, but holding onto those becomes harder and harder until those too slip from you leaving you completely blank.

    Doe didn't know what to expect when she was being summoned by Alice again. The only thing she wants to do at the moment is look herself up in her room and nurse her aching heart. The betrayal of that man is stinging deep and the thought of Alice forcing her to face him yet again only makes it worse. On the way some of her fellow rubber anthro girls have been giving her their most sincere apologies, but it barely helped. Bracing for the worst she opens the door.

    Inside she finds Alice alone sitting in the chair he was originally strapped to holding and cradling in her arms a brown shape.

    Confused, Doe looks around the whole room until she speaks up. “W-Where is he?”

    Alice puts a finger to her lips. “Psst! She's sleeping.” She reveals the shape in her arms to be another rubber doe. Though this one is clearly only a child, about primary school age. She looks like a younger version of Doe, but with darker fur and a couple white spots spread across her face.

    Alice softly speaks up. “Who cares where that man went? The only thing that matters now is that he is gone and that this cute, little fawn will need an older sister to take care of her.” She looks Doe deep into her eyes. For a moment it seems like all light vanishes from her eyes as her mind is being prevented from making the connection between the man’s disappearance and the fawn’s appearance.

    But as soon as it happened Doe is back to normal. “Of course I can take care of her.”

    The little fawn stirrs and with a cute yawn wakes up. She looks around before settling on the rubber anthro rabbit holding her and asks in a squeaky voice, “Where am I?... Who am I?... What am I?”

    “You're our dear little rubber Fawn and that's all that's important.” Alice boops her on the nose. “You’re safe now and with girls who will take care of you, like your older sister over there.”

    The moment Fawn lets her gaze fall on the Doe and immediately feels a deep connection to the rubber doe. And why shouldn't she, they're sisters, after all.

    “Big… Sis?” She jumps down from Alice’s lap. Fawn clearly has some problems keeping her balance on her hoofs, as if she's walking with them for the first time, but still manages to tumble forward to Doe without falling and hugs her. “Big Sis…”

    Doe can feel a warmth enter her, filling first her body and ultimately even her wounded heart. She kneels down and puts her own arms around Fawn to complete the embrace. Who needs these awful men when she has an adorable younger sister to pamper and give all her love to.

    Alice watches this heartwarming scene with joy. Her girls being happy is what genuinely makes her happy. And now nothing can disturb their happiness, she'll always make sure of it.
