Even going so far as to make entreaties with Lamashtu Morningstar,” Gemyem finally adding.

    “Ammit Morningstar,” Gemyem sounding defeated; “before our agents could arrest and execute her, she fled the third circle to who knows where. But before she fled, she opened up every prison cell and freed all the human slaves.”
    “He was right,” Nergal muttering to himself; “that slimy centipede demon was right.”
    “Who was right my lord,” Gemyem not certain if she should press for information or just beat a hasty retreat.
    “Never you mind,” Nergal sighing as he finally opens the scroll he was given; perusing the contents; they confirmed everything that his grand chancellor has stated. His grip on the nine circles was tenuous and slipping. He had days before he would soon be seeing a besieging army at the gates of Dis.
