    Life Begins At 40: A Day Long Dreaded

    Branch Full view

    From the Writing.com story of the same name.

    Summery: Paul Taylor was a normal 17 year old guy in his last year of a normal High School in the UK. He had
    good mates, a hot girlfriend and bright future. In short Paul had a perfect life until a freak accident
    changed his life forever, leaving him stuck in the body of a middle-aged woman.

    Unlike most Body Swap stories, his friends and family are fully aware of the swap and the story picks up
    several weeks after the accident as he prepares to re-enter his life in his new body. How he copes is up to
    you.

    This is a theme that I am thinking about writing a story for posting on Fictionmania and I am interested in
    getting some ideas for content. As such I'm going to give a little guidance to push the story in the right
    direction. Paul is not happy with this swap and this is not a story where he suddenly adapts and enjoys his
    situation. As far as he is concerned, his life has been ruined.

    How will Paul adjust to being a middle-aged woman? Will he stick it out and finish school? How will cope with
    wearing female clothes and, worse, the school uniform? How will his friends and family treat him? Will his
    girlfriend still want to be with him now that not only is he a woman, but he's also old enough to be her
    mother?

    Are they're any hangovers from the life of the woman who's body he inhabits? Is he stuck with her habits and
    addictions, her like and dislikes? How about the family of the woman? Do they want him to try and live her
    life instead?

    Characters
    Paul Taylor - A 17 year old High School student in the final year of high school cruelly swapped into the body
    of a middle-aged woman. Until the accident he was popular, bright and a talented footballer (soccer), poised
    to go to university on finishing his A-Levels and looking forward to pursuing a career as a physiotherapist.

    Rachel Jenkins - One of the the schools hottest girls and Paul's 18 year-old girlfriend. She is popular but
    friendly and well liked by most. They have been dating for a year and their relationship has recently become
    intimate. Rachel was looking forward to going to uni with Paul later this year, but he has refused to see her
    since the swap and she is anxious about the future.

    Colin Howards and Dave Reynolds - Pauls best friends since they were kids. Both play on the same football team
    as Paul and are pretty typical 17 year old boys, interested in girls, cars and turning 18 this year so they
    can finally go to the pub legally. They have also not seen Paul since the accident.

    Linda Parks - Paul's 38 year old mother and part-time receptionist at his school. Linda raised Paul on her own
    after his father left them when he was a baby but has recently remarried and, whilst she and Paul are very
    close, she was looking forward to him going to university so she can give more attention to her new husband.
    Initially shocked to find her son is now a woman older than her, Linda has been Pauls main support since the
    accident. She is convinced he should finish school and continue with his plans for life and is determined to
    help him adjust to being a woman.

    John Parks - Linda 39 year old new husband and reluctant step-father. John and Paul have a strained
    relationship, but not openly unfriendly. He met Linda through friends two years ago and enjoyed a whirlwind
    romance that resulted in their marriage six months ago. He was also looking forward to Paul leaving home and
    was hoping to convince Linda to move abroad when he did.

    Of course add your own characters as the stories develop. Some choices for Paul's new body are provided in the

    Chapter 1:

    As the grey light of dawn crept into his bedroom Paul woke from an uneasy sleep and stared up at the ceiling,
    feeling sick with anxiety. He had been dreading this day for weeks, ever since that fateful day four weeks ago
    when his life was ruined forever. Paul had taken his girlfriend shopping in celebration of her 18th birthday.
    It had been a pretty good day, despite waiting for hours whilst Rachel endlessly tried on clothes and shoes.
    They had finished up with a drink in the pub and Rachel took great pleasure in teasing him with the fact it
    would be another 2 months before he could legally join her.

    It was when they left the pub, Paul made a decision that would change his life forever. It was an early
    december evening and the weather was atrocious. Cold rain was hammering around them and forming large puddles.
    Paul knew the truck was in trouble as soon as he saw it. No one would ever know what had caused the driver to
    lose control but the wet leaves on the road and heavy rain meant he had no chance of recovering once his
    ungainly load started to jackknife. Paul's first thought was what was such a big truck doing driving down a
    small town centre road. But that thought didn't last long because then he noticed the woman standing in
    directly its path. She was talking on her mobile phone and holding an umbrella, completely oblivious to the
    overturned truck bearing down on her.

    Paul had only seconds to react and had set off at running without even shouting a warning. Young, athletic and
    fit, he had easily closed the distance between him and the hapless woman before the truck straight. Tackling
    her at full pace they had both landed heavily in a large and deep muddy puddle just as the heavy vehicle
    slammed into an adjacent wall with an appalling crash, spilling its strange load of machinery into the puddle.
    Soaked and heart racing with adrenaline, Paul sighed with relief and lifted himself up to ask the woman
    underneath him if she was OK. However, before he could, she screamed pointing behind him. Paul span round just
    in time to see an electricity pole dislodge by the crash fall, sparking, in to the mix of broken electronics
    and muddy water in which they were lying. Paul's last thought, before the high voltage electricity shuddering
    through his body rendered him unconscious, was that being electrocuted to death should be more painful.

    Paul had been in the coma for a week when he finally awoke and he soon wished that he hadn't. His mum standing
    over him in tears told him to be brave and the doctor told him to prepare himself for a shock before showing
    him the mirror. He'll never forget that scream. Mainly because he had never screamed like that before. He had
    touched his face in disbelief that the reflection of the woman in the mirror was now his own. In the end, the
    doctor was forced to sedate him before he went back into shock.

    Hours later, once he had calmed down and still under the fog of sedation they had explained what had happened.
    The truck was carrying top-secret experimental equipment ironically designed for accessing the minds of
    comatose patiences. The mixture of electricity and water had somehow swapped his mind into the body of the
    woman he had saved. Or at least that was the theory. No one could quite figure out how it had happened. They
    had only realised this had happened the day before when the woman had awoken in his own body and started
    raving that she was someone else. They certainly didn't know how to reverse it. Not that it mattered. Even
    sedated he had been horrified to hear that the woman had not survived the shock and the heart of his injured
    former body had give out just hours after being sedated. He was stuck like this for life.

    Paul had spent most of his time in the weeks that had followed sobbing on suicide watch. His mum, Linda, had
    been a rock staying with him most days and trying to convince him his life was far from over. That being a
    middle aged woman would not be so bad and that it needn't change the plans for his life. Paul thought she was
    talking crap. How could things ever be the same again. But he had finally agreed with her, if only to convince
    the hospital to let him leave and go home.

    That had been a week ago. Today he would go back to school. Mainly against his will. He knew his mum was
    talking sense, that he needed the qualifications to even attempt the life he wanted but he was still dreading
    it. "Just try it!" his mum had begged him, "It's six more months and then you're free to do whatever you want
    with your life. If you hate it as much as you think then you can leave and you'll be no worst off." Paul could
    never refuse his mum, even now he was older than her. He almost completely refused when he found out that the
    school would still insist on a middle aged woman wearing the girls uniform. But as always his mum had talked
    him round. He was dreading the reaction of the people at school when he walked through the doors looking like
    this. By now everyone knew what had happened. He sat up with the uncomfortable feeling of his female body
