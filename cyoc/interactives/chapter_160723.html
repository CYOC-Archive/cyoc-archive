Giving Freddy the number, Michael watched as his husband dialed the number on his own communication device and then waited for the call to go through.

    In an apartment in Sacramento, a little boy could be seen running around the living room.

    “Slow yourself down, young man!” a woman could be heard yelling from another room.

She appeared to be middle aged, despite being hundreds of years old. The years since the age freezing spell had been undone saw her grow up rapidly from an eternal teenager to an older, but still beautiful woman. Although she looked like a mother, most people who had known her would still be able to recognize her. This woman, the mother of this child, was Sofya, the daughter of the evil wizard.

    “I said—“ Sofya tried to say again, before a buzzing could be heard.

    “Mom” the boy said “I think you have a call coming in.”

    Sofya looked down at her communication device, confused as to who might be calling her today. She didn’t have a lot of friends. The ones who she still spoke to on a regular basis knew to call her at night, when her son was already sleeping. She herself was not a big sleeper, so she could talk late into the night without issue. For someone to call her now was extremely unusual, to say the least.

    When she saw the screen of her device light up, the name “Freddy Benson” appeared.

    “Freddy Benson?” Sofya thought to herself, as she picked up her communication device from the coffee table. “I haven’t heard from him in years. I wonder what he wants now.”

    Answering the call, Sofya did her best to put on a friendly demeanor.

    “Freddy!” she exclaimed happily. “I haven’t heard from you in ages!”

    “Mom?” Sofya’s son asked. “Who is that on the phone?”

    Sofya shushed him, using some magic to send him away. Her son ran into his bedroom and shut the door behind him.

    “Uh, hi Sofya” Freddy replied nervously, as Michael held onto his hand tightly. “Listen, I—“

    “I haven’t heard from you in so long” Sofya interrupted. “It’s been over twenty years now. I really thought I’d never hear from you again. How are things going these days?”

    “Alright, I guess. Listen, I don’t have a lot of time to catch up right now. I just really need your help.

    “Oh.”

    “So that’s what this call is all about” Sofya continued, with a bitterness in her voice.

    “I mean, yes” Freddy corrected “but I’d like to catch up with you, too. It’s just that I have a crisis on my hands and you’re the only person I know who can help me.”

    “The only person? What kind of problem could you possibly have that—“

    “Someone stole the Ruggeri ring from the National Museum in Rome again.”

    The call fell silent, as Sofya processed her thoughts. When she took a moment to compose herself, she responded carefully.

    “Wow. That is a pretty big problem.”

    “It is, Sofya, and that’s why I need your help.”

    “Let me guess, to track it down?”

    “When was it stolen, exactly?”

    “Well, about six months ago. It—“

    “We were thinking of trying to take care of this ourselves” Michael said with an irritated tone in his voice.

    “Uh, who is that?”

    “That’s Michael” Freddy replied. “He’s here too.”

    “Hi” Michael said coldly.

    “Oh, hi Michael” Sofya said nonchalantly. “I’m assuming you two got married or something.”

    “We did, Sofya. Fifteen years ago.”

    “Oh, well congratulations for you. Anyways, what the hell, Freddy? You never should have waited this long to—“

    “The past is the past, Sofya” Michael interrupted.

    “We just need your help now” Freddy added. “When can we come over to talk about this?”

    “Right now, actually” Sofya replied. “I’m just at home watching my son. I don’t have any other plans this evening. If you guys can stop by now, we can talk this over in person.”

    “In our hometown.”

    “Seriously? You never moved out of that God forsaken place?”

    “Actually” Michael corrected “we lived in Sacramento for about ten years before we moved back to our hometown ten years ago.”

    “Oh my fucking God” Sofya said. “You two lived so close to me all these years and you never thought to stop by and pay me a visit. Jesus.”

It’s to prevent potential terrorists from just crash landing anywhere they feel like it. These things, although they aren’t as big as commercial planes, can still do a lot of damage under the wrong hands.”
