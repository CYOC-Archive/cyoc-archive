    Welcome to "Little Asia"

    Because the name of the Asian Colony was still undecided, it's unofficial name was Little Asia. But what mattered was the official tour of he colony, which was the official First Look created by Animalia Ambassadoria. But while the official coverage only lasted for two days until Fall break was over, there were plans to spend a week there during the summer.

    ---

    One of the big things that was covered was the Hot Springs Inn. While this confirmed that the volcano on the island was still good to go, so to speak, it was confirmed to be safely dormant. While the spring was big enough to hold eight Animalians comfortably, there was absolutely no sex there, much to the slight disappointment of some people.

    ---

    Another thing that was shown was the martial arts monastery that had been included as part of the elements introduced from China. While it was similar to the Golden Mountain Dojo back in Animalia, it had several features that were more in line to the original Shaolin Monastery. But there were also some modern amenities that weren't available in medieval days simply because they weren't invented yet, as well as some things to keep tourists from getting hurt from sparring with the "masters" there.

    ---

    The other thing that was covered was the Ninja House, as part of the elements included from Japan. Officially, as it was back then, it was a landed noble house, but there were features that made it like a living museum. There were also included the false walls, hidden doors, and concealed hiding places, they weren't to the extent of those who got their ideas from reading graphic novels.

    ---

    There were also several "attacks" from both Chinese and Japanese "demons" who were actually monkey Animalians in costumes. These were also times to demonstrate the Asian version of the Rhino Guard. While there weren't any rhinos officially there, these were highly trained martial artists who were converted to function in a similar way. These were often those who were demonstrating either sparring at the dojo or samurai training, but they also demonstrated they were quite capable of dueling with "live steel" and dealing with "troublemakers".

    ---

    While the colony was already getting to be a popular tourist attraction, they couldn't stay as long as they would have liked. First of all, fall break was only four days long and they had to get back to school. Second of all, Spookyville was starting soon, and they wanted to get ready for it.
