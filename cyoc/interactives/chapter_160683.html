    Standing up, Michael left his parents bedroom and headed back downstairs. Evan sat on the couch, while his mother was in the kitchen.

    “I was making Evan some brunch” Michael’s mother called out. “Did you want some before you leave?”

    Michael groaned, as he looked at Evan with an annoyed expression on his face.

    “Are you serious?” Michael asked. “You just ate two hours ago.”

    “I was hungry” Evan complained.

    “It’s alright, Michael” his mother replied assuringly. “I don’t mind at all. Your growing boy should get all the food he can eat. He’s so skinny. It’s like you starve him or something.

    “I’ll make extra if you want some” his mother said, ignoring his comments.

    “I’m good. I should be getting back now, anyway. I don’t want to leave the house unattended.”

    Michael bent down to give Evan a kiss and a hug.

    “Be good, Evan. Don’t give your grandma any trouble, ok?”

    Evan nodded, as Michael pulled from his embrace.

    “Love you dad” Evan said.

    “Love you too, Evan” Michael replied.

    Michael walked into the kitchen and gave his mother a hug.

    “I hope you don’t mind” he said “but I’m taking the box of old photos from your closet.”

    “Of course” his mother replied. “If anyone should have them, it’s you.”

    “Thank you for doing this for me, mom. You don’t know how much of an immense help this is for Freddy and I.”

    “I offered it” his mother replied “so of course I intended to honor it. I couldn’t stand to see those police officers targeting my precious grandchild like that. I’ll keep him safe and won’t take him out of the house like you insisted.”

    “Let me help you out with the bills for the time he’s here.”

    “You don’t have to do that, Michael. I’ve totally got this.”

    “Considering I’m keeping you a prisoner in your own home for more than two weeks and making you homeschool Evan on top of it, it’s the least I can do to repay you.”

    “It’s totally fine, Michael. I like spending time with my grandson. My only complaint is I don’t get to do it often enough.”

    “This is more than him spending time with you, though. This is basically being his babysitter, teacher and parent all wrapped up into one. Besides, you’re not in a good financial place at the moment. I have to make sure that at least Evan’s expenses are covered for while he’s here with you.”

    “I’m totally fine, Michael. With your father’s investments and social security, I’m managing just fine.”

    “You’re not totally fine. By the time…”

    Michael paused, before he sighed and changed the subject.

    “Never mind. I don’t want to talk about that now. Here, this should cover it.”

    Michael typed in some numbers into his communication device and pressed send. His mother’s device pinged a second later and she went over to look at it.

    “No, Michael. I can’t take that much from you.”

    “It’s actually less than I should be giving you, but it at least covers Evan’s expenses for the time being. I’ll send you more if I have to keep him here longer.”

    Michael gave his mother a kiss on the cheek.

    “Love you, mom. I’ll see you in a couple weeks.”

    “Stay safe, Michael. Tell Freddy I said hi and that I hope his parents are ok.”

    Michael nodded, as he went over to Evan to give him one last hug.

    “Be good” he whispered in his son’s ear.

    Evan nodded, as Michael left his mother’s house and made his way back into his car. On the drive home, Michael had a mix of emotions swirling in his head. He wondered if he was making the right call leaving Evan alone with his mother. They would be unprotected. If someone were to barge in on them, they could be hurt or killed. Hopefully, he had dropped Evan off discreetly, so that no one knew where his current location was.

    Driving back to the house, Michael could see that Freddy and Chris had already left for the day. Heading inside, Michael placed the box of old photos into his room before heading back downstairs.
