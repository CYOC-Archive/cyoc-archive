    Interview With A Sphinx

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added 5 years ago A BM Mythological

    "...Thanks for the info, I suppose, Professor." I retorted, shaking his hand in the process, obviously thanking him for providing me with such information. Suffice to say, at least it made me understand things with a bit more clarity regarding Molly's situation.

    "Anytime, lad! Please give my regards to Ms. Rhoer for me." Professor Brooks replied, as he watched as I headed off to find Molly and Julia. After all, they have to still be in the Museum somewhere.

    --- 10 Minutes Later ---

    It took me around ten long minutes of searching around the Museum to find Molly and Julia. At first, I was wandering around a bit aimlessly. But fortunately for me, I coincidentally stumbled upon one of the reporters attending the museum, who was currently looking through the photos in his camera.

    But before I approached him, he suddenly ran off further down the hall. Of course, I followed him, and fortunately for me, chasing the reporter led me straight to Molly, who was currently being interviewed by over a dozen reporters and paparazzi.

    Obviously, Julia was very much beside Molly, answering some of the questions thrown their way, as I heard one reporter ask, "Is it true that you and several Archeologists had failed to open the Sphinx sarcophagus?"

    "...Yes. We were unable to open the Sphinx sarcophagus when we brought it here. Fortunately, Ms. Rhoer here had arrived, and somehow, she was able to effortlessly open it as if it were merely a paperweight." Julia explained, just as numerous paparazzi continued taking photos of my beloved Molly, who stood there in a sultry flirtatious pose.

    Of course, I slowly made my way through the crowd to get to Molly, just as another reporter spoke out and said, "And is it also true that Ms. Rhoer is a legitimate Sphinx?"

    "...According to various resources, yes. Molly is indeed an actual Sphinx. More specifically, a Human-Sphinx hybrid." Julia flatly answered, as the photographers continued their flashing assault upon my sister and girlfriend, as I continued making my way through the crowd at a relatively brisk pace.
