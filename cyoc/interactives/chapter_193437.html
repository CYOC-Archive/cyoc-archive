The muscular man led you into a small, gray, windowless room. It smelled of bleach. There were two chairs. A gray-haired older gentleman was seated in the furthest chair, with his hands neatly folded in his lap.
    "Ah, this is the patient, Lee?" he asked, in a surprisingly warm voice. Lee nodded.
    "Very well, leave him here with me." The old man said, then he turned to face you. "Don't worry, this is just a check-up. We're still not certain if you're suitable for our needs."
    You opened your mouth to reply, but you couldn't find your words. You were still in a daze from the drugs.

    You almost nodded off right then and there, and soon found yourself lying down on an examination bed staring up at the bright lights hanging from the ceiling. You were completely nude and you weren't sure how you got here. It was like you were fading in and out of consciousness.     "Your assessment?" she asked, coldly.
    "I would say this one is just about perfect." The doctor replied. "He should be placed towards the top of the list of surgical candidates."
    "Very good." Said the woman. "The drugs should wear off in an hour or so. We will take him back to his home immediately."

    You were raised to your feet again and led out of the room with Mr. Lee keeping you steady. You blinked and found yourself outside. You were being loaded into back of the van again. Soon, you were nodding off.
    When you next opened your eyes, you found yourself somewhere familiar. Your own apartment. You were lying on the couch, and for a moment you though the whole thing might have been a dream. But then you saw the envelope resting on your stomach, and realized that you were still dressed in a patient's gown.
    You fumbled with the envelope for a moment and then produced a crisp sheet of white paper. Your vision was still blurry, and you had trouble focusing, but slowly you pieced the message together.

    "DO NOT TELL ANYONE ABOUT THE EVENTS OF THIS NIGHT.
    WE KNOW WHERE YOU LIVE. WE KNOW WHERE YOUR FAMILY LIVE.
    DO NOT MAKE ANY PHONE CALLS.
    WE KNOW EVERYTHING WE NEED TO KNOW ABOUT YOU.
    PRETEND THIS NEVER HAPPENED. PERHAPS YOU WILL NEVER SEE US AGAIN."

    A cold sweat broke out on your forehead. Your hands trembled and you clutched the paper tighter.
    What did these people want with you? You faintly remembered something about surgery.
    Could you really just carry on with your life, pretending this never happened?

    You needed to think of a plan, quick.
    You could try calling the police, but somehow you doubted that they would believe this story.
    Then there was your family. The note claimed that these people knew where they lived, but it could have been a bluff.
    You hadn't spoken to your family in a while, not after you dropped out of college. But they were still your family. They'd cover for you if you wanted to lay low.
