    He was about to pounce on the little squirt, but then the small adult part on him urged him to try something else, he wasn't little, Justin was the little boy. But it was so easy to fall into those childish traps, kids really knew how to be mean... "Be mean." Oliver thought.

    "If I'm the baby, how come you are a chicken?" was the incredibly lame retort Ollie come up, but surprisingly it did the trick.

    "I'm no chicken!" Justin replied taking the bait.

    "Yeah, right. I bet you are not brave enough to do what I did." taunted Ollie.

    "Like what?" asked Justin.

    "I came here from my h-" Oliver caught himself before revealing the beans and quickly changed it to "my friend's uncle's house, next door in just my undies."

    "So, that's stupid."

    "Because you are afraid that they see your chicken-butt?"

    "NO!!!"

    "Bawk-bawk-bawk-bawk" Ollie was actually making the chicken sounds and doing the dance in just his underwear on his neighbours house. He would have lost it, but at this point his impulses had overwritten his adult thoughts.

    "I'M NOT. STOP IT!" demanded Justin, but Ollie was relentless until finally Justin got up tossing his action figures aside and started undressing down to his Adventure Time briefs.

    Ollie saw the other kid in the same state of undress as he, and suddenly felt much more relaxed and confident. "Nice yellow undies, have you pee in them?"

    "It's Jake!" said the kid charging at Ollie, so far for a pacific resolution. Both boys started to wrestle one another across the living room trying to pin the other. Each trying his best to squirm and trap the other, before long what started as aggression, had become somewhat fun, specially when each other boldly proclaimed: "Aha!" when he thought he had the other only for him to escape and hold him instead. Soon laughter invaded both kids, wrestling was fun, but they were getting tired, they finally had each other in a lock. "Say uncle!" "No, you say it!" "No, you say it!" "..." "..." "On three?" one suggested. "One." replied the other. "Two." continued the first. "Three!" proclaimed both at the same time tightening their lock until both could no more and were both heaping on the carpeted floor.

    "What's your name?" asked Justin.

    "O- Cody." replied Oliver.

    "I'm Justin... do you like the Avengers?" proposed Justin.

    "Do I!" and in reality Oliver did like them, even as an adult.

    "So..." said Justin going for his action figures. "Iron Man and Cap has found that Ultron has captured the president!" Justin explained his scenario and Ollie step in to play with him.

    When Melissa returned with Justin's mother, they found the pair of children making battle noises, playing with the dolls in their undies.

    "Justin! Why did you took of your clothes off?." His mother demanded an explanation.

    "Cody was just in his undies..." Justin offered as excuse, his mom gave him a look but couldn't help to chuckle. "Boys... what are you going to do." said to Melissa. "Now, now. Cody is getting dressed so you too."

    "Awww... ok." said Justin somewhat disappointed.

    "Come, Cody. Let me help you." said Melissa holding the shirt for him, to the chuckle of Justin, for which Ollie reacted.

    "I can do it, myself!" exclaimed, taking the shorts and putting them on. Melissa hoped it was Oliver's adult mind the one offended, and not Ollie's responding at the other boy obvious taunt. Either way he was finally dressed in a T-shirt, shorts and flip-flops.

    Justin handed the doll to Ollie, but Melissa intervene, "Sorry, Justin but I have to take Cody right now."

    "Awwww... can he stay just a while longer?" pleaded Justin.

    "Yes, please!" pleaded Ollie too.

    But Melissa gave him a look so serious that brought him back to his senses. "Your mom is waiting!"

    "Come play again!" said Justin beaming to Ollie, who also replied with a smile. Melissa thanked Justin's mom for the clothes and went directly for the car. Driving to the ice cream parlor. Melissa hoping that she could fix this mess before Oliver faded any more.
