Rather dense reading for an 8 year old, but he found he could follow the book with no problems, as if his vocabulary had greatly expanded in a short time.

Luckily, Gus was an excellent swimmer and had spent a lot of time in the local river that went through his hometown. And the island’s magic helped boost swimming ability just to be on the safe side. Whoever had created the magic spells on this place had done a fastidious job of ensuring safety.

He just kept wanting to wear the same stuff but his new Dad told him that they had to dress nicely before going out to dinner, the same way Ray used to do. But whereas Ray had always lavished attention and gifts on Gus, Gus had transformed to be the responsible one of them. Ray was usually concerned with making sure his new son was having fun, but Gus had loved having new clothes the last few months. He was now much more concerned with appearance and making sure he was wearing clothes that didn’t make him appear poor. He wanted to wear suits! He thought of it with heartache. The best he could muster was a dress shirt and light khaki pants, whereas his son had plenty of suits to choose from.

    “I’ll show you, son.”

    “Hey, how come I forgot? I used ta know how to do all that stuff. I reckon maybe it’s the magic huh Sir?”

    “You reckon right, boy. But don’t you worry. Daddy has got this covered. Now. Don’t you look nice? Look at yourself in the mirror, boy!”

    “Wow! I look like a grownup!” Ray exclaimed, even though he hadn’t been a child in decades. He was 32 years old, after all, he thought, with some mild confusion. “I’m forgettin how to do adult stuff, huh Dad?”

    “Looks like. But that’s alright with me. Now, let’s get some dinner!”

    During dinner, Ray kept rubbing his stubble as it were a new toy, like he had never felt stubble on his face before. Gus had another beer, this time a dark ale. It was richer, he thought. Smooth and rich. I like beer. It’s a man’s drink, alright. He allowed his 32 year old son to have some lemonade as a special treat. Ray smiled giddily.

    “Thanks, Daddy!”

    “You’re welcome, boy. Cheers, son!” The 8 year old boy held up his beer and clinked glasses with the man he was about to become. “And now, because you were a good boy today and you followed my lead and obeyed me like a good boy should, you may have a small piece of this lemon cake they have on that cart over there.” Gus motioned with his fork in the direction of the cart. Ray looked behind him with almost uncontrolled excitement. His legs tap danced a little bit because he was in hyperactive kid mode all of a sudden.

    “Ooooh,” Ray said, his mouth salivating. “Thankyouthankyouthankyou!”

    “You’re welcome, boy.”

    After they had retired for the evening to their room, there was a knock at the door. It was Veronika.

    “Hello, Mr. Upton, I hope you have been enjoying your stay at La Vida Variado.”

    “What can I say? I feel like a new man!” Gus spread his arms out with a cocky grin.

    “Yeah, me too!” Ray said, excitedly. “I mean, sort of, cept’ in the other direction I guess.”

    “So how does this work exactly? When do we swap bodies?”

    “At noon tomorrow you will be accompanied to a special hot springs not far from here. That’s where it will take place. After that, you’ll be in your new bodies. As for your names, you will switch those as well starting tomorrow. You’ll be Ray,” she told the boy “And you’ll be Gus,” she told the hulking 6’4” manchild. “Is there anything else we can do for you tonight?”

    “No, sweetheart, you’ve done a great job so far. Here, you take that for your troubles,” Gus winked as he slipped her a five dollar tip.

    “Thank you, Mr. Upton. I’ll see you boys tomorrow!”

    “I’m gonna be a boooy. I’m gonna be a boooooy!” Ray sing-songed as he jumped up and down.

    “Yes and I’ll finally be a man. You did a great thing by bringing us here, Ray my boy!”

Ray felt too bulking and his legs felt too long. Gus felt like he should be taking up way more space.
