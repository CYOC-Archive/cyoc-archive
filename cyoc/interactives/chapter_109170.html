    "But enough about Seattle and all that," Vivian commented from her seat, her face looking firm, "the dirty rat is gone and getting his just deserts for ALL that he's done over the years."

    Amy blinked, "You two must have really liked Tad's dad..."

    "Well, for a male, I suppose he was rather dashing," Vivian gave a smile, being a lesbian werecat, she really didn't have any interest in males. "But we liked that he made our daughter happy and that she was so bonded to him."

    "The good things he did for Jen is what we liked," Karen commented and then leaned over to ruffle Tad's hair, "starting with this charming young man."

    "Grandma..." Tad ducked down, making Amy giggle a little.

    "In the meantime," Vivian commented to Amy, now looking much more cordial and friendly, "why don't you don't you tell us a little more about yourself, dear."

    Amy slowly nodded and looked between the two women. Both looked to be rather young, but then their werecat scent made that understandable and thus how they looked like they were about the same age as Tad's mom despite the fact that the the two were Tad's grandmothers. She straightened herself up and prepared to answer.

    "Well... I'm a werewolf, like Tad and I would say that I'm quite pleased to have met him and bring him into the pack that I'm in," Amy commented.

    "I'm sure," Karen commented, "we've heard enough stories on the phone and in letters to know that our grandson will be quite the catch for anyone when he grows up."

    Tad gave a weak smile and tried to hide a bit of a blush that was forming in his cheeks. If Amy noticed it, she didn't say anything on it and continued on.

    "I've actually enjoyed many of the same sorts of games and computer stuff that Tad's been into," Amy commented, "I'm not sure it's what I want to do with my life... but I've enjoyed playing a couple of online games."

    "Like solitaire?" Vivian asked.

    "That isn't an online game," Tad corrected.

    "Think more like Knights of the Old Republic," Amy added.

    Vivian looked to her wife, who shrugged, and then shrugged as well. Weres might not age as quickly as normal humans, but that didn't always mean that every were stayed up to date with technology and how it advanced in every way.

    "Well... perhaps you'll both find something you can both play as a team together," Vivian finally said after a few moments.

    "Perhaps," Amy and Tad agreed.

    The was a moment of silence before Karen spoke up.

    "So, you are a werewolf...how do you look in your wolven form?" Karen asked, "If you can tolerate a bit of curiosity."

    "I don't mind the curiosity," Amy said slowly, "my wolven form is all white. The only part of me that isn't white is my hair... which isn't too far from white."

    "Interesting... like Yin and Yang," Vivian commented, "the perfect pairing with Tad's black coat."

    "I had noticed," Amy commented with a chuckle.

    "So... if you stay on the straight and narrow, you'll be a great pair," Karen said to them, with Tad and Amy both smiling.
