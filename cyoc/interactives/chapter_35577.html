    Beer is Good!

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added by Wolfy 15 years ago O


    You want to make sure you grab the best kind, since it was free, looking the labels over for a short period of time and find that only one was really calling out to you. There was a bottle of Full-Moonshine with just an image of a huge full moon either rising or setting over a large forest. For whatever reason, that just felt like the one to try.

    Seeing that you made your choice, Peter smiled and then claimed the rest of the beers. "Good choice, that was the kind I tried when I first came to the brewery!" He exclaimed while walking up the side of the stairs with the bust of the wolf on the head of the banister.

    When you get up to the double doors, you see a large cut out piece of the door which contained a sample tray and a beer bottle opener at the top of the space. You look over to Peter and he just nods while pointing at the space, obviously directing you to go ahead and open the bottle there. Finding it to be slightly weird, you walk up to the thing, open the bottle and some of the contents flow out from the neck. Suddenly, you hear and see the doors slowly open and Peter motions for you to take a drink while heading on inside. You just shrug your shoulders, put the bottle to your lips and take a quick swig of the beer. As you felt it flow into your belly, you realized that you liked the taste and would fully enjoy your tour since you were about to get as much of that beer as you wanted. Finishing the bottle where you stood, without even stepping foot into the brewery, Peter just smiles at you. Smacking your lips to get the fullness of the taste, you realize that it had a bit of a strange after-taste being kind of sweet and thick while being slightly bitter.

    "Ready for your next bottle and to begin the tour?"

    "Yeah, you bet!"

    Peter just continues to smile, while you walk past the open doors, discarding your empty bottle and pick up another from the mechanical arm that was apparently there to serve you. Picking up the new bottle, you notice that it was already opened for you and you did not have to do anything more than simply to enjoy yourself.

    Ready for the tour, Peter hands the rest of the two cases to the mechanical arm and begins the tour by giving you some background about the brewery, which you barely paid attention to because you were already feeling a bit buzzed from downing that single beer. You just stand there, staring at Peter and the mural of a large full moon behind him, not even noticing the changes the both of you had gone through.

    What do you do now?
