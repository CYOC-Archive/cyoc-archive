    Why are you telling an alien lizardess to chill, when you're just a dog?

    There's a pause. Both of your tails idly sway side to side, although yours makes faint whirring noises. Your 'host' breaks the silence. "Did you just tell me to chill?"

    You thought about it for a moment. Actually, wait. You didn't quite think about it. You knew it. Or rather, your mind was working faster than you thought you could ever think of before. This body of a machine was better at thinking than you were, which sent you down a spiral of self introspection about this whole ordeal. What does it mean to be alive? Is being a machine really better? And how about-

    The lizardess snaps her fingers, catching your attention. You raise your head to have your robotic eyes meet hers. "Why...am I a robot, may I ask?" You asked as The lizardess points at the smoldering wreck of a machine. "That," she says matter-of-factly, "...is a teleporter. But as you can see, it's not exactly working."

    "So, you put me into the body of a dog?" you say, trying to grasp your artificial body while sitting down like any dog would, to make a point. Only, you let out a light whimper that an unexpected feelings wash over you. This robot body was alot more different than what you were used to. If you could blush, you'd be blushing extremely hard right now. Hell, maybe you can. You don't exactly have a mirror, unknowing about your new ear servos folding back on your canine head. "I-I'M a dog! And I don't have any idea of being one! How isthis okay?"

    "Look, kid. I didn't want to put you in my extremely expensive (warranty-less) back-up body, damnit. Do you feel that 'exis-cell' in your back?" She began to say as you felt something in the back of your neck, with your hind leg while feeling an itch.

    "That there stores you. The entirety of you. Your body, your mind, the works. Until I get that teleporter fixed, I had to put you somewhere. Would you rather be a toaster? Or even one of my computers? What if I just left you like that for awhile? Because I'm sure having your mind being stretched out into data fragments is so much fun. Get what I'm saying?" She said, now angry at you.

    Suddenly, she seems alot more domineering, and she appears to be towering over you as you slink down in defeat. "Oh, and there better not be a scratch on that body before that teleporter's fixed. I'm fixing it as of now, so don't get too comfortable in it. Otherwise, I may decide to actually keep you in there for myself, as a pet." She said for What is there to say that's not yet been said? The lizardess struts out of the teleporter room before you can follow her.

    Again, silence. Maybe that's for the best. You need a moment to catch your breath, and maybe...test out your new "functions". Mayby being her pet might sound strange as it be fun to even call her master, even for a little while. Regaining your (metal) composure, you think (compute) on what the next (most logical) course of action is...
