She remembered that the high school kids back then were more hip and wild, sometimes dressed in tacky attire and speaking in 90s lingo. She felt a bit of nostalgia remembering such things.

Kids now where normal clothing, locker rooms were decorated with cut outs and marker graffiti. She could see some kids looking at their phones, watching Tik Tok videos and hash tagging and rarely interacting with their friends. Certainly a far cry away from what high school kids did at the first time at sixteen.

Now she only was a beginner. A small price to pay for sacrificing motherhood to relieve high school life again.

What she could see a young Filipino teenage boy with bronze skin and a toned build underneath his basket ball jersey and jacket and cargo pants. Seeing the boy, her body stopped moving and her heart was beating at a rapid pace. It was Antonio, Jeff's best friend and now her crush. Once, she was a mother, she would've had the confidence necessary in talking to someone. Surprising that you three managed to get the spot at the homecoming halftime. Que asquerosa... How can you three get the spot and not us. I mean, look at me. You need..." she said before getting interrupted.

Course, as the argument was going on, Jamie remembered a new memory where she and her friends were able to beat Lola and her team for the spot at halftime. So hearing Lola wanting a rematch, sickens her.

    The three soon ran down the halls of the school to get to their class. Jamie's first re-experience of high school was something. It was nothing compared to her first time as a teenager of the mid to late nineties, but she knew, this was going to be quite a week.
