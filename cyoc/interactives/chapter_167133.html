Miles wanted to be a jackrabbit, his father, David, wanted to be a macaw, and his mother, Louise, wanted to be a dolphin.     "But would any of them suit me?" asked Louise.

    "Here's one you might like," said Miles, pulling up a picture of a dolphin with a long snout, a bump on its head, and bright pink skin.

    "Is that photoshopped?" asked Louise.

    "No, it's an Amazon river dolphin, also known as the pink river dolphin or the boto," said Miles, "Largest and most intelligent species of freshwater dolphin, has a special organ in its head for echolocation, and it's, er, venerated in Amazonian folklore."

    "Well, it does look cute," said Louise, "Probably easier than trying to find the fossil of some prehistoric dolphin ancestor."

    "And since I'm becoming a macaw, I may as well be one from the Amazon rainforest," said David.

    "I don't think they have jackrabbits there, but I'm still going to be going rabbit," said Miles.

    "Hare," said David.

    "Yeah, right here," said Miles.

    "No, the jackrabbit isn't a rabbit, it's a hare," said David.
