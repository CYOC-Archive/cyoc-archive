    “Was the damage really that bad?” Nate asked. Back when the Andersons first moved to Moon Lake, there was a bit of a fight between two students. The reason behind the feud was still a bit fuzzy, but the general consensus was that the two hot-headed male werebears got into it over something trivial.

    “As the Dean put it, the only thing more dangerous than a grizzly bear, is an angry werebear. Because, unlike animals, weres known exactly what they’re doing.” Williams said.

It was like someone set every nerve on fire, but not a painful fire. More like an aching fire, that needed to be put out with sex.     “Not sure how good you are at solving functions and quadratic equations, but I think I’ll be okay with the rest. Mostly just worksheets, and I have to read a few more chapters of Beowulf.” Nate said. And that was one book he was sad to learn wasn’t actually about a wolf. Eric chewed him out the night he came home and commented on how he thought the book was about some European werewolf.

    “Sorry, that’s not my field.” William chuckled. Though both he and Martha were registered teachers, their combined knowledge of mathematics began and ended with 2+2=4. Which is why he taught English, not Calculus. And Martha taught third grade, where the only math you needed was addition, subtraction, and some multiplication and division.

    “What time will Mom be home?” Nate asked.

    “I’m not sure, she’s got a meeting after school. Why do you ask?”

    “Um, there’s something I want to ask you two.” Nate said, shifting nervously. He’d spent the car ride home deciding what would be the best way to ask his parents if he could spend a weekend with a complete stranger (at least to them). Nate was sure his parents would want to meet his “friend with benefits”, and they had every right to.

    “Really? In regards to what exactly?” William asked. He had a feeling it had to do with Nate’s night excursions. Again, he wasn’t upset at his son for not telling him and Martha, but seeing as this had been going on for at least a week it would be nice if Nate told them who this girl was.

    “Uh, Friday night.”

    “We’re having dinner with the Twists and the new neighbors.”

    “Ya, I mean afterwards.” Nate said, not looking up from the floor. He felt an uncomfortable heat rise to his cheeks, and his father’s eyes examining him.

    “I’m going to blow this,” he thought.

    “Alright, we’ll talk more about this when your mother comes home.” William said. Nate felt a weight leave his chest, and he quickly excused himself. As soon as he was in his bedroom, he breathed a sigh of relief.

    SURE. WHEN? Eric asked, deciding not to think too much of the request. Julie’s response was sent the second he finished typing and hit send.

    HOW BOUT FRIDAY? OR WILL U & UR MOM B BUSY?
