    To The Mountain

    You and Ben look at each other, standing inside your house, right at the door and see an unknown in both of your eyes. You hear the wind rustle outside, blowing furiously, whistling and howling as it blows through the seams of the doors, trying to invade the house. You two know that you want to go in there, but you were both afraid. Ben smiles, places his hand on the door and turns. Suddenly, the wind picks up speed and flings the door wide open, knocking you and Ben back a little. Then suddenly, just as quickly as it rose, the wind died down and everything was calm again.

    "Man... that was crazy, don't you think?"

    You just nod silently, standing there in your Family Guy t-shirt with Brian the dog making some sarcastic remark along with your blue jean shorts that end just below the knee. You knew that even though the wind was going crazy, it was still warm outside from the above average summer temperatures everyone experienced during the day.

    "Well... I suppose we should get started. It should only take us ten minutes to get to the base of the mountain from here, then it is who knows how long till we get to the cave entrance since from what I have heard, you have to do a bit of hiking along a trail that winds right up the side of the mountain to the mouth of the cave."

    This time, when you nod, you do it enthusiastically since you really wanted to see what was in that cave when no one had been known to go and come back. Granted, it may be dangerous, but you were a very adventurous sixteen year old guy and you didn't care what was there, so long as you can come back home and tell everyone about how you bested the mountain and the scary cave.

    You and Ben hopped on your bicycles and pedaled down the sidewalk, talking the necessary deviation and quickly found yourself at the infamous beginning of the foot- path.

    Jumping off your bikes, Ben looks at you with a very serious look on his face. "So, are you ready to do this? Are you ready to show the whole town... no the world that neither of us are scaredy-cats and we will go to any length to find out what others tend to be too afraid to find?"

    You nod in approval, silently as usual, and Ben just gave you a pat on the back with a smile. Then before you could blink, Ben was staring up the trail and you look up to see that the sun had finally finished setting and the night was quickly rolling in. As the two of you walked, you both tried not looking over the side of the cliff because you both could tell that you were pretty high up off the main ground. You knew that if you slipped and fell, it would be a long way down and more than likely result in death. Being wary the whole time, you follow close behind Ben who had always been a natural leader. Keeping your mind on the objective, not thinking of anything else, you both reached the mouth of the cave before you even realized it.

    Peering into the cave, albeit slightly, you could head heavy breathing and a rough snort through what was probably large nostrils. You look at Ben, questioning what you were going to do now that you were both there and Ben looks back with a very blind stare.

    In a somewhat of a whisper, Ben says "Well... let's go back! We are here, we know something does live inside and we can come back again after we report that there is something that lives in the cave."

    You give Ben a very stern look, not very happy that he even wanted to do that. You keep looking at him as he turns to walk away, but is stopped before he could move an inch.

    "Hey... you two! I know you are out there, why don't you come in and say hello? "

    With the sound of the voice, their fears were subdued slightly, since the voice was very deep and rough. You and Ben turn to face the entrance of the cave once more, then begin walking in. As you entered the cave, you were both a little shocked by what was inside. In the center of a very large cave that didn't go any where and provided a decent shelter for the occupant, was a large fire that was built like they would back in boy scouts. Looking beyond the fire, they saw who... no, more like what spoke to them. On the ground, sitting cross legged was a very large wolf and human mixture. At that moment, only one word came to mind of you both and that was werewolf!

    Immediately sensing that you both wanted to run for the hills, the creature stood up on its massive hind digigrade legs. "What, you two scared of this little old wolf?" He finished with a chuckle.

    "You my... er, um... friend... are not little."

    "You are very observant young one, indeed I am not little, in fact nothing about me is little." He finished with a sly smile as he looked himself over, especially when he looked at his own package.

    "Wha... what do you want from us?" You finally asked, breaking your code of silence for what Ben saw was the first time.

    "Well... you both are my visitors for the first time in ages and I wanted to greet you."

    "What do you mean, 'first time in ages'? There have been stories among us that the people that have gone missing went up to this cave and they never returned, nor were they seen or heard from again."

    "Oh... you silly humans! I have been watching your kind for a while now and I know when something is being said to be a cover-up. I have also been watching your town very specifically and I will just say that there are more problems than either of you would even know. Care for me to enlighten you further?"

    You both stare at each other, contemplating your decision. Then Ben, being the more courageous between the two of you finally spoke up and said ____________
