    "I'm afraid he has a very strict policy against allowing apes on the premises."

    Agent Johnson kept his badge out. "And I might have to respect that if I'm ever here again, For now though, I think you'll have to agree an exception needs to be made."

    The panther quickly considered his options. Most likely he was here about the missing girls. No real need to worry there, but turning him away would be far too suspicious, and there were greater concerns at the moment. "You have quite a point, but I'm afraid the Minotaur is away at the moment."

    "Is he now? When do you expect him back?"

    "I wouldn't know. He left just this morning, in quite the hurry, without any explanation to anyone on staff."

    "I see," Agent Johnson replied, no doubt thinking how difficult a time the Minotaur would have not being spotted if he fled the inevitable charges. "Well, as it so happens, your employer is wanted for questioning with regards to a number of very serious allegations. Would you mind if I came in and had a look around while we wait?"

    The panther stopped him when he started to pull forth the relevant paperwork. "I had no idea! Right this way."

    It seemed a change of plans was called for. Originally, the plan was to coerce the Minotaur into selling his shares of the company to someone of a properly predatory persuasion, and then arrange for him to disappear. Of course, if he could be made to take the fall for the abduction charges, maybe some of the other shady dealings going on, that would serve the same purpose. Of course, for it to be believable, he'd have to disappear a bit earlier than expected. After escorting the agent to the Minotaur's study, he excused himself, getting on the radio to explain this latest development and look into sneaking the Minotaur out to join his earlier guests. There was some risk involved, but this place wasn't called the labyrinth for nothing.

    Mark's announcement didn't sit well with everyone else on staff, and a bit of an argument ensued over whether he had the right to let some of the subjects free without consulting anyone. Still, he had a point. If there was a federal investigation about human rights violations at the park, it wasn't a question of if someone would be looking into their work, it was when. Most decided they'd rather risk an arrest and just play dumb as well as they could, particularly from the security staff, but a few felt a bit adventurous. One researcher insisted the safest option was to shut themselves in with the other subjects. Nobody would suspect it, and it wasn't exactly hard to fake being a homeless bum with no I.D. "Besides," she said, "with how well that mermaid form worked out, I've been wanting to try on a snake tail." That logic won over a fair number of the others.

    The only person willing to follow Mark's plan to flee the country by air was Tran Nguyen. "I think this plan of yours is completely nuts, but I've spent too long working on that template to let you sneak off and take all the credit. If we're going to do this though, I want a full test, not that winged mammal compromise I saw you working on before. I want to observe the psychological effects of becoming a true egg-laying species."
