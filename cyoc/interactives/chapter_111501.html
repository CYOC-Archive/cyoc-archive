Posters hung on the walls of advertising pep rallies, after-school clubs, and other news. While she was moving, she couldn't help but notice how unusually attractive everyone was. The boys all seemed to look like they worked out, and she couldn't help but stare at them. "So, it says here that you just moved here, and your father, Harold, enrolled you in last week. You just moved here yesterday, correct?"

    "Yes, ma'am," Melissa replied.

    "I wonder if you're related to that werefox couple that's getting married. Well, no matter. Welcome to Moon Lake High." The woman took a moment to read through her file. First, we don't allow any romantic gestures here, including hugging, kissing, making out, or anything like that. Weres have high libidos, and mix that in with teenage hormones, and it's a miracle that teens aren't having sex in the halls. Second, transformation is generally prohibited during school hours. While we do make exceptions, such as Halloween or in cases of emergency, in general, no one is allowed to change into their fursonas while here at the school. Last, it is against the law to turn a normal into a were against their will. We take that very seriously here at Moon Lake High, as well as your safety. If anyone tries to scratch you, bite you, or otherwise tries to change you here, let a teacher know immediately. So, do you have any questions for me, Ms. Jenkins?"

    "Um..." Melissa said as she paused to try to take in what she heard. "No. Well, except where's my class room?"

    The woman smiled and said, "Well, you're in room 153. Just turn left after leaving this office, and it'll be off to your right. Now, I know that we're almost two months into the term, but I think you'll be fine here. If you have any questions, or need help catching up, just let a teacher know, ok?"

    "I will," Melissa said in response.

    The woman handed Melissa a slip of paper and said, "Here's your schedule for the rest of the day. Have a nice day."

She opened the door, and saw a room of teens talking to each other in small groups. When she walked in, almost all of them looked up right at her. Not wanting to be the center of attention, she quickly made her way to the nearest empty desk, putting her backpack down. She couldn't help but notice that many of the people were still looking at her. Fortunately, it wasn't long before the teacher walked into the room and started class, much to Mel's relief.
