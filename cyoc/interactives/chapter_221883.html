    Mary found herself in a narrow metallic chamber. She could barely breathe, and her heart was racing. She tried to scream for help, but no sound came out of her mouth. Suddenly, she saw a spider crawling towards her. It was unlike any spider she had ever seen. It was larger than a tarantula, with long and hairy legs. Its eyes glowed with an otherworldly light, and its fangs dripped with venom. Mary felt trapped and helpless as the spider crawled closer and closer to her face. Just as it was about to bite her, there was a bright flash...

    ***

    The confused girl woke up in a cold sweat, gasping for air. It took her a moment to realize that she was still on the mountain. The nightmare had felt so real, and she couldn't shake the feeling that there was something important about that spider... and the chamber... The darkness of the night enveloped the jungle. The moon and stars shone brighter than she had ever seen, as there were no other sources of light. Perhaps her improved vision helped, too, because she could see everything as well as in broad daylight. Even though it was still the middle of the night, she felt refreshed and rested and ready to continue. All the tiredness caused by the big hike and climbing was like wiped away. It was as if her body was recovering from exhaustion faster than before. She had barley slept 4 hours... Or maybe she was just afraid to risk another nightmare.

    The cold of the night didn't bother her, either. Her tough skin covered her like a warm layer of fabric. Mary's heart still raced as she tried to calm herself from the haunting nightmare. She couldn't shake off the sense of foreboding it had left behind. Determined to put some distance between herself and the unsettling dream, Mary quickly packed her belongings and prepared to descend down the mountain.

    With only the pale moonlight guiding her, Mary made her way through the dense foliage, carefully navigating the rugged terrain. The jungle was eerily quiet at night, with only the occasional rustle of leaves or the distant calls of nocturnal creatures breaking the silence. As she descended deeper into the darkness, the shadows seemed to dance and play tricks on her mind, heightening her sense of unease.

    After what felt like an eternity, Mary reached a point where the path abruptly ended. Before her stretched a vast, open space, and the faint sound of rushing water reached her ears. As she approached cautiously, she realized that she had come upon a high cliff, overlooking a deep ravine. The sheer drop was awe-inspiring, as the moonlight illuminated the cascading waterfall below.

    Mary's heart skipped a beat as she stood at the precipice, contemplating her next move. As she pondered her options, a small rock dislodged from beneath her feet and tumbled down the cliffside. With a mix of curiosity and concern, she followed its fall, tracking its trajectory with her enhanced sight as long as she could. However, even with her heightened vision, the rock eventually disappeared in darkness hundred of feet below.

    A sense of unease washed over her as she realized the magnitude of the jungle's vastness. The realization that she was standing at the edge of an immense and unfamiliar wilderness struck her with a profound realization: finding a way out would be no easy task. The jungle stretched far and wide, concealing its secrets and potential escape routes.

    Mary's determination wavered for a moment as she contemplated the daunting nature of her situation. Thoughts of being lost in this untamed expanse crept into her mind, threatening to erode her resolve. But deep within her, a flicker of resilience sparked, reminding her of the strength and abilities she had discovered within herself.

    "I won't let this uncertainty defeat me," Mary whispered to herself, her voice firm and resolute. "There must be a way out, and I will find it."

    With renewed determination, she shifted her gaze from the fallen rock to the deep cliff and vast jungle that lay before her. The challenge ahead loomed large, but Mary knew she had to keep moving forward. She needed to explore, to search for clues, and to uncover any sign of civilization or a way back home.

    Taking a deep breath, Mary made her decision.
