The ruins that I’ve brought you to have been hidden away from the world for thousands of years thanks to Pan’s magic.     Now Alex was rather intrigued. Ruins that few humans have ever seen? What exactly was Orion about to show him? He wouldn’t need to wait much longer to get an answer, as the white-haired man stepped out of the way, revealing what he had been blocking from Alex’s vision.

    Before him was an old stone road that was lined with ancient buildings. The buildings showed the telltale signs of old age, yet they were remarkably well preserved despite being almost reclaimed by nature. Further down the road, Alex could make out what appeared to be the remnants of a fountain where the people who once lived here would go to get their drinking water. A few villas stood tall further into the ruins, but not as tall as the trees that protected the city from the sky above. Alex was in awe at the sight before him. Never in his life did he ever think he would be able to see such well preserved ancient ruins with his own eyes!

    “Orion…what is this place?” the young man asked, shifting his attention to the man in front of him. Orion grinned and gave a slight chuckle.

    “This, Alex, is the city of Lycaea,” the man explained. “It was built thousands of years ago by King Lycaon. It was home to hundreds of people, and it was a rather popular trading town thanks to its close proximity to the Alfeios and Lousios rivers. It is a place of great significance to werewolves, as this is the place where the modern werewolf was born.”

    Alex looked at the buildings in awe as he began to walk down the ancient street. He looked peered in a few windows, looking into the darkened interiors of the ancient structures. “So those who were bitten or scratched by Lycaon lived here…and they had children who could control their transformations, right?”

    “Exactly.”

    Orion chuckled. “Well I’ll be damned…you actually paid attention. Normally, people don’t bother to remember anything I tell them about werewolf culture.”

    “Well I think it’s fascinating,” Alex responded as he walked inside what he believed to be an old shop. “I never knew that the history of werewolves was so complex, let alone that werewolves even existed before this trip. Out of curiosity, why do you keep this place hidden from the rest of the world?”

    “Call it a precaution. We’ve seen how idiotic some humans can be. They destroy priceless relics and ancient ruins when attempting to get a good picture for their social media page. So, in order to prevent that from happening to one of our most sacred ancestral sites, Pan decided that it would be best to keep it completely hidden from the world. Even then, we still have pack members patrol these grounds since the resort owns the land.”

    “It’s incredible how well preserved this city is,” Alex thought to himself. “I can only imagine how much some people would pay to see this place for themselves. It’s a shame that they-”

    The sound of something falling over in a nearby building made Alex snap out of his thoughts. The young man looked towards the source of the sound, peering into the darkness of a nearby building…only for his gaze to meet a pair of glowing yellow eyes…

    Before he could react, a large shape jumped out from the darkness and lunged at him, tackling the young man to the ground and knocking the wind out of him. Panicking, Alex looked up at his assailant. Standing on top of him was a large, feral wolf with grey fur. It bared its teeth at the young man beneath it, snarling aggressively.

    “State your business here, human,” the wolf spoke, much to Alex’s surprise.
