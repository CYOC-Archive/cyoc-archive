    Daniel rubbed his eyes and he took in what happened in his room - books and baubles were strewn across the floor, many broken. He saw scratches and claw marks on the walls and furniture. The room had a familiar, yet musky odor. It could be best described as a wild animal having lived in the room however Daniel was unaware that the smell came from him just hours ago. Daniel looked out the window to see it was only sunset of the same day he transformed.

    Suddenly remembering how his mother and sister swapped sexes when they transformed, Daniel hastily looked down to check down there. Daniel sighed with relief when he saw his usual plumbing down there. As he relaxed, he tensed briefly, feeling a strange "spark" go through him. He remembered exactly what brought about the curse - emotions! Daniel steadied himself and was able to clear his mind with a few seconds of deep breathing. The "sparks" faded away as he calmed himself. Although he wasn't sure how long the effects of the cookies would last, he remembered his mom changing twice and didn't want to risk getting emotional and transforming again - he needed to get to the bottom of these cookies and to cure himself (and his family) from this curse.

    Daniel changed into a new pair of clothes and disposed of a few ruined clothes and belongings of his, his nose wrinkling at the heady odor that was on some of the clothes. Daniel quietly shut the door to his room and made his way slowly downstairs. Below, he heard the TV running and what sounded (and smelled) like someone cooking dinner. Entering the kitchen, Daniel saw his mother cooking what appeared to be some sort of meat. His mom turned around to grab something and noticed Daniel.

    "Good to see you again Daniel, how are you feeling?" She asked, with a hint of teasing knowing what likely happened to Daniel.
    "Fine. What's for dinner." Daniel retorted. "Is sis in her room?" Daniel then asked. He heard something from her room when was headed downstairs but he didn't investigate it.

    "Let's just say she won't be joining us for dinner. This meal is for us and she will have what's leftover." Daniel's mom said as Daniel peered behind her.
