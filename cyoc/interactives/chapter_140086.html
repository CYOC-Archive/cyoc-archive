    "Right," agreed his wife Martha. "If they changed their plans for some reason and went some place other than our house or hers, they should of called."
    "But what if they tried and couldn't reach anyone because of the outage?" wondered their oldest son Nate. "Depending upon what they're doing, both of them know they're safe within Moon Lake. For all we know, it might have been something they knew everyone would approve of if they could have gotten hold of you."
    "Nate, until you're a parent yourself, I don't think you'll fully appreciate a mother's worry," replied Martha.
    "Err..." began Alex Ramsey, Nate's boyfriend and future husband, uncertain what to say.
    "I didn't mean any offense Alex," Martha quickly added. "I just meant that until Nate has a child of his own to worry about, I don't think he truly realizes how much goes into being a parent from our perspective."
    Alex decided to let the moment pass without a response.

    "Is everything all right?" Francis Twist asked, coming out to see what had kept her wife Samantha from checking to see if today's mail ever came.
    Samantha quickly explained the situation to her.
    "Eric and Julie are two of the smartest people I know," observed Francis. "Whatever's going on, I'm sure they're all right."
    "I sure hope so," replied Martha. Then a thought occurred to her. She turned to face Melissa Stovall and started to ask "You don't suppose..."
    Melissa couldn't think of what Martha was wondering at first, but when it dawned on her, was quick to answer. "As much as our children love each other and plan to spend the rest of their lives together, there's no way Eric and Julie would elope prematurely. They wouldn't want to risk any of their prophecy dreams not coming true."
