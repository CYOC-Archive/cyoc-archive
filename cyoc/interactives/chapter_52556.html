    When Felix left the mall, he felt a bit disoriented when he walked into the parking lot. It looked as if though all the vehicles became smaller, and he couldn't find his truck right away. When he did find it, he thought it was a bit smaller than usual too.

    "Whoa, I'm really out of it." Felix said to himself, scratching the back of his head in confusion.


    Felix hopped in his truck and quickly drove to the beach. It wasn't too far from the mall, so it wasn't much of a trip, but he still wanted to get there to get a decent spot. Though when he got there, he found parking easily and the beach wasn't as full as it should be for a Sunday. You know, except it was Monday - not Sunday.

    "Oh... That's right... It's Monday... All that rushing for nothin'." Felix muttered to himself as he took his beach bag out from the trunk of his car. "Man, I guess I need a bit more sleep."

    Felix walked down by the water, noticing that all eyes of him, as usual. Well, it was just another normal day at the beach for him. He even saw some attractive guy checking him out, one Felix had seen many times, but never actually spoken to. Before he could even lay his beach blanket down, the man came up to him and tried to start a conversation.

    "Hey there! I never seen you here before. You on vacation or something?" He said, eyeing Felix up and down.

    "Er... No, I live around here. I always come here." Felix nonchalantly replied.

    "Really? How could I not remember a guy like you? You're huge!" The man said, putting on a charming grin.

    "Ehehe, thanks... Um... I'm Felix." Felix said as he offered his hand.

    "Craig." The man said, shaking Felix's hand. "Um, I'm just chilling here alone, trying to work on my tan. Wanna sit over by me?"

    "Uh s-sure." Felix replied, trying to keep his eyes focused on Craig's face and not the rest of him... For his own sake.

    Craig wasn't the world's sexiest guy, but he was defiantly the most straight-forward. Craig had short blonde hair and green eyes; an automatic winning combination in Felix's book. His face was far from hideous, but it wasn't ridiculously handsome either. If it weren't for the stubble on Craig's chin, it would've just looked plain... Maybe because he was so pale. His body, however, was quite a sight. He must've stood at 6'2" and had perfect definition everywhere, probably weighing about 200 pounds - no fat to be seen. He had some blond body hair, but it wasn't much. His skin was also pretty pale. Craig used green swim briefs to cover himself up, but it left little to the imagination - the front pressing up against his amazing manhood. When he turned around to walk towards his blanket, Felix got an eyeful of a scar going diagonally across most of his back.

    "When I come here I usually chill under an umbrella... But I really need a tan. I'm a freakin' ghost." Craig jested. "Looks like you don't need to tan at all."

    "I'm just here to relax. I use high SPF suntan lotion so I don't get much darker than this." Felix said as he put down his towel.

    "I just use this stuff here." Craig said holding up some oil. "I tan real well, but I've been inside for a looooooong time. Anyway, mind puttin' some on my back? Then I can help you with your back."

    "Uh... Sure." Felix meekly agreed, taking the bottle of oil from Craig.

    "I bet you're wonderin' about this scar, right?" Craig asked as he sat in front of Felix.

    "Was it that obvious? I'm sorry..." Felix apologized as he rubbed some of the oil on Craig's back.

    "It's no big deal. I just got in an accident and a piece of metal scraped along my back. I stayed away from the beach because of it... But I don't care anymore. I love this place, and its not going away..." Craig explained, shifting a bit in discomfort. "Does it freak you out?"

    "Nah. I was just wondering is all." Felix softly replied as he began rubbing in the oil. He didn't notice it, but his hands were gliding over Craig's shoulders and arms as well. Craig let out a small sigh, his shoulder relaxing.

    "Very nice... Felix... Thanks. Now it's my turn to return the favor." Craig cooed. He did the same thing to Felix, rubbing his muscular arms and his shoulders. "Hey uh... I usually head off the beach around one o'clock to make lunch. My apartment is just one block away so... Do you wanna join me, Felix?"

    "Sure." Felix simply replied. He didn't know why, but Craig was just such an amazing guy from the start. Sure he looked pretty good, but he also didn't fawn over Felix's size like everyone else did. At least that's what Felix thought, anyway. His memory was way beyond hazy due to the change. As far as Felix knows, he's always been huge and the change never happened... Craig also sounded kind of desperate... Lonely, even.

    "Alrighty... Done. I suppose we have about two hours until lunch then. Uh, mind waking me up if I fall asleep? I don't wanna get burnt up." Craig said with a slightly forced smile.

    "Yeah, I can do that. After all, you'd be a weird ghost if you were glowing red."
