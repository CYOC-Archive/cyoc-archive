    Worth of a Wish

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added by MysteriousMist 13 years ago O

    The bell suddenly rang out clearly through the room, and the two of us let go of each other in surprise. He gave a little chuckle as he turned to me, and I did the same as I looked back up to him. "I guess we lost track of time, didn't we?"

    "Er... Yeah. I guess we've been a bit distracted, haven't we?

    "A bit." I chuckled as I picked up his shirt before tossing it at him, it landing over his head. "You'd better get that on before you end up 'distracting' everybody in school." It took him a moment to get it, but he gave a quiet laugh as well, his voice deep and rumbling as he did.

    "I don't know if that's enough to help." I smiled as I watched him pull the shirt on, it tight around his arms as he struggled to pull them through the sleeves. His clothes had grown somewhat with him, but his body seemed to cling to the fabric as he pulled it along, and with the way lines in his clothing seemed to appear as he pulled it down his chest and over his abs, he may has well have been shirtless, for all the good that it did hiding his body. Everybody already gave him a few glances whenever he was around, but now, there was no way he'd avoid getting stares from anybody that could see him.

    I shook my head a little, trying not to stare any more than I already was. "Don't worry. We'll have plenty of time later, too. I need to take care of something first, but I'll see you in class soon enough." We both looked at each other for a moment, and I hesitated before leaning in and giving him a quick kiss to his cheek. I could see him blush for just a moment before he suddenly pushed his head forwards, returning the favor before he started to head out.

    "I'll see you soon, then! Don't take too long!"

    I had decided to give enough time for him to head out, and would wait until I was sure nobody was around. It only took a minute before I had heard his footsteps fade. I shook my head a little bit, knowing they were a lot heavier than when he had come in. At least it would be easy enough to keep track of him now, but that didn't mean that there weren't any new problems.

    I pulled the eight ball from my pocket, looking at the window and speaking clearly to it."Nino! We need to talk!"

    "About what?"

    I turned around, seeing her standing behind me, a smile on her face as she looked at me.

    "About Rasler! I didn't wish for that! I said I would wish for him to be happy, not that I actually wish for it!"

    She tilted her head a little as she looked at me. "I see. I may have misunderstood your words. However, as a rule, I try to grant wishes whenever I can see the intent for it, even without the specific words. And, as far as I could tell, you certainly intended for your friend to be happy."

    Okay, at least I hadn't tried to get him maimed or something by accident this time. It was a bit of an improvement, but even so, it wasn't exactly the best wish. With the way things were...

    "Fine, then. What, exactly, did you do to him?"

    "Why, I did precisely what you asked me for, master. Or, more accurately, what he asked for. Part of that was to be stronger. Of course, I wouldn't want him to simply see himself growing that way. Life would be much too tricky if everybody saw it, so I made sure to put in the usual memory tricks. He'll be the only one to vaguely remember being his normal size. However, he wished to be stronger..." I heard her giggle, and the sound of it just continued to aggravate me. "Such a delightfully vague wish! There were plenty of things I could've done. Turned him into an animal, made him too thick to move... But then I wouldn't get to watch you two go at it!"

    "Go at it? What's that supposed to..." I paused for a moment before I could feel redness starting to appear into my cheeks, already able to guess what was coming."

    "Master, you had wished for him to be happy and to get what he wants. He said that he to be stronger, but that was just one thing. Wasn't it clear enough that he wanted you? That he liked being that size, but it was since you liked it too? After that, I just had to get you two together properly!"

    I stammered as I heard her say it. "You... You made me attracted to-"

    Nino only giggled mischievously as she shook her head. "Oh, I didn't need to do any magic for that. Once you made him start talking, I didn't need to do anything else! If I wanted to, I probably could've given you a prod in the right direction, but it was so much more amusing to watch you two mumbling to each other to get it out in the open!"

    "Great. I make one wish to hear the truth, and then I make him spill everything. Just how could-"

    "No, no, no!" Nino just kept on laughing as she looked at me. "You wished that he would tell you the truth if you wanted it. How much did you want him to keep talking after you heard him start?" I tried to respond, but my mouth had to close as I looked at the floor. After he had started to talk, I certainly didn't want him to keep on admitting something like that. I had wanted him to give some excuse, so he wouldn't make life horrible for himself.

    "You mean, he could've stopped anytime? Then, why wouldn't he?"

    "Boys like you really are clueless, aren't they? I've seen it far too many times. Always so ignorant when someone confesses to them…" Her laugh was really getting annoying by now, but I did my best to bear it as she shook her head before turning back to me. "Once you made him start talking, he didn't have any reason to stop anymore. There's nothing keeping from saying the truth if he wants to, and once he had said that you guys were more than friends, he didn't want to stop. You simply gave him the nudge that he needed to admit everything. Just because you were too nervous to admit there was something between you two doesn't mean he was."

    "Well, of course I didn't want to say that! This kind of thing could ruin him! People don't all think this kind of thing is okay, and I'm not going to mess up his life again! I mean, he could get kicked off of every team he's on, and everybody could think that he's-"

    "You realize, of course, how easily you could wish for people to be accepting? For him to stay on his teams without trouble? For it to seem normal?" I paused as I heard her speak, turning to look at my feet as I heard her speak about it so obviously. "You really are clueless about it, aren't you... Him being accepted was probably never a problem. You just tried to find an excuse for why you'd be able to get away from it, didn't you?" She giggled again. By now, I didn't know how in the world any sound could be as annoying as her chuckling, and I had to clench my palms to try to restrain myself. She was taking way too much pleasure in pointing out what an awkward guy I was. "Oh, don't worry... You wished for him to be happy, and while he didn't talk about it, I'm sure he has acceptance in his mind, too. I make sure to be very thorough with wishes... I get to have more fun with them that way! In fact, this one still has so much potential..."

    "What? You're still-"

    "You can see for yourself. Should you get to your class, too?" I blinked, but the moment my eyes were open, she was gone again. I wanted to shout at her, but I was interrupted when I saw others starting to enter the locker room. I quickly passed by them, eager to avoid any awkward questions about why I was there, heading back to my classroom, and back to where Rasler was again.
