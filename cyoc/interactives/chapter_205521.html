    Nadia Phillips, a recent convert to the goddess cult that had started up in town, was walking home alone from work one evening.

    It was a pleasant summer night. Balmy, with a wind blowing in her face that tickled. Her windswept hair blew back dramatically.

    As nice as the evening was, she was having trouble enjoying it.

    She had heard about the tragic murder of Martina Grisham, and bristled. All young women were being put on notice.

    As if they didn't had enough to deal with already. Roe v Wade being gutted by the Supreme Court back in June was evidence enough that women were second class citizens in America.

    And the world, if Nadia was being honest with herself.

    She worried about the state of the world, wondering if this was the 1930s all over again. Authoritarianism was rising everywhere.

    "Did I make a mistake in joining the cult?" she wondered.

    What if nuclear war happened? She wouldn't die. She'd survive, but would she WANT to survive? Would she want to live dealing with cancer and radiation sickness in a barren wasteland?

    She prayed that the goddesses Meiko and Brenda had an answer for that.

    Shit, even ignoring nuclear war, would she want to live forever *period*?

    Out of a dark alleyway, a stranger suddenly interrupted Nadia's existential crisis.

    The angry looking white man grabbed her throat and pointed a gun at her, pressing her up against a brick wall.

    Nadia's feet, lifted a few inches off the ground, were flailing.

    "He's strong", Nadia thought fearfully.

    The man appraised her with hate in his eyes, as if he were staring at something less than human.

    "Young lady, you're coming with m-"

    He suddenly felt an overwhelming electric shock course throughout his body.

    The man didn't have time to shout or scream in pain. He didn't have the ability to do anything but realize the consequences of his mistake. He silently dropped Nadia back to the ground.

    Nadia, catching her breath, playfully shook her finger at him.

    "Don't do that again."

    The man, still in pain, didn't respond to the tiny woman.

    "And the same goes for the guy in the van!" she yelled as loudly as possible, turning her head, as she continued her walk home.

    "How'd that bitch know I was here?" the man in the forest green van parked on the corner asked himself.
