The other chimps weren't that fond of Victor either.

He had to do some of the less dignified tricks, being trotted out and used for reality show gags - any time a suitably irresponsible show needed a chimpanzee, poor Trevor was chosen. He had the most marketable look, see. Chad almost envied Trevor for the way that the other chimp got to leave the area and go see the world. Though in other aspects, he didn't.

Pushed him in front of dazzling lights and exposed him to loud noises. He had to eat sickly sweet cakes and candies. Despite his preference being for fruits and meat. He'd prefer to sink his teeth into some capuchin instead of the cakes and candies that Victor insisted he adored.

"Man, if only she knew - if only she knew. But she's visited this zoo several times now. And she had no idea." Victor chuckled to himself, and Curious found himself chortling as well - not even recognizing who he was laughing at.

Even Victor for all his ego knew chimpanzees needed to be treated with a degree of restraint. The only way in and out was a small land bridge and a gate Victor would open and close with a key card. He treated this as...sufficient security.

Of course, now I'm the human. I'm the one in charge. The one in control. You guys make me a lot of money in a hurry every time."

But once Victor started walking away, he got a call on his phone. Seemed to be a reboot of a certain franchise, who wanted their third episode to involve a guest star chimpanzee, imminently. As in, tomorrow - due to this being a recent addition to the script. With the sizable backers that studio had, he knew there was only one chimp for the job.

    "Well, Trevor, looks like you're getting another job coming up real soon. Let's get you to the travel cage." Victor promptly walked over to the entry point to the enclosure. Trevor flinched once he heard the enclosure's gate click and swing open. He may have been a chimp, but he could recognize that sound as good as anything. They were smart apes, regardless what Victor thought - they could recognize patterns and behavior.

Trevor never behaved like this, and Victor usually just got whichever he needed to play a role. More assertively. And the nervous chimp was now left shivering, twitching, as Victor tried to start back toward the entrance to the enclosure. Things - despite how odd and unusual the entry was - seemed to be proceeding toward a normal outcome. Curious would just sit there watching, and the magic man Victor would just carry Trevor out to go do a TV Show.

The chimp was flailing in his grip, Trevor letting loose simian profanities and statements to how he didn't want to go again - which only Curious really understood. Victor managed to get out, "Stop---Stop thrash---" And then it happened.

    Trevor sank his teeth into Victor's throat. The chimp's eyes widened as he seemed to realize some ancient violent impulse that had gone long denied in his time within the ape trade, long and strong arms whipping and finding purchase. He was thrashing with a purpose now. Victor was gurgling - and his pained whimpers and cries, screams, all they did was encourage the wrathful ape. Trevor screamed and cried and hollered, grasping with his hands and ripping skin - muscle - tendons - in those instants, Victor learned a very, very sensible lesson.

    A chimpanzee is not a toy. It is a carnivorous ape.

Curious was left whimpering despite his greater stature than Trevor. He didn't even dare do a thing - this was after all something he barely understood. But he knew Trevor was his pack friend. Trevor wouldn't do this without good reason.

    Even as Victor's eyes rolled back in their sockets, the life being beaten and ripped out of him, Trevor kept on going. He outright dragged Victor over toward the middle of the enclosure, the only resistance he faced being a weak slap at the ape's chest. Most of Victor's head was...just gone. And Curious for his part felt something odd going on. A tingling, twitching and strange sensation inside of his head. Something told him the spark of life went out in Victor.

He ran and didn't look back, even as he registered his fur starting to trail as he escaped out the gate.

The zoo was a large and frightening place as the young man's mind asserted itself over remaining instincts. Chad ran, evading the sound of bootfalls and the worried chatter of the employees. He didn't even notice his feet starting to shift, his hands likewise beginning to alter.

More awkward. His features continuing to shift as he landed. Black hair fading into curly blond on top of his head as he ran for it. He glanced at his hands, then at his fully human form in the windows of the car next to him. "I'm...I'm human again."

But when he managed to hear some activity going on in the zoo, he knew he couldn't chance the people in there not knowing what Victor was up to. He didn't - so long as he thought that zoo might be anywhere near him.

He didn't even know how long it had been - despite "Curious" having heard all of that. He didn't know if he was missed. He didn't know if Karen knew he was gone - if she assumed he flaked, he didn't know what would happen with his friends on the sports team.

    Did they assume he was dead?

But he could keep going, once he caught his breath. But that didn't come. Evidently, a cop had been called somewhere along the line. Hearing about a naked guy running along the side of the highway. The cop, a rather fair faced ginger haired young man, seemed ready initially to ask how much Chad had to drink - he looked thoroughly unamused...

    "...Chad?" The young cop asked. "Chad Jones?"

    "Yeah...?" Chad blearily caught his standing and looked at the cop, "Who's asking?"

The jock just slacking and relaxing against the seat. "Where---what happened? Why were you naked and running along the side of the highway?"

He'd need to---to boil it down, once he was capable of explaining what happened. But right now, Chad was exhausted, he was out of breath, and he needed to get back to town. "I'll...I'll uh. I'll explain---you just...just get me home. Please." He'd never talked like that to his bros or anyone at school - but then, extraordinary circumstances demanded extraordinary responses.

    "Will do. I'll bring you down to the police station first, let the missing persons case runners know that you've been found." And with that, Beau drove Chad down to the police station - and the jock just relaxed for the time, reclining in the seat.
