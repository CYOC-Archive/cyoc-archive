    General Heroud stood inside one of the library rooms on the second floor looking out the window toward the city he loved, the city with its population in the millions and one of the largest cities in Europe. The capital of the nation he loved and served.

    "Infested with an American's attempt at playing God," Heroud grumbled to himself (obviously in French).

    "We have easy access to various tranquilizer darts, general," a colonel reported to him, "the only thing we need is time to create more of the formula that will overcome the unbridled lust in so many of these Kats."

    "And what of those that are attacking our soldiers and policemen?" General Heroud asked back, "I'd rather have them being mindless sex maniacs. They're more prone to making a mistake that way and easier for us to deal with. Give them their minds back and they become far more dangerous... the reports we've received on when we've killed them has only been when we've gotten a round through a major artery and they bleed to death..."

    The colonel sighed heavily. The reports of killed human police officers were true and several of the dead Kats had been armed with weapons that combined with their physical abilities would make them even more dangerous. That was why the army had used lethal force in establishing the quarantine. An aggressive Chakat with all the abilities the scientists said they had was extremely dangerous. It made the stories of the Beast of Gévaudan seem like a bedtime story. Thankfully, despite having the organs to survive what might be fatal hits in humans, the army had proved that these Kats were not "Wolverine" fast healers. A wound that cut a major artery or vein would still bleed out uncontrolled if nothing was done. It might take a Kat longer to bleed to death due to their larger size and thus having more blood, but they would still bleed to death, and that was the fate of those that had been aggressive toward the army or the police.

    "I would agree that we should kill those Kats, General," the colonel acknowledged, "but perhaps we should give them their minds back so that we can find out which Kats are merely victims of the virus and which are part of the aggressive group that has started this. After all... the young Bourbon de Moltitier seems to be in her right mind and isn't supporting those who attacked us."

    General Heroud only sighed, but said nothing for a few moments. Eventually he conceded the point.

    "Very well," General Heroud spoke slowly, "Once we have enough of the tranquilizer darts to issue we may begin going in and using them and seeing who are supporters of the Republic and who are supporting the lunatic... but should our troops be attacked..."

    "Lethal force is still to be used," the colonel nodded, "of course sir."
