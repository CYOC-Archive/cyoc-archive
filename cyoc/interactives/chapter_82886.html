    Something had to be done, Harry thought, but he was in no position to do it. Clearly, there was some serious corruption in the park, involving
    several people in a position much higher than his own. It might even be the entire company. He had Naomi's word his phone was never
    monitored by anyone in upper management, but what did she have to base that on? There was a clear divide within the company between the
    huskies, there to maintain a security presence in the park, and those involved in the business side of things. Even under the best of
    circumstances, there was something slightly sinister at play there. Not only did he have no real authority to pursue matters of security that high
    up, he, and everyone else in his pack, had been given a form specifically tailored to show an almost blind level of loyalty to unaltered humans
    when they were present. If he tried to pursue things from this side, he could be dismissed, fired, or possibly even just scolded until his
    instincts forced him to drop the matter. The best he could really hope for at this point was for his friends outside the park to really bring down
    the hammer and take things to court. With the sort of information Charlotte was compiling, moral outrage would trump what advantage the
    company had from its legal team. Of course, the downside to that plan is how much ammunition it would give to those "moral majority" types
    who'd been trying to have the park shut down from day one. At the very least, they'd have the entire place shut down for the duration of any
    serious investigation. What would happen to everyone? Forced back to human forms by a device whose workings were only known to someone
    who was suspected of being on the take from foreign powers? Just sent home as they were? Harry was fairly sure he could work security
    anywhere, but some forms just weren't suited to life outside the park. Just a couple days ago there'd been that incident with the rabbit girl
    someone found catatonic in a ditch after that whole Lone Wolf incident-

    Harry felt dizzy for a moment as a rather horrific potential connection came to mind. Charlotte had just mentioned transforming "political
    dissidents" and criminals into "loyal" huskies and concubines. He thought back to the list Naomi had left him. He'd completely forgotten until
    now but that was the same rabbit, wasn't she. The one who'd come to the park to research some hack "documentary" about the possibility of
    the park doing exactly the sort of thing he was learning was happening behind the scenes. First off, it was clear everyone owed her an apology,
    but the last he'd heard, she had earned a reputation as a bit of a sex addict even by rabbit standards. And then there was Goldie. They didn't
    come much more loyal than her. It could be coincidental, but if someone did want to test the effectiveness of pacifying dissidents and
    criminals, you couldn't pick much better test subjects than a vocal filmmaker who'd been making waves for weeks before signing up and a
    slimeball who'd only just been given a lifetime ban and got caught by a last minute scan. Naomi would be disappointed to hear that one.

    None of this would really suggest someone was spying on their internal reports of course. The rabbit girl was a lot more well known outside the
    park than in, and he'd obviously had to send the Lone Wolf's ban up the chain of command to enforce it. Still, at least he couldn't think of any
    older oddities that matched up with any of this, so odds were against any of the real sinister elements being reported on stretching back more
    than a couple of weeks. At least as far as trial runs went. Of course, now that he was coming to think in such paranoid terms, another point hit
    him. The management rep he'd talked to about all this stag stuff that was apparently just the top of the iceberg had told him to his face some
    of that dirty money was going into salaries for this latest batch of new huskies. Any given one of them could be some kind of double agent,
    keeping tabs on him and furthering some of these crooked plans. Hell, all of them might. The only ones he could really trust were Katie, and
    funnily enough, Goldie. He still had his doubts about where her loyalties lied, and if the whole "enhanced loyalty" thing was true, there were
    some serious ethical issues there, but at least he had a solid handle on what made her tick... or used to. The "Lone Wolf" had needled him
    about bribery, but that was clearly just a head game trying to keep from being ejected. Obviously he'd valued personal freedom over money.

    Harry picked the phone back up. After giving Charlotte his feedback, he'd have to tell her not to call anymore, and any serious meetings would
    need to take place outside where there was nowhere to put a microphone. He might have to let things slide with that stupid tournament the
    bucks were holding, but they were pacing it out over several days. He had at least a little time to work with there.

    Harry's timetable overlooked an important point. When real bucks clashed every spring, it wasn't to settle power struggles in a men's club, it
    was a display to help them attract a mate. The plan Chad and Vinny had for the bucks who dropped out early wasn't to humiliate them after
    everything was over. They wanted their new does watching the rest of the action, at least for the final day where the best of the best were
    decided. The thinking was that instinct would win over, just as it had the last time this sort of event had happened in the park. Just as it was
    now in fact, for those at least relatively natural does watching from the nearby rocks for the matter. The newly minted does would be
    overwhelmed by the desire to mate with the strongest bucks, and as Chad put it, "forget being weak little beta males" letting everyone else
    walk off with a "prize." A few had already been eliminated and marched off for their conversion. The way the agreement they'd signed was
    worded, they weren't all exactly sure what they were in store for, and those that knew figured it was just another form of hazing. Spend a
    couple days looking like a girl, playing cheerleader for their seniors. They'd faced more humiliation than that before now. Most of the "local"
    bucks who'd agreed to enter were still in the running, showing experience did mean something against a physical edge. Those who went down
    early from that group however were safe for the time being. They hadn't signed any pledge, but they had agreed to accept the rule of whoever
    won. Assuming it was someone from the Stags, that would be another story. Chad, meanwhile, knew exactly what was on the line. He'd started
    all this expecting to win, and he wasn't going to let some stupid busted antler stop him.

    To his credit, Chad's injury wasn't necessarily crippling. A wise old buck with years of experience could even turn a thing like that into an
    advantage. Charge a bit off center, really throw his shoulder into it. He'd catch his opponent off balance from the side and send him twirling
    into the dirt. A wise old buck would almost certainly do something like that, but this was Chad.

    As he charged forward, he simply bent his neck to one side. This left his good antler nice and centered, and even put the points farther ahead
    which he figured would give him the advantage of reach. No, wise was not a word that described Chad at all, and despite screams from the
    sideline from people with a better grasp of anatomy and physics, he refused to straighten out his neck. This time, it was more than an antler he
    broke.

    Further fights were cancelled for the rest of the day, with very little resistance. Katie swore under her breath as the medics loaded Chad onto a
    stretcher to rush to the exit. Chad was loud, arrogant, sexist pig, and apparently the one who lead this sideshow, but if the transformation
    process didn't happen to be the best cure in the world for a broken neck, he'd be paralyzed for life if not worse thanks to his own stupidity. At
    least this meant an early end to crowd control duty. Harry was supposed to be getting another call about this whole mess, and she was all for
    leaving early to hunt him down.

    Max tossed the plump squirrel he'd snagged for breakfast up in the air, watching it twirl and panic before catching it again by the tail. Playing
    with his food was almost as much fun as playing with his toys. Of course, the way things were looking, he'd have to move on soon enough.
    He'd had to abandon his old toys when a plumber had heard them screaming. He'd thought he had plenty of sound proofing down there, but
    that pipe he'd chained them to carried sound surprisingly far.

    He twisted his catch around, then grabbed two legs and began to pull. No pipes here, no basements either, and no chains. That's why he only
    had the one at the moment. He'd found it in a cave. Plenty of big rocks to hobble it with sitting right there, and a few narrow passages in the
    back it just barely fit through to hide it. That wouldn't last forever though, It was a risk bringing it food and water to keep things going, and if it
    really had enough determination, it could probably pull itself back through the gaps, at least far enough for sound to carry outside.

    He tossed the squirrel up one more time, this time catching it in his teeth. He should probably finish it off before leaving. Can't have it talking
    later, that's where the rookies around here had all slipped up.
