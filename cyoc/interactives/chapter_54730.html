    1681

    "So how are you guys doing?" Marie laid back on the bed in what resembled a small four
    person bedroom. Mutt played with a cat on the floor.

    "Very well thank you." Said Gita, convinced her experiments concerning the lion cub Rosa
    were a success since after a year the girl had shown no signs of aging. But Lilith had
    warned her that if all the children in town stopped aging all together at once that the evil
    adults would get suspicious.

    That Rosa was suffering teasing from her litter mates that she had stopped growing and
    was going to be a dwarf hadn't even registered in Gita's brain.

    Of course, by now, Gita was seventeen, practically a young woman. Goldwyn was sixteen
    like Marie and Griselle was fifteen.

    Of course, Gita absolutely refused to acknowledge even remotely they could possible be
    'one of them!'

    Even since that horrid nightmare years ago, Marie and the witchlings had not spoken about
    it once, fully convinced it had simply been a 'bad future' as Herodias described it where
    they friends had turned evil.

    It would be four more years before Marie Cruz realized the full truth, and the three little
    girls would forever be lost and be the monsters that the people of Glendale would know
    them as.

    Marie off handedly mentioned 'Jack'.

    "Wait...wait..." Said Betty breathlessly, "So, Marie Cruz accidently created Jack The Ripper?"

    The fact that the ripper might have been a european branch of the wolf pack was another
    matter.

    "The name of the killer was never actually 'Jack.' "

    "Whose Jack The ripper?" Lilly asked.

    "A very bad person you don't need to know about." Maddex's two heads said, "And don'
    worry, that wolf wasn't related to us just cause he had deep black fur, Zackery and Alpha
    Jensen have the same colored fur and aren't related."

    "Charcoal black." Betty corrected.

    "Eh?"

    "Zackery's fur is charcoal black, Jensen and Jenny's fur is midnight black."

    "Oh, right, I forget."

    "How do you forget thing like that?"

    "Well, I meant Yomato and Amy have similar colored fur and aren't related." The DNA
    testing had been quite conclusive, June even wondered if she had a Japanese ancestor she
    didn't know about.

    Glass Marie nodded at that "Right."

    Marie Cruz and the girls chattered about this and that, and what was what. Neither ever
    mentioned precisely where they lived, almost as if they subconsciously held it back.

    Marie mentioned girls who would never stop gossiping, and the witchlings mentioned
    adults who wouldn't shut up.

    Marie Cruz awoke from her friendly chat to find herself in the carriage that was taking her
    the final stretch back to the Vatican after her months of travel across Europe.

    Of course she had to fall back on some of her old pallor tricks in order to legally travel, but
    she had years of experience.

    She was asked hours later, "I trust you found all the books you were looking for on your
    trip Marie?"

    "Yes father." Marie answered, "I did. They've proven most insightful."

    "I should hope so, I don't approve of you doing this, you know that 'assignment' was only
    to get you out of the council's elbow room."

    "I know. But I did as I was told like a good Inquisitor."

    "You're still too young, you're still a child."

    "I'm sixteen."

    "You have no idea how young that really is." Said her god father as he leaned against his
    cain, when was no longer precisely for show. He had needed it for two years now.

    Marie said, "I'm sorry father..."

    "You should go check in to formally acknowledge you're here."

    Marie started but then realized what her father meant and nodded. "Yes father."

    Marie made sure to burn the jacket and trousers she had brought back with her before
    even getting within sight of the Vatican.

    Marie had gotten fifteen steps within the Vatican walls when she side stepped someone
    trying to hug her from behind causing the person to almost trip over their own two feet.

    "whao-, ugh, I knew that smell! Marie you're back!"

    Marie said calmly, "Yes Kathrine, I am."

    The young Felman woman who looked back at her had fur that in another time and place
    could have been mistaken for cotton candy. Her fur was primary powder blue, with bright
    pink fur on her belly and other odd places, and all of it had flashy green trim.

    The sight of her fur actually at times made Marie ill.

    "I wish you had sent more some letters while you were out. It gets lonely without you here."

    "I'm not the only person in the Vatican."

    "None of them are Marie Cruz."

    Kathrine was keeping to the shadows of the building to starve off heat. She looked up at
    Marie quite literally being several inches shorter than her (then again, humans were to
    felmans as lupmans were to humans in terms of proportions).

    Marie Cruz had long accepted her name as such, much as she accepted her god father as
    her parent. The witchlings had actually helped with that, she didn't have to pretend to be a
    boy and not pretend to be anything around them.

    "Whatever." Marie walked along.

    "Why do you do always have to be like that?" Kathrine followed along like a lost pet.

    "I didn't ask you to be my friend."

    "No you didn't but you sure looked like you needed one!"

    Marie thought back to when she had been transfered for the first time really to live with
    children of her own gender... 'I didn't make the mistake I made before, this time I just kept
    my distance... too bad this one just won't go away.' "That's your opinion."

    "Yep! And I said it's kinda good one too." Kathrine said smiling. "I just want to get to know
    you."

    "I'm not a safe person to be around Kathrine. If you don't want to use up all nine of your
    lives before you hit middle age you should stay far away from me."

    "They're my nine lives and I'll use'em how I like! Ha ha!"

    Lilly asked, "Do felmans really have nine-"

    "No." EVERYONE in the mirror room said.

    "Do you always have to be so cheerful like some kind of doll?"

    "... Toys are made to be there for children when they're alone. Toys are there to comfort
    those who need comfort. I'm -sorry- I thought you were a boy pretending to be a girl
    when you first arrived...I read it in a book once."

    "I forgot about that a long time ago."

    "Oh... okay, that's good! Then we can be friends if everything is forgiven and forgotten!"

    "Who said I wanted to be your friend?"

    "Well I'm -your- friend weather you like it or not, so there, nya!" Kathrine stuck her tongue
    out.

    "She sure was cheerful." Maddex's two head said.

    "She had to be." Glass Marie said, "Or rather, she was expected to be. Red furred felmen
    were expected to be passionate, blue furred were expected to be calm and logical, yellow
    to be timid and submissive, and so on. Kathrine due to her fur color was expected to act
    perky and happy-go-lucky. It was a system that died out by the final years of the nineteen
    century. By the time Marie moved into the girls' living quarters, the others had grown tired
    of Kathrine's happy 'rain or shine' attitude. She felt she had failed the expectations of her
    family like that and saw a sudden late comer arrival like Marie as a chance to make up for
    it. Inside she was practically begging Marie to accept her. Even when the others decided
    Marie wouldn't open up to anyone, Kathrine kept trying."

    "She never heard of 'kick the dust from your sandals and move on?' " Lawrence had to ask.

    "Maybe she did. But she also honestly wanted Marie's friendship."

    "So was one of them wasn't she?" Betty asked, "One of the three Marie took with her across
    the Atlantic."

    "Marie had intended to bring Mutt with her, if her dog hadn't died of old age within the five
    years she was looking for allies. The hound dog by then was ancient. Kathrine would first
    almost have her soul ripped out of her by a rebel angel... Marie thought that would have
    scared Kathrine into never coming near anything related to her again... instead, she
    decided just how BADLY Marie needed a friend... in particular when her father finally died
    of old age in the next few years..."

    "What were their christian names?" Betty asked. "The girls?"

    "They never used them. Nor even bothered to memorize them. They picked them
    practically at random, caring practically nothing for the entire ceremony... and they would
    come by new names themselves four years from then.... And it was around then that Marie
    realized that Kathrine was much more a friend than the fair weather friendship the
    witchlings offered..."
