    The Fate of the Grounds

    Outside, Marcy continued to run about the the grounds. The woman that "caught" her earlier had only managed to do so by getting the help of the others to trap her against the hedge fence, but was unprepared for getting bit. As she ran, Marcy chuckled at the woman's startled yell. Served her right for grabbing the wolf-pup. But, Marcy didn't repeat the mistake of getting to close to the edge of the grounds. If they were going to try and "divide and conquer", she would stay more around the more open areas of the property or the mansion, where her father, mother, the twins, or Martha could come to her rescue.

    As she made another turn around a tree, Marcy heard a familiar voice call out to her.

    "Marcy!" Jensen's voice was heard, "to the other door, now!"

    Marcy was quick to bolt toward the mansion.

    "Who was that?" the woman that Marcy had bit as she kept running after the pup.

    "What I want to know is how did he know your name?" one of the other men as they joined the woman grounds-keeper.

    "I go by Marcine," the woman, Marcine answered.

    Marcy, meanwhile made it quickly to the door and rushed through the pace after it opened. The reddish brown pup, however was carrying too much speed, and ended up crashing into Alice, who had been waiting there after emerging from a preset mirror.

    "Oh, I'm sorry," Marcy spoke quickly to the glass wolf pup, "I didn't see you there."

    "It is alright," Alice responded as thy got up, "I am rather indestructible."

    "In... In... In-de-struck-a-who?" Marcy panted after all the running.

    "Can't be broken," came Kaylee's voice from the interior doorway.

    Marcy looked up to see that Kaylee and Jenny had arrived. Both of them them helped Marcy and Alice back up.

    "You have your instructions?" Kaylee asked Alice.

    "Yes, Alpha Female," Alice nodded, remembering the lengthy list of instructions that Martha had given her before she went into the mirror in that room to come to this room.

    "Quick, she went in here!" came a male voice from outside the room.

    Marcy quickly moved to stand behind Kaylee and beside Jenny, while Alice moved to the center of the room. She waited patiently for the groundskeepers to come rushing into the room. All of paused and gasped when they saw the creatures in the room.

    The men were quickly attracted to a seven foot tall red furred she-wolf, standing protectively over a black furred female wolf pup, two years older then the pup they had been chasing, as well as the pup they had been chasing. Marcine quickly noticed a lone glass wolf pup, slightly older then the pup they had been chasing standing in the center of the room.

    "They're multiplying!" one man gasped.

    "Come now," Kaylee countered, "we aren't rabbits."

    The groundskeepers found themselves chucking but tried to remain firm.

    "How do we get them out?" the fifth in the group asked, "Mr. and Mrs. Smith aren't going to like a bunch of wolves running around the property, let alone inside the house."

    "Actually," Jenny spoke up, "I'm quite fine with it."

    She then put one arm around one of Kaylee's legs and licked Marcy's cheek for solidarity. Alice only gave a slight chuckle as the the grounds keepers looked between themselves. The gathered wolves, flesh and glass, weren'tall that bad, but there was no way any of them could be Mrs. Smith h... maybe the red furred adult, but she would have to be wearing a very complicated costume, which they didn't expect.

    "You'd best ask them the question, Alice," Kaylee instructed.

    Alice nodded, "We'll gladly answer all your questions, if you play a game with me."

    "And what game is that?" Marcine asked curiously.

    "Transform or dare, to all five of you," Alice spoke calmly, letting Marcy and Jenny keep their turns.

    All of them looked rather curious at the gathered wolves and then at each other. They had heard of the game, like many of Glendale's residents, but they either didn't understand who these wolves could get it, or didn't believe it.

    "Transform, I guess," Marcine answered, guessing that the game would have to make her act like she had become something.

    "I won't let her go alone, transform," one man answered as he took Marcine's hand.

    "Dare," the third man spoke, not wanting to take any chances.

    "Dare," the fourth spoke, joining the the third.

    "Da-," the fifth began, but stopped when Kaylee gave a low threatening snarl.

    "TRANSFORM!!" he finished quickly, not wanting the she wolf to attack him.

    "Okay," Alice smiled and turned to Kaylee and the others, including Jensen, who had just arrived from the other room where she herself had transformed from her previous form, Adam, and then turned to the first two, "You shall.."
