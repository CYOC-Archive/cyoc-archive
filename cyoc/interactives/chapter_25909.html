    Wendy looked up groggily. A woman who looked to be in her mid-thirties stood beside her, looking on her with concern.

    “Wendy, I’m Jennifer Grogan with the Evansville Resident Agency,” Vix introduced herself flashing her badge. She closed the door. “Blackhorn wanted you to know that you are on recuperative leave for the next month. He’s also writing you up for a commendation.”

    “What about Ramer?” Vix’s face hardened.

    “He’s currently the subject of a state-wide manhunt. He almost killed you; he DID kill a deputy named Juan Enriquez who had a wife and child, and he may have started a war with the local covens.”

    Wendy felt her insides churn. “Why would MY being shot start a war?” Vix grinned and shook her head. “Wendy, it’s all right, we know your secret, there was no way you would have survived with your heart being stopped as long as it had. We managed to convince the doctors that you’d been specially trained in Zen Buddhism and that your body is exceptionally resilient.”

    “You mean that they didn’t come right OUT and say you were full of shit?” Wendy asked wryly.

    “Well, that excuse will do for now. Agent Patel, you still have a career with the FBI, but Blackhorn will probably reassign you to a different investigation. Frankly, I hope you stay here in Indiana. Did you have any questions?”

    “Have we contacted the group? I’d much rather concentrate on catching Ramer than worrying that my sisters are about to do something on their own.”

    Vix was quiet. “Wendy, you DO understand that CATCHING Ramer creates a whole lot of problems. If he is caught, he goes to court. If he goes to court, he’s afforded all the rights he has coming to them, including the right to an attorney, the right to testify on his own behalf in court, and the right to a jury trial.”

    Wendy had considerably more perspicacity than most people who had been clinically dead less than twenty-four hours beforehand. “In other words, if he’s caught…we get a lot more attention than we want.”

    “Exactly, Agent Patel, you must realize that your actions have put us in a hell of a situation, we either kill Dan without a trial or he blabs in open court or he gets away scot-free. Why didn’t you just come forward with the information you’d been compromised?”

    Wendy sighed. She couldn’t very well say that she was going to work as an intermediary between the FBI and their happy band of werewolves. “Well, Dan was a bit of a psychopath, wasn’t he? Surely the FBI realizes that they missed that important detail during his initial psychological profiling?”

    “You could have gone over his head. Look, I’m not going to hassle you about it, just understand that, while you DID do us a great favor by showing us how unstable Ramer was, you’ve also created a hole for Dan to run through. I highly doubt that he’s going to stop with his attempted killing of you.” Vix paused to let that sink in. “That’s all I have for you now, I understand that you parents will be along shortly. Take care now.” Vix exited the room, frustrated. Killing Wendy now would do nothing to improve things.
