Of her 3 children, two of them became puppy-boys, a labrador and another dalmatian, adopting the natural colors of each, while the third became an orange newt, white spots ringed with black over her body. They were splashing in the surf as their parents looked on, unaware that anything had changed.

    Another pink pig-woman was sunning herself on the beach, naked and soaking in the rays.

    The other lifeguard surprised the ram and lizard, by changing into an anthro shark, the sunlight glistening off his new blue dorsal fin and black striped flanks and tail, fading to pale white on his front. His face flowed out into a shark snout, mouth filling with rubbery triangular teeth.

    A few families of dolphins were spotted next, colors ranging from sunset pink to black.

    A rubber pangolin made his way out of the surf, two-toned body of blue and light tan. He plodded up to a latex wolf woman, and a rubber wolf-child in her arms.

    A few more bulls were made, and a few more lizard-types, their families and significant others changing with them, sometimes the same species, sometimes not.

    Another surprise for the pair, a woman changed into a rust colored snake, her legs merging and lengthening into a prodigious tail, as she slithered onto a rock to sun herself, her rubber cobra hood retracting to the sides of her head. Her husband, or significant other at least, became a rubber rhino, increasing in size as his skin darkened to a deep emerald green.

    More and more changed, the beach becoming a mass of rubber animals in every color imaginable. Primates, mustelids (a family of rubber skunks cavorted in the water, normal colors inverted), avians, reptiles, pachyderms, anteaters, the list went on and on.

    There were a few people who did not change, Casper noted, probably because they did not have a favorite animal, or their favorite animal was not a vertebrate.

    "What's wrong?" he asked, putting an arm around her shiny shoulders.

The lab where they were created had several machines that could be used to mold new Synthetics, taking materials from the parents, themselves breathing life into the new Synthetic body.

    He remembered that Synthetics had existed for at least 40 years, the civil rights movement for their personhood and rights as sentient beings being only 30 years ago. He marvelled that humanity has been so accepting of the new creatures, perhaps he was making the world a better place by changing people, he thought.
