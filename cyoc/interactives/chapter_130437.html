    Edwin, now Lavi, experimentally tried out the new wings on her back, moving them back and forth. She noticed twinkling sparkles coming off of them as she tried them out.

    "Ah, I see you've begun to try out your new wings!" Roseli said. "Some of the prettiest I've seen, if I do say so myself! And I do! Tee hee!"

    Lavi frowned. "They feel so weird though... how would I even begin to use them?"

    Roseli, skipping behind her, reached for Lavi's wings, stretching them out and examining them. "Well, they're perfectly functional! How's about you learn how to fly?"

    Lavi thought for a bit, but then nodded. Flying would be amazing, and it seemed this would be her new life. "Allright! How do we start?"

    Roseli clasped her hands together. "Well, that's easy!" Her hands began to glow with a bright red aura, and she snapped her fingers.

    In an instant, they teleported to the top of one of the shelves in the shop. Lavi, who had never really been a big fan of heights, hugged Roseli out of fear. Normally, the shelf wouldn't have been taller than 4 feet. But now, the new fairy couldn't have been more than an inch tall. "I-It's so high!"

    Roseli was surprised, but she comforted Edwin, running her hands through her hair. "Hey, you can do this! You're a fairy now, which means you can do something as simple as flying!"

    Lavi, still trembling, let go of Roseli. "O-Okay...." She said, walking with her bare feet to the edge. She looked down, whatever resolve she had gathered, gone.

    Roseli sighed at Lavi's antics. "Guess you'll have to learn like I did...." She said, walking over to Lavi.

    "What do you mean?" Lavi asked, before Roseli shoved her off of the edge.

    Freefalling, her hair and boobs waving wildly, she couldn't help but scream as she fell. "AAAAAAAHHH!"
