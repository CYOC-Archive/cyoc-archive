    A Day at the Mall

    21 year old Brian left his house on an early Saturday afternoon for an visit at the mall. He lived close by, and the weather was pleasant, so he decided to walk there. It was a sunny day, with a cool breeze gently blowing, rustling the leaves on each tree.

    Nearly to the mall, Brian turned a corner and spotted a shimmering object on the ground. He walked over and knelt down, picking the object up, realizing it was a shiny amulet of some sort, with the letters “Z U L O” etched into the metal on one side. He wasn’t a penny pincher or a pack rat, but he figured that this was a unique item, and thought that maybe he should look it up and find out if anyone had lost it here. He buried the medallion in his back pocket and continued on.

    He arrived at the mall and had some lunch in the food court. After he finished eating, he pulled out his phone and looked up “Zulo” to try and find out what this medallion was or who it belonged to. It took some digging, but on the 16th page of Google, he finally found something. It was an old PDF on the “Medallion of Zulo”; an ancient magical artifact said to be a myth. It was told that for anyone who came across the medallion, they would possess the power to transform into anyone, simply by wearing the medallion and touching an item of clothing. By doing so, they would transform into the previous wearer of the clothing. If it had not been worn by anyone, the person would simply transform into a modified version of themselves.

    He sat back and pulled out the medallion, thinking this had to be some sort of joke. But then again, he felt deep down that this thing was real, and that he could be ANYONE he wanted. He needed to test it out, and what better place to do it than a huge shopping mall?

    The question was, where would he go first...
