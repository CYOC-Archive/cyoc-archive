Your eyes start to glaze over as your brain starts to freeze, but you give one last weak struggle, forcing your less frozen leg to take one last step forward.

It was still extremely cold in the cave, cold enough that you were still in danger of freezing if you stayed in the same position for too long, but it you reason was nowhere near as bad as the surface above you, with its ice-covered landscape and sleety gusts of wind. But the features of the cave weren't what caught your attention.

You couldn't tell what features they had from this distance, but you could tell that they looked to be a slime like you, seeming to be a pure white in color. You try to quickly get to your feet, but your body doesn't move as quickly as you want to. You end up rising jerkily, the frozen parts of your body crackling with each attempt to force them into motion. You take a step forward, only to be stopped by a weight on one of your hands.

She would be mad at you for that when she defrosted. If she defrosted.

With crackling of your nearly frozen body echoing throughout the cave, you were certain that there was no way the slime creature in the center didn't hear you coming towards then.

She also had a snout on her face, but it wasn't as slim as yours and had a distinctly wolf-like appearance.

You try to frown, but your face remained fixed into the slight smile you had made before. You attempt to reach out and touch the white wolf slime, but your arms remain firmly frozen at your sides. "H-h-hello!" you manage to shout, your words coming out much clearer this time. You're met with cold silence again. You hobble around to the front of the white slime fox anthro, your legs refusing to bend anymore, making sure to stand directly in their field of few. This is when you notice something off. The white wolf slime hadn't moved an inch from their position this whole time.

    You couldn't tell due to their coloring, but the white wolf slime was actually covered in a thick layer of frost, telling you that she had been frozen here for a long while. She, just like your blue fox slime companion and yourself, was well on her way to becoming a slime anthro ice sculpture. If you could gasp, you would, but your face wouldn't move anymore. In fact, not a single part of your body was responding to your commands anymore. You stood where you where, frozen firmly into your standing position with a slight smile on your face. If it wasn't for the fact that your body was green, any onlooker would see nothing but an ice sculpture when looking at your body.

You look around for a moment before your eyes settle on the white wolf slime anthro statute in front of you. The white wolf slime's eyes lock onto yours at the same time and widen in obvious surprise. In a frantic jerking motion, the white wolf slime's eyes jerk downwards, clearly glancing towards the floor. You look down and notice a bundle of frost-covered sticks arranged on the cave's floor. It seemed that the wolf slime had been trying to make a fire right before she froze in place, as her frozen hand was still clutched firmly onto a stick of her own.

    It was obvious what the white wolf slime wanted you to do. She wanted you to rub the sticks together and start the fire, a task that seemed easy, but with the body of a slime ice statue, it was impossible. Your eyes narrow as you try your hardest to move your body, and to your surprise, you here your joints crackling. Yes! You could move, even if it was at a glacial pace!

    You slowly lean down and pick a stick up, placing against the pile and rubbing it against the rest of the sticks. Your body might not be cooperating, but there was no way you would let yourself and your companions stay frozen!
