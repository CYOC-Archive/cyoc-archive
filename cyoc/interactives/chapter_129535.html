    He could hear some of the floorboards give a slight groan as he and Virginia made their way up the stairs. He knew this related a weretiger's urges to protect things, but he also knew that it was something that was worth protecting. Melissa was all he really had left from his life before coming to Moon Lake, and that made her precious. He couldn't let her be in danger of attack or being drugged or anything like that.     "Good," Harold answered as he took the now folded bedsheet and placed it with the other. He was about to turn back to Virginia when there was a loud knock on his front door. "Who could that be? It's too late for trick or treaters..."

    Virginia only shrugged and the two slowly made their way back downstairs, though remaining in their were forms. Changing back would then require dressing which would take time, and if someone was knocking on the door at close to midnight, then it would have to be important. They arrived to find Officer Leprune standing there quietly and with a small pad and pen with him.

    "Is something wrong officer?" Harold asked as he looked to the officer and with Virginia coming up behind him.

    "Mostly just getting the necessary statements relating to the incident at the dance," Officer Leprune answered, "and make sure we have the evidence to see that justice goes forward. Do you have a few moments? Both of you?"

    Harold glanced to Virginia and then stepped back. He held out one arm to let the officer in. Officer Leprune then followed in the direction of the living room where he turned to watch as both Harold and Virginia then moved to sit on one couch. Harold offered him a chair, but he remained standing, as he was on duty.

    "So... what will you want to know?" Harold asked.

    "Mostly details that relate to what I'd found on the security tapes," Officer Leprune spoke, "they revealed that you were both guarding the spiked punch and making sure people didn't drink from it. Neither of you left that area until after the spiked punch was taken away... Which means you had to have known it was spiked... or at least suspected it was, and has other potential questions..."

    Harold gave a slow sigh, "well... we couldn't know for sure that it was spiked. However, given some of Alice Decrux's efforts to try and get at me and the fight she started with Virginia... Combine that with the fact that her sweets shop provides the dance with its candy prize, and there was the suspicion that she would try something like that."

    "So, you blocked people from getting punch without anything beyond suspicion that it was spiked?" Officer Leprune asked.

    "But it was spiked," Harold pointed out.

    "Yes, we know that now, and in the specific circumstances, I don't think either of you is any legal trouble," Officer Leprune said slowly, "but there will be questions as to how you knew to act... and in future, it would be advisable to make certain before taking similar action. For suppose that punch WASN'T spiked and all that was added was more sugar. You'd both face criticism and potential disciplinary action for simply acting out of paranoia."

    Harold and Virginia nodded, but said nothing. There was Melissa's seemingly sixth sense or women's intuition or something that had alerted her to the fact that the punch had been spiked, but if this was going into a police report that could be requested by anyone, including those outside Moon Lake, it'd make sense not to say anything for the moment. Officer Leprune, however, then finished.

    "But... given the circumstances, you did quite well," Officer Leprune told them, "anyway... when you noticed that the spiked punch was brought back... what happened there?"

    "It was a surprise at first, but then I noticed my daughter... Melissa... being lead out behind the high school," Harold began, "However, since none of the boys around her were her present boyfriend, I knew that something had to be up and went after her."

    "And so, with other chaperones on duty, we both went out after her," Virginia added, noting Officer Leprune writing down everything as quickly as he could, "with the hope to catch up and make sure she was okay and that no one took advantage of her. Though by the time we could make it through the crowd she had disappeared into the woods and there were enough scents and sounds to confuse us as to which way she went."

    Officer Leprune nodded. Weretigers, like all feline weres had a good sense of smell, but it wasn't as good as canine weres, which meant that too many scents could be just as troubling for them as too few scents. However, he didn't say anything and kept writing.

    "Thankfully we weren't the only ones that had responded and come after her," Harold then concluded, "her boyfriend and his friends came out after us and DID find her, though Virginia and I didn't know about that at the time. In fact it wasn't until after Melissa had been rescued that I even knew that anyone besides Virginia and myself had come out."

    "Did you know for sure if she was drugged?" Officer Leprune asked.

    "We never actually found her," Virginia answered, "it was her boyfriend, Trevor Tyson, and his friends that found her. From what we were told she went with Trevor to get her things at his home and likely return here..."

    "Though she did send me a text when we returned to chaperoning the dance which gave me the indication that she and Trevor were..." Harold said slowly, "being intimate."

    "Forced himself on her?" Harold gasped, "No... From the text I got, it would say anything like that... Though I would also say that she was very interested in him and wanted to be with him... If anything, I'd think that after something like tonight... she might have put pressure on him to be with her for fear that someone or something might take her from him or something like that."

    "And I think they just got here," Harold said slowly.
