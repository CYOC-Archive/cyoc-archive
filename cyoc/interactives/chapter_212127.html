    The battle was over, Darren had defeated the pair of undead wizards. While Fukushu should have been happy, he realized this was only the beginning of the conflict. As Darren now spoke with the ghost of Kellie and Mika, Fukushu excused himself. He had a feeling neither would be thrilled to see him.

    Entering Yurikos damaged home, the kitsune now studied the frantic scene before him. Akira lay unconscious on the couch, his wife Ayami attending to his wounds. Sachiko stood nearby, the white kit sobbing as Raiden tried to comfort her. Fukushu could only sigh, Akira would recover but he wondered how his life would be moving forward. Beckoning Abolith over, the pair now entered a side room to talk. "Where is Yuriko?"

    "Ayami sent her into the kitchen to find medicine," Abolith stated. "Who did this?"

    "Morgan," Fukushu growled. "She attacked out of malice before summoning her mothers spirit. Where is her apprentice?"

    "Escaped in the confusion," Abolith sighed. "As I suspect Morgan has as well." Fukushu simply nodded, the kitsune sporting a grim expression. "I need to speak with Yuriko," he stated. "During our battle I saw something from the Catalyst. She need to be told." Abolith simply nodded, the elf assassin looking to leave before Fukushu grabbed her arm.

    "It would be wise if you kept out of sight beloved."

    "Why," she asked, casting a kitsune lover a curious look.

    "The Catalyst will be bringing the ghosts of Kellie and Mika here to talk," he stated. "He may have broken the spell Morgan cast but we both know neither will be happy to see you." Abolith nodded in agreement, if she was spotted it could prove problematic. "What will you do?"

    "I have a plan," he stated. "Just trust me. After I speak with Yuriko I must go. I will return shortly." The elf gave her lover a stiff nod, placing a gentle kiss on his cheek as they now parted. "Be careful."

    Heading towards the kitchen, Fukushu now beheld Yuriko. The elderly wizard was shaken, her face ashen as she tried to gather supplies. "Yuriko a moment." Yuriko paused, glancing up at the kitsune with red eyes. Fukushu could see she had been crying, the injuries to her long time guardian proving to be difficult. "Yes Fukushu what is it?"

    Fukushu now stood before his old foe, explaining what he had seen. Darren's magic was growing stronger, the young teen progressing faster than either of them would have anticipated. What concerned the kitsune though was Darren seeming to evolve. The boy pushing past his magical limits into something he had never before witnessed.

    "Your student is evolving Yuriko," he stated. "The power he wielded far exceeded any magic I have ever seen. The boy moved faster than thought, the power he generated was almost godlike. Have you ever heard of a Catalyst reaching these limits?"

    "No I can't say that I have," Yuriko replied softly. "At Katie's peak she was the epitome of magic. Stronger than perhaps any magical being, her strength borderline divine but never crossing into the arena of the gods. She was also much older when she accomplished those feats. To think that Darren has already reach them at such a young age..."

    "I know," Fukushu said softly, the two understanding that Darren was entering into previously uncharted territory. "Yuriko I need to depart," he stated. "The Catalyst has the ghosts of Mika and Kellie with him. Neither will be happy with my presence."

    "Where will you go?"

    "Morgan is still a threat," he grumbled. "As is her apprentice. There is something I must do before I return." Yuriko simply nodded, watching as the kitsune now exited her home.
