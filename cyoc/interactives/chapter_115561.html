She watched in shock as her feet pushed longer and longer, thinner and thinner, as her toes morphed into small, tighter digits, and her big toe seemed to shrink away into nothing. These were big clumsy hairless paws! She looked to her companions in shock, finding a surprised and worried Jamie, and Jennifer just barely stifling her laughter. Kim's expression turned to anger. It must be Jennifer's doing! Apples can't do all this, it seemed crazy!

    "VERY FUNNY!! Now quit it! I'm not exactly keen on keeping these, asshole!!"

    "Heheheheheh heh.. hah... I'm not doing anything to you! I even warned you those apples were enchanted! I've been trying to help you! And anyway, even if I wanted to curse you, which honestly something to shut your mouth would be lovely, but I couldn't curse you! My Magic doesn't work that way, cursing isn't exactly my thing. I've focused on sensing magic and scrying, I'm a wayfinder. That's why I'm working with you and Jamie."

    Kim huffed absentmindedly itching her newly changed feet, "if you're so helpful, then change me back!"

    "I told you, that's not what I do! I'd most likely make this enchantment a lot worse than it already is. You'll have to ride it out until we get to the next town. We'll find someone to remove the curse there. For now, take this as a lesson, maybe?"

    "Yeah? What's having a tail and paws gonna teach me?"

    "Well, for one, it may teach you to be less stupid and reckless about what you put in that mouth of yours."

    Kim rolled her eyes and scoffed, giving up on the argument for now.... damn her foot itched. She scratched a bit more, looking down.. then stopped. Ah. That makes sense. Fur was growing all along her newly changed set of paws, a shade of tan to match her tail, though slightly darker around the toes. Then she felt a pressure in her nails, and she moved her foot so she could see... seems these things are going all the way. Her nails grew out, long and curved, quickly becoming predator's claws. She frowned, and flexed her toes... seems she'd have to live with this a few more days then. She watched as pads grew in along the bottoms of her new paws, and sighed, moving to try and stand.

    "Anyone feel like helping this way at least then? Or still busy getting your kicks?" Jamie was the first to come give kim a hand up, shaking his head. "Let's 'ope ya do learn from this fer once then. Steady does it." Kim frowned, and found her balance easy enough. She caught herself using the tail to help. Seems there's no choice but to keep it out now. She grumbled, then got back in their cart. "Well lets go then! I don't want to stay like this any longer than I have to!" Her companions shook their heads and obliged. Maybe she wouldn't learn after all.

    They continued along their trail, but Kim was distracted as usual. Sure, her complaints for food had grown much more infrequent, but this was because she was too busy playing with her new additions. Wagging her tail, flexing her claws... it was all strange and new. Hell, she's never had fur before! But then as she explored, she felt a now familiar pressure...
