    As you checked out the map, something strange had got your attention as you were reading it. The map of the zoo listed a human exhibit, so apparently they even kept humans as zoo animals here.

    Weirded out, but curious nonetheless, you wanted to check out what this human enclosure was. Follow the directions of the map, you soon found yourself at the entrance of this enclosure as a zookeeper was waiting by the entrance.

    Walking over to the zookeeper, he quickly took notice of you and responded to your approach.

    "Welcome to the human enclosure, how may I help you?" He gleefully announced

    "I'm more just curious about this human enclosure. Could you tell me a little about it?" You respond to the keeper.

    "Sure, I can. Well first off, the human enclosure as you can guess is home to humans in their natural habitat." Explains

    "In urban areas on their phones." You jokingly retort to the keeper.

    "No. In fact, the humans in our zoo are in actually natural habitats. We have the African-back humans that live in Savannahs; the Asian-mountain humans that are found in the Himalayas and east Asia; the caucasian-skinned species that live in Europe and America and plenty more species to discover. Each type of human also lives in enclosures that are fitting to where they came from, does that answer your questions?" The keeper finishes his speech.

    Hearing the zookeeper, his words confuse you even more but they peak your intrigue. "Yeah, thank you for answering my questions. Now may I check the enclosure out?"

    "Sure, but just a few things before I let you in. 1. It's reccomend you dont bring any food with you, the humans can get a little feisty. 2. Don't attempt to pet/play any of the humans, they can see that as a sign of being interested sexually. 3. Don't hurt any of the humans even if they get close to you. 4. Don't let any of the human succeed in ripping your clothes off, they like to do that to people so be careful. If anyone of these rules are broken, you will receive an appropriate punishment." The zookeeper finishes his speech

    "O-ok. Thank you." You say as you enter the enclosure, curious to see what it's all about.
