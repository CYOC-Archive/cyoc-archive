    As the water war began to power down, Kirk continued to follow the wrestling match between Saba and Paula. Saba seemed to have a lot of experience, as if she'd been trained in various forms of non-lethal combat and had some experience at wrestling. Kirk guessed she was just a natural with her sisters to practice on. Paula, as Saba had said earlier, was a quick learner and had figured out a way to use her smaller size to her advantage.

    Without a pad of paper, though, Kirk found it difficult to actually keep score. He mostly found himself making sure that the two didn't hurt themselves and that neither violated the rules of the match... as that was the best they could do. Neither of them deliberately anything against the rules, but Paula did have some difficulty with her claws, as wolf claws were designed purely for traction while running and thus weren't retractable. But there was no real problem.

    As Whitney and Amethyst gave the ten minute warning, Saba finally managed to get a grip on Paula's shoulder and pushed until the topaz colored pup was on her back.

    "Pinned ya!" Saba cheered.

    "Saba wins the match," Kirk said nodding.

    Paula looked a little down, "sorry I'm so new with all of this."

    "You did good!" Saba encouraged, "a little practice and you'll be the best wrestler in the Pack... and we'll take Glendale to state!"

    "State?" Paula asked curiously.

    "The state wrestling tournament for high school wrestling teams," Kirk explained, "although the school board has tight restrictions against leomen and lupmen."

    "Why?" Saba asked.

    "Our size," Kirk sighed, "we're too big and strong to safely wrestle humans... or they're too small and fragile in comparison to us. I heard a story that before these restrictions were put in place, a leoman male accidentally broke an opponent's collar bone during a match."

    "We could still join the team, couldn't we?" Saba asked.

    "You could... but unless one of the other schools has leoman and or lupman wrestlers on their squads, you'd essentially be a mascot as you wouldn't be allowed to compete."

    Saba looked down at that.

    "You can always practice with me," Paula offered, "It was kinda fun, even if I'm no good at it."

    "You are good," Saba answered and hugged Paula.

    Kirk smiled at the bonds of friendship being forged.

    "Come on you two," Kirk spoke calmly, "let's get this mat picked up and go on in. I believe your nannies said it was dinner time."

    Saba and Paula nodded and quickly set to rolling up the mat, which was nothing more then a spare cloth the Pack kept for such outdoor events.
