    After saying her goodbyes to Ester and giving Lila a perfunctory nod; Amanda leaves the Carpenter house and heads home in her Tesla. Amanda’s car clearly faster and capable in handling tight turns and far more maneuverable; parking her car next to Benton’s red Lamborghini Veneno. Her Tesla; an electric powered sports car with a navy blue paint job, standing in stark contrast to Benton’s gas guzzling status symbol.

    Inside, Amanda was greeted by their new butler; a were-tiger named George, with a British accent. Informing him that Frieda will soon be home along with her friends; Eric and Spencer. Amanda heads to Benton’s study; opening the door to see the room empty; Amanda was surprised to see the room empty for a change. Closing the door, Amanda heads to the kitchen to see Ethel preparing dinner. She and her husband James’s their chauffeur were the only original staff members left. Benton’s rage in knocking out their old butler Jeffrey prompted the entire staff to quit in protest. Amanda was only able to convince Ethel and James to return.
    “Ethel,” Amanda grabbing an orange from the table; “where is Benton?”
    “From what the maids said earlier,” Ethel stirring something on the stove top; “he is passed out on the sofa in the living room.”
    “Okay,” Amanda responding as she peels her orange; “Frieda will be here as well as her friends. George already knows.”
    “I’ll have some snacks laid out for them,” Ethel commenting as Amanda leaves; seeing no one there the cook goes back to preparing dinner.

    Entering the living room, Amanda sighs as the large wall mounted flat screen is tuned to a news channel displaying the latest stock market results in a scrolling marque at the bottom of the screen. Turning the tv off; Amanda see her husband in his were-panther fursona sprawled out on the coach sleeping soundly. Benton’s clothes strewn about the place; Amanda assumed that her husband gave strict orders not to be disturbed.
    “Those were-cheetah maids,” Amanda shaking her head; “I’ll have to talk to those two later.”

Purring as she enjoys the shifting into her furry self; Amanda quickly climbs on top of her husband. Squeezing the orange in her paw, letting the juices drip on Benton’s muzzle; she soon licks Benton, causing him to wake up.

    “It was fine,” Amanda sighing; “I didn’t know anyone there. All the people present were Lila’s friends and former co-workers.”
    “You didn’t try to pick a fight now did you,” Benton looking at his wife as she eats and orange wedge.
    “No,” Amanda looking at her husband; “I kept to myself. Like I said, I didn’t know anyone there apart from Ester and Lila,” Amanda sighing; “Frieda will be home in a few minutes.”
    “So no time for a bit of fun then,” Benton looking at Amanda longingly.
    “Maybe later tonight,” Amanda licking her husband’s muzzle; “right now we got to get ready for diner.”
    “That’s not for a few hours,” Benton grinning; wrapping his paws around Amanda; “lets both you and I snuggle upstairs for an hour of two.”
    “Well your certainly in the mood,” Amanda looking bemused; “I hope you had a long enough of a nap, because Eric and Spencer are pulling up right about now with Frieda.”

With knowledge that Jack Crawford’s wife will also be running for mayor and that Arnold Steward Stevenson is her campaign manager; a were-donkey that Benton also loathes, Lance sighs if his older brother would be interested in getting involved.
