    Scrolling quickly through page after page in the incredibly long list of packages Jordan skipped over most of
    them, barely skimming, until he caught a glimpse of one called simply "Scanform". His curiosity piqued in a
    way that the other packages hadn't managed, he double clicked the link and slowly read the description and
    then smiled. The description read as follows;

    Scanform is my latest attempt at providing a package capable of translating my favorite images into forms for
    the Transfone to endow others with. The package's programming will allow a user to choose a focus from a
    scanned image and then dynamically isolate that portion and create a customized form based on the image. Using
    the newly released QIT (quantum information tunneling) features in the latest package developers kit from the
    company it even allows the realization of traits such as personality, knowledges, skills, or talents that are
    not clear from the image by itself. In addition, this package allows the user to make the standard adjustments
    to the image, including changing gender or age, or setting memory priority. Hopefully this package will serve
    you as well as it has served me in making my fantasies come to life. Enjoy,

    ScanuriJack

    Jordan smiled as he came to the end of the short description and thought a moment. "Cool." He said after that
    moments thought, "This package can do pretty much anything then." He clicked the download button and waited as
    the package downloaded onto his desktop, in the meantime clicking through his collection of images relating to
    his special interest before selecting one and deciding to test out the phone and package combo on himself.
