    Outside meeting

    Brody swore it could've taken hours to get a grip and regain his lucidity. There was no denying that becoming a Doberman did shed some confusion to him, but he had to act now before someone would find him and accidentally send him to the pound.
    Getting up, he got low and crossed the barrier between him and the School. How was he going to leave seemed a gamble, but he still pressed his weight on the door knob.
    Luckily, it wasn't that hard, especially considering that some schools had specially-designed doors for such occurrences.
    Unlocked, the bathroom door wide opened, and the alleyway was devoid of any presence. He ran, not hesitating to jump out of one of the opened ground windows.
    The reception in the flowers could've been better, but now he was outside, thanks to his sleek athletic body.
    For one second, he wished he kept this form for much longer. But he shook off his furred head : he was human in the first place, and intended to return to this state.
    Settled on this matter, he trotted in direction of the Library, looking for Maggie.

    It only took a few miles to get here. The proximity with the School grounds had always been a selling point for recruiters, seeking for new students to join in.
    Maggie was outside, seemingly taking a break from searching. She spotted the Doberman, and said nothing, thinking it was a stray.
    She changed her mind when Brody sat next to her and waited with a confident doggish look. There was no way a stray dog would do that.
    " Do I know you ? " she asked, unsure.
    He nodded vigorously, before pulling a paw on her knee. She then realised what that meant, wide-openly fixing the mutt.
    " No way... Brody ?! "
    He barked happily for an answer.
    She raised her hand in a waiting stand, while pocketting out the phone by the other. She dialled a button, and after a quick flash, something in Brody's throat went sour.
    He tried to bark, but it sounded more like a grunt.
    Wait... A grunt ? A human grunt ?
    " Hold on... " he tried to say, before looking shocked. " Oh ! Oh snap, I can talk ! "
    " It seems to work... " notted Maggie.
    The Doberman stood on all fours, jumping of joy. He quickly calmed himself though.
    " Having a voice sure is nice, but maybe there's something you can do about that stupid artificial curse ? "
    Maggie looked stern, intensely staring at the Doberman.
    " Well " she did answer, " about that... "
