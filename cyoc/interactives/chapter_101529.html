    The young girl was sitting in the middle of the steps, blocking the door. Kim and Mary Lou were standing in front of her, waiting for her to move. They could not get by unless she stood up.

    "Excuse me, young lady, but I am afraid you have to move. We need to get by, " said Kim sweetly.

    The young girl looked up, trying to locate the source of the strange voice. She was shocked by the face of the odd creature that stood before her. A pair of thick sunglasses hung on a short snout. A silly hat was covering its head, but she could still see that it had green scaly skin. Her eyes went wide with shock.

    Kim saw her surprise. "It's just a costume. Now, can you move?" said Kim.

    "Sure, kid. Knock yourself out, " said Kim. She lifted her tail and dropped it down in the girl's hands. The girl squealed in glee and began to run her hands along it.

    "Wow, you are even better than the Ninja Turtles, " said the girl. "I bet you could beat them up."

    Mary Lou scoffed. "A turtle is no match for a powerful gator, " said Mary Lou. She disrobed and flexed, producing a small bulging bicep muscle.

    "Do you think I could become one, too? Please?" asked the young girl.

    "Maybe one day, child. Maybe one day, " said Mary Lou, as she ruffled the kid's hair and walked past her up the stairs. "C'mon, girls. We still have work to do."

    Kim gave the girl a wink and a smile, then followed Mary Lou into the apartment complex. Evelyn hung behind, and leaned down to talk to the girl. She motioned for the girls to go ahead without her.

    "What's your name?" asked Evelyn.

    "Amy," replied the girl proudly.
