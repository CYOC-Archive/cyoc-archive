    Henry Masters, principal of the town middle school, once again found his thoughts turning to his four teachers who were no older than most of his students. He could only wonder what sort of achievements they'd reach in their lives ahead of them, being intelligent enough to learn and graduate collage and become licensed teachers by thirteen years old, there was simply no telling what they could achieve.

    "Though I do worry," he said to himself, "Are they getting the EMOTIONAL and SOCIAL lessons they need? At least they have each other."

    "They sound like fun Henry!" Said a ghostly white fox that slipped in THROUGH his closed window.

    "Hello Sachiko," the principal said calmly. "Just don't... play rough with him."

    "Oh Henry, you know I wouldn't hurt them."

    "Just remember they can't teach classes as statues, or as forest animals, or dolls, or-"

    "I promise I won't, cross my heart, hope to die, stick a needle in my eye," said the ghostly fox as she sat on his desk. That did give some relief to Henry, in all the decades he'd known Sachiko, she'd NEVER broken her word, not once.(Though she had lied about eating all the Christmas cookies one year.) "Besides, Granny Yuriko doesn't let me take stuff from the store unless I say what I'm gonna do with it."

    Henry remembered Sachiko's tales of her father's family. After the first two tenko, or 'heavenly foxes' had come into existence by a pair of kitsune surviving to be a thousand years old, many other magical creatures had feared what would happen if more survived to that age... So the more tails a kitsune had, the bigger the bounty that was offered under the table. Only due to a tension lowering (in no small part to the next generations having been purposely raised as friends), did Kitsune only feel the need to hide their kits in pocket universes.

    One elderly vixen had become a third tenko, before she'd have been killed before even getting close to 1000. She apparently owned a public art gallery composed of transformed humans... who had begged her to 'restore them to their true selves' after being made flesh and blood again by a band of wizards that were close friends with Sachiko's family. The wizards awkwardly apologizing as the works of art eagerly resumed their eternal stations. Apparently some people didn't mind being admired their beauty by crowds for eternity.

    Sachiko as a one tail naturally did NOT have that kind of power, her shape shifting and pallor tricks were the limits of her REAL magic. Anything else she did were from trinkets borrowed from her godmother's shop in the mall. Henry remembered from his childhood, Sachiko had used one such trinket on Halloween to turnea gang of bullies tormenting Henry into the goblins they were wearing masks of... They'd made very lucrative lives as bouncers in the goblin realm at a night club, and their grandchildren now not only owned the club, but had introduced electronics to the goblin realm, which the mad tinkerers had eagerly assimilated into their own culture.

    "So why the visit Sachiko?"

    "Do I need a reason to visit a friend?"

    "No, but you've always loved to give news in person."

    "Mom and dad say now that I'm eighty years old, I should spend a year in second grade among humans!"

    Few people had ever put together that the previous Sachikos ten years apart from each other had been the same girl, most looked the other way and told themselves it was a generational thing. Those who did notice were sure she was an android, or maybe some rich person putting their brain in a new body. Sachiko found the rumors about her funny.

    "I hope you make new friends Sachiko."

    "No matter how many I make, you'll still be my friend Henry."

    "Thank you Sachiko." He never admitted how seeing Sachiko barely age had made him feel old, but the woman he'd fallen in love with, his children and grandchildren, he wouldn't trade for the world.

"He's like my dimwit little brother that I'll stick by come Hell or highwater!"

The poison would get spread by someone in your social circle, and once you were hooked, you'd seek out the poison peddler themselves! Why? Because THIS happens when you try to act like a drug dealer from a PSA!" She kicked him between the legs.
