    His Mermaid Girlfriend: Potential Arrangements

    Previous chapter

    added by Stark 24 days ago O Mythological Magic Aquatic

    Gerald Cooper was a well-to-do merman of Atlantis whose means were not inconsiderable. In the auld country, which in his case had been the Irish Sea, his family's name had been Mac Cúipéir, and their trade had been a simple craft, the construction of waterproof casks. Once the famed sunken city of the ancients had been discovered by the merfolk, though, and then reoccupied as a city-under-the-sea in its own right—this happened sometime all the way back in the 1780s—many merfolk changed their names and their trades when they immigrated. It was hardly a new story even then, or a unique one. The ancestors of Gerald Cooper took up other businesses and did well enough for themselves that Gerald himself was now something of a force to be reckoned with in the world of undersea finances and stocks.

    Gerald's mate of many years (for of course, even though the merfolk had no formal marriage traditions, they did still regularly practice long-term monogamous pair-bonding of a very similar sort), Clara Cooper, was a known gossip and busybody. She had been held as exceptionally vain, even for a mermaid, in her younger days; and now that she was deep into middle age, her chief concern in life was getting her own way wherever matters involving her family were concerned.

    When the family nautilus drifted to a bobbing halt in the garage/hangar appended to the side of their larger-than-average home, Gerald and Clara Cooper were ecstatic. Their daughter, gone these five years off on an important mission up to the surface world, honored as one of the scientists tasked with saving their race, was finally coming home for a visit! As soon as they had heard that, Erin's mother Clara had gotten right to work. It was time to arrange a surprise for their daughter—the best gift they could think to give her.

    * * * * * *

    Maeve let go of the control-yoke and faced Erin. "Sis… there's something I need to tell you before we go in there."

    Erin didn't like her big sister's worried tone. "What is it?"

    "Well, I don't know how to tell you this," said Maeve. "And I would have brought it up right away, if John hadn't been here—"

    "Me?" asked John. "Is… is my being here going to be some kind of problem?"

    "No, of course not!" said Erin. Then she turned to her sister for confirmation. "It won't, right?"

    "Well, you see, the thing is," started Maeve, "I mean, that is to say, Mother and Father thought it would be a good idea—I mean, good for you—"

    "Oh, for Poseidon's sake, just spit it out!" said Oisin. "Erin, your parents sent for Cullen MacLlyr to come visit today. He's probably already inside the house right now, waiting to meet you."

    "Cullen MacLlyr, who's that?" asked John.

    Erin racked her brains, trying to remember. "Um… some socialite, I think? No, wait—he's a politician's son. The son of…" Erin's eyes went wide. "…the king! He's the fucking Prince of Atlantis!"

    "Okay," said John, still confused, "so your parents want you to meet this prince? That sounds kinda cool—"

    Erin replied, "Except, Mother wouldn't dare unless she…" Erin's cheeks turned aqua-green with rage. "Ooh, she wouldn't dare, not unless she were trying to set us up—as arranged mates!"

    John's eyes practically bugged out of their sockets. "Can she… do that? I mean, is that how your, uh, customs and traditions work?"

    "Only for the super-rich (like the Prince) and social climbers (like our parents)," said Maeve. "But, yes, our Mother has every right to insist that Erin meets Prince Cullen. Beyond that, it's up to them. And I wouldn't worry—after all, we've all seen you without those covering-cloths you wrap around yourself."

    John blushed and said nothing.

    Erin, meanwhile, took John's hand into hers and said, "She's right. I've already chosen you, because I love you. I won't take another mate, not on your life."

    "Are merpeople allowed to have more than one?" asked John quietly.

    "It's not common, but yes, absolutely!" said Oisin helpfully.

    "You're not helping," hissed Maeve.

    "I'm just explaining our customs and traditions to the foreigner!" said Oisin to his mate. "He deserves to know these things. At the very least, it will explain why other mermaids—"

    "Quiet!" said Maeve and Erin together, shutting him up.

    John remained silent. What in the world was that all about?

    "Well," said Erin at last, rising up from her seat on the bridge, "we've been holed up in here long enough. Time to go face the music. John, you ready to meet my mom and dad?"

    "As I'll ever be," said the human with a rueful shake of his head. For his part, he was wondering just when exactly it was that his life had become a soap opera.
