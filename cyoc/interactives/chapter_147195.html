    Remorseful Reinvigoration

    Panting heavily, Kairos falls to his hands, sweat trickling down his brow. The sunlight flooding into the forest clearing completely blinds the blue eyed human, causing his eyes to close. Taking in deep, slow breaths, relief washes over his face at the refreshing scent of morning dew, the thick smell of blood having long faded.

    Opening his eyes, a slow smile spreads across his face. His hands were not clawed, furred, or scaled, but human. With regained strength, he pushes himself back up to his knees before rising to his feet.

    Sighing, he looks down at his fully clothed body in a mix of puzzlement and relief. His red tunic, silver chainmail shirt, and white undershirt beneath clung to his form, rendered wet by the moist grass. Bringing his hands up to his face, a slight chuckle slips from his mouth, wondering how he had not taken notice of his gauntlets.

    It shouldn't have made any sense. It didn't make any sense, but at least he wasn't stuck in some cursed forest without any gear. It saved him the embarrassment from having to walk around naked too.

    Kairos glances downwards as his booted foot bumps against something. Crouching in curiosity, his eyes come upon two unusual objects; a pair of masks.

    One mask was of a wolf, it's golden fur soft and pristine. The other mask was of a snake, it's blue scales smooth and cool to the touch. The empty eye sockets on each mask beckoned for them to be worn.

    Shaking his head and rising back to his feet with both masks in each hand, Kairos stares in thought at the pair of items. The fur on the wolf mask gave off an uncanny warmth, while the scales on the snake mask felt wet against his fingers, leaving a slight residue on his skin. They were inanimate for sure, yet it felt as if they could actually have been apart of a face at one point.

    As tempted as he was to wear one of them, he had a more important mission to do than putting on a false face. Fiddling with the belt around his waist and bringing one mask to each of his sides, Kairos clips both masks securely on each hip.

    Gazing around the clearing, the blonde haired warrior grins as his eyes fall upon his iron sword and shield. Oddly enough, they weren't scattered about, instead calmly leaning up against a tree, as if someone had placed them there.

    Not dwelling on the oddity, Kairos quietly walks over to his weapons, lifting them up. No blood stained the shield, in fact, it's iron surface looked as if it had been recently polished. Strapping his sword on the baldric at his back, the red clothed human clips the shield on his scabbard after a moment of fidgeting.

    Upon walking throughout the seemingly endless forest, the lost human enters another open part of the forest. Examining the grove, Kairos’ brow furrows in confusion. The geography and layout of this grove had an uncanny similarity to the grove he'd awakened in.

    Upon glancing at the ground, his eyes widen in silent horror, a tinge of remorse present.

    Kenos’ reptilian body lay limp, the runes on his bare scales having faded away, leaving scars. A puddle of dried blood had formed around him. Despite the blood loss, he had contained enough will to rise up and fire another spell during their battle.

    Crouching besides the lizardman's malnourished body, Kairos glides his hand over his face, silently closing the reptile's dull open eyes. Gazing around the open space, the human's eyes fall to the large robe Kenos had worn. Rising and walking over to the torn clothing, he picks the cloak up, shaking it free of any dirt before trudging back to Kenos’ corpse.

    Wrapping the reptile's white scaled body in the warm clothing, Kairos stands over Kenos. Closing his eyes as they begin to water, remorse fills the warrior, his hand clenching into a fist.

    The mission… He had completed it, but there was no gain from it. No joy could ever be derived from such a hollow victory.

    Orveus’ death had left a hole in his heart. Even if they had only known each other for a few months, their bond was as strong as steel. Kenos truly was nothing more than a child, no doubt having suffered abuse by a parental figure and forcibly being initiated into a clan.

    “F-Father I-”

    His blue eyes open, gazing solemnly at the dead and robed form of Kenos.

    Had he craved death?

    Kairos turns away from the lizard's body, unable to bear the sight any longer. Yet his gaze ends up falling to the tome Kenos had wielded, the eye symbol on its cover faded. Picking it up, his eyes fill with sorrow as he flips through it's pages out of curiosity.

    Crude drawings and runes litter the pages, drawn and written in a dull brownish ink. His face scrunches up as a familiar scent fills his nostrils. Desperately flipping through more pages, he slams the book shut.

    The entire tome had been written in it's wielder's blood.

    Holding the book close to his body, the human sighs as he safely tucks it inside one of the satchels at his belt. He couldn't leave it here, lest some other mage come and use it for intentionally malicious purposes.

    He couldn't allow it.

    Wiping a stray tear from his cheek, the human whispers a prayer, before standing in complete silence. Steeling his will, Kairos looks up to the sky, his blue eyes burning with resolve.

    Nothing was going to be accomplished if he stayed here mourning. Orveus wouldn't want that. Composing himself, the human begins to walk in the direction of the rising sun.

    As Kairos’ form vanished into the woods, watchful eyes peered from within the shadows of the trees. One pair of eyes was red and bloodshot, it's gaze falling to the cloaked form on the ground, igniting in rage.
