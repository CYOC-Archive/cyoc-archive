    The captain of the fortress troopers sneered as he surveyed the motley band of women warriors at the gates. “I wonder if they stitched their clothes themselves out of burlap sacks.” he snorted, looking with disdain at their ill-fitting armor in an assortment of colors and shapes.

    “Sir! They’re requesting permission to cross the bridge.” a sergeant reported.

    “Cross the bridge? The path to the bridge passes thru my bedchambers.” the captain leered. “Muster the men! Light assault gear!”

    “But Sir, don’t we need to inform Lady Atelia before deploying any troops…”

    “Don’t be ridiculous! Look at that – it’s just a bunch of women decked out like a circus troupe! Are you trying to make a fool of me in front of the Lady?”

    Moments later, the fortress gates swung open and the captain led his troopers out. Waving his broadsword, he attacked the women commander, and just as he expected, she got tired after exchanging a few blows, and fell back with the other women in a hasty retreat. “Charge!” the captain yelled. “Don’t let a single one get away! Any woman you catch will keep you company in bed tonight!”

    The troopers vigorously pursued the women warriors until they were a considerable distance from the fortress. As they rounded a bend, a signal flare went off. Abruptly, the ground beneath them burst into flames. Screaming in pain, the troopers tried to retreat, but found their return path blocked by a band of acolytes led by an enormous ogre. Just then, another group of acolytes helmed by an improbably muscular barbarian ploughed into their left flank, and then the warriors they had been pursuing wheeled around and attacked them from the front.

    Scorched by the flames and assaulted on three sides, the troopers fled in disorder towards the remaining direction straight to the river. Upon reaching the shore, they plunged into the cool waters and bobbled around gratefully, relieved to soothe their parched skin and extinguish any articles of leather clothing that had caught on fire.

    Another signal flare went off, and Mika released the magical dam that he had conjured upstream. The river uncoiled like a mighty serpent, and a roaring wall of water slammed into the hapless troopers, sweeping them away like leafs in a whirlwind.
