Deciding that you've put more than enough distance between yourself and that nightmarish house, you wander over to a red bench, sitting down and reclining on it, trying to relax for a few minutes (after what you've been through, you'd say you're entitled to at least a little rest!). You calmly tilt your head back, looking up into the open sky and gazing at the stars, and can feel yourself beginning to calm down... until a green, broad-faced, black eyed monster moves its head above you!

When you note how rubbery they feel, you almost groan - haven't you had enough of this rubber stuff tonight? - but you're far too afraid to do much of anything but screaming. The monstrosity soon moves its fingers to your throat, tilting your head upwards and holding you there. You're completely at its mercy.

    It opens its mouth, and you expect its tongue to dart out into your face. Instead, the slimy red tongue slowly snakes its way between the frog's "lips", slithering through the air like a snake approaching its prey. As it inches closer to your face, you try harder to pull away, but the more you struggle, the more tightly it holds you down. You close them tightly, but as always your efforts do no good - he presses until he finally pushes between your lips, meeting your clenched teeth. At the very least, he won't be able to get through those. At first, it looks like you're right, as he tries to push through the solid teeth and simply fails to do so.

A few squeeze through your teeth, and a few fall from your mouth, little red tadpoles wriggling down your chin before falling to the ground. Just the thought of some of them getting into your mouth is enough to make you gag... and in doing so, you open the floodgates. The entire tongue, at least two feet long, collapses in an instant into a mass of writhing red tadpoles, and the ones that manage to land in your mouth flow straight down your throat as smoothly as a glass of water. You eventually choke on some of them, and stop yourself from swallowing half of them, but you're still left with a belly full of tadpole... things. Its work apparently done, the frog lets you go before making a gigantic leap into the nearby trees, quickly escaping from view.

    Looking around, you find yourself quite alone, aside from the tadpoles that remain on the ground, dying and shriveling into nothing as they're exposed to the cold night air. Soon, all that's left is you and your full, jiggling gut.
