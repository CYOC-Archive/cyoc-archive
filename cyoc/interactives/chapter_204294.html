There were sliders and buttons she couldn't begin to understand, except for one labeled "Repeat Previous Settings." She clicked it and the image morphed into a copy of Dan's current giant appearance. "Oh! I get it." Catherine nodded. "This is CGI movie software. Cool, I guess. I don't even watch hero movies." She absentmindedly clicked CHANGE and ignored the warnings on the screen.

She idly browsed through her QuickPost blog where she posts recipes and food photos. Just the same 15 mutuals liked her latest post, as usual. She reminded herself to find some fad that will get some easy clicks. While she was in the zone, she ignored the sensation of her basic tomboy outfit growing tighter and tighter around her, her skin turning green underneath her clothing, and her hair falling out one strand at a time. She finally noticed when her sickly-green thumb grew too big for the phone keyboard. "What the hell is this...?" She stood up and looked down. She was a foot taller and her baggy clothes were now a size too small on her!

    Catherine rushed downstairs in a panic. "Mom, Dad! What happened to me?!" The folks were in the garage, taking some boxes of garbage out for pickup. "Look! I'm huge!" she yelled, getting their attention.
    "What seems to be the trouble, dear?" her dad replied.
    "Well, just... look!" She showed her clothes, which were slowly ripping apart at the seams as she stood there.
    Mom looked up and down Catherine's form with little surprise. "You look healthier than ever, Catherine."
    Catherine just groaned in pain as her now clawed feet tore through her socks and sneakers. "Don't you... see what's happening?" she replied.
    Dad shook his head. "Just that you need new clothes. If there even ARE clothes in your size."

A minute later, her fangs and cyclops eye grew in, blinding her long enough to make her trip on a car and shatter its frame and windshield. As soon as she could see, she lifted herself up and kept moving, picking little bits of glass and metal off her skin. By the time she stopped growing, she could see Dan stomping around like he hadn't a care in the world.
