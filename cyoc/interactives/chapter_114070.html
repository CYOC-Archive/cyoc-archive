    Rebecca makes a new sister

    While Michael was busy playing with Legos, his now older sister Rebecca had been waiting to see the results of his change. The nine year old was something of a tomboy, and she always wished she wasn't the youngest sibling of the three. Now with the possibility her brother might change for a week, she had her chance. Minutes passed into an hour, and still no sign of her brother emerged from his room. Her parents and her older sister were busy doing other things, so Rebecca decided to check herself.

    Being as quiet as she could she tiptoed upstairs and peeked into Michael's room. What she saw there made her gasp. She saw a little boy that looked just like pictures of her brother when he was a tyke. His room, his clothes, everything were all changed. Rebecca couldn't believe it when she saw it, and gasped, but luckilly the little boy was too busy with his legos to notice her. She watched for what seemed an age. Eventually, Michael must have gotten bored or tired, because he yawned and tiptoed over to his bed to lay down. Within moments, he was asleep.

    Rachael remained at the door a moment. Then she opened the door and poked her head inside the room. Her brother lay on his bed, smiling as he slept. She crossed the room to look at him.

    "Wow, he looks like such an angel amazing. All because of some computer program..."

    A sudden curiosity filled her. If a computer could do this to him, what else could it do. She saw the machine still sitting on his desk, though the computer was a bit different, more fit for a six year old. Sitting down, she clicked the screen and sure enough several windows opened up showing his current status.

    SCAN RESULTS:
    Name: Michael Drake
    DOB: March 25, 2010
    Age: 6
    Ethnicity: Caucasian
    Height: 3’ 8”
    Weight: 47 lbs
    Sexual Preference: N/A
    Hair color: black
    Eye color: blue
    Occupation: Student; 1st grade

    An option of the awareness of the world was clicked on, along with several more that seemed to change the way everything else interacted. Rebecca couldn't believe such a machine could change the very world around her. If he'd wanted to, he could have made her forget everything.

    Rebecca wasn't a malicious girl, but she was only 9. Her curiosity got the better of her as a sudden inspiration came to mind. Clicking the screen, she began to make some changes.
