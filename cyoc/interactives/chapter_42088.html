    She saw several of her former classmates around; tinier than even the Junior High girls, they were the center of attention. Too bad they only had eyes for each other; Jen couldn't find a high school girl who wasn't holding hands with another girl!

    Having removed her makeup, and tied her hair back, no one noticed her, even as she longed to confess her love to another girl...argh!

    A really cool car pulled up at the gas station next door, and girls flocked to it. The licence plate said KYL RULZ.

    Hoo boy. He still had Fiona with him; to Jen's horror, she realized that Kyle had put the former librarian into a child's safety seat!

    Fiona didn't seem to mind, but Jen felt angry that any girl would be treated that way. Which brought on a fantasy of her saving Fiona from Kyle, and being rewarded with a night of passion...

    Plan, plan, plan.

    Ok, Kyle didn't seem worried, had he used the curse on Fiona?

    She sighed and finished her lemonade. This sucked.

    That's when she realized someone was talking to her.

    "Um, hi, mind if I join you? The other seats are taken."

    Jen looked and found herself staring at an angel. Long, golden blonde hair, china blue eyes, and skin the color of cream. The girl's voice was pleasing to hear, with an almost musical quality about it. It took her a few moments to realize she was looking at a Junior High girl!

    "Um, sure, please!"

    The other girl curtsied....actually curtsied (!) before sitting down. "Thank you. I don't think I've seen you at school, I'm Paige, Paige Bishop."

    "Uh..I'm Jenni...Jen. Hi."

    Paige smiled and it was like watching the sun rise. "Jen? I like it, it suits you. I don't have many friends...I just moved here recently, and the other girls don't seem to like me much...oh what am I saying, I'm sorry."

    Jen blinked. "No no, it's fine. I'd love to be your friend." Gah. Paige couldn't be more than a 7th grader, and here Jen was getting...urges. She was falling hard for the other girl!

    Paige's smile somehow brightened, if that was possible, and she grabbed Jen's hand. "Oh wonderful, thank you so much. I miss all my old friends at home..I mean..back in Oregon."

    Oregon? Jen had never met anyone from Oregon. She knew where it was, but...come to think of it, she'd never heard of anything happening in Oregon.

    "So why did you move to Glendale?"

    Paige sighed. "My dad got a job at OmniTech, and this was the closest "decent community" he could find for us to move to. He doesn't care for the big city, which is a shame, I miss stores being open 24 hours, and always having something to do. I mean, no offense, your town is lovely but.."

    Jen laughed. "It's a bit boring, trust me, I've been here all my life."

    Both girls shared a laugh. "But Jen, um, is there something wrong with all the older girls? They're all so...well, tiny."

    Jen shrugged. "Got me, nobody else seems to notice."

    Paige frowned. "Yes, well, about that. I happen to know that girl there, she's a mean one, Clarissa Nolan. Now that's her, over by that car, and the girl she's towering over, isn't that her older sister, Brooke?"

    Jen grinned, seeing Brooke, once the school's most promising female athlete (and a personal rival of hers) reduced to a 4' 6" shadow of her former self, being towered over by her "little" sister.

    "Yep, that's her alright."

    "Well Jen, um, the problem there is, I was told Brooke was a state champion girl's basketball player. But look at her, there's just no way she could play at that level!"

    It had to be because she was from out of town, another limit to Kyle's power...or maybe it's because Paige seemed to be very smart, if not brilliant.

    "I can explain, Paige, but I don't know if you'd believe me."

    Paige smiled. "You're my friend, I'll believe anything you say."

    "Ok, but first, can we go to your house?"

    Paige took her hand and Jen felt like she was floating on air the whole way to the other girl's house.
