    As Gloria and Lisa made their way back to Gloria's house, Lynne was practically dead silent. She hadn't said anything after school because she had been so mad at everyone for her awful day. Just as they made their way to the front door, they stopped when they heard their mom talking to herself from outside the door.

    "I've got to say that you still look fabulous, even with all that baby weight." Lisa said to herself.

    "Thanks, first time you know, I can't imagine how Kellie ever managed to make it through the whole thing carrying Katie like that." Lisa seemed to say to herself.

    Gloria and Lynne quietly came in and saw that Lisa was indeed talking to herself, at least she was talking to her future self.

    "Mom?!" Lynne asked in surprise.

    "Yes?" Both the young Lisa and the older Lisa both asked making the other giggle.

    "I....how....why are you here now?" Lynne asked her future mom.

    "Well....it's actually because of Gloria." Future Lisa answered.

    "Huh?" Gloria asked not knowing what she meant.

    "Yes. You see, Gloria wrote down in her diary today about all of what you did today and she thought it would be good if I traveled back to today to talk to you about your day." Future Lisa explained.

    "How....how could you have known that? I only wrote it down a few hours ago?" Gloria asked her future mom.

    "You called me earlier....your future self did and said that today I should travel back in time to visit Lynne after her first day." Future Lisa explained.

    "Come on sweetie let's leave Lynne and me alone for a while." Present Lisa suggested as she slowly got up due to her pregnant body.

    Future Lisa then raised an eyebrow at her daughter and patted the couch to have her come and sit down, which she did.

    "Mom I swear I was just...." Lynne tried to explain herself but her mother held up her hand.

    "You were very rude in Mr. Harris's class today, you almost let slip that Ms. Dean and Mr. Harris are going to get married someday and I know that you turned the principal, excuse me, ex-principal into an air-headed cheerleader, and you insulted a good friend of your sister's. Lynne, tell me...why did you do all of this?" Future Lisa asked her teen daughter.

    "Because....I don't want to be here mom. I want to be back home in the future, where I actually have more than two friends, where my older sister isn't such a rule abiding dork. I just....I just want to go home." Lynne said sulking into her legs.

    "Sweetie, you've got to remember that you can't let people know about things from the future, it might cause the future to change so much that you don't recognize it. You can't let Mr. Harris and Ms. Dean know about their future relationship, you can't transform other people just for the fun of it at school, and you most certainly shouldn't insult a good girl like Sasha. Now promise me you'll behave more while you're here." Future Lisa asked her daughter.

    "I promise mom." Lynne said nodding her head.

    "Good, now I was actually talking before with my past self about how I should still punish you for what you did today." Future Lisa said.

    "Punish? But mom, I apologized." Lynne tried to argue.

    "Oh, I know you did, but that doesn't excuse today. Gloria, you can use the ring now." Future Lisa said as Lynne and Future Lisa both began to regress in age.

    Future Lisa's grey hair pretty much disappeared as her beautiful auburn hair became bright and beautiful again. Her outfit changed into something more appropriate for a teenager as she reached 17 years old. Lynne on the other hand went from her teenage years to being a 9 year-old girl wearing a 'My Little Pony' t-shirt.

    "Mommy this isn't fair!" Little Lynne shouted in her young voice.

    "Like what today wasn't fair either?" Future Lisa said as she raised her eyebrow at her daughter crossing her arms.

    "Anyway, your dad, my past self and Gloria are all going to go out to dinner tonight so I'm going to be the babysitter and watch you tonight." Future Lisa explained as she waved goodbye to the others.
