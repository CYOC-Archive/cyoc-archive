    Jeff looked through all the packages in the Chronivac and couldn’t believe all the choices that they have. Animal, Transgender, Age, Inanimate, Growth, Shrink… There were so many options that he doesn’t know where to begin. But as he looked through the list, he went to the very bottom and found an option at the lowest of the list. “Hmm… What’s this?” he asked as he got a closer look at the link. It says… “Delete Package? What is that?” Out of curiosity, he clicked on the link to see what it is. What he got is just a name box with a ‘delete’ button below it and a huge message over it. It reads…

    ‘Welcome to the Delete Package. This page is where you can delete the name of a person, whether it be from the past or present, alive or dead. The rules are simple. Just type the name of the person you want to delete in the name box and then press the delete button. Once clicked, there is a slow process of deleting the target from existence. From moving the person one part at a time, rearranging the world where the target doesn’t exist, to wiping the memories of the target out of the person you’re deleting. This would include the host as the memories would be removed, but you will still remember someone you deleted. It is perfect for any occasion, be that revenge, removing dead weight, or suicide. But before you can jump into it, there are a few warnings for you to follow.

    The first is that is removing the person out of existence, there is no way in recovering them. They are pretty much removed and will never return. Even if you made a mistake, you cannot undo your judgment of error and ultimately never see them again.

    Secondly, if you removed a part of the family member, like a father, mother, grandparent, aunt, or uncle, then half of the family line would be removed as well to cover up the existence of the target. For example, if you delete a mother or father and have three kids, then those three kids need to be deleted as well. Or if you choose a grandfather with five children and two grandchildren, they must be removed as well. And if you delete the first relative at the bottom of the family tree, then half that tree will be chopped in half. So if you wish to remove someone in the family line, be cautious in what you choose.

    Finally, whatever you do, do not remove any important historical figures or celebrities. You are allowed to do it, but doing so will alter any or all historic events are that past or present. Anyone from Washington to Lincoln, Ford to Tony Hawk, will be removed and any event, historical moment, or recent news, will be wiped from the history books. So do not risk anyone historic or important.

    Enjoy the Delete Package and hope you enjoy the Chronivac 4.0!

    Chronivac Corps. is not responsible for death by deleted person.’

    Jeff read the instructions and warning and finds it perplexing. A package that would delete someone out of existence and wipe any evidence of them? It is quite a strange one. Why would the Chronivac try to do something as extreme as that? Maybe it is for laughs for something. But regardless, it does look like an interesting section and he wonders if this package can actually delete someone. So thinking it, he decided to test this deletion feature out. But who to test it on is the question.
