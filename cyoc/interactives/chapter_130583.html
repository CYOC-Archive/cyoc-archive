    "So, in this world, you two were born as conjoined twins?" Derek asked.

    "That's right," Ryan replied. "They're two heads, two arms, two legs, and one wide body."

    "Hey, what about us?" Thatcher asked.

    "What?" Bradley asked his older brother.

    "Do we exist here?" Thatcher asked.

    "Oh, yeah," Bradley answered. "All four of us exist here."

    "Then who do we have for brothers here?" Derek asked.

    "Well, us obviously," Ryan answered. "Derek, here you have an identical twin named Eli. You both have black hair, but here your hair is dyed purple in the front, and Eli's is dyed orange."

    "And Thatcher," Bradley told his brother, "you're one of three fraternal triplets here. Their names are Tyler and Dylan. You're on the body's left-hand side, Tyler the right, and Dylan is in the middle. You still have your black hair and hazel eyes, Dylan has brown hair and green eyes, and Tyler is dark blonde with blue eyes."

    The older bros were fascinated by the descriptions of their ConjoinVerse selves.

    "Come on! We can show you were we live here," Ryan said.

    After bidding farewell to the two-headed docs, the four brothers walked outside towards their ConjoinVerse house. Along the way, Ryan and Bradley explained most of what they learned about ConjoinVerse: that their families were conjoined since their mothers and fathers were both sets of twins here and in NormVerse, that they all lived in one house in the same place as in NormVerse, that all seats were wider here due to wider bodies, that they were getting stares right now because it was extremely rare to have just one head here, and the counterparts of their friends that they had meet so far--Leo-Donnie-Raph-Mikey, the nine-headed bros and their family, Dustin-Justin, and lone Ty.

    After turning the corner to walk down their street, Ryan and Bradley were greeted by seven familiar faces: Derek and Eli, Terry, Dylan and Thatcher, and their own counterparts of ConjoinVerse. After an exchange of greetings, ConjoinVerse Ryan and Bradley invited everyone back inside the house. Fortunately, their moms Helena and Lisa had gone shopping and their dads Sean and Shane had gone to the movies. It would be a while before either pair was back, which would give the brothers and cousins time to connect with their other selves (pun intended).

    "You told your brothers, too?" Ryan asked.

    "Yeah, they couldn't stop thinking about when they saw you two at lunch," said ConjoinVerse Ryan.

    "So we decided to show them the docs' portal," ConjoinVerse Bradley added.

    "But clearly they didn't have to," ConjoinVerse Derek added.

    "It's not every day that someone meets himself from another universe," ConjoinVerse Thatcher agreed.

    "At least you guys exist there," said Eli.

    "That is true," Dylan agreed.

    "It's nobody's fault, guys," Tyler told his brothers.

    After talking for a while, the four Normies and the seven Conjoiners wondered what to do now.
