    “Mom, Dad,” Frieda addressed her parents like she was addressing the king and queen of Denmark. She even bowed a tiny bit.

    “Sit down Frieda, we need to talk to you about something.” Benton Knight said. He towered over his daughter at 6’4’’. In his fursona, he was 11’5’’, almost double his normal height. Frieda was glad her father remained in his normal form. On occasion, he’d transform for dramatic effect.

    “I got the strangest phone call today. You’ll never guess who it was.” Benton continued. Frieda gulped, and tried to cut the tension by guessing it was the President of Greece. Her father didn’t appreciate the sarcasm one bit.

    “No, it was Douglas Cooper. Asking if you were home because Jack Johnson, the prosecutor for the Stovall case, wanted to speak with you and some boy named Eric. Now, I calmly asked him what the bloody hell he was talking about. To which he said,

    ‘Frieda was involved in an altercation with Julie Stovall the other day, along with a fellow student Eric Anderson. She will need to be called upon to testify for the prosecution, as she also witnessed an attempted Statue 1A attack.’ Would you care to explain yourself young lady?”

    Unable to come up with a plausible lie, or an explanation that wouldn’t result in her being sent off to another country, Frieda kept her mouth shut. Benton’s frown deepened, and he demanded Frieda tell him what she had gotten herself mixed up in.

    “I don’t know who this Eric Anderson is, but you’re sure as hell not going to see him again. Or Julie for that matter.” Benton huffed.

    “You can’t do that!” Frieda exclaimed, leaping from her bed.

    “Sit, down, NOW!” Benton said. Frieda did so, only out of fear. Her father wasn’t a violent or abusive man, but he did have quite a temper. And she also knew her mother wouldn’t stop him from striking Frieda if it came to that.

    “This is NOT how we raised you Frieda. You have the entire Knight name to uphold. Your mother and I go away and expect you to behave yourself, and keep your nose clean. Now we return home to find you’re involved with a delinquent, and some random boy? What do you think this says to the other families in Moon Lake? Our daughter is…”

    “Julie started that fight! I was only trying to help Eric defend himself. Otherwise Julie would have…” Frieda stopped herself before she revealed that she’d been the one who changed Eric before Julie could. That would most certainly earn her a one way ticket to the U.K.

    “Julie would have what?” Amanda Knight spoke for the first time. Her voice was calmer than her husband’s, but her gaze held just as much fury.

    “Nevermind, it doesn’t matter.” Frieda muttered.

    “Yes it does. Until we get a straight answer out of you, you’re not to leave this house. I don’t care how much school you miss, we should have never let you go to public school to begin with. Unless you decide to tell us why you’re mixed up with these people, you’re grounded!” Benton said.

    As much as she wanted to protest, Frieda kept her mouth shut. Her parents stormed out of her room, followed by a loud click, which signaled them locking her bedroom door. At least they hadn’t taken away her computer or cell phone. But she’d have to be careful about using them. She knew it wasn’t too much to think her parents would be monitoring what she did online, or offline.

    But she had to warn Eric and Spencer. Her parents, more specifically her father, would stop at nothing to get as much dirt on the Andersons if he thought they were trouble. And based on that conversation, it was safe to say Benton Knight wasn’t a huge fan of Eric’s. He didn’t know the whole story, but that didn’t matter.
