She'd gotten pretty good at disassembling and reassembling various tools and pieces of equipment. She'd learned how to build computers as well when she was younger, and there was a bunch of computer equipment and loose wiring lying around. She swiped it all aside and turned on the small lamp she kept at the bench.

    Then, almost reverently, she took it out. The ray-gun.

    She let out a snort of a laugh. She couldn't help it! It looked funny. So unassuming. It really did look like some kinda hokey prop from a 50's sci-fi movie.

No evident power source. No indication that it had been welded shut. The lack of any visible configuration options was a little troubling - and annoying. Perhaps that meant that there was some other way of controlling it. Maybe there was an additional piece of equipment that was meant to go with it. Like...some kind of goggles or a glove or something. Or maybe it needed special software to configure it. There had to be something - she just had no idea what.
