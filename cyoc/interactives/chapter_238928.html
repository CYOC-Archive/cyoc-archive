    The flight to Costa de Cabra was relatively quick from Paris, and Raoul did find the area warmer, even as the sun began to set after the plane landed at the airport for the Spanish city that Irena's villa and related colony grounds was a part of. A part of him wouldn't mind actually staying in Spain for the duration of the honeymoon with Juliet. For while he'd had occasional moments to travel in the past, a lot of his own work for the French government often did keep him busy and thus his vacations were few and far between. He did however know bits of Spain's history that included long periods in the Medieval period where they were under Islamic rule. And while Charles Martel had saved the Franks from that political fall, Spain had remained under Islamic rule for years and it took time for the peninsula to be reclaimed... or reconquered by the Spanish Christians

    Raoul also knew that with that extensive history came a fairly decent sized cultural impact, as while various Muslim kingdoms were ultimately forced out of Spain, it took time. They ruled at least part of the peninsula from the early 700s into the late 1400s to early 1500s. That had to have provided a lengthy period of influence on culture, architecture, and even learning, even outside the Muslim kingdom in Spain. After all, trade and learning were not things that magically appeared in the twentieth century. To varying degrees, even in the Medieval world, there was international trade and diplomatic relations. But Raoul was not good enough on Spanish history to know what parts of Muslim rule survived the "reconquest" and thus couldn't know on what influence might have been had in the area around the part of Spain where Costa de Cabra was.

    It was something he could have wished he knew more on. Knowing that would give him something to compare to the stories his own parents had told him on their role in France's rule in Algeria and the service that lasted through to the end of French rule in Algeria. He'd stories on how the region was exotic and over time had mixtures of both France and Algeria as a result of the efforts at colonization that had been there in the 1800s. But, for the moment, that wasn't on the agenda, and to a great degree, Raoul agreed with that. Juliet was smart and kind, protective, and caring. Her dedication to education and helping those that were confronted by mental disabilities was impressive and had been a big part of what had drawn him to her, even as an official in France's education ministry. Now as husband and wife, they would have the opportunity for so much more... and he was looking forward to the conversion.

    Though, as Irena escorted Juliet to the medical wing of her villa, Lopez escorted Raoul to the guest room that the two of them would have for the night.

    "Shouldn't... shouldn't I be there for her?" Raoul wondered as Lopez opened the door and allowed him to enter the room, and placing the bags on a chair that was along a fair wall. He then backtracked through to an end table by the bed and turned on the electric lamp that was there.

    "It's just a shot... and one that would make the Animalia Virus in her system infectious once more," Lopez answered, "you don't need to be there for the shot... and given that this is your wedding night, I'd assume you'd rather have it here than in a hospital room."

    "I suppose you're right, there," Raoul managed, "thank you."

    "You're welcome," Lopez answered with a smile and then left the room.

    That left Raoul to pace the room for a few moments while he then waited. He assumed that Irena would escort Juliet to him, as that had been part of what had been discussed at dinner. It wasn't as ornate as what had been there at the reception in Paris, but Raoul also knew more than anyone that the government was trying to put on something of a show to draw attention away from the standard chaos that permeated French politics. At the same time, Raoul also wasn't one to really care about the food on his plate. He'd come from a decent family, but they'd also lacked the historic wealth that Juliet's family had, so he could easily accept food that wasn't ornate. Juliet had accepted it too, though, Raoul also remembered both of them staring into the eyes of the other as they ate dinner and had barely noticed Irena and Lopez smiling at each other at the time.

    Eventually, Raoul sat down on the bed and set about taking his shoes off. They weren't uncomfortable and were different from the formal shoes that he'd worn with his tux for the wedding, but that didn't mean they were something he'd be able to be comfortable in for a long period of time. Raoul also had the recognition that as an Animalian, he’d likely be unable to wear those shoes again. In fact, much of his clothing, or at least his pants, would need to be different. That brought a small smile to his lips as the door to the room opened and Juliet entered. She entered in the same soft blue dress that she'd changed out of her wedding dress for. He also suspected that the lace, itself, offered to many potential openings that would tug at Juliet's fur when she moved, and thus why a more solid white underdress had been fashioned to fit under the outer lace dress.

    "Thank you," Juliet smiled as Raoul approached her and the two of them hugged. It felt good to be close to him and for the moment not having work or other emergencies to interrupt them, "and you are correct. Human shoes would not fit a Fox Animalian. Though... neither will your pants... but with this being our wedding night, that can be dealt with."

Foxes were relatively small predators and even the largest species, the Red Fox, was not the top dog in any environment where a larger Canid was present or where a comparably sized Felid was present.
