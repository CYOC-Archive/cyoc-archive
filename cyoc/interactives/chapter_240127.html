    The Magic Shop

    A Magical Leather Jacket

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added by Anonymous 2 months ago O Clothes

    You spot a leather jacket hanging on a rack, its texture inviting your touch. Slipping it on, you find it hugs your shoulders just right. A satisfied grin spreads across your face as you move towards the counter.

    The cashier, an elderly man with silver streaked hair and robes that brush the ground, shuffles closer. His gaze is intense but kind, eyes like twin lanterns in the dimness.

    "Ah, you choose well," he says, his accent thick but words clear. "You feel good, yes?"

    "Yeah, fits like a glove," you reply, nodding. "How much is it?"

    He calculates in his head for a moment before speaking, "Fifty. Very good price."

    You hand him the bills, relieved at how reasonable it seems for something so well-crafted.

    As you turn to leave, the cashier gently places a book into your hands. "Don't forget manual," he insists, holding your gaze with urgency.

    "Manual?" you echo, thrown off balance. "Do jackets need manuals?"

    "It... help," he replies cryptically, waving you on with a small, knowing smile.

    You exit the shop, clutching the jacket and its mysterious manual, questions swirling in your mind like autumn leaves.

    You stand in front of the mirror, examining the reflection. The brown leather jacket settles perfectly on your shoulders, the material soft yet sturdy. It enhances your silhouette, lending a certain sleekness to your appearance. The sleeves are just the right length, stopping neatly at your wrists. The rich color of the leather complements your eyes, making them seem more vivid.

    A zippered front adds a touch of modern style, while the collar frames the neckline neatly. The jacket cinches slightly at the waist, giving you a streamlined look. You turn from side to side, digesting how well it fits, the jacket fitting like a second skin. It's undeniably handsome in its simplicity yet carries an undeniable charm.

    You examine the manual, its leather cover smooth and slightly worn. No title adorns it, save for its understated presence. Opening the pages, you discover handwriting flowing in neat, looping cursive. The characters seem unfamiliar, maybe Latin, their curves and angles foreign to your eye.

    The pages are rich with illustrations, resembling an eclectic fashion catalogue. Images of people clad in various outfits, animals in peculiar attire, and an array of other oddities fill the manual. Each sketch bears a number, with lengthy blocks of text corresponding to each figure.

    Your gaze settles on an illustration: a man standing confidently beside a motorcycle, clad in a sleek racing outfit. The jacket catches your eye, marked by a letter that leads you to its block of text. Drawn by curiosity, you read it aloud: "Virtus velox, corpus in armis mutare, effigiem draconis assumere."

    The words seem to resonate through the air around you. You watch as the jacket you wear begins to evolve, material shifting under your touch. It morphs into the robust form of a motorcycle racing jacket, complete with protective padding and ergonomic design.

    The material now hugs your torso with refined security, the protectors affixed at strategic points like the shoulders and elbows. The gleaming surface reflects light, offering an air of impenetrability. Zippered vents provide functionality, while the collar molds snugly around your neck. As you move, the jacket flexes with ease, balancing protection with comfort, and you admire its transformation on your form.
