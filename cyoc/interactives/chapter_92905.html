    The Vials

    Mary assesses the Animal Vial Options

    As Mary slid open the ‘Animal’ Drawer, the sheer variety she had open to her was plain to see. So many to choose from, she wasn’t quite sure where to begin. Though she had already tried the Bear Vial, in that Feline Grouping there were also:

    Cheetah
    Cougar
    Jaguar
    Leopard
    Lion
    Lynx
    Panther
    Smilodon (Sabre-toothed Tiger)
    Tabby Cat
    Tigress


    Another Group referred to as Canine offered:

    Arctic Fox
    Alsatian
    Collie
    Coyote
    Dalmatian
    Dingo
    Red Fox
    Grey Fox
    Jackal
    Labrador
    Wolf


    For Aquatics, she could choose from:

    Dolphin
    Eel
    Fish - Cod
    Fish - Flying
    Fish - Tuna
    Fish - Swordfish
    Orca
    Porpoise
    Shark - Great White
    Shark - Hammerhead
    Shark - Tiger
    Shark - Whale
    Whale - Blue
    Whale - Sperm


    Reptile Options that Mary had included:

    Alligator
    Chameleon
    Crocodile
    Lizard - Gecko
    Lizard - Iguana
    Lizard - Salamander
    Lizard - Whiptail
    Snake - Boa Constrictor
    Snake - Green Adder
    Snake - King Cobra


    While the Ungulate Vials had:

    Antelope
    Camel
    Cow
    Deer
    Donkey
    Giraffe
    Horse
    Llama
    Zebra


    “So which one do I go for?” Mary asked herself as the obvious challenge face her. The sheer quantity of what she had available to her was somehow both the greatest benefit and greatest pitfall that had been offered to her.

    Deciding to take some time to try and properly think it through, Mary had since settled herself on her couch as she debated with herself over what to do next. As her experience with the Bear Vial had demonstrated quite aptly, if she went all the way, she would need a means of being able to take the Return Vial once she was done, especially as it appeared to be the only means there was to reclaiming her current form. There was one other factor to consider given her location, some of the vials weren’t really an option, a lack of large amounts of open water save for the medium-sized swimming pool wrote off most of the aquatic options, the fish and sharks having gills and no lungs making them pretty unviable for her a this time, while the whale and orca were simply too large for her to be supported here. So she would have to select one of the smaller land-based animals for her next run, which still left her with a large option range.

    The Bear Vial had been feline so she decided to opt out of them to trim her options, dropping it down to just the Canine, Reptile, and Ungulate Vials to pick out from.

    “So which of the thirty plus to go for?” Mary pondered, running a finger over them as she slowly began to make up her mind.
