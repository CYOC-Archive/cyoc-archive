    William felt a bit more comfortable with some things after the conversation with Samantha and Frances Twist. Both he and Martha had been to the City Council meeting and thus they both knew how the vote was reached. William had never studied politics and never pretended to understand them, beyond maybe how writers portrayed politics in their literature, but he did get the sense that Tom Herald and used some facts in making his argument. William could commend the man for that much, even if he had his own worries about the possible negatives that might come out of a reveal.

    And those negatives were all possible, and to a certain level, both William and Martha worried on that possibility. For if the response was negative, it would come back to hurt their family, which neither wanted. William knew that he'd made his fair share of mistakes in his life. Not noticing or realizing that Martha had been struggling with being bisexual when they first married and then letting himself be lead by his own lusts was the biggest of them.     "There will still be some concerns that are there..." Martha spoke as she watched Samantha walk around the table and then sit down in the chair next to Frances and her own chair.

    "Though... you've put some of our greatest worries at ease," William added, "and for that we thank you."

    "I'd think that if you went to the meeting, you'd be a bit more aware on the reasoning for the vote," Samantha commented.

    "As said... we know how they got to the decision they made," Martha answered, "but not on how it would work out in the long term... For if they rush things and the response is negative..."

    "Well a negative response would be bad, regardless of how long it takes to make the decision," Frances corrected, "the bigger thing for the moment would be more that making sure the plan is coherent and covers everything and gathers as much information as possible to assure a more positive response is likely."

    "Which is what the City Council will do," Samantha nodded in agreement with her wife, "if the vote had been more one sided... then "rushing" things might be possible, but given that this relates to the were secret, that sort of vote was likely never likely given the way things are right now."

    "And that has put our worries with the potential negatives at ease," William nodded in reply.

    "And the worries with the potential positives?" Frances asked.

    "More on what we'd be allowed to do if the response is positive," Martha spoke.

    "Such as being who we truly are more often," William nodded, "the fox in me... he seems more real to me than anything that I was before moving here. And since you turned Martha... she's been happier and more confident in herself, which makes the fox in me happy to see her happy."

    "Which is good to hear," Samantha commented, "but as said earlier... all of that really won't be determined until we know more on the nature of the response. And until then... Moon Lake will do what it can to make sure that the worst is avoided and that the town stays safe."

    "And that's really all we wanted to hear," Martha reached over to touch Samantha's hand.
