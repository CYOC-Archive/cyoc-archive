    Marinie had spent the Christmas season practically worshipping the ground Calce walked on. Celce was gonna be a magical girl, just like in all the cartoons! Little Marinie's destiny was set in stone as soon as she had laid eyes upon a real life magical girl. Even if she wouldn't remember the encounter itself in later years of life, she'd remember that big sister Calce had always been her role model (even still referring her to big sister even after her age was frozen at ten years old, and would be biologically three years Calce's senior).

    But this isn't time for that story.

    Calce's family, like all the families of the Stonewood students, were surprisingly accepting of their son coming home as a seven year old girl with magic powers. Then again, they'd already accepted their son might end up a bunny girl at some bar, a statue in an art gallery, or any other number of transformation fodder for the exceptionally powerful.

    Being turned into a bunny girl or statue could never be considered murder since the person wasn't legally dead, and it couldn't be considered slavery since the bunny girls' new self was happy being a witless cocktail waitress. On the bright side, race and religion were non-factors on who ended up TF-fodder, it was down to who could change or be changed.

    So all in all, Calce's new existence was more than they'd been actually hoping for!

    But even this wasn't the major thing.

    They'd stopped at Bear Burgers... run by a mix of human employees and life size teddy bears. The teddies spoke in the same voice, the same body language, the same vocabulary, and had no need for breaks or pay. The human employees seemed to always be on edge.

    Lucy had ordered their food (including the special meal for with the toy for Celce). Celce saw all the happy designs, everything carefully made to appeal to the customers as much ass possible right down to the pattern on the plastic seats.

    But Celce felt something off, something... WRONG about this place! Something wasn't right about it. And the giant teddies LOOKED cute, it wasn't some failure on the designers part that had left the teddies in the uncanny valley, it was something she FELT!

    Celce told a slight fib in that she needed to go the bathroom (which was true, but also wasn't why she wanted to go).

    Celce followed her instincts, something in her telling her where the wrongness was centered... and she went into the 'Employees Only' sign... she felt very bad about breaking the rules, she hoped that she didn't get in big trouble for this.

    She saw a pockmarked skinny young man in the store's uniform. Towering over him was an obese man with big muscles on his arms.

    "Please! I couldn't understand the man's accent! I didn't mean to get his order wrong! I'm sorry! I won't do it again! I promise!"

    "This much is true..." Grinned the big man, the manager?, in a predatorily way. "But you know what your contract said."

    "You said it was just standard stuff I didn't need to read!"

    "And you believed me."

    The manager grabbed the skinny youth, and in a flash, his skin became faux fur and cloth, his inside became stuff. His eyes became glass beads, his body bulged and morphed, into one of the endless identical teddy bears... the manager pulled out a glowing red heart shaped gem out of the bear's body, and it went stiff and lifeless.

    "Now, let's just erase your name, your past, anything really except how to be a good worker really, oh I can feel you're scared, don't be, you won't feel anything in a -"

    Then it happened. Celce became the first.

    Calce transformed.

    In a flash her clothes became a mass of sparkles, she was in a swirling pocket reality of green leaves of various kinds and shades, and a giant drop fell from one above her, and struck her, and her clothes reformed into a frilly light green dress with trim that matched her hair color, with silk frills on the back that resembled transparent fairy wind.

    In her little gloved hands appeared a gold and emerald staff that was a cross between a wand and a scepter that she twirled around and managed to avoid tripping herself over with.

    "HALT VILLAIN! Bounty Celce is here to stop you!" She shouted, striking a pose, that was supposed to be dramatic, but looked adorable instead.

    "What this? A kid in a costume? Don't you know there's punishment for customers trespassing?" He took a step forward.

    The entire restaurant shook. The teddies stopped what they were doing. Lucy quickly went looking for Celce, looked everywhere, and heard her voice behind the forbidden door, and her love for her family overrode her fear and looked in.

    "Oh thank you!" Said the teddy in the room with a name tag saying his name was Jimmy. Using body language and tones that were different from the rest. "You saved me!"

    Lucy saw Celce in her magical girl costume, next to an ugly looking tree that was stuck in the ground in the shape of a man.

    "Anything in the fight against evil!" Celce said with a smile, but Lucy could tell she was shaking inside.

    "CELCE!"

    "Lucy!" Celce looked embarrassed, and hide her wand comically behind her back.

    "What did you do?"

    "She saved my life," said the teddy bear. "I'd have been just another zombie if not for her... "

    "I'm not done," Celce whispered. She quickly went to the other human sized teddies, and began to bop them on the noses with her wand, to the confusion of the customers. The teddies blinked, and began to move like individuals.

    The teddies found some red ribbons to wear as bowties or bow to establish which of them had been boys and which ones had been girls.

    "You..." Lucy was confused. "You're a person?"

    "You bet!" Jimmy said in his own voice, but more cutie than before.

    Jimmy would admit later that the teddies were still driven by their purposes to be restaurant servers... but Bear Burgers suddenly had an entire group of Teddies demanding workman's comp, breaks, and being PAID.

    Celce had done what was thought to be impossible, she'd restored people who had been reduced to non-human objects. Jimmy's coworkers described her time with their selves wiped away, not as happily working with their fellow bears in a zen-like bliss, but empty vessels following instructions.

    "Lucy..." Celce asked finally letting herself show fear. "Are you mad at me?"

    Lucy hugged her.

    The Dean got the phone call.

    "Assign Celce a bodyguard AT ONCE! Tell the teddies we're willing to offer them protection if they want it! ... and make Celce a giant ice cream sundae in honor of being the first to transform."
