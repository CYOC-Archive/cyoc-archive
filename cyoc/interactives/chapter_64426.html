    A Costume for Cory

    Kylie stared at the now very masculine form of Jennifer Smith, gorilla like arms hanging awkwardly at her
    sides, football jersey taut across her broad shoulders. Beside her stood the now pretty and buxom form of Tom,
    breasts large and round pressing tight into his cheer uniform. Both stood still, resigned to their fates and
    praying they would be let go. Kylie decided to grant them mercy.
    "You both can go, I've had enough fun with you... for now." With those words tension bled from the two swapped
    teens and they fled the stage, Jen clomping heavily and Tom stepping daintily down the stairs. Once they had
    rejoined others Kylie swept her gaze around the costumed figures, looking for a new victim. She stopped when
    she saw the boy not wearing a costume, leaning against the wall with a couple other boys. She pointed, and he
    came, calmly and cool. He didn't falter a single step.. until he saw Kylie's eyes.
    "Well well, Cory. Too cool for Halloween? Couldn't manage to dress up for us?" She nodded over to the other
    boys by the wall. "Or maybe you didn't want to be embarrassed in front of your friends?" She grinned, showing
    all her teeth. "If thats the case I don't think you're going to manage that tonight." Cory looked a little
    uneasy at the announcement but did not back down. Kylie continued. "I'm going to play a little game with you
    Cory. A game that you, unfortunately, can't really win but you will gain a costume out of it. I am going to
    ask you to start dancing. And you will dance Cory, or you will wish you had. As you dance I am going to have
    some fun. So... Dance." Cory almost managed to keep his nerve and stare down the green eyed witch but as she
    raised a hand he began.He danced awkwardly and slowly. Having no music playing he had no rhythm. Kylie watched
    him for a moment before saying one word.
    "Grace." She said and Cory's movements immediately changed. His jerky motions were now a bit more fluid, each
    step and movement of his body seem smooth and purposeful if not in an exactly attractive way. Cory didn't seem
    to notice.
    "Flexibility." Kylie said. Once again his movements changed. His back bent and curved as he moved. He kicked
    his legs up high, pointing his toes. He still had no skill at dance but just watching him move was far more
    impressive.
    "Femininity." At this Cory's body changed as well as his motions. He form seemed to shrink in on itself a bit
    while at the same time his legs seemed to grow, long and thin. His clothes hung loosely over his new form. He
    was still very much a man but with a body type that now resembled that of a woman.
    "Agility." Cory spun. He twisted and moved to no music and with no order. Yet his movements were fast and
    accurate. He moved with a smooth grace and unpracticed ability that seemed to make up for his apparent lack of
    skill.
    "Passion." Kylie almost whispered. Cory moved. His whole body swaying and flowing as he moved across the
    stage. He whipped from side to side and spun in smooth arcs, moving from one motion to the next quickly and
    flawlessly.
    "Femininity." Kylie repeated. This time the changes were far more obvious. First to change was his clothing.
    He now spun and dance around the stage wearing a tight pink tutu which his body was rapidly changing to fill.
    His hair fell around his face, longer than it had been but still short. His breasts swelled to fill the tight
    top, petite and perky. His legs seemed to go on forever, lithe and strong, wrapped in pink tights. His feet
    were small and dainty, wrapped in a ballerinas slippers.
    "Skill." Kylie said finally and Cory flew. He spun and whipped around the stage, landing triple spins easily
    and flawlessly. His leg kicked high above his own head and curved back down as he twisted. Even when he moved
    slowly there was a sense of speed and passion about every movement, even to the slightest motion of his
    delicate fingers. Finally he stopped, panting and sweating. He noticed the changes but did not freak out, he
    only looked at him self and then back at the crowd and bowed low, smooth and full of grace. He left the stage,
    each step placed perfectly before him, without looking back at Kylie. She only smiled and shrugged. "I did
    tell you that you would get a costume out of it."
