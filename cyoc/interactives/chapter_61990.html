    Adam Gets Dean Away From the Party

    Adam was at a loss of what to do. He knew coming to this place was a bad idea. And now he was looking at metallic statues of his older brothers. Metallic statues that were being used as kegs for a frat party no less. Logically speaking, it wasn't possible for a human being to be turned into living kegs. However, Adam had seen his fair share of weird ever since he found out about the supernatural from his brothers and had joined them in hunting monsters. He had seen some weird things before, but after touching metallic Dean, and feeling slight warmth coming from it, Adam knew this had to be something supernatural. Adam cringed at seeing the tap where Dean's genitals should be. If Dean was alive, being tapped by a bunch of drunk college students must have hurt like a bitch.

    "Hey, are you just going to sit there or are you going to drink?" a voice asked behind Adam.

    Adam jumped when he heard the voice. He turned around to see Aaron there glaring at him. He had to think fast.

    "Uh...It's uh...Tapped. No more in this one." Adam said. "I better get these two out of here."

    "Both of them? But this one hasn't even been tapped yet." Aaron said as he moved over to metal Sam and patted the smooth bulge of Sam's groin.

    "Uh, well it's better save than sorry. Maybe it's gone toxic or something." Adam made up. He knew it was a weak excuse. Especially when Aaron glared at him. He had to get out fast. He didn't want to leave Sam behind, being closer to the middle brother than he was with Dean. However, he knew if he got Dean out of there and some how find a way to restore Dean to normal, they could come back for Sam.

    "Just get out of here kid." Aaron said as he took the tap and hammer the caretaker had given him. Aaron went to tap Sam. Adam had to close his eyes and turn away when he heard the sound of metal against metal. He prayed that Sam couldn't feel anything. Being frozen and unable to do anything as some weirdo slammed a tap into your groin was one thing. But feeling it. Adam couldn't stay and think about it. He lifted Dean up onto the trolly that had been left behind and rolled Dean away.

    "I'll be back Sam," Adam whispered as he weaved through the crowd, excusing himself now and again. Once Adam was out of the thrall of the party, he looked around for the basement door. For some reason of other, when people wanted to do evil rituals, they always did it in the basement. He didn't know why, but they just did. It was weird. Like the basement was the center point of evil magic or something. Adam wheeled Dean through the house, unaware he was being followed.

    Soon, Adam found a lock door and after checking to make sure the close was clear, raised his foot and kicked the door. It took five tries, but Adam managed to kick the door open, breaking the lock. "I need to start working out more. You're probably making fun of me for this aren't you?" Adam asked the metallic Dean.

    After carefully carrying Dean down the steps, with only a few stumbles here and there, Adam managed to drag Dean over to one of the tables. He looked around. It looked like he was in the workshop from that House of Wax movie. However his eyes caught something on the ground. Pushing away one of the tables, Adam found a giant transmutation circle painted on the ground. He knew a thing or two about alchemy when he found an old leather book on their last hunt. He recognize the design as a powerful one used for turning flesh into other materials. He also knew there had to be an instruction manual. Looking around, Adam went through the desks and papers before he found a leather book.

    "I knew it. There's got to be a way to turn people back to normal in here." Adam mused. He found it and turned to Dean, who was laying on the table directly in the center of the circle. "Hang on Dean, I'll fix this."

    "Oh no you won't."
