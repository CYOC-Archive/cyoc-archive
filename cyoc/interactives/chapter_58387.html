    lay. They had left their families back in America in Glendale, planning to be the first
    Lupmans to climb Mount Everest WITHOUT breathing equipment. A risk a thousand times
    over, some called it even suicidal, but people of history where those who took risks, those
    who dared.

    Felmans actually had scaled Mount Everest first, their bodies requiring less oxygen,
    gargoyles didn't breath, but were not a species known for ambition seeing things age of
    old age around them. Humans had been a close second. Leomans and Lupmans were only
    able to reach the top without falling into unconsciousness and needing to be carted back
    down once Oxygen masks had been invented. A few dragons in ancient times had
    supposed tried and succeeded, but hadn't come back down the most sane of sorts.

    The felmans simply didn't require as much air, when the amount Lupmans and Leomans
    required made scaling the mountain a herculean effort on the best of days for the most
    experienced climbers.

    These Lupmans, whose fur had been the colors of precious stones, had sought to challenge
    the mountain. To prove they were stronger than it was . . . The mountain proved itself
    superior. The last member of their group had actually died about a dozen yard from the
    very peak . . . but snow had hidden the bones.
