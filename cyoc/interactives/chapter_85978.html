    How Do You Solve a Problem Like Brian?

    added by Rawshock 10 years ago O

    The wizard pondered. "Hm, well, now that you have seen this much, I can hardly just let you go and tell everyone, can I? Especially not the townspeople. No, that would complicate matters greatly, and I would rather avoid that."

    "But everyone in town already seemed to know that you were a wizard," Brian protested. "What harm could it do for them to know?"

    "They know that I have magical power, yes, but they have not yet begun to suspect just how powerful I am... or how old I am. They call me 'wizard' more as a joke than anything, really. Ignorant cretins. In addition, altering reality is illegal under the laws of the realm, though of course that is a hard law to enforce considering that it could only be detected in certain circumstances... circumstances like a traveler from another world running his mouth off. And even as powerful as I am, there is only so much that my power can do against a large, angry mob."

    "I promise I won't tell, then! Please just let me go!" Brian pleaded.

    The wizard shook his head. "No, better not to take that chance at all. It would be much easier and cleaner to just change you and your reality as well. Fitting, too. Assuming, of course, that your wizard agrees."

    Brian's eyes widened in panic, but he felt relief as well, since the wizard had asked for Ty's permission. Mike... whoever he was... might have agreed to this, but Brian hadn't, and he knew that his good friend Ty would never betray him... or at least he thought he did, until he saw Ty grinning and nodding. "Sure, of course," Ty said affably, "I can totally understand. I wouldn't want anyone blabbing about my powers, after all. But whatever you change him into, just make sure that it's interesting."

    The wizard nodded back at Ty and grinned in response, while Brian stared at his former friend, shocked and hurt. He wracked his brain for some way out of his predicament, and recalled, "You said you could only change Mike's reality because he agreed to it! I don't agree to any of this."

    "Yes, because he is from another universe," the wizard explained patiently, and a little condescendingly, "but now that I have a consenting wizard from your universe here with me, the two of us will be able to change you however we want, assuming of course that the change does not involve a third universe. Now, let us see, what to transform you into? There are a great many things that I have needed, changes that would be useful, but which would be best? Hm..." The wizard stroked his chin pensively, and Ty shrugged. "Whatever you want, man, I'm just here to help."

    Brian's panic grew, and he looked around the stable for a way to escape. There had to be some way out of this!

    "Aha," the wizard snapped his fingers as the answer came to him, "I know just what you can be."
