    Ty held her close, her own joy welling up inside. This was right. Everything within her knew that this was how things were meant to be.

    When the hug ended, Alissa backed up, her face now a bit more serious. “There is one unexpected complication, however. Come, you must see this.”

    With a flick of her tail, the mermaid swam towards the shore. Instinctively, Ty did likewise and glided after her. The water rapidly grew shallow around them until they drew themselves up onto the sand. For the first time, Ty felt long wet hair plastering her back as gravity once more took effect on her, and she maneuvered herself into at sitting position next to her mother.

    “So, he was also a mermaid like me?”

    Alissa shook her head. “I’m not sure. Nothing like this has ever happened before. To be honest, much about humans remains a mystery to us.”

    “Well, can we change him back?”

    “That is for us to determine later. For now, we must return to our own world. Lemuria needs its princess, and the Queen has waited far too long to see her granddaughter.” Alissa gently lifted the unconscious mermaid into her arms. “We will take her with us and figure this out.”
