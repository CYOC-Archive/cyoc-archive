Some of that likely related to what had happened with the Jenkins family and with Jenny, which Peter figured was understandable.     "I wasn't asking about your physical health," Peter answered and slowly stroked her back, "and it's clear that something is bothering you."

    Karen only sighed. Part of it was from contentment at Peter being close to her and rubbing her back in a way that betrayed great strength while being quite gentle at the same time, but it was also from some of the frustrations and worries that she had. She was glad that Huck Jenkins was going to be okay and that the police had the shooter in custody and might even have the evidence to get Dieter Wilhelm behind bars for a long time, but the fact that the man had been brought so close to death by a gunshot that not even Huck's being a were could save him on its own unnerved her. Peter was big and strong, but if a gunshot could do that, a part of a her now feared that Dieter Wilhelm might be better armed than the police could be prepared for and nervous thoughts of Jenny or Harry and Peter being gunned down at the wedding flashed in her mind.

    "It's all that happened," Karen answered as she looked up to Peter, "this agent and what he did... Jenny and Harry both said that from what they knew... that if it weren't for Betty and the officer that had been tailing the agent, he would have died there! And if this Wilhelm guy has sent two agents and one actually carried out a shot... who is to say that he won't try to shoot Harry or Jenny in person? Who is to say he won't try to shoot you or me?"

    Peter only held her tightly and let a low rumble escape his lips. Werelions and weretigers, like mundane lions and tigers couldn't purr, or at least not in the way a house cat could purr, as their vocal chords were geared to roar. The closest that werelions and weretigers could approximate to a purr was to try and give a low rumble, usually on the exhale. It wasn't a purr but it worked for werelions and weretigers.

    "Everything will be fine, I promise you," Peter promised.

    "But what if Wilhelm is armed?" Karen commented, "I'd thought that only silver bullets..."

    "Weres aren't invulnerable," Peter admitted, "but we're STILL tough to kill and can recover from a lot. Harry and Jenny even told you that he'd be well enough to be discharged from the hospital Saturday morning at the absolute latest, which would be far sooner than what a normal would spend in a hospital with a similar wound... provided they even make it to the hospital."

    "But I don't want to see that happen to Jenny or Harry... or to you," Karen spoke as she looked up into Peter's eyes.

    "And it won't," Peter promised and held Karen to him tightly, "the police caught the shooter and have the evidence to arrest Dieter Wilhelm in relation to calling for Harry's murder. And we will all be paying attention to make sure that you, Harry, and Jenny are safe. Everything will be fine. Harry and Jenny will get their happy wedding..."

    Peter paused to run a finger under Karen's chin, "and then you and I can have our own happy rendezvous."

    Karen smiled at that and ran one hand down Peter's chest. It was well sculpted and she could easily feel the strength in his pectorals through the shirt he wore. If she weren't so worried and nervous about Dieter Wilhelm being better prepared than the Moon Lake police, she might have seriously considered getting her pills from her purse and give Peter a wonderful night and become his lioness. But she was still fairly nervous. Peter's confidence and warm embrace helped, but until Jenny became Mrs. Harry Jenkins, Karen was certain to remain on edge.

    "I hope so," Karen gave a weak smile, "a lot of this just has me so completely on edge. Jenny deserves some happiness in life and parents should allow for that. My parents did for me... but from everything I've heard... Jenny's parents haven't. It's unbelievable... and the guy they have chosen isn't someone Jenny likes and has already demonstrated that he isn't above criminal action to get what he wanted. It just has me nervous and fearful that things could get worse."
