The premise of the story was: The year was 1510. Princess Jeff was travelling in a luxurious looking European medieval carriage. She was travelling through the English countryside to meet the King of England. With the Princess in the lead carriage was her lady in waiting, whilst the Japanese Ambassador and his clerks were in a following vehicle. They were escorted by 50 Samurai who looked splendid as they rode alongside the two carriages.

    Akari realised the nonsensicality of the situation as England was on the other side of the world and there was no contact between the two kingdoms until centuries later. She decided to treat the story as a ‘What if?’ situation. How the Princess and her entourage had managed to get to England would remain a mystery.

    Akari liked that the Princess was a bit mischievous. The 4th picture was of her looking out of the carriage at her escort. Whilst in the 5th picture, she was reprimanded for her uncouth behaviour by her Lady in waiting, whose name was Niwata.

    At last Akari came to her list of choices for the 2nd Chapter.

She also dismissed the last suggestion, guessing it was a mistake in translation somehow. That left her to choose option 1 ‘Princess Jeff arrives at the Kings Court safely’

It appeared that as she was a princess, she was not allowed to look out of the carriage windows which seemed such a stupid rule. However she had to go with the flow, so had to amuse herself with what was inside the carriage. There was a trunk on the seat beside Niwata and Jeff asked the woman “What’s in that trunk?”

    “Your jewels. You have 5 other trunks on the back of the carriage, which contain your clothes and other possessions”

    Jeff eagerly opened the trunk to see what she now possessed. Amongst the items was a jewelled looking glass. With that Jeff was able to examine her reflection. She was in awe that looking back at her was a beautiful Japanese girl. She had flawless skin, big brown eyes and a petite nose. Her hair was done in some intricate fashion held together by multiple pins. Niwata warned her against fiddling with the pins. Also, in the trunk were multiple pieces of gold and silver jewellery, which must have been worth a fortune and supply of silk scarves elaborately embroidered.

    Jeff had to wait 5 more minutes before she was allowed to exit the carriage.

It was long sleeved, with the sleeves hanging down almost like a cape. Jeff seemed to instinctively know it was called a furisode. It definately wasn't the sort of outfit you could jog in.

This woman will show you to a room where you may rest and clean up”

The woman gestured towards a bed and a chair. Her language was also unintelligible to Jeff, but Niwata seemed to understand her. Niwata translated “She says you are free to make use of anything in the room. The chair contains a chamber-pot under the cushion. She says they are honoured that royalty are visiting their humble establishment” The woman bowed before leaving the room.
