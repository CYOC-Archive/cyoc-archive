    "You made these trees?" Rosa asked, thinking about what Titania had said about the trees.

    "No," Titania answered, "the witches made them."

    "And you support them?" Mal'akh asked, "the witches?"

    "Not truly," Titania fluttered up to Mal'akh, "they reunited our families after being alone and apart for a long time... though I can not remember what made us split up."

    "They didn't turn you into fairies?" Mal'akh asked.

    "Of course not," Titania answered, "only four humans have joined the Fae Folk. And that transformation was done by my "sisters". I assure you though, their transformation was willing."

    There was an awkward silence before Monica spoke up.

    "And how do the Fae Folk feel about the witches?" Monica asked nervously.

    "They reunited our families, and we were indebted to them for that," Titania answered, "but recently, before all this fighting began we felt that our debt was paid and we had to go seek our fortune elsewhere."

    "And your're still here?" Monica asked.

    "My family is still in this area," Titania answered, "moving takes a lot of time and can exhaust the Fae Folk. We swim in magic, but we are drained hollowing out a tree so that we may live in it while insuring that the tree is not dead or sick."

    "How can a tree be sick?" Mal'akh asked.

    "They are living things," Titania shrugged, "it is only logical that they can get sick."

    "And you acknowledge God?" Monica asked.

    "We know he is stronger then us, yes," Titania answered, "he is a higher power. Why do you ask this question?"

    "The trees you are mourning were protecting the coven of witches you say reunited your families," Monica began.

    "Yes."

    "Those witches have been defeated," Monica continued.

    "So they are dead, like the trees?"

    "No, their "hunters" intend to try the witches for crimes they have committed against the people of Glendale," Monica spoke, "We have been sent by them to find someone who can serve as a Jury and possibly as witnesses."

    "I am hardly a jury," Titania answered.

    "But there are other fairies, right?" Rosa asked, "Your people could be the jury."

    "You would ask that of the Fae Folk?" Titania wondered.

    "You've said that you didn't blindly follow the witches," Rosa said, "you would be a fairer jury then most others we have come across this day. The hunters I think would be inclined to find them guilty, and citizens of Glendale, so entranced by the witches would find them innocent, regardless of the evidence. Your people would be a fair choice."

    "I see," Titania answered and then paused, "I suppose it could not hurt. Olga said something about getting a chance to insure there is light if I headed out now..."

    "Thank you," Rosa answered, "the hunters are at the witches' manor, I think the trial will be held there."

    "You are welcome," Titania answered and flew off toward the forest to collect her friends. She only hoped that Beatrice was feeling better after the pain she had suffered. The first pregnant fairy the Fae folk had... only to suddenly have her real in pain and lose the tiny life inside her... which had appeared to be male. Her husband, son, and daughter ended up trying to cheer her up for days.

    Present Day

    Harold played on a pipe flute in the top branches of the tree that the Fae now resided in. As he played, he thought back on the long ups and downs of his life. His time as a human, meeting and marrying Beatrice. Their first two children, Jasper and Ruth. Then his family joining the Fae Folk, though he and Jasper had been shrunk down so that they were the same size as the fairies.

    Life among them hadn't been bad for him. Beatrice still loved him, though she also loved all the other female fairies. Harold soon learned that they'd all come to love him. He tried not to think of Beatrice's miscarriage and the witches' trial. From what Beatrice had told him, the witches didn't take kindly to them not freeing them from their bindings.

    But life went on. The Fae Folk had found their new home where Glendale's curse would never threaten them, and Harold had sired multiple children with the female fairies, who came to him when they wanted a male lover. As a result, there were soon more then enough male fairies to finish the transformation for him and Jasper. Their magic wasn't as strong as their female counterparts, but it was enough.

    "I thought I'd find you up here," came Beatrice's voice.

    Harold turned to Beatrice flutter up to him, her hands resting on her womb. A new batch of children for the two of them on their way.

    "Just enjoying the afternoon," Harold kissed her and helped her sit on the branch beside him, "and partially celebrating the end of the Curse the witches brought."

    "How do you think Olga knows all these things?" Beatrice gave a polite chuckle.

    "She is the strongest of us," Harold shrugged.

    "I suppose that's it," Beatrice answered, "so what will we name these ones when they're born?"

    "I'd like to wait until after they're born to name them," Harold answered.
