    The Zoo

    Starting the canine way of life

    It takes a moment for you to gather yourself and calming down your tensed muscles. You swallow in fear as you realise that you’re standing natural on your four paws. The only problem is that it’s natural in a canine type of way. Paws: The very word makes you whimper in sorrow now as you know they’ve replaced your useful hands and possibly they have been replaced for good. “Another transformation well done by my special collars” The zoo lady says with content and examines a collar, just like the one you’re wearing. You look around and suddenly you realise that all of the dogs are wearing the same collars, just like the one’s that is around your neck and the one that’s in her hand. But if they’ve once been humans just like you, then why didn’t they try and stop her from changing you? You ask yourself. The zoo lady looks down at you and smiles. “Now your new name is Fox. You’ll learn quickly to listen to this name and not your old human name. Now I’m just going to throw away these rags that some irresponsible human have left here. Then I’m going to fix you all some food. Doesn’t that sound great everyone?” The whole dog pack starts barking and howling with joy and the zoo lady just snickers and picks up your clothes. Right at the moment when she picks up your old clothes you start to panic. Even though those clothes are useless to you now, they’re still the last proof that you’ve once been human. You let out a bark as you rush after her and start to tug and pull one of your old pant legs. Suddenly you start to feel uneasy as she starts to smell angry and you start to have second thoughts of starting this struggle. When her eyes meet yours you feel a discomforting feeling, making you want to look another way and not at her eyes. “Fox” She says with a stern voice. You freeze… you’ve lost all courage to move an even a muscle. “Fox, Sit!” She commands. You sit down on your haunches with out even thinking. “Good” She praises. “Now stay. Stay” You follow her with your eyes, but you don’t walk after her.

    A short rush of happiness fills your mind when you hear her praise, only to discover moments later how fooled you’ve been. She’s just about to throw your old clothes in a large laundry basket standing inside one of the employee’s rooms. A scent suddenly gets picked up by your nose… the smell of perfume and even though you don’t know how you know, you know it’s a woman approaching. After about a minute you actually see the woman enter the kennel. The kennel gets filled by happy dog barks and all of the dogs’ rushes to greet her. You also feel this happiness to see this woman that you’ve never met the feeling is so strong that you find yourself rushing forward to her just like everyone else. But right out of the blue you feel someone grabbing your collar. You let out a coughing sound as you’re almost strangled but the sudden yank. The zoo lady quickly lead you away, pushes you inside of a room and quickly closes the door behind you. As the door clicks behind you, you understand that you’ve been locked into the room. The room is filled with several canine toys, there’s a food and a water bowl and in the corner there’s also a pretty large dog bed.

    What do you do now?
