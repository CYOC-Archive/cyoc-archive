    Janessa's Growing Crush

    A blizzard howled outside the castle, bringing with it snow, ice, and sleet. But the crackling fireplace in Lilia's study kept the room warm and cozy despite the storm raging outside. Janessa finished tending to the fire, then sat back down on her chair and resumed her needlepoint.

    "Thank you for taking care of the fire," Lilia, who was sketching something, said.

    "It is my duty, Princess," Janessa said softly. Lilia scoffed, making Janessa smile. "Anyways, I was getting cold."

    "Yes, you still have not gotten used to our weather here. Do not worry, you will soon." Janessa nodded. Today was a Sunday, and Sundays were spent engaging in "properly ladylike activities". With the weather horrible, Lilia and her ladies-in-waiting had to resort to indoor activities. Not that Greta was actually doing her activity, embroidery. She had offered to get tea an hour ago and had yet to return. No doubt she was flirting for information from the guards again.

    "So..." Lilia started. Janessa looked up from her needlepoint. It was an activity Lilia insisted she learn, and to her surprise, Janessa enjoyed it. "Did you like the ball yesterday?" Janessa nodded. "You did?"

    "Yes. I was nervous at first, but gradually felt more comfortable, and enjoyed myself greatly."

    "Oh, wonderful!" There was a mischievous glimmer in Lilia's eye. "Would that have anything to do with the company of Sven?" Janessa felt her cheeks burn. Lilia giggled. "Yes! I knew it! I saw the two of you dancing together over and over, and I saw that look in his eyes, and..." Lilia sighed. "It was the same expression Vidar made when we danced for the first time."

    Janessa was sure her cheeks were as red as her hair at this point.

    "I told him he could pay me a visit," she said softly. Lilia let out a very un-princessy squeal of delight.

    "Perfect! Janessa, he is such a good match for you. You will adore him."

    Janessa thought to herself that she already did, somewhat. She was... weirdly looking forward to seeing him again, going on a formal courtship. Maybe even kissing. She hadn't done anything romantic or sensual since...Antyny. This...this could be good for her.

    And Sven was so delightful...

    "Sorry I took so long," Greta said, coming into the room with a tray of tea. "The kitchen was busy."

    "I rather think you were," Lilia teased. "But that is fine, as long as you do not break any hearts. After all, it is Janessa who is my little matchmaking project, not you."

    Greta grinned as she set down the tray.

    "Did I miss anything?"

    "Just Janessa confessing that Sven is going to court her," Lilia gushed. Janessa blushed again. Greta beamed at her.

    "That is most excellent news."

    It certainly was causing a warm feeling in Janessa's chest. She smiled, already looking forward to seeing Sven again.
