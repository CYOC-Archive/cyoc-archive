    It was early morning when everything within the entry chamber vanished. The AI instantly detected a number of irregularities. Large amounts of metal were present along with the "single" lifeform it had initially scanned, but that was an easy separation. Next there was smattering of biological material on the floor of the chamber. No problem. That can be added back to the transfer subject. However there was suddenly a large amount of biological material in the transfer that was not in the initial scans. In fact, the mass was more than twice initially what was scanned.

    The AI was very good at making adjustments based on irregularities. It wasn't a self-conscious intelligence. It was more akin to being one of the smarted "video game AIs" ever made. However, in the end it adjusted things based on the assumptions Shelly had programmed into the system. One assumption the code made (not explicitly, but just from how things were written) was that once the door was shut, the number of lifeforms in the chamber would remain constant.

    So an exception was thrown. The exception was then caught by another subroutine that quickly analyzed the error and ran through a checklist of possible explanations. Only one came up.

    The initial scan missed some of the biological material of the single lifeform in the chamber.

    One thing to note is that this exception occurred several thousand times each teleport. The scan wasn't instantaneous, and any living thing was made up of bits that were always moving. It was, in fact, the reason why Shelly had so much trouble doing the teleports on living things. Sometimes bits were missed, and didn't teleport, thus causing structural damage; or everything teleported, but the animal ended up being dead and still - just as if the entire body instantly halted all at once.

    Shelly had corrected for that. She made sure that tiny bugs and things like that would be noticed and excluded. Regardless of Zoey's earlier comment, she had see The Fly... or at least the remake from the 80s.

    However the AI had analyzed the blood on the floor and all of Zoey and determined those things were not the usual parasites, internal or external. So... it had to be part of the subject of the transportation. Shelly.

    That said, merging that much mass into a single organism while holding to a self-sustaining shape would be difficult and take a lot of processing. The AI continued to run for days on end, chunking data and merging things together as to fill in possible gaps made by the initial scan in a best-fit manner. Shelly would had been proud of her code. It ran without an fatal errors.

    Unfortunately, Zoey left some bloody footprints out in the hall. The remains of the janitor were found. Police were called in. The area was sectioned off as a crime scene. The chamber doors were solidly locked, but everyone who looked inside could plainly see nothing was in there. So samples were taken and facts were cross-checked with the police-officer who had confronted Zoey in her apartment. He had claimed she attacked him and that's why he discharged his weapon. Internal Affairs became convinced he was hiding something, especially when he initially claimed he didn't hit her (due to there being no blood at the apartment), and yet the bullet was found in the campus labs. The police came to realize Shelly was also missing, and they began to suspect the doc was a victim of some sort. Her car was at the lab, but she was missing. The police put the women's descriptions out. They were to be brought in for questioning.

    All the while, nobody bothered to turn off the servers. With the exception of some people who removed the test animals from the lab for the time being, nothing else in the lab was touched for days. Then the floor and door were cleaned. The hallway was mopped up. And things went back to normal.

    Roughly one month later, just before the administrators were to meet together to figure out what to do with the lab and Shelly's research, the AI finished its work.
