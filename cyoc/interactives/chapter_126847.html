    "Come on sweetie, it won't be that bad. Now get dressed and come down for breakfast."
    Katie sighed and took the clothes from her mom and. started getting dressed, her mom walked back downstairs while she got ready.
    She wore a white short sleeve blouse with a black tie tucked into a green plaid, pleated skirt with white high socks and black loafers.
    The girl rubbed her shoulder as she let some of her hair fall over her almost glowing blue, cat eyes.
    "Can I at least wear a hoodie or something for my...ears." She mumbled.
    "Honey, it doesn't matter if you're a little different, you're still a beautiful young girl." Her Mom assured.
    "I'm a freak..." Katie replied quietly, avoiding eye contact.
    "No! You are not a freak! You're my little girl and I love you!" Her Mom retorted and hugged her daughter,
    "Things will get better, I promise." She added.

    When Katie walked into the classroom she sat quietly in the back corner, still avoiding eye contact with any of the other students. Class was uneventful, the cat-girl fiddled with the hem of her skirt with her claw-like nails, just wanting the school day to be over.
