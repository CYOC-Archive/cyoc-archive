    Goldie had awoken in Sam's arms, something she was quite happily getting used to, but at the moment she was alone with her thoughts. Everyone was saying her testimony had been a huge help, and the defense had been grasping at straws trying to undermine her credibility, but the whole experience had been humiliating, and emotionally devastating. There was nothing in the world she wanted more right now than to just go back to the park and never think about any of this again. She had every intention of just hiding in the motel room, face buried under pillows until it was time to fly back, but Sam had some "big surprise" he'd left to get ready that he swore would help cheer her up.

    The phone rang, and after a bit of inner debate, she finally answered. Apparently there was a fancy car for her outside, with tinted windows. At least she wouldn't have to worry about being recognized by anyone passing by. With a shaking voice, she said she'd be down soon. Pulling herself together, she sighed, realizing she'd have to put her court clothes back on to avoid offending anyone, alien as they felt. Apparently, Sam was meeting her somewhere. She'd expected another lawyer's office, and was rather surprised when the driver delivered her to some high class restaurant. What was Sam thinking? Didn't he realize this was the last sort of place she'd want to be right now? She felt like the lowliest worm, avoiding eye contact with everyone as she was shown to her seat.

    Sam, of course, was being as kind and supportive as he could. Ordering her a fancy steak, with a game hen on the side, showering her with compliments, but it all felt a bit hollow. Eventually he stood up, and leaned over to her, no doubt planning to let her know she was making a scene. Then he pulled something out of his pocket.

    "I realize sometimes you feel like you aren't welcome anywhere," he said, "but you're always welcome in my heart. Will you marry me?"
