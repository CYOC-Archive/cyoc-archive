    added by Stark A month ago A BM Mythological Aquatic

    As the Barrier around Mermaid Island faded away, so too did the differences in the two dimensions' geographies and time-streams. Now you're pretty sure that time passes on the island at the same rate as everywhere else on Earth. The other mermaids there all believe this now too, because at night, when they look up at the sky, they see familiar stars and constellations and the occasional planet that they recognize and know, consistently from night to night.

    You've been back on the island for another month now, living your life as a merman—still the only merman, and the official King of the mermaids. Yasmin and Alex are both accorded the title of Queen, and alone among all the mermaids, Yasmin is acknowledged as your wife. (None of the other mermaids ever deign to call you Yasmin's husband, though. You get the sense that you're regarded as a piece of communal property on the island.)

    Life is good. Swimming in the sea, basking in the lagoon, lazing on the beaches. Making love in the waves to Yasmin and any other mermaid who fancies a frolic with a merman at the moment.

    When you, Yasmin, and Alex first left—disappeared, and without explanation, from the perspective of the other mermaids—everyone had simply assumed that you'd all swam off to explore the oceans or seek distant shores. After all, plenty of other mermaids did exactly that (and some of them were making waves in the worldwide media for their troubles).

    It wouldn't be long before knowledge of the existence of mermaids was common. Perhaps one day the island itself would be discovered by the wider world.

    In the meantime there are still at least twenty mermaids in total living on the island; and after your disappearance, the remaining girls waited a month and then went searching for viable fertilized eggs.

    They found six, and brought them back to the lagoon to be watched over and protected.

    When you and Yasmin look at the helpless little clutch, you both get a particular feeling about one of the eggs. That one, you decide, is definitely Yasmin's child.

    Another one seems to share a similar bond with Alex.

    More intriguing, for reasons you can't quite explain, you're sure that only three of the eggs are going to hatch into females; the other three will be males. You're going to have three daughters and three sons.

    Like the Brady Bunch. But with fins.

    And after ninety days' total gestation, the eggs one day hatch . . .
