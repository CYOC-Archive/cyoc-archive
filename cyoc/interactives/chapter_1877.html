    "Ahhh," the Old Man sighed, he had been sitting there the whole day without a single visitor, no one seemed to care about his mysterious store. Maybe he would have better luck tomorrow in another town.

    But if he could get just one visitor in his store he could have some entertainment for the evening. After all, he made sure the board game 'A Game of Change' was available again after getting it back from that last set of kids. The game, which he created after seeing the movie 'Jumanji' worked the same way as the game in the movie, but the Old Man expanded on the transformational aspect. This in turn created for a few great nights of SRU-TV (The Old Mans way of watching his (un)lucky victims, much easier than teleportation and the crystal ball thing was so out of date). But the game always seemed to take forever to make its way back to the store; it would sometimes take months before he could sell the game again.

    The game worked by transforming the players into different things while making their way toward the winning square. Whoever makes it to the winning square first gets one wish, which sometimes causes problems in itself. Plus few remember to wish to change back to their previous form, guess that happens in the joy of winning.

    Just as he was about to magically close the store for the day the Old Man heard the bells on the door!

    "Finally, a customer!"
