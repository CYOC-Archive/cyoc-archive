    Amy walked down the road rubbing away the remaining tears she had. Ruby had gone too far this time. She had bumped Amy near the end of school and her friend Sarah had stepped on it with the most obvious fake "oops" she had ever heard. Now Amy couldn't read. She didn't know why those two bullied her. It could be she was shorter than average with a slight belly and acne. It also could be she was one of the smartest people in school who always tried to help. However, due to none of the teachers being willing to help out her trust in them had waned. She knew the reason, both of those girls had rich daddies who paid the school's funding. She wished there was a way to get back at them.

    All of a sudden she felt the need to turn to the right. There she saw a decent-sized building, but she couldn't see what its sign said. However, she felt drawn to it. Once she entered it felt like she was where she needed to be. She approached one box but she couldn't make out what it said. "having trouble there?" a voice called out. Amy looked to her left in shock. While she had trouble reading without her glasses she could have sworn no one was behind the counter when she walked in. "yeah, my glasses, um, broke today" she bit her tongue from saying what happened. The man smirked and tapped on the counter. Amy then saw a stand of glasses on the counter. Once again she swore it wasn't there when she passed it. Regardless the man picked up a pair and reached out to her.

    "I don't normally give out my products but you can't buy anything if you can't see the 'price'" Amy didn't notice when the way he said that last part. Instead, she grabbed the glasses and looked them over. They looked very similar to her old pair. Putting them on suddenly everything came into focus. It was like she was looking through a high-depth TV or something. "I don't know what to say" Amy grinned. The man leaned back in his chair as he spoke "As long as you buy something else I'll be fine".

    Amy then looked at the box she had seen before. Now able to read she saw it read 'Sol's potions: everything you want/needs under the sun'. Normally Amy would scoff at anything revolving around magic but now she felt like this could be real. An odd idea began to form in her mind. 'those two need to be taught a lesson. No, not just them. The whole school. They watched as I was pushed down. Those lousy teachers allowed those brats to do whatever to me. They'll all see' she grinned as she picked up the box. It was large but oddly light enough for her to carry. She then placed it on the counter. "will this be enough" she pulled out a twenty. The man smiled as he rang her up.

    After he left the man's form shifted to reveal a tanuki. He then picked up the glasses stand and read the sign attached above them. "pity she didn't read this sign. 'desire delivery: brings your hidden desires to your life" he let out a chuckle as the store vanished. In its place, a gravel-covered lot appeared.
