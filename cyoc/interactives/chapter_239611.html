Given the odd dog vest that Austin was currently wearing, turning him into a breeding slave might not actually result in a real change. After all, the app had turned Steve into a drug addict’s “wife” – even going so far as to make Steve pregnant with the addict’s baby – yet Steve had never stopped being Steve.

    *BREEDING SLAVE*

    “Yes, I know…” Steve muttered under his breath. The presence in the car was suffocating and Steve got the distinct feeling that if he didn’t comply, and promptly, that Steve might end up getting added to Austin’s bitch harem.

    “Austin is an anthro GSD in the community center.”

    So, what should he change? “is an” and “in the” were both obvious choices, but that would result in the app getting a lot of discretion in how it cleaned up the syntax. Could he add the words? No… this new version of the app did allow you to add or subtract words, but only one at a time. No wait, that was the solution – he could add slave first and breeding after since neither would really change the meaning of the sentence.

    Steve selected the empty space between “GSD” and “in”, putting the word “slave” in between them.

    Hitting submit, Steve looked back up just in time to see the circle of players part, giving him a full view of the now standing Austin. Looking the German Shepherd up and down, Steve didn’t see any obvious changes to the anthro’s body.

Then he realized what was going on – it was dark outside, so the glass was acting like a mirror to anyone inside of the community center. Austin was using that to examine the biology of his new body.

Austin’s vest had been replaced with a dog collar that had a small metal tag on the front. That was good, Steve thought, since it didn’t look like Austin was acting like a slave. As it had done with Steve earlier, the app had left Austin’s mind intact and whatever backstory the app had written for him was completely pointless in a world where no one had any awareness of what it was.
