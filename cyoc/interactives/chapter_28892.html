    "Hey, Matt," Lucy piped up, as Eve led them to the mall where she'd found the shop, "Michelle lost her magic when the game ended, but...what about you? Leprechauns are magical creatures, right?"

    Matt shrugged as he rode on Erica's shoulder. "I dunno. I'm not sure I'm technically a leprechaun anymore; I certainly don't look the way I did. And that gold-sense I had is gone, so I guess I'm just the World's Smallest Man now. Besides, isn't it supposed to be that to get a wish from a leprechaun, you have to find his pot of gold? I don't have one."

    Lucy sighed. "I suppose so. Hey, speaking of gold, I bet this would fetch a pretty penny." She removed from her head the silver crown that had appeared when she'd first become a harpy. "I suppose that's one good thing about my clothes having stayed the same."

    Alex gasped. "A crown!? No fair, Mo-uh, Lucy!" She began to mutter to herself, irritated at the persistent mistake.

    "Don't take it too hard," Lucy advised. "I'm constantly having to remind myself that you and Marcia aren't really my daughters. It's irritating, but on the other hand I'm glad we didn't receive any significant mental changes." She glanced at Eve and Sam. "Well, any that stuck, anyway. All I'm saying is, call me whatever you want, and don't feel bad about making that mistake."

    Alex shrugged. "I'm definitely waiting until we find out if we can change back before I consider calling you 'mom.'"

    Lucy nodded. It was strange; only a few short hours ago, Alex had been a friend and they'd both been teenage guys. Now, though she'd only recently become a teenage girl, and Alex had even more recently become her daughter, Lucy felt what she knew must be mother-love for the little girl walking next to her. It was more than just the adoration any stranger might feel towards a cute little girl (for Alex was cute, despite her still-masculine gait and embarrassed scowl.) It was more even than the pity a friend might feel to see a child in such distress. She felt it too for Marcia, the quiet child clinging to her side, who'd barely said a word since her transformation into a little harpy-girl, but who'd hardly been away from her since. It was a hundred feelings at once: pride in her children, a burning desire for them to succeed in life, a willingness to give even her own life for their safety, a readiness to comfort them in their sorrow, and a powerful, deep-seated love for her children, the fruit of her own body.

    They were, weren't they? Lucy knew that she'd never been pregnant, let alone with the children who had, mere hours ago, been friends her same age, but she knew also that in this new reality, Alex and Marcia had come about as the result of an accidental, experimental pregnancy on the part of the nine-year-old girl who'd grown into her. Though she couldn't remember the actual events, Lucy found that she knew what it all felt like: the growing weight in her abdomen (which seemed, thankfully, to have returned to its original size after her pregnancy,) the awe as she felt the first kicks inside her, the stabbing of the birth pangs, the tender warmth of holding the new life she'd brought into the world, the feeling of nursing the fruit of her womb, the babies suckling at her breasts, breasts which had formed during her pregnancy, long before any other girls her age had them, the soft cries of her girls as she rocked them to sleep for the first time, the wondrous joy of choosing their names, Alexis Bridget Connor and Marcia Annette Connor.

    It was all there in the recesses of her mind, and her rising maternal instinct was linking it with the Alex and Marcia who now walked beside her. She knew that this was probably a minor mental change thanks to the game, and she knew that if there was a way to change back she'd lose them, but in the meantime, or if they couldn't find a way back, being a girl wouldn't be so bad if it meant she could feel this for someone. Or two someones, as it was. Maybe, she thought, I could even make it three or for, if I ever met a nice... She stopped there; she might have her newfound maternal instincts, but she wasn't quite ready to contemplate that yet.

    Lucy was snapped out of her reverie when Eve spoke. "Well," she said, as they strode through the mall, "it should be...right over here..." She took a corner and stopped abruptly in front of a donut shop. "Oh no," she whispered.

    "What?" Michelle queried. "Where's the shop?"

    "Here," Eve said. "It was here. Oh no..."

    "Are you sure?" Matt asked. She nodded. "I made special note of it, because I was expecting the game might be stupid and I'd want a refund. It was here. It's just not now."

    "It's one of those shops, isn't it?" Erica murmured. None of them knew where it had originated, but they all knew the cliche of the mysteriously appearing and disappearing magic shop.

    They all stared at the donut shop for a long minute before Marcia and Alex, not caring whether it made them seem like little girls or not, burst into tears. Lucy knelt down and gathered them in her wings, trying to comfort them, as the others tried to figure out what to do.
