    While they waited for the staff to get ready, Carl and Wendy were now allowed to talk to each other except for
    anything game related, which was verboten.

    "I missed you so much during the isolation" said Carl. "You wouldn't believe the things I had to do to get

    "Yeah" agreed Carl. "It's just that I haven't used my vocal cords in so long. They needed some exercise."

    "Save it for the show, ladies. Please."

    Despite his hulking appearance, he had a soft and gentle voice, and Carl and Wendy reciprocated his request.

    Listed on the application for both twins were Wendy's parents, June and Julius, who had been flown out to Los
    Angeles to do a thirty-second bit where Carly and Wendy officially get their "key"s to enter the Big Brother

    show on TV, corny as it was, and she was honestly psyched to be doing it now for real. Once her folks got off
    the plane and were driven to meet her and Carl, Wendy wished for her parents to remember her being in her
    early twenties instead of her late thirties like she been but a month before, and that she had a twin sister
    named Carly. After a quick change of clothing for the girls, all four were moved to a small house in South
    Pasadena(the same location where the first Halloween movie was filmed), and given not quite a script, but a
    set of cues. Carly and Wendy were both asked to be super-enthusiastic about going on Big Brother and for their
    parents to be supportive. The doorbell would ring, and two envelopes stuffed with an oversized plastic key
    with their names on it would be given to them by a member of the production. Wendy's wish had made her parents
    supportive of her and Carl's endeavor anyway, so it was all quite redundant, but all four people put on a good
    show for the cameras and were done with it faster than they had realized. Carl had never quite gotten along
    with Wendy's parents, being seriously annoyed with his nagging mother-in-law(insert cliched joke here), and
    somewhat intimidated by her demanding father, but their new memories of Carly made them affectionate and
    loving towards their temporary new daughter. Play-acting was kind of fun, Carl had to admit, and he was at
    least a little used to Carly's female proportions now, so it wasn't as traumatizing as it would have been when
    Wendy first turned him into her sister.

    Jon showed up on set after the key segment, and asked Wendy and Carly how things were going.

    "Great!" shouted Wendy.

    "Yeah, great" answered Carly, not amused.

    Jon looked over at Carly and smiled.

    He introduced himself to June and Julius, and the five of them had a late lunch together. Carl had to remember

    wish all the unwanted weight away. The parents were skeptical of this man and his intentions towards their
    daughters, but he managed to ooze his way out of the awkwardness, the slick guy that he was. Wendy asked if
    all the contestants had been treated this well, and Jon said "Nope. Only you two. You two caught my eye."

    This made both Carl and Wendy somewhat suspicious as well as curious, but they brushed it off once the
    waitress came back with their orders.

    Once lunch was through, June and Julius were given a fond farewell by Wendy and Carl(y) and given two checks
