    "It worked! It worked! I am a genius!"

    Lily looked around the room. It looked like she was in some kind of lab straight out of a bad horror movie from the 1930s. Strange machine were against the walls, lined with blinking buttons and long, heavy levers. A large tesla coil sparked electricity across the room to another coil. In the corner of the room Lily saw the man jumping up and down with excitement, hugging a small troll-like man.

    "It's alive! It's alive!" he screamed with excitement.

She could swear that she was almost six feet tall, but it was hard to tell in the ridiculous heels she was wearing.

    "Why, just what you asked, my dear. You wanted a new body and a new life. And I just happened to have a new body available and waiting for a volunteer to use it. Quite fortunate I would say. For both of us," said the man.

    Lily stared at the mirror in shock. The man had swapped her brain into this body - that was crazy, impossible..... Lily saw a strange tag hanging behind her ear. Without thinking she tugged on it. Her skin began to painless peel, slowly at first, then with more speed as she continued to pull. The large flap of skin sagged down off her face, revealing a hard metal chassis underneath.

    "What have you done to me?! Where is my body?" she practically screamed in a metallic screech.

    "Easy, easy. It's perfectly safe, I assure you. We are taking good care of it. Just calm down," said the man, pointing to another corner of the lab.

    Lily turned and saw her old body laying on another table, hooked up to several machines. She could see with some relief that the machines were helping it to breathe, so it must have still been alive. But that did little to quell her anger.

    "Change me back! Right now!" she yelled, still in her French accent.

    "I am afraid it is not that simple," said the man, furiously nodding his head and trying to signal for help from Igor. "You see, the process is quite complicated and it takes time for the body to recover from the transfer. It might be dangerous to try to reverse it prematurely...."

It's just well..... my robots have been acting a bit screwy. They follow commands but lack common sense. So I decided to give them a little help from a human brain - yours. Don't worry - this is just temporary. As soon as the computer learns a few basic reasoning skills from you, I will transfer your consciousness back to your old body. That is, if you want me to do so. I mean, your old body is nice, but I daresay this one is quite an improvement. I would be happy to let you keep it as compensation after the experiment is over. So, what do you say? Do we have a deal?"

    "Not really, " said the man. "Maid, begin cleaning procedure alpha..."
