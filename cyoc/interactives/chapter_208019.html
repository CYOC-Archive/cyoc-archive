    Colin started noticing it as soon as he left the field. As he was chatting with Eve about her interests, her favorite books, her family and especially Snow, his saw out the corner of his eye as one of the electronic roadside signs glitched out, the text within distorting and shifting to display a single word: "Colin."

    "Huh." he pondered but didn't pay it any mind.

    That got harder as all along the road it began to spread, first the road signs, then advertisements, every monitor he passed shifting to display his name. Even Eve took notice.

    "Look Uncle Colin, that's your name! Are you famous?" she asked, eyes shining.

    "Eh... no. Must be some weird glitch." He responded.

    They arrived at Eve's house and she waved him goodbye. For a split second he thought about taking her all the way, but he didn't want to risk meeting Snow's sister as appealing as the thought was. To take his mind off of things he put on the radio, hoping some nice tunes would distract him.

    "Somebody's Watching Me" began playing. Colin wasn't a huge fan of the song, so he switched the channel. The next channel was playing "I See You." Switch. "In My Mind." Switch. "Thinking Of You." Switch. Switch. Switch. "Always on My Mind." "Shape of You." "My Beloved." Whatever channel he listened to, each of them was playing music around similar themes. Even the talk channel had a documentary going on about Cold War espionage and spycraft. Colin tried to switch the channel but the radio wasn't working, switching back to previous channels, even overlapping the signals so he was drowned out in a cacophony of weirdly directed love songs. He was so distracted that he didn't notice his car turning onto the oncoming lane before a truck horn snapped him out of it seconds before collision. Before he could even turn the wheel the car acted by itself, turning him off the oncoming lane as he breathed heavily, panicking over the near-death experience he just had. Something was definitely up.

    When he arrived back home, Sam, Lucy and Marinette were up front speaking heatedly with a bunch of deliverymen hauling around packages of different sizes. Colin's jaw dropped as he saw that they contained everything from chocolates and flowers to the latest gaming consoles, comics, coffee makers and even a jacuzzi.

    "Colin, thank goodness you're here." Lucy rushed to him. "These guys just started hauling all this crap here!"

    "What? Di... did someone order all of this?"

    "Not that anyone... HNGH! No one atleast confesses." Sam grunted, pushing her way past the dozens of boxes that were starting to block out their doorway.

    "What is all this? We didn't order anything, and especially not... this." Colin said to the deliverymen and gestured out to everything, especially the jacuzzi. The men shrugged.

    "Beats us. But all of this was put in order just an hour ago to one Colin Greys. Flagged as "Ultra-Priority Delivery." All paid for. Sign here please." A foreman approached Colin with a tablet.

    Thirty minutes later the boxes had been shipped, the ones they wanted to keep brought in and the rest were left outside awaiting use, returns or selling. Angela was already working on tracing and handling the packages. The rest were gathered in the living room.

    "Clearly Colin has a secret admirer." Jessie stated.

    "Gee, thanks, Captain Obvious. But who'd be willing to spend that much on him?" Lucy pondered.

    "Whoever it is, they clearly have some problems. We don't have room for all this crap! Who do you think could be behind this. Colin?" Sam asked.

    Colin was deep in thought. The day just kept getting weirder and weirder. He stared at Angela working furiously at her laptop, her back facing the others as her other hand was accompanied by a cellphone she was currently talking to.

    "Oh yeah. Find anything, Angela?" Sam called out.

    The businesswoman turned around and covered the speaker of her phone with the other hand.

    "Nothing at all. The order came in out of nowhere, from someone with access to high priority deliveries. Not too many of those, but apparently none of them have made such orders. It's like a ghost delivered those packages to us.

    "A ghost, huh..." Colin thought, the mystery nagging at his mind.
