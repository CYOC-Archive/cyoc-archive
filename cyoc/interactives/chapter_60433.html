    Holding the box up to her face, Rylene whispered to Vinnie, "Now how do you feel, knowing how much your

    The basketball hoop that had hung over the garage was gone. She sighed, and went inside.

    "Hello, Mom? Dad?" Rylene called, but no one answered. She was alone in the home.

    She went upstairs to her bedroom. Except it wasn't her bedroom. It was her brother Todd's bedroom. He wasn't
    there. That meant she had his bedroom, she frowned and walked across the hall. The bedroom door was closed,

    She chuckled and opened the door. Rylene blinked temporarily blinded by all the hot pink. "Oh, I can't believe
    everything's so pink!" she lamented as she went to examine her room. It was smaller than her old room, but it
    had a walk-in closet and its own bath. It was perfect for a girl.

    "I should just wish things back to normal," Rylene said, "But I can't."

    She set the package down and lay down on her soft pink flowery bed. Even the ceiling was pink. She closed her
    eyes. The doorbell rang.
