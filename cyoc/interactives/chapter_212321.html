A lot of it was fairly low-level stuff, and to Richard, something that he might have thought would be fairly typical for any local news station. He'd thought of an escape plan, supposed to begin early yesterday morning, and ended up in an argument with Rose over that idea. The result of that led to Rose essentially playing to his own bravado and essentially daring him to go out and make contact with a were, to actually LEARN something. He killed the offending animal and saved Rose, but she had to go to the hospital and that left him feeling a bit ashamed. He looked down on that as he then began to shave and feeling the blade cut the coarse stubble from his face. He'd let himself get pulled by new instincts and nearly lost Rose as a result, and he felt lucky that Rose did live.

And the water was warm, and that let him think over things that came from the previous night before he and Rose went to meet Elijah Hunter to essentially begin Richard's training with regard to running one of the smaller pride units within the Moon Lake werelion community, with Rose at his side and with Shelia likely to be part of his and Rose's pride for the time being. For that was the thing he didn't want to say openly to Rose at the present time and wanted to wait until things were more appropriate.

So far as he knew, Rachel had been with Melissa Jenkins, an old friend from Michigan, but other than finding that friend as a werecow, he hadn't found Rachel. He then followed Rachel's trail, and it ultimately led him to Tachibana’s, right next to his family's new home.     Frank was rather quiet as Rose talked with Rob in that morning. It reflected a large amount of the strangeness that had hit the Van Direstag family after moving to Moon Lake and even with some of the struggles that had been there, how they'd ultimately accepted things. For a lot of it related to many of the challenges that had been there for the family before the move and those things only added to what they were dealing with now. For Frank knew that Richard and Rose Van Direstag were good parents and would do almost anything for them. But there were always some things that the Van Direstag kids would have some interest in that would go beyond just what was expected from them. It was this sort of thing that led to Rachel's at times rebellious and Rob getting adventurous and forward at times.

    Some of it, Frank knew was more a result of his own growth and development over the years. He'd always been decidedly average in many regards and had never been able to stand out. He played a bit of sports in high school, mostly to try and show some ability, and while he got plenty of family support, he never really was able to stand out and that meant that colleges weren't going to be knocking for a sports scholarship. He was never dumb, but neither was he always the smartest person around and that had ultimately held him back in a way. He graduated from the Ann Arbor school district, but his grades, ACT, and SAT scores were only just good enough to qualify for acceptance into most colleges, and it made for a rather trying college search.

    Frank even remembered apologizing to Richard for not being "good enough." The lecture Frank then got on that he WAS good enough was as big as the search, and then they found Moon Lake College online and found that they were willing to offer scholarships to anyone willing to come across the country to attend. It was a lucky break and Frank and Richard even got to tour the college the previous summer before his senior year in high school, and again when budget issues meant the Van Direstag family wouldn't be able to move before the school year started, the college even accepted having him start with a more online course load, and Frank did his best to pass those classes. That then brought the Van Direstags to Moon Lake. And it was a very different place.

    Frank had been there when Samantha Twist had provided the message and proof of Moon Lake being populated mostly by weres. It was a surprise, even to Frank, but because Samantha had never attacked anyone, and he didn't have some other place to go, Frank had been more open to at least learning something. And while he focused on his study material online, he didn't miss some of the other things going on. Rob and Rachel had definitely caught on to some things, even though they had been at school when the "welcome to Moon Lake" was made, and Frank also knew that Rachel was smart enough to not be fooled by any blatant lie. That was in part why Frank had urged his mother to be firm with getting Richard to not carry through his "escape plan," though going out into the woods, and leading to what ultimately lead to both Van Direstag parents becoming werelions wasn't what Frank thought would happen, though to a degree, he couldn't argue with the results in that fleeing or not learning anything was no longer an issue.

    But as he ate breakfast and Rose talked with Rob, Frank could also see where those old issues remained. For it seemed that Rob and Rachel had come across things that made them even more interested in Moon Lake than before. For Rachel it was a reunion with an old friend from Michigan. For Rob, it appeared to be meeting some people that were a bit more forward at things than he'd had experience with in Michigan. It was something that actually made Frank a bit happy that they had friends or were making friends, as he didn't think his little brother was really the sort to be a problem to people. Now, they had to work through those last things and Frank had things of his own to work through, namely turning his computer in and transitioning into the in-person classes. And Frank thought on that even as he finished breakfast and finished getting ready.

    He was only coming down with his things and the computer to turn in as he saw his father shut the front door and hearing Rachel and Rob say their goodbyes as they headed out to the high school.

    "I'm ready to go day, if you are," Frank said to his father and was glad to see that Richard seemed to be in a reasonably good mood after everything.

    "I am, let me go get the keys," Richard answered him, "and your mother?"

    "I think she was still getting dressed," Frank answered, "though the door was shut, so I didn't barge in."

    Richard nodded and he moved off, leaving Frank just in front of the front door. Richard returned a few moments later and the two then made their way down one hallway that led into the attached garage. Frank was able to easily set his things in the front seat's floor area while then climbing into the front seat himself. While he did that, he could hear his father lifting up the garage door and then coming back to the driver's side of the van. From there, they were soon backing out after Richard started the vehicle.

    "Your mother and I will be having some work today in setting up our store, so you may have to walk home when you're done with classes," Richard told him, "Can you handle that?"

    "I'm pretty sure dad, and I have everything I need for the day," Frank answered.

    "Good... though if ANYTHING comes up, I'm sure they'll have a payphone on the campus somewhere," Richard answered, "call the house if you need to."

    Frank nodded, though he didn't think anything like that would come up. He'd been managing his grades fairly well so far; the real challenge would be more in adjusting from the online versions of the courses to their in-person versions. That wouldn't be too hard. The biggest thing that actually stuck in his mind was the run in he'd had at the college bookstore while getting his books for the semester. There was a young woman there who worked at the store, though he couldn't say how old she actually was, who seemed to be stressed by something. Frank didn't know what it was, but a part of him just wanted to help, as she seemed like a good person and shouldn't be under threat. Though, it wasn't likely that he'd see her again, regardless, he hoped she was alright and whatever had been the trouble, it was over. His thoughts drifted on that as they rode along, and it let him zone out for a few moments.

    "Frank!" came his father's voice snapping him out of it.

    That got Frank to shake his head and realize they were parked outside the administration building again, and it was about fourteen minutes to eight AM.

    "We're here..." Richard said slowly.

    "Sorry... I was just... thinking on some things," Frank said slowly.

    "We can talk about that later," Richard said with a small smile, "will you need me for anything?"

    "No, but thank you," Frank said as he unbuckled himself and got out. He collected his own bookbag the laptop he'd been assigned before the Van Direstags could arrive in Moon Lake. "I'll see you at the end of the day."

    Richard nodded and watched as Frank shut the door and then made his way up to the administration building.
