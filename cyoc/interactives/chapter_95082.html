    "You idiot! You fool! Don't you ever listen to anybody?" shouted Mika, the years of biting his tongue and keeping quiet before his master suddenly over. "Can't you see that he wants to be a guy? Can't you see that I love him? I don't want a wife because I want a man!"

    "Silence both of you!" shouted the Master. "You'll marry the girl and like it!"

    "No" said Tobi. "He won't." She waved her hand, and Luka was Luka again.

    "Enough of this nonsense!" the master turned to the barbarian and prepared another spell. Tobi put her hands to the wizard's back and an expression of agony transfixed her.

    The Archmage turned around and tried to pull away. "What are you doing?" he screamed.

    "If you can't use your power properly." Tobi said through gritted teeth, "You can't be trusted with it."

    With a crack loud enough to be heard for a mile, even over the chitterings of the macaques and the mutterings of the peasant women and others that the Master's magic had saved from the burning tree, the Master pulled himself away from Tobi, then slumped exhausted to the ground.

    Tobi lay beside him in a dead faint.

    "That bastard." muttered Luka, looking down at the unconscious wizard, his hand straying to the hilt of his sword.

    "Master, master!" wailed Mika.
