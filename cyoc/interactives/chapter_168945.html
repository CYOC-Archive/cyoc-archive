    Oliver nodded his head, "Uh huh...but can I make one more wish first before my nap?" he asked his kangaroo friend/caretaker. Kristi thought it over in her head and decided to allow it. "Okay fine but just one sweetie and then its off to bed for you" she said kindly. Happy to have her approval he then fought off his increasing sleepiness as he tried to think of what to wish for.
    Meanwhile Jeeves, Billy-Bob, Johnny and Ricky crowded around their young toddler friend, eager to hear what he was going to come up with. Looking at his magically brought to life stuffed animal friends Oliver realized just how great his life was at the moment, he was a toddler in diapers with no adult responsibilities and five people who would love, play and take care of him no matter what. Thinking it selfish to keep such a wonderful life all to himself. The idea for a big wish immediately popped into his head, one that if he had the energy would have made him jump up and down in excitement.
    "Okay I got it!" he grinned as everyone gathered in close. Letting out a big yawn Oliver drew in a deep breath, fighting to stay awake as he announced in as loud and as clear a tone as he could muster, "I wish that everyone in the world was between the ages of three and ten years old and had stuffed animal friends like me that would protect and care for them!" He made the low end of the regression three years old so that a few lucky people could get the chance to not be potty trained and get to wear diapers again.
    Unable to stay awake any longer Oliver succumbed to sleep within seconds of making his wish, barely making out a ding sound as a pair of fuzzy arms grabbed and lifted him up. Some time later he woke up to find himself lying in his bed, still four years old and still padded. Speaking of which he quickly checked his diaper and found it to be quite damp meaning he had peed in it at some point during his nap. Oliver wasn't that surprised as he remembered having nighttime accidents at this age. Thinking back to what he did last before falling asleep he recalled the wish he made. Eager to find out if it worked Oliver climbed down from his bed when a familiar witch kangaroo walked (or rather hopped) into the room just as his feet touched the floor and he turned around. "Looks my little joey is awake, and apparently needs another change" she said noticing the yellowed front of his pull-up. Waving her paw he was once again surrounded by neon green sparkles and lifted into the air. Oliver smiled as his soggy diaper was removed and disposed of, giggling and kicking his legs as the wipes that appeared assaulted his crotch. His smile widened as baby powder quickly followed and he was dressed in a fresh dry pair of padding. The magical change completed he was levitated over into Kristi's waiting arms. "Thanks for the change" he chirped to which she responded, "You're welcome sweetie."
    "So did the wish I made before my nap work?" Oliver immediately asked. "It sure did little Olly. We watched the news shortly after you fell asleep and everyone in the world is regressed with stuffed animal companions to look after them, just like you" she replied before bopping him playfully on the nose. "Cool, so that means everyone is basically a kid or a toddler now." "That is correct but you should know that also includes your parents" Kristi reminded. "That's okay, they're still not home yet so we got some time before they return." Kristi's warm smile turned into a frown, "Actually they came home just a half hour ago and are downstairs playing with their new friends. Jeeves, Billy-Bob, Johnny and Ricky are all down there too keeping an eye on them" she revealed as Oliver's eyes considerably widened. "T-They're here" he stuttered at the news. "Yes and they wanted to see you the moment you woke up from your nap. Now I know you're scared sweetheart but your mom and dad love you and that hasn't changed just because you've all gotten younger" Kristi assured her young charge. "Shall we go meet them? I promise to be right by your side." Feeling better about confronting his regressed parents with the witch kangaroo being there with him Oliver nodded his head. "That's my brave boy" Kristi praised before carrying the toddler boy down the stairs. As they neared the living room where all sorts of childish laughter and shouting could be heard he mentally prepared himself for what he was about to see.
