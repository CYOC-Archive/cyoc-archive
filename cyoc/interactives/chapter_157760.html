    Now the thing was "old Amanda 333" was a rather ancient small
    farm tractor built by a mysteriously hard to trace manufacturer
    named Amanda International Manufacturing and Equipment
    Enterprise (AIMEE )
    half a century earlier.
    Basically Farmer Brown had tried to contact AIMEE many a time
    needing a rebuilt starter motor,glow plug,fuel pump gasket kit,etc
    to no avail.
    It appeared AIMEE had gone bellyup decades earlier,which
    would have made Amanda 333 and her parts of little
    value beyond melting down for scrap.

    Had that occurred I'm sure the flesh and blood Amanda would have been in serious trouble in that arc furnace.

    Farmer Brown spent the next few days considering this
    Final Option,since the Saloon had launched a legal
    proceeding against him.The farms insurance only covered
    issues that occurred on the farm,not run away tractors
    elsewhere.

    So Chuck was desperate and had realized even selling old Amanda for scrap wasn't going to help him.

    It had been his daughter who suggested he try the
    mysterious cell phones SEARCH function one last time
    for AIMEE.

    Maybe there was company coverage on their farm machinery
    that Chuck could use.

    So Farmer Brown flipped open the cell phone and
    was most pleasantly surprised to discover it had a
    app
    not for tf themed stuff
    but for repair and upgrades of AIMEE products,which
    though it surprised Chuck to suddenly have AIMEE
    suddenly be findable again
    was a beginning.
    So Farmer Brown went through the various AIMEE
    product lines till he came across the
    Amanda 333 farm tractor line,which according to the app had been manufactured in town from 1939 to 1955.

    Farmer Brown was a bit surprised on that bit of information
    but was more interested in the repair and upgrade
    options available for the Amanda 333 models.

    According to the app
    for $500 Chuck could get a Amanda 333 repaired
    and for $5,000 he could get a Amanda 333 upgraded to the
    latest model
    a heavenly looking big machine called the
    Amanda 3100
    complete with artificial intelligence self driving,voice
    interface,nifty 750 horsepower dievel electric engine,4 wheel
    drive,etc all the bells and whistles.

    Well Chuck didn't really have a spare $5,000
    but he was also savvy enough to realize AIMEE was offering
    him a $100,000 to $125,000 top of the line farm tractor for
    $5,000.

    So he raided his kids education accounts ,wired the money
    like the app told him to
    and then following instructions on the cell phone
    marched outside back to the broken down tractor and
    pushed in the various Product Key Codes he was told to type
    in.

    As he and his two ticked off children watched in
    astonishment the dirty old wreck of a Amanda 333
    glowed intensely and began to shift ,grow in size and alter
    till where it had stood now sat a nifty new paint job fresh
    big powerful Amanda 3100,its engine purring away
    and its computerized systems booting up.
    Then came the feminine sounding voice from the huge farm
    tractors speakers.....
