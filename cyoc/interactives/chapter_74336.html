    Some time in the 22nd century:

    In the far future, the world was in the midst of a chaotic war between humanity and extraterrestrial forces
    from outside our solar system. As the years drew on the alien forces were gaining the upper hand with their
    biological weapons, but the humans were able to hold them back for a long time with the use of cybernetics,
    machines, and superior tactics. With the aliens nearing victory, the director of Earth’s military defenses,
    with the approval of the many governments, chose to initiate “Directive Armageddon”, a program where a series
    of drones were launched into orbit, and launched a destructive series of bombings that destroyed everything in
    orbit, including the invaders. While that happened, those same satelites bombarded the planet’s surface
    destroying nearly everything on earth. With the aliens defeated, and Earth rendered sterilized of a majority
    of sentient life, the remainder of the human race, scattered all over the world, is left to start over.

    Having emerged from protective bunkers that shielded them from the bombardment, people have already begun to
    rebuild and repopulate the planet. One part of the world where Humanity has managed to rebuild is at what is
    known as the United States East Coast, where a newly established American government has prospered for some
    time now. In Japan, citizens have managed to rebuild Tokyo, and have begun to develop highly advanced
    technology with computers and cybernetics.

    Unfortunately, some parts of the world have been dramatically changed due to Directive Armageddon. In the
    South Midwestern U.S. and most of Africa and South America, where there’s nothing but a sandy desert, people
    are forced to live a nomadic life as they go from one place to another. Europe, Australia, and Asia has been
    engulfed in chaos as civil wars have broken out in several countries, anarchy has reigned in others, criminal
    organizations have obtained dangerous bioweapons from the war; and then there are the monsters. Some parts of
    the world have even become very dangerous for humans in general. Many of the chaotic regions regions are
    completely flooded, while others have been buried in deserts and radiation. Another problem is the fallout of
    the alien invasion itself. Members of the newly reformed U.S. military have gone to different parts of the
    world to discover that some of the alien bio-weapons have survived, or have mutated some of the indigenous
    life forms in some way. In other words, the survivors of mankind now have to deal with creatures like
    werewolves, nagas, dragons, and even zombies. In some cases there are people being turned into said creatures.
    Other reports indicate sightings of sentient robots patrolling the deserts in Midwestern U.S.A., bearing
    markings of what used to be the Earth Defense Network.

    As Mankind continues to rebuild, we take a look at the lives of certain survivors of the war, and witness the
    decisions they make, and how their experiences make them different people in more ways than one.
