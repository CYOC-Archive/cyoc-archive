    Influenced by Anya's plans, Bo decided to have her group of sisters go after their previous loved ones as well. Even though they were no longer male, the only thing that this group of ten had were some muscle, scars, and marks from their previous lives. They also seemed more rough in attitude compared to the other recruits.

    "Hey Bob, I mean Bo, what's the plan?" asked an older dullahan. Her hair was gray but she appeared only slightly older than Aesnat. Then again, converting human age with dullahan age along with mentality can mess up some things.

    "I have come to the conclusion that you are to find houses that suits you all the most and turn any human there into a dullahan. Don't give a shit on what they say or who they are. They will be our sisters whether they like it or not. It is for the best," Bo said.

    "Yeah!" shouted the dullahans.

    "Should be easy as taking candy from a baby. Though it feels off to actually do it," said a dullahan with red hair. "Huh..."

    "Is something wrong, Robin?" Bo asked.

    "Never mind that, mate," Robin said as she suddenly has a headache. The group following suit as they had an image of a man with red hair talking about having standards of not attacking children. All while explaining the importance of children.

    "Argh! Forget about it!" Bo said. "March!"

    As the dullahans ravage a housing area that felt familiar to them, they found themselves surrounded by a big group of dullahans led by Anya. Bo could only express disappointment and looked over his sisters as if to say that he can't believe that they used to live here too.

    "Ah, Bo. I must warn you that some of the people here are armed with gold. There are others that escaped as well," Anya said.

    "Very well," Bo said. "I thank you sister while I apologize in getting in the way of your work."

    "Aren't you a pretty thing?"

    Bo saw a dullahan with shaggy blond hair go up to another dullahan with brown hair with a ponytail. The shaggy dullahan seemed attracted to the newly recruited dullahan with the new one blushing. Bo would had intervened but then Robin held her back.

    "I'm a bit curious as to where this goes," Robin said.

    "I'm still remembering a name to introduce myself," said the brown haired dullahan.

    "Me too. But I felt that you work as a Selena," said the shaggy haired dullahan.

    "I see you as a Cypher," Selena said.

    "Bull! I am a woman but if you insist," Cypher said. "I'll think of a more fitting name later."

    "Besides, we know you had something with Delia. We just don't know what," the elder dullahan said.

    "Silence, Garth," Bo said. "Oh... is that your name though?"

    "Hmm, it feels like it," the elder dullahan said. "Very well. I am Garth!"

    "Perhaps I made the right move here," Anya said as the rough dullahans then follow suit to reunite with ones that were possibly their loved ones in the past. "Those that lack any familiarity, follow me and Bo! Bo clearly needs to fulfill the quota somehow."

    "Sister!" Bo whined.

    Anya and Bo rode off as only Robin following suit. The funny thing was that even Garth had a dullahan that attracted her and it was the dullahan that was previously Mary's grandmother.

    "I feel so much relief seeing you for some reason," Garth said to a dullahan with medium gray hair like hers.

    "Same can be said with I," said the dullahan.

    "Mother?" asked a girl.

    "It will be fine. The man was a hero back in his day. I bet he at least put up a fight," the mother said. "Besides, we also got this."
