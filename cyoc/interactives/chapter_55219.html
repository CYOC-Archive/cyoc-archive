    Ulysses found Sarah sorting several items in one of the supply closets in medical wing, mostly bandages and excess things. With no classes scheduled for today, as a result of the wounded needing the attention of the Pack's doctors and any available helpers. He didn't really know where Mary and the other cubs were, but he didn't know what to do if he were to find her. He and the other teen wolves had heard the cries of the quadruplets at the loss of their mothers.

    Unfortunately for him, he couldn't think of anything to cheer up them up, which part of him felt he had to do. Mary was cute, for a leoman cub and Brian had on occasion trusted him to watch his daughters. This led to some "layered" levels of baby-sitting. Mary watching Alice, Abbey, and Samantha and he watching her and her half-sisters.

    So that sent the purple furred male in search of the she-wolf that had become his adoptive big sister while his parents were off on their mission to enlarge the Pack's territory.

    "Do you need any help?" Ulysses asked as Sarah pulled down several bandages that Fran had requested for those wounds that would need to have the dressings changed. The broken bones, protected by casts couldn't be changed, but there were a few open wounds that would require changes in bandages.

    "Oh, Ulysses," Sarah gasped, "Of course, thank you."

    She then handed him some of the bandages she'd been gathering. It was then that she noticed the look on his face.

    "Is something wrong Kiddo?" Sarah asked, "Maxine and Robyn being too rough on you again?"

    Ulysses shook his head. The pink and magenta wolves were pretty cute for females, even if some of their antics made him nervous.

    "No, this has to do with Mary and her sisters," Ulysses spoke weakly.

    "You can't bring their mothers back, Kiddo," Sarah told him.

    "No not bringing them back," Ulysses answered, "I'm talking about cheering them up."

    "I'm not sure there is anything that can be said or done to cheer them up," Sarah answered, "death is always hard to take. It will take time for them to heal. Eventually they'll be the same happy and cheerful cubs they were before, but it will take time. My best advice is that you just tell them that you are there for them, and that you will always support them."

    "But what should I say?" Ulysses asked.

    "That is up to you, Kiddo," Sarah said to him, "just make sure it comes from the heart. And make sure not to say anything offensive."

    "I'd never offend them."

    Marie found herself wandering through the Vatican almost absentmindedly pondering the problems she faced. She had a rare off day from various Inquisition missions and she was using the time to think over her problems.

    "The witches could just turn me into a toadstool from a distance," Marie said aloud, although as quietly as she could manage, "I have nothing to use that would protect me or my allies... If the Inquisition accepted the mission, they would provide the relics needed for such a fight... but even there that wouldn't work. Every church claims to have fragments of the True Cross, and if all the splinters they've gathered were put together, they'd have enough wood to make more then one cross..."

    Marie then sighed and glanced a fountain.

    "There has to be something that can be done to protect us, but what?"

    She sighed again and looked out over the courtyard. She soon noticed a figure making his way across the the far end of the courtyard. The figure wore the robes of a monk, though he was much larger then the average human... almost as large as an adult leoman male, but was also wearing bandages, as if trying to hide what he was, and reminded her of Draco.

    "Draco had said something about dragons being immune to magic... or was it resistant?" Marie thought, "it's been so long... but he's not connected with the Inquisition... and if I show myself to him as an "Inquisitor" I could be in real trouble."

    Marie then sighed, "but if I can't figure this out, we'd all end up enslaved to three girls who have not been taught properly..."
