    Elle took out a plain looking medallion attached to a thin ribbon. Turning it over in her hands, she looked for any special markings or identifying features but found nothing that seemed to suggest any sort of otherworldly supernatural power. She hesitated before putting it on, whether because she thought it might be magic or because she was afraid it was not she didn't know. But steeling herself against her fears she slipped the band over her head and let the medallion rest on her chest. Fingering it idly, she waited for a spark, a tingle, a glimmer, anything. But no sign was forthcoming. She felt like crying at this disappointment and hated herself for it. When the buzzer on the dryer went off she welcomed the distraction and went, with the medallion still fastened around her neck, to sort the laundry. If she had stayed long enough she might have found the instruction manual, and then she might have found the page inside with the picture of the medallion on it and in doing so she might have stayed to read the following excerpt and realized the grave mistake she was making.

    The Medallion of Zullo
    The Medallion of Zullo allows the user to take on the appearance of anyone he or she desires. In order to take on the appearance of a specific person, place the Medallion around ones neck, then touch an article of clothing belonging to the person to the Medallion. The body wearer will then transform until it is an exact duplicate of the person to whom the article of clothing belongs. The transformation process takes approximately one half hour, however the process may be sped up by prolonged contact with the article of clothing. Alternatively, if an article of clothing is used that has not been worn by a specific person before, the wearer of the Medallion will be transformed into someone who would fit, both in terms of physical dimensions and personality, the article of clothing in question. After the transformation is complete, at least twelve hours must elapse before the Medallion can be used to return the user to their original form or take on a new shape. It is prudent to remember this at all times.

    But she didn't stay and she didn't read. Instead Elle walked down to the basement where the dryer was. Opening the dryer door, she began throwing the clothes inside a hamper. As she did, one of the shirts brushed the medallion. Elle felt something like an electric jolt run across her body, seemingly from the medallion. Looking down at it, she saw nothing amiss and dismissed it as a static shock from all the clothes she'd just touched. After shoving the last of the clothes into the hamper, Elle shut the dryer door and began carrying the hamper upstairs. Most of the clothes belonged to her oldest daughter, Alli, so she carried them up to her daughter's room and began sorting and folding them. As she did, her hair kept falling in front of her eyes. Elle normally kept her hair short but as time went on she noticed she was having to brush it away more and more frequently.

    When she felt her hair brush her shoulders she stopped folding and went into the bathroom to get a better look at herself. When she saw her reflection she nearly jumped in shock. The woman looking back at her did not look a day over 30. Elle knew the location of every wrinkle on her face, but she could not find a single one on the face in the mirror. Reaching up to touch her face, her hands met only smooth skin, and as she moved her hands away she saw they were also much smoother and less worn. Elle was excited now, and she leaned in close for a closer look at her youthful face. However, as she continued looking she thought that she looked younger than she had a few moments ago, and her hair seemed even longer. When she had first looked her hair, normally only a few inches long, had been just brushing her shoulders. Now it hung a few inches below them. As she watched it lengthened a few fractions of an inch.

Her excitement turned to puzzlement however as she noticed her eyes changing from their normal brown to a light blue. Elle guessed she looked around 25 now, but not like she had at 25. It almost looked like her, close enough that the face could belong to a sister. It didn't make any sense to her, until she remembered the what had happened in the laundry room.
