In a few moments, they they had turned into nubs that were twitching and lengthening. His nose shriveled away, and Dan had to breathe through his mouth. By this time, two flexible stick-like appendages longer than his forearm were swiveling around above his mouth. He could smell his own sweat, and the water dripping from the faucets and sinks. If he wanted to smell something more closely, all he had to do was hover an antenna over it like an insect.

    "Like an insect," he thought, taking a deep breath through his mouth. "Is that my inner animal? At heart, am I really just a bug of some sort?" he thought.

    Nervous and hoping to find his parents or Kat, Dan started pacing through the house, panting and twitching his new antennae, until he started to feel something else. His body seized up, and he clutched his chest as his breathing stopped and he felt like his lungs were dissolving.

    "Is this it? Am I dying? Am I having a heart attack or something?" thought Dan, looking back on his short life, wishing he hadn't taken it for granted, and especially wishing he had gotten along better with his sister even if she was betraying him like this now.

    "What's happening?" he thought. "I'm still not breathing, am I?" He couldn't feel his chest expanding, but he also didn't feel like he was suffocating anymore. In fact, he felt the flow of air... pretty much everywhere.

    That's when Dan found one of the new holes that had opened up on his side. He found he could close it or open it at will, or force air through it with muscles he didn't have before there. The boy didn't need lungs anymore, because like an insect he know had breathing tubes all through his body.

    "This is freaky," he tried to say out loud, only to discover that he no longer had a voice.
