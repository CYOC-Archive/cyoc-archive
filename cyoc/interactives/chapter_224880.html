    Justin thought about this sentence for a long time. He never knew that Father Gregory had interest in him. He was always so old, but now he remembered how Father Gregory had always shown interest in him and helped him out when he was training for the altar boy role.

    Justin decided to to test his fate

    Changed interest to love

    The new sentence read as

    The young priest showed love in the young boy

    Father Gregory ended his sermon and walked with the procession to back so that they could change. He was headed to his office and asked Justin to join him. Justin dutifully joined him.

    "Ah Justin, I just wanted to say you did really well today." The priest said

    "Thank you, sir." Justin replied

    "Justin, I have to say this. I think you are a very beautiful boy and I know I am a man of the cloth but I think I love you."

    "Sir, I have been having these feelings lately and I think I might like boys too." Justin replied almost in tears

    Father Gregory moves closer to Justin and wrapped him in his arms. They embraced for what seemed like hours. Justin would nuzzle his head into the priest's chest. They finally broke apart and the priest kissed Justin on the cheek. To both that seemed right as they kissed again this time on the lips. After some more time together, they left each other in a state of happy bliss. Justin couldn't believe the power he had in his hands and how it literally just changed his life.



    Weeks later, Both Father Gregory and Justin have been spending long hours together. Justin's parents were getting worried he was spending too much time with the priest. They would question him about his time with the priest, but he would tell them he was just studying with Father Gregory to become a better altar boy.

Justin sat at his usual seat. Father Gregory got up out of his seat and came over to Justin and kissed him square on the lips in front of his parents. Justin was afraid his dad would shoot him,but instead he encouraged the kissing. He was proud his son had such an open relationship with the priest.
