Which is why you need to sign the waiver," said the doctor, handing Sylvia a clipboard with a paper attached to it, "If you don't want to risk it, that is acceptable. Most of if was the usual agreement of consent stuff, acknowledgement of risks, etc. However, it did say that Horizons Lab was willing to pay her handsomely for agreeing to this extra scientific experiment and an even larger compensation if the x-rays have a negative effect on her.     The last thing Mike remembered was the raft going over very rough water before he was suddenly pitched back. Then he knew nothing but darkness that was sometimes punctuated by dreams. He dreamed of many things, of his family, his friends, but most often, he dreamed of otters. He always found them both cool and cute, though he'd usually just called them 'cool' to others. The river otters in his dream frolicked in the water, caught fish, laid on the riverbanks to sleep. Sometimes, Mike dreamed he was one of the otters, having the time of his life without a care in the world.

    Mike had been having one of his otter dreams when there was a great change. Suddenly, the other otters started growing larger, changing in size. Mike was one of them, feeling his body shift dramatically. Soon, every otter there was a humanoid creature, looking bewildered at themselves and each other. Mike looked down at his webbed hands tipped with black claws in utter confusion.

    Then Mike woke up. Everything was very blurry and bright at first, like he hadn't opened his eyes in a very long time. He felt very stiff and something felt very off. He tried to sit up, but his muscles were having trouble obeying. He also felt a large amount of discomfort underneath him, like he was somehow lying on his own leg.

    Suddenly, two people appeared in his vision. Their details were still blurry, but Mike could hear their voices.

    "Mike, thank God you're awake."

    "I thought we lost you."

    "Mmm...Mom? Dad?" asked Mike. His voice sounded really different to his ears, deeper for some reason.

    "Mike, there's something we need to tell you," said his father's voice, "You were in an accident."

    "Yeah, the raft," said Mike as his memories started to come back in, "The water was too rough and I fell out."

    "You hit your head really badly," said his mother's voice, "You've been in a coma for a long time. The doctors thought you would never come out of it, but there was this new project..."

    "What? What happened?" asked Mike, his vision starting to clear more, allowing him to see his parents' anxious faces.

    "It's...best if we showed you," said Mike's father before he helped his son climb out of the bed. Mike noticed quite a few things as that happened. First of all, his dad looked a lot shorter than he remembered. He had always been smaller than his dad, now he was at least a head taller. His feet also felt wrong and there was an alien sensation near his rear. "Now, Mike, you need to understand a lot of changes have happened, but it's all for the better," said his father before he guided his son to a mirror.

    Mike had a little trouble registering it was a mirror at first because the image in it was impossible. Being supported by his father was a tall, muscular otter man wearing only boxers. Mike stared in confusion before starting to understand the otter man was mimicking his movements exactly. He looked at his hands to see they had webbing and claws just like in his dream. He looked down, seeing his broad fur-covered chest. He felt down it, feeling the hard muscles. His hand briefly touched his crotch before it jerked back. Whatever was there felt a lot bigger than what he had before. He patted his backside and soon found his new tail. The otter man in the mirror would have been unrecognizable as Mike if it weren't for his shaggy brown hair, a shade different from the rest of his fur, and his blue eyes.

    "You see, this project was part of a group called Animalia. You wouldn't know about it because you were in a coma, but a virus got loose and changed many animals into humanoid animals and they can spread it to humans, but it makes them healthier," said Mike's mother, "So we thought if this virus could regrow limbs and cure cancer, surely it could bring you out of your coma."

    "How long was I in that coma?" asked Mike shakily. His voice was definitely that of a grown man's. And yet, his parents didn't look much older than when he had last seen them.

    "You were in a coma for 8 months," said his mom.

    "8 months? Then why am I older?" asked Mike.

    "Well, that's what happens when you're given two doses of the virus," said his dad, "You didn't wake up immediately after the first dose so we decided you might need another."

    "Just sit down and we'll try to explain it all to you," said his mom, "And watch so you don't sit on your tail."

    Over the next half hour, Mike's parents told them about how the uplifted animals came together to form a community called Animalia and how much development and progress it had made. As Mike was processing this, he found that he was actually not upset about what happened to him. If anything, he was excited. He was an otter, he had a grown, muscular body, his parents accepted that, there were other humanoid animals like him, and he wouldn't be an outcast of society. In fact, there was only one way this could be any better...

    Just then, there was a knock on the door. "Oh, that must be Mary," said his mom. She got up and opened the door to the room.

    Mike's jaw dropped as Mary walked into the room. For good reason as she was the most beautiful otter woman he had ever seen. Ok, she was the ONLY otter woman he had ever seen, but she was still a beauty. She had a very fit physique and a curvaceous figure. "Glad to see that you're up," said Mary cheerfully.

    "Mary was one of the otters who were first uplifted in the outbreak," said Mike's mom, "She donated some of her DNA so you could be treated."

    Mike just continued to stare slack-jawed. Mike's father chuckled and said, "You're an otter now, not a fish. Close your mouth unless you want to catch flies."

    Mike managed to control his jaw and said, "Nice to meet you, Mary."

    Mary giggled and said, "You're even cuter now that you're awake."

    If otters wagged their tails for happiness, Mike would have been going a mile a minute. This beauty of an otter thought he was cute? Maybe he was still in a dream, one he didn't want to wake up from.
