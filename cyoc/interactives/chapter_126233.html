No matter what.” She looked Casper in the eyes. “Do you still want to stay with me?”

Her toes stretched out into longer, thinner but still dull talons.

Casper smiled to himself as he watched the little Pterodactyl shakily flew around the playground, apparently still learning to use her wings.

Eventually however, she finally managed to get a grip, and lifted him a few inches off the ground for a few feet, causing the triceratops boy to gasp excitedly before she let go and he fell to the ground again, with little more than a squeak.

    “Now that’s cute,” Ashley said with a giggle. Casper smiled as well. Even changed by the ring, kids were still kids.

    Casper and Ashley finally made it to the college’s gymnasium, with a little time to spare. They headed into the pool room, where some of the inflatable otters were already warming up, and took a seat by the bleachers. Casper noted how the otters seemed to take some effort to stay submerged, much like he and Ashley had a few days back at the beach. Still they seemed to have a better grip on it than most synthetics, which made sense, given their species, and status on the college’s swim team.

    More people began to come in, mostly the family and friends of the guys on the swim team. Quite a few of the families had already been changed during Casper’s little “survey” from earlier this week (about eight of the boy’s families had been changed, if he remembered right), and evidently one of the lions from Casper’s classes had a friend on the team as well, but the rest were still human.

    Ashley was smiling as she saw a decent number of people pile on the bleachers on either side of the pool. It still wasn’t as big a showing as there would be for say, a football game, but there were still plenty of people to work the ring’s magic on.

    The last of the audience soon took their seats, and the otters got into position in the water. Casper tensed his body at the same time as they tensed theirs. As the orca coach blew the whistle, the otters began their dash across the pool, and the ring’s magic began to flow.

    It affected the unchanged families of the otters first, their clothes disappearing as their skin turned into smooth, shiny latex, mold lines appearing on their sides. Their new skins were a variety of colors; while about a third of them had more “realistic” mixes of brown, beige, and white, there were also a rainbow of more vibrant colors, especially among the children. The fathers all sported bulges on their groins, and while the mothers had nipple-less breasts (a few of which may have gotten a little larger during the change, though Casper and Ashley hadn’t seen clearly with so many changs going on). Meanwhile, the siblings that hadn’t gone through puberty simply bore a smooth torso all over.

    Men, women, and children alike all had long, flexible tails inflate behind them, as bar codes appeared on their sealed rears. Their feet grew slightly wider, a thin “webbing” forming between their toes. A similar webbing formed between their fingers as they bulged and rounded out slightly, looking like a mix of paws and human hands.

    Their jaws and noses pushed forward slightly, becoming short muzzles with little black noses. A few had even gained some painted lines on the sides of their muzzles to give the impression of whiskers. Their ears all became a little smaller and rounder, moving upward on their heads by a little under an inch. All of the new male otter’s hair vanished, leaving them ‘bald.” About half of females went ‘bald’ as well, though just as many had their hair join together into a single inflated piece. Though their changes progressed at different rates, each one finished with a nozzle popping into existence from their belly buttons.

    A yellow lizard man.

    A bright red cardinal woman.

    An orange goat woman.

    A couple of dalmation guys.

    A yellow turtle man.

    And grassy-green mare.

    663. Almost at the number of the beast. Casper mused that he might change 666 and 667 together to avoid a bad omen.

    All of the newly-synthetic people continued watching and cheering on the swim team, with just as much passion as they had been before.

    “I think I’m remembering something again, from our history class,” Ashley whispered. “The reason our college is so popular with synthetics is that it was apparently one of the first places to accept synthetics, even before they had attained legal ‘personhood.’”

    “Thanks, Ashley.” So, apparently, the early synthetics weren’t thought of as ‘real people’ are first? Casper hoped they hadn’t been treated too poorly. He idly wondered if that might start to change, if enough of the human population became synthetic that humans were in the minority. That probably wouldn’t happen in his lifetime, (assuming synthetics lived as long as an average human did) but it was an interesting question, nonetheless.

    He put it aside for now, and went back to watching the swim team seemingly effortlessly glide through the water. And occasionally bounce on the surface of it.
