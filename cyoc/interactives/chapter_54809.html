    1681 December 18th

    The little white boy cub didn't like this game anymore. The nice ladies had given him what
    they called a 'magic wishing rock' and said all he had to do was say 'I wish my parents
    were like me' and they be with him forever and ever and always be his friends and not
    shout at him for misbehaving and he wouldn't have to see disease take any more siblings.

    At first it had been fun, his new brother and sister never got tired of playing with him, the
    first few days had been fun, very fun. But then food began to run low, and the young cub
    realized his brother and sister were no better at hunting than he was...

    He had finally tried to use the wishing rock to make everything alright again, but nothing
    had happened and nothing had worked. Finally the three had wandered out looking for
    food, had gotten lost, and now were crying. The little male white leoman cub cried most of
    all, for he now realized the heavy weight his parents had always carried for his sake, and
    now they were gone, he had wished them away, and his new brother and sister had only
    him to be looked after, and he couldn't help them.

    That was when the oddly dressed humans found them.

    1681 December 19th

    "Where is Marie?" Father Gabriel asked.

    The felman Inquisitor, and the Plague Doctor/Priest's contact between himself and the
    council when he was not able to meet with them himself said simply, "She's being taught a
    lesson in being an impulsive child who kills heathens who were supposed to be taken alive
    for interrogation. Don't worry Father, this is barely even undercover. A small circle of
    child molesters need to be caught and arrested. But only the fear of catching the plague
    will have them let anyone in. The wretches shouldn't give you any trouble."

    Father Gabriel sighed later as he put on his raven's mask and slipped on his black hat and
    dark coat taking his cane.

    The holy man considered many things to write as he felt his defective heart flutter. 'There's
    nothing left that I haven't already tried to teach her.'

    Finally he wrote down,

    'Marie, God's universe is one based on truth, remember that if nothing else.'

    He remembered telling her that every time he went on a mission when she was small. But
    she wasn't so small now.

    "Has it really been only eleven years?" He said to himself. "I'm sorry I won't be there in the
    flesh when you turn eighteen Marie... I'd share one last christmas with you if I could. ... But
    not everything in life is fair. Vatican, take care of her."

    The Plague Doctor who had haunted the streets of Europe for decades now walked out of
    his church for the final time, knowing he would only walk into it one more time.
