    “Well, there are a few available apartments near the beach,” Kat says, “I know that they could be expensive, if that’s the case, I’ll see if I can find a cheaper place outside of town”.

    Janet nods, “sounds like you have a plan,” she says, “I’ll support you, no matter what you choose, just make sure it’s the right place, that you feel safe, and it doesn’t cost to much!”

At first I thought she might have something to do with his disappearance, but I can’t imagine she’d be capable of doing something to Dan, her powers have just awoken, she is not powerful enough to do anything major. I think it’s because they never got along and she is just happy to not have her annoying little brother around anymore,’ and with that Janet simply goes back to cleaning the house.

    “You are wriggling your but and calling that lady “mistress”… you’re acting as if you were her… pet dog or something,” Cody says, “it’s freaking me out!”

    Kat chuckles, “sorry, Cody, if it bothers you,” she says, “Ben loves playing pretend, so I promised him, whenever he sees me, he can pretend being my pet dog”.

    “That is so freaky and weird,” Cody says, noticeably bothered by the way Ben is acting.

    Not wanting Cody to make a scene and draw attention to them, Kat quickly comes up with an idea on how to deal with the 11-year-old. Kat’s eyes begin to glow blue, “silly Cody, it shouldn’t bother you at all,” she says, “after all, you are nothing but an object, and a object doesn’t think nor care about anything!”

    Cody looks up at Kat, directly into the eyes of the witch, “what are you talking about,” he asks, “you cr-craaaazy…,” the boy suddenly falls silent, his eyes beginning to glow in the same blue color, his body slumping slightly as it relaxes.

    Ben tilts his head, “mistress, what’s wrong with Cody,” he asks, leaning in to sniff at his friend, letting out a whimper, “is… is he sick?”

    “Can you help him, mistress,” Ben asks, looking up at Kat hopefully.
