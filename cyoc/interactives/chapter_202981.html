    The thought blows your mind, but Mary Lynn is right. You spend the rest of the morning watching as the crowds who went through the gate start hunting down people throughout the city and shoving them through the gate too. Those crowds then join in the search, but what's incredible is the view you have of nearby towns. You don't notice at first, since you're still so focused on what's going on in the city below, but apparently as soon as people start going through the gate, a few of them jump into a car and start driving away. As soon as they reach a new town, they apparently need only to touch a building in the center of town to turn it into a new tower, with a gate as large as the first. Then it's a simple matter of dragging a few people through, and then that town too suddenly falls to the black towers of Mary Lynn's awful vision. You can't believe this is happening, but within the hour, most of the neighboring towns have fallen under Mary Lynn and her army of newly empowered women and their male slaves.

    From your perspective in the cup of Mary Lynn's bra, you can feel the giantess's heart beating faster as the two of you watch this drama play out. You're sure that the police or even the military is going to step in and attack, but they never do. Sure, there's occasional resistance, and sometimes even a setback. But there's never the full-fledged onslaught you'd expect, and you never find out why. Eventually you hear rumors that some of the military in the armed forces intervened and managed to stop those orders from ever being carried out, but you never know for sure. All you can tell at this point is that the black towers are popping up everywhere, and everyone in every town is being forcibly dragged through the gates. Some of the women, you notice, are going through voluntarily. Some of them are even running through, holding hands with each other. This is bad.

    And that's the way things go for the next few weeks. It is literally a matter of days before most of the world's population centers are taken over by the black towers, thanks to the wonders of air travel and a concerted, effective strategy by the women who have gone through the gates. Mary Lynn doesn't even have to do much, though she occasionally wanders about the countryside admiring her handiwork. You have to admit, it's really amazing how quickly everything changed. After those first few days, those under Mary Lynn's control are mostly hunting down people, usually men, in the countryside and in back rooms. At this point, humanity as you know it is gone forever.
