    "What are you?" the dentist asked in a shocked voice as he looked at Jensen as the black furred wolf sat quietly in his chair, although most of his legs dangled way out over the edge.

    Jensen was also slightly surprised. Having people ignore his species and nudity was something he accepted, but to have the dentist recognize him was totally unexpected. Especially since he recognized Jensen by his teeth. His teeth were very different from what they had looked like when he was a human teen-aged girl... maybe he had done something that made him stand out during his last known visit or he was simply a good judge of teeth.

    "I'm a wolf, that should be obvious," Jensen answered.

    "And a very masculine one at that," the dentist muttered, "How did this happen?"

    "Let's just say I ended up on the wrong end of a question," Jensen answered, trying to be cryptic, since he had left Isabelle in the waiting area. Having her rush in to use her turn would only make a mess.

    The dentist, however, seemed to be understanding and carried on.

    "So, Jennifer, what can I do for you today?"

    "I go by Jensen now," Jensen told him, "and I need this canine capped."

    The dentist gave Jensen nervous glance as he opened his mouth and pointed to the chipped tooth. The guess was correct that the tooth would need to be capped, but he was still struggling with the fact that they weren't human teeth.

    "And you want it to match..." the dentist began nervously.

    "My other upper canine," Jensen explained, "I kinda need it that way."

    "Ah ha," the dentist said nervously, "You wouldn't mind me taking a mold first? Make sure that the cap is carved accurately?"

    "So long as the job is done," Jensen answered, with a cringe, knowing that the stuff that dentists used to make molds of teeth tasted nasty.

    "Are you a werewolf?" the boy asked again.

    Isabelle put her magazine down and brought her head down so that she was looking the boy in he eye.

    "And what would you do if I told you "yes"?" Isabelle asked.

    "Are you here to bite people?" the boy asked curiously.

    "My... my Alpha has not commanded me to, so no," Isabelle answered.

    "Do you want to?" the boy asked.

    "Not really," Isabelle answered and then blinked when the boy looked depressed.

    "I always wondered what'd it be like to be a werewolf," the boy commented and went over to where they had a pile of blocks for kids to play with.

    "Weird," Isabelle thought to herself.
