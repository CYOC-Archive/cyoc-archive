    As Nate browsed the room's collection, he began to remember several characters from old TV shows and movies, mostly the ones with deformities and extra body parts; with a room like this, why couldn't he be one of them?

    He decided to start by building a new body from scratch. First, he found a massive pair of legs, about three times as wide as his own, and set them up in front of the bed. He then found a pair of arms to match: they were, like the legs, three times larger than his own. Then came the hard part--finding a good torso to work with the arms and legs. After some searching, he managed to find one. The lower half was twice as wide as Nate's, but the shoulders and chest area were three times larger--four and a half feet across! Nate decided that no other torso in the room would work, so he set this one down on the large legs; they fused upon contact, as did the arms when Nate attached them to the torso's shoulders.

    Nate then sat down on the bed to admire the body he had built; he was ready for it to become his. Nate then put his hands on either side of his head, and pulled it from his shoulders. Nate's old body retained its consciousness for ten seconds, just enough time to turn Nate's head around and place on the massive body. As soon as Nate's head touched the broad shoulders, he could feel his new body as he watched his old one lie down on the bed, now lifeless. Nate was amazed that he now had a completely new body, and knew he would enjoy it; however, he felt that a body such as this was too large for just one person, or rather one head...

    Nate walked around the room with his new body, looking for some new heads. He eventually settled on two heads--they both looked like mannequin heads, but they were flesh and bone. Nate used his new arms to place the two heads on either side of his own; they fused to the shoulders right away. Then, the heads began to change--in five seconds, they looked exactly like Nate's own head. Indeed, Nate was now a massive, three-headed boy!

    Nate got along easily with his new heads--apparently, they each acted as though they had been like this forever. They found some trunks underneath the bed, filled with all sorts of clothes. After a lot of searching, they found an interesting outfit that all three heads liked: massive brown boots, brown pants, a pale blue long-sleeve shirt with three neck holes, pale blue gloves, and some shoulder pads. Each head got a makeover, too: red face paint for the right-hand head along with dreadlocks and a face mask, blue paint and goggles for the middle head, yellow paint with a jaw brace for the left-hand head, and a large, tight gold necklace for each head. When the body bros looked in the room's mirror, they didn't see themselves--they looked more like a three-headed professional wrestler, and a lot like one Nate had actually seen in an old show from 2003.

    After some decisions on their individual names--the three heads wanted to keep in mind that they were all individual--Matt, Nate, and Benedict settled on their new names in the following order: Matt the yellow head, Nate the blue head, Benedict the red head.
