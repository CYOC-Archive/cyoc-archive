    Penny sat nervously in her wheelchair as the group began engage in a series of friendly conversations with her mother hugging Kirk. She seemed to be as glad to have him back as Penny did. Then, Penny felt something flutter through the back of her mind... something about her mother saying that Kirk was a fine male and she might have to test him. And that memory made her gasp...

    She remembered the parents of Penn Glen being intensely loyal to one another, but then lions were in nature polygamous had little loyalty to their mates. A female might be impregnated by one male one year, and then roughly two years later being impregnated by a different male if a pride had more then one male. And that pride males commonly mated with every female within the pride.

    She wondered if that was a side effect of their transform that they were gaining certain things that mundane lions did as part of their transform? Or was his something else? Something related to the overall effects of reality? Penny didn't know, and prayed that she'd be able to figure everything out before everything about her disappeared and all that remained was the lioness she had become.

    "But then, motherhood would be nice," a voice spoke in the back of Penny's mind.

    Penny shuddered and shook her head.

    "Is something wrong, dear?" Mother Glenn asked from beside her.

    Penny nervously looked over to see that her grandmother still sat beside her wheelchair.

    "It's nothing to be concerned about," Penny spoke, "Just some things I'm thinking about."

    "Future cubs?" Mother Glenn teased.

    "Kirk and I haven't even gotten to the details for our wedding," Penny retorted, and the thought, "and I'm not in heat right no, so I couldn't get pregnant if I tried."

    "Is this to deal with the rest of the Pride?" Mother Glen asked, "you said they were hurt. Are they at the hospital? Do you want to visit them?"

    "They aren't at the hospital," Penny answered, "and with my legs as they are, I can't really go much of anywhere. Stairs are impossible for me."

    "Your future Prime Mate and his family, and your father and mother would be more then happy to help," Mother Glen told her, "and I can carry you... I can remember you enjoying to ride sidesaddle on my back when you were a cub."

    That made Penny sigh. She remembered enjoying horseback riding, and remembered that her interest in becoming a policemen was after a trip to New York and seeing that some of the policemen there still road horses. She remembered as Penn wanting to join Glendale's police to do the same... and then learning that Glendale's police did not have "mounted" officers. She did not, yet, remember riding anything sidesaddle.

    "Well, I would like to visit them," Penny spoke, "but..."

    "Then where are they?" Mother Glen asked, "we'll all go. To help you and to check on our pride mates."

    Penny wanted to say that they couldn't, but seeing the firm look on her grandmother's face, she couldn't help but back down.

    "They're at Wolf Manor," Penny admitted, and then wondered to herself, "where did that come from? It's Smith Manor as far as I know... but Mother Glen can't go there! All the wolves are there, as is that red and white pup..."

    "I wonder how Blackie is doing these days?" Mother Glen then wondered.

    "Blackie?"

    "His name is Patrick," Mother Glen answered, "but I've always called him Blackie... even if he has some gold in his fur."

    "You know one of the wolves there?" Penny gasped.

    "Of course I do, dearie, we all do," Mother Glen answered, "and Patrick and I grew up together. He has been a very good friend over the years."

    Penny didn't know what to do. The way her grandmother was speaking, it sounded like the wolf pack and the pride had been friends for years... but the pride had only just been formed to counter "Jensen's" use of the game. Was this some side affect of her transform? Was this related to something else she was somehow sensing.

As they watched, Mbili wondered a bit about the water war that had been paused, and Saba hoped Paula would be okay while they were gone. In Alice's presence, the Pack's master of semantics, Mbili found herself too curious not to ask.

    "Toni told me about how your prefixes all go," Mbili told Alice, "it's left me curious... how do you feel about not getting a prefix?"

    "It is no real concern for my sisters and I," Alice answered, "they are only prefixes, and has nothing to do with our names."

    "Prefixes?" Sita wondered, not having heard Toni, Jay, and Amethyst's explanation earlier.

    "Largely letters of the Greek Alphabet to represent our rank in the Pack," Alice explained, "though, because our pack is so big, keeping track of who holds what rank gets tougher and tougher. Father has decided not to do that any more. The only prefixes that are likely to remain are Alpha and Beta, but they will become a title of office, given to the Pack's leaders and "second in command"."

    "Toni seemed to think that all of that would be done away with," Mbili told the glass wolf.

    "The rigid placement of ranks will be done away with," Alice replied, "but the Pack will always need leaders. Someone to protect and lead us."

    "So who will lead when Jensen and Kaylee step down?" Moja wondered.

    "Either Jenny or Bart," Alice told her, "as for the most part, leadership has largely followed father's bloodline going back to when the pack was first gathered... but that doesn't mean that others have been alphas besides father's ancestors. Some have challenged for the lead and have taken the reigns as leaders until one of father's ancestors was able to restore the original line."

    "How other wolves challenged for the right to lead the pack?" Tatuli asked.

    "For awhile there was real combat, which could result in serious injuries," Alice spoke, "but within the last hundred years, it has become more ritualized, rather then actual combat. Wrestling matches and so on... and Mr. P is good at it, despite not having thumbs."

    "Where did that come from anyway?" Tano wondered, "Isn't his name Patrick?"

    "It is, and it is my fault I am afraid," Alice chuckled, "I had trouble pronouncing Patrick, and the only thing I could manage to say Mr. P."

    "You couldn't say Patrick but you could say Mister P?" Nane wondered.

    "I was two at the time," Alice answered, "I can not explain it."

    "But great-grandpa's been Mr. P ever since," Abbey spoke, "He doesn't mind... it's sort of a nickname now."

    "You now," Nneli commented, "we've been playing the water war with your pack mates, it's just now occurred to me that we haven't seen their folks around... or at least can not remember seeing them."

    "Well..." Alice sighed, "that can be difficult. Laura and Zulema are the daughters of Abdul and Ingrid, which is where you'll get the explanation why Zulema has an Arabic name. Giselle and Gretchen are the daughters of Abdul and Martha. Cassandra and Lilly, whom you likely haven't seen today, are the daughters of Maddex and Whitney."

    "How come their fur colors don't match perfectly? Other then Laura and Zulema... anyway?" Saba asked.

    "Don't know," Abbey shrugged, "Mommy ran paternity tests. They are the pups of our "Shiva" wolves. She also found that the genetic mutations that made the Shiva wolves grow their extra limbs are not present in their children."

    "But aren't they "Omegas"?" Mbili wondered, "Mary's told us that omega wolves generally don't breed. Only the alphas."

Jensen's tried to do some digging, and Aunt Julie's gone to some fortune teller to try and find their biological families, but so far no luck."
