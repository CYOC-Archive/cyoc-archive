    Donovan stared at his phone. "C'mon Matt..." The name suddenly felt a bit weird. "Mark." Donovan shook his head, unsure how he could mess up his name like that. "Did it come or not?" He stared at his phone, knowing full well that either the young man was going to answer or he wasn't. Donovan gave up. "I'll just order something else." He went back to the website and tried to think about what his son would like. 'Men,' the thought ran through Donovan's head, and he instantly shook it out.

    Even if that was true, Jackson technically hadn't come out yet. And he wasn't about to pry to make his son even less comfortable around him. And it's not like he was against it or anything, just didn't know how to relate. "You're just getting him a birthday present. Quit making it weird," Donovan told himself as he scrolled through the website. "Just get something simple." He came across a bunch of shirts with anime characters on it. "Fine. Whatever. One of those." Then quickly picked one that had a ton of reviews. It's not like he knew what any of that shit was. If anything they all looked alike to him anyways. He typed in his information then closed the website.

    "Dad?" Jackson came into the room. "You're the greatest!" he quickly ran over and gave him a hug. Then headed for the door. "I'll be back later. Don't wait up." Giving a knowing smile.

    "Uhh... Okay?" Donovan was a bit stunned. "Be safe!" he called out.

    "Of course!" Jackson said, then closed the door behind him.

    Donovan's phone vibrated. It was Mark, texting him. 'Don't worry about the return. I'll pay you back later sir.'

    'What did they send!?' Donovan wanted to text; but held onto it. Did he really want to know? He knew Jackson and Mark were getting close, he just didn't know how close it really was. And with a guy like Mark, could Donovan really worry? Maybe it'd all be better that way anyways. He deleted the text. Then sent, 'Keep Jackson safe.'

    'Yes sir!'

    Donovan let out a bit of a snort as he read the text. It was completely on brand for Mark, yet he still wanted to roll his eyes. "At least I can trust him..." he sighed.

    But a heavy knock to his door brought him back with a start. "The hell?" he wondered as he walked over to it. It looked like a man in a delivery uniform. "At this late at night?"

    "Package for Donovan DuPointe," the man said, handing Donovan a clipboard. "Sign here."
