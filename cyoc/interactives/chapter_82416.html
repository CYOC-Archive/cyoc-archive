    Rachel looked at her reflection in the mirror. She looked normal again, did that really happen? Of course it
    did. Not 30 seconds ago she was completely flat-chested. There was so much potential in this, but Rachel
    couldn't shake the feeling that she might've been wrong. What if one dark chocolate doesn't necessarily
    counter one white chocolate?

    Rachel turned to the box of chocolates, she wanted to eat more, to experiment. But she was afraid she wouldn't
    be able to fix the changes. "If only the box had instructions." She muttered to herself. Then something popped
    into her head... "Maybe it did..."

    Sure she'd checked the box of chocolates without finding any instructions (other than the vague picture), but
    she hadn't searched the box that contained the box of chocolates.

    Rachel rushed to the package and searched it. The only thing inside was what appeared to be a syringe, and the
    note she had read earlier. She grabbed the note and reread it. This time she flipped the note over and looked
    at the back. Written in crude handwriting was this note:

    "White chocolate shrinks; Dark chocolate grows. You can melt chocolates together to further add possibilities.
    If you combine two matching white chocolates you can reduce the number of the part in question (ie two white
    chocolate legs will cause you to lose a leg). Two matching dark chocolates will do the opposite (ie cause you
    to grow an extra leg). Matching a dark chocolate with a white chocolate will cancel the effects and just let
    you enjoy the chocolate.

    You can also combine two non-matching chocolates, like an arm and a leg. Depending on the flavors the effects
    will differ. Two Dark Chocolates or two white chocolates will switch the location of the body parts (ie arms
    for legs and legs for arms), the difference being that Dark Chocolates will switch all body parts connected to
    those being swapped while white chocolate will not (for instance dark chocolate arms will remain attached to
    your hands, while white chocolate will only swap the arms, leaving the hands connected to whatever the arms
    are swapping with). If you eat one white and one dark chocolate, the dark chocolate part will replace the
    white chocolate part, (one Dark Arm and one White Leg will cause your legs to become arms, leaving you with 4
    arms)."

    Rachel looked the note over, admiring how such a long message could fit on such a small page. Then armed with
    the instructions, her insecurities were put to rest. She returned to the box of chocolate and a smile crept
    across her face.

    "Now, what should I do first?"
