    The Toy Comes To Life, Jeff Has No Idea What A Huge Mistake He Has Made!

    Branch Full view
    added by LittleBoy 9 days ago A I

    At first nothing seems to happen, Jeff scowls, “pfff! I KNEW this was all just bullsh… wait… the… the joints to move the head, legs, tails and arms… they… they are… g-gone,” he says, his eyes then widen when he notices that the plastic body of the toy seems to look more like… flesh, the painted on scales, not looking like paint anymore, “oh… sh-shit,” Jeff whispers.

    As Jeff continues to stare in disbelief at his toy T-Rex, his eyes widen when he notices the long thick and strong tail starting to move, then suddenly the head of the T-Rex turns and looks right at him, its eyes full of life, but also a hint of confusion. The Dinosaur then opens its mouth, seeing all those teeth which are able to rip him apart, causes Jeff to regret what he had done.

    The T-Rex and Jeff stare at each other in silence, the Dinosaur then opens its mouth, seeing all those teeth which are able to rip him apart, causes Jeff to regret what he had done, “pl-please… d-don’t eat me,” Jeff says.

    The T-Rex blinks in confusion, “then you should better start explaining what the FUCK just happened,” the animal suddenly says.

    Jeff’s eyes, if possible, widen even more, “I… I can’t believe it actually worked,” he says, then, by the look his former toy is giving him, Jeff quickly explains everything to the large animal.

    “So, let me get this straight,” the T-Rex says, “you got this weird program and thought it would be a good idea to use ME to test it on?”

    “Um… y-yeah,” Jeff mumbles, “I… I didn’t believe it would work!”

    The T-Rex rolls its eyes, “well… duh,” it says, “now what? If you think of transforming me back into a lifeless object then…”.

    “I wasn’t,” Jeff quickly says, the T-Rex tilts its head, “I… I always dreamt of… well… Dinosaurs to be real!”

    “Why,” T-Rex asks.

    “Be-because… Dinosaurs are awesome,” Jeff mumbles.

    The Dinosaur snorts, “haven’t you thought what the consequences could be by bringing me to life?”

    Jeff nods, “that is why I have altered the reality,” he says, “everyone on this planet thinks you have always been real”.

    “Oh really,” the T-Rex asks, “have you thought about that your kind might start hunting me down when they see me?”

    Jeff again nods, “th-that is why I have also made sure that in this reality Dinosaurs and Human live peacefully with each other”.

    “Don’t tell me you are planning to bring MORE toy Dinosaurs to life,” T-Rex says.

    “Why not,” Jeff asks, “it… it would be so cool! Besides… y-you’d need some food resources!”

    T-Rex snorts, “if you haven’t noticed: I am NOT the original size of a T-Rex, so the size of a deer will satisfy my hunger,” it says.

    “True,” Jeff says, “but… I’d still like to bring a few more Dinos to life”.

    “Well… I can’t stop you,” the Dinosaur says, ‘this human… such a fool’.

    “So… um… what now,” Jeff asks.

    “You tell me, kid,” it says, “YOU brought me to life!”

    “My… my name is Jeff,” Jeff says, “well… yeah… but I haven’t thought so far ahead, because, as I said, I thought that the program was bullshit”.

    The T-Rex lets out a huff, it then turns its head to look out the window, then it turns back to look at Jeff, “if you don’t mind, Jeff,” it says, “and IF this reality alteration thing you have activated is true: I’d like to go outside, loosen my stiff legs and just get a feel for my new appearance”.

    Jeff nods, “okay, why don’t we head into the woods,” he suggests, “you’ll have more room to run around”.

    “Fine with me,” T-Rex says and starts to slowly walk towards Jeff’s door, knocking a few things over with its tail.

    “C-careful with that tail,” Jeff says.

    T-Rex turns his head and frowns, “don’t blame me,” he says, “I haven’t been real a few minutes ago! I don’t have my body under control yet and I still have to figure out how it works!”

    “S-sorry, you’re right,” Jeff says, getting to his feet to follow his former toy, “um… may… may I call you “Rex”,” he asks.

    “Sure,” Rex says without turning his head to look at Jeff as they head down the stairs, Rex being careful to not knock any pictures from the walls with his tail, soon both are heading for the woods, which are directly behind Jeff’s house.
