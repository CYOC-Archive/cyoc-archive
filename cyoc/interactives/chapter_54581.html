    One night in early 1676...

    Goldwyn found the dream-scape somewhat different from before. Buildings seemed older, and the church in the praying town looked like it had a life sized cross with a wooden figure that looked like her foster father nailed to it. Looking down at herself, she saw that she looked older.

    "Goldwyn, is that you?" a voice asked.

    She turned to see Gita and Griselle standing there... both as adult women.

    "Sisters what happened to you!?" Goldwyn gasped.

    "This is a future you see," Herodias spoke from the ground.

    "A future?"

    "The future is always in motion," Herodias explained, "and it changes with every action taken in the present... proving these Christians that believe in Predestination to be absolute idiots."

    "They're idiots for all their rules," Goldwyn spoke.

    "What is this place... Hell?"

    They turned to see a lone blond woman wearing what appeared to be dark clothing. Under her arm was the raven mask they had seen before.

    "Such darkness, such evil," the woman gasped as she walked down the street. She walked toward the church and seemingly recognized the statue of of the crucified man, "such sacrilege in front of a house of God!"

    "They brought it on themselves!" Goldwyn told the woman, coming close, "Oppressing people with their rules."

    "So it is right murder people? To steal from them? To covet that which is not yours?" the woman, Marie retorted, "that is the right way that will make everything better? Humanity would vanish completely as people murder each other indiscriminately. That is why we have rules. To protect that which is good from that which is evil."

    "You lie!" Goldwyn screamed, "rules killed Joella. If that wolf had let me do as I pleased, then there wouldn't have been a problem. I wasn't hurting anyone."

    "Lupman are protective of their territories and their perception of their territories," Marie answered, remembering learning from Lobo why he was with the church, "if you were trespassing, which is a crime, it doesn't matter what you were doing. To the lupman, you could have been plotting to kill his pups or drive off his food."

    "He had no right to know the truth," Gita spoke taking a flanking position beside Goldwyn while Griselle moved to the other side.

    "And if you'll defend the evil laws, then we're afraid you're just as evil as they are!"

    As Marie noticed the eyes of a crow, a large red boa, and a black cat glow, she screamed.
