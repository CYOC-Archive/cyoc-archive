    Beams of sunlight slipped through the large canopy of branches overhead, birds flying among them. Two men wandered through the shade of the woods, the soft underbrush of the forest floor crunching beneath their feet as they continued through the endless expanse of trees.

    The first man stood at a towering 6’5, his skin tan and scarred from years of battle. A scarf of fur hung over his muscular, hairy bare torso, providing warmth among the fresh breeze of the woods. He would've been entirely naked had it not been for the pauldron at his right shoulder and the baggy trousers on his legs with faulds of iron for protection. His brown hair, while short, was rather unkempt.

    The other man was noticeably shorter, being 5’9. His fair, smooth skin and features contrasted the larger, battle scarred male next to him. A red tunic was worn over a shirt of silver chainmail, with a white collared long sleeve shirt underneath said chainmail. Black trousers covered his legs with plates of armor at the knees. The soft breeze caused his blonde, curtained hair to sway along with the leaves above.

    Adjusting his fingerless leather gauntlets, the smaller male removes a canteen filled with water from the belt at his tunic. Chugging it down and sighing in nourishment, he clips it back onto the belt. Bringing his hands to the baldric at his chest, he tightens the strap to prevent his sword and shield from loosening and falling off.

    “You humans carry an unusual amount of gear with you. Doesn't it ever get tiring having to carry all that around with clothing and armor?” The larger, beastial man exclaims as he glances back at his companion in amusement. The warrior's ocean blue eyes gaze back in annoyance.

    “My apologies, Orveus. I must have forgotten that I'm not a hulking beast of a man that can change into a hulking beast of a werewolf whenever he pleases.” The larger man known as Orveus merely grins in response, his abnormally sharp canines glinting.

    “You sure you wouldn't like a taste of the lupine life sometime, Kairos? All I have to do is just give you a liiittle bite,” The wolfman snaps his mouth to demonstrate, “and you'll have an eternity of pleasure!” Kairos’ blue eyes roll, an amused smirk forming across his face.

    “I think I'm perfectly fine being human.” The blonde haired warrior replies. “I already get enough looks in town. Can you imagine what more I'd get if the townspeople see me with nothing but a pair of trousers on? I'd be imprisoned for public indecency!” A hearty laugh resounds from Orveus, the human wolfman joyfully patting his human companion on the back.

    “Whatever you say, Kairos. If you enjoy being human then I can't blame you. I'm a werewolf because of my father anyways. Such a change to someone who isn't inherently a werewolf would be life changing for sure.” Orveus chuckles as Kairos gazes back with a victorious smile.

    “Exactly. Besides, fighting with weapons allows one to adapt to different combat situations much more easily than if they only had fangs and claws.” The red clothed warrior gestures to the sword and shield at his back. Orveus rolls his eyes.

    “I forgot how prideful you warriors are of your vast knowledge in weapons. Then again, you were trained by her.” Kairos nods at this, his eyes closing at the mention of his former mentor.

    Verve, known for the spirit and enthusiasm she brought with her into battle.

    Glancing into the canopy above, the sky was tinted a bright orange, signalling sunset. Wandering aimlessly in the forest at night would be dangerous, as experienced as the pair were. Kairos and Orveus eventually entered into a small clearing, the entirety of the full moon shining far above. Said clearing was perfect for a camping site for the night.

    Gathering firewood proved to be of little trouble due to the countless trees present throughout. Positioning a few fallen logs near the newly lit campfire proved to be of little difficulty due to Orveus’ immense strength. Warmth embraced them as a small fire was set.

    “Kairos.” The blonde haired human glances at his lupine friend at the sound of his name.

    “What is it, Orveus?” The human asks as he removes his baldric, laying his iron sword and shield to his side. Focusing his attention on his friend, the human's head tilts at the rare presence of seriousness on the wolfman's face.

    “Well?” Kairos waves his hand to catch the werewolf's attention. Orveus shakes his head, recovering from his trance.

    “This is going to sound incredibly bizarre, but do you remember the legend of this world?”

    The human falls silent, staring at his friend with a nervous smile. “Orveus… We both know I'm not the type to read into such pointless myths.” A hearty chuckle from Orveus causes them both to laugh.

    “I suppose all that training you underwent rendered books obsolete.” Sighing in defeat, the wolfman appears to fidget with something behind him, before glancing back up at Kairos.

    “Well, you remember why we came to this forest right?”

    The human nods, brushing aside one of his bangs. “We came here to capture and kill the mage that's been causing trouble.”

    “Correct!” Orveus joyfully exclaims. ”But here's the thing; this mage we're here to kill was relatively unknown until just last month. Overnight, an entire family vanished, and all the sources have led back to this person we've never heard of before.” The blue eyed human stares at his friend in puzzlement.

    “I don't understand what you're trying to get at, Orveus. Mages that cause mayhem pop up out of nowhere all the time. This current one is simply the latest in the batch. We kill him, the village is safe for a while, then another pops up.”

    Orveus gives a toothy grin, “You took the words right out of my mouth. This endless cycle is simply the result of--” The wolfman's sentence is cut off as a loud tremor suddenly wracks the forest, leaves raining from trees as the ground trembles beneath them. Looking around for any potential source, Kairos’ gaze turns to the sky as the endless sea of stars lights up.

    “Orveus, look!” Kairos yells, pointing skyward.

    The wolfman's golden eyes widen as they follow the light's descent into the forest.

    Wasting no time, the blonde haired human hastily grabs his baldric, drawing his sword and shield as he slips the belt over his shoulder. Ignoring Orveus’ protests to wait, Kairos sprints in the direction of the light, his weapon drawn.

    Nearing the potential landing point, the human's pace slows. His blue eyes shut as the open grove is illuminated by an overpowering, blinding light. Darting behind a nearby tree for protection, Kairos peers into the small clearing as the light fades.
