    ---

    What was probably the biggest thing, in terms of attention, was the IAC Games, which still had both the "Beach Bod" Competiton on Mieni Atoll, the IAC Flyer Games, which was divided into "modern birds" and "dinosaurs" (since flying reptiles technically weren't dinosaurs), and the "throwing" and "jumping" part of the Track and Field events. One, relatively minor, thing became a low-level meme.

    One mare Animalian competing in the long jump was caught adjusting her track bottoms after her jump. The caption that accompanied the image was as follws:

    It's not enough for female long jumpers to jump farther than 99% of all people. They have to do it in tiny underwear that gives them a wedgie when they do so.

    Another thing was an indirect result of Lars' and Phillip's conversion: a large number of divorcees coming to Animalia for conversions, and for new lives as well. It was no real secret that the majority of them were men who were on the receiving end of divorces. What made it surprising, though, was that a large number of them had roughly the same story: they'd been cheated on, dumped, then divorced. Fortunately, Animalia didn't discriminate against anyone who sought conversion, but they did require any planed meetings with exes be supervised by members of the Rhino Guard to prevent harm in case some divorcees considered revenge against their exes. While there were no threats of violence, the Rhino Guard's body cams did record some downright priceless reactions, mainly by the exes.

Like usual, he had broken even at the casino, but what made it different was that he had covered one of Miss Mallow's book signings.
