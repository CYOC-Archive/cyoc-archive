    added by SketchySeraph on 09/21/2016 22:25

    Tom didn’t need to try to respond that time: his stomach loudly rumbled again, seeming to echo. He ducked his head in mild embarrassment.

    It had been a few hours...

    Dawn laughed, slipping the vial out of her bag.

    “Oh! But first… I think we need to show the world that just because you look like a monster, you’re not really one. So give the camera a cute smile and a beg!” she said with an uncharacteristically happy chirp.

    Tom caught onto her meaning.

    He spun to Chris, beginning with a low hiss that evolved into a loud, clacking roar. Tom made sure to flick out his tongue and waggle his mandibles, enhancing the effect. And the second he ran out of air, he mewled a little, holding his mantis arms out in a praying posture as he spun to Dawn. He wasn’t sure how well puppy-dog eyes worked when you were a monstrous chimera, but it looked to do the job well.

    Dawn laughed, cupping his head under her hand to seal off his jaw. Then, she poured the vial into his mouth and Tom drank heartily. He gulped down the last bit, shuddering as his new limbs receded into his body and his old limbs returned to their normal state.

    “Oh, wow,” Dawn muttered, watching as Tom’s face reshaped.

    His muzzle pushed into his face first, leaving him with a tiger’s nose and the same feathered visage. Not to mention the fangs or the mandibles…

    The worst part of it all was how his mandibles were the last to return to normal--those were things that had no place on a person.

    “On the bright side, we can definitely use that footage to freak Philip out…” Dawn chuckled, petting Tom’s head as feathers gave way to hair once more.

    To her eternal delight his wings molted before pulling into him, leaving her with plenty of feathers.

    And to Tom’s delight, his human form didn’t have a trace of the chimera he’d been moments before.

    “All right,” he said, working his jaw just to make sure everything there was back where it should be. “That’s a wrap.”
