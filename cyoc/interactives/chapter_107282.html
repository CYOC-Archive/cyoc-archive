    “Maid.” The reedy voice of Jenny’s Employer rang out from the door.

    “Y-Yes Master Kelson?” Jenny tried, but failed to stop the stammer. She always became nervous when he spoke to her, not least because the manner of his speech to her was nearly always obstinate.

    “I am expecting guests within the hour, I want the meeting room ready for them before they arrive. Is that understood?” His tone made clear there was no room for debate.

    “Yes, Master Kelson.” Jenny replied submissively.

    “Good. Get to it.”

    The meeting room in question was a desk, several comfortable leather chairs, and a number of assorted decorations that all needed a good clean. With a sigh, she began at the top with the lighting and slowly worked her way down. The wall, paintings, the furnishings and only then could she sweep up the oaken floor, treated and maintained to assure a complete lack of cracks and splinters.

    “At least you do your money’s worth.” Richard Kelson finally stated after his inspection following her fifty-minute task that she barely finished when he entered. The closest thing to a compliment she could ever receive from him. “Now while they’re here, I expect you to remain out of their sight, so I want up the stairs and clean my daughter’s bedroom, followed by the bathroom. That should keep you busy long enough for me to complete my business with them.”

    “Yes, Master Kelson.” Jenny answered with the minimum words needed.

    Amanda Kelson’s Bedroom, something tugged on Jenny’s Heart when she saw it. The massive bed set to one side, the chests most likely filled with her toys, large wardrobe and drawers filling one whole wall, a desk and lamp on the other side. It, reminded her so much of what she used to have.

    And as she slowly wiped away every piece of dust, collected the ‘dirty’ clothes and bed sheets for washing later, and tidied up the desk. It slowly dawned on Jenny, that this, all of this, she had taken for granted back before…

    But there was nothing she could do now. Thanks to how Kyle had worded his wish, there was no way for her to reverse the Curse. All she could do anymore, was try to live as best as she could with what she had.

    Which wasn’t all that much really, with only 30 hours work each week - split into six five-hour shifts that meant she only ever saw Amanda on the Saturdays - that allowed Mr Kelson to pay her at the lower rate. Her parents working 40 and 48 hours a week respectively for not that much more. True, they were making enough to get by, but her new memories of this world told her that that made them better off than so many other lizard families in this New World. In fact, not even half could regularly afford their own clothing to wear.
