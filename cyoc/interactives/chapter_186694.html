    After becoming a new movie directors for TV Team Studios in Hollywood California, Stanford Starlight was in trouble. It was up to him to make a movie for the studio. Stanford had all the producers, studio executives, makeup group, camera crew, and of course lunch buffet to help him with that sort of stuff.

    There was only one thing missing, as he told his agent about it. "I need one more actor for this role," Stanford explains. "But sir, all of the other actors in town are busy, and we basically got every actor willing to sign up for this kind of movie," the agent says. "Fuck," Stanford comments. "I am definitely fired, if I don't find the leading role soon." "Too bad we can't just find a random person from a suburban town in this country to have him, or her become the leading role," the agent says.

    Suddenly, Stanford's face changed just as soon as his agent told him that. "Great idea," Stanford says. "What idea?" the agent asks. "Go take the limo, and find any suburban person I can have to be the lead actor," Stanford answers, by giving his agent the keys to the limo. "Sir, I was being sarcastic when I said that," his agent says. "It'll take days to find someone for the leading role." "I don't care if you have to grab someone from a house or anything," Stanford explains. "Just go find someone, or I'll be opening another role for my new agent." "Yes sir," the agent says, and uses the limo to drive to town, and find someone useful to be in the leading role for Stanford's movie.
