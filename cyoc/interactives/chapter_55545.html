    Kathy didn't know why she felt the need to check up on her family tree. Maybe the incident
    with the Game turning her into a mouth-piece puppet to mutate her family and everybody
    nearby had left her curious about that period in history.

    Sadly all the archives about such things were in Glendale, and after the horror of Kurse,
    Kathy felt even less eager to return to that cursed town.

    This left Kathy with some faded family albums, the words of her grandparents (her
    mother's, father's, and step-father's), and a 'sweet dreams' fairy figurine goodluck charm
    that had been in the family for generations accord to her real father.

    The half-dragon twins (born after her mother had remarried) were of course scared stiff by
    what happened, but had quickly recovered and now wanted to know all they could about
    the game (likely fantasizing using it to becoming full dragons, or super heroes, or ninjas,
    or pirates).

    Glendale had been under the effects of a plague at the time, so records however dealt
    mostly with that rather than the so-called myth of witchcraft.

    Finding a family tree in the attic with yellowing paper, Kathy found that her family had
    come across the atlantic around the year 1800, and had split up settling in Salem,
    Frostvile, and Glendale. Of course Kathy knew that already, her family had relocated here
    because they were fellow Felman here.

    Kathy was glade the curse at least hadn't turned her into a leoman, or a gargoyle, or a
    lupman.... all their fur colors were so drab, so faded, so dark, so bland. Kathy's
    grandparent had said they had to be logically related to the Wolf Pack, since those
    wonderful bright colors in the lupmans' fur had to come from somewhere.

    Kathy remembered somewhere that the Indian word for vegetarian was 'bad hunter', she
    didn't know if it was true or not, but had been a joke among the other hunting species
    eons ago that felman were pathetic hunters since they had all the sublimity and stealth of a
    marching band due to fur color that you'd also see on poison arrow frogs.

    Some even figured that felman had survived BECAUSE their bright fur colors made them
    resemble poisonous creatures, like supposedly some species of butterflies had wing
    patterns that imitated more poisonous species of insect.

    While leomans might have invented civilization, humans and felmans were the first to pick
    up on it, lupmans and dragons stubbornly rejecting it for half of recorded history of the
    species' of man.

    Kathy found what she already knew, that her tattoos (and those of her brothers, mother,
    father and one set of grandparents) were ancestral and were supposed to contain bits of
    her family history if she knew how to read them. Apparently it was a trait felman in Africa
    had picked up from the local leoman, which had since mostly fallen out of style.

    Apparently some felman had been brought over as 'discount' slaves from Africa, brought
    over purely as an afterthought since they couldn't fit humans into the space they had left.
    This had been in the 1820s.

    'At least that explains where the half-naked members of my family came from.' Kathy
    thought.

    However, for leomans, only the matriarch of the Pride was tattooed anymore. And the only
    felman besides Kathy and her relatives who still practiced it were some fundamentalist
    pockets in Africa, and a the first born of families in Asia... There were some teenage
    felmans in Japan who still apparently ancestral tattooed themselves. But their style was
    considered a total bastardization and generalization of the tradition and style even by
    those who didn't practice the ritual tattooing.

    Kathy couldn't find anything with her limit research material on WHY her forefathers had
    come across the ocean. She figured it must have been opportunity, or good old fashioned
    curiosity (though Kathy cringed at the stereotype).

    Kathy also figured it might have been the traditional fear of oblivion, and wanting to
    spread their seed as far as possible least another plague hit Europe. After all, even today,
    there were only sixty million felman in the world.... one percent of the entire world's
    population. Dragons numbers were even smaller.

    "I hope my letter gets to that boy... he was a real hero for someone who wasn't gifted to be
    born a felman... Well, it's not like I'm going to get a response in a day.... " Kathy wasn't
    about to let it consume her ever waking thought however, and thought instead of the
    modeling agency that had offered to hire her as soon as she graduated.
