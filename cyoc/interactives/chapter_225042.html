    The agents continued towards the security station, it was a rather tall black building with a helipad. The path they took was scattered with pieces of another helicopter, it seemed this chopper was sent crashing like their own transport. It seemed most zombies they encountered on this path were dressed in security uniforms with body armor, the two assumed the security forces were scattered.

    —————————

    A green magical window closed and Jolie bit her dark green painted nails.

    “Damn it! Why is that mennie Geshp here?!” She wined and floated towards a mass of flesh that formed a cocoon in the middle of the room. Flesh like veins protruded from it, snaking off in every direction and giving off an ominous green glow.

    “No no no! This means I have to move up my plans, I’m so close, that damned demon is going to ruin everything!” She hissed.

    The teenage necromancer adjusted her long, sleeveless lace dress with a corset that managed to plump up her small chest. Jolie had icy blue, almost white eyes and her arms were covered in various sinister markings and arcane symbols, her dress was long enough to cover her bare feet and her white hair was in a messy bob.
    Jolie Deathflower used to be a vampire and was killed by Kane. But she was an accomplished enough sorcerer that she was able to resurrect herself using a special ethereal flower that was her last-namesake and became a necromancer, freeing her of her vampiric limitations and giving her the ability to see into the realm of death. This allowed her to find souls that others could not.

    The girl turned to her latest creation, a winged beast of sickness and bone: the Drakmire. An undead dragon brought to life with a thousand souls. Its fire turned to acid with corruption, skin poison to the touch, its claws and teeth festering with toxins: Death made flesh!

    “This should keep that demon busy for a bit.” She said and then opened a green portal,

    “Maybe a few hundred undead holy knights for insurance, their souls are extra hard to reach but well worth the effort.”

    Jolie giggled in childish glee, she excitedly twirled and used her magic to teleport the small army near Geshp’s location and turned her focus to the two agents making their way to the main security building.

    “I think you two will have a hard time getting past the special surprise I have waiting for you!” She giggled.

    Inside the security building a pair of red eyes glowed in the dark.

    While Hailey hiked along the metal strewn trail she was more uncomfortable at how that demon had leered at her, it sent shivers down her spine just thinking about it.
    Henry seemed to notice this and broke up the awkward silence,

    “Hey don’t feel so bad. I mean he didn’t even look at me, it’s like all those hours a day at the gym were for nothing.” He said with a smile that seemed to infect her.

    “You weren’t the one who was practically in her underwear in front of some wannabe playboy.” She chirped back.

    “If you want I can give you my shirt.” He said.

    “No thanks. I think I rather be naked.” She replied in reference to his stupid Hawaiian shirt.

    “How about this: next time we run into some double d demon lady I’ll rip off all my clothes.” He suggested.

    Before the blonde could respond to this a sound from the treetops caught the pair’s attention. Henry pulled the hammer back on his pistol and Hailey got into a fighting stance, they were both ready for whatever was nearing them.
    More undead but these ones were different; they appeared more emaciated and their skin was grey and sallow. They screeched like hellhounds and lunged at the two with their long claw like nails and pointed teeth.
    Henry began firing shots at the air bound creatures and when they got close Hailey sent one flying with a kick and fought the rest back with her fists. After the first wave was dealt with they heard even more mc reaching from the trees. They looked at each other and realized they could fight back any more of those things and took off running with incredible speed.

    “The security station is just ahead, we’ll be safer inside!” She shouted.

    Henry agreed with a nod and the two sprinted to the building, smashing through the glass doors. Henry landed on his back with his pistols drawn where they entered from but the creatures stopped a few feet from the building.

    “Hey, why do you think they’re not chasing us in here?” The blonde said uncertainty.

    Henry looked to the creatures and back to his partner.

    “I don’t know but my gut’s telling me that there’s something in here they don’t like.” He responded.

    “That’s good right?” She asked.

    “I won’t be so sure, there’s probably something worse in here. But we don’t have much of a choice.” He said as she helped him to his feet.

    “Let’s just find the armory and maybe some clothes for me.” She said.

    Henry agreed and the two wearily made their was deeper into the building.
