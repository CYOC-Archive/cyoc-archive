    "Well," said Chris to his sometime lover, "are you going to move or just stare at the pretty, blinking lights?"

    "I'm thinking about it ... I'm thinking about it..." Dave sighed as he considered his options. While he didn't think it was likely he'd be changed into a lion like Sid, practically anything could be on the next card. His mind boggled at the sorts of changes this game might have in store for its players. He shook his head. "Sorry, Sid; I just can't take the risk. I'm goin' the long way 'round..."

    Taking the piece in his finger tips, Dave moved the remaining spaces to the green square.

    "Ok," he said looking to his left. "Your turn, Chris..."

    "Wait; look!"

    Everyone stared at the board. Like a wave washing over a beach, all of the green squares on the board shimmered. Small, white letters appeared on each space, scrolling as if windows in a slot machine.

    "What ... what do they say?" asked Chris, staring, confused, at all the flickering green squares.

    The words started to slow and the four friends could soon read them. "MENTAL", "SOCIAL", "PHYSICAL", "MYSTIC" and "RANDOM" were the apparent choices. Dave felt his heart fall and suppressed a shudder.

    "Oh, no..."

    "Shit; this is worse than the cards!" muttered Tyler with an exasperated look on his face.

    Soon, each green square slowed, slowed and came to a stop. Each one now had the small word "MYSTIC" on it. The remaining blue, red, yellow, pink and black squares still had no writing.

    "Mystic?" asked Dave, incredulously. "What's that supposed to mean?"

    Before the others could answer, however, the small, clear space in the center of the board wavered as if suddenly covered with mist. However, the blackness there slowly expanded, pushing the white wisps into solid shapes of letters and words. Before the surprised eyes of the players, a rhyme formed in the center of the board.

    TEMPORARY CHANGE
    - 5 turns -

    "As in the past, were the Daemons laid low,
    So shall you be lest an answer, you know.
    More valued than power or riches or fame,
    On penalty of curse, shout your True Name."
    "What th' fuck does that mean?" demanded Dave of no one in particular. "I mean, my True Name? What's that mean? My name's 'Dave'!"

    Chris shook his head and quickly put his hand on Dave's wrist. "No, no... It's not like that, hon. It's an old tradition from mythology."

    The other two looked at Chris. As a student of mythology if anyone would know this one, it was him. Having put aside his hurt feelings from Dave's earlier comments, the effeminate player tried to explain.

    "It's like this: in old legends, the true power of man was supposedly that he could name things. That was what he could do. Nothing else had that power. Not even angels or demons. In fact, only God could name an angel. So demons, being soulless, had to have someone give them a True Name in order for them to come to the physical world or do anything. Otherwise they were just sorta ... helpless; nothing more than spiritual energy.

    Chris continued, his effeminate lilt almost making the dire words sound silly. "The thing is, once a demon had been named by a mortal it could be controlled by anyone who knew that name. So, if it could, it would try to kill the person who'd named it, keep the name and then keep it hidden so no one else could ever command it." He sat back, looking into Dave's eyes meaningfully. "I think what the game wants you to do is shout out your True Name..."

    "That's easy...!" chuckled Dave, a wave of relief rushing over him. Standing up, he looked at the ceiling and shouted, "MY NAME IS DAVE KEENE!"

    "WAIT! Hon, no!"

    But Chris' warning came too late.
