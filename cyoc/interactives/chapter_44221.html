    Nothing happened.

    He tried again.

    Still nothing. Maybe it wasn't magic after all? He didn't really believe in all that stuff anyway, but what about the apartment building? Apartment buildings didn't spontaneously turn into chicken coups, he didn't believe in that sort of thing either - or that people could turn into chickens. But he had seen that.

    Maybe the movies got it all wrong, maybe it didn't work by rubbing it. "Genie?" he tried calling into it. He knocked on it, "Hello, anybody home?"

    Still nothing.

    "Dammit," Jay muttered. He chucked the lamp across the room in frustration. "I was stupid to think it was magic anyway. I wish for once something would work in my life."

    "That was easy."

    Jay jumped. A beautiful blonde haired woman dressed as a genie was standing next to him. In fact she looked just like Barbara Eden from the old sitcom "I Dream of Genie" that Jay had watched reruns of as a kid at his grandparents. He couldn't believe it, this was too good to be true.

    "You- you're... a genie." He stuttered.

    "Yes Master," she smiled at him.

    "M-m-master?" Definately too good to be true. "I'm your master?"

    "Yes Master," the genie smiled patiently. "I am bound to serve you and grant you any wish for as long as you are my Master."
