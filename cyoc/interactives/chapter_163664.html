    You couldn't see a thing in the pitch blackness that surrounded you, but you could tell that you were being carried forward by the mysterious machinery like a toy on a production line.

    Blinking gizmos lined the walls of the chamber, stacked all the way up to the ceiling. A full size mirror was mounted on one wall, and there was a large mechanical door marked "EXIT TO PARK".

    Unfortunately, the same rabbit who had gotten you into this mess was now standing between you and the exit.
    "Hey, what's-", is all you managed to say before you froze in shock at the high-pitched, cutesy voice that had just emerged.
    "W-what's wrong with my voice?" you squeaked.

She didn't have hair, but a small cluster of feathers on her forehead mimicked bangs, and at the top of her head towards the back, more feathers had been tied into a bunch which stood straight up.

You raised a feathery arm and touched the soft, downy fluff that now covered your cheek, and the doppelganger in the mirror mimicked you exactly. That's when you noticed the tag tied around your wrist, resembling the kind you might see on a new toy.

    "ANIMAL FRIENDS SERIES 5 - DIXIE DRUMSTICK" It read, with a winking cartoon depiction of the mascot character you now found yourself trapped inside. What the hell was going on here?

"THANK YOU FOR AGREEING TO TAKE PART IN WHIMSY WORLD'S ONCE-IN-A-LIFETIME MASCOT EXPERIENCE. YOU MAY NOW CHOOSE THE LENGTH OF YOUR STAY. YOU WILL BE COMPENSATED FAIRLY AT THE END OF YOUR SERVICE AND YOUR ABSENCE WILL GO UNNOTICED.
    CONTRACT WILL BE TERMINATED AT END OF DURATION - UNLESS YOU CHOOSE TO TAKE ON A PERMANENT POSITION.
    PLEASE SELECT DURATION NOW."

    "Wait what? I didn't agree to this!" you squawked, flinching again at your squeaky new voice.
    The rabbit just shook his head and pointed at the device.
    There was a keypad with numbers from 0 to 9, a green button labeled OK and a red button labeled NO.
    It was unclear what these numbers even meant. Days? Months?
    "How do I cancel?" you demanded, but the rabbit just shook his head again.

    Feeling frustrated, you mashed the NO key, hoping it would register as a cancellation.
    The device beeped in acknowledgement and the rabbit quickly snatched it back from your feathered hands.
    "YOU HAVE SELECTED: NO TERMINATION DATE.
    CONTRACT HAS BEEN ADJUSTED TO: PERMANENT.
    THANK YOU AND WELCOME TO THE FAMILY." it buzzed in a detached, emotionless tone of voice.

    Your mouth - or rather your beak - dropped open in shock.
    As you began to protest, the rabbit mascot pressed a button on the wall and a soothing, somewhat distorted song began to play from speakers mounted close to the ceiling. The music, sounding like a crackly cross between elevator muzak and a child's lullaby, seemed to instantly sap away your energy and you felt your mind clouding over, your senses becoming dull.
    Your vision became increasingly blurry and you could just barely make out the shape of the white rabbit mascot, who now appeared to be gleefully waltzing around you in circles to the tune of the mysterious jingle. Soon your vision and your consciousness faded entirely.

    You found yourself in the middle of the park's main plaza, posing with a pair of twin girls no older than 8 as their proud father took photo after photo.
    Yes of course, that was your job. You remembered them approaching you, and the adorable glee in the children's faces as they asked for a picture. How could you possibly refuse?

    After your little photo session concluded and you waved goodbye to the girls, you found yourself compelled to roam the park in search of more kids in need of a little cheer. There was something else though, in the back of your mind. You still felt groggy, but you were certain that something else had been bothering you.
    You managed to recall something through the hypnotic fog. Your... family? You wanted to find them for... some reason?
