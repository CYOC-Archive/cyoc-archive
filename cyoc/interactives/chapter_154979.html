    added by Stark A month ago A Mythological Reptilian

    Erin was a lamia. She appeared to be completely human from the waist up, but from the waist down she had the body of an enormous serpent—with bright green scales like those of a tree python. Her tail was coiled underneath her body so that she could hold herself upright, and even then, it was long enough that the rest of it stretched out behind her and continued all the way back into the kitchen, where it disappeared from view.

    Erin's human half was absolutely gorgeous: the only article of clothing she wore was what appeared to be the top half of a golden metal bikini, like a bra made from links of mail and little round golden plates that resembled coins—very "Dejah Thoris"—which did surprising little to hide her impressively large, gravity-defying breasts.

    Her face was cute and framed by her short bob of dark red hair, which contrasted with her green snake-scales in a way that reminded you of the Little Mermaid.

    "I hope you like steak," she said, holding up a platter with some choice cuts, hot off the grill.

    "I—uh—it's my favorite," said John, doing his level-best to keep his voice even. He stood motionless, rooted to that spot on the floor, and babbled, "Yep, yessiree, yes-indeed, I love to eat some snake. I mean some steak. I mean you're a snake. I mean—you're a . . . a serpent-woman?"

    Now it was Erin's turn to boggle at John. She stared at him for a moment, her jaw dropped, doing a fantastic impression of a surprised fish. Then, in a very timid voice, she said, "You can see my—my tail?"

    "It's pretty hard to miss," said John.

    "Oh. Oh, wow." Erin looked as if she were going to faint. She slithered over the dinner-table so that she could set down the dinner-platter. Then she pulled out one of the chairs and sank into it, a look of fearful anticipation on her adorable face. "This must be such a shock to you," she said. From her tone, John could tell that she expected him to flee screaming out into the streets.

    "You could say that," said John. "But," he added with a shrug of his shoulders, "I like to think I'm open minded. I like to read a lot of science-fiction and fantasy, and that's probably why I'm not freaking out right now." He paused. "I *should* be freaking out right now, shouldn't I?"

    Erin smiled weakly. "That *is* how most guys normally react when I finally show them the real me. But I've never sprung the fact that I'm a—a big, creepy-crawly snake-monster—on a guy during the first date."

    "I don't think you're a monster," said John, walking over to the dinner table and sitting down. "But now you do have me curious. What's it like to be a—" John thought for a moment and tried, "—naga?"

    "Lamia," said Erin with a weak grin. She held up both her arms, waved her hands, and said, "I have arms. Therefore: lamia, not naga."

    "Lamia, then," said John. "How do you live around here, without being found out? If you don't mind telling me, I promise not to run away screaming."

    "Then . . . you're still interested in our date?" asked Erin. Her voice was still meek, as if she feared rejection, and there was no disguising the hint of hope in it.

    John thought about how disastrous his last few dates had been, and how much nicer and more normal Erin seemed, even in spite of the fact that she had the lower half of a giant prey-crushing constrictor. "More than ever," he admitted.

    "All right," said Erin. "Let's have dinner, and I'll tell you anything you want to know."
