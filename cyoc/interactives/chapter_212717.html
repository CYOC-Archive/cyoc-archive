    Chad watched with awe as his friend quickly moved up the trunk of the massive tree, grabbing vines and branches with perfect timing to move higher and higher into its canopy. As Rob got higher up, however, Chad frowned as he noticed Rob beginning to change before his very eyes. Rob’s short hair slowly began to grow longer, flowing past his ears and over his neck until it hung just past his shoulders. Not only that, its sandy brown shade became deeper and glossier, taking on the color of dark chocolate or black coffee.

    “Rob, stop!! You’re losing yourself to the jungle!” Chad cried out, his voice cracking one final time before settling on the fine soprano of a young boy. “You can’t go any higher or-”

    “Na-na-na~ I can’t hear you~” Rob teased childishly in a cracking tenor from high above. He hooked his slender legs around a sturdy tree branch and hung upside down, staring down to the ground at Chad while sticking his tongue out tauntingly. “You’re just jealous that I’m the best climber in the whole tribe! You’ll never catch me!”

Putting his hand over hand, he ascended the vine straight upward until he had nearly caught up to where Rob was, not even noticing the tickle on the back of his own neck as his hair began to straighten and darken to a similar coffee shade as Rob’s. Chad swung his legs to pick up enough momentum and leapt from the vine, landing perfectly on the nearest branch before clambering up the tree’s trunk after Rob. By now, he’d half-forgotten exactly why he was chasing after Rob as the childishly competitive spirit that was blossoming inside him was beginning to take over.

No sign of clear-cut jungle, no cities rising over the trees, not even the slightest indication of a road winding through the seemingly endless jungle. For all he knew, him and Rob had not just been transported from their homes to this wilderness, but they’d also been thrown thousands of years back in time.
