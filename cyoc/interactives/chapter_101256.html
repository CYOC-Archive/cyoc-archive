    “We’re home!” Francis and Samantha Twist called into their home. They found Ben and Alex in the kitchen, eating cinnamon pancakes. The smell of the food masked any other scents floating around the house. One of the reasons Ben chose to prepare something with such a strong smell. And it was his favorite breakfast, so it all worked out.

    “Did you two have a good night?” Ben asked.

It was nice, something they hadn’t done in a long while. Sure they had their “fun” nights, with or without additional company, but this was different. They spent the night talking about some more private things. And of course a great deal of the night had been reserved for cuddling (wink wink).

    “Thank you.” he said after Francis pulled away. Samantha laughed, the poor kid seemingly dumbfounded.

    “Do you two have anything planned for the day?” Ben asked.

    “Not really. Why?” Samantha asked. She helped herself to a plate of food, while Francis started a pot of coffee.

    “I’m going over the Lawsons for a few hours before I leave. Ben said he was going to be doing chores.” Alex says. His mothers look at Ben surprised.

    “Got nothing better to do. Homework’s finished. And I don’t have interest in motorcycles.” he explained. Though it left more questions than it answered them.

    “Spencer’s dad rebuilds motorcycles.” Ben said.

    “Oh, right. Maggie said he had a rather messy hobby.” Samantha chuckled. Maggie had sent emails detailing the Sundays her husband would come inside from the garage covered in motor oil and touch everything in sight. On one occasion she’d locked him out of the house until he washed himself off outside.

    “That’ll be nice. But I wasn’t aware Eric had any interest in that sort of thing.” Francis said.

    “He doesn’t. It’s probably just an excuse to hand out with Spencer.” Ben said. Again, his mothers gave him confused looks. Ben and Alex both explained the events of last night. Francis and Samantha were both a bit surprised to learn the younger Anderson was the one who ended up with Spencer.

    “Ya, we thought she’d end up with Nate too. But Nate’s got some other girl apparently. No idea who it is,” Ben said.

    “Well, you wouldn’t mind too much if we tagged along with you Alex? Seems like Martha and Maggie are both going to be bored out of their minds later.” Samantha said, only half joking. Alex shook his head, and said it wasn’t his place to tell them what to do.

    “I spend so much time here, you two shouldn’t have to ask my opinion of things. And on that note, I’d better check in with my own parents.” Alex said.

    “I’ll drive you. See you later mom,” Ben said, quickly following Alex out the door. Samantha and Francis watched as their son left, arm-in-arm with his boyfriend.

    “You think they’ll ever get married?” Francis asked.

    “I think there’s a strong possibility. But who knows, they may find other people who make them even happier. I went through high school with several different girlfriends, each of whom I could see myself with for the future. But I’m glad I waited.” Samantha said, kissing her wife.
