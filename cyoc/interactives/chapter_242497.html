    "Can't you tell, babe?" Harper grinned, already geeking out. "She's a sarugami, also known as a saru-yōkai or enjin. They're monkey people who live in the mountains of Japan."

    "And in the mountains and forests of China!" Linghua sniffed indignantly. "And in the jungles of India and Southeast Asia, for that matter. And we don't go by those Japanese names in other countries. To me, we're called hóu yaoguai. In India, we're known as vanaras. But enough technicalities. I'm so excited to meet you all!"

    "So, why is this gaijin here?" hissed Shizuka, who was slouched against the wall of the dormitory dodging work.

    "Nobody asked you to be rude, Shizuka-san!" Kyūri barked. "Now get back to sweeping or I'm tossing you into the river!" Kyūri then turned to Beck and Harper, a kinder expression on his turtle-like face. "To answer Shizuka's question, I invited Sun Linghua here to give us a demonstration on staff fighting, the signature martial art of her clan. She's supposed to be quite proficient. I'll be letting all of my students challenge her. If Linghua-san wins, she gets cash prize of 15,000 yen per student she beats. That's about 103 dollars in your American money. If she beats ALL of you, she gets a 35,000-yen bonus. Any rikishi who beats her gets an extra portion of my chankonabe."

    "Heh, the magic words," Beck snickered to hir mate.

    "It will be a prize for beating our esteemed guest at staff fighting," Kyūri said. "Meet Sun Linghua, champion staff fighter."

    "Staff fighter?" Ellie asked.

    "The great art of my people!" Linghua grinned, twirling her staff so fast in her hand that it blurred. "Taught to us by our beloved king Sun Wukong himself!"

    "Sun Wukong?" Harper grinned. "From Journey to the West? He's real? Awesome!"

    "Hey, it's a popular story in Japan, too! They named Goku after the Chinese pronunciation of Sun Wukong's name. Japan also made a live-action TV adaptation of Journey to the West called Saiyūki. There's an English dub called Monkey that became a cult fave in Australia."

    "The 1986 CCTV series was better," Lignhua chuckled. "That show is required TV viewing if you're Chinese."

    "Yaoguai watch TV?" Harper marveled.

    "Hey, some of us believe in keeping up with the times," Linghua grinned. "Aren't most nekomimi J-pop idols these days?"

    "Grandpa, what's all the talking about?" asked little Nariko as she scampered out to find her grandfather.

    "Linghua-san is here, Nariko," Kyūri responded. "Could you ring the gong to summon all the rikishi? They should meet her."

The more of you I beat, the more money Big K has to fork over to me! If any of you somehow beat me, you get an extra bowl of that chankonabe that makes you into big ol' porkies. I don't understand the Zhu Bajie look myself, but to each their own. Now I'd like to get to know you all a little better. Don't be shy!" She winked roguishly.

    "You're the substitute Big K, got it," Linghua nodded.

    As Haia returned to the crowd, Shizuka reluctantly marched up to Linghua. "I guess you sorta know me already from your arrival," shi grimaced. "Let's keep this short, okay? My name is Shizuka Zayasu, I'm a bakeneko, I'm a direct descendant of angry ghost cats, I don't do well with humans or foreigners, and my hobbies are sumo wrestling and glaring at irritating people until they leave me alone. That's really all you need to know, monkey." With that, the surly cat yōkai sprinted back into the crowd with surprising grace considering her impressive sumo girth.

I love sumo, drumming, dancing, blowing loads, walks by the river, and hanging out with all my friends! Wanna be my friend?!" "I'm Maruko Futobara. Please excuse my appearance. I had a rough breakup and lost a lot of weight. I'm slowly gaining it back thanks to the chankonabe, but I'm still pretty deflated."

    "Same!" Linghua grinned.

    "I came here because I wrecked my wing and got to feeling pretty worthless," Tsubame went on. "I'm hoping that sumo can give me a purpose."

    "I bet it can," Linghua encouraged. "Staff fighting gives me a really strong sense of identity. I bet sumo will be the same for you." Tsubame nodded and returned to the crowd.

    An oni with pale green skin, wild black hair, a single rhino-like horn growing from hir forehead, sharp claws, and a pair of wicked tusks jutting up from hir lower jaw was next. Shi had a decent layer of pudge clinging to hir powerful muscles. "Pleased to meet you, Linghua-san. I'm Kijo Onidzuyo. I've been rocking the amateur sumo circuit for years. I came here to show Japan that girls can be great rikishi... and then Kyūri-sensei had a witch put a dick on me." Shi rolled hir eyes. "Oh well. It makes for some pretty good masturbating.     "Agreed!" Hōman nodded with a smile as shi returned to the crowd.

    A two-tailed kitsune with just a hint of pudge walked up next. "Hi, Linghua. I'm Misaki Satō. I actually haven't been a kitsune for very long. I was a human girl, a miko at my father's Inari shrine. I stumbled into this heya by accident, and one thing just sorta led to another."

    "That's how life should be," Linghua smiled. "One crazy thing leading to another."

    Next came a smiling kawauso who was only beginning to develop the first hint of a gut. "Hi there, Linghua! I'm Taki Morikawa. We kawauso have a long and storied sumo tradition, but we're being forgotten as our numbers dwindle. I came here to remind the yōkai community what the kawauso are made of."

    "I wish you luck," Linghua nodded with unusual respect for the cheeky monkey.

    Kristi and Joelle came next. "Hi!" Kristi said. "My name's Kristi Wenzel, and this is my girlfriend Joelle Bowman. I was a camgirl who specialized in the BBW subculture. Then I accidentally put on a werefox transformation belt and got saddled with werebeast metabolism. I'm here to get my belly back... and maybe find a new passion."

    "And I'm moral support," Joelle added. "I had to become a futa kawauso to stay here, and it's honestly working for me!"

    "Always nice to discover new things about ourselves," Linghua nodded.

    The werehyenas all came up together. They all politely bowed. "You kinda already know us," Ellie began, "but we're Ellie Rubenstein, Harper Browning, Beck Hamilton, and Brianna Dixon. We're Americans like Kristi and Joelle, and we're in a werehyena pack from Washington state. Our Japan adventure's happening because I used to be human and enjoyed being chubby, but my werehyena transformation flattened by belly. Then Beck got interested in getting some flab, and our mates tagged along to keep us company. Plus, Harper's a weeb."
