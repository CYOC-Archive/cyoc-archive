    "Goddamnit!" Dan yelled out. "How can we live like this?" He said. Sean was so sorry but he knew they couldn't
    do anything now. Jay didn't seem so angry but he definitely didn't like it.

    "Dan, calm down. Sean, you're sure, there is absolutely no way to reverse this?" Jay asked somewhat calm,
    surprisingly. Sean nodded unable to speak at the pain he was causing his friends.

    "Dan, get back here." Jay's tone made it certain that Dan would listen. Dan rejoined their body reluctantly.
    The fusion back to the body was much smoother and less straining then the separation.

    "At least, we can fuse back together pretty easily, right?" Sean said, but he could feel their emotions.

    "Dan, Sean is just as struck by this event as we are. We have to comfort him because we're are all in this
    together, til the end. We do everything together, anyhow. I really don't see what closing the gap will
    change." Jay said, making Sean feel much better about the whole thing. "Dan? Do you have anything to say?"

    "I guess you're right." Dan said, with a still grumpy face. "We do do everything together. Now, I suppose we
    have to. We'll have to go get our classes switched around and make compromises but I suppose we can live with
    this. At least, we can say we're unique." Dan said. "But let's never have our faces conjoined like that again.
    That was just weird." Sean smiled knowing his friends weren't as angry with him now.

    "Do you guys wanna experiment with our form?" Sean asked his friends.
