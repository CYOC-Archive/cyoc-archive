You dial the landlord's number, and then you here him pick up his phone on the other end. "Hello," Gus says. "Excuse me, Mr. Armstrong," You begin to say.

    "Ah, Mrs. Hyde," Gus responds. "What can I do for you?" "I want to sell my apartment," You explain. "My aunt has left me in charge of her old antique store, while she's moving to New York to meet with her fiance. So I was hoping you could take the apartment off my hands, while I move into the store." "Well considering that you've been paying rent, sure I'll help sell," Gus says. "But, I must warn you, that your Aunt's old shop is very old, meaning no one has allowed rental to the apartment upstairs there."

    "Wait, does that mean I don't have to worry about the landlord there, because there isn't one?" You ask. "Of course, and we could strike a deal, if you can get me something from the shop that might strike my fancy," Gus answers. "I'll see what I can do," You say, and Gus hangs up. You then decide to think on what to get him, while you continue packing your things to help move in.
