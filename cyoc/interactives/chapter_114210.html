    With a variety of roars, barks, yells, and other sounds you usually associate with animals, you watch from your living room window in horror as the horde of animals comes rushing forward, all of them hitting the front side of your house at once. They begin banging on your locked front door and windows, all making strange noises and staring at you with lustful eyes. Horrified by what you have done, you back up with your hands out in front of you just as a scaled lizard hand punches through the diamond of glass on your front door and begins scrabbling around for the lock. You turn and run up the stairs, not looking back.

    Not sure of where to hide, you turn and dive into your bedroom just as you hear the triumphant noises from below as your front door is muscled off it's hinges. With really no other option, you run into your closet and shut the door. The door is one of those with downward, diagonally-slanting slats of wood, so you can see out, but no one in the bedroom can see in. Breathing heavily and sweating, you pee out with apprehensive eyes.

    At first, you hear the rampage downstairs as all the animals tear apart your living room, kitchen, and dining room lookin for you. You breathe a sigh of relief, hoping that the transformation has rendered them dumb enough not to look upstairs. But this moment of comfort is quickly dispelled by the sound of heavy footfalls on the stairs, followed by the door of your bedroom bursting open.

    Gulping, you watch as an anthro gorilla walks into your line of sight, colossal and huge, almost blocking out the light from the fixture above your bed. You watch as it sniffs, looking around the room, but it appears not to think of the closet. It walks out your line of sight just as you breath a sigh of relief...

    And suddenly, with a loud yell, the gorilla tears open the door to your closet and dives for you, dragging you over to your bed. Screaming, you beat and kick at it's huge legs and arms, but to no avail. The gorilla throws you on the bed and pounces on you. You still try to wrestle it, but it easily pins you to the mattress. It yells out again and you close your eyes, ready to accept the loss of your humanity. Surprisingly, you feel you arms grow huge and bulge rapidly, ripping the sleeves of your shirt. You look over to see our hands withering, becoming black and leathery. You realizing that you now have the arms of the gorilla that is pinning you.

    When all of a sudden, a loud roar echoes from the door of the room. Both you and the gorilla turn around in puzzlement just as an anthro jaguar appears in the doorway. It huge and muscular, perhaps even bigger than the gorilla. Outraged that it will not be the one to take your humanity, it roars again and dives across the room, knocking the screaming gorilla off of you and onto the carpet on the other side of the bed. You feel funny and look down at your right leg, and notice that it is bulking with muscle like your arms did, only it quickly grows spotted, tawny-and-black fur as your feet merge, and fatten. Your leg has transformed into the leg of the leopard. It must have grazed you as it dove for the gorilla. You hear them wrestling, and given their sameness in size, it appears the fight will go on for a long time. You take the opportunity and run out the door, leaving the animals fighting on your bedroom floor.

    However, just as you run out, you are kicked in the left leg by something. Looking up, you see the anthro deer from earlier in your yard. It's triangular head and black beady eyes stare at you with triumph as it kicks you again. You howl out in pain and watch as your left leg becomes slim and thin, fine brown hair smoothing it out as your foot balls up and turns hard, becoming a deer leg. You raise your gorilla arms up to ward it off as it lifts it's hand up to punch you in the face, but it misses and ends up crashing down the stairs behind you, knocking itself out.

    You hear the gorilla and jaguar still fighting, but the rest of the animals seems to be downstairs. You look yourself over; you have the arms of a gorilla, one leg of a jaguar, and one leg of a deer. So it appears that the transformation from these animals are localized to where theY touch you. But questions are running through your mind. Are the changes permanent? Ify ou got touched on the arm by, say, a horse, will your gorilla arms change to horse arms? And what if a human touches you? You're not sure, but you decide you better figure this out quickly.
