Amy didn't feel like taking the time to find him, wanting instead to go home and get started on the mountain of homework she had to do.     Amy shrank in her chair. "I'm not exactly hiding it," she said guiltily. Her mother looked her over and scanned the room. "Well whatever it is, I can't see it. What is it?"

To tell you the truth, I'd like one myself!" She gave Amy a kiss on the cheek. "Now finish your homework and enjoy those strawberries." She left Amy's room, closing the door behind her.

    "No way!" Lina said in disbelief. "Your mother...liked it?!"

    Amy had dialed Lina up on the phone just after finishing her homework. "She said a lot of people would accept it. That magic circle must have done more than change me, it's making people think it's okay!"

    "But then why don't I think that?"

    "Well," Amy pondered. "You were there when my tail grew. Maybe the fact that you saw it cancels the spell. Like, it can't take your memory of it away."

    "I guess," Lina conceded. "But you're still taking a huge risk going to school with that thing."

    "I've gotta hand in my book report; it's already late," Amy countered. "Don't worry about me. But in case there really is a problem, you're gonna back me up, right?"

    "Of course," Lina answered. "I can't believe your mom liked it...and she wants her own? This is definitely magic all right."

Then she tried them on to make sure they worked. Threading her tail through the hole was difficult with its thick cuff, but when she finally did the rest slipped through easily. The pants fit just as well as ever, and her tail had no complaints. She looked herself over in the mirror. Something about this made her really giddy. She was now clearly unique, and she even had a unique pair of pants for her special new feature. Her tail swished anxiously behind her, making her even more excited. Tomorrow was going to be interesting.
