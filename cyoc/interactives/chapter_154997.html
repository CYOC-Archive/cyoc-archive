    Breaking News This Hour: Mermaids Sighted!

    It took some convincing, but you managed to get your parents to let you stay with Uncle Jack for another month, the first of the summer break between school years, under the pretense that you'd be working at his shop for a summer job. They still didn't know anything about Yasmin, or about Alex's unexpectedly rapid transition to a female-presenting identity. You figured that you'd have to tell them everything eventually, but for now it was easier to stave off the hard conversations and unbelievable stories for as long as possible.

    The path of least resistance.

    Alex . . . Alex was not settling back into normal human life nearly half as well as she thought she would. For one thing, the wood-nymph costume made her supernaturally attractive to just about everyone—regardless of gender, which suited Alex just fine—but while it could fuel all the pick-ups and hook-ups and one-night-stands that Alex could ever dream of, that was about the extent of her nymphly powers. She wasn't developing any new attachments with anyone, no prospects with the potential to become a long-term relationship. And from the way she would wistfully speak of your time on Mermaid Island and wax nostalgic about the mer-girls left behind there, you were starting to really suspect that she missed that place and its quirky inhabitants even more than you did.

    And Alex had only been there for the three months that you had, which was more than enough time for her to grow quite attached to some of the mermaids, Sally in particular. Yasmin had been among them for forty years—it was more than apparent that she missed them dearly. They were family.

    By the time another two weeks had passed, your resolve to eventually return to the island had only deepened, though you still hadn't quite worked up the courage to formally pop the question to Yaz. But then, what even was a wedding ring or a marriage license on Mermaid Island? Kind of meaningless, when you really thought about it.

    Still, the wait was interminable, knowing that Uncle Jack couldn't send you back for another—you were keeping count—eleven weeks, going on ten.

    So it was to your great surprise and your great hope one day when you turned on the TV and discovered that every single channel was reporting on the sudden flurry of credible mermaid sightings in the Caribbean and Middle Atlantic Ocean. There were phone-recorded videos going viral on the internet, coming from several different island locations or ships at sea.

    "Hey Yaz! Alex!" you shout, knowing that they're both hanging around the costume-shop somewhere, more likely the warehouse than the storefront. You bring them into the office and show them the breaking news. Mermaids are real: film at eleven.

    Alex snaps her fingers and cries, "Of course!" She grabs Yasmin and hugs her. "Don't you see? With the Barrier down, the Island's not in a pocket-dimension anymore! It's been back in the real world all this time!"

    "It has?" you say, stunned. "You mean, we could just . . . go there!?"

    "Of course!" squeals Yasmin, and pretty soon she and Alex are both jumping for joy. "With no Barrier, we can come and go as we please! We don't have to pick one world or the other—we live in both!"

    "But, how do we find the island?" you ask. "Think Uncle Jack can just zap us back there now?"

    "Maybe," says Alex. "If it exists *completely* in our dimension now, he shouldn't need a portal. On the other hand," she pauses and stares at a map of the ocean which has appeared on the TV screen, pinpointing all the locations of the sightings. "Look at that," she says. "The epicenter is a dead ringer for the Bermuda Triangle."

    You and Yasmin look at each other. Of fucking course it is.

    "That explains why the portal isn't always open," says Alex. "Wizard shit 101: pay attention to the ley-lines, and always follow the mana. The Bermuda Triangle is one of the top three nexuses on Earth, right up there with Stonehenge and those Nazca lines."

    "So . . . no portal," you mutter.

    "No, of course not," says Yasmin, "that's not how you get lost in the Bermuda Triangle. You need to take a ship, or an aeroplane."

    "I think we can manage that," you say. Get to Florida. Rent a boat. You and Alex are kids of small means, but what do you need savings for now anyway? You're going back.

    Alex and Yasmin echo your thoughts. "We're going back!" they cry, hugging each other again and laughing.

    You run back to your room and retrieve the merman tail costume. You don't put it on yet, it's not the right time, but you do have it rolled up and held under one arm when you get back down to where Yasmin and Alex are listening to a news reporter interviewing a very excited, very drunk cruise-boat captain.

    You take Yasmin aside and lead her out to the costume warehouse. The place where it all began.

    When you get on one knee and ask her to be your wife, she's elated. You don't have a ring; she doesn't seem to care. She just says, "Yes!" and kisses you deeply, wrapping the coils of her sea-serpent tail all around you as she does so.

    * * * * * * * * *

    Alex, meanwhile, has finally tracked down Uncle Jack, who was . . . elsewhere, doing something wizardly, he didn't care to elaborate on the details. But the point is, Alex was able to summon him back to the shop from the mysterious mage's elsewhere and ask for his help in arranging transportation.

    "To the Bermuda Triangle?" Uncle Jack says to his niece in astonishment. "You're right, a ship *would* be much faster than waiting on the portal to align. All right, I'll see what I can do. I think I can have something ready by tomorrow."

    Alex kisses her wizard-uncle on the cheek and tells him, "Thanks for everything, Uncle Jack. We're not going away forever, but it may be a long time before we come back, so . . . we'll miss you."

    Uncle Jack smiles, only wanting his kids to be happy. But he promises to look in on them from time to time, and also to leave them a little better prepared this time around for an extended stay on a magical island.
