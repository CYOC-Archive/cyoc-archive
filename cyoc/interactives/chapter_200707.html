    Private Urmak was tired. She was not only pretty, but came from a branch of one of the city's ruling families, whereas Urmak came from a working-class family with a rather visible trace of orcish blood. Even though they were theoretically equals, he knew Sharona was destined for command, and he would be a groundpounder all his days. Urmak braced for the sudden rush of power to his muscles that accompanied the strength potions the commander bought from the gnome. Instead, he felt something entirely different.

    A rush of oneness with the nature that surrounded him. A feeling that he was not in the forest, but part of it, that his being extended into the grass and trees and animals. And a rush of unity with his fellow soldiers, even, and particularly Sharona. My sisters, he thought, even though over nine out of ten were male. Military hierarchy, social distance, none of that mattered as he felt love for all his comrades.
