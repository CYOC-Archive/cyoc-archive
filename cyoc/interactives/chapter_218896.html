    In Idaho, Mikey Bearstone looked forward to the end of the year for all the reasons that any other young kid would want. Summer would mean baseball and more free time for all sorts of things. Though some of the bigger things had more to do with his older brother and adoptive caretaker, Lenard. And a couple of the other boys noticed some of this excitement on Mikey's part as the school went through it's last days.

    "You'll be okay, Mikey?" a boy next to him as the bell in their elementary school rang to mark the end of the last school day of the year.

    "Yeah," Mikey answered, "It's been great and will be great. You'll come out to the cabin on some free days?"

    "When we don't have little league, yeah," the boy smiled, "your brother will be there?"

    "Most often," Mikey nodded with a smile, "that's who I go to next and then we'll go home."

    "And that'll be it?" the boy wondered and looked over one piece of paper, which had been part of the school's listed day for the last day of school. Which in their grades wasn't too far from essentially being a borderline party. The papers also included various sketches of tigers, including two tigers together.

    "Tuxing... sounds Chinese," the boy said slowly.

    "She is Chinese, but she's also a Tiger Animalian and among Animalia's Five Elements and won the Year of the Tiger competition last year," Mikey answered innocently, "and she's made Lenny happy. And in a way... she's a bit like mom."

But prior to meeting Tuxing, the two of them had largely been alone, and while Mikey had made friends, it was still something alone. Tuxing was someone who was putting the Bearstone family back together, and Mikey enjoyed seeing his big brother smile. And the coming summer was a big part of that.
