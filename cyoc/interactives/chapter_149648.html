Staring up at the darkened ceiling, thoughts about being drugged and poison ran through his mind as he struggled to stop the spinning feeling and force himself to stand. Wanting to tell her to let go, Erick listened helplessly as Angela squealed, "Kit. You know that tea time means you must sit up properly at the table," in a scolding tone.

Attempting to scream, "What the fuck did you do to me," but still unable to open his mouth, he stared up at the girl and listened as she said, "I think it is time you and Lilac got dressed for school," in a motherly tone and then reached down beside her. Picking up several things he couldn't quite make out at first, he wished he could groan as she began displaying different garments, all clearly for a girl Erick noted, and prattling about how pretty they were how Erick and Lilac would look in each one as she did.

And Lilac can wear the white skirt and purple shirt," Erick found it disturbing that all he could do was lie on the table as he was forced into what he was slowly coming to understand was a set of dolls clothes.

Setting down Lilac and Summer, she promptly held Erick up before the reflective device and proclaimed, "You look so pretty, Kit. And now you are ready to go to school. And I will be your teacher," and gazing at his reflection, Erick found that, if he'd been able to, he would be screaming.

Studying his reflection, thinking his legs and arms didn't look like front and back legs of an animal, but would appear normal on a human, except that they were covered in red and black fur, he took in how much he looked like a girl fox. Unfamiliar with the word anthropomorphic, horrified by what he was seeing and how the doll clothing he'd been forced into enhanced the appearance of him being a girl, he wanted to cry. Afterward, she lit another lamp, which he wasn't sure how she did as she did it without any fire, yet it was clear the wick had been lit, and stood before them and said, "Today, we are going to learn to count," in her best authoritative voice, which Erick thought it made her only sound more childish.

I don't want to be no damn stuffed animal, let alone a girl toy," as he tried to come up with a way to either wake up from a situation he desperately wanted to convince himself was simply a bad dream or find a way to make himself move so that he might be able to get away.
