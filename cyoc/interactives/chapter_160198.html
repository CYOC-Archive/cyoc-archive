    Kellie and Jack glared angrily at the eight tails as he now freed himself from the demonic hands Jack had used to restrain him. "Dont even think of moving," Jack growled.

    Fukushu smiled, wizards could be so inventive sometimes. Glancing at the pocket watch he now noticed the time. 30 seconds to go.

    "Well played," he grinned.

    Slowly the eight tail now stood dusting himself off as he carefully watched the two teens. "Illusion and necromancy, he stated. "I admit I did not expect that. I was certain you would charge through with reckless abandon."

    "Always trying to impress now where's our son and the others," Kellie asked.

    15 seconds to go Fukushu thought. "Your son is right there," he said, pointing towards a small swaddled infant. As Jack moved towards the infant, Fukushu conjured an iron chain, which now wrapped itself around the teens ankle.

    "Let him go," Kellie bellowed as she now prepared another spell.

    Fukushu gave the teen an impish grin as he looked at his watch. Time was up, he had accomplished his goal. It didnt matter if they rescued the child now. Releasing Jack from his chains he now watched as the teen raced towards the infant. "Jack how is he," Kellie asked.

    Jack slowly removed the child from his blanket, noticing the babies delicate features and wide innocent eyes. "Hes fine," Jack sighed. "I think....hes smiling?!"

    "Definitely gets that from you," Kellie grumbled, a nervous laugh now escaping his lips. "Okay so hes safe now where are the others?"

    "What others," Fukushu asked coyly.

    "Dont play dumb," Kellie groaned. "We know someone reached you. Aiyo's reaper released her soul because someone got here before us. Now where are they?"

    "Oh that's right," Fukushu said, feigning ignorance. "Your daughter and her friends. I sent them back to the hotel. I'm sure Akira can handle them. And before you ask they are unharmed."

    Kellie and Jack now angrily glared at the kitsune, something didnt seem right, this was far too easy. "What's your game," Jack asked.

    "You'll find out soon enough," Fukushu smiled. Before the teens could ask another question he could see the rest of Kellie and Jack's friends arrive. "Fewer than I expected," he stated. "Akiras attack must have rattled you. And I see my beloved parents have joined you."

    Fukushu said nothing as his mother continued to gaze at him, a loving look upon her face. "Your father and I know who you have pledged yourself too," she whispered. "You know what he is son. He and his followers will destroy this world."
