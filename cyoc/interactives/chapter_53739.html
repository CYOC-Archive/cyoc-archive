    "So where are to keep her?" Griselle asked nervously, "Mr. Running-Bear doesn't keep such things.

    "I don't know," Goldwyn sighed, "can't we repair the damage done?"

    "The damage to be undone will require more power," warned Princess from around Goldwyn's feet.

    "But she's a friend, she agreed to let us make her good," Goldwyn said, "and all we did is damage her."

    "I know," Princess purred into Goldwyn's leg, "but the rules of the magics you practice state you must gain more power before you can fix what happened... otherwise, you might lose her."

    "That's not fair," Gita spoke.

    "Yes," Princess nodded, "it isn't, but for now, the world isn't fair... but in time it and all the rules that are trying to oppress you will be gone and NOTHING will ever threaten you. And you'll do anything you wish... but that day is not today."

    Gita liked the idea of being able to anything. If it helped them cheat death, they might be able to bring their mother back, their grandmother back, their father and grandfather back. And make it so that none of them would ever die again. And if they had to wait a little bit to gain the power to do that, Gita would wait.

    Goldwyn nodded as Princess voiced her support. The rules seemed to hold everyone back, and they seemed to punish Mr. Running-Bear's people, and they all seemed to be a creation of adults. It would take time, but with Princess's help, Goldwyn felt confident that they would eventually become strong enough to fix all of the world's wrongs.

    "How can we become stronger, Princess?" Griselle asked the cat, "We've tried lots of stuff together. Nothing has worked."

    "You will need familiars," Princess explained, "animal guardians who will help you channel the Infinite Chaos... and who will love and stand by you always."

    "Where can we get them?" Gita asked.

    "Searching for potential familiars for you has been something I have been working on," Princess explained, "I personally wish to work with Goldwyn... but the choice is hers to make... and I have found some possible candidates for you and Griselle to choose, Miss Gita."

    "Thank you," Gita answered.

    "Where are we to put Joella though?" Goldwyn asked.

    They were presently in a quiet woodland outside the Praying Town where Joella's form had been hidden by bushes and by the witchlings draping tree branches over it. It, however, wasn't a good spot as human hunters occasionally came into the area hunting White Tails or Gold Deer. And if it was going to take time to gain the power needed to fix what went wrong earlier, Goldwyn didn't want to leave Joella in a spot where she could accidentally be shot.

    "The lupmen maintain a large hunting preserve around their estate," Princess spoke, "and they do not use guns, and would probably run most trespassers off, but they can not threaten you."

    "Is it remote?" Goldwyn questioned.

    "Parts of it are," Princess spoke.

    "Can you show us?" Goldwyn asked.

    "I can show you, Goldwyn," Princess promised, "and no one will notice us. Your sisters can guard Joella."

    Goldwyn nodded.
