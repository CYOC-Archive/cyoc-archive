That simple action was the most surreal feeling I've had in my life (at least for now) and I didn't know why. It was like I was on a slingshot, an elevator or a escalator. My head was spinning too, so when I talked to my uncle, I didn't realize I was seeing him at eye level.

" First of all, the watch in your hand (I hadn't noticed it was in my hands until he told me) it's not a regular pocket watch. It's a magic watch that I found almost a hundred years ago. And before you asked about it, yes I'm way more older than you thought. In fact, I'm not really your father's brother, but that's a story for later." I wasn't understanding any of what uncle Eric was saying, but I hadn't any other choice but kept listening. "As I was saying, that watch is magic and I found it by accident during a travel to South Asia."

    "Uncle Eric. I don't get it. Why are you telling me this? Why did this thing cause such a blast?" I was asking when I noticed something very odd. My voice... It didn't sound like my voice. I sounded like someone else, perhaps someone older, because my voice was definitely deeper. My uncle didn't reply to me and kept talking instead. "I don't know how it works, but I realized that watch can change time and space around it. At first, just like you, I thought I was getting crazy or so. But, with further experimentation, I finally understood how to take advantage of it's capabilities.

    "I used it to stop time and do all the things I can't do before because I hadn't time enough. When I ended up reading all the books I want and learning all the skills I always dreamed of. I started to get bored of it." My uncle had a brief melancholic look on his face before he kept talking. "I felt so lonely, that I quickly understood eternity wasn't as great as some people think. I had too much free time, so I decided to live my life to the limit."

    "That's why you're a stunt man?" I wondered and he simply nodded.

    "But most importantly, I decided to have a family." He smiled a little. "I met your grandmother during a roadtrip in Texas. I'll be honest, I'd had tons of women before, but none of them captivated my heart like she did." After hearing that I started to connect dots. "Are you saying that... you... in fact... you are..." He grinned when he noticed I was realizing it. "Yes, in fact, I'm your grandfather..."

    "But that's impossible! We were at your funeral!" I was agape while he replied. "It was an empty coffin. When your grandmother passed away, I was devastated. I offered her to use the watch to live longer but she refused it. She didn't want a long life but a plenty one." He had a sad smile on his face. "That's when I understood that messing with time wasn't something a simple human must be able to do. So, I decided to use it one last time to become younger again and have a regular life. Of course, I hid the watch in a place where I can keep an eye on it."

    "Well, it a little complicated..." He told me. "Your grandmother knew about it. I told her before I marry her... And when your father was born, we thought it could be fun if we become kids his age to play with him ocasionally." He smiled. "After a few times, your grandma got tired of it, but I kept doing it for several years... or I must say I'm still doing it. Just that now, I'm doing it permanently. Your granda helped me a lot to keep the story going on."

    "So, even after I was born, you were still playing that fake life?" I was shocked in a non positive way. My uncle wasn't really my uncle, and my grandfather wasn't really dead... I was so upset that I almost forgot I was watching him at eye level. "You've been lying to us all this time?!!! How can I believe in you after this?!!!"
