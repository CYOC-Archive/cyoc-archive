    Two loud screams broke the morning air, waking up Rose and Taylor who looked around confused. When they heard a sobbing noise from the bathroom down the hall, Rose instinctively jumped to her feet and runs to find the bathroom door locked. The rest of the house arrive with only Lisa and Monica unaccounted for, Rose assuming the two are showering together due to the water bill. “Taylor…go back to my room and get my medical bag.” Rose ordered her roommate to grab her customized first aid kit, readying herself for the worst. By knew, the sound of Lisa softly crying is heard.

    “Lisa…Monica...it’s alright.” Rose reassured her while putting a put on gloves and a mask, though it might be pointless since they lived so close together for over two weeks that chances are she and the other girls were already infected. “Can you open the door?” The crying stopped for a moment as the door unlocked, Rose closing the door behind her. The shower was still running as an astonished Rose sees Lisa and Monica with a towel wrapped around their fused bodies, the two girls having merged into a two-headed woman. With the girls' permission after talking them to disrobe for a moment, Rose gives them a look over as they bodies somehow merged from the necks down with their blended skin tones making them resemble more like sisters in appearance.

    “Alright, I am going to take some photos for the doctors and then we will get you dressed.” Rose explains as she pulled out her phone to take pics of them. “Where will we go without infecting everyone?” Lisa inquires. “Lisa…," Monica started. "We all share the same kitchen and two bathrooms. We're all likely infected." "I know a professor of mine who may help…,” Rose says as she finishes taking the pictures. “Alright, let’s get you dressed.” Rose helped Lisa and Monica get dressed before opening the door to see that the rest of their friends stood in the hallway waiting for them. As everyone goes to their rooms to check for any signs of the virus, Rose stood in the hallway and began to dial the number to a local transformation center.
