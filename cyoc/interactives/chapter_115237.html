    James hadn't really had much of a plan when he ran away from Kayla beyond escaping the crazy witch woman and her misfiring magic. Before long he realised he may have been a little hasty in his actions. He had no idea what Kayla's father looked like or where he was, or whether he would be prepared to help him. Returning to human form would also be a problem right now. Quite apart from his continuing frustration at recent events, his pants and shirt had fallen away while he was scurrying from the tent. He figured he would have few problems staying in canine form as long as he needed to - he had to concentrate pretty hard to get back before - but being appearing naked at a crowded fair or in front of the wizard father of some crazy girl he had just met might not be the smartest idea.

    Searching for inspiration, James heard a voice he thought he recognised. Looking up confirmed it - it was Becky, someone he was on familiar terms with in his human existence. Though not a friend, Becky was friendly, a warm, caring person and an animal lover. Maybe she could help him somehow? He wasn't sure, but he was willing to try anything at this stage.

    James trotted over to Becky, barked and wagged his tail furiously. Sure enough, Becky followed him as he ran into a deserted tent nearby. Once she caught up with James, Becky smiled, knelt down and began stroking him.

    Becky was fairly attractive, in a dorky sort of way. She was around 25 years of age, James guessed, tall and slim with long brown hair, glasses and a cute, slightly nervous smile.

    "Oh, hello! Aren't you adorable? Were you trying to get my attention? Are you lost?" James tried not to react to the way this girl was fondling him right now. He certainly couldn't turn back in front of her. Perhaps this wasn't a good idea.

    Moments later, Kayla appeared as if from nowhere.

    "Excuse me lady!" she snapped at Becky with clear irritation "Can you hand me back my dog!"

    "Well there's no need for that attitude!" Becky huffed back "And how do I know he is your dog anyway? He doesn't seem very happy to see you"

    "Look I'd suggest you walk away now" Kayla warned.

    "Oh yes, or what?" Becky folded her arms and raised her eyebrows confrontationally.
