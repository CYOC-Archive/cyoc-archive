    Suddenly everything went black. When Eric came to, he was passed out on his couch. As he slowly sat up, there was a weight on his chest that certainly wasn't unfamiliar by this point, but that he knew shouldn't be there. Almost too afraid to find out, he looked down at his chest.

    Yep. Breasts.

    If there was one small mercy in the whole situation, it was that none of the other changes had stuck. Eric might be a girl, but she was at least a normal human girl. But...she'd still been robbed of her masculinity, apparently permanently. She screamed in rage and sorrow.

    That woke the others up. They were all sprawled out over the living room. One by one, they sat up, only to discover that...they hadn't been changed back at all. Okay, that wasn't strictly true: Luke and her "daughters" had hands on their wings now, positioned like a bat's claws and missing the pinkie finger (because, biologically speaking, the wingtip was the pinkie finger,) but otherwise fully functional. All of them were in regular clothes, too, except the harpies, who kept the tunics they had had on (and, needless to say, Luke kept her ancient Greek brassiere.)

    "God damn it!" Mitch shouted. "I'm still a sprite! What the hell did the damn thing think we meant by 'back to normal!?'"

    Luke didn't know why, but the language bothered her. "Mitch," she said,"could you please not swear? My daughters are present after al-wait, my daughters!?"

    "Sorry," Mitch said. "Um, wait, do you really think they're your kids?"

    "I don't know," Luke replied. "I...I don't see how a seventeen-year-old...girl could have two eight-year-old children, but I can't shake the feeling that they're mine."

    "Pregnancy's been known to happen as early as five years of age," Eve offered, dusting herself off as she stood up. "Let's just hope you didn't wind up with some twisted history of child abuse."

    Luke blanched. "Oh...oh god, I hope not. I...would you watch the kids for me? I gotta go." She sprang out the door.

    "Hey, Eve," Sam said, "you're doing something more than awaiting orders now."

    Eve brightened. "Hey, you're right. Um, given that, could you call me Evan again, at least until we find out otherwise?"

    Sam smiled. "Sure. As long as you call me Sam and not Zelda."

    Evan laughed. "Hey, you're free, too! Hey, Matt, how are you?"

    "Still an Irish-looking midget," Matt replied. "But at least I'm my own proper age, i.e. beardless, and wearing normal clothes. I'm wondering, though, exactly how this constitutes 'back to normal.'"

    Eric shrugged. "I don't know. I think the machine was just designed to twist wishes. Maybe I got mostly changed back since I'm the one who made the wish. I wonder to what extent the rest of you got changed back. Mitch, I'm really afraid to ask this, but can you still do any magic?"

    Mitch concentrated. "Is your hairdo changing?" she asked. "That's the only thing I know I could do that I think you wouldn't mind."

    Eric sighed. "Nope," she said. "No changes. Crap, I guess we're going to have to find another way to change back."

    Alex and Mark stood up. "Where's Mom?" Alex asked. "Wait, 'Mom?' I mean Luke...where's she?"

    "She left to try and find out how you two could be her kids when she's only seventeen," Mitch said. "She'll be back before too long, I think."

    As she said this, Luke returned. "Well," she said, "I've got some good news and some bad news in regards to our predicament."

    "Good news first," Eric sighed.

    "The good news," Luke said, "is that I wasn't abused as a child, and that we're not going to get hauled off to a research facility somewhere. The bad news is that Alex and Mark really are my daughters, and as far as the rest of the world is concerned nothing changed between last night and this morning."

    Evan groaned. "You're kidding."

    Luke shook her head and sighed. "Nope. I found a diary belonging to the new me. As far as everyone else knows, all of us except Matt have always been girls. I'm Lucy, Evan's Eve, Sam's Samantha but goes by Sam, Mitch is Michelle, Eric is Erica, Alex is Alexis but goes by Alex, and Mark is Marcia. Michelle and I are the way we are because my dad, bioengineering geek that he is, was mixing up some sort of bizarre concoctions when we were nine, and we got into them. They changed us into what we are, and got me self-pregnant with Alex and Marcia. Apparently my parents offered to take care of them them for me, but I opted to raise them as my own daughters. Eve might not be stuck in servant mode, but she actually is working as a maid. Sam's resemblance to Zelda is purely coincidental; the pointy ears are just a minor birth deformity. Matt's always been a midget."

    Eve sighed. "So not only are our lives completely different, nobody's going to believe us when we say so."

    Lucy nodded. "I'm afraid so. I guess the only thing we can do is either try the game again or go back to the shop Eve got it from and see if they can change us back."

    Erica shook her head in disgust. "We're not doing the game again. If it twisted my wish this much, God only knows what it'll do the second time around."

    "The shop it is, then," Michelle said. "Lead the way, Eve."
