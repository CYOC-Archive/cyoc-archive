It made for a rather pleasant walk and since there was no "time limit" when it came to walking home, the pace could be rather relaxed rather than the more hectic pace some had when it came to getting too school. Thus why the morning buses often had more people on them than the afternoon buses. Of course, Conner and Ryan following along after school came off as a bit odd, but that had more to do with Moon Lake's geography than anything else.

    In most other cities, the town was made out in grids, which then set up blocks that houses and residences could be packed onto. And only space or the terrain would limit that, and this meant that unless someone lived on the opposite side of town from someone else, it was fairly easy to take alternative routes between certain points and not have to double back. Thus one could always be going in the general direction of their end destination. Moon Lake, with its wagon wheel layout, didn't really have that. There were a couple of "blocks" in the center most point of town, and much of this seemed to include the oldest parts of Moon Lake when it came to what was built in the town. But the further one got from the center of town on the straight streets that made up the "spokes" of the wagon wheel, it would mean that there really weren't the streets available to then turn from Tad's home and then move directly toward where Ryan and Conner lived, on a different "spoke."

    This was especially true when the Decker house was about halfway toward the outer edge of town and the Howlers were a couple houses down on the opposite side of the street. While Ryan and Conner lived on the same street, it was a different street than Tad and Amy's homes, and the Ross family had bought a home that was closer to the edge of town than either Tad or Amy on that street. Now this wasn't necessarily a problem, as from what Amy had told him, Moon Lake was generally pretty tolerant of people cutting across lawns to go through the woods that were in the areas between "spokes" that made up Moon Lake's straight roads... but if one were to stay on the sidewalk, they would have to double back toward the center of town before moving to the appropriate "spoke" when it came to moving from Tad's home to the Ross's.

    Cutting through the woods wasn't a "bad" option, normally, but Tad did worry a bit for Conner. Some of it was the reveal which had so many people seemingly on edge. Things seemed to have quieted down a bit at school, but Tad still had the impression that that had more to do with the fact that the school had come down firmly in response to the fist-fight that had broken out at the start of the school week that just ended. At the same time, there was also the potential for wildlife to come into the forested areas that "grew wild" around Moon Lake and leading to some potential conflict. And from what Amy had told him, normal animals did not respond well to weres... at least not wild animals. Pets and livestock might do well around weres, but that was more due to their domestication, wild animals seemed to either be more willing to fight if attacked or would openly confront what they thought was a were. Tad knew he could be strong enough and fast enough to deal with most animals that were in Washington State, for he was a werewolf. Ryan could probably handle one or two mundane wolves if there was an encounter and could scale a tree to escape from larger predators.

    But Conner was still a normal human. And as a normal human, he would carry all of humanity's weaknesses when it came to dealing large predators, like Mountain Lions or Black Bears. Conner couldn't outrun them and couldn't fight them and win. That was something that Tad remembered a couple of the rumors that went around in the week before the reveal had happened. It wasn't super big at the time and didn't relate to him, so he put it out of his mind, but according to the rumor, a different family had just moved to Moon Lake shortly after Halloween. According to what Tad had heard, the mother and father had wandered out into the woods, which lead to the father becoming a werelion and the mother getting attacked by a Mountain Lion... but Tad didn't have enough information on that to know for sure and decided to not get involved. But if it was true, there was the fact that Conner could be in danger if he and Ryan ran into anything on the way.
