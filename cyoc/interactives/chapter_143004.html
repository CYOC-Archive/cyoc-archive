    There was many things coming to and from the PR-MD offices, while those that came INTO were filed into either "Allow" or "Reject" files, while those that came FROM were often news reports of previous projects.

    ---

    The biggest report from the PR-MD office was that that the Titanic 2.X was nearing completion. The ship, which would have a moored tour before it's maiden voyage back to Europe, which was scheduled for June. While it was designed for a luxurious "recharging" cruise ship for Animalians, it was also Exhibit A of the old saying "Those who don't know history are doomed to repeat it.". Not only was the hull made of a single piece of "space-age" (as it's sometimes referred to) alloy designed to buckle, but not break, on impact, and be easily repaired when in dry dock. In the rare case that the hull was breached, the inside of the hull was portioned out into multiple, fully sealed, compartments designed to be easily emptied and repaired, again while in dry dock.

    There was also an interview by Travel Channel with one of the well known "dark side" Animalians, Lucy Greenwall of Wiccan's Woods. While most of it was actually quite common for business interviews, there was one question that seemed to strengthen the beliefs of many people who did believe in witchcraft.

    "So your old owner left her grimoires to you?" asked the interviewer.

    "Before she died, she taught me how to read the Theban Alphabet, which is often believed to only be understood by true practitioners of the Craft." Confirmed Lucy, who often went by Miss Greenwall while working, "This is the only thing that is written in English in my old owner's grimoires." When she opened the book to the front page, there was an ominous warning.

    'To those NOT of this Craft - the reading of this book is FORBIDDEN! Proceed no further or justice will exact a swift and terrible retribution - and you will surely suffer at the hands of this craft.'

    After the episode featuring the interview appeared on iTunes, there was one comment that was considered the most mysterious.

    "I believe that witchcraft is real, but there is a fine line between knowing how to do witchcraft, and knowing how to do it right. If a witchcraft spell fails, it probably wasn't done right."
