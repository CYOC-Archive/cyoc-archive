    "No, no, no!" Zeus said quickly. "No need to do that! We can go back to the current day."

    "Lovely," said the youngest Fate, twirling her drop spindle. The light outside the windows flashed day-to-night and back multiple time, and then Ty suddenly remembered everything that had happened, up to an including his death at his mother Circe's hands!

    "It's just..." Zeus sighed. "All my sons are heroes! They didn't do anything bad."

    The old crone Atropos raised her eyebrows. "Oh? I distinctly remembered something about Hercules killing his entire family in a fit of pique. And Dionysus rather regularly goes on a bender. It's sort of what he does."

    "But..." Zeus said.

    "If it's any consolation," said the youngest Fate, "this boy is not actually your son."

    Zeus paused. "What? Circe told me that she had slept with no one else, mortal or beast, for quite a while." His face grew red. "Circe!"

    In a swirl of emerald enchantment, she appeared. "Oh what is it, Lord Zeus?"

    "You broke your word! To me! King of the Gods!"

    "Actually she didn't," the youngest Fate said.

    "Clotho knows the beginning of all stories," Lachesis reminded Zeus.

    Everyone turned to Clotho, who related a simple fact. "Neither of you have ever actually had sex with each other. You both had sex with Proteus, god of shapeshifting, who amused himself by impersonating each of you to the other."

    "Rather badly, I might add," Atropos added tartly, glaring at Zeus. "Do you honestly believe that the enchantress Circe, who has a reputation for nasty revenge, would ever become a 'good girl'?"

    Clotho looked at Circe. "You can be forgiven for thinking that Zeus wanted to have sex with you. You are one of the few goddesses he hasn't done yet."

    "I thought I did a rather good job of it," said the dresser, which then formed into an anthropomorized dresser, like one of the furniture people from Beauty and the Beast.

    Ty was freaking out. "My parents are an enchantress and a dresser?"

    Circe looked slightly embarrassed, then said to the dresser. "He certainly doesn't get his intelligence from me."

    Zeus stood there, sort of shuffling his godly feet until Atropos said. "Yes, you are excused. But no more mucking around with our tapestry, or this time we'll unravel everything and let your dad eat you instead!"

    Zeus nodded, then tried to restore some godly decorum, telling Ty, "I may not be your father, but that will make it all the easier to fry your ass with a lightning bolt if you try any really nasty shit. Understand, boy?"

    "Come along, ladies," Atropos said. "I think we've said our piece." She hobbled into the closet with Lachesis and Clotho following her.

    This left Ty alone with his parents, Circe and a dresser.

    "Hi son," said his father. "I'm Proteus. Want me to fix you up with a sexy loveseat?"

    Circe rolled her eyes. "I'm sorry, I'm going to go deal with Landon. We have seven new children, and there's a slight chance that ONE of them might have an ounce of intelligence." She disappeared in a flash of emerald light.
