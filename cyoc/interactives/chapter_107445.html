    “While it may come no surprise to most of you know not what this means,” Tachibana standing next to Lance; “but a bi-polar were is extremely dangerous when not properly medicated.     “Care to explain then,” Leon Talbot raising his paw. “why should we be so concerned that Benton is bi-polar?”
    “A were that is bi-polar can go feral if not treated,” Andrew Curry shouts not even bothering to raise his hand.

Despite our longevity and our ability to shrug off most injuries and diseases we are still just a frail and vulnerable as regular human beings when it comes to old age and mental issues. Along with bi-polar we are also susceptible to Alzheimer’s and dementia as well as mad cow disease. These illness can also make a were feral. In the event of Mad Cow, there is no hope for a cure or treatment as the prions eat away your brain.”

    “As for why, these ferals are locked away;” Tachibana sighing; “it was discussed decades ago that they would be kept isolated from each other and the population as a whole, in an attempt to one day cure their condition. It saddens me that your parents who once sat on this council never once informed you of this prior to your induction.”
    “Did Benton know of this ward,” the were-rat council member shouting.
