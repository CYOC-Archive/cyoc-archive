    "Okay, bro, I need my rock back and you're going to be late for your
    date," John said entering the bathroom.

    He averted his eyes in case his bro was jacking off again. He'd caught
    his 20 year old brother more than once "priming" himself before a
    date. The bathroom was almost totally silent. He heard a faint
    splashing coming from the tub. He looked down into the tub, and saw
    his transformation stone glowing fluorescent shades of green, yellow,
    pink and orange. The only thing moving in the tub was a very large
    tadpole with hind legs swimming around.

    John ran to his brother's door to the bathroom, and it was locked.
    That was signature Ocean, he'd lock the door he came through from his
    room for privacy, and forget to lock the door to John's bedroom. Still
    if it was locked on the inside? John felt his brother's hanging
    towel, it was bone dry. He looked down into the tub and asked, "Ocean?
    "

    The almost developed young frog stopped swimming. It turned and forced
    its head out of the water. It looked up at John pleadingly. They
    stared at each other for more than a minute, but Ocean was still
    breathing water, and ducked his tadpole head back into the bath. John
    couldn't help but laugh.

    "Well, Ocean, I'd have to say you're going to be late for your date.
    I'll go get the instructions, and see if there's anything about
    brother's turning into frogs. Worst case scenario, I can take you on
    your date, and we'll see if her kiss can change you back. Or maybe it
    has to be a princess or someone of royal lineage that kisses you?"

    John ducked out of the bathroom. He came back laughing harder, wearing
    rubber gloves and carrying the terrarium.

    "Dude, I just remembered you were trying to come up with a costume for
    your party. I think this qualifies as a prize winner, if anyone
    believes it's you," John said reaching his gloved hand into the water
    to collect the stone.

    He carefully put the glowing stone into the terrarium. He had already
    made a few modifications to the contents of the plastic box. He had
    dug the "pond" larger and built up a gravelly hill. He now set the
    terrarium on the tub edge and used his gloved hands like a bowl to
    scoop up water. Water leaking from his hands dripped down like rain
    into the terrarium as he moved his hands over the new "Big Pond" and
    let the water drop half filling it.

    Then he knelt with his hands bowled beneath the water, and called out,
    "Okay, Ocean, time to get going. Swim into my hands, and I'll put you
    in my terrarium until I can figure out what to do with you."

    The tadpole swam over to John's hands and treaded water just outside
    of the bowl they made. He forced his head out of the water and croaked
    faintly, "John..." he ducked back in the water and croaked again,
    "help...me." Then he took a small leap over John's fingers and into
    the bowl of water made by his hands. John felt a swelling inside his
    jeans as the tiny creature's body moved around in his giant cupped
    hands. He felt very powerful as he lifted the tadpole and handful of
    water out of the tub and added it to his terrarium.

    Ocean blinked as he swam around the plastic and gravel pond. He could
    feel the power of the transformation stone at work around and on him.
    He could feel the strange warmth permeating his new plastic home.
    Little plant shoots were visible sprouting everywhere. It would soon
    be a lush jungle. Duckweed was already starting to float on the
    surface of his pond. It reminded Ocean of that scene in the Star Trek
    movie of the Genesis planet coming to life. Of course, this was
    impossible, he told himself. He must've slipped in the tub, bumped
    his head and be hallucinating. His mind couldn't deny though that he
    could feel the water and he was in fact swimming.

    John added more water. Drained the tub, and put the lid back on his
    terrarium.

    He talked thoughtfully half-to himself and half to Ocean, as he
    carried the Terrarium back into his room.

    "Your arms - or are they forelegs - are visible just beneath your
    skin, bro, you'll soon be breathing air, hopping around and eating
    flies," John chuckled. He really wanted to see Ocean eat a fly. He
    added, "I bet you're pretty hungry too. I'll see if I can catch you
    some flies for dinner. It'll make a great youtube video. Wish I'd
    captured your transformation in the tub. I bet that was cool. Oh, man,
    I wish I hadn't already done my Senior Science Project, I could use
    you, and do it on the development of a tadpole into a frog. I still
    could. The project's not due until Friday. Oh, wait, Mr. Waters would
    probably want me to dissect the frog at the end of the project. And
    as much as you get on my nerves sometimes bro, I wouldn't do that to
    you."

    He set the terrarium on his desk and marveled at how much life had
    appeared since he added the transformation stone. Tiny colorful flower
    buds were visible among the now colored gravel. There were tiny
    shoots growing and twisting up the plastic plants. John set up his web
    cam to capture all the changes, and then went to carefully re-read the
    box and instructions that came with the terrarium.

    Ocean could hear the dripping sound of condensed water falling from
    above. The stone seemed to be heating the terrarium up to jungle like
    temperatures. Ocean let his back emerge from the warm water to enjoy
    the moist hot air. It reminded him of a cross between a steam room
    and sauna. He was sure he could hear the life growing around him. He
    felt safe and secure and filled with wonder. For the moment he forgot
    he had ever been human, and was definitely going to be late for his
    date.
