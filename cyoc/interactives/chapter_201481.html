    She fiddled with a switch or two, and then decided it was best to leave it alone.

    "Paul baby, where did you get this camera?" She asked in a stern tone.

    "Uhhh." Paul mumbled, not being able to recall where he had picked it up in the first place.

    "No baby, remember, it's wrong to steal." She said, placing the camera down on the nearest bench. "We'll leave it here so the owner can find it." She explained.

    "Okay Mommy." Paul grumbled, wanting to play with it more.

    "Now, who wants ice cream?" She cheerily asked as Paul gleefully yelled 'me' at the top of his lungs.

    Paul and his new mother walked off without a care in the world, unaware as to just how different their lives had been mere moments ago. There the camera sat still on the bench. The day was sunny with a slight breeze, and the sound of people echoed all around. Of course, the camera's alluring presence would not go unnoticed forever.

    Eventually, someone sat down on the bench, and took notice of this strange, out of place device. But, who was it?
