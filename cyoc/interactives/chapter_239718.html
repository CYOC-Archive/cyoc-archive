    Rob had just removed the saddle and harness from Chad after a couple hours of training with the jockey Julio, when he heard a voice behind him.

    "Hey, Rob, saddle that horse back up, I wanna take him for a ride," said Nick the boss's son.

    Rob cringed, and continued removing the bridle. He smiled and said, "Hey, Nicky, this is your father's new race horse. Nobody rides him but jockeys. Besides he was training all morning, and needs to rest now. I can saddle up Mr. Ed, or Stormy for you."

    "Jeri told me that my old man was getting me a new polo pony. That isn't it?" he asked pointing at Chad.

    "The boss tol' me he is planning to get you a polo pony. He thought he had one lined up, but that deal fell through. Not sure why, thought maybe the horse went lame. He picked up this race horse a few days ago. He's still working on a polo pony for you. He wanted to surprize you, will probably be mad at Jeri."

    "But Jeri said-"

    "Guess Jeri got it wrong," shrugged Rob. "Boss, will tell you himself when he gets a good healthy trained polo pony."

    "But Jeri was so sure that he got-"

    "Mr. Nick, the boss was getting you one as a surprise. If you know, he might decide to change the surprise? You know your old man."

    "Yeah. Okay, I'll take out Stormy today. Chad's kind of an odd name for a race horse, don't you think."

    Of course, the name was on the stall door. Rob shrugged again. "I think the full name is Hanging Chad or something like that. We jes' call him Chad while training."

    Nick sighed, "He does look like good horse flesh. If Chad doesn't make the cut as a racer, I wouldn't mind having him."

    Rob had hurried over to Stormy's stall to get him saddled up. Nick hopped the railing, and dropped into Chad's stall. Chad snorted.

    "Hm. Easy, Chad. You do look like a fine piece of horse flesh. Bet you could make a good race horse. You know that stuck up quarterback that beat my team in the championships went missing last week, and- fuck, there's no way 175 lb man can become a thousand pound horse in a couple days. Never believe rumors," Nick muttered to himself. Chad just munched on his oats. Nick ran his glove clad hands over Chad's naked equine body. Nick shook his head, as he lifted Chad's head and looked in his mouth. He saw the tattoo, Rudy had done a good job aging it so it looked at least a year old. Nick frowned. Then petting Chad affectionately, he added, "just as well for you stud, my school requires that all the polo ponies are geldings."

    Chad didn't react.

    Rob announced that Stormy was ready, and Nick rode off.
