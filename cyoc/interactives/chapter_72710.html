    As Chris sat there, looking through the options, he heard his dad come out of the shower.

    "Hi, Chris, who was it at the door."

    "Just a delivery guy, dad. He brought this weird remote control here," Chris showed it to his father. "It's a Chronivac, which apparently can change reality. Wanna try it?"

    "That sounds improbable. Well, what can it do?"

    "Pretty much anything. According to the options, I could change your age, I could get you into shape, I could even change your sex or occupation."

    "Heh. I wouldn't mind being a few years younger, in better shape and having a better job."

    Chris twiddled with the machine. "There, dad. You should soon be back to 28, and I've upped your job to high level management. I've also set you up so that you'll get the knowledge you'll need for your new life. Now on to me."

    "So, what do you want to change, then?"

    "Er..." Chris reddened. "This might sound a bit odd, but I was thinking of trying out being a girl for a while. I'm just curious, you know."

    "Okay. Well, if that is what you want, son, er, daughter, it's okay with me."

    Chris was relieved that he had such a liberal and nice father.

    As he clicked to change his sex, an info pop-up showed up on the Chronivac screen. The info window told him of ways to do quickly flesh out a gender change. One option popped out at him: "match to other person." This option allowed him to make the changes so as to fit in well with someone else, and he figured that using his dad would be a good idea. He wanted to keep his good relationship with his dad.

    He closed down the pop-up, and saw that all the options on his screen had changed to "match to Mike Sanderson". He decided to make sure he didn't end up his fathers young daughter, so he manually sat his age, putting it to 22. As he had done with his father, he sat his mental change high so as to have the skills and everything that he would need. As he clicked the activate button, another pop-up came up. "Error: Current relationship type (parent-child) does not work for set parameters. Changing relationship type." "Whew," he thought. "Dodged that bullet. I'd be changing into an little girl now, if I hadn't thought of that."

But it wasn't only their bodies that were changing. The room around Chris grew as the furniture shifted and warped from old and worn to new and expensive. This rapid warping of space made him momentarily disoriented, and he staggered to his knees.

    "Thanks, daddy," she said as she got back up to her feet.

    "Daddy? No. I'm certainly not your father anymore." Mike looked around for a second, confused. "Uh, Chris. I think you're my..."
