    #Dvreson, Voyager
    Hello? Is anyone out there? If anybody is getting this then I am currently somewhere in space. It seems. I think I'm safe in whatever room I'm in right now, even if its a bit disorienting with this clunky armor. I was on Room 45 of Floor 6 with this weird shooter game and it just exploded? I don't know what's gong on, please help its like a warzone I came out of. Or a horror movie...

    Despite some unsettling cries for help, screaming, beastial roaring and other horrible sounds surrounding his room; some more distorted than others; Adrien found his eyes drawn to the message that had suddenly appeared. It was daunting to him as he thought over how horribly things could go wrong on a potential venture to this random individual. What could he encounter on his way there? Would the elevators still work after all of the explosions and presumed damage, and glitching, to the building? What if he ends up falling through a pixelated hole in the ground or clipping into an object or...
    But then he turned to the smartdevice, his discolored eyes suddenly widening in realization. If this was sent through some kind of network, then the internet at least still functioned in a way... And as a Cybeast, Adrien could theoretically try to enter the 'net and maybe even come out of the other side, or at least assist this other player in combat. And if his guess was right earlier, then this player was still stuck on the first mission or the tutorial of the game, so all he would need to do is get them through the level and guide them to the exit!

    "A person saved by my heroic knowledge of gaming!" Then he bit his lip afterwards, shaking his head as he stood up and looked around his home. "That's... That's not enough, obviously." He said to himself, looking around his oddly more vibrant living room as he tapped his foot. Soon taking a look outside of his window, only to be greeted by an empty void once again.
    He quickly pulled down his blinders and looked away from it.

    "What else can I lose?" He soon asked himself as he went towards the smartdevice where his recording room's door was, and picked it up in his claws. Focusing on it for a bit before having his sight overloaded with an expanded menu! With nodes for all kinds of different locations in his apartment that were linked to the webverse. From his TV to his oven and even the lights, it seemed like this entire place was connected... But even with access to this menu, he couldn't enter the webverse!? What was he doing wrong?
    He kept on holding the device for a bit more, before thinking it over a bit. What also happened in the inspiration, for the game to switch from reality to the virtual world?
    And with widening eyes, Adrien decided to try out this slightly cringe-inducing line.

    "Cybeast Adrien, j- link up!" With a gasp and sudden apprehension at finding himself somehow saying the wrong words with such unnatural enthusiasm, everything turned white.

    -

    He found himself falling onto a strange digital platform, unlike the sleek circles and lines of the tutorial or the box-art for Cybeast, this was corrupted. The varieties of programming languages making up this realm, biting into his modified flesh as he soon rose back up, groaning as his eyes adjusted to the maelstrom of information sent throughout this instance of the web. From processes that were no longer compatible with each other, to entirely new programs that almost meshed seamlessly into the preexisting network. It was overwhelming for Adrien as he shut his eyes and tried to continue forward, only occasionally opening them to make sure he wasn't going to fall. At the very least for this first introduction to the net though, the future Cybeast was able to soon arrive to his destination.
    With a deep breath as he cautiously looked to make sure that this really was the origin of the message, he went to focus on accessing this weakening signal, before unsurely saying, "Cybeast Adrien, link out."

    Where did he end up?
