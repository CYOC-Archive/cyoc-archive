However, she awoke curled up on the floor of Jenkins' office. From what she could see, she was covered in fur and the experience hadn't been a bad dream.

    This time it wasn't the bizarre sway Jenkins had over her that compelled Christina to the mirror. It was her own curiosity and the burning need to know what had been done to her. She stood up on all fours, and walked to the mirror, to see a chocolate lab staring back at her. The one odd thing of the lab was the bright blue eyes like she had back when she was human.

    Her owner walked up next to her and started to pet her head. "I remember my first competition. I was 12 and my father entered me as a malamute."

    "I'm sure you were a lovely malamute Mrs. Hollingsworth." Jenkins commented. "In fact, if you ever wanted to compete again, I could offer you the change at a discount."

    She smiled at him. "That is a tempting offer, but I'm afraid that Westmorphers is a young person's competition. I couldn't possibly compete in the agility portion now, and I refuse to stoop so low as to lower my age to give me an edge like Loretta." She frowned at the name. Then smiled at Christina. "How do you we feel showing miss high and mighty up today?"

    "That's the spirit Christina! Good girl." Mrs. Hollingsworth told her.

    Jenkins grimaced. "I must insist that you speak with a bit more respect. I would prefer we don't dehumanize the poor girl by talking down to her."

    "Come now Jenkins. Good girl isn't all that degrading. I was praised many times as such as a malamute by both my father and husband while competing. I usually praise my own son when he competes."

I'm afraid she thinks this is all some prank but she wants to see how far he's willing to go to pull it off. I swear, if she doesn't dump him over this I should ask her to be my cat for a day with how curious she gets."
