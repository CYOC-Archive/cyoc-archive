    Dan sped through the streets, racing towards the home of Subject One. Slightly addled by the alcohol, he nearly clipped several cars and raced through a red light just as it changed from amber.

    This last infraction caught the attention of EPD Sergeant John Thompson, a burly but jovial officer just a few months away from making Lieutenant. He flicked on the blue and red light bar, settling into position behind Dan’s BMW.

    Dan immediately saw that he’d been noticed and stomped on the accelerator, realizing that even if they didn’t connect him with his FBI car, he’d still probably be arrested for DWI.

    “Oh come on kid, you don’t wanna do this,” Thompson muttered to himself . He picked up the radio. “Hey Cindy, I’m west bound on Division Street pursuing a black or dark blue BMW M3, Virginia plates, K12 734FI. Anything on it?” He waited several moments. “Virginia K12 734FI registered to Dan Ramer, currently wanted on suspicion of homicide in the killing of two law enforcement officer. Subject is a federal employee; assume to be armed and dangerous. Exercise extreme caution.”

    “Roger, get me some backup here!”

    “Shit,” thought Dan. Well, first order of business was to get these city cops off his tail before they brought out their heavy equipment. He turned sharply right at the next intersection, tires squealing, the white squad car and black Mustang barely keeping his BMW in sight. He took the next right off Weinbach onto Franklin, and then left onto Kelsey. Thompson saw the black Mustang slide in front of him. “Dispatch, I have a black Mustang with FBI plates. Are they in on this?”

    “Roger, continue pursuit.”

    The BMW continued north on Kelsey, then turned right onto Virginia. Thompson barely saw the BMW turn left onto Alvord, pursued tightly by the Mustang. Dan pushed his car harder heading east onto Iowa, into Wesselman Park.

    Dan turned off the headlights as the car entered the park to evade his pursuers. Though the darkness provided some concealment, it also hid some highly immobile botanical features that stopped Dan’s car quite permanently as he missed a turn and ran into one at forty miles per hour.

    Stunned and thankful for airbags, Dan collected his belongings and ran off into the woods as the police and FBI drew closer.

    “Dianna Vixian, Federal Bureau of Investigation.”

    Vix nodded. “As long as your people understand that the United States has priority with this, we appreciate your help.”

    Eight in the morning. September 23, 2006

    Heather felt the warm rays of the sun on her face and heard the distinct crackling of bacon cooking. She sat up yawned and stretched, then reached over to shake Dita awake. The teenagers made their way downstairs to find Debra making bacon. Debra smiled as they entered the kitchen.

    “Morning ladies. Dita, your mom has had a rough night, but we got some good news from the hospital when we had the meeting; Wendy is alive!”

    Dita and Heather squealed joyfully, hugging each other and Debra. “How??” asked Dita, not really caring to hear the answer, just wanting affirmation that she hadn’t lost their new friend.

    “She was smart, Dita. She brought a bulletproof vest to the meeting. I guess the man that shot her used a high powered pistol, because even with the vest she was knocked unconscious. There was enough silver kicked up in the impact that her heart remained stopped, but they were able to resuscitate her.”

    Heather thought a moment. “Will there be any …permanent damage?” Debra answered, “If she was still a normal human being, she probably wouldn’t have survived at all. Her heart and lungs was badly bruised, and she’ll need some time to recover, but in a few days to a few weeks, she’ll be the same Wendy.”

    Dita asked, “and the deputy?” “No, honey, there was nothing they could have done for him. According to the news, he was married with a six year old son. And I’m afraid the killer is still out there, though he’s being sought by the FBI and local authorities.”

    Heather and Dita exchanged worried glances. “So what does that mean for us?” asked Heather. Debra smiled broadly.

    “As we get the tickets, and find out what we’re going to do with the other children of the pack, you two are going to Ireland for a couple of weeks. One or two other girls might join you, almost certainly Rachel since her grandparents are there, possibly Katie as well.”

    “IRELAND!?!” squeaked both girls happily. “We thought it would give you a chance to stay safe, since the person we’re looking for won’t have a chance to leave the country. We also figured you should have a traditional werewolf experience in Europe for you birthdays.”

    “Mom, I spoke with Mrs McDonald yesterday before gym, to get excused from gym for a few weeks. If Rachel is coming with us, isn’t she going to find out, since supposedly Dita and I will be out of commission for three days.”

    “Well you can still go on your date, just call him up and schedule it to end earlier. But you do need to be involved with this.”
