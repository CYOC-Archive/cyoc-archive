    Bob stood on the front sun porch of the Mackinaw Island cottage and reading the welcome letter from the government, he then gave it to Kevin. Both friends knew that day would come, each felt somewhat glad to see that someone took the liberty to allow them their preferred animal choice as being Equine. It bothered them greatly to read of what else it said about the loss of all they owned going to officials in the government. The service office of Farmland Security was above all other governmental agencies, even that of the court system. As the news reports stated openly, of those deemed risky or decided as of better use being in animal form, the wealth of their belongings ended up in the hands of those eager to get gain from others laboring bestially or in pain.

    Kevin quickly signed his name to the form and handed it back to his best friend, as Bob pocketed the letter, he undecided whether to sign or leave the country he loved, going to live where extradition was not a likely prospect.

    Consideration and caution was a dire thing to consider, as if Bob chose to run and hide, if caught, his then given form and placement would be by the hands of those who would profit most by his animalistic situation. He remembered and knew well of a friend that ran, hid and was found, ending up as a Welsh pony working in a sulfur mine and placed there to work, live, and be forever in the dark, dusty depths of the earth.
