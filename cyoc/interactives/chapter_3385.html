    Question #5- How big do you want your territory to be?

    After choosing Herding Dogs as your answer for the fourth question of the Canine Personality test, you go back to remembering the days of your childhood when your dog, Harry, was still around. And you realize that the choice you made just recently on the test was made because of him. He was your best buddy back then. Whenever your parents let you outside to play in the backyard, they always sent Harry out to keep a protective eye on you. And he always made sure that you didn't wander away from your home or get into trouble while playing. When you did so, he was there to herd you back. And whenever you had a bad dream and woke up from it upset, Harry was there to make you feel better. He was always there for you as you were growing up.

    As you daydream and remember all of the fun times you had with your dog, Harry, you remain blissfully unaware that you are still changing. And with the answering of the last test question, you take one step forward in your transformation. This time you no longer bear just a slight resemblance to a large, shaggy dog. This time you bear a really strong resemblance to a large, shaggy dog. If you had a mirror with you and were to look into it and not be afraid of what you see, you would have seen that you are starting to bear a remarkable resemblance to your former pet. Your head grows a bit larger and longer toward the back of your skull. And your ears continue to slide up the sides of your head. The hair on the top of your head has grown thicker and is starting to hang down over your face. The hair on your head is very distinguishable from the hairy pelt that is growing all over your body and on your face. Your forehead begins to slope sharply back over your eyes and lend some length to yout head. Your nose is now wet and black like your ragged lip-like flews. Whiskers now adorn themselves around your nose and mouth. Your teeth are beginning to realign themselves and reshape themselves for a very different diet from what you are used to. And your tongue is beginning to grow longer and flatter. The beard and mustache on your face has grown thicker and heavier. The beard especially as it begins to grow down the front of your neck, which has started to grow thicker and longer.
    You scratch your chest with your right hand unaware that your chest has begun to deepen, broaden itself at the shoulders and narrow itself down at your breastbone. Your hips and arms remain the same as does your hands. But just above your hips a bulge begins to form. Your legs also remain the same but inside your shoes and socks, your toes start to become paw-like. And the long bones of your feet begin to stretch themselves out of your shoes. As for your stocky, muscular body, the layer of body hair on your body has grown so thick now that it is beginning to resemble a fur coat.

    Unaware of what is happening to you, you unconsciously pull the chair you are sitting on closer to the desk and the computer with your legs and arms. As you do that, you accidentally leave behind your shoes on the ground behind you. But you don't notice that as you are now busy studying the next question for the Canine Personality test.

    Question #5- How big do you want your territory to be?

    (A) "A small apartment would be nice as long as I get dibs on the room with a view."

    (B) "A large dwelling would be even better. More room for me to stretch my legs."

    (C) "A large dwelling with a yard would be great. Plenty of room to romp around it, sticks to chew on and more places to bury things."

    The choices offered by this question make you think back again to your days with Harry. You make your choice and the woman's voice then praises you for making such a good decision. The voice asks you if this is the answer that you want to submit. You reach over to the mouse with hairy mitt and click on the yes button. The woman's voice praises you again and makes you feel real good inside. It then tells you that you picked...

    What do you do now?
