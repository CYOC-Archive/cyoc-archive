You're a very handsome weremer, Eric. You have every reason to be proud in your form... regardless of whether or not you're transformed or not."

They found Melissa Stovall working quietly in the kitchen with what smelled like pancakes, fruit, and sausage patties. It smelled good.

    "Well it's good to see that you're up," Melissa commented, "and I trust that nothing has changed since yesterday?"

    "Other than our love growing stronger?" Julie answered, "No... though we are looking forward to a happy future."

    "Junior and Melinda?" Melissa asked.

    Melissa managed to stifle a chuckle as both young weremers looked a bit surprise.

    "The intercom button must have stuck," Melissa said to them, "I'd just ask that you both WAIT to start your family until your older."

    "We've both seemed older in our dreams," Eric commented, "like we're in our twenties or thirties..."

    "Just remember that while your lifespan has extended by becoming a were, your aging is going to stay the same as a normal's until you turn either eighteen or twenty," Melissa spoke, "so that could mean that if Julie becomes pregnant now... in seven years you'd look like you're in your twenties with a seven year old son."

    "Oh... I have to," Melissa smiled and then then rubbed Julie's head, "I'm your mom. They'd take away my "mother's licence" if I didn't worry."

    "We'll be careful," Eric promised, "as... I don't think either of us are ready to be parents yet."

She could easily see that the two of them were destined for each other based on what Julie had told her about her relations with Eric and how the two acted together. Eric might still be adapting to how open many things in Moon Lake were with regard to sexuality and so forth. She mentally chuckled remembering once asking Martin if Moon Lake was founded by hippies before she found out about weres as she thought about many of the things that Eric would be adapting. In time, Eric would be like just about every other were in Moon Lake that had once been a normal from outside it. The main thing that she hoped for was that Eric and Julie remembered to take it easy and tried their best not to get too into the more "adult" aspects of life that married, and even a fair number of unmarried, weres engaged in. She knew she couldn't stop them, but she also knew they would have centuries for that. It wouldn't necessarily hurt either of them to wait or to at least limit the amount of love-making that the two of them engaged in.

    "That's good," Melissa commented, "pancakes are almost ready... there is some independent fruit slices along with some sausage patties."

    "Sounds good," Julie and Eric both said together.

    "So, what's the plan for today?" Melissa wondered as she moved to place servings on a set of plates sitting on the island in the Stovall's kitchen.

    "We figured we'd go for a nice walk and enjoy life," Eric commented, "enjoy being together... and let me get a look at Moon Lake now that I'm a were now myself."

    Julie squeezed his hand lovingly with a devoted smile, and was something that Melissa didn't miss. It reminded her of when she and Martin were dating. Eric was quite a handsome young man, even before he became a weremer. He wasn't as handsome as Martin was to her eyes, but to Melissa no one could be as handsome as her own husband, her own one true love. However, that didn't mean that Melissa couldn't be happy for Julie finding such a polite young man for her own one true love. There might be a lot that she personally worried over, but that didn't change that she was happy for Julie and Eric.

    "Well, I'm sure that'll be very nice for the both of you," Melissa spoke with a smile as Eric and Julie collected their plates and the three of them headed for the dining room to have brunch, where margarine and syrup were already waiting. As they arrived, Melissa then asked, "what will you two want to drink?"

    "Milk would be fine," Julie spoke. Eric nodded.

    The two of them sat down to brunch while Melissa went back to the kitchen to get two glasses of milk and to retrieve her coffee.
