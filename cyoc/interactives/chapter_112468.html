The battle for Sorcerer(ess) Supreme. The contest and its rules were simple: Bring life back to a dead world. A world that had been destroyed by evil magical forces billions of years ago. The gods who were overseeing the contest watched as dozens of would-be wielders of the ultimate forces of the universe tried their best to reignite the process of life. The two gods, a brother and sister, both had a lucky horse that they were betting on. The brother was betting on an Earth woman named Aziza al-Rashid, formerly a young man from one of the poorer regions of Saudi Arabia. She was a smart but reckless girl who possessed a practically intuitive understanding of how things worked. Magic was no exception, as Aziza had already mastered several difficult spells beyond her paygrade. The sister, Inanna, was rooting for Regina Chung, another Earth man turned woman, from America. She was a scientist, or at least she used to be, and up until a few days ago in Earth time, had been a high school teacher who had resigned in shame and fear once she saw the laws of physics crumble beneath her feet. But she was beginning to open her eyes to the bigger mysteries of the universe, becoming at once a better scientist, and someone who would soon do things that would place her own will above the laws of nature. The older and more methodical woman, Regina was incrementally discovering what happened to this place of death and pain.
