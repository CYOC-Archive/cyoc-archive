They're just humans born with a connection to the magic of the being the monsters call Mother Night. Some end up befriending the monsters or committing crimes in their own right, but most are perfectly decent human beings who just happen to be able to cast spells. Werefoxes don't think like humans, not even a little bit. They don't understand responsibility, they don't understand consequences, they don't understand stability, they don't understand compassion, and they don't even seem to fully understand love."

Besides, that potion only solves one problem. It doesn't make the drinker mind her manners in general. Even if Ruby Reynard isn't a philandering heartbreaker anymore, she's no doubt still a thoughtless prankster and serial shoplifter with itchy feet."

"Are you sure that your relationship with Ruby is secure?" the monster hunter asked. "Yeah, Ruby's eye won't go wandering anymore, but there's other ways she could betray you. For instance, werefoxes are cowards through and through. They may blindly leap into stupid stunts without considering the danger, but they WILL always run like spineless children once the danger presents itself. Do you honestly think Ruby will stick with you once she hears that I'm in Stokerville? No! She'll crap her pants and skip town with the rest of her worthless skulk!"

WOULD Ruby run when she heard there was a monster hunter in town? An overprotective monster hunter related to Renata, at that? What was more important to Ruby? Her love for Renata or her physical safety?
