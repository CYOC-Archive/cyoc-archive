    Well, Hannah thought, she was hoping that her fellow does didn’t throw out common sense today, and it seemed very much so that they hadn’t. But they did seem to think that Hannah herself had.

    Hannah didn’t believe Lateisha was right about the park staff was lying about there never being a rape in the park. It didn’t fit their motives with the revelations she learned from Juanita. Or did it? The whole point of the park, or at least a major park according to Juanita was to build up good public relations between the public and modified half-animal humans. Could they have been covering up such incidents so their park goers didn’t go telling the press that people who were half animals were acting like animals? Of course Hannah knew that the real reason was that they were awful people on the outside already, but the media and the short attention span public wouldn’t see it that way.

    Juanita didn’t seem like the kind of person who would do that, but Hannah had to actively remind herself she had only spoken to her for a few minutes, and also Juanita was not likely the singular driving force behind this park, and there were always people willing to let ‘ugly things’ be swept under the rug for their desires.

    Oy, two sentences from Lateisha and Hannah was already calling into question what little she knew about the park’s management. And seriously, it was incredible how little they did know, and how little everyone thought about the park’s management. If Monty was there, she knew he’d blurb ‘sub-sonic mind control messages’ and she knew she’d punch him in the mouth for ruining her train of thought on such an important matter. How important was it? Wasn’t she more concerned about facing life outside the park with a new body that society would need getting used to? Or was that the point?

    Or was she just getting too introspective at the wrong time right now? While she knew she could never have Sam, she honestly wanted to see how far he got in the competition. But still, if some or all of the bucks really were planning something dark. Would the staff get here in time? Would the staff do anything? That was absurd of course they would, wouldn’t they? It wasn’t like this park was cut off from the rest of the country as a sovereign nation or something. But she also realized she had no idea of the park’s legal status.

    She knew that Sam would look out for her if something happened, but would he be able to fend off however many tried something if they did try something?

    Well, after worrying about her ‘sisters’ losing their common sense, she wasn’t about to ditch hers. Hannah would have to admit that Lateisha did have more experience as a female than Hannah did, so it was in her own best interests to rely on her best judgment. And the all the bucks were distracted right now with their competition.

    One of the strangest sights of all though, was how the pecking order seemed to be changing as it went along, apparently whatever authority these boys or middle aged men held in the outside world, didn’t hold any real water here in this park. A buck who had been a figure of fear among his peers when they first came to the park, was now reduced to a subordinate after a former pledge he had apparently taken much delight in hazing when they were human had rendered him into submission.
    Why they’d have given free time in the park to a collection of frat boys Hannah wouldn’t know, except she had seen how diverse the people in the park were already.

    Curiosity said wait to see how Sam did, common sense said go with Lateisha. Hannah might not have been a cat, but she wasn’t about to find out if that applied to deer too!

    Slipping off the ‘empress’ throne’ as she had come to think of it, Hannah being as quiet as a doe can be slipped away from the clearing, the bucks who did notice her assuming she was off to relieve herself. Lateisha herself looked quite happy that Hannah hadn’t chosen to see how things turned out.
