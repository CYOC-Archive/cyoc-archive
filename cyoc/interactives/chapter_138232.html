    Kazumi had auditioned for the role of another Kaiju, but ended up getting the role of Gojira (or Godzilla as the Americans called it).

    It was actually the role she really wanted. The producers basically were looking for an actress with the right physique, which ended up being her. She was athletic and built well enough to handle the weight of the required suits, but she was also skinny enough for them to emphasize the bony and skeletal design of the Shin-Gojira suit she’d be wearing.

    Today was the day they’d film the pilot ... and she was in the changing room, clad in only her black, two-piece bathing suit, staring at the monster suit hanging on the wall in front of her.

    She was grateful that unlike most nano-suits, she didn't have to step into it nude. But it also wouldn't reveal too much. Just her general feminine shape for the sex-appeal, nothing too explicit. Which is what the producers were counting on to make this series a hit: classic Tokusatsu film-making with some added sex appeal.

    Kazumi was also glad that she would only have to be in the suit for about a month, as that was all the time they needed to film the first season. American actresses in these types of programs would usually spend most of the year in their suits.

    The effects people also warned her that her suit was a special prototype model. In order to successfully imitate Shin-Gojira, they also had to recreate his evolutions from one form to the other. So the suit would alter its design whenever they needed to do one of these scenes.

    They assured her that they had tested the suit with another woman, and it worked just fine. But that didn't mean Kazumi had no right to be nervous.

    The suit's starting form was that of the short, armless first form of Gojira, with a yellowish, tan colored skin, and massive gills on the side that would spray fake blood.

    With a deep breath, Kazumi stepped over to the suit and picked it up.
