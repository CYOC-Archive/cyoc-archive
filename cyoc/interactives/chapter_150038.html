Though going to a dance club wasn't really much better.

They had clubbing dresses which fit their older bodies and were particularly designed to show off their bigger curves and leave plenty of cleavage to be seen. Inside, the room was a little dark, but lit up with strobe lights. The music was loud, but not unbearably so. Lisa and Kumiko looked around the place with raised eyebrows. "Wow, things sure have changed since last time," said Lisa.

    "I suppose we should have expected this," said Kumiko. They could see plenty of anthro animals and demons mixed in among the people here. At least the animal people weren't naked, though most of them weren't wearing much at all. Clearly their different feet didn't get in the way of their ability to dance.

    Lisa and Kumiko made their way over to the bar. Unsurprisingly, the barkeep had also changed. It was a little hard to tell if his new winged, horned, reptilian appearance meant that he had become a demonic lizard or simply an anthro dragon. They hadn't seen many anthros based on magical creatures.

    "This place is more jumping than I was expected," said Lisa loudly once they got to the bar.

    "Yeah, but it makes it more fun," said Kumiko before looking at the various guys, "You know, as long as we're this old..."

    "Hey, none of that," said Lisa sharply, "We're already inside this club. I don't want to make things more complicated by bringing guys into this. Kellie would turn us into frogs if she found out we brought guys home."

    "We aren't in amphibian territory now?" asked Kumiko sarcastically.

    "Well, as long as we don't do anything real stupid," said Lisa before picking up the drinks menu, "Huh, bunch of new stuff on here."

    "What about not doing anything stupid?" asked Kumiko.

    "We'll only have one drink each," said Lisa, "It's not like Kellie hasn't had a few nips herself. Like when we came back from Circe's park the first time."

    "Or we could practice some of our magic here," said Kumiko.

    "Maybe," said Lisa, "Let's figure out what we want to do."
