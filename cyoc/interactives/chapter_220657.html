Her Mom was actually one of the few people who knew what Emily’s powers were. Long before she met her Dad, her Mom used to be a young man and she and her old friend owned an antique camera with the same set of powers before it transformed them both and disappeared forever.     Emily’s face lit up at her Mom’s words. “Thanks Mom! I won’t let you down!”

    “Just be careful when using your powers sweetie,” Rebecca warned her daughter. “I know the chaos they’re capable of causing. I was changed by it too, remember?”

    “Mom, I’ve told you a hundred times. I can change you back if you want. Really, I can.” Offered Emily.

    Rebecca came closer to her daughter. “I know you can sweetie and I love that you’re willing to do so. But I’m happy with who I am now. I’ve got a wonderful husband, a lovely baby boy, who just happens to be a girl this moment,” she chuckled. “And a beautiful daughter who makes me proud every day. And as long as I’m still with the love of my life, no matter who they are to me, I don’t want anything to change.” Rebecca embraced Emily in a hug and lovingly kissed her forehead.

    “I love you, Mom.” Emily cooed, tightly hugging her Mom. "I'll be careful, I promise."

    “Love you too, Rosebud.” Rebecca replied back, calling Emily the same pet name she used back when they were boyfriend and girlfriend long ago. Rebecca swore to herself to never tell Emily that the friend from her camera story was actually her. “Have a lovely day sweetie! My and Erin will be here all day if you need us.”

    Emily chuckled at her brother-turned-sister before giving her own wave back. “Bye you two!” Emily stepped out the door and exited her house, taking in the fresh air. She had big plans today.
