    Question 1: If you were a girl... would your handwriting be nice?

    "What kind of question is that?"

    Question 1: Your dream girl... would her handwriting be nice?

    Bobby blinked at the change in questioning, and assumed that he had just read the question wrong.

    He wrote down, "Yes, she would have nice handwriting."

    Bobby didn't mind pink himself. Thinking that the maxim that "pink is for girl" was arbitrary, he wrote down "Yes, but she would like all the colors, and her favorite ones would be different shades of green."

    Bobby loved curly hair on women, but not all the time. He wrote...

    "She would have extremely curly hair when the humidity level is high. Curly enough without it being an afro, but in less humid weather it would just be wavy."

    Bobby didn't notice as all the hairs on his head began to become more curly. It looked as if he had just gotten out of the shower.

    Question 4: What would be your dream girls favorite color?

    Bobby already answered this. Annoyed, he wrote down "Green, all shades."

    Question 7: Would your dream girl like makeup?

He's probably just excited and psyching himself out, Bobby rationalized. This test was getting a little weird, but it was indeed fun and he couldn't stop now.

    Question 9: Would your dream girl wear high heels?

    "Sometimes" was quickly jotted down. Bobby didn't care either way what she wore.

    "Yes, but no more so than pants, shorts, jeans, dresses or whatever. Skirts would be just one part of her wardrobe."

    Question 11: Would your dream girl wear jewelry?

    "No." Bobby didn't care for jewelry. He didn't even like carrying his iPhone, much less something superficial.

Neurons fired up in ways he couldn't even had pondered a moment earlier, and he suddenly became both very excited and very depressed. Depressed because he was so far above everyone else in intellect now, and happy because...because he was so far above everyone else now. If he had to be a girl temporarily, it was worth it just to feel the oneness with reality he now felt.

    "15 years old, 134lbs, and I would currently be 6' even, but I would grow to a maximum height of 6'3 when I turn 18."

    Question 21: If you were a girl... what type of shoes are you wearing?

    Bobby jotted down: "Just comfortable girl's sneakers. Pink."

    Question 22: If you were a girl... what other accessories would you be wearing?

    "Just my watch."

    Question 23: If you were a girl... how long would your hair be, and how would you wear it?

    Bobby liked long hair, but not so long that it got in the way of belt buckles and going to the bathroom, so he wrote:

    "Long enough so I can put my hair into Princess Leia buns if I wanted to. I would also have bangs that covered my entire forehead."

    Bobby's mom had always told him that his name if he were born a girl would have been Candice Marie Searle. He thought for a moment if that's what he should change his name to. He toyed with making a distaff tribute to his comic book hero, Reed Richards, considering calling himself Rebecca Renee Searle. But his mom was the one who birthed him, so shouldn't she decide her name? Bobby couldn't make heads or tails of it, so he acquired a quarter from his new purse, and tossed it in the air. "Heads, I'm Candice. Tails, I'm Becky."

    If you want to have a happy life as a girl, pass this on to the next person. Boy or Girl, doesn't matter.

As she walked out of the cafeteria and into the hallway, she was ogled by everyone, and her confidence level was at an all time high. She was the best of all worlds: brainy, beautiful and brawny.
