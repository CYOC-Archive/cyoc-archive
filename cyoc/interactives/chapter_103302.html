    Jennifer thinks. Which does she choose? Transform would ruin her, but who knows what Dare would cause. She chose.

    "Transform. Please don't ruin me." She says whinny like. "If this dumb curse is even real."

    "Well. I've always been merciful, so you will become a werewolf." Kyle says. "To be clear, every night you will experience small transformations like growing a tail, sharper teeth, all that starting at 12:00 o' clock. That nights transformation will dissappear by the morning. But, sometimes you will completely turn. These times will be unpredictable, and random. Got that?"

    Jennifer nods, listening in facination, even though she didn't believe this. "Yeah, I got it."

    "OK, well, when you turn completely, you will go feral. The next morning you will be guilty and sad about what you'd done. Sometimes you will keep small transformations in the day; I never said this would be easy did I?" He laughs. "Anyway, you will need to be stratification about this. Be careful and plan your days. Take notes; what triggers transformations?"

    Jennifer begins to sweat. He was telling the truth. She could tell.

    "Well, I got a school to get to. Good luck, and have a good day. Honest."

    Kyle hangs up. Jennifer feels humble. Kyle could have done a lot worse. But he didn't. Why? She puts the rest of her clothes on and heads outside.

    As she waits for the bus, she pulls out her phone and looks up "werewolf", "Transform or dare", and "are werewolves evil". Suddenly Peter walks up to her, pushing up his glasses. He was waiting too.

    "Huh. The bus is late." He says, then smiles at Jennifer.

    Jennifer sighs. "Whatever..."

    Peter frowns. "Why do you popular people ignore average people? Or be rude to them? Just because you think you are better doesn't mean you are."

    Jennifer thinks about that. Suddenly she was hit with an intense headache. "Ouch," She sighs.

    "You OK? " Peter asks as the bus pulls up.

    "Yeah, just a headache." Jennifer says.
    Was it really? Jennifer wasn't so sure; maybe it was a transformation. She boards the bus with Peter behind.
