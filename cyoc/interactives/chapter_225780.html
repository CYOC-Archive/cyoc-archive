Perhaps it was the admission from his crush that he harbored similar feelings… Or, on second thought, it was definitely that. He hastily brought it over to Scott, his awkward gait slowing him down just a bit as he adjusted to the distinct feeling of the training pants. “Don’t forget this.” He lifted his adorable, pudgy hands up to offer the device to his accomplice.

    Scott winced, dreading the thought of losing his independent mobility. “Right as always. Okay, let’s get to it.”

    Kai nodded and made his way to their housemates, walking imperfectly the whole way out of the room. Left by himself, Scott was feeling more determined than ever to steal NGA’s tech. It was, after all, their idiotic, sadistic gameshow that had done this to him and Kai. Now, it wasn’t just the righteous cause of sharing the tech’s miracle with the public that propelled him; there was an equal part petty revenge that wanted to stick it to the producers for putting him through this mess. Hell, he’d love to give those guys upstairs a taste of their own medicine… but that was a long way off. The first step would be checking doors.

    With a determined spirit, Scott left the bedroom, holding down the button of the camera looper while doing so. He could move freely without worry of the audience or the producers catching wind of his and Kai’s plot. Immediately across from their room was another room. Scott was confident that this was Ernesto and Jackson’s room, and peaking in through the unlocked door confirmed as much when he saw the identical setup to his bedroom.

    Continuing through the upper level, the young agent came across two separate bathrooms, some closets, and two grand gathering areas with even grander windows that spanned a sizeable portion of their outer walls. That was just in his first five minutes of perusal of the level. As Scott explored further, a surprising truth revealed itself to him: even though Barrie had said there could be locked doors in the house, Scott had yet to find a single one! He’d found even more bedrooms, unused, and there was even a small library room. In his thorough inspection of each area, no hidden doors made themselves apparent to him. Then, a daunting realization struck the boy: what if the tech wasn’t even in the house? After all, Kai had pointed out that the tech could be used at long-range, as demonstrated by his water park regression. What if the tech was contained within a neighboring house? What if the tech was miles away, with the ability to manipulate the age of a subject from anywhere in the world? That would make sense, of course, from the producers’ perspective; keeping the tech out of reach of those most affected by it would help ensure its safety. Scott felt beads of sweat forming on his neck, the buzzing swarm of anxious ideations bombarding his very sanity. Had all this been for nothing?! Was there ever any hope of getting the tech at all? If not, then Scott would have to actually compete in this stupid game show. What’s more, he’d be pitted against Kai, and there would be no chance of the two ending up together, at least if he wanted to win. If they both ended up in diapers, they could grow up together, but would he really want that, really risk that? Was a schoolboy’s fleeting crush worth sacrificing his very status as a man?

    Taking a deep breath, Scott realigned himself. Calming himself down in stressful situations was, in fact, part of his extensive agency training. He pulled in the air thoughtfully through his nose, letting it out slowly from his mouth. This soothing cycle would carry on for as long as was necessary, until the agent could once again think clearly. The boy spent a good couple minutes centering his thoughts. If both Kai’s employer and the agency had suspected the tech of being housed within this sprawling Tudor, then there was likely a very compelling rationale for the two of them being here. Scott knew what he was worth to the agency, and seeing Kai’s skills, he was sure his competitor had also made himself indispensable. There was no way either of their employers would send them in here without good reason to believe they could succeed.

    Scott lay back onto the floor of the hall, relaxing for a moment before continuing his search for inaccessible doors. That was when something odd caught his attention. There, right above him at the end of hall near the library, a faint, almost indiscernible, rectangular outline was carved into the ceiling. It was unmistakably the mark of an attic door, all without the telltale string and trim border. Without having checked the lower level, Scott had the distinct, unshakable feeling that this was the door they’d need to bypass in order to succeed in their mission.

    Two problems immediately came to mind, however. The first was that a ladder, or any sort of height boost, would be necessary to reach the door. Then, the second and more concerning issue was that the door had no distinguishable means to open it. No string. No handle. No knob. It was perfectly flat against the ceiling, and the minuscule gap between the door and surrounding ceiling was likely far too small to jam a crowbar in. The most likely explanation was that the door was opened via an electronic remote, possessed only by the producers and show staff. He and Kai would either have to get very clever, or they’d need some means of contacting one of their employers to request a door-opening dead drop of some kind.
