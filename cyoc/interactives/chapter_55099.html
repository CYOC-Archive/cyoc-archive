    existence of witchcraft or sorcerery!" The Council of the Suprema had told her to her face
    on a Tuesday afternoon (since that was normally when they were closed and not in
    session), "And we will not grant resources to such a ridiculous fantastic quest!"

    Marie knew her limit was five years. She had exactly five years to find allies and to find the
    witches and kill them. She was an Inquisition she didn't cringe from killing.

    "Miss Cruz, if I may ask, what do you plan to do with these 'witches' if they really exist,
    and if you're really able to find them, and you're really able to subdue them?" Asked a lanky
    young man who looked like he had just earned his robes as a cleric.

    Marie remembered the last thing her god father had ever said to her,

    <i> Her god father placed his hands on her shoulders and knelt down looking her in the
    eyes, "Marie, God's universe is one based on truth, remember that if nothing else." </i>

    Marie turned to look at him with her good eye and smiled, "They'll face the one thing
    people who despise order hate above all else: due process."

    Marie found her way to the perch of a very familiar Gargoyle. He had read through the
    records. She looked at Vatican, had she really left this all behind for a year before coming
    home? Her home? The only real home she had left in this world?

    Then again, Abraham, or rather, Abaza, had shook all of them to the core. Princess of

    order whose duty was to protect pilgrims on their journey to the holy land. But your king

    faith. As a Knights Templar, I ask you to fulfill your duty one last time."
