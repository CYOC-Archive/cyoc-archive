    Tina finishes her shopping, making sure to throw in a pack of diapers in Leah's size. Leah wants to protest, but at the look on Tina's face, she stays quiet. What had happened earlier? Why did she want to have an accident just because she saw a baby having one? And why did it feel so good while it was happening?

    In the checkout lane, Leah feels a twinge in her bladder. Excitement overcomes her at the opportunity she has. Peeing her underwear had been one thing, and an enjoyable one at that. But peeing her diaper? That sounds even better!

    Eagerly, Leah pees, a smile on her face as warmth spreads through her diaper. When her bladder empties, however, she suddenly is disgusted and ashamed. Why is she doing this?! But she can't deny how good it felt while she was peeing.

    At the car, Tina buckles Leah into a car seat, given her babyish appearance. As she sits Leah down, she can tell her stepdaughter's diaper has a squish to it. She shakes her head. The diaper was the right choice.

    Leah sits in the car seat on the way home, messing around on her phone. They've stopped in rush hour traffic when Leah feels the need to poop again. She's always been one to have more bowel movements than average. There's a gas station nearby that Tina can easily pull into so Leah can use the restroom.

    But...Leah doesn't want that. She wants to use the restroom she's wearing. A smile on her face, Leah begins to push. A log enters her diaper.

    Tina looks in the rearview mirror. She can hear grunting from Leah and smell something. Her suspicions are confirmed. She works in a daycare. She knows a happy pooping face when she sees one. This is beyond strange. Leah has always had poor potty training, but she's never seemed to enjoy her close calls. And right now, she's clearly pooping herself on purpose, just because she likes it!

    Tina sighs.

    Leah finishes pushing out her poop. She settles back in her seat, enjoying the feeling of the mess in her diaper for only a few seconds before, again, being disgusted. Something has to be wrong with her. She shouldn't like pooping and peeing herself! But then she remembers how it feels, and smiles again.
