    Younger than expected

    added by AnarchyNinetyNine 3 years ago I O Reality alteration

    Unbeknownst to Braiden. Maggie had vanished from existence as soon as he pressed that button. Quite simply; she no longer exists. Not even as an object or anything. Completely gone. Lost in time.

    But Braiden does notice a message on the top bar of the phone. He drags it down and read it. 'Subjects are actually in grade six. In middle school as part of a taster day.' Braiden panics as soon as it gets to his head. The transformed group are actually younger than expected, and Brodie is still in grade 10 which means he is graduating this year.

    Braiden rushes to 'his' class. It is full of grade six students. He also sees Callum's body that is occupied by Max. But not vice versa. Callum is in another group, which makes sense considering that the body is from a student from a different school.

    Braiden sits down on a chair just in time and puts the phone away. He doesn't need it getting confiscated right now. He listens to the teacher talk about life in middle school. Braiden has heard it all, he had the talk a few years ago too. Before the regression. Then he remembers the dreaded part: grouping up with randoms and talking about themselves. Braiden has always hated that part and it's made even worse by not actually being this young.

    Sure enough. The groups are formed and Braiden is forced to talk to kids their junior but acting like they weren't. This is made even worse when he has to speak with current grade eights and treat them like elders.

    But somehow. Braiden passes through the chats without a slip up. He came close to it but held his tongue. That was also when he suddenly realised uniform isn't required at the school.

    'Oh man. What else has this device changed.' He asks himself. Then he picks up on it being a middle school and not a high school. Another difference that passed Braiden.

    The lunchtime bell rings and everyone is forced into a cafeteria. Once again: something never experienced. He sees Callum as Max and walks over. Callum notices, sighs and smiles. "Man am I glad to see you."
    "Why? What happened?" Enquires Braiden.
    "The usual group chat. Except. It turns out Max is a bit of an extrovert, and his friends noticed me being a little more introverted and quiet. They were wondering what was wrong with me."

    Max in Callum's body places a tray down and sits. "Got any good news?" He asks.
    "Not yet," replies Braiden, "Just that a few other things have changed without warning."
    "Like the casual clothes and being a middle school?" Asks Callum.
    "Exactly," replies Braiden, "I definitely didn't do it."

    A few minutes pass. Callum asks Max some questions that would be enough to pass if he faced more questions. Then he asks him how to be more extroverted. Max has no idea and replies with, "It just came naturally to me. I guess you could imagine everyone in their underwear?"
    "Ew! No. Not that fat pig of a teacher," Callum fakes a throwing up motion causing Braiden and Max to laugh.
    "So. We'll meet after school," suggests Braiden.
    "Sure," reply Callum and Max in unison.

    After lunch. They walk back into their respective classrooms.
