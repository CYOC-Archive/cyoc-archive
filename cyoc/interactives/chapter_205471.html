    Facing their respective cameras were four completely random individuals who were choosing to play, or at least check out, A-Game-Of-Change: Online. Each of them were unique in their own way, but were defintiely about to get a lot more familiar.

    “So… do we greet each other before we play this?” A voice asked. Looking at their profile, the name was Trenton. Trenton was a Caucasian male with long wavy blonde hair plus a fairly thick amount of stubble matching his hair. He was wearing a fairly plain sweater over a shirt. From a glance, you could assume he was probably in college. His accent was defintiely American.

    “Yeah, I guess so,” another voice replied. This one was female, named Judy. She was black but her accent suggested she was Australian. Her hair was long and straight, and spread over her chest. Notably, her defining feature would probably be her chest. She didn’t seem to be emphasising it, but she was clearly well-endowed, stretching her lavender top.

    “So has anyone not done this before?” A male voice asked, this one belonging to a man named Adam. Adam had a rich latino look, with a moustache and soul patch plus a little beard under his chin. His hair was black and curly, neat. He was wearing a very casual t-shirt as well, something you’d wear if you knew things might get messy…

    “I’m new to this, I got recommended by a friend,” the fourth and final voice said. Harper. She sounded British, and definitely had the highest voice. Her whole style didn’t quite match her voice however, with dyed black hair, eyeliner and darker eye-shadow. Her lips painted a burgundy red. Despite the very casual nature of the rest of the group, she seemed to have put in some effort to her look. She was wearing a very low-cut tank top despite lacking in certain departments, with the edges of her bra visible to the camera. Something about her screamed attention.

    “It means change. Literally,” Adam stated, “once we start, the game is going to change you. Physically, mentally, emotionally. And none of it can be versed until the very end of the game,”
    “That doesn’t make much sense?” Judy said,
    “I know,” Adam said, “but trust me, as weird as it sounds, and as weird as it will be, it’s totally worth it!”

    READY

    “Hey!” She snapped back at him, “who said you were the ‘know-it-all’ because you played a couple games already,”
