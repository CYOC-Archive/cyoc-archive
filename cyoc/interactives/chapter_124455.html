    The day they all went to the dogs

    added 5 years ago A BM S

    Leaving the library in a rush, Maggie and Brody found themselves outside with Callum and Braiden, watching over the dogs who used to be their former comrades in dread.
    " Maggie. " stammered Brody, nervous, " There's no way we can let them stay like... Like this ! I can't think of them as dogs ! What about school ? And their families ?! What are we gonna expl- "
    " Brody, stay calm ! " asked Maggie abruptly. " I'm trying to think about a solution for that matter, and you're not helping in any case ! "
    - Alright ! Alright ! I'm sorry...
    Brody looked over the dogs, which were sticking close and staring at them with a glimmer of hope, whimpering meekly.
    - Look, if we're using this horrible device again, can't we use it at least in a less crowded place ? I'm not comfortable with the idea that someone might freak out after witnessing the resolution of this problem...
    Maggie considered her options, and did exactly the same actions as his friend by also glaring at her furry companions.
    - You kinda make a point. But where should we be go ?
    - Why not one of our places ? It's best if we don't stray any farther from there if we plan on testing some inputs...
    She made a faint smile. Callum and Braiden panted while watching the whole scene.
    - Well, I'm in.

    Brody's house, fortunately, was vacant at that time, his parents working all day up until the evening.
    Maggie checked on the phone during the trip, trying to seek a particular input which would sensibly help them on this case, but it seemed to end up confusing her a bit more. Still, she looked like she had at least an idea of what to do.
    Brody wasn't that reassured, but he was forced to trust her since he had no clue on how to operate such a peculiar phone without making the situation worse.
    Meanwhile, Callum and Braiden were sitting still, accustoming to their new forms and careful not to break anything valuable, for they knew of Brody's mom' tempers on the subject.
    " Hum... I might have it... " started Maggie.
    " Please, tell me you know how to use it properly... " urged Brody.
    - I do. I think...
    She glanced at the dogs, trying not to look silly with her doubt.
    - Okay, boys, come here now. Im going to bring you back to your original selves.
    They did not asked twice for that to be repeated.
    - Good ! Now, checking the options... pointing the device at them... " fix to normal " ... Applying changes...
    - Finally ! cried out Brody in his breath.
    - And... Go !
    Just like earlier at the library, there was a sudden flash which engulfed the whole group.

    Brody knew even before that it had definitively not worked the way it intended for everyone. As far as he was concerned, he did not recall that he used to sit on his haunches, and on four members nonetheless.
    When he did finally open his eyes, he wasn't even disappointed to still see Callum as a Shepherd and Braiden as a Corgi, staring both at him and Maggie alternatively with worried looks.
    While he was on the same eye level as them.
    He felt sick, thinking about the inevitable and the oddness of his body, before deciding to slowly give a glance at his phone-expert friend.
    Maggie as a human was long gone. That, or maybe she actually went outside home after putting an Ainu dog at the exact spot where she stood. The female-human-but-now-dog looked confused, before looking around and get a catch of what was happening. When she saw Brody, she immediately made a bark of surprise and jumped out of fright, before whimpering when she realised the outcome of those actions.
    Brody got up on his haunches, and trotted over to the hallway, reaching out to the mirror that faced the shoes and coats closet at the front door, willing to get over with his situation and to know what happened.
    Just in front of him, mimicking his very movements, tail wagging and tongue lolling out of its muzzle, the spitting image of an annoyed boxer dog was staring right at him.
    He stood this way several seconds, raising a paw to test the reality of the situation.
    He went back to Maggie, still shocked, comforted by her other dumbfounded canine companions.
    " Well, it did not worked that good, did it ? " Brody barked, looking not surprised at all.
