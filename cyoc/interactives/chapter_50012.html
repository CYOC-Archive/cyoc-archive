    Patrick Hounds Sr. a man who fought and lived through two wars, and had made
    cooperate enforcers run away scared once in his life, and had bought a small failing
    business for a song and made thrive had seen the unbelievable, the illogical, and the just
    plain impossible, and like anyone who had lived long enough had come to one of two
    conclusion, either he had seen it all or realized he never would see it all (he was the
    former). And so when faced with the impossible, nay, beyond the scope of all his long
    life's experiences (a bunch of savage beast creatures going about like people), he had done
    the only thing his brain could manage and blacked out.

    When he began to come to, his hope that he had just been dreaming were instantly
    dashed.

    "Gramps always was one for surprises." He heard a little girl's voice say.

    "He's not a mundane wolf now, I guess that runs down the idea of someone's Transform or
    Dare triggering if they enter Glendale." He heard a boy above the age of the girl say in a
    calm collect and somewhat disappointed tone.

    "Let's turn him into a she wolf! I have this acrylic bright red color I saw on an anime poster
    that would be perfect for fur and I wanna see how a she wolf would look like with a G
    cub!" He heard a woman's voice say with a tone and attitude that said she was in charge
    and knew it and had no problem in reminding everyone she was.

    "Not now Kaylee." He heard a gruff voice says in a firm and no sense tone.

    "Yes Master." Replied the female voice.

    "Well, I say we go with what we were gonna go with before." Said the little girl's voice.

    "I want to add some improvements to that idea." Said a younger girl's voice but sounding
    more semantically precise than the older one. "What if he was like Martha?"

    "I'm not watching something grow another head again Alice." Said the woman's voice,
    apparently Kaylee.

    "I did not mean like that Alpha Female." Replied apparently Alice politely. "But if he is to be
    a pet for us, should he not be an interesting one?"

    "Have you -really- tested the ability of the game to make people into objects yet?" Said a
    adult male voice less rough than the first, "I think the fountain outside has always needed
    a statue."

    "Be quite Zackery, the alphas are discussing." Said Kaylee.

    "And we DO know that," The little boy's voice said, "There was an incident with a tea pot
    apparently in our family some years back. But doing that would leave that turn lost forever
    and since we're not expanding the pack as much after yesterday we can't afford to lose any
    turns."

    "I still think-" Zackery began but was cut off by a teenage voice that reserved, calm, but
    sounding deadly serious.

    "Master Bart has spoken Master Zack, now be quite."

    "... I offer my deepest apologies. I am sorry if I overstepped my bounds, forgive me."

    Patrick felt there was just something not right about the apology, but he was apparently
    the only one who picked up on it.

    "Forgiven."

    "Thank you Kaylee."

    "As I was going to say and am now going to say," Began Alice, "Why not make him an
    interesting wolf? If more than one head is too much, how about making him bigger to help

    "How about he has a loco on his side like Bolt does?" A younger little boy's voice asked.

    "That would be very interesting indeed." Said Alice in a curious voice.

    "Hey I think he's awake." Said the big rough male voice. "Come on great grandpa, I can tell
    you're faking being asleep again, I remember that much about you. Everyone else thought
    you were a tired old man but I know you better than that."

    Reluctantly Patrick did open his eyes. Found them facing the biggest and most fearsome
    wolf he had seen in his life. Patrick realized his arms were tied up, but not his legs. He did

    "Father!" "Papa!" "Papa!" "Master!" "Lord-Dad!"
