    You grin back. "Well we're on a tropical beach, we've got all the time in the world, and this—" you point at your tail "—is like the world's best-ever scuba gear. So why don't we go for a swim?"

    Alex agrees. "Sounds like a plan, cuz! Last one in the water is a rotten egg!" She coils her tail underneath herself and pushes off, and you do the same, and pretty soon the both of you are seal-flopping back into the surf, at least until the water is deep enough for you to wiggle your tails a bit and start to kind-of swim.

    While it's still shallow enough that your heads are above water, Alex giggles. "This feels so weird. The sand is tickling my boobs!"

    "Gah," you sputter, "you're my cousin, don't talk to me about your boobs."

    Alex just giggles again and dives under the water; you follow her, and you're astonished to find that in addition to feeling pleasantly cool (not at all cold) and breathing comfortably, you can also see for quite a distance with perfect clarity. It's like one of those Discovery Channel documentaries with the underwater cameras: and the sight is amazing. The beach itself, even under the water, is little more than a barren, sandy slope with patterns of ridges formed by the ebb and flow of the tides, and a smattering of pebbles, shells, and the occasional starfish strewn about. But once you swim past the sand-bar and enter the deeper waters on the other side, you find reefs.

    Coral reefs! Wondrous sights of color and life: branching corals, brain corals, sea anemones and sponges. Shoals of pelagic fish, a pod of dolphins, some surprisingly playful basking-sharks and sting-rays, and even sea-turtles. You and Alex swim together for what seems like hours, frolicking with the sea life, laughing, exploring. It's absolutely incredible, maybe the most amazing thing you've ever seen in your entire life. You feel like you could probably do this for days and never get tired.

    But you do eventually notice that you're getting hungry, and when you say as much to Alex, she agrees. (Your voices sound very strange under the water: muffled, and lower in pitch. But you can still talk, and you can hear each other just fine: both your vocal cords and your ears have adapted to the water along with the rest of you.) "I wonder what mermaids eat?" she asks.

    "I don't know," you answer. "I wish Uncle Jack had prepared us a little better for this stupid getaway of his."

    "Well I don't think it was his idea," says Alex. "He didn't know we'd pick the merman costume."

    "How do you know?" you grumble sullenly. "The guy's a wizard, maybe he read our minds."

    "Oh, whatever!" scoffs Alex. Suddenly she flicks her tail and darts off like a torpedo, and you only have a second to whirl around and realize that Alex is now hovering in the water behind you, holding onto a huge, struggling tuna-fish. "Come on!" she grunts. "Help me drag Charlie here back to the beach!"

    You shake your head. "Please do NOT name the food."

    * * *

    A very trying two hours later, you and Alex have discovered a few new things. One, merpeople are very good at catching fish. Two, unlike certain movies and TV shows that try to be "realistic," merpeople do not have scary teeth or claws that make them particularly good at butchering fish. So you had to make do with sharp pieces of coral and shell, to limited and varying degrees of success. Three, making a fire on a tropical beach is damned-near impossible when you can't get very far inland to find dry brush or kindling to burn. Four, raw tuna doesn't taste that bad, at least not to to the palate of a mermaid and a merman. In fact, it's pretty great.

    As the sun goes down, you and Alex are reclined on the beach together, finishing off the last of the tuna. Between the two of you, you ate the whole thing, and greedily; maybe you worked up an appetite with all your activity that day, or maybe merfolk just had powerful metabolisms. You didn't know, but you hoped it wasn't the latter, or you'd be spending a large part of this vacation hunting for food.

    You look up to the top of the cliff, where you can still see that line of jungle-trees. You figure that that area is totally inaccessible to you, so you probably won't be having any coconuts anytime soon.

    You look back at Alex, who is now combing her golden hair with a broken bit of coral. The fading sunlight is glistening off her tail-scales, and curve of her generous bust is accentuated by the deepening shadows. Not for the first time, you regret conjuring up the dream-girl mermaid in your imagination just as your cousin was about to reappear and be transformed. Alex was just so indescribably beautiful, alluring like a siren of yore . . . for a moment, you're actually wryly thankful that both of you appear to lack genitals. (At least, you think that's the case; but you haven't really looked hard enough to be sure, and certainly neither has Alex. Honestly, you're kind of afraid to confirm it. But for now, all you can be sure of is the fact that if you were still human and ogling a mermaid this stunningly hot, you'd definitely have a raging boner right now, and instead, below the waistline, you presently feel zip, zilch, nada, nothing at all.)

    Still, thinking this way about your own cousin isn't something you like the notion of. You'd rather it not become a problem in the following weeks. So you start to wonder: are there other girls around here who could maybe distract you, draw your attention? Other girls on this beach somewhere, or maybe (you can only hope) other mermaids out in the ocean nearby? You can't say, but it's worth exploring.

    "Hey, Alex," you say, as you stretch out on the beach and make yourself comfortable. "What say we go looking for other people tomorrow?"

    "What, you mean like human-type people?"

    "Maybe. Or we can see if Uncle Jack sent us to a place with other mer-people to meet."

    Alex too lies down and snuggles into the warm, tropical sand, placing her hands behind her head like a pillow. She looks up into the evening sky as the first stars appear. "I guess. At the very least, we should swim up and down the coast a ways, see what there is to see. Do you think we're on an island?"

    "Maybe. I dunno." You close your eyes and try to drift off to sleep. "Guess we'll find out in the morning."
