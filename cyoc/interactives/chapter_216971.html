The wereotter was tall and willowy, using her sturdy tail to propel herself through the water.     Olivia put a webbed hand to her whiskered face. "That's horrible! What did you do?"

    Olivia's eyes lit up with glee. "Camp Lutra? I go there every year! Yeah, they'll be able to make you a wereotter. My sister's an otterborn human, and the shamans will perform the turning ritual on her now that she's old enough."

    "What's an otterborn human?" asked Pearl.

    "It means a child of wereotters who's a regular human," Olivia explained. "The ritual was created to cure them so they can stay with the romp. Fair warning, the ritual has parts that can be a bit... sexual."

"It used to be Pearl Silverscales, but I changed it when I decided that I'd become a wereotter instead of turning back into a mermaid. Because, y'know... otters don't have scales. Uh, you pr-probably n-n-noticed that." Pearl blushed a deeper shade of red, bashfully hiding her face behind her long blonde hair. Why did she have to get so awkward when she was naked with a beautiful girl?

    "Aww!" Olivia smiled. "That's so sweet! Let's keep meeting up even before camp starts. I come here to swim every day."

    Olivia gave Pearl a whiskery kiss on the cheek. "I'd like that. Maybe I can even introduce you to my romp. My mom's sister, Aunt Ottoline, is our shaman. She's also Camp Lutra's director. How does meeting the Laughingbrook Romp tomorrow afternoon sound?"

    "Sounds storming!" Pearl laughed.

    "Storming?" Olivia repeated, raising a furry eyebrow questioningly.

    "Mermaid slang," Pearl explained. "It means 'cool' or 'awesome'."

    Olivia smiled. "Oh, then I guess it is one of my more storming ideas. But for now, I say we enjoy a little swim in our birthday suits."

    "You wear yours so well," Pearl said with a wink before diving into the lake with Olivia.
