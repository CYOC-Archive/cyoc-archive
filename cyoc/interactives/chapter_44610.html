    Dustin was dizzy and confused from what just happened, not to mention
    the feeling that everything in the room was smaller than it was
    before. It occurred to him distantly that to him, everything now was.
    He put his hand to his forehead and felt it slick with sweat, like he
    had just had something a bit too spicy. In fact, his stomach felt like
    that, too.

    "So, what just happened?" Dustin felt like the slow kid in class for
    asking that question. Matt turned to him from the stove with a grin
    filled with warmth and pride.

    He came to the table and said, "Sit down, son," just as he did the
    same. Dustin heard the slight creaks in the chairs as they took on
    more weight than they ever had before. "Now, I can see how this might
    be hard for you to understand. Believe me, it wasn't easy for me,
    either. You see, I never knew my daddy. My mom had to raise me herself
    after he died when I was just two years old. When I was thirteen she
    told me some things that I think you oughta know, too, now that your -
    my son." The last part came with the slightest of pauses, causing both
    Dustin and Matt to smile.

    "My father's name, my name, and now your name is Langley. And it's a
    name to be proud of. You see, there are three things you must know and
    always remember about the Langley men. First, the sons always outgrow
    the fathers. My mama told me dad was a little over six feet tall, and
    his father just under. You know how tall I am, and, if I have my guess
    right, you're probably right around six foot five, give or take."

    "No, I'm --"

    "Not any more," Matt resumed with a smile. "Don't you worry though,
    sport, you got some growin' left in you. I at least hope you'll grow
    into those feet," Matt joked. Dustin looked down self-consciously. He
    had thought his feet pretty hefty at size fourteen, but there was no
    way he could squeeze his massive dogs into those any more. Especially
    if the ruined remains of his old shoes were any indication.

    "You will grow into them," Matt said firmly, noting the concerned look
    on Dustin's face. "And until we get some new ones, you can wear my
    shoes, though they might be a bit tight. As I was sayin, you are gonna
    be a big boy. But that's just the first thing. The second is that
    women find Langley men hard to resist, and during some phases of the
    moon, they go crazy for us. I just want you to be prepared. Always be
    prepared. And if you need extras, just ask me." Dustin couldn't
    believe he was talking about this with his dad, but even more
    unbelieveable was the fact that it seemed so natural. Like he was some
    combination of older brother, best friend and great dad. Ty had really
    struck gold with this one.

    "Finally," Matt concluded. "Langley men are warm and passionate, but
    we also have a temper. We have a hard time taking anything from
    anybody. So, you must ALWAYS treat people with respect. And if they
    still won't quit, well then, I'm sure you can use your imagination."
    With that Matt grinned, stood up and pulled Dustin up with him. He
    looked at his son for a moment and said, "Yep, just about six five,"
    causing them both to smile again.
