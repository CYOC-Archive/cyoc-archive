    Personally, Naomi thought that the decision about Spike was way too merciful, but 'Miss J'

    Still feeling a subconscious intimidation of the predator species, Jenny nodded. The idea of
    a fawn, not that it was inside her brain, felt more tempting by the minute, a sense of,
    completion came with it.

    Su and Lateisha knew that look.

    "Just don't do anything impulsive Jenny, and don't sign anything without talking to a
    lawyer." Su Yin wisely advised. Jenny nodded. Ironically, Jenny's position with the bucks,
    enabled her to be the best one to gauge their behavior. Having slept with nearly every
    buck in the park (excluding Spike, Sam, and Drake), she was able to tell that many of her
    mates had suddenly developed the habit after Sam's orders, to actually -talk- before and
    after the union, something Jenny realized she found a strange breath of fresh air. Maybe a
    long term relationship wouldn't be so bad, and with Spike in tow, she knew she'd likely
    scare away the short term thrill seekers. And maybe she'd find someone for Hannah too in
    the process.

    Lateisha shook her head, sometimes she felt like she was the only normal one in this
    group. Yes, a group of evolved deer with two of their number having been originally
    males. Okay, so many normal wasn't the best word. But she wasn't a collage professor in
    the outside world like Su Yin, or had personal issues with the outside version of herself.
    Almost made her feel... left over. At least she was here to offer girl advice when Hannah
    was distracted. She just hoped Jenny didn't regret this decision. She didn't know how much
    of Jenny the doe was an act, but she certainly acted a little naive sometimes.

    Hannah... made it a point not to ask about Sam. She better than to ask. Sam no longer
    existed except as a memory in her head now. She had dived into her self appointed task of
    helping the intentional and accidental TGed anthro park guests. Making sure they didn't
    go crazy, or make some decisions they shouldn't. She had taken Laura's advice, and done
    her best to let Sam go.

    Leading Jenny though the forest, not using the Husky Only rout, Jenny, feeling nervous all
    the same but strangely obedient, followed the predator.

    Arriving that camouflaged building, an opening appeared where there seemed to be none
    before, and the two entered the 'air lock' the handleless door on the other side opened on
    it's own, and Jenny was split between her dislike of the unnatural place, or being nearer
    Naomi's teeth.

    Naomi made a point on not having Jenny needing to encounter any humans along the way.
    She had enough to deal with. The hallway and room that she lead Jenny though was as
    stark and featureless as the airlock. It was like the the Managers WANTED these guests to
    prefer being naked along the bushes.

    The doors opening automatically for them, Jenny was soon in a bright, featureless smooth
    room, with a fawn sitting by his lonesome inside.

    Jenny started at the urge to hold the fawn close to her. After all, an abandoned fawn needs
    a mother. She knew this was no fawn, this was Spike, the monster who had nearly reeked
    havoc in the park. But at the same time... she found she was starting to have trouble
    connecting the two.

    Naomi just stood back her arms crossed observing the scene unfold. Seeing the hypnotic
    look in Jenny's eyes that appeared when an anthro's instincts were having a nice little
    sweet talk with the rational part of their brains.

    The exact same look was in Spike's eyes.

    Spike had been confused and disoriented since he had been de-aged and taken off his
    painkillers. They told him it was a necessary after effect of repairing his body, not
    technically a lie. His brain felt like it was in a fog, or soup. Thus he accepted this
    explanation without question, after all, the grown up had told him it was so, and grown
    ups knew better. This combined with the trauma he had suffered in the first place, had left
    his mind like clay, impressionable, and easily moldable.

    Spike took one look at Jenny, and instead of seeing an object to stem his lust like he
    would have before, his brain now saw a missing piece of the puzzle. Before he even knew
    what he was doing, he was holding her legs. Jenny meanwhile, before she even knew what
    she was doing, had her arms around him.

    Spike didn't fully understand it, but he knew he felt a hundred times safer just with the
    doe holding him. While Jenny felt... like she was more?

    Spike followed Jenny without being told to.

    Naomi knew the Managers would be very pleased with this result.
