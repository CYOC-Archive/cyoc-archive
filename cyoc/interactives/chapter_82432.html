    Lady Runs Away

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added 9 years ago A

    After Marcy left, Lady started pacing back and forth. What was she going to do? She'd made an impulsive
    decision, once that she really hadn't given any thought to. Now, she was trapped.

    It took only a short while before she decided she needed to get out of here, out of the house. She needed to
    go out and talk a walk.

    Given that she still had her human mind, she was easily able to figure out how to open the back gate (it had a
    bad latch, a couple jumps were all that were needed to open it) and took off into the night. Maybe she'd find
    the answer out there.

    **

    Two hours later, she was already regretting yet another impulsive decision.

    She was tired and thirsty, and had somehow managed to get so far from home that she wasn't sure exactly how to
    get back. And for that matter, with all her frustration with perhaps being stuck as a dog, she didn't really
    think about what running away meant. The best case was getting found by someone and returned home and being
    stuck where she started. Worse would be getting adopted by a family that didn't know she was once human.

    Worse would be getting stuck in the pound and put down as an unadoptable stray.

    She dearly wanted to return home, but felt like she was too tired to move.

    "Oh, you poor thing," she heard a woman say from not far away.

    Lady stood, panting and tired while the woman approached quietly and gently. She sniffed instinctually at the
    offered hand, finally giving it a friendly, tired lick.

    The woman took her home and gave her a bowl of water, which she drank greedily. It turned out to be the home
    of three small children who fawned over the poor, lost dog.

    "Can we keep her?" one of them asked.

    "She's missing her collar, but she's owned by someone. We need to figure out who she belongs to."

    I'm missing my collar? thought Lady. That doesn't make sense. I didn't remove it.

    It was getting late, so the father of the house laid a few blankets along the wall of the living room, where
    she slept.

    **

    She fully expected to see Marcy in the morning, but she didn't. The family put signs up in the neighborhood
    looking for her owners, but no one called. In the meantime, she played with the children, feeling for the
    first time since right after the transformation happy with the dog body she now wore. After a few weeks, the
    family stopped looking.

    She was given a new name by the youngest child, "Goldie", and a new collar and tag. She was taken to the
    groomer, given shots at the vet, and all the while spent each afternoon and often all day on the weekends
    playing with the children. The few hours of solitude she was left with between the family leaving in the
    morning and coming back after school were spent resting, getting ready for play. It had only been a few
    months, but she had long given up waiting for her old owner.

    The puppies weren't really planned by anyone. No one was home when she discovered a stray neighbors dog in
    the yard. The German Shepard had dug under the fence and was sniffing around. She had gone out to
    investigate, and hadn't realized the strange feelings she'd been having were being in heat. She forced the
    thought out of her mind almost until the moment she let the strange male take her.

    It would be weeks before her family realized what happened, but they rolled well with it. As she grew, Goldie
    couldn't move as fast, but she could still play with the children. Finally, she knew her time had come and
    with the family watching she gave birth to five large puppies.

    She allowed her instincts to kick in, grooming them and pushing them toward her teats for food. The children
    were delighted. Goldie could feel them nibbling at her teats, feeling a bond with them that she knew would
    only last a few weeks. After all, these were puppies, not children. Someone would adopt them after a time.

    She wondered if Marcy had planned this. She wondered it several times until the day had arrived for the
    family to start adopting out the puppies and a familiar teenage girl showed up with her parents to adopt.
    Goldie looked at Marcy and a knowing look passed between them. They chose a female pup and left. Goldie
    never saw Marcy again.

    Marcy, for her part, had been angry when Lady hadn't returned and had planned on leaving the dog at the
    strange house for a couple days as a punishment. As time passed, though, and she realized that Lady was
    actually happy where she was, she was actually getting to feel loved as a dog, not just as a companion for a
    superbeing, she decided to leave her there.

    She felt just fine with that choice.
