This was obviously with some consideration for the larger Animalians, like the Elephant, Rhinoceros, and Hippopotamus Animalians in mind, though as Abby and Samson filmed the front of the hotel, they found that it would apply to more than just those megafauna. Abby held the camera and did a wide shot that ran from the hotel's far front corner, which came fairly close to the inside of the islet and the atoll's central lagoon and then then across the front of the atoll. As they came across the front, they found Shuixing coming out the front door.

    "Hello, Shuixing," Samson called politely, "You're staying here?"

    "Yeah," Shuixing gave a small smile to the stallion, "mostly for the headroom."

    "Headroom?" Samson wondered, sounding a bit puzzled.

    "Yes," Shuixing nodded with a small smile, "If we're just coming up to the top of my skull, the normal hotel is fine and I can manage it... but I do have my horns and they stick up a bit further. If I'm not careful with them... well... I risk goring the ceiling."

    "Which I'm sure Mieni doesn't want to have to repair," Abby chuckled from behind the camera, "what do you and the other elements think of Mieni?"

    "It's interesting," Shuixing gave a smile, "the water smells a bit salty... but it is still good to swim in and my sisters and I have had some fun as well... And Banmul has had its own fun with some of the other guests here."

    "Other guests?" Samson wondered, "you mean the Dinotopians?"

    "Yeah," Shuixing nodded, "they can be rather fun to talk too... though some of them are so big and strong... they put me to shame as I am now."

    "Well... they were among the biggest animals to ever live," Abby commented, "you haven't had too many competitions?"

    "No... I'm here for some warmth and the sun," Shuixing smiled, "which Banmul is also good for."

    Abby and Samson both nodded at that. While all of Mieni's islets had had some degree of forested coverage, likely the result of seeds floating on currents or being brought in by humans, by accident or on purpose, when the hotel on Banmul was built a lot of the forested areas on that islet had to be cleared to make room for the hotel. It actually made for some good spots for sunbathing, which as Abby turned back to the lagoon, she could spot Rachel Stega laying on the beach, with a happy smile as a lone man applied sunscreen to her back and backplates.

    "Which shows just how far things have come," Samson commented, "and how Mieni is working to be available for all its guests, big and small... Animalian and Human..."
