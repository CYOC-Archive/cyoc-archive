But this was the next best thing.

    "For the upkeep of your establishments?" asked the interviewer.

    "Well, yes, but I'm also concerned for the physical and mental well-being of my employees," said Fabina.

    "Most...purveyors of adult entertainment don't care so much about their employees' health," said the interviewer tactfully.

Their employees need healthy bodies to their work and they can't work well if they're suffering from emotional issues of various kinds.     Fabina pointed at the African-American and said, "Barb here is rather prickly about the ordeal she's been through. It's not really a surprise that she decided to become a horny toad."

But at least Amos wasn't lonely now.

    "Isn't that a little season?" asked the interviewer.

    "Caribous exist all year long," said Fabina, "Holly wouldn't choose a caribou if she didn't want to be that 365 days of the year." What Fabina didn't mention was that Holly went for an extra feature and became a herm as well. Apparently she had gender disassociation or whatever the proper term was called. Fabina only didn't mention because that could be a touchy subject to say on live TV and Holly could decide for hirself when shi wanted to come out.

    "And what about the last one?" asked the interviewer, pointing at the Latina.

    "Ah, well, Quetzal is a bit of an odd one," said Fabina, "She's always believed that she was destined for something great. But she never had much of a clear idea of what that destiny is supposed to be. It's made her a bit suggestible, which is how I suspect she ended up falling in with that particular crowd. But she has a clear goal in mind now."

    "Let me guess," said the interviewer, "She's become a quetzal bird."

    "You know, we all expected that too," said Fabina, "She was probably going to become a resplendent quetzal at first. But apparently she had heard some things regarding summer plans, particularly the Around-the-World-in-Eighty-Days tour. We're still looking for someone to be our albatross, but she's volunteered to be our Quetzalcoatlus."

    "That's the largest pterosaur ever found, right?" asked the interviewer.

    "There has been some argument about that," said Fabina, "Some particular specimens found bigger than others and such. But I think the general consensus is that Quetzalcoatlus is the largest."

    "Won't that cause...housing trouble?" asked the interviewer.

    "That's precisely why Dinosaur City was founded," said Fabina, "Not to mention, Dinotopians aren't nearly as large as their prehistoric counterparts. You just have to look at our sauropods and theropods to prove it."

    "And I understand that Dinosaur City doesn't have the same, er, adult services as Lyre," said the interviewer.

There's more than enough to make up for her. Not everyone entered the adult entertainment service because they wanted to, after all."

    "Well said," said the interviewer, "Anything else you'd like to add."

    "That we hope that we'll be able to open more fully as vaccination gets around," said Fabina, "And we hope things will be back to normal by the time of the Animalia Games."
