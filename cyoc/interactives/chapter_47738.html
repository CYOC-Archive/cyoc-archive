    Jacob falls down a very large rabbit hole

    Jacob's first thought was rabies or mange-there was something wrong
    with the rabbit, and it seemed to be contagious to humans. The run
    back to camp was interrupted by his leg pain, but it was the sensation
    of fur filling the space between his skin and clothing that really
    freaked Jacob out. He knelt there in a crouching position
    unconsciously twitching his nose. Suddenly, he felt a bulge explode
    at the base of his spine. He jumped forward - which was really back
    toward where he had first encountered the rabbit. He had a tail. His
    ears felt a bit weird too, but he refrained from reaching up to touch
    them. He was amazed at how far his legs propelled him through the
    forest. He was sailing through the air a good 20-30 feet.
    Unfortunately, he hadn't thought about landing until his body hit the
    pine needle covered forest floor and began tumbling through the bushes
    and brush.

    He had the presence of mind to jump and get airborne again, as soon as
    he was able. He was now sailing toward the big wooden log that the
    rabbit had been sitting on. He adjusted his feet to land on the log
    and was pleased at his success. His shoes felt uncomfortably tight,
    but he wasn't about to run around barefoot on pine needles. He looked
    at his furry hand, and then got up the courage to reach up and touch
    the floppy ear hanging off the side of his head. He shook his head.
    This couldn't be happening. His natural stance now seemed to be a
    somewhat hunched position, but he forced himself to stand upright. He
    wobbled a bit, and stumbled backward off the log. He felt the pine
    needles giving way beneath him. He was sliding backwards. The next
    thing he knew he was falling down an earthen tunnel or maybe a steeply
    sloped well. He bounced on his butt, slid, spun and tumbled all the
    way down into the deep dark hole.

    Finally, he rolled to an abrupt stop against a wall of roots. He
    opened his eyes, and moved into his now natural crouching position,
    and his eyes adjusted to the light. He couldn't quite figure out the
    light source, but guessed it might be phosphorescent mold that was
    making the cave-room glow. It was most definitely underground with
    dirt floors and roots sticking out of the dirt walls, but the walls
    were all flat and squared. Framed pictures hung on the walls. There
    was an oriental rug on the dirt floor with a table in the center of
    it. There were some items on the table. Then Jacob noticed the ball of
    white fur disappearing down one of the three hallways that branched
    off from the room. He leapt into action, bounding across the room. But
    the white rabbit was faster and disappeared down the dark corridor,
    apparently turning right about 30 feet down the tunnel. Jacob through
    all caution to the winds. He wanted answers and despite the growing
    discomfiture with hid ill fitting clothes and shoes, he bounded down
    the hall after the rabbit. He skidded a bit nearly missing the turn,
    then found himself descending deeper into the earth. The hall he was
    plunging down got steeper and steeper, until he found himself tumbling
    forward and downward again. This time he caught himself with his
    forepaws and landed like a proper rabbit. This room was brightly
    plastered in pink with rough hewn door frames and door. The shaft he
    had fallen out of appeared to be insurmountable. He turned his
    attention to the door on the opposite side of the room. The rabbit was
    nowhere to be seen.

    Jacob's attention was drawn to the sign on the door which read,
    "Dress-Code Strictly Enforced: Please Change Into Proper Attire Before
    Entering Realm - By Order of His Majesty The Queen."

    Jacob rolled his eyes. He had always considered Alice in Wonderland a
    kid's fairy story. But now the words "Off with his head" echoed in
    his mind as his hand instinctively ran across this furry neck. Then he
    noticed the racks of clothing in the two corners of the room. On the
    left side hung clothing that looked conventionally human, and on the
    right were some outlandish brightly colored garments that didn't look
    like they'd drape right on a human. He glanced down at his own
    clothing with fur sticking out of the stretched spaces in his shirt
    between the buttons. He wasn't sure that he'd meet the dress code the
    way he was dressed, and this clothing and shoes were binding in all
    the wrong places. He began to unbutton his shirt. He was curious to
    see what he looked like since all his fur had sprouted too. Maybe
    there would be a mirror on the other side of the door.
