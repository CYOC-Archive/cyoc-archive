    When Becky recovered from the shock of the bolt that had hit her bare butt, she opened her eyes and quickly tried to determine what the latest blast of the wand had done to her. Rising to her feet she was immediately relieved to notice that she was still in human form, and a quick check of her naked body revealed that everything was still in place and as it should be. But something was definitely wrong, her perspective had changed significantly and the relatively small tent she had been standing in seemed to have vastly increased in size. The pile of Kayla's clothes she had been so close to before now seemed well out of reach but also much, much larger than before. A discarded coffee cup which had been placed on the floor nearby was now taller than she was. It was obvious what had happened.

    "Fuck! No way!" Becky began to panic as she processed just how much the stupid, malfunctioning wand had reduced her by. She could have been no more than four or five inches tall maximum. How was she going to get out of this? She looked across at the wand, still laying broken on the floor, reachable but impossible for her to pick up like before even if she could work out how to use it to fix this mess and not just randomly create chaos. Maybe this was just a 24 hour spell and the smartest thing for her would be to just get out of the wands range for now?

    As Becky pondered her options she began to notice a rumbling sound in the distance. It had been feint at first but was growing ever closer. Soon the source of the noise became apparent. A large, greenish brown creature had entered the tent from outside and was leaping urgently towards her. This was nothing more than a regular sized bullfrog, but at her current height it was larger than Becky and quite monstrous looking to her. She let out a nervous yelp and tried to move out of it's path but it very deliberately followed at a quicker pace than she could manage and stopped right in front of her.

    "Ribbit!" the frog bellowed angrily. Becky's eyes widened as a sudden realisation hit her.

    "James?" she asked nervously.

    "Ribbit!" James confirmed agitatedly "Ribbit! Ribbit!"

    "Well at least you didn't just disappear into thin air" she offered by way of consolation "Look, I'm sorry but this isn't completely my fault you know? And look at me! I'm the size of a mouse!"

    The moment the words left her lips another realisation hit Becky and her face fell again. Sure enough, she swivelled round to find a now very terrifying rodent which from her perspective appeared the size of a large dog was making it's way towards her. She really hoped that Kayla wasn't the violent type.
