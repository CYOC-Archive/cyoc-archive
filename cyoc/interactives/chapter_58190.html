    “I get to be my own Clown?!?” Caitlyn asked her mother excitedly.
    “Yes, forever too!” Alice told her daughter getting excited for her too.
    “Let’s do it now mommy,” Caitlyn told her mother while jumping up and down.
    “First we have to make a list honey, of all the clown features you want”
    “How come?”
    “Because the man said if we read the list as we apply the ointment you’ll end up just how
    you want!”
    “Okay! I’ll get the paper!”
    Alice smiled as her daughter raced from the room, but was quickly interrupted by her older
    son Mike. “You really gonna turn the shrimp into a clown?” Mike asked.
    Alice looked at her 17-year-old son and smiled. “This is what she wants. You’ve seen all
    the clown things she has collected over the years.”
    “Yeah, but its forever,” Mike stressed the last word, “It seems a little irresponsible.”
    “Hush. The magic will make her happy.”

    paper and pencil.
    “Mike! Mike! I’m gonna be a clown,” Caitlyn told him excitedly.
    “I thought you already were,” Mike teased back.

    “Okay my funny little girl let mommy put on her gloves while you take off your shoes.”

    “Oh boy!” Caitlyn shouted.
