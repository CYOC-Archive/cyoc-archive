    A Solution, Or A New Problem?

    Ronnie's pacing again, but this time it takes him longer to get from one end of the carpeted floor to another. It's still distracting beyond belief. You feel the beginnings of a tension headache coming on, and rise from your chair, deciding to try and distract yourself from the situation by inspecting yourself more closely.

    Now that you're paying attention, you can tell your forehead looks noticeably larger than before. Your hairline has apparently started receding, and a quick rub of the back of your head reveals that the hair there is somewhat thinner than the rest of the hair on your head.

    Without the baby fat softening your features, you look… stern. Your eyebrows are somewhat pinched, resting above a strong nose, and your eyes look much colder than you recall them being before. Even with the subtle roundness brought upon by age and weight, your jawline is still impressively sharp, your mouth thin and pressed into a tight, formidable line. If you were still 16, you'd be scared to approach yourself. You've grown into a hard, imposing man, despite your weight gain.

    You find yourself distracted by Ronnie's incessant pacing. It's insufferable, really. Your memories inform you that, in this reality, Ronnie is one of your accounting firm's interns. You'll readily admit that he's good at his job, but, well… your mouth pulls into a mean little sneer. If he put as many hours into his work as he puts into going to the gym, he'd have the potential to be-

    You shake your head abruptly. This isn't you! Ronnie's your friend, you remind yourself. Even if you'd only really known each other less than 24 hours ago - which is a real headache to contemplate - you're both in this together now. You know Ronnie's always been one to do rather than to think, so giving him something to occupy himself would be a nice reprieve for your steadily fraying nerves.

    "Ronnie," you say. It comes out gravelly, sharper than you'd intended it to be. Ronnie practically jumps, startled as he is. You make the conscious gentle your tone a little. "I've been thinking. It's quite obvious that the beans seem to follow some sort of uniform scheme - dark blue beans mean age, light blue beans mean youth."

    Ronnie's eyes light up. "Wait. So all we need to do is eat some light blue beans and we'll be okay!"

    You shake your head. "No, unfortunately. It's not that easy. It was an odd coincidence that we both underwent a transformation of the same amount of years. The first light blue bean I consumed only resulted in a reduction of 4 years."

    "Oh," Ronnie says. He looks dejected, like a kicked puppy. It's such a ridiculous look to see on a hulking, hairy man that you have to stifle a derisive snort.

    You sigh, realizing how easily you slid back into the thought patterns of this reality. Seems that the bodies you occupy have some influence on your personality. "Cheer up. Not all hope is lost - when I originally sampled these, there was a particular jelly bean that reversed all the changes and restored me to my former self. But," you hurry to add, when Ronnie instantly perks up, "I'm afraid I don't recall what color it was. That entire afternoon seems to be hazy in my memory."

    "So…" Ronnie looks at you. "We just need to eat every type of bean and figure out what they do. Right?"

    "Correct," you say. You eye the beans with some trepidation. It may be odd, but you're feeling more and more at home in your new body. You barely notice the dull ache in your lower back, and you're loathe to admit it, but you've grown perhaps the tiniest bit fond of the added weight around your middle. It wouldn't do to get too skinny. A businessman needs some measure of physical presence, after all. Maybe you'd simply pawn it off on the intern, much like you'd do with-

    "So, um," the intern says, startling you out of your thoughts. Wait. No. Ronnie, not the intern. He continues on, oblivious to your inner turmoil. "I guess I'll go first, then, since I'm partly to blame for…" his eyes rest on your prominent belly. "You know…"

    You bite back a surge of irritation. Of course he'd be judgmental of your weight. Little did he know that it was the result of working hard, neglecting the unimportant aspects of your life in your feverish attempt to reach the top- You pinch the bridge of your nose. "Fuck," you murmur, under your breath. Then louder, so Ronnie can hear, "The beans seem to be having a pronounced effect on me, perhaps because of the amount I consumed. I think it would be best if we both sampled one. Otherwise, I'm not sure how long I'll be able to contribute to this discussion civilly."

    "Oh. Oh, shit," Ronnie says eloquently, before rushing to the jelly beans and shoving his fist into the packet. He draws out a few, some falling to the floor in his haste, before practically running to your side. "Here you go!"

    The bean he hands you is a dark red, with dark stripes crisscrossing over it every so often. It reminds you a little of plaid. Horrendous fabric, really. Simple colors are by far superior, for all intents and- you shake your head. The beans. Focus on the beans.

    Ronnie's holding a white bean with little designs on it. When you squint, you can just barely make out little blue arrows, all pointing in different directions. "Plaid for me, blue arrows on white for you," you remind Ronnie, before quickly popping the bean into your mouth. Ronnie follows suit a moment later.

    It only takes a second for you to feel the telltale lurch of the beans beginning to work their magic.

    What do you do now?
