    "Oh, Kat, you're so lucky," gushed a voice Dan recognized as belonging
    to Kat's friend Emily. "To think you have your own milk cow. Those
    hot milk baths do wonder for the skin."

    Dan rolled his eyes, as he heard the girls chatting as they
    approached. From the snippets of conversation, he quickly surmised
    that they were coming down to milk him.

    "Well, here's Dan," Kat said opening the barn door.

    "Uh, you mean Danielle, right? Dan's a boy's name," said Ginger. She
    was constantly goading Kat, and considering Kat's new powers, Dan was
    surprised that she hadn't transformed Ginger.

    "Actually, I call it Dan to annoy my kid brother," Kat explained
    cheerily.

    "Oh, speaking of Danny, where is the cute boy," Marta asked.

    Kat was slightly caught off guards, but she answered, "Well, it is my
    birthday, and the little troll was having a cow, so he's not going to
    show his ugly little face around here today."

    "Oh, too bad," murmured Marta.

    "Say, speaking of having cows," Emily said trying to change the
    subject since she could see Kat was upset, "Do you plan to breed your
    cow?"

    Kat's face lit up, "What a splendid idea! In a few years, I could
    have my own dairy farm, I guess all I need is a bull."

    Marta wandered over and was petting Dan. She pulled a blue ribbon out
    of her hair and tied it to Dan's tail. She said softly, "I bet you'd
    like a big strong bull to make you a mother, Dan."

    "Yes!" said Kat with maniacal glee.

    "Oh, come on, if you want to watch animals hump, we can turn on the
    Discovery Channel or Animal Planet or PBS," said Ginger, "It smells in
    here. Let's go back to the house."

    "Well, I did want you all to see our new family cow. Here's a bucket,
    Ginger, why don't you go first!"

    "Go first?"

    "Yes, we're going to have a time milking contest. We'll each take 30
    second turns milking the cow. The one who gets the most milk in that
    time wins."

    "You're kidding?"

    "No, and you're first," Kat said opening the half gate to the stall
    and sliding a small stool next to Dan.

    Ginger sat down her cold icy fingernails dug into Dan's udder and he
    lowed in pain. She moved her fingers and managed to get 3-4 squirts
    in her bucket before Kat yelled time. Each girl was given a plastic
    bucket and each collected several ounces of creamy milk from Dan's
    udder. He was horrified as the girl's squeezed the "cow juice" out of
    him. Marta had the warmest gentlest hands and see managed to nearly
    fill her little bucket in the time alloted. Marta was declared the
    winner, and the girls carried their buckets of Dan's milk up to the
    house, where they'd whip it into whip cream- Ginger corrected Kat
    again, "You mean creme fresh!"

    While the girls were hard at work with their whisks, the door bell
    rang. Kat raced to the door thinking it must be a special gift. She
    looked up into the school's star receiver's eyes. She licked her
    lips, he was a senior, and she didn't think he even knew she was
    alivve.

    "Hello, Kurt, thank you for coming to my party," Kat gushed.

    "Your party? Oh, I'm sorry, I didn't mean to interrupt. I'm here to
    see your brother Dan, he's tutoring me in math," he said with a silly
    grin, which said yeah, a freshman is tutoring a senior in math.

    "Well, Dan's not here right now. Perhaps you'd like to join my party?"

    "Oh, thanks, but no thanks. Dan told me to be here now so I'm sure
    he'll be back soon. I'll just sit here on the front step and wait."

    "Really, Kurt, you could at least wish me a happy birthday," Kat said
    in disgust.

    "Sure, happy birthday, uh, Dan's sister," Kurt said. He didn't know
    her name.

    Kat rolled her eyes. Then she smiled as an evil thought crossed her
    mind. She noticed the bulge in Kurt's jeans. He was sort of built
    like a bull, and she did want to teach Dan a lesson. She was far from
    done toying with Dan.

    "Wait, Kurt, what am I thinking, Dan won't be coming back here. He's
    down at our barn. Just go around the house and follow the path to the
    barn. You should find him in the stall down there," Kat said pointing
    the way.

    "You could've said that right away," complained Kurt picking up his
    back pack and heading down the path by the side of the house.

    He didn't see Kat raise her hand and make an odd gesture at his back.
