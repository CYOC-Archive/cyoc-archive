    Tad was grateful that his afternoon went quietly. Some of the rumors that went on about this supposed fight between a teacher and a wereraccoon from the previous day was, thankfully, the "biggest" thing that seemed to attract a lot of attention. Tad understood it related issues that weres didn't like, particularly when a were went rogue, but for the moment that seemed to be rather small in the grand scheme of things. This issue would blow over. If either the teacher or the wereraccoon had broken the law, they'd likely see trial and end up behind bars. That was the way of things and they would surely blow over once that happened.

    The biggest thing that Tad was concerned with was with regard to Dymixious and the likely investigation that could very well draw attention to the existence of weres to the world. Revealing the secret might be something that would have to come sooner or later, and Tad had put in his Government class's assignments his ideas to let that go easily and successfully, but things wouldn't go well if the revelation of weres came as the result of either a rogue were or a confrontation between weres and police where the secret couldn't be kept. It was why the arrest of Lionel Ulysses had been such a big thing when it first happened and why Tad got the sensation that Dymixious's over-sized ego wouldn't make it easy for the secret to be kept so long as the police were investigating his involvement in Ulysses' murder. That would be especially true if the police agencies hunting Dymixious were outside Moon Lake agencies. So far, there had been no sign of further problems from him. In fact, neither Tad nor Amy noticed any sign of him as they walked home from school.

    "Well... today proved to be good and quiet," Tad commented as they walked along.

    "And a good thing too," Amy agreed, "between all the other stuff going on... more excitement was not something that could be wanted or needed at the moment."

    Tad nodded as they walked along, crossing a street to get onto the street that ran out toward where the Decker and Howler houses were located. The two shared some smiles, and Tad let the fact that the day had been generally quiet to offer a question that came to his mind. It might be the fact that he was with his girlfriend who was beautiful, but Tad figured that was something everyone did when they were with the people they loved.

    "So... do you want to do something together this afternoon? Homework or something like that?" Tad asked.

    "I'd love to do something," Amy smiled and then sighed, "but my folks want me to help with some things that we'll be doing to get things ready for when Jay and Leon transform. So, I can't do anything this afternoon."

    Tad sighed and looked down for a moment. Amy caught onto this and placed a hand on Tad's shoulder as they walked along.

    "However, by this evening," Amy then spoke, "I'd be free..."

    "You would?" Tad looked up.

    "Yes," Amy smiled, "by then, we'd be through with dinner and we could go out for a nice fury walk in the woods... for some other sorts of fun."

    That REALLY peaked Tad's interest and he couldn't help but give a fairly goofy grin at that, which made Amy laugh.

    "I take it you're interested, then?" Amy gave a laugh, to which Tad nodded.

    By this time, they'd arrived at the sidewalk that lead directly up to the front door of his home. Tad nodded and moved to head up to his home.

    "See you tonight!" Tad called to her.

    "Right," Amy smiled and moved on toward her own home.

    Tad walked into his home and found his mom quietly reading in he living room.

    "Hey, mom, did you have a good day?" Tad asked to.

    Jen looked up from the couch and nodded, "Sure. It went well. I could have wished there were some answers to my job applications... but they may only be looking through them at the moment. It may take time for them to actually make a decision on who they wish to interview."

    "Well, I'm sure you'll do well, Mom," Tad said to her, "I'm going to work on my homework, you'll let me know when dinner is ready?"

    Jen Decker nodded.

    "So, how's Amy doing?" Jen asked, "you both seemed a little nervous yesterday..."

    "Oh?" Jen raised an eyebrow.

    "A furry walk," Tad gave a small smile.
