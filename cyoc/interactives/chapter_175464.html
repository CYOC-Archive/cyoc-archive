    She pulled out the app again while Henrietta recalled her childhood to a less than interested Will. The sentence that generated was "A 40 year old woman tells a story about the past to a college student."

    Jenna silently chuckled to herself. She knew that it was probably safer to start changing her friends back, but the thought of seeing a younger Will was too cute to pass up. "Well... maybe just one more change." She thought.

    She decided to change his age by changing his type of school. She edited "college" replacing it with "Preschool" student and waited to notice the change, but nothing happened.

    "Huh, why no tange?" Jenna mumbled. "It work before, why not now..." She whined, unaware that the "college student" referred to in the last sentence was herself and not Will. Now sitting where the college student named Jenna had been was a tiny preschool girl in a high chair.

    "What was that Jenny?" Will asked nicely, looking down at the little girl.

    "Notin." She lied badly.

    "Hmm hmm." Will chuckled. "Can I have my phone back now?" He asked.

    "No!" She yelled, not wanting to give up the fun of the magical app.

    "Jenny!" Kylie scolded. "Give my friend his phone back.... you don't want me to tell Mom later that you were acting up?" She threatened.

    "No no!" She whined. She wanted to keep changing things, but it was still hard for her since she couldn't read. "I wiked you better when you was a boy." Jenny scoffed as she handed the phone over too Will.

    "Ha, what?" Henrietta snickered. "You were a boy huh?" She asked Kylie jokingly.

    "Ugh, obviously not. My sister is just an imaginative brat." She sneered. Jenny stuck her tongue out, and Kylie countered with her own tongue.

    Meanwhile however, Will was really weirded out. When he opened his phone again, it looked like it had been opened to some strange text app. There was no way Kylie's little sister had been using this since she was in preschool, but he didn't remember ever downloading it at all. What was weirder was the description, as it mentioned that the app could supposedly alter the world itself. He clicked the building where all the icons were and suddenly a single sentence popped up.

    "This restaurant is for everyone."

    "What the hell?" Will thought. "How'd it know this was a restaurant?"

    He then thought back to what Jenny had said, glancing up to see the toddler looking upset as she scribbled all over her kid's menu with a crayon. Could she had been telling the truth about Kylie being a boy? Will had serious doubts, but part of him was curious. He quickly deleted the word everyone from the sentence and replaced it with "big titty goth gfs" as a joke, but then an error popped up/

    "ERROR. One word can be changed only to another single word. For more changes and more words, pay for the full version, only 2,000,000 dollars!"

    "What the hell... is this thing for real?" Will wondered, now more curious than ever.

    The sentence reversed back to the original, and Will decided to try it out. After all, what's the worst that could happen? If it didn't work, whatever, but if it did, then he could always change it back right?
