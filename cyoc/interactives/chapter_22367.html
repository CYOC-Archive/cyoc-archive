Diane was told exactly what to say, and was then given the salad and the wine glasses. With care, Diane took the first course through the door into the dining room. She said the salad is particularly good, made with a special dressing that is simply delightful." Diane waited, as instructed, until the three started to serve themselves some salad. She then dashed into the kitchen, fear evident on her face, tumbling into Mary. Just before Mary fell, she noticed that the wine glasses were not arranged as they should have been. Problem was, she didn't see who had gotten which glass.

Her feet had changed, causing them to break out of her shoes. Mary now understood the fear on Diane's face. The changes were freaking her out. Mary cautioned Diane to keep quiet, and climbed to her feet. The sound of lettuce crunching made Mary creep to the door, and when she peeked out, the wine glasses were hidden from view because of Jim's back. All three were well into their salads, eating more quickly by the moment. Jim mumbled around his food that he was feeling voracious, and his parents mumbled agreement.
