    "Hello everyone and welcome to a new episode of Animalia Ambassadoria." said Sarah, starting the second episode of the Animalia ComicCon coverage.

    "We're continuing from part one of our coverage of Animalia ComicCon, as well as covering some new things that happened during this time." said Malice.

    ---

    Jen-Jen was interviewing another member of Joanne's staff when another fit of shouting broke out a few floors up, shortly followed by another "I am never speaking to you! I can't!"

    "So what's the finer details of that?" asked Jen-Jen, referring to the shouting.

    "So, one of our servants chose to be a mare Animalian," said the staff member, "and the simple fact that her coat came in strawberry red is causing her daughter to have a hissy fit about it because of what it resembles."

    "Right..." said Jen-Jen, not saying much else."

    ---

    "Not everyone took annoyance of the travel band during the pandemic." said Abby, leading the camera over to the GGAC's booth. "The Japanese agents that were interested in our turtles not only didn't mind it too much, but were able to fine tune their ideas."

    Then the segment followed an interview with Jerry and a Japanese representative which helped explain what they had down so far. While it was basically similar to The Matrix in some ways, there were some differences, like the primary antagonist being something similar to the Illuminati from Deus Ex, but with some supernatural elements mixed in as well, such as a dryad turned into a monster because of toxic chemicals leached into her main tree. But there was one surprising thing that made some people roll their eyes.

    "So what are you doing for our ComicCon?" asked Abby.

    "It's no secret that, in some martial arts graphic novels, the martial artists are capable of energy blasting with Chi and Ki." said Jerry. "We've been working on a display on how certain effects in movies are achieved. In one scene/episode (since we haven't decided if its going to be a tv show or full on movie yet), our characters travel to the astral plane to begin training their Ki powers. And due to the fact that we've had an influx of upper classes seeking to join Animalia, we've now got a stock of tacky collectibles that even Ripley's won't accept. So we've been blowing them up safely using small amounts of plastic explosive on a bombing range. One representative happened to see them doing that and decided to add it to what we've been working on."

    ---

    "In news from Heiya Island," said Johan Eecks, acting like a somewhat realistic newsroom reporter, "Another human trafficking ring has been uncovered by the Asian Authorities and Interpol and taken down, only this time these were specializing in male Animalians. But whether they are 'leftovers' from the original ring or an entirely new one is still unknown. Also, because of the focus on the Island, Interpol decided to build an office on Heiya island while matching the overall theme of the island." At that there was a scene from The Simpsons where the character says, "Of course! Genius! Why didn't I think of that?!"

    ---

    "That's all we have for part two of Animalia Ambassadoria's coverage of Animalia ComicCon." said Sarah, after some more news and funny clips. "See you all next time in the Wilds of Animalia."
