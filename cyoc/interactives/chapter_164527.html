    A message from Will, her boyfriend

    Her pink cased smartphone was glowing with several messages and notices. Most were her standard Insta-likes or some of her friends sharing snaps of Jessica on the runway, but one really peaked her interest. It was a text from her boyfriend Will! She smiled, always happy to hear from him. She had hoped it was from her performance just now.

    "I wish I didn't zone out back there... then maybe I could have looked for him." She sighed to herself as she opened the message.

    To her disappointment, it was just another excuse. Apparently, he was running a bit late to the mall and told her he'd see her later performances. She rolled her eyes. She did love him, but he could be SO immature. "Though I suppose it is my fault. Here I am, 28 years old and dating a college student." She thought.

    She sometimes just wished he could be a little more interested in what she enjoyed doing as well, or at least came to understand that with an older significant other came with it an assumption of maturity. She closed the messages app with a pout and was about to shut her phone off when she spotted an odd looking app.

    Normally she didn't care, but something in the back of her head told her to open it. She clicked on it and up came the familiar website!
    "Oh my goodness!" She thought. "I completely forgot about this reality changing app... somehow?" She tried to think on the details but it all came back fuzzy.

    "I really have to start eating more before shows." She shrugged it off, pulling it up once more.

    It scanned the environment and gave her ample options of people to select. She wondered if she really had time for this right now, but her doubt vanished upon seeing a name in the high end movie theatre next door. It was Will's name! She selected him rapidly and dropped her jaw at the sentence.

    "Will is delaying going to his girlfriends's runway performance."

    Jessica couldn't believe it, the immaturity! The absolute gaul! "That's it, I'm gonna make sure he learns from this." She angrily muttered to herself, reselecting a new sentence.
    "The man watches a movie, and will soon go to the fashion mall."

    Jessica changed.
