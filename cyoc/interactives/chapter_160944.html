    Buddy hams it up for the camera

    The dog park in town was a happening place that morning. Since at least 7 AM, the commercial crew worked tirelessly to set up for the day’s filming. Town residents gawked from the street, as tents were erected, trailers were parked and equipment was brought into the park. The commotion brought police officers to the set, to keep the giggling gaggle of gawkers away.

    “Keep away from the set” the officers would say to anyone who tried to get too close.

    As the film crew continued to set up shop, the production van came and parked nearby. Getting out of the van was the PA and Buddy.

    “Come on, boy” the PA said, as he grabbed Buddy’s leash. “Let’s get you signed in and introduced to the crew and the director.”

    “I have to admit” Freddy thought to himself, as Buddy hopped out of the van and followed the PA onto the set. "This is pretty cool."

    “Ah!” an onlooker exclaimed.

    “That’s such a cool dog!” a little boy commented.

    Buddy happily panted, as the PA walked him to where the crew was working.

    “I’m a little excited about this, actually” Freddy admitted to himself. “I’m also really nervous, for a lot of reasons. I really hope I don’t change back before this is over. I hope they don’t make me do anything too embarrassing. I also hope this helps Josh with getting back on his feet. Speaking of which, where is my brother anyway?”

    Just as Freddy pondered this, Josh came running up towards them. He was in an ecstatic mood, seeing as the town gave the production company the required tax credits to set up shop for the day. Not only that, but the mayor generously gave the production extra money than the minimum. Josh’s producer friend was thrilled, considering shooting more commercials here if the town kept up with its generosity.

    Josh was also happy with his choice of cast. Evan was a good kid and Buddy looked like the ideal dog for the shoot. Spotting the PA walking with Buddy on his leash, Josh ran to bend down and scratch Buddy behind the ears.

    “Aren’t you a good boy” he said, before getting back up. “You’re going to be in a for an action packed day today, Buddy. Hey Gary. Where’s that guy, Tom? Buddy’s owner?”

    “He asked me to check on something first” Gary, the PA, replied.

    “Oh? What’s that?”

    “He has another dog he wants to bring to set today, but she’s in heat.”

    “Oh. Wow. Well, hmmm. I don’t know.”

    “I was going to go ask the director first, before I brought them to set.”

    “Was Tom in the van with you?”

    “He refused to go unless we figured out what to do with his other dog.”

    Josh slapped his forehead and groaned loudly.

    “Man, shit. He needed to be here to sign in. The director is not going to be happy to hear about this.”

    “What am I not going to be happy to hear about?” the director asked, as he quickly approached them.

    “Oh, hi boss” Gary replied. “I managed to bring Buddy to set.”

    “He’s a perfect dog. Just what consumers are going to want to see. It’s amazing you could find such a perfect specimen of the breed like this, Josh, and on such short notice, too. Where’s his owner, Gary?”

    “That’s what I wanted to talk to you about, boss. I went to go pick up Buddy’s owner Tom at his house and he has another dog he wanted to bring to set.”

    “What kind of dog?”

    “A female Shepherd like Buddy, similar coating and all.”

    “Then what’s the problem? In case something happens to our boy here, it’s good to have a backup just in case. Is it over money?”

    “No, boss. The bitch is in heat.”

    “Oh.”

    Josh fidgeted nervously, as the director thought about what to do.

    “I’ll tell you what” the director said. “Bring them to set and we’ll see how things go. Worst case scenario, we keep the other dog in the trailer, so she’s not getting all the other dogs in the neighborhood running over here to greet her.”

    “Ok sir” Gary replied. “Let me get the production van to go pick up Tom. Josh, do you mind watching Buddy for a few minutes until Tom gets here?”

    “Uh, sure” Josh replied, as Gary handed him Buddy’s leash.

    “Oh great” Freddy thought to himself, as Gary ran back to the production van. “Now my own brother is dog sitting me. This day is getting off to a great start so far.”

    “Come on, Buddy” Josh said, tugging on the dog’s leash. “Let’s get you something to eat.”

    “Hmmm. I wonder what Josh has in mind for me. I could use a real meal.”

    Buddy sniffed the air, noticing something tasty off in the distance. As Josh led Buddy to the craft services table, where breakfast was being served, Michael approached a production assistant in his car, who was blocking off the street where the shoot was being filmed.

    “Hi” Michael said. “My son is here to star in the commercial today.”

    “A casting confirmation, sir?” the PA asked.

    Michael pulled out his communication device and showed the PA Evan’s booking info.

    “Gotcha” the PA replied in the affirmative. “We have a spot for you just a few cars down.”

    “We’re not all coming onto set today” Sofya said, as her eyes turned pink. “It’s just my friend Michael, his son Evan and his mother. I’m taking the car back to the house when they’ve been dropped out.”

    “Sure thing. Want to just let them out now?”

    “Not just yet. I want to make sure that the director okays the arrangement. Sound good to you?”

    Sofya let out a seductive wink, as the PA began to drool.

    “Yuh, yuh, yeah, sure” the PA stuttered out, as Michael drove and parked the car in the designated spot.

    When they were settled in, the four of them got out of the car and walked over to set. Their goal was to find Buddy and whoever else was in charge of making the final decisions. A Production assistant ran up to them to try to move them away from the set.

    “Cast and chaperone only” a female PA said to them.

    “The director agreed to two chaperones today” Sofya said seductively.

    “I don’t think he did.”

    “Oh, Michael” his mother said. “I can just—“

    “No!” Michael snapped. “Stay right here.”

    “Mind bringing the director over?” Sofya asked. “I’m sure he’ll understand.”

    “I don’t know” the PA replied reluctantly. “My boss is very busy today and I don’t think he’s going to want to be interrupted.”

    “Hey guys!” Josh called out.

    Josh ran up to them, with Buddy on his leash.

    “There he is” Michael thought to himself, as they approached them.

    “Michael!” Freddy excitedly thought to himself, as Buddy ran up to him, barking as he did so.

    “Easy boy!” Josh said, as Buddy hopped up onto Michael and licked his face. “Get down from there! Bad boy!”

    “That dog really seems to like you” Michael’s mother noted, before turning to look at Josh. “Hi Josh. I haven’t seen you in…when’s that last time I saw you, anyway? You weren’t at the wedding, so when did we last saw each other?”

    “Wow” Josh replied, as he went to hug Michael’s mother. “You actually recognized me. I didn’t think I looked anything like I did all those years ago.”

    “You have that Benson look, like your brother Freddy. Speaking of which, where is he? The news said he disappeared after that incident at the auditorium and hasn’t been seen or heard from since.”

    “Really? Michael said he went out for a jog yesterday.”

    “Uh” Michael replied nervously.

    “He’s out on business” Sofya replied. “He’ll be back later today or tomorrow at the latest.”

    “This close to the election?” Josh asked. “That’s not good. If Freddy wants to win this thing, he needs to be in town campaigning in the final days of the election. He can’t just go running off like that.”

    “Especially since a lot of people thought he was dead” Michael’s mother added.

    “Oh great” Freddy thought to himself, as Buddy whimpered and laid down in the grass. “I totally forgot about that. My chance of winning this race is totally shot now. Of course, there’s probably more important things to be worried about right now, like changing myself back again and getting out of being Tom’s pet and Lady’s lover forever. Plus, we still have to get the ring back from the mayor.”

    “Things will be fine” Sofya said assuringly. “In the meantime, Josh, where is the director?”

    “Right here” the director replied, as he approached the assembled group.

    “Oh good, I’ve been looking for you.”

    Sofya gazed into the director’s eyes, flashing a pink light from her own right at him.

    “I know normally the child actor is only allowed one chaperone to accompany him on set” she continued. “However, I was hoping you would make an exception for today.”

    “What kind of exception?” the director asked, smiling brightly as he spoke.

    The PA stared at her boss, confused by Sofya’s ability to lower his inhibitions like that.

    “Evan would like to have his dad and his grandmother with him on the set today. I’m sure it shouldn’t a problem, right?”

    “Not at all. They can both stay.”

    “Great.”

    Sofya’s eyes returned to normal, as she walked back to Michael’s car.

    “I’ll catch you guys later” Sofya said, as she headed off the set to go back to Michael's car.

    "Need the keys?" Michael asked, as he tossed them at Sofya.

    "I do, thank you" Sofya replied, as she caught them, before heading off set to drive herself home.

    “Um boss?” the PA asked. “Are you alright?”

    “Yeah, yeah” the director replied, motioning Michael, his son and his mother, along with Josh and Buddy, towards him. “Come with me. I want to talk to you all about how today is going to go.”

    The group followed the director, as they went to a more private area of the set to talk.

    “So” the director continued “I want to start by asking if the two stars today have been properly introduced.”

    “They have not” Josh replied, as he commanded Buddy to sit.

    “Ok. You’re Evan, right?”

    “Yes sir” Evan replied.

    “I’m going to be the director on this commercial today. Nice to meet you, young man.”

    Evan shook the director’s hand, before he was instructed to look at Buddy.

    “This is the dog you’re going to be working with today” the director continued. “His name is Buddy.”

    “Hi Buddy” Evan replied, as he rubbed the dog’s back. “Nice to meet ya, boy.”

    “Boy” Freddy thought to himself, as he let out a whimper. “You’re my son. How do you not recognize me?”

    “He seems a little nervous.”

    “I’m sure he’ll warm up in due time” the director replied. “At least, I hope he does. A lot is riding on this commercial.”

    “Of course” Freddy thought to himself. “Duh. He can’t recognize me. I’m not his papa to all these people. I’m just a dog. As far as they all know. we’ve never met before.”

    “Speaking of Buddy, where is his owner?”

    “Sir!” Gary exclaimed, as he ran up to him, with Tom and Lady by his side.

    “Uh oh” Michael thought to himself, as he got a look at the German Shepherd bitch at Tom’s side. “She looks like she’s in heat. That’s not good.”

    “Ah!” the director exclaimed, as he went to shake Tom’s hand. “You must be Tom, Buddy’s owner.”

    “Yes” Tom replied shortly.

    “Nice to meet you. I’m the director for the shoot today.”

    “So I’ve heard.”

    “Have you been signed in yet?”

    “They signed me in on the way over here.”

    “Good, good. And who is this other dog here with you?”

    Lady panted, as the director scratched her behind the ears.

    “This is my other dog, Lady. I hesitated to bring her today, because she’s still in heat.”

    “Oh god” Michael thought to himself, as he noticed Buddy panting and looking in her direction. “He better not have done it.”

    “Well” the director replied “let’s keep her away from the set for a while, unless we absolutely need her. Good to have a backup just in case. Did you get something to eat?”

    “I did not” Tom replied.

    “Well, breakfast is being served now. Gary will show you the way.”

    “Ok” Gary replied. “Tom, let’s get Lady stashed away for a while and then I’ll get you some breakfast.”

    “Sounds good” Tom replied, as he took Lady to follow Gary.

    “So” the director continued to the remaining people and Buddy. “Did you get signed in yet?”

    “I’ll do that now” Josh replied, as he pulled out his communication device.

    “After you’re done with that, you guys can get some breakfast and then I’ll give you a rundown on what we’re doing today. Sound good?”

    “Yup” Evan replied. “I sure am hungry.”

    “Didn't you eat breakfast?” Michael asked.

    “Evan could always use more food.” his grandmother replied.

    “Yeah, I just had some toast this morning. They look like they have a whole breakfast table over there, dad. Eggs, bacon, waffles. The works.”

    “Yes” the director replied. “All that and then some. We sure do like to eat around here.”

    “Awesome!”

    Buddy barked, leaving Josh's side, as he ran over to Evan.

    “The dog really seems to like you” Josh noted, as Buddy nudged his head into his son’s leg.

    “He seems like a good boy” Evan replied, as he rubbed Buddy’s back. “Just the kind of dog I’d like to have, if my dad wasn’t so opposed to them.”

    “Let’s go get you breakfast” Michael said, trying to change the subject.

    “Sign this first” Josh replied, handing Michael a stylus.

    Michael signed all of the digital documents, while Evan continued to play with Buddy.

    “What kind of tricks does he do?” Evan asked, as he stood up to face the director.

    “I don’t know” the director replied. “We’ll go talk to his owner, Tom, before we start shooting.”

    “We’re ready for you guys to eat breakfast” Josh said, as Michael finished signing the documents. “I’ll take you guys over there now.”

    “Great. You have a 15 minute break for breakfast. Then, we’ll meet over by the gate to the dog pen, after hair, makeup and wardrobe have seen you first, Evan.”

    “Ok. Come on guys, I’ll show you the way to breakfast.”

    Josh led Michael, his mother, Evan and Buddy over to the breakfast area. Seeing that Tom wasn’t around, Evan grabbed Buddy by the leash and walked him over with them. When they got there, they could see a ton of crew members, from PA’s, to grips, to the camera operators, to the makeup people, getting breakfast and chatting with one another. Evan’s mouth watered at seeing all of the food on the tables.

    “Wow!” Evan said, as he took in the sight and smells.

    “Now this is a meal” Freddy thought to himself, as Buddy licked his snout and happily panted. “Maybe they’ll let me have some of this.”

    In that moment, Tom saw Evan holding Buddy by the leash.

    “Hey!” he yelled, as he ran over to Evan and snatched the leash from his hand.

    “What’s the big idea, buster?” Evan replied.

    Buddy growled at Tom, who ignored it and sternly looked down at Evan.

    “Don’t take my dog!” Tom scolded.

    “I just was keeping an eye on him, so he didn’t run off. You don’t have to be such an ass—“

    “Evan” Michael hurriedly said, as he dragged him away from Tom and Buddy. “Let’s get you something to eat.”

    “Oh hi, Michael” Tom said.

    “Hi Tom.”

    “Ugh!” Evan exclaimed, as he grabbed two plates and began to serve himself food.

    “Fancy seeing you here” Tom said to Michael.

    “Yup” Michael replied coldly.

    “You’re not going to try to steal my dog here today, are you?”

    “Of course not, because I’ve never stolen your dog. Haven’t we gone over this already?”

    “I know, I know. I’m sorry. I’m just paranoid about something happening to Buddy, that’s all.”

    “Well, then you should have been nicer to my son, Evan. He was just trying to keep your dog from running off just now.”

    “Oops.”

    “Wow, remorse. Who would have thought you were capable of it?”

    This comment flew over Tom’s head, as Michael got himself a plate and served himself breakfast. Buddy growled at Tom, who looked down at his curiously.

    “You don’t like him, do you boy?” he said.

    “No” Freddy thought to himself, as Buddy shook his head.

    “Woah!”

    Tom jumped for a moment, which caught the attention of the assembled crew members.

    “Everything ok?” a key grip asked.

    “Uh yeah. I’m fine. I just got a little startled for a second.”

    The crew resumed eating their breakfast, as Tom looked down at his dog curiously.

    “Huh. I wonder if Buddy is smarter than he looks.”

    “Oh shit” Freddy thought to himself, as Tom dragged Buddy to get them something to eat. “I totally gave myself away just now. I hope Tom isn’t wise to the fact that I’m not really his dog.”

    When Michael, his mother and his son got themselves breakfast, Josh led them to a trailer on the other end of the set.

    “A trailer?!” Evan exclaimed, as Josh unlocked the door, which had Evan's name on it.

    “Yup” Josh replied. “Your own personal trailer.”

    “Awesome!”

    Josh led the three of them into the trailer. Inside, there was a couch, a makeup chair and a rack of clothes. Some snacks were laid out on a table, things that Evan enjoyed.

    “Cookies!” Evan exclaimed, as he put down his plates full of food. “Chocolate! Candy! Chips! This is the best Halloween ever and I didn’t even have to put on a costume to get it!!!!”

    “Thank you uncle Josh! You’re the best!”

    “Thank you, Evan” Josh replied. “You guys really helped me out in a pinch. It’s the least I could do to repay you. I have some other surprises for you today, as well.”

    “I’m looking forward to this” Michael’s mother said. “The anticipation is killing me.”

Right next door, Tom was being taken to the adjoining trailer with Buddy. Buddy looked up to see his name on the door.

    “Sure thing” Gary replied, as the other PA left the trailer.

Tom ate a full plate of food in his trailer, occasionally feeding Buddy and Lady some bacon. Both of the dogs barked happily in appreciation, as they devoured the pieces that Tom gave to them.

Over the course of a few minutes, Evan stepped out into the three outfits, in the order the wardrobe assistant instructed him to wear them. She wasn’t particularly impressed with any of them, until she had him make another change.

Evan was having so much fun. Not only was he able to play with his papa as a dog, which he had wanted to do since Freddy first transformed in his office months before, Evan was getting paid to do it. What more could he possibly ask for?

At first, he was a little timid with the actions, but the more he did them, the more Freddy lost himself in his canine instincts. He didn’t want to admit it, but he was having fun playing with his son. It was, hopefully, a once in a lifetime opportunity for them to bond and for Freddy to spend quality time with his son.

Tom was away in his trailer and the cameras were getting their necessary shots. However, at a certain point, he’d need to get Freddy out of there, before the shoot wrapped for the day. He was glad Tom was kept in his trailer, as it would be easy to sneak Freddy out without him noticing. Michael was just waiting for the right opportunity to pull off the dognapping heist that was required to rescue his husband.

    What did bother him, though, was the female dog in heat.

    “My instincts were right about that” Michael thought to himself. “I don’t know how Tom even got the money to get another dog, but he had one, as I suspected. I really hope Freddy resisted the temptation and didn’t breed her.”

Michael didn’t want to leave Freddy unattended like that, but he didn’t want to disobey the director’s orders either. Sighing, he made his way into Evan’s trailer, as he watched Gary pull Tom out of the other one.
