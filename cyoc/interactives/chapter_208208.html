    Their plan was to make Beth marry a Pig. Not a transformed Citizen, but an actual Animal.
    It was hard for them to broach the subject of marriage to the Queen, but after almost three weeks, they finally got her to consider it.
    She was presented with paintings of her potential partners, who she was made to assume were transformed of Nobles,
    but that actually all depicted the faces of ordinary Pigs. Beth, unaware of this fact, deliberated a long time before choosing the face she felt most attracted to, something the Nobles took great statisfaction in.
    Everything was going smoothly and the Wedding was quickly planned, which Beth believed was due to the Nobles trying to please her.
    They planned out every single detail, from Beth's dress to the venue, and it took less than a month for everything to be prepared.
    What they paid the most attention to during that time, aside from ensuring the Queen stayed in the dark,
    was that the marriage would actually be binding, and not just from a legal standpoint.
    The Contract was magically enforced and stated that her Husband would marry into the royal family,
    with no chance at succession or political power, have no other partners, and made divorce almost impossible,
    while all Beth had to do was give birth to a successor that could take over his title.
    It took them a lot of time to get Beth to agree to the contract, but she ultimately did, as it seemed to heavily favor her.

    The day of the Wedding arrived, and Queen Beth was walking down the aisle towards the Altar.
    Her Dress was glorious, making even the massive Sow Woman seem graceful.
    Beth's Face was hidden behind a thick Veil, that she had trouble seeing through, something she only put up with because of how magnificent the Dress that had been prepared for her was.
    She could vaguely make out her future Husband's Face after she had stepped up to the Altar, but even that was hard to see.
    The Priest's voice resounded throughout the Church, and it wasn't long until it was time for the exchange of Rings.
    Beth could not make out who the Hands that put the Ring onto her Finger belonged to, but she assumed them to be her Husband's.
    After that the Priest only spoke a few more words, before it was time for the Kiss.
    Beth leaned in with her Eyes closed, while her Veil was lifted by the enchantment on it.
    Her Lips made contact with his, and she felt his Tongue explore her Mouth with such passion, that she could not help but deepen the Kiss.
    It was only when the Kiss ended, that she opened her Eyes, allowing her to see the Man she had married.
    She was confused for a moment when she noticed the elevated Platform below him, until she looked past his Face and noticed his Body.
