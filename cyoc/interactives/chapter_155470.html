    Erin's new client was an orc. Or, more specifically, an orcess—a seven-foot-tall bombshell of a female orc, with a killer figure practically poured into a painted-on red dress that clung to every taut muscle and feminine curve. Her high-heeled shoes were red as well and *clacked* on the office's tile floors. She wore a wide-brimmed hat with a black veil hanging down in front of it, but even through the veil John could see the orc-woman's piercing red-and-yellow eyes, cute upturned nose, and two fetching little tusks jutting up from inside her pouty (and red-lipsticked) lower lip.

    "Hello," said the orcess in a soft and sultry voice. "I'm looking for a Ms. Erin Cooper; do I have the right office?"

    Erin trotted out from around her desk and said, "That's me. How can I help you?"

    The orc spared a glance at John, who still had his feet up on the desk, but was now watching this drama unfold like a bad daytime TV serial. Then she said to Erin, "My name is Bonecrusher — Mrs. Tonya Bonecrusher. I have a problem that I think you can help me with. What are your rates?"

    Erin quoted the woman a dollar-figure per day, with the private eye's usual clause: "…plus expenses incurred."

    Mrs. Bonecrusher nodded. "That seems acceptable. Well then, I'd better tell you my story. You see, an item of great sentimental value has been stolen. For reasons I'd rather not get into, I cannot simply go to the police; and bringing the insurance companies in on this matter would make things far too unpleasant and complicated. Therefore—"

    "I understand," said Erin, "and I can assure you that Arrow Investigations keeps all cases confidential, and operates with total discretion."

    The orc sniffed the air, glared at John again, and said, "Discretion, you say? It doesn't take an orc's sense of smell to tell that you're banging your secretary. That doesn't seem very discreet."

    John practically fell backwards in his chair.

    "Mr. Robertson isn't my secretary, he's my partner," said Erin coldly, "and what we do when we're off the clock is of no concern to any of my clients. I'm sure you understand."

    "…Of course," answered Mrs. Bonecrusher with just a hint of condescension in her voice. "To business, then. It would appear that I have lost track of my—
