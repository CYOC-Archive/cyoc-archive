    Still carrying the two drinks (I didn't intend
    to be delayed for long), I followed Doug to
    a quiet room upstairs. It was a spare
    bedroom. Why he had chosen this, I
    couldn't imagine. He always was one for
    elaborate ritual. I just hoped to get this
    over with soon.

    The music was chest-thumpingly loud, so
    I didn't object when Doug shut the door
    behind us. After all of this, I definitely
    wanted to hear what he had to say, if only
    for the laughs I would get out of it later.

    "So what is it?"

    "Well," he said, "I guess you know I
    always thought that you somehow tricked
    Danny to leave me," he started.

    I rolled my eyes. "You know we didn't even
    MEET until a month after you broke up."

    "Yeah, whatever. Anyway, I guess I always
    wondered what you had that I don't, you
    know?"

    "No," I said through gritted teeth. Where
    was this going?

    "I mean, you are taller than I am, true, but
    you're not any better looking."

    I was so irritated that I didn't see him take
    out his handheld computer. It was just
    suddenly in his hands, and he was
    fiddling with it.

    "This better be going somewhere."

    "It is. It's going somewhere really fast.
    Because you aren't any better looking
    than I am. You're not smarter. And now
    you're not even taller."

    This last corresponded with a great lurch
    in my body. Despite myself, I sloshed
    both of the drinks I was holding out onto
    the light carpet. I wrote it off to my fury at
    the direction this little tete-a-tete had
    taken.

    "What the hell is this about, you fucker?
    What are you talking about? I may not be
    better than you at some things, but I can
    still beat the hell out of you."

    "No, you can't."

    Another odd lurch, as if I'd been walking
    and tripped down a stair. Both of the
    glasses crashed to the floor. Bob would
    be furious, I thought, but then I noticed
    that my sleeves covered my hands.

    "Doug . . . what?"

    My pants fell down. Horrified, I grabbed at
    their waist, looking reflexively up at Doug
    before I realized that I was looking directly
    into his stomach.

    I screamed! I'm sure it was as loud a
    noise as a human my size could make,
    but unfortunately "my size" is the key part
    of that concept. I was small somehow,
    and getting smaller. And the music was
    so loud . . .

    "What the hell is happening?"

    Doug grinned. Even from below (maybe
    especially from below) he looked like a
    lunatic. "Let's just say that, where Danny's
    concerned, I'm in and you're on your way
    out."

    "You stay away from Danny!" I yelled, but I
    was by then below his crotch level. Terror
    made me turn around and flee, but I was
    so tangled up in the ridiculous tents I had
    been comfortably wearing only a minute
    before. I fell, sprawled across the floor. By
    the time I got up, shrugged my way
    naked, I was smaller still, not able to
    reach the doorknob. Still, I ran to the door,
    began to beat furiously on it, but the
    sounds were lost in the stereo's
    percussion. Outside, I could hear
    laughing and the animated conversation
    of normal people having a good time.
    Here, only a few feet away, I was
    undergoing the most horrifying and
    bizarre situation anyone could imagine.

    "Help! Help me!"

    "Too little, too late," said Doug. His
    footsteps behind me shook the floor, and
    I reluctantly turned around to find myself
    eye-level with his shin.

    Nearly insane with horror, I collapsed and
    curled into a ball. By the way the carpet
    rubbed across my body, I could tell I was
    still contracting. For a brief moment, I
    thought wildly I might never stop
    shrinking, that Doug had found some
    maniacal way of erasing me from
    existence, but then, suddenly, I did. I was .
    . .
