His dull lessons being repetitive teachings of this equation or that formula. But now I was hoping it would just be an ordinary lesson.

Floor shining and smooth, pillars connecting them to the mausoleum like roof. I recognised it, the Astidian Network? I never managed to come here, but it was one of the main hub zones for spellcasters. The doorways replaced with rifts and portals. I could make out what was through them more often than not.

Robed onlookers watching, a demon like creature at the front as he gestures to each part of the deceased. Claws starting to dig into the body, dark energy being transferred from creator to createe, as the corpse gasps and springs to life.

Some orcs, a drow, lizardfolk and kobold amongst other creatures. The lot of them organising themselves in a circular plain that looks to be in the aether itself. Mist-like energy obscuring the ground, knee height for most of the average sized students. The energy changing colour as it moves throughout the room, from blue to purple to green to red. Connecting with itself at the classes edges to make a hardlight wall. Rising up far into the sky where bright light shines down upon the students. A single strapped dress covering her chest, made of an assortment of leaves that extends all the way to her legs. Held around her waste by a cord of leather, the teacher leaning forward to greet the class for their lesson. The front row of humans entirely spaced out and dormant, my bracelet starting to unload new sensations and temptations as I feel a need to join the others at the front.

The lesson straight underway as Miss Hosler says something about magic that I can barely understand. I’m too numbed by the bracelet. Only able to focus on the students behind me. A weak kobold that looks like he needs someone to protect him, a burly orc looking for a person to pin down, and a regal drow that gives an aura of superiority. I look to the other students to check them over, but by the time that I’m glancing at the next guy, my vision goes dark.

    Miss Hosler has pulled a VR set over my eyes. The games HUD on full display as I’m in the Summoning training hall, and still just my level 15 self. The other students logging in, Miss Hosler going to the back of the class, finger in the air.

    “Now find your human and practise your new skill, students.”

    Shit. Why did I have to zone out during the class? What did they plan to do? The other humans were still just staring at the wall, easy pickings for the other students. Meanwhile I at least had my senses back, I could maybe pick or at least direct the creature that might be easiest to resist. Though I don’t even know what I’m resisting.

    Out of all of the options, it’s the kobold that looks the simplest to overpower. They’re not exactly known for their strength, and this one is no different. Light green scales hidden underneath a black and blue tunic. It’s a one piece garb, the hinges trimmed with silver. The soft cloth accompanied by a few metal strappings that cover his arms and legs. Tail swaying absently and a few horns crowning his head. It’s Starter get up, there’s no guild symbol. I felt confident in my choice, the kobold looking at my approach and offering a wave.
