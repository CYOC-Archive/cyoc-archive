    “So…uh…did you find anyone to talk to?” Todd asked, trying to start a conversation (much to his own surprise).

    “Hmm? Oh yeah, I talked with a few people,” Sam replied. “Mostly upperclassmen. Nice guys. Unfortunately, they’re all straight. The struggles of being gay, am I right?”

    “Tell me about it,” Todd said before taking a sip of his water before the realization of what the other had said hit both men.

    “Wait, you’re gay?!” both of them asked in unison, which caused them both to start laughing.

    “Dude, my gaydar kept telling me that you were straight as an arrow!” Sam laughed, giving Todd a playful punch. “Guess I need to have that thing checked out!”

    “I just tend to assume most people are straight,” Todd admitted with a slight grin. “You honestly hide it pretty damn well.”

    “I kinda had to for most of my life!” Sam gave another laugh before his expression shifted to one of sadness.

    The sudden shift in Sam’s mood caught Todd completely off-guard. Although he hadn’t known Sam for a little over a day, he’d only ever seen the happy-go-lucky side of him.

    “Homophobic family?” Todd asked quietly, to which the blond-haired jock nodded his head.

    “Severely,” he responded. He paused and took a deep breath before continuing. “I’d always been taught that being gay was wrong, namely because of my older brother. He came out to my parents when he was in his sophomore year of high school and I was in my sixth grade year of middle school. My parents, they…they didn’t take it well…They kicked him out and completely cut him off from the family. It was heartbreaking...I loved my brother more than anything, and to see him get kicked out just like that? It really fucks with you. So then imagine how scared I was when I realized in my freshman year of high school that I liked men. I couldn’t say anything to my parents, so I had to keep all my emotions bottled up for years on end. I just felt so…so…alone…That’s why…” Sam paused again to take another deep breath. “That’s why when we were told about how we’d be starting completely new lives, I was excited. Yeah, I was still a little bummed at first, but I was excited to finally be free of my parents and completely start over as a new me. The real me…”

    Sam felt someone place a hand on his right hand, which he had placed on his knee. He turned his head to see Todd looking at him with a soft smile on his face. “You don’t have to be alone anymore,” he said, no trace of a stutter or nervousness in his voice. Sam used his free hand to wipe a tear out of his eyes before giving Todd a determined look and a nod.

    “Thanks, Todd,” he said, getting up from the couch before turning around and offering his hand to the other man. “Come on, let’s get outta here.”

    Todd nodded in agreement and took Sam’s hand.
