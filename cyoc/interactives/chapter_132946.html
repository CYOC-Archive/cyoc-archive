    Heather, Tyra, and Jeremy were waiting anxiously as a call was made to Costa Rica about Heather's escape. The official then hung up his phone to reveal some surprising news.

    "Heather, the Costa Rican government is willing to let you off with a black mark on your record for 'reckless experimentation' and drop the other charges." said the official.

    "Wha...?" said Heather, clearly not expecting that.

    "Apparently the tracks you left led the authorities right to a drug lord's home." continued the official, "And you won't believe what was seized there."


    "Go ahead." prompted Tyra, still not believing her ear holes.


    "Not only did we find more than $22 Billion in American cash, hidden in everything from suitcases to cabinets packed neatly to the top, and not counting the Chinese yuan that was seized as well," Said the official, reading from the notes he had taken, "we also found more vintage/rare/exotic firearms than most legitimate gun museums; gold-plated machine guns; many art pieces illegal to own (not counting the fact that some of them were registered as stolen from their origin countries); many exotic animals that were well taken care of, such as white tigers, panthers, leucistic lionesses, the whole nine yards; a man-made cave with hot tub; a drug lab for manufacturing and distributing cocaine, meth, and heroin; a marijuana farm; as well as something truly disturbing."

    "What's that?" asked Heather, clearly both wanting and not wanting to know.

    "There was apparently a plot on the table there to hire illegal poachers to obtain Heather's head. Literally." said the official. On seeing Tyra's enraged expression, he quickly added, "That drug lord is already being jailed for life just on the drug charges alone, so you don't have to worry about him anymore. That's why Costa Rica is going to let Heather off easily. But it's also going to be part of an event in Animalia this June. They're going to showcase everything seized from that drug lord's den to showcase why the war on drugs is being waged."

    "Good," said Jeremy, glad that Heather's life was out of danger.
