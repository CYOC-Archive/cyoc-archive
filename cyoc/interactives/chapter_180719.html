    Sapphire couldn't help but smile, clearing a table as she now collected the tip. Business had been steady but nothing she couldn't handle. "Slow day huh sis," Ruby asked. Sapphire shrugged gazing at the half filled diner. "A bit but not everyday can be a busy one."

    "Girls," Emerald stated as she poked her head out the kitchen. "One of you want to take that group of kids?" Sapphire and Ruby glanced over at the booth wincing. Teenagers could be troublesome, some okay treating the Soul Drives respectfully while others were incredibly rude. More often than they cared to remember they had experienced teens being rude, skipping out on the bill or even trying to sabotage their systems just for laughs. Needless to say, it was a coin toss as to how it could go.

    "Come on girls," Emerald said. "Its our duty to serve! Let's give these kids the best experience possible."

    "Then why dont you serve them," Sapphire said. Emerald now began nervously glancing around the restaurant trying to come up with an excuse. "Im..um...working the grill today," she said quickly. "And besides, I'm ready for a recharge. Bye!" The green gynoid now quickly disappeared inside the kitchen, leaving the blue and red gynoids alone.

    "I swear she downloaded a program for excuses," Ruby grumbled. "Remind me to delete any recent downloads shes had after work."

    Sapphire giggled, smiling at her sister. "I got this Ruby," she said.

    "You sure sis?"

    "Yeah I'm sure," Sapphire stated. "Know you havent been thrilled to deal with teenagers after those kids tried to download a computer virus into you."

    "You're a lifesaver," Ruby sighed. "I owe you."

    "I'll keep that in mind next time we go out to get our surface's buffed and waxed."

    If Sapphire was programmed for apprehension, she felt she would be experiencing it now. Humans, especially adolescents, could be the worse. She distinctly remembered a group of football players throwing their drinks on her before running out on the bill. Still, this was her purpose and she was only too happy to perform it. "Hi," she said cheerfully a smile forming on her feline face. "What can I get for you kids?"

    "How are your milkshakes," Sachiko asked.

    "Best in town," Ruby smiled. Sachiko smiled happily as the group now ordered up a round of milkshakes. Ruby now went about mixing up the shakes in front of the teens, much to Sachikos delight. "Mmm," Sachiko exclaimed, her eyes going wide. "This is delicious! Thank you!" Sapphire smiled, seeing the looks of gratitude on the teens faces. Maybe this would go well after all.
