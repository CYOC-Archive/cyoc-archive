    Waking up several weeks later, Maggie stretched herself out of her bed, watching by the window her surroundings and possessions.
    For the first time, she was truly happy with her decisions. She feared that boredom would grow on her rather quickly, but it wasn't hopefully the case.
    And she was getting used to the chores : milking out Callum, tending to the crops, feeding the group, those were small moments that she cherished, for they reminded her of what she accomplished beforehand.
    There was several times, although, when Maggie wondered if it would be time to bring back her friends.
    They weren't gone forever, of course, and yet she couldn't help but postpone the event, not out of malice, but a bit more like without thinking about it.

    She was pondering about it after breakfast and her chores for the most part.
    And then she ultimately resigned herself to commit the transformation back to square one : even with the best intent, what she did was not pleasant at all.
    Reuniting the animal trio, she went upstairs to find the device.
    The plan was simple : she was going to first bring their consciousness back, froze them, and finally turn them back to their human state before undoing the freezing.
    While pondering, she unexpectedly missed the last bits of the stairs. Tripping, she made a memorable fall, the device flying out of her hands.
    Maggie desperately tried to reach out for it, but landed flat on the wooden floor of the living room.
    The device kept flying, but soon met the same fate.
    And as it activated itself right in front of Maggie and engulfed her in a white flash, her last thoughts were washed away by the effects of the device, dulling to a simpler, animalistic kind.

    The rest of the animals eagerly waited, they quickly scattered when a female wolf emerged from the door.
    The wf seemed terrified, not knowing why she was in a human habitat, before deciding to head out to the forest.
    The cow and the bull, not willing to spend their time to a predator, simply went to the neighborhood pastures, joining a much larger bovine heard.
    Lastly, the boxer dog, confused and affraid that his mistress might be gone, headed out to the road, searching for her.
    And then, the farmhouse felt terribly silent from that day on.
