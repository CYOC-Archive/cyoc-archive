    She looked down at the plastic gun in her hands. She could hear Curt-Dono’s every thought, past or present, and her maid’s instinct to meet his every want and need, combined with her self-made backstory incorporating him into the role of the goddess’s reincarnating lover, made it SO hard not to simply teleport right to him, return his gun, and ease his worries in every and any way she could. She knew he already felt deeply uncomfortable with three goddesses calling him “Master,” not feeling worthy of such a high level of importance to them, and knew that making even MORE goddesses would just amplify those feelings.

    But she also knew from her understanding of the functions of the universe, that the world now required the existence of multiple gods and goddesses to function, even if it had, supposedly, started without one. She was powerful yes, overwhelmingly so, but she couldn’t manage the whole world on her own. And no god in this universe could create a being of greater power than itself, and creating an equal would be difficult even for her on her own. No, she needed to hang onto the gun. It was the only way to create a new pantheon. For the safety of her Master, and the universe he lived in. She could try to make it up to him later.

    First, she reached out for any traces of the divine already out there, Ammy-Chan and Issun-Chan aside. To her surprise, she found one; the little dragon girl, Simone, had a spark of the divine coursing through her. In time she could grow to become a goddess of dragons and justice. But Tomoyo decided to let her enjoy her childhood for now, rather than retconning her into being the right age right now. With only three dragons in the world at the moment, including Simone herself, that was a lower priority.

    With no other divine sparks out there, Tomoyo begant to consider what roles needed to be filled. Her first two goddesses already covered life, death, the sun, and darkness. A moon goddess would be a handy counterpart to have. She’d also need to at least cover the four classical elements of earth, air, water, and fire.

    She looked down at her own blazing body, and mentally checked “fire” off the list.

    Other “elements” could be given some consideration too; electricity, ice, plants. And of course, a divine over the non-anthro animals would be needed, too. Then, for the human experience, while friendship had already been covered, love, family, knowledge, and creativity, would be a priority.

    With the “must haves” on the list, she could get to work. She’d focus on minor gods for agriculture, industry and the arts after that, and perhaps go further in making goddesses for more specific niches when they were finished. Issun-Chan had become a goddess of paperwork after all, so no task was too small.

    Teleporting back into the mortal world again, she mused on how poorly this would thought of gods; regarding them as cosmic bullies and final bosses for JRPGs when they were acknowledged at all. And their followers were treated even worse, seen as weak-minded simpletons or conniving schemers. Hopefully, they could work to change all that.

Her old family, friends, and history was drastically altered, having been alive for several centuries at this point. She could tell from memories shared with Tomoyo that she had been human in a previous timeline, and that their Master had been indirectly responsible for her upgrade, but the nature of her old life just felt… wrong to her somehow. She was the moon goddess Lucielle, and Master Curt’s humble maid, now, and those facts were what was important to her. “Tomoyo, thank you, and Master Curt, for awakening me to my calling.” She bowed. “I will not disappoint. The moon will keep its phases perfectly, and its orbit will keep the tides in the needed ebb and flow, and Master will be tended to.”
