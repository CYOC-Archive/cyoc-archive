    “What if...what if I let you stay home today?” asked Molly desperately just before Miles was about to unlock the front door. He paused for a moment to consider.

    “Why?” asked Miles, genuinely perplexed, turning around to face his mother.

    Molly's face reddened a little, struggling to think of how to breech the subject without revealing her true intentions...

    “I...Miles, I'm going to be honest. I'm a witch. I want to prove to you that magic exists. I want to...show you your true calling” Molly bluffed.

    “Whatever” scoffed Miles, disappointed at the letdown of an explanation. He thought that his mom had something important to say for once. Instead, she's either going crazy or is lying for attention. “Maybe she's getting lonely in her old age?” he thought briefly before turning around to unlock the door.

    “Please believe me! I can prove it” said Molly insistently, holding her hands in front of her in a vulnerable looking display.

    Miles turned around one more time.

    “Magic. I'm assuming you're not talking about card tricks?”

    Molly shook her head.

    “No. More esoteric stuff. Weird stuff.”

    Miles sighed.

    “Mom, I don't know what's gotten into you. Are you going senile or something?”

    “Miles!” Molly shouted angrily, before composing herself. “OK, tell you what. I'm going to show you a very old spellbook that I own. I just want to see what you can do.”

    “Why?”

    Molly didn't tell Miles how she had always wanted a sister.
