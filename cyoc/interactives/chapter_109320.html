    With the talk done with, Lesily told the crowd, “It would seem that they are at the time before Michelle was to be married. And apparently Michelle was a real party animal. That could explain why Jane was a crazy one back in college. Let’s try something else.” She turned to the two and said, “Now girls, let’s got back to the year of 1969, the day that Woodstock took place.”

    And so the two regressed to that year. Michelle’s body thinned and toned to the point that she looked like a super model. Her breasts perk up and were the size of basketballs. Her hips curvy and stomach toned up that it showed some nearly visible abs. Her arms and legs had feminine muscles and were flexible at every turn. Her face was extremely beautiful with flat cheekbones, glimmering eyes, plushy lips, thin dainty nose, and a pointed chin. Her hair collapsed and lost the Afro and cascaded down her shoulders. Now her hair was all wavy and flowing through the wind. Her clothes kept on changing from the seventies to the late sixties. The changes are done and Michelle was now twenty-six. But then Lesily noticed something different in her clothes. What she was wearing was a tie die shirt that expose her stomach, a silk purple flowing skirt, a pair of purple round sunglasses with the piece sign on each lens, and she was barefoot. Not only that, but her long wavy hair had flowers and sparkles dotted all over the place. Lesily looked at that and could tell that Michelle had become a hippie who she believes participated at Woodstock.

    Wendy’s regression was making her feel better. Her body continued to thin and she was gaining muscles. Her breasts went up and were getting a little plumped for the next few years. And the various veins were noticeable on her arms and legs. Her face tightened a little more and the wrinkles on her face seem to be less noticeable. Her gray hair then shortened and permed up, making it look like a style resembling that of a late sixties woman. Lesily then took a close look and noticed that Wendy’s hair had changed color as a few of the strands now had dull blonde on them. The performer thought about it and find it strange that all three generations had different hair colors. For example, Jane’s hair color was sandy brown, Michelle’s hair color was black, and Wendy’s hair color was blonde. Her probable guess was that they took their hair colors after their fathers. But she decided to brush that aside and watched on as the changes kept going. Her clothes also changed to match that sixties look. So now Wendy has become 53-year-old woman who has just left middle age behind. She was wearing a Perma-prest shirtdress that goes down to her mid legs with a leopard print design, white pearls dotting her necklace, and a pair of tan three inch high heel dress shoes.

    With the transformation done, Lesily approached the two and went on talking. She first went to Michelle and asked, “How old are you Michelle and what are you doing today?” Michelle smiled and told her, “I’m 26 and I’m hear with my friends for Woodstock. This is a groovy experience. We get to see great artist like Hendrix, Grateful Dead, The Who, Jefferson Airplane, The Band, oh just about the most psycadelic bands in the universe. I love every bit of it! This is by far the craziest of times I ever had in my life. Peace, Love, and Music my people! Peace, Love, and Music.” Lesily nodded and now knew that Michelle took part of Woodstock. Turning to Wendy, she said, “And Wendy, what about you? How old are you and what do you think your daughter is doing today?” “I’m 53 years old.” Wendy responded, “And my daughter is with some friends on a road trip. They’ve been going around the countryside for a few days and she’s been calling me to let me know where she is. I can’t believe she’s living it on her own. It almost feels like yesterday that I was raising her.” Lesily nodded and could tell that Wendy didn’t know her daughter was at Woodstock. But she brushed that aside and left the two as Michelle walked into the hall of mirrors.
