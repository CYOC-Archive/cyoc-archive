Worse yet, the hooves at the ends of your wrists wouldn't stand a chance of ripping it and there's no way you could hold a knife to try and cut it off.

You begin to wonder if any sort of normal life would be possible with this kind of body. On closer inspection, you realize that it's probably the only part of you that's made up of real hair, and not only that, it's the same color as your hair was before the change. Thinking that this might not just apply to the tail, you reach up a hoof to see if you have any hair on your head. Just as you'd suspected, there's hair on your head as well - you have a long, thick mane running down the length of your neck, again made of real hair and again the same color as your hair was before. At least some things haven't changed, you think to yourself.

Frustrated, you pound your hoof-hand down on the phone, instantly breaking it to pieces. You try to scream but just end up letting out a loud, angry whinny.
