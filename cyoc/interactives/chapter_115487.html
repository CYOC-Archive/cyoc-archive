    Recently, a website was put on the internet. The website, called tubelooks.com, was originally a kid's science fair project. The original program was a photo progression software to show a picture slowly morphing into another picture. The only problem was that if you used pictures of 2 people, it would turn the person in the first picture into the person in the second picture. The student won first place, and was offered 1 million dollars for a copy of the program, which the student did. Afterwards, the program had been reworked with a few modifications. Now, if you input a picture of you and a link to a YouTuber's account, it would scan through every video within a specified date range and transform you into that YouTuber from that time period. The website said that it would perform these things for free under 2 conditions:
    -The person must tell a person the website within one month.
    -If the person told the other person that the website was more than a photo progression software, all effects would be reversed and they would never be able to use the software ever again.

    One day, Blake Seanson was emailed about the website saying that is was "extremely realistic", but Blake thought it was a load of bull. So he decided to check out the website. When he logged on, it prompted him with a welcome message, saying to "Input change subject. NOTE: Subject must be willing and in knowledge of the website's affects". "I guess that was for copyright reasons?" Blake said as he put in his own picture. Next, a message with a textbar said "Input YouTube channel link".
