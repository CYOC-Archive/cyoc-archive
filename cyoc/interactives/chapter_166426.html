    Now contrary to what many assumed at first the reason Gen-tech had a third section labelled 'others' wasn't over some 'returned stock discount' or 'cheep defective knockoffs' merely being sold to clear the other areas.
    No this section was exactly a mixture zone but a more extravagant option really dealing in mythology creatures bio-suits and parts. many due to their type merely having human and animal parts. that said when one normally chooses the section it is either they are aware of this fact (employees actually are required to explain the truth when asked) or are exploring without much thought to what changes they want.

    Good thing Jessica had such keen eyes for certain details to know just from looking that the other section really had more advanced style stock in her opinion so she choose to explore the creatures that were once a fantasy for something.

    among these options were:

    -Dragons: the legendary fire breathing winged lizards that came in various styles from 20 feet tall to about the size of an average 5 year old, all sizes also came in a myriad of colours and were split between the traditional four-legged stubby variation, Chinese slim super-long variation and even a more human posture variation... only drawback due to the dangers of over stretching the user's DNA was that from 9 feet onward all sizes required multiple people.

    -Unicorn: really a branch of the basic equine bio-suits from Animal, but all colours are exclusive and these suits retain the wearer's ability to speak in human tongue. Jessica often recalled a number of girls growing up having their parents rent such suits for birthdays (without parents buying their own children were prohibited from owning bio-suits themselves).

    -Merfolk: the legendary half-human, half-fish that were prefect for users who loved swimming yet retaining s sense of human parts. while there were a few forms that were clearly hybrids of human and aquatic creatures like dolphins or octopus (like a certain sea-witch) most actually had forms unlike traditional fish for the logical reason there shape conflicts with the upper human build. as an added bonus users can choose the usual human alterations for their upper bodies when customizing their form.

    -Centaur: the half-human half-horse originally from Greek mythology. accomplished by carefully modifying standard horse bio-suits to function and change only below the user's waist. recently with their growing trend it was becoming a new theme at the horse races to see centaurs and centauresses as opposed to merely jockeys riding horses (contrary to rumours the bio-suit only gave the appearance of adding muscle but without training it would look like a muscle-head failing about trying to life a quarter of their weight). like merfolk upper human body modifications is doable.

    -Nymph: a female only (as in male users become female while wearing them) option that looked human bar the exclusive green colouring of the skin and colours like purple or pink in hair. these suits were popular with nature lovers. more so these suits are often sold alongside Gen-tech created trees for extra effects that are linked to the suits and in cases of group purchase many can share a single tree. wearing nymph suits without additional clothing was more common then seeing them with it though.

    -Stayr: a male only (as in female users become male while wearing them) option that like in mythology act as a counterparts to nymphs that like merfolk and centaurs have an animal lower area which in this case is a goat but also having horns. popular with lovers of music like the stayrs of legend and at the same time a choice of nature lovers.

    -Harpy: a feather and winged humanoid form that actually come in both genders. users are often lovers of flying or the skies. due to design unlike other human-hybrid bio-suits the alteration of human parts is extra limited (this over the areas usually being charged in such options being feather covered.

    ...alongside many other options that escaped Jessica's mind at this moment.
