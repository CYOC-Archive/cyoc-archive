    Learning to Hunt like a Wolf

    "Now wasn't that fun?," Amy asked Tad, kissing him.

    "It was fun," Tad said, blushing as she kissed him, "I guess this means we're a thing now?"

    Amy smiled and nuzzled him. "I'm all yours handsome," she said.

    Just then, a growl resonated from Tad's stomach. "I'm getting pretty hungry," he said, "Let's go back to the party and get something to eat." He started to walk off, but Amy grabbed his furry hand.

    "Why do you want to go back there?," she asked, pulling him back towards her, "We could always get something better to eat."

    "Like what?," Tad asked, nervously.

    "How about some fresh meat?"

    "What?"

    "We go and hunt some deer," Amy said, "Come on! It'll be fun!"

    "But I've never learned how to hunt," Tad said.

    "Then I'll teach you!," Amy exclaimed, "Follow me!"

    Amy pulled Tad into the forest for a while until she stopped and gestured for him to kneel down behind a bush. "There," she whispered. She pointed through the bush at a deer grazing on the grass. It was surprising that the deer hadn't fled the area, as the pack wasn't that far away from where they were now.

    "What if that's a were?," Tad whispered.

    "It would be walking on two legs like most weres then," Amy responded, "Besides, it doesn't smell like a were."

    Tad sighed silently. "Alright," he said, "How do we do this?"

    "It's simple," Amy responded, "We're werewolves, so we hunt just like a normal wolf, although it will be a little harder. After all, we're much bigger than normal wolves. Anyway, we just need to sneak up behind it and pounce. If it starts to run away, I'll go after and catch it for us."

    Tad smiled. "Thanks Amy," he said, smiling.

    Going around the bush, Tad crouched down and moved towards the deer slowly. It didn't seem to notice yet. He crept up until he was about a foot away from the deer. Then, he pounced at the deer, biting at it's neck. The creature writhed and tried to escape Tad's jaw, but was unsuccessful. It eventually stopped trying.

    Once the deer died, Amy came out of the bush clapping. "Not bad for your first hunt," she said, kissing his bloodstained muzzle, "Now let's eat!"

    She then ripped a chunk of meat from the deer and ate it. Some of the blood from the chunk leaked out of her mouth. Tad hesitantly took a chunk of meat from the deer and bit into it, his expression instantly changing from one of disgust to one of amazement.

    "This...is...AMAZING!," he exclaimed, devouring the chunk of deer meat.

    "I thought you'd like it," Amy said, smiling, grabbing another chunk of meat in her mouth.

    After eating most of the deer, Tad yawned. "I'm getting kind of tired," he said, "Do you want to go back to my place?"

    "I don't see why not," Amy said, "Let's head back."

    The two weres walked back to the pack meeting. There were a few weres sleeping around the fire, while others were changing back into their human forms. Tad went over to where he left his bag and slung it around his shoulder.

    "Ready to go?," Tad asked Amy, who had her bag around her shoulder as well.

    "Yep," she said, holding Tad's hand, "Let's go!"

    Tad and Amy started to walk back towards the neighborhood.
