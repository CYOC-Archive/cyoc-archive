    New Neighbors : Dinner at your house

    added by Nobody 5 years ago A

    Frank and Bonnie had to duck down as they stepped inside. You found it amazing that their hooves managed to find balance and locomotion on the hundreds of pounds both easily weighed!

    Bonnie's figure was that of a body builder, so it was easy to understand at first glance you might have mistaken her for a male until you saw between her legs and her chest registered (which was proportionally sized to the tall Zebra woman).

    She was actually more well built than Frank, Taller too, she easily had more muscle. But Frank showed no embarrassment at being coupled with a taller woman. Given her chest size, you guessed he had no room to complain!

    If Frank was an equine barbarian king in his body figure, Bonnie was definitely an amazon queen.

    Your mother Whitney came out to greet your new guests along with your other two sisters.

    Grace skipped around the pair with unlimited excitement. She looked like she was two seconds from asking for a ride from one of them.

    Mother looked down and up, and up, and up at the married couple, and she blushed hard, and covered her mother. "I-I! Welcome to our humble abode! I-I see you've met Zoe, my littlest, Amon my son. And this is Lucy and Zoe!"

    Your oldest little sister, Zoe, was scooping out Frank like he was a piece of meat, you didn't blame her, she was hitting that age where hormones hit hard. She didn't seem to find the fact a naked stallion man was in front of her.

    Zoe didn't look up from her Gameboy, just giving up a mumbled, "Hello." You wonder if she even noticed the new neighbors weren't human.

    "W-Well! We have extra seats at the table, and we were about to sit down for dinner, and we have plenty of extra. Why don't you sit down?"

    "We wouldn't want to impose," Bonnie said politely.

    "I-it's no trouble! No trouble at all!" Your mom said putting on her best practiced hostess smile.

    It seemed that either NOBODY wanted to bring up the fact their guests looked out extras from Island of Dr. Monroe, or you were the only one to actually find that detail strange.

    As they ducked into the living room, your mother saw at once that the chairs might be a little too cramped.

    "Amon, get our guests some metal stools from the kitchen!"

    "Yes mom." You obeyed.

    As you sat down to grace, Bonnie and Frank joined in without missing a beat, your mother's eyes glanced at them for a moment but continued and finished with, "And bless our new neighbors, Bonnie and Frank, amen."

    "Amen," everyone at the table echoed.

    Bonnie and Frank avoided touching the steak but made no protest to it being on the table, feeding themselves with the potatoes, bread, and asparagus being served.

    Zoe couldn't stop looking at your house guests. And neither could Lucy, but for very different reasons, Frank did his absolute best to pretend he didn't notice. You could tell mother was going to have to have a chat with Lucy later. Zoe was off in her own world as normal doing her best to avoid reality. Though reality had changed some of the rules to your observation.

    "So... why didn't you visit sooner?" Whitney asked.

    "We were settling in," Frank said. "We go to bed early and wake up early. And we had to do some renovations to the house. The carpenter didn't have us in mind. Our furniture took a lot of effort to get in just right, and all of Bonnie's equipment."

    "You could work out more too you know." Bonnie remarked.

    "I work plenty," Frank replied.

    You were surprised when it was Bonnie and Frank who began looking around the table awkwardly.

    "Excuse me," Frank said. "Forgive if this is an odd question but... where is your husband?"

    You could hear a pin drop.

    Mother twitched. "H-He's... he's not here right now."

    "Business trip?" Frank asked obliviously. Bonnie glared at him. You couldn't believe the naked horse man had stumbled on the one subject more awkward than his entire existence.

    "Just... away," Mother said painfully vague. Your sisters knew better than to say anything, and so did you.

    "Oh! Well I hope we meet him soon, ow!" Frank said as Bonnie gave Frank a slightly kick under the table.

    "Yes, that would be nice," Mother said simply. Then said quickly. "Who wants ice-cream for dessert?!"
