Mostly as now they'd been able to put in place specialized orientations to give new students tours of the building, thus letting them know where things were. It avoided the scenes of people getting lost that happened a bit the previous year.

    There was also some time to catch up with some friends other than the core group of Animalia Ambassadoria, whom Abby remained closest to. Animalia Ambassadoria's "broadcast" schedule and the increased reporting responsibilities had generally limited the time during the summer to catch up with some of the others that Abby had met and befriended, particularly after Animalia got started. With the IAC the previous summer that was even more of an issue. School in that sense let her catch up with other friends that she'd made.

    "Okay, what's that smirk for?" came one voice from across the hall as Abby came out her first period class.

    Abby turned to see Marcy coming up the hall. The Polar Bear Animalian wore some light and loose clothing that was supposedly supposed to help one stay cool, though clothing wasn't exactly what Marcy was talking about, as she'd also noted a small smirk on Abby's equine muzzle. That had to mean that something was up beyond clothes or something that happened in class.

However, Sylvia seemed more interested in flirtation than work, and since she's there as a sort of community service thing..."

    "I must say... a barn isn't the normal place where I would do this sort of work," the man commented as finished plugging in some extension cords to handle the job that was required. "I mean... I though you Animalians didn't like tattoos."

    "The latter issue might be a bit difficult to avoid," the tattoo artist commented, "the ink I use MIGHT dye the fur and hair as it regrows... but it will need to be shaved before I can apply it."

    Martin heard a few snorts of approval and watched as both Josephine and Abby shook their heads. Samson nodded slightly, but he also looked a bit uncomfortable, which Martin understood. Sylvia was there to work the farm and essentially help make some things easier for Samson and Abby with the school year coming. However, Martin had noticed that Sylvia seemed more attentive to the Animalian stallions that came to help out from time to time. Delilah and Calamity had transferred into a online program that was helping them earn nursing degrees along with interning at Horizon Light and were doing fairly well there, but their mates Thunder and Red Cloud did help out with the farm work from harvesting apples to helping control potential insect pests. Not to mention carrying pails for when any Animalian female needed to be "milked" and didn't have a mate to deal with that in private. It was something that among the various chores that were part of the work running the Brown Farm.

    However, Sylvia was often found shamelessly flirting with almost any stallion that she came across, regardless of whether it was Samson, Red Cloud, or Thunder. Privately, Martin even wondered if the Bavarian Warmblood Mare might even flirt with him if he didn't have Josephine nearby. It was something that got in the way and slowed work down, particularly when Sylvia was not one to back down and once even offered the idea of a threesome between herself, Calamity, and Thunder. Thus, Martin and Josephine came up with the tattoo branding idea as a "punishment."

    Typically, in the ranching business used branding as a means of identifying ownership. It helped ranchers on the open range tell who owned what animals, and while the use of open range wasn't as big as it was in earlier eras, the Browns did use it as well before Animalia. Typically the process involved using a hot iron and placing it against the rear flank of cattle or horses, and best done when the animal was young so that way it could be better controlled. The branding wouldn't necessarily remove fur, but the scar tissue coming from the resulting burn would be permanent enough that mark would be forever present, like a surgical scar. However, after Animalia got started, it was found that the uplifted cattle and horses that the Browns owned had lost their brands, likely fully healed as a result of their uplifting, and it was figured that placing a hot iron on an Animalian would be "cruel and unusual" and would go beyond what was even tolerated with other Animalians or Humans.

    The tattoo, however, would be different. As Humans went to tattoo artists all the time to have images put into their skin, thus it wouldn't be cruel to use it, and if it worked as imagined, the ink would then dye new hair and fur as it grew through the inked area and thus would show through. The plan was that Sylvia would be marked with the words: ein kokettes Bayerisches Warmblut, which when translated from German would read: a flirty Bavarian Warmblood and would essentially be a reminder to Sylvia on her behavior. Oddly enough though, when Martin and Josephine had given their displeasure with Sylvia's shameless flirtation and stated the idea for this "branding" as punishment, Sylvia had seemed more than okay with it and even offered design alterations to have little hearts to dot the "i"s in "ein" and "Bayerisches." Odd... but if things showed through her fur, it would be noticed by anyone she was intimate with in the future.

    "Okay... I think I'm ready," the tattoo artist commented, "bring her in and get her shaved."

    Martin nodded and moved to the barn door, where he found Sylvia waiting with a smile and holding her pants over her shoulder and displaying that the pink panties she was wearing were a bit small for her.

    "Okay... I'm ready..." Sylvia said with a smile and glanced to see that Samson was also present. Finding out that he was spoken for was a bit depressing, but she figured since it wasn't a formal marriage, it wouldn't hurt to try. And a part of her was even up for including Abby in the fun too, but given the Brown parents protectiveness of Abby, Sylvia figured it best not to try and push anything there.
