    Susan had to grit her teeth and ignore a lot of pain, but she had the strength for a pushup. Hard to say if it was enough for what she needed to
    do next, but it was promising. Under normal circumstances, she would never consider the risk, but the one thing that had kept her sane and
    maintained that glimmer of hope was knowing that she'd have her own body back when this was over. Sure, that bastard had taken an eye,
    broken her legs, snapped her fingers, and inflicted dozens of other injuries to get his sick thrills, but if she could get out of this hole, that
    would all go away.

    She tried standing, and almost immediately collapsed. The breaks in her legs had had time to heal up a bit, but they'd set wrong. If she put too
    much pressure on them, she'd end up even worse off than when that damn Wolverine first attacked. She repeated her mantra. Pain is freedom.
    Light is salvation. It sounded a little too religious for her taste, but then if this didn't work, it might be time to try embracing some faith
    anyway.

    She still couldn't believe she'd gotten into this. She'd opted to attend the park as a bear to get some rest. The pressure at work was really
    getting to her, and she couldn't relax at home with the kids bothering her. Just a nice relaxing stretch as a bear, resting lazily in a cave, at the
    time it sounded perfect. It was too, right up until that maniac had smashed her up and forced her through that narrow crack...

    She'd lost weight since then. He was only feeding her enough to keep her alive. Not so great when it came to retaining muscle mass, but the
    trip back out wouldn't be nearly as tight a squeeze. Her arms would matter more than her legs for this. All she needed her legs for was
    reaching the crack. She tried standing again. Wobbly, but if she was careful, she could manage. She felt along to ceiling, trying to find the lip in
    the dark. There it was. She let herself rest some more. Higher than she'd hoped. She'd have to leap a little to get a good grip. That was going to
    hurt. She had to ready herself for that, make sure she didn't lose her grip from the pain. She rubbed her shoulder for luck. She was pretty sure
    her arms were up for this. One had been dislocated not long ago, but she could push herself off the floor, and they were still stronger than
    what she had as a human...

    She ran through the whole plan one more time. She'd leap to the hole, and catch a handhold. That would be it for her legs, and it would be up
    to her arms. She'd have to pull herself up, at least to the waist. From there she could rest a little, just enough to get her strength back. A bit
    more crawling and she'd be in the main part of the cave. She had to have been missing long enough to be missed. They'd be combing the hills,
    wouldn't they? If she could just get outside, be seen, it would all be over. Even if he came back early, from up there she could scream. The
    acoustics would carry it, right?

    One last time, she carefully pulled herself up. She confirmed her target, crouched down, and leapt...

    TGs tend to gather, and get some lessons you all clearly need. You can head that way, get to know the rest of the deer in the park, maybe

    find you all a chaperone to keep you from getting yourselves in any trouble. Pick one, and let's go!"

    There was a bit of hesitation, but eventually the does began to disperse. If any objected to her authority, they certainly were keeping quiet
    about it.

    Goldie returned to base, hoping to find Zack or Harry to give her new orders, but instead finding only the twins. After making sure they'd been
    informed of the circumstances under which the security detail had been cut short for the day, she stood at attention, asking if she could be of
    any further use.

    The twins sighed, in unison of course. "Look Goldie. Harry's been busy all day. First there was a phone call from Naomi checking in on things,
    and then someone from management called him in for a meeting about something, so he's going to be busy for a while."
    "And Zack's busy with something else today, with plans to meet up with us later tonight. Things seem to pretty much be under control today,
    so you may as well take a break."

    Goldie was a bit disappointed. She was already feeling a bit guilty that she had let herself get distracted when there was a disruption before.
    "Ma'ams, I'd be happy to volunteer for any extra duties you might be able to think of, ma'ams!"

    The twins rolled their eyes. "You realize there's such a thing as being too helpful, don't you?" Goldie gave a worried look. "You're relieved of
    duty for the rest of the day." Her tail curled between her legs with a bit of a whimper." "OK how about this? We order you to go relax. Now

    Goldie wandered away, feeling rejected and trying to work out what she'd done wrong, to find Sam waiting outside.

    "Hi again! Busy?"

    "No! Not at all. Is there a problem somewhere?"

    Sam chuckled. "Nothing like that. I just thought we could take a walk. Come on."

    Goldie's tail started to wag. He wanted to take her out on patrol? Eagerly, she trotted over, to follow where ever he went. Wait, why was it she

    something she'd wanted to talk to him about. Combat training? She pictured him wrestling her to the ground and... no no! She had to clear up
    that whole thing about him being attracted to her, that was it. "Sam?" she asked...
