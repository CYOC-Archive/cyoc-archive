He could see why she was considered a Loser in the by the lower classmen of Shermer. She wasn't all that much to look at and her voice sounded like a dying frog. Bill was suddenly grateful that as a Senior he...But the thought died as he realized that he was no longer a Senior. He wasn't graduating in a few weeks, he was a Sophmore all over again and somehow back in October 2018!

He hoped it wasn't too 'out of character' for the girl he was now.

This was not the way her Willow acted, so...reserved, especially with Paige. The two girls loved each other like sisters, and Martha was glad that Willow had someone looking out for her in school. But this...This was so out of character for the both of them. Maybe Willow was just nervous? Her daughter had a penchant for over-investing in things and when they didn't pan out, sometimes she could get a little emotional, so maybe she just needed to have a good cry in private. But regardless, her words seemed to break the tension, at least for Paige.

    "C'mon Wills, let's head up. You can show me that new picture you were working on before you left!" The tubby girl said and grabbed Willow's arm, this time a bit more forcefully, and headed towards the stairs, practically dragging her BFF with her.

    "Uh, sure. The, uh, drawing!" Was all Bill could say as his skinny form was jerked forward. The two girls plodded up the stairs and down the upper hallway, passing Kimberly's room, in which the pre-teen was dancing around with her eyes shut, a pair of headphones on. And then they were at what was once Bill's door, but now it was clearly someone else's.

Beneath it was some weird starfish looking thing that was saying "Praise Helix!" in different handwriting, clearly not the same as the Pidgey. But as interesting as that was, Bill tried to examine the pictures on the door before Paige opened it and pulled him into what had once been his bedroom.

    Everything was different. And pink. Just a few hours ago, Bill's bed had been against the far wall, his desk in front of the window, but now those things were reversed, with his bed sitting right next to the window and decked out in pink and white blankets and way, way too many plushies. And his computer was different! Instead of a slightly outdated gaming rig it was now some dinky looking Apple computer with an art tablet next to it! Posters of boy bands and anime shows hung on the walls instead of his much smaller collection of Star Wars and Marvel posters. And what was with the bean bags? Were those...Were those Barbie dolls set up like models?

    'Oh my god' Bill thought as he wandered in, not realizing Paige had let go of his arm and was quietly closing the door behind him, beady eyes staring at him as he carefully made his way through the feminized room for the first time.

    'It's like she's...She's never been here before. Did she hit her head or something at the gym? Did...Was Matt there? Did he do something to her? What is going on?!' Paige said as Bill finally found a uneasy seat on the edge of the bead, his frizzy face still staring around the room in shock, confusion and...anger? Willow was never angry! She was too kind for that! There was something off about Willow's eyes...Like there was someone else behind them? Paige knew she was a full fledged geek, maybe even a nerd, but she was firmly planted in reality. Life's cruelty and the need to look out for a Dreamer like Willow made her the cynical realist of their little friend group, but for some reason she couldn't shake the feeling that this was not the Willow she knew, that something had changed inside her best friend.

    The two girls locked eyes as Willow finished looking around the room, and Paige couldn't see the same excited sparkle in those green-blue pools, just...clouds. It was too much for the overweight girl to bear as suspicion crumbled into concern, her eyes watering up behind her glasses.

    "Willow, ar-are you okay? Did something happen at the gym? You're...This isn't you! You're acting like you've never been here before, and like...like you hate me!" Paige said, her voice struggling not to break. "

    'Shit shit shit!' Bill screamed to himself as he tried not to panic. He couldn't escape, the fat girl was blocking the door, and it was clear that his mom wasn't going to save him...save her or whatever this time. He had to think of something, and fast!
