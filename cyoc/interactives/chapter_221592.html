    Adrian stared at the protein powder with a very skeptical look. 'Works so well, because it's magic,' he read the tagline. It seemed rather dubious. He'd always heard that the better results that the muscle growing products claimed, the more likely they were a scam. Excluding hard work and dedication, there were no shortcuts to getting big. Still, a bit of curiosity filled Adrian as he went to go pay for it.

    "Find everything alright?" the big guy behind the counter asked. His shirt stretched tightly across his chest and the sleeves clung to his biceps. They stretched around the hard muscle as he picked up the container. He clearly worked out a lot. However, his eyes narrowed as he looked at the bottle. "Where'd you find this?"

    It took Adrian a second to realize what the employee had asked. He was a bit distracted by how handsome he was. "Oh? That? It was..." Adrian looked to the back of the store where he'd picked it up. However, it was almost like the area had changed. He specifically remembered it being pointed to by a standee of a buff wizard in the back. Based on the rest of the layout of the store, that didn't make any sense. There was an almost minimalist nature to everything. Most everything just had the labels or a symbol like a lightning bolt or barbell. There were a few standees with large muscular men without their shirts on or an attractive woman in blue scale. But no buff wizard. "I don't know..."

    "Weird..." the employee looked over the bottle a bit more. "Warning: Be careful where this is ingested. Results are instantaneous and contagious. Keep out of the reach of children." He read off. "Weird... I don't think I've ever seen this before..." The royal purple color definitely stood out compared to all the red, black and greys that the other bottles had.

    "Is that a problem?" Adrian asked.

    "No," the employee shrugged as he rang it out. $21.99 appeared on the register and Adrian paid the amount.
