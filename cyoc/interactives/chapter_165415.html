    It had been nearly a month since James boldly re-invented himself. She felt a little trepidation about how he might react, but despite the initial shock he was nothing short of gentlemanly.

    Despite this, Jennifer couldn’t help but feel an air of unease around the entire situation. While Henry was still her father, she found that she no longer associated him with the man that had raised her. She wasn’t sure when that notion had changed. Was it when Henry had begun to change himself? Was it when she had accepted her new identity? In the end she accepted that she would likely never know, but the thought led Jennifer to other revelations.

    She found herself happy for Jess and Henry. Happy that Henry was so supportive and loving and happy of the bond that the two of them now shared. But Jennifer knew she would just be lying to herself if she said that there wasn’t a hint of jealousy in her thoughts. How nice it would have been for Henry to have said Horse, to have chosen her for his mate instead. But those feelings quickly subsided, and Jennifer found herself yearning to join the Happy couple as often as she could. In fact Jennifer had organised a dinner for them both, celebrating one month since her re-birth. A milestone that she felt comfortable celebrating. It was also a month since her last slip up, and from when he had last seen Henry and Jess, it was well over that for the entire family, at least as far as she could tell. For her, that deserved celebration.

She was happy to start munching away and wouldn’t fully focus again until she had finished. But soon enough the three of them pushed their empty plates towards the table and patted their full bellies.

She blushed before she spoke, looking down at the table to avoid making eye contact. “I just thought that we owed it to ourselves to celebrate a little.
