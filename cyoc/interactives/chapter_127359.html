    "You know what Dad, I'd like to try something to give you a bit of a break from taking care of me."

    "Nick, what are you going to do?"

    "Well, I thought that I could age up to be older, get a preview of what being an adult is like, while you could be my age--or younger if you want. Just for a week."

    "So switching roles so that I'd be the son and you the father?" asked Ron, feeling uncomfortable at the thought.

    "I wouldn't make it permanent. It'd just be a break for you, and an... opportunity for me to get a real hard look at what being a grown up is like. You know how there's always those episodes in kids shows where the kid and parent switch roles for an episode and the kid learns a valuable lesson? I just thought, well, maybe there might be something to that." said Nick.

    "You just want to see what you'll look like when you're done growing up, don't you?" asked Ron, cutting through all the extra hoops his son was jumping through to try and convince him.

    "Well, yeah..." admitted Nick, with a blush that crept across his face, clashing wildly with his own red hair that he'd inherited from Ron.

    "Only a week, Nick. At the end of it, no matter what, I want things to go back to... well, normal. That's half the fun of pretending things like this--is that it's only for a short time. And, some of my best memories come from being your Dad, and I don't want to lose them for forever."

    Nick's face lit up and he immediately nodded his head and hugged him.

    "All right, now how old do you want to get?

    "Thirty-five. Not too old, but not too young."

    "Thirty-five? That would mean, if I were to become your age, you'd have had me when you were... young. Real young, like while you were in college, young. That might not be the best thing for your adulthood..."

    "Okay, but you don't have to be exactly my age, you could be younger too," suggested Nick as he leaned over and entered a single digit for Ron's new age. Ron looked over and saw that Nick had inputted a nine.

    "That'd be better, but I didn't think I'd get so young as that!"

    "It's a break, Dad. Being a teenager is just so stressful, do you really want to go back to puberty and high school?" questioned Nick, as though he'd already had this answer lined up.

    "Stress knows no age, Nick, it just takes on different forms... that's... well, you'll know that soon enough," said Ron as he looked over the profiles, but there was something still blinking on the screen. from their set ups, Ron scrolled over to get a better look at the offended input.

    "It's asking who to list as my new mother, and whether or not she should be part of the scenario."

    "I'm not sure I want to include anyone else in this week," answered Nick tentatively. And Ron nodded and marked his new mother as "not part of the scenario" which automatically made her marital status appear as "separated from Nick", that didn't solve all the issues however.

    "Okay, but it still needs a name."

    "Then... put down Selena Johnson," said Nick with another blush. Ron smirked, recognizing the name of one of Nick's long time classmates with whom he'd had a puppy love crush on for many years, but never the guts to say anything about. A few more pop ups appeared asking how far this should alter reality, and other safety measures, such as whether to forget this current reality's memories or not. Ron and Nick talked each as they came up agreeing to alter all of reality to suit what they'd imputed, to keep their current memories, but also gain the new ones they'd need so that they'd fit in the new reality, and that for the transformations to automatically expire at the end of the week. They also set the transformations to take place over the course of an hour so that they could take the opportunity to witness their transformations and not just have everything change in one click. Satisfied with all the options, Ron and Nick clicked on "Begin Transformation(s)" and an hour's timer appeared on the screen.
