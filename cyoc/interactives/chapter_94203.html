    Mommy/Daughter snuggle time

    "How about we order something in and the two of us sit on the couch. My feet are exhausted." Morgan says.

    "Yeah, I'm a little tuckered from the plane ride too." May says. The two remain seated on the couch as Morgan called the front desk.

    "Hello, I'd like to order room service." Morgan says.

    "What would you like?" she asks.

    "I'd like a spaghetti with parmesean cheese. May, what do you want, honey?" Morgan asks.

    "A pizza with ham on it." May answers.

    "A cheese pizza with ham." Morgan answers.

    "Size of said pizza?" she asks.

    "What size? Large, and can you send some Dr. Pepper with that?" Morgan asks.

    "Certainly. That'll be about 20 minutes." she answers.

    "Thank you, bye." Morgan says, then joins May back on the couch. May snuggles up to her mother.

    "Mommy, why did Dad leave?" May asks, having always wanted to ask.

    "If you ever thought you were why, you aren't. He ran off with some cleaning lady he met while attending a wedding in Vegas. I didn't need him anyway. All he ever did was bring me down anyway. I got you out of it, so it wasn't all bad." Morgan answers.

    "Mommy, some people call me spoiled and say that I'd turn out bad." May says.

    "Ugh, mostly our relatives, and that's part of why we don't visit them much if at all. May, I had nothing growing up. My parents spent their money on their greedy selves. Buying fancy cars, nice clothes, cds and all sorts of other stuff. Mostly, I had some clothes and nothing else. I made a promise to myself that I was gonna give my child everything I could. My grandparents paid my way through college because they didn't want me to have that and a job at the same time. To think they were so rich, were all around nice people and then their son is a snob. Honey, you will always be the best thing I've ever done on this planet and I'm so glad you aren't gonna turn out like my parents. If you EVER act like them towards your children, I will have my foot so far up your ass, it'll give you a headache." Morgan explains.

    "Mommy, about that time I asked to wear diapers....." May says.

    "Ugh, as I said earlier, I regretted it. I wanted to say yes so you could be who you are. I just didn't think about it at the time, sweetie. If I could take it back and do it all over again, I'd have changed you into one without saying yes, my little bumble bee." Morgan says.

    "I know that now." May says. Their food soon arrives. Morgan takes it and hands the person a tip as well. They then sit and eat their food. Then decide to eat first, then decide what to do next
