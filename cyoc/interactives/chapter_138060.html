    You pull out your wand and examine it for a bit. You then walk around, looking for one last animal for an experiment. You soon find a bald eagle on the ground, seemingly looking for something. You close your eyes as you point your wand at the bird and shoot a stream of black latex at it. It squawks as it is hit. You fear that you’ve killed it too when you open your eyes and see it changing. It doesn’t take much time at all for the bird to turn into a black latex dragon, about the size of a horse. You smile, happy that you’ve finally found a way to get animal minions. You start to go around turning every animal you see into latex with your wand. At the end you have an army of latex beasts. You have dragons, horses, gryphons, Pegasi, Deers, cows, birds, bunnies, etc. In the water you have orcas, fish, dolphins, whales, etc. From your experiments your realize a few things:
    1. Humans turn into anthros while animals stay feral.
    2. The animal turns into a latex creature regardless of it’s original species (as demonstrated by the bald eagle turning into a black dragon.)
    3. The size seems random (from deer you’ve gotten cat sized dragons and house sized dragons.)
    4. The animal can turn into a creature from the past, the present, the future, or a myth. (You’ve gotten a t-Rex from a bunny, a cow from a blue jay, a strange cow like monster with a clear and squishy jug instead of an udder with valves, and you’ve gotten a gryphon from a rat.)
    5. Any attempt at turning an animal into latex with a latex anthro results in failure while the wand can turn anything. (It can be assumed that animals can only turn animals and will kill humans, but they can eat humans and store them for an anthro to turn them (as demonstrated when a hiker found you after you turned a dog into a huge wolf.)
    6. Land animals can turn into land and air latex animals and the same with air animals but water animals can only turn into water latex animals.
    7. Humans always turn into anthro of the same gender while animals can change genders. (You’ve seen a female deer turn into a male compy, a male rat turn into a female t-Rex (who then immediately mated with the male t-Rex you got from the bunny), a female deer turn into a female dragon, and a male deer that turned into a male dragon (who then mated with the previously mentioned female))
    8. The color of the latex animals turned by other latex animals depending on the previous latex animal’s color.
    9. From the two mated animals, you found out that they get pregnant in minutes, lay eggs or give birth a few minutes afterwards, and the eggs hatch in a few minutes. The young are of the parent’s species but the color and size are random. The animals only mate with the same species (color and size don’t matter) and only have one mate. Though you have seen m/m, f/f, and f/m sex though they don’t produce young (the f/m can but only if they are mates, if they walk away after, they aren’t mates and don’t produce young, this can also happen across species.)
    Those are all of the things you could figure out from your experiments. You dismiss the latex animals under orders to turn any animals into latex. You let the new latex anthro horse go with them to turn any humans that the latex animals capture (you gave the animals the order to find the anthro immediately after finding and capturing humans.)
