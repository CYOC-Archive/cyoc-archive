    Dan meanwhile is still mad at his sister for transforming him into such a tiny creature, ‘god, if only I knew how to fly,’ the boy-turned-fairy thinks to himself, ‘I’d be able to escape the second my psycho sis or someone opens this bloody jar I am trapped in. Also, aren’t fairies supposed to have magical powers? If only I knew how to tap into those powers! I’d show her! Maybe… maybe if I focus long enough on the wings I’ll at least will be able to get control over them!’

    Unaware to Dan, his sister has sent some of her magic towards the jar she had trapped her brother-turned-fairy in, and as Dan concentrates on his wings he suddenly feels them stating to move. At first the boy is surprised that it seemed so easy to get control over his wings, but he simply assumes that it’s due to being a fairy, that it’s just “natural”, he also is suddenly able to feel some sort of energy deep inside of him, and suddenly understands that that energy is his magical core, and he even knows how to tap into it.

    Dan is super excited, unaware that all this is part of Kat’s plan to get rid of her brother, to replace him with Ellie, by having Dan swap bodies with the little girl. And knowing how desperate Dan will be to get his real body back, Kat also knows that Dan will not be thinking rationally: when swapping with Elli he’d lose all his magical powers, and Ellie, who’ll be in HIS transformed body will receive all his magical powers, so the only way for Dan to get his body and life back, would be for him to team up with Ellie and have Ellie somehow convince Kat to transform Dan back. Kat, however, grins, knowing that this will not go the way Dan is thinking, the evil girl grins wider, knowing Dan will be stuck as little weak girl forever.

    The teen-turned-fairy hears the door open and soon finds the jar he is trapped in being grabbed, the little “fairy” scurries into the furthest corner as the evil grin of his sister’s face comes into view, “time for you to meet your new owner, ahaha,” and with that, Dan finds himself thrown into Kat’s handbag.

    Dan feels himself being tossed around as his sister walks hastily, ‘where in the hell is she taking me,’ he asks himself. After a while the tossing suddenly comes to a halt and Dan can her some muffled voices, he then is suddenly blinded as the handbag opens and a large hand grabs the jar.

    “Well, here IT is,” Dan hears his sister say and feels himself, or rather the jar he is trapped in, being handed and grabbed by someone else. When Dan looks he notices the excited and smiling face of a young girl, “well, I better get going. Have fun with your new pet, Ellie,” what Dan doesn’t notice is his sister and the young girl wink at each other, the teen-turned-fairy is to stunned, and as he turns his sister is long gone.

    Dan looks back at the young girl, the girl still grinning, “you are such a cute little thing,” she says, “let’s get you home,” and with that, Dan once again finds himself tossed into a handbag and once again finds himself being tossed around as the young girl hurries home.

    Finally, after what felt like hours to poor Dan, he finds the tossing stopping once again, then the handbag opens and a hand grabs the jar he is in, next thing he knows he finds himself on a desk in a little girl’s room.

    “You are so darn cute,” he then hears a young girl’s voice say, looking up he sees the same girl who had grabbed him from his evil sister, “I thought fairies weren’t real, and yet, here you are: a REAL fairy!”

    Dan groans, ‘kids and their over excitement,’ he thinks to himself, “please… little girl… please let me out,” he then says.

    “Only if you promise not to fly away,” Ellie says.

    Dan grumbles, but then an idea comes to his mind, “okay, fine, I promise,” he says.

    Ellie opens the jar and carefully tilts it to one side and Dan topples onto the desk, the “fairy” gets to its feet and dusts itself off.

    “Thanks, that jar was pretty uncomfortable,” he says, he then looks up at the girl, ‘I got to play this cool. I really don’t want to do this, I don’t want to be a little girl, but this is the only way I’ll get my life back,’ he then thinks to himself, “tell, me, what is your name?”

    “Ellie,” the girl says, “what’s yours,” she then asks, of course she aready knows, but for Kat’s and her plan to work, she has to pretend she doesn’t know who the fairy is.

    Dan is about to say his name, but thinks better of it, “we… fairies don’t have names,” he says.

    “Hmmm… I’ll call to “Zoom” then,” Ellie says, ‘he… I know who you are Dan, though playing this mind game with you sure is fun. Hehe… I can’t WAIT until YOUR body is mine and you’ll be stuck as me…. Hehehe!’

    “Zoom? Really?”

    “Well… I think it’s fitting, you’re probably pretty fast, right?”

    Dan rolls his eyes, ‘typical kid,’ he thinks, “well, better than nothing, I guess,” he then says.

    “Can you do magic,” Ellie then asks, “can you give me wings? I wish I had wings like you! I wanna be able to fly like you!”.

    This is what Dan had been waiting for, he smirks, “sure… I know a spell that will “give” you wings,” he says and taps into his fairy magic and starts concentrating on the spell that would swap their bodies.
