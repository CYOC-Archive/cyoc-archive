Kylie said, "No later than 10." It was now 7:30. "OK, that's good," he said. Jamie then stepped up and said, "Mr. and Mrs. Perkins, Kylie asked if I could stay over tonight so we can help each other with math. If that's alright with you, of course." Kylie's mother asked, "Are your parents OK with that?" She replied, "Yea they don't mind it's a school night." Kylie's mother said, "OK then. See you later." Kylie said, "I was wondering that myself, Jamie." Once again, Jamie used her skill for rationale to explain herself. "Somebody's going to have to help Kylie get her morning routine down. Were you guys going to volunteer?" They all admitted that Jamie was right. Kylie gave a Jamie a smile of appreciation. Now both of them wondered what was going on between them.

Miranda never realized that she was going to be dumped by that guy anyway. She chose to blame it on Jennifer. In that sense, Miranda and Jennifer were eerily similar to each other, kind of like Hitler's Germany and Stalin's Russia: Very close in spirit to one another, but too much to be anything but enemies.

Then we'll discuss this more." Trish had a knack for keeping Jennifer level headed. She did calm down. She talked at Kylie, sitting in the backseat.
