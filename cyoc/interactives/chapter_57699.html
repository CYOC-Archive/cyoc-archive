    Linus Sure looked at where his hands used to be. He didn't feel sadness or dread, only a
    powerful frustration! All his hard work, all his experience, much of it rendered useless! 'I
    almost might as well have remained a naked armless woman statue!' Linus Sure thought of
    embracing that suicide, of embracing that simple, blissful existence of being just artwork
    to be simply admired and looked at, no other purpose. But his sense of duty would not
    allow that, he would not be a muted nothing! And he would not grant these witches
    another mark on their trophy scores!

    Linus Sure looked at the chairs, nicknacks, pitchers, art, and other things in the witches'
    house hold... and wonder how many of them had been people, reduced to nothing more
    than material objects that happened to have souls with no clue they had been anything
    different or still remembered and were silently screaming. One was murder, one was Hell.

    He looked at a smiling monkey statue, and wondered if it was screaming inside, had no
    mind, or thinking repetitive perky tripe. And the soul of the grandmother's killer continued
    to experience what could be for him best described as Heaven.

    'I only worry those fairies aren't going to be unbiased as they say and are going to declare
    the witches not guilty out of loyalty for being their creations or sympathy for being
    creatures of magic as well.'

    In their privacy as the jury discussed, Hermia the fairy thought to ask, "Do you think it's
    true? That were once humans?"

    Gruoch the fairy answered first, and the others consented, "It doesn't matter. This is the life
    I have now. This is the life I remember. This is the life I am living. And this is the only life I
    want. If what the human said is true I don't know if my life is better or worse than the one I
    had before if what the human said was true. We do not know if it is true or not. Do any of
    you want to be human?"

    They all shook their heads.

    "Neither do I. We are with our families, that is what matters... and it does NOT relate to
    weather the witches are guilty or innocent of what they have been charged with."

    Hermia nodded as did the other fairies.

    Sister Kathrine looked in a mirror. And remembered her short time before Maria had struck
    down the witches' power and restored her, Draco, and Linus to normal from what they had
    been while they bought time. Sister Kathrine shivered remembering the sweet purity and
    innocence of being a child again among the familiars, of seeing the world in much simpler
    and peaceful terms. She could be perky and happy and not be pretending. She could have
    friends who didn't judge her.

    How easy it would be to just have accepted that paradise and let everything else go. 'I
    wonder if that is a form of paradise... but if it is... the witches aren't the ones to bring it
    about... ' Sister Kathrine thought of those the witches had made into minions, protectors,
    slaves, objects, all in the name of tearing down oppression and bringing them happiness...
    'They aren't the ones to bring it about... I doubt any mortal is the one to bring it about...
    only the Lord is the one fit to bring it about.'

    Father Draco Fire sitting with Marie Cruz unlike Linus and Kathrine shivered with absolute
    disgust at the memory of being made a feral dragon with the attitude and brain of a puppy!
    Reduced to the mentality of a loyal pet! It disgusted him, being reduced to nothing more
    than animal! The witches had given him a DISEASE among dragons! The dragons of legend
    who never stopped growing, went on all four like the animals they could no better reason
    as, and sought the flesh of that of Man. Yes, there was a very good reason like Father
    Draco Fire had no desire now or ever to discuss what the familiar had turned him into.

    'Forced Salvation is no Salvation at all... ' Was all he thought of the witches her wish to be
    queens of the world even if in their madness they didn't recognize that was precisely
    they'd become even as they shouted they wanted only a world that was flawlessly fair and
    perfectly equal.

    Sister Marie Cruz merely looked at the Witch Pride, Child familiars, and the human
    retainers who served the witches, most as far as they were concerned of their own free will.
    Their unconscious bodies organized and categorized as best they could be. 'Will killing the
    witches really wake them up? Or will that kill them utterly? Are we just telling ourselves
    they'll wake up so we don't have to think that killing them wouldn't have any price that will
    be paid by anyone but them? Will killing the three of them just doom their creations to
    sleeping forever? Are the witches the only ones who can actually wake them up? ... I'm
    sorry you had to become a killer like me Kathrine, I'm just so sorry... ' Marie Cruz looked at
    her hands, clean, but with little scars, she thought of the criminals, spies, assassins,
    monsters, horrors, abominations, and other dangerous things that went bump in the night
    she had killed over the years and how many had ben before being made pawns had been
    innocent people who had been in the wrong place at the wrong time... Maybe at the end,
    I'm a thing of the dark that belongs stalking the shadows as much as they do... '

    Dr. Azrael merely stood observing. Of course he was several places at once, in fact, he was
    many, many place at once, whenever a creature that had a soul, sapient or not, he came
    when their fate had run it's course. The world didn't stop for this trial. A war was still be
    waged. People were still dying, killing each other, some calling out for him to end their
    misery, others screaming for him to visit their enemies so they themselves might go home
    and return to their loved ones. These three witches of course would face a completely
    different judgement when the time came for their death. And that judgement would be
    final and decisive, each of them individually judged.

    "What shall we do with their familiars?" Father Draco Fire asked Linus and Marie even as the
    two were on opposite sides in the current decision.

    "Nothing." Marie Cruz said simply, "With the witches dead, their familiars will have no
    power. A familiar's power come from their master. They'll be but stupid, mindless,
    mundane beasts again."

    Linus looked at Marie Cruz, rather vexed she'd over speak him when his experience in
    such matters out stripped hers.

    Father Draco Fire asked, "Then why did you go to all the trouble of talking of the children?"

    Marie Cruz, "Because from what I understand, you aren't supposed to use something that's
    already a person, already self aware, and not a dumb animal, as a familiar, otherwise they
    develop and grow magic on their own... or that's what I've been able to dredge up."

    Linus said nothing for the moment.

    and we fell to a mere human with bones stolen from our own grand mother... it's not... it's
    not... IT'S-NOT-FAIR!!!!'

    Golgtha thought of what little her sixth sense could tell her through the fog of whatever it
