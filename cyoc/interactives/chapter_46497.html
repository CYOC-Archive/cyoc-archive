    "The dream you had is like a Lucid Dream" Dawn said speaking in a soft voice. "This type of dream is something more. We call it Dream Weaving."

    "What's that?" you asked not sure where this was going.

    "Dream Weaving is controlling your dreams and making changes in the dream that also happen in waking life" Dawn explained. "We call ourselves, the ones that can weave dreams, Dream Weavers. Everyone in this sorority is a Dream Weaver."

    "Everyone?" you asked starting to think you had a false awakening and that you were still dreaming.

    "Yes everyone" Dawn said nodding. "That includes you."

    "But... but... but I can't be a Dream Weaver" you stammer. "I don't control my dreams."

    Dawn smiled and said "Control comes with practice." She then frowns and gets a sad puppy dog look in her eyes as she asks "You don't remember the first dream you met me in do you?"

    You shake your head and say "I don't usually remember my dreams."

    Dawn nodded saying "You did. Now the sorority sisters her use the dream version of the house as a private place. There are wards put up so the only way someone can come in through a dream is to be invited or be a Dream Weaver. Since I was in the house and you came in without being invited I knew you were a Dream Weaver. We introduced ourselves and I told you that I would have to talk with my sisters. We decided last night to accept you into the sorority."

    "This is unbelievable" you say looking back down at your body. Did you really want to join this sorority?

    Dawn laid back down saying "Believe it. You are now a nineteen year old girl going to college."

    You laid back down too though a bit more heavily than Dawn saying "This is a bit much. I don't know if I'll be able to get my course work done."

    Dawn giggled and said "One of the advantages of being a Dream Weaver is that you can do work in your sleep."

    "Why haven't I heard of Dream Weavers before now?" you asked thinking that people with such power would be able to cure all the world's woes.

    "There are only fifty in the whole world now" Dawn said. "Seven of them being in this sorority. Before you ask you are the seventh one in this sorority and the fiftieth in the world."

    "Why don't the Dream Weavers fix the world's woes?" you asked. "I'll certainly be doing that. Perhaps turn certain people into pigs or maybe trash."

    "It doesn't work like that" Dawn said with a heaviness in her voice. "Our power has limits. We can only change a persons age, sex, or anything about their body. We can also swap two people with each other. We can't turn them into animals or plants or inanimate objects. We can't change anybody mentally. Then there's the fact that the world's woes are really big."

    "But reality does change through our dreams, right?" you asked uncertain if you wanted to know the answer.

    "It does change" Dawn confirmed. "Though we can not control exactly how it changes. Since we can't change someone's mind the changes are what they would make in that body. Take you for example. You came wanting to join the sorority and go to college. Now reality has changed so you are though the courses your taking are the courses you chose. We didn't choose them for you."

    "I see" you said. "How long will it take me to learn to control my dreams?"

    "Depends" Dawn said. "Some are able to learn to control their dreams in days where others take weeks or even months to learn." She rolled over on her side and kissed you. Pulling back and smiling she asked "So should we get up or stay in bed? We still need to go get you some clothes so maybe we should get up and go shopping after taking a shower of course."
