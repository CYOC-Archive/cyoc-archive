    Marge frowned at the mighty river blocking her path. Its dark currents swirled treacherously, and she could barely see the opposite shore. Even for a powerful Amazon warrior like herself, this was impassable. If only she could turn Robin into a gryphon and ride him across, she thought as she scowled at her submissive squire, who had collapsed to the ground panting hard from overexertion.

    Further up the shore she could see a bridge, but it was guarded by a forbidding stone fortress. The last thing she wanted was another confrontation with whatever was in there, but she knew she was being pursued by a mass of wizards and warriors, and there was no other way across. Sighing with resignation, she headed towards the bridge. With luck, it would just be a garrison of muscle heads, soon to experience the simple joys of prepubescent girlhood, she smiled as she fingered her transformation stone.

    Trying her best to slip past unnoticed (a plainly unrealistic goal for a hulking Amazon), she soon heard shouts and the noisy clatter of armor. Readying her stone, she turned to face the small group of heavily armed troopers approaching her. A tall, imposing man wearing rich velvet robes and a fur-lined coat stepped out from behind.

    “I see that you come from Lavenguard. Did you by chance spot my son Henric? He set out weeks ago, and I haven’t heard anything from him since.” the nobleman asked in an imperious tone.

    “Henric?” Marge blanked for a moment. Oh yes, wasn’t that the arrogant young nobleman who had taken every opportunity to grope her butt and chest? She had been especially pleased to hear that Robin had made his stableboy-wizard change him into a Clydesdale stallion.

    “Never heard of him.” she replied unconvincingly.

    “You’re lying.” The nobleman’s eyes turned cold and flinty. “I’m going to keep you prisoner here until you tell me what happened to my son!”

    “I don’t think so.” Marge clasped her rock. The nobleman’s fine clothes vanished, replaced by a tattered loincloth. His powerful, muscular body lost its tone and definition as he started to shrink, his limbs becoming lanky and thin. The deep chestnut hair on his head turned light and sandy, his beard vanished and his body fur receded, making him smooth all over. Within moments, he was just a pretty boy in his late teens. Scampering forward, he prostrated himself before the Amazon and started kissing her feet.

    With a loud cry, the troopers charged, but a chill wind abruptly blew out of nowhere, paralyzing them. A raven spread its wings and swooped down from the parapets above, morphing into an elegant enchantress, with emerald eyes and high cheekbones, clad in a black iridescent gown.
