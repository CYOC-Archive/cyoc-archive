    Dixie A. Fox moaned softly. Her son shouted again, "Ma, where's my lucky shirt?"

    She looked at the ruined shirt and sighed. She would have to tell him that she had been cleaning it with bleach, and gotten distracted by a phone call, and it had pretty much dissolved when she remembered it 6 hours later. She could already hear him moaning. Unless? She looked at the "lucky" rabbit's foot hanging on the wall. It wasn't it a monkey's paw, but if there was an identical shirt out there, it would end up here - she hoped. Dixie reached up and squeezed the rabbit's foot.

    "Ethan's Lucky T-shirt?" she added, "Please."

    At that very moment Kyle fell to the floor as a t-shirt. When he faded out of existence at the school, he appeared on the ceiling of the Fox house. Dixie caught him before he hit the ground. She stared at the shirt in amazement, and back at the rabbit's foot. She turned the shirt over to make sure it looked right. It was a brand new version of Ethan's old shirt. But she doubted he'd notice.

    She gently patted the paw in thanks. It had been a full moon when that nasty rabbit-man had tried to steal her vegetables. He had gotten away, but she had managed to get one of its feet.

    "Ma!" Ethan said storming down the stairs, "I need my shirt!"

    "Here you go, Ethan," she said handing him the freshly folded Kyle. Who appeared wearing only board shorts, and the first thing Kyle noticed was how hairy Ethan's legs were.

    A furry, clawed hand snatched him from the woman. He nearly freaked. Ethan wasn't human, but he was humanoid. If he waxed his white hairy chest, and brown hairy sides, shoulders, and arms he might- nah. The pointed ears, snout and bushy tail screamed fox. He was some kind of anthropomorphic fox. He pulled Kyle over his foxy head.

    "Well, you can't go outside like that," his mother enjoined as he headed for the door.

    "Huh?" he asked then he noticed his hand, "Oh, sorry nearly forgot."

    Dixie shook her head. Ethan stepped in front of the hall mirror, and stared at his reflection and concentrated. Kyle would have let out a yelp, if he could. The snout and ears shrank into the head, and the fur was absorbed into human skin. He recognized the young man wearing him. Ethan D. Fox had gone to Kyle's school. He was a few years older, and had gone off to college last year.

    "Better?" Ethan asked.

    "Comb your hair," his mother added.

    Ethan sighed, combed his hair. Put on his socks and shoes, grabbed his board and headed out to skate with his buds.

    Peter C. Tayle glared at the Fox house from his upstairs window down and across the street.

    "They're using my luck again," he grumbed.

    "Well, it's not worth losing another foot or worse, Petey," said his mother.

    "There's got to be a way to make my luck work for me and against them!" he shouted.

    His mother was quiet for a moment, and then said, "Well, there may be a way, but it may be too dangerous."

    "Tell me."

    "There are actually three ways that I know of, and each is riskier than the last. The first is probably the easiest but the riskiest. There's this curse going around town that's called Transform or Dare, but you might end up a furry pillow or worse."

    "Transform or dare? C'mon that's an urban myth."

    "Jes' like were-rabbits and were-foxes?" his mother sighed.

    Petey paid more attention.

    "The second is the universal spell buster. You need a first class witch or wizard to cast that, and as luck would have it there are several in town. But that spell may do more than simply make your missing hand unlucky, it might turn it back into a human hand. It might trap the were-vulpines in whatever form they are in when the spell is cast- human or foxman. If you're too close it might effect you too."

    "And the third?" he asked staring at his prosthetic hand.

    "Oh, that's not an option. It's too dangerous. Shouldn't have mentioned it at all. Now forget about the curse or witches and wizards, and come eat your carrot stew with pine nut pasta."

    "Witches and wizards, hm?" Petey said to himself.

    In the meantime, Ethan was boarding at the Reservoir with his buds. He had foxed out again, because well, he was with his own kind. Bruno the werebear and Pepe the were -skunk were in anthroform, while Kasem the were-snake was in human form (because anthrosnakes weren't built for skateboards).
