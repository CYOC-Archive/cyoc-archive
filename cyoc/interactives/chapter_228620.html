    As the months go by, Jeff starts to feel more and more content and happy, even proud, ‘I wonder what kind of Oak tree I’m becoming,’ he thinks to himself.

    The months turn into years, and by the time the “tree” is around five or six-years-old, Jeff knows what type of Oak he’s becoming, ‘I’m White Oak,’ he exclaims, ‘cool! White Oak is considered the most expensive and is even one of the most desired hard woods out there which is suitable for building furniture, flooring, even houses and cabins! I wonder if I’ll be manufactured into something!’

    By now Jeff has forgotten of ever being a human, he sees himself as a normal Oak tree, so he doesn’t worry about being cut down and made into something, after all: THAT would be totally normal!

    The years come and go, over the years various types of animals, ranging from bugs, to bird to Squirrels and other mammals “move in” to the “tree” and Jeff enjoys the company, even if he is unable to communicate with his inhabitants, at least he didn’t feel lonely.

    When Jeff finally reaches the mature age of 50 years, and feels HIS Acorns finally grow from him, the former human feels so proud of himself, Jeff by now is fully grown and is huge: 100 feet in height and 90 feet in width.

    ‘I feel so strong and powerful,’ he thinks to himself, ‘I’m the tallest tree in this area! My trunk and branches are beautiful and straight, my bark and leaves are perfect! I am one hell of a majestic White Oak! It would be a waste to NOT manufacture me into something, my wood is the BEST! I hope soon someone will put me to good use!’
