Mostly instinct, though, which Paul had managed to keep under control. In nature, the orangutan had no contact with any species of lion. The Asiatic Lion was limited only to a small forest in western India, while the pride seemed to be based off of the African Lion which largely only lived south of the Sahara Desert. He guessed though that their cat-like forms did remind him of a predator that the orangutan did instinctively know and occasionally fell victim to... the Sumatran Tiger.

    "Ecckksss...uuueess meee," Paul spoke clearly to draw their attention.

    "What is that?" Bernie asked as she approached and sniffed at the vials.

    Paul swatted her nose away, just to make sure she didn't blow a vial of the table, ruining Serenity's hard work. He then began signing, which most of the pride could understand. Those that couldn't received a translation from those that could.

    "These vials contain a powder that channels white magic," Paul signed, "the counter to the magic the witches used to curse Glendale, and dates back to Merlin Ambrosius. It can and will kill any turn of the Game, and is how my brother and I killed the turns forced on us."

    "It can cure Frank and Daryl?" Bobbi asked, looking interested.

    "No," Paul signed, "It does not affect your form, and wouldn't affect your "true love" powers or the extra abilities given to specific members of Jensen's pack. It would only remove their turns of the Game. Once they are freed from their brainwashing, you are to use the powder on them. Their turns will be dead and gone, and with their minds free, it is unlikely that those in the pack that have extra abilities like yours, with the exception of the Hardys themselves, will ever use them as most intelligent people prefer to remain human."

    "So do most of us," Bobbi spoke, "and I know I want to go back to being human when this is over, no offense."

    "Unfortunately, that is impossible, as I have seen the transform the Hardys gave you," Paul signed, "you CAN NOT be turned back, which is also why you will be expected to take the cure once Jensen and his pack are freed from the curse's grip to insure that the Curse travels no further."

    "We can't..." Bobbi gasped, her hopes at being Bob again dashed.

    "There's nothing wrong with what we are," Wanda commented, "we all chose this... and we don't need the game to make more lions, and when we do, and when our children do, all of them will be willing transformations, unlike Jensen which isn't giving anyone any choice."

    "The curse is the curse," Bobbi argued, "It doesn't MATTER if this was our choice or not. It's like trying to say suicide is better then murder because the victim was also the killer. The fact of the matter is that the victim is still DEAD."

    "Jensen might have tried to transform or dare you into something else," Brian spoke, "that's why we had to lock your forms forever. That way we will win without fear of them using the game against us."

    "You could have at least explained what you planned to do when you started this," Bobbi spoke, sounding like a person who was trying to advise a lifelong friend, "or let us help you word out the transform so that those of us who DON'T want to be lions forever could have a chance to being what we were before the game hit us. I wanted to be who I was before I was turned into Bobbi... a human male. I don't blame you, as I think you were trying to be helpful, but there is such a thing as too much help."

    "I'm sorry," Brian and Felicity admitted, "we didn't want them to do anything to you... but then the wolves forms are supposedly locked too, yet we broke Ashley's brainwashing, maybe we can figure a way around this "lock"."

    "Brian, Serenity's been trying to become who she was before she became Madame Serenity for practically a hundred years," Bobbi told him, "when a form is locked, it is locked. You might be able to alter some small things, largely personality, but their physical form can not be altered in ANY way. If there were a way around a lock, Serenity would have found it by now."

    "I'm sorry," Brian told the other lioness, "Maybe we weren't thinking."

    "It's alright," Bobbi sighed after a moment, "I got used to being Bobbi... I guess I can get used to being furry and nine feet tall..."

    "How does the cure work?" Wanda asked Paul, trying to get things back on track. She privately loved her new form, and would never give it up, but did agree that the possession of a turn would be too tempting, and as earlier presented, the pride didn't need the game to transform willing pride mates.

    "Once the wolves are freed of their brainwashing, and your need of turns is over," Paul signed, "You are to circle one vial's worth of powder around one individual. Once the circle is drawn, you must speak the words "in the name of the Divine Lord, purge this curse forever more", but in Latin, of course. Serenity has the translation in her bag for you to look once we've captured the wolves. Although it has only one use per person. Which means the process must be repeated for each turn holder."

    "Will it work?" Bobbi asked.

    "It worked for my brother and I," Paul signed.

    He was about to sign more when the door opened and a uniformed officer came in.

    "You might want to get down to the Gallery," the officer spoke, "a call just came in from a Armisael, said that Penny got jumped there and can't move very well."

    Paul and the discussion over the transform the Hardys used to transform the pride was forgotten as they all rushed out, the urge to protect a pride member too strong. As they left, only Mary was left alone with Paul and Trevor. Neither of them knew sign language, but Mary got the clue from the discussion that Paul had signed something that went against her father's idea.

    "Why did you sign that?" Mary growled at the ape, "Daddy made everyone better."

    Paul found a pen and paper and carefully began to write his answer.

    "Maybe, but he took no account of what his pride wanted," Paul wrote, "he simply wanted a pride, since you have thrown your humanity away, which is likely what the curse wants..."

    "But I wanted to because of..."

    "That wolf boy has NOTHING to do with what the Curse wants," Paul wrote, "This Uly may be your excuse, but ultimately the decision is the curse's because that way it can survive and spread and ruin lives. And it does not care if you are civilized or not. Think of it like this, before you were cursed, would you have wanted to be a lion forever?"

    "No."

    "So why is it that a wolf is responsible for you wanting to retain your own cursed form?" Paul questioned, "that you wanted your parents to be like you, and that you are defending the permanent transformation of twelve officers of the law into lions themselves? How is one wolf responsible for that? How is he able to affect your mind? Your decision came from the Curse's influence. This Uly was only your rationalization for that decision."

    Mary didn't say anything.

    "For my brother and I, for Serenity, the real enemy in this saga is not Jensen or his Alpha Female, but the Curse itself," Paul wrote, "Your father and the pride are so focused on Jensen that they do not see the REAL enemy. Jensen is a victim, being influenced by the game, just as you are, and as your pride is. The only way to weaken it is to kill the turns both your pride and Jensen's pack hold. Which is why I signed what I signed. If I didn't, under the Curse's influence, they would never use it on themselves. The Curse would urge them to keep their turns as a sort of backup. This game has to stop Mary, and the only way is to kill the turns... and the only way that can easily be done is if we are all truly honest with one another, regardless of the consequences. There is NO such thing as a good lie. And it isn't Christian."
