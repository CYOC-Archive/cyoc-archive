    Dressed like normal, Federal Bureau of Investigation agents (although they were far from anything that a regular human would consider "normal") the men of the Luna Brotherhood led their prisoners out of Betty Van Rosenberg's residence and toward their waiting vehicles, a pair of nondescript vans with tinted windows to prevent anyone from seeing within them.
    "Listen fellows," began Alyssa Cooper in a low voice. "Please. This is important. I honestly didn't know Julie Stovall was an escaped prisoner fleeing Moon Lake on Statute 1A charges. I saw her walking down the road hitchhiking on my way to the Seattle Airport and, since I do know her, offered her a ride. But she only told me that she was running away from home. I knew absolutely nothing about her escape, let alone the fact she might have killed someone doing so."
    If any of the Luna Brotherhood was listening, let alone even cared, they gave no sign of it as they drew near their vehicles.
    "Please. I have to be back at work in California by Monday morning, and very few people down there know I'm a were-mermaid," added Alyssa.
    The procession didn't stop until they reached their vans, but at least one among them had been listening.
    "Beta Two," said a man, standing near Julie Stovall.
    "Yes Beta One*," replied the one called.
    "I shall radio ahead to notify the Council, but take Prisoner Cooper directly back to the Moon Lake Elders', for her situation needs to be expedited if she told the truth about her living status."
    "I did," swore Alyssa. "Does this mean I'm free to go?"
    "No. Only that under the circumstances, you shall face justice first," snarled Beta One. Then he turned back to face Beta Two. "Tell our Alpha and the Elders that we are following procedures with Prisoner Stovall and shall be back by dark."
    Beta Two simply nodded, knowing what that meant, but unhappy that he wouldn't be part of the proceedings.
    "Yes sir," replied Beta Two, before leading Cooper and his team away to the other vehicle.
    "Err... what happens to me?" asked Julie Stovall, who until now had remained silent.
    Although still in their human forms, every werewolf present could smell the prisoner's fear and the fact that she had already soiled herself at least one since being led outside.
    "We shall cover our tracks and make sure you get what's coming to you," said Beta One with a smile, as Julie started wetting herself again.
