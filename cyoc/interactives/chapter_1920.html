    Ever Lost a Boyfriend at a Party?

    "Bob!" I said in a surprised tone as I
    entered the bathroom. "What's going on?"

    Bob, leaning on a mop, looked extremely
    irritated. "Some jerk drank too much, got
    sick and threw up all over the bathroom," Bob
    said. "I've got a house full of guests, and
    this guy tries to take out the only bathroom
    on this floor with a bio-hazard attack!"
    I moved closer to the toilet and stared down
    at the tile floor. I didn't see any sign of
    Matthew.

    "Are you looking for something?" Bob asked
    me.

    "No," I lied. "Who was the guy?"

    "What guy?"

    "The drunk guy?"

    "Oh, I don't know. I don't know half the
    guests. I sent him off with a friend. I think
    they were going to call him a cab."

    Bob, having finished cleaning, started to
    leave. I stopped him by asking if he had seen
    Doug.

    "Your ex?"

    "Yeah, have you seen him?"

    Bob gave me a disapproving look. "Baby, just
    stay as far from him as you can," Bob said.
    "Trust me. He's no good for you."

    "I know that," I said, somewhat irritated by
    Bob's lecture. "But I really need to speak
    with him. If it wasn't important, I wouldn't
    be seeking him out."

    "Well, so you say," Bob replied. "But, to be
    honest, I haven't seen him."

    Great! So, not only had I lost Matthew, my
    current boyfriend, I couldn't find my old
    boyfriend to make him restore Matthew.

    "Well, back to the party," Bob said.

    "I'll be along," I said. "Got to use the
    facilities."

    After Bob left, I gave the bathroom a
    thorough search. I found no trace of Matthew.
    I began to get worried.

    Had he fled in the face of the onslaught from
    a drunken giant? If so, where should I look
    for him?

    And, to increase my unease, I knew that
    somewhere out in the commotion of Bob's
    party, Doug was still on the loose.

    What do you do now?
