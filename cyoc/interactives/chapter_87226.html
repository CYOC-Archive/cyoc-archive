    "Or how about we make high school easier for Jered?" Jeff suggested.

    "You mean, other than by making him straight?" Riley asked.

    Jeff shook his head again, annoyed. "Dude, that wouldn't be fair for him."

    "We can make it so he doesn't know," Riley argued.

    "But does he deserve it?" Jeff asked. "No. My Chronivac, my rules. Okay?"

    "Fine." Riley conceded. "What did you have in mind?"

    "Well, didn't you say last week that you wanted to get back at Tony for being a prick to you?"

    "Are you thinking of making my older brother gay?"

    "Yep, and be Jered's boyfriend. Think about it: Tony is a popular senior and a big linebacker. If he is Jered's boyfriend, then Jered will probably enjoy high school more. Plus, no one would dare to try teasing them for being gay 'cuz your brother would beat them to a pulp!"

    "But that popularity thing could go away."

    "Not if we lock that popularity status on Tony. The only thing we change is his orientation and who he's dating."

    "And how would that help me?" Riley asked.

    "Your brother would know what it is like to deal with intolerant people. There will be a greater chance that he won't treat you like crap," Jeff answered.

    Riley sighed and shrugged. "Fine," he said. "If it doesn't work, then we can always change him further."
