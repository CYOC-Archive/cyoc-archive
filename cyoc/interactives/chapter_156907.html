    Probably Just Tired

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added by JayeKayes 3 years ago A BM

    With more certainty than she'd ever known anything in her life, Jeanne knew she was pregnant. She had no idea how--she hadn't been with anyone--ever, in fact. Still, as she lovingly cradled her slightly bulging belly, her thoughts wandered back to the birthday card. "Consider your wish granted," it had said. What had she wished for?

    Something exciting and unique to happen to her, something to help her not be so lonely, something to add some variety into her boring life.

    Well, this would qualify, she supposed. She knew she should be afraid, but something about the burgeoning life inside her made her calm, filled her with warmth and love. She smiled. This was good.

    Untucking her blouse so it would cover where her pencil skirt would no longer fasten--though her shirt, too, pulled tight against her new roundness--Jeanne exited the stall, but her reflection again caught her attention in the mirror. Peering more closely, she inspected her face. Her eyes looked like they were practically buried in dark circles. "I'm probably just tired," Jeanne thought, though as she lifted a hand to her face she immediately dismissed that thought.

    The dark circles were a light coating of hair that was growing from her face, extending from her hairline and, as she watched, sprouting down her cheeks as well, leaving only the center of her face uncovered. "Well. This is certainly unexpected," Jeanne thought as she touched the black hair. In the center, the skin that had only moments ago been bare was now filling in with a light dusting of white hair that extended down her throat.

    Heading back into the stall, Jeanne saw that the white hair kept on going down onto her chest. She was aware of a strange feeling on her back and down her legs, and knew hair was growing there as well. Grasping her belly, Jeanne ran out of the bathroom and to her boss.

    "Gotta go, not feeling well, call in tomorrow," she said as she grabbed her things and ran out the door, not wanting anyone to see her.
