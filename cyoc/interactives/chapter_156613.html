The zapped girls seemed to survive entirely on sweets, especially Leyla; it seemed that she ate ice cream for every single meal.     "11. Why?"

    Miki stepped into the room, assaulted by the amount of people, sound, and color. Just being in the room felt like a panic attack.

    Miss Erlkin stood at the front of the classroom, and shouted "attention", followed up with an "in formation." Quickly, all 5 of the children sat down in a grid pattern on the carpet, and said their names one by one. This wasn't mind control - she was just a really good teacher. She had the children sing a short song, and then went over the schedule - theater until 9, followed up with arts and craft, and then P.E. at 10.

    "Now, has anyone here heard of Revolutionary Girl Kahihen?" The kids gave out a resounding meh, but Miki perked up. "You might not have seen it, but a lot of the older kids here have. That's why we're doing a production of it!"

    "Erlkin-sama?" A yellow haired girl said without raising her hand. "What's Kahehe about?"

    "Thank you for the question Zitrona. Kahihen is the story of a girl who's in love with another girl, and has to keep winning swordfights to keep her safe. Now, who wants to be the main girl?" About a dozen of hands went up. "Well, it seems we'll have to do auditions!"

    Kihihen wasn't really a story you can adapt for a one act play with first graders, but Miki had to applaud the effort.

    Erlkin went on. "Now, we have to get this ready in time for the school festival... That's only four months away, and there'll be people from all over the area here, not just your classmates. So, I hope we're all gonna put on a great show!"

    The children all cheered. Even if the show wasn't gonna be great, they'd all at least have fun with it.

    In P.E., the kids were all playing a game of "magic missile", which just dodgeball with a different name. This gave the teachers and Miki the opportunity to talk, sitting at the back of the room. Miki couldn't get their eyes off of the P.E. instructor, she was just adorable. Her dark blue hair was done up in little spirals, and she was wearing a cute sports bra... She also happened to be the first magical girl that was taller than Miki, she must've been 5'2"...

    "Can you stop staring, dude? It's kind of freaky." Miki was pulled aback. Had they really been staring that long?

    "I'm sorry, it's just that your outfit's really cute."

    "Yours too. I really love your hair -"

    "Thanks I, uh, shaved it the other day. Impulsively, y'know"

    "Yeah. Y'know, I'm not allowed to see my students but.. what's your number?"

    They exchanged numbers before they knew eachother's name. When Miss Bonite heard the name Miki, she fell down laughing.

    "What is it?"

    "You're in my class, next period." She tried to regain her composure, before getting up and addressing the children. Erlkin started glaring at Miki.

    "Why do you look so mad? Were you interested too?"

    "You know she's taking advantage of you, right?"

    "If I get to go out with a hot girl, who's loss is it?"

    "If I hear word of this going any further, I'm calling the dean."

    "Suit yourself."

    Miss Bonite came back, smile gleaming. She was downright excited to hang out with Miki.

    A new semester meant new classes, and Tuesdays and Thursdays, Miki now had Drill with Miss Bonite, learning the basics of close quarters combat. Draw, on guard, stab, slice, sheath. After that, who knows what could happen. Draw, on guard, stab, slice, she-.

Miki had remembered a lecture they'd been in months ago - Androjuv's process will sometimes accelerate under "stressful or overtly adult situations"; Miki thought this would happen over the course of weeks, not moments.

They tried the drill again - draw, on guard, stab, and then stumble. Miki had lost several inches, as well as plenty of muscle mass, and she could barely hold the sword. They kept on straining herself, until she fell to the ground, unconscious.
