    A week after your potential "incident" you had finally arrived to the home of the white Mage. Enven Laxorious you believe his name to be, as you see the small but grand temple that stood proudly before you.

    The stone temple stood around a half circle of large oak trees. It was quite overgrown, vines, moss, and grass nearly covered the bottom portion of the two story building. Despite this, it seemed to have quite a peaceful and mystic aura to it. As if it knew things people from your world would never understand.

    Several violet banners with a symbol of a golden eye with a curvy line going through it decorated the front enterance. Off to the side was a small fenced off garden full with beautiful floors, your nose smelt the calming scent they made.

    You walked up to the woods door and knocked three times. A voice from inside yelled "Coming! Is that you Lex? Have my lavender shipments arrived yet?" You heard the sound of footsteps draw closer to the door. In those few moments, you nearly had a panic attack as you waited outside. Would he kill you as soon as he saw your true face? Would he even let you inside?

    You briefly considered running away until the door squeaked open. Despite you having your hood covering most of your face, you watch as his eyes widen as he looks at you and then turn into a snarl.

    He holds up his hands and a ball of white flame bursts from it, you recoil in pain, as the white light seems to cause you an extordinary amount of pain. You take a couple of steps back as

    "Demon!" he yells. You see him about to throw the ball of white flame towards you, but you quickly scream "Six Petals, Six Petals, Six Petals!" Over and over again.

    A shocked and confused face crosses his face as he hears those words. The white flame dissappears from his hand as his rage turns into concern and confusion.

    You breathe a sigh of relief as the pain disappears along with the white fire.

    Standing in the doorstep the Mage says "Who are you? How did you know those words?" he says, suspicion deep in his eyes.

    "Graven told me to come looks for you, he said you could help me." you say, pulling back you hood and revealing your true face. You see the Mage scan your face, his eyes quickly taking in every detail. His eyes soften and he ushers you in quickly.

    "Very well then, please come inside quickly, we don't want anyone seeing you now do we?"

    You walk in and thank him.

    The inside of the temple was a mixture of a living space and a library. Bookshelves dotted the walls and floor as you walked in. In the middle of the room was a table along with two chairs.

    As you look around at all the books, the Mage says "Go ahead and sit down, I'll make some drinks for us, then we'll have another hopefully more peaceful, chat."

    You sit down and wait. The Mage quickly goes into another room and bring back a plate with two cups and a tea pot. He sets them on the table and after pouring a cup of tea for himself, he says "My name is Enven Laxorious. I'm the local white Mage. May I ask your name?"

    "It's Alfred, though my friends would call me Al. Heaven sent me here because he said you could help me with... this." you say motioning to your body. "I used to be human; but not anymore."

    At this Enven perks up, he takes of sip of tea and says "Human? Interesting, only a incredibly complex blood ritual would be able to transform a human into a lust demon. How did this happen?" he says.

    You tell Enven the entire story. From how you first came to how you met Graven. You even embarrassingly told him about your encounter with your penis. He doesn't seem to mind though as he patiently listens. When you completely finish, he speaks.

    "That my friend, is quite a unique and fascinating story. Heck, I could probably believe as a novel. Here, let me explain everything I can. However there is some bad news with it.

    From what you told me, about how everything was at first transparent and ghostly, that is simple. You came from another world. While the common folk are largely unaware, Mages, Clerics, and Sorcerers have all commonly accepted the idea that other worlds exist. They could range from a world with two Suns to a world with a completely different laws of reality and physics. Only the most powerful of mages or gods could ever hope to even travel to other worlds. So, in theory it is possible to send you back to what ever world you came from."

    You smile and perk up at this news, you could go home.

    "However, that is the good news. The reason why you transformed into a demon is completely unknown to me. I've never heard of such an occurrence. Perhaps you might of been cursed by a powerful demonlord as you entered this world.

    You see, when a power hunger fanatic attempts to transform him or herself into a demon, the ritual is complex, but if it were to work there wouldn't really be much of a difference. Magic comes from threesources of energy. Blood which fuels most dark magics, the Mind, and the Soul.

    However most blood mages fail to realize that why their physical body and therefore blood has become demonic, their soul and mind remain the same. This means they lack many of the powers even the weakest of demons have.

    However when I first saw you, my spectral eyes could sense that your mind, body, and soul have all become demonic. Not even a shred of human lies within you. Had only your body been demonized, it would've been simple, however there is no way to Uncorrupt your soul. It seems for you my friend, and it pains me to say this, the only two options are die or be sent sent back to your world as a freak."

    You barely manage to hold back tears as you hear his words. Either die or become some freak show back in your world.

    "Is there no other option? Isn't there even a archangel or something that can cure me?" you say weakly.

    Enven shakes his head. "I'm not sure if it is even possible for a soul to become demonized. It has been unheard of. Even if I were bring you to an angel, despite their kindness and compassion towards humans, their hatred for demons is fiery, they would most likely kill you on sight, even if I were to explain what happened to you. "

    "Then what now?" you ask?
