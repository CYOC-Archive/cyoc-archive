    They Grow Up So Fast

    “I think I’d like a picture of the boys together,” said Becky.

    “Ok,” said Kylie. “Jacob, Henry, could you stand right there? Put an arm around each other, yes, just like that. Now look at me and say cheese!”

    FLASH!

    “Kylie, please tell me you got the picture!” Jacob groaned. As an energetic young teen, he would rather be almost anywhere else. Like in the backyard, throwing a football around with his dad. Or lifting weights in the basement.

    Kylie examined the photo and said, “All right Jacob, you’re free,” she said with a touch of sarcasm and a grin.

    The boy grinned too, and begged Greg, “Dad, can I please go lift weights?”

    “Ok, but only for half an hour. I want to actually see you tonight, son,” Greg told him.

    One could tell that weights and sports were a large part of Jacob’s life simply by looking at him. At only 13, the kid was ripped, with thick chest and arms, visible abs, and full legs. He was good-looking too, with a full head of golden blond hair and blue eyes and a cheeky grin. All of this added up to a boy who had already had a few girlfriends and attracted the attention of many others.

    Jacob ran off, leaving Henry standing there by himself. He walked up to Kylie and rested a hand on her back. The two had been dating for a little while (he was 16 and she was 15), but shy as he was, Henry was unsure of himself every step of the way. But Kylie found him and his demeanor cute and charming in a quiet way.

    And besides, he was handsome too. His smooth, gelled-bangs black hair topped a square-jawed face with stylish glasses over his green eyes. He was tall and lean; he didn’t look athletic, but secretly he did work out. In fact, he had a 6-pack and a decent chest, but his baggy clothes hid it in a way Jacob’s tight clothing never did.

    “Thank you, Kylie,” Becky said with a smile. “It’s always nice to have a decent picture of my boys. Normally I can’t get them to sit still long enough,” she laughed, gently punching Henry’s arm and taking the camera.

    “No problem, Mrs. Baker,” Kylie replied.

    Greg and Becky left the room and Kylie turned back to Henry with a sly smile on her face.

    “Now, where were we?” she asked.

    The two made out, feeling each other up, unnoticed by his parents.

    Where would the camera strike next?
