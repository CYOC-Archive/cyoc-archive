    It had been a long trip with nothing interesting to see, as far as Julie was concerned. She had crossed the border into Merlon with out any trouble, there being no security at them and she had even passed a few small villages with 30 to 40 people in them. They had given her a few odd looks over her clothes and sword but no one troubled her at the inn she had stayed at. Luckily for Julie, the horse's saddlebags had contained a purse with some silver coins in them. They had covered her fees and kept her comfortable that night.

    Julie had asked the locals about the capital and under the guise of worry, asked if there were any brigands on the roads. She was informed that a murderous band of dark wizards was on the prowl and took refuge in the Forest of Shadows. Julie had thanked them before turning in for the night.

    The next morning found her up early and on her horse to arrive at the capital city of Merlon at noon. Magus Cardios was a city, Julie gave them credit for that, but it seemed to be a crowded one from her vantage point on a nearby hill. Looking down on the city she saw white pristine buildings behind a large white wall that circled the city. In the center of all of this was a gothic castle, with a wall surrounding it, that reminded Julie of pictures she'd seen of Notre' Dame. What really drew Julie's interest was the shanties that protruded from the outer wall by a good two miles. Large groups of the poor usually indicated problems with the government's infrastructure.

    Julie saw a lot of hard working people outside the walls, who seemed happy and she wondered what was causing the separation in the classes. When she arrived at the outer wall she got a good hint as there were two lines at the gate, one line showing a stick to the guard who weighed it before giving it back. The other line was a group of people who were showing a paper before going through. Curious, Julie got in the latter line and when she got up to the man he held out his hand,"Entry papers, please."

    Julie shrugged her shoulders,"Fraid I don't have them. I'm from out of town and am delivering a message to the King and Queen of the realm."

    The guard shook his head, "I'm sorry ma'am but no one may enter the city without papers or a wand examination."

    "I don't have a wand though."

    "Then your out of luck. Orders are clear about this. No one may enter the city without magic for their own protection unless they have been given special exemption."

Get lost, before I put your insides on the outside." Julie stepped back from the sheer menace in the man's voice and her hand went to her sword. His face extended out into a muzzle and his eyes turned golden. Black fur that was graying at the tips sprouted all over his body and his mouth filled with fangs. "How kind of your master to surrender. How about you just tell me and I'll pass along the word?" The werewolf sniffed disdainfully at her but something in her scent made him freeze.
