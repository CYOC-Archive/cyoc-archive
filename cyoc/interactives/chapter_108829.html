    “Yuck it up why don’t you,” Alyssa sneering; “you going to help me or not?”

    Back in Judge Flagg’s court room; Alyssa was sitting in the defense chair while her former fiancé argued to have the charges dropped; looking sullen for she had no choice but to agree to her former fiancé’s demands.
    'I want your share in the cost of the wedding that had to be cancelled, plus the engagement ring. Also I never want to hear from you again, save for your repayment.'

    “Your honor, how can my client be charged with breaking and entering when she knew where the key for the front door was?”
    “Your client did not have permission to be at Sheriff Coopers property;” the judge answers.
    “Did the sheriff have permission,” Jimmy retorting; “to deny my client food while awaiting for her bail hearing today? Must I bring up the cruel and unusual punishment argument? When I visited my client an hour ago all she had to eat was a slice of processed cheese between two slices of bread. Inmates in the cruelest prison around the world are fed better than that!”

    Twenty minutes later, Alyssa walks out of the courthouse a free woman; despite getting off on the charges thanks to her cousin’s forgetfulness in breakfast that isn’t just coffee and aspirin, Alyssa is about to head off when she sees her cousin waiting for her at the street.
    “No more free passes cousin,” Sheriff Cooper looking at Alyssa; “Head back to California. Leave town.”
    “Kind of hard to do that,” Alyssa being flippant;” when you impounded my rental and put my purse and other essentials in one of your evidence boxes.”

    Sheriff Cooper reaches into his cruiser and tosses Alyssa’s purse back at her. She opens it up to see her keys, wallet, phone and other items, woman usually carry. Looking up to stare at her cousin; “and my clothes?”
    “All packed in your suitcase,” Sheriff Cooper answers; “all waiting for you in your rental sitting in the police parking lot.”

    Sheriff Douglas Cooper turns around and heads back in his car. Driving off; he leaves Alyssa to fend for herself.
    “I’m not leaving town until I take care of my business with the Stovall’s and Brian Walker,” Alyssa smirking.

    ‘We need to talk. Meet at same place like on Sunday.’

    ‘My son Brian is unavailable to talk to you. Ever. Contact him again and I’ll have you charged with harassment. Emily’

    Alyssa puts her phone away and begins walking to the police station. ‘I will not be denied;’ is all Alyssa thinks to herself as she makes her lonely trek to her rental.
