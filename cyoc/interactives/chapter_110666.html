The cashier's register made a few buzzing sounds and the man there turned to the register and pulled a small slip of paper off and presented it to Martha. He instructed her to sign one and the other would be for her own records. Martha signed and thanked him before turning to join with Nellie, who had also paid for her items at a different check-out isle. The two met near the doors to the store and began to walk out together.

    "Oh... but it is nice," Nellie smiled as they made their way across the parking lot. "And learning your bi was also a pleasant surprise... very pleasant."

Now, they had the plan to be more than friends, but Nellie had made the mention that Nellie had had those feelings since high school. As they approached a fairly nice looking but slightly older Ford Taurus Martha found she had to ask.

    "Nellie... you said you wanted to be more than friends since high school?" Martha asked as she heard Nellie unlock the doors to the car, allowing her to set the bags in the backseat of the car.

    "Yeah," Nellie said with a weak smile, "You were cute, smart, funny, and fun to be with. In a way, you were someone that I felt I could bond with... And I'll admit that I followed you to college and as an aide in Minnesota in the hopes of being more than friends one day... but at times you were distant and seemingly confused over something, but wouldn't say and then you fell in love and married William... At the time, I didn't want to get in your way. I found John, and we've been and still are very happy together and settled for just being friends."

    The two then moved to sit down in the front seat of the car. As Nellie started the cars engine, Martha leaned across the center armrest between them and kissed Nellie's cheek.

    "As I said earlier... high school and college were fairly rough for me with regards to who I am," Martha said slowly, "My folks and sister all felt being strictly straight was the way to go and constantly talked down about lesbians, gays, or those who were bi... It wasn't hatred, but more like they were disappointed in those who were and my family simply couldn't be bothered to interact with them any more than they might absolutely have to. A live and let live sort of deal, though not necessarily cordial."

    "Ouch," Nellie sighed.

    "And it lead to me repressing that I was bi when I began having thoughts that included women in my fantasies as well as men," Martha sighed, "Now... my love for William is real and since we've become weres... I LOVE having my werefox at night... But as a werecat, I've also realized that I was repressing a part of myself and accepted fully who and what I am. It was rough for me through high school and college... but I'm better now."

Me and my Red Fox and you and your..."

    "Okay now... I texted John while we were in the store and he'll pick up our girls at school on his way home, so we're free for the afternoon," Nellie commented, "All we need now is the directions to where you and William have settled in..."
