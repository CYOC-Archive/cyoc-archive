    Dad continued. "On top of the money we are being paid we will be given a new house that is a lot nicer then what we had before. Your old bodies will be in the neighborhood too, and they will be sending over their clothes to our new place to get you started on your wardrobes. Obviously we will send your clothes to them."

    "Just my clothes right?" I asked. I hated how my voice sounded near tears.

    My mom rubbed my back. "Of course sweety, there is no reason to send over your video games or other things."

    Dad continued as if he was never interrupted. "The neighborhood is a gated community and every one inside will know about the swap. There will be plenty of kids your new ages who are going through the same thing, which should be very helpful with adjusting."

    Dad cleared his throat before continuing. "Outside the neighborhood however, we need to keep our cover story straight. Brad is our 17 year old son who due to amnesia has trouble remembering things. He will also attend a special school to help with those needs." Dad paused. "David will be our youngest daughter and will transfer into 6th grade. A different school than Hailey's of course. Also we should discuss whether or not you want that as your new name"

    I just shrugged.

    "Well, we can just call you Hailey while we get breakfast so no one will question it."

    "I have a question." Lucas spoke up. "Mariah and I didn't swap bodies with anyone. Can we still hang out with our friends or do I have to give them up even though unlike everyone else, I voted against this experiment."

    "You can hang out with them, but they can't come to our new place. It will bring up too many questions of they knew about the old Brad and David."
