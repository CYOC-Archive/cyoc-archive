    Six months ago, she would've written the ad off as some kind of prank... but six months ago, the news hadn't been dominated by stories about superhuman bimbos and magic-slinging special forces units.

    The world had gotten very strange, very fast ever since the recent revelation of magic and the supernatural to the general public. All sorts of people and creatures who'd been forced to hide their paranormal natures had suddenly become a lot more open - and despite the initial shock, the world was adjusting. There weren't any major riots in the street, no organized anti-magical racism yet, no people organizing mass witch hunts or trying to murder werebimbos in the street. No, the Earth had been shocked for about a week - and after that, the supernatural had very quickly become another mundane part of life.

    Brianna thus failed to so much as bat an eye as she read the job listing on her phone. The Guild of Hunters, a group that had been highly secretive a month ago, had suddenly seen itself thrust into the spotlight - because while most magical creatures were integrating into society, there were more than a few who'd taken the lifting of the Veil as an opportunity to strike out and seek power, or just fulfill homicidal urges. They were in need of new recruits to help with their vastly expanded workload... and they were offering enhancements. Codeword for magical transformations, an extremely popular luxury in the last few weeks.

    Hell with it, she could probably work that kind of job. Brianna was relatively young, in excellent shape, even a little bit pretty with her platinum blonde, near-white hair, robust jawline, and icy blue eyes. Ex-military to boot... well, she'd been a cook in the Navy, but still, she knew how to use a rifle. If nothing else, it'd get her out of the house and put some money in the bank after a whole fat lot of unemployment following her discharge. God knows that dad would be eager to see her save up and get her own place - he was constant in his reminders that she needed to go be a functioning member of society now that she was out of the service.

She was greeted almost immediately with sights that would've made her think she was going insane not long ago, but the variety of inhuman creatures both on and above the street hardly fazed her anymore. There just wasn't anything all that weird about seeing a harpy flapping above the rooftops or a mermaid in a wheelchair heading down the street, not when there were potions available in quite a few stores that could let one become another species for a day or two. Nashville happened to have ended up at the forefront of replacing infrastructure with magical means, a process people expected to see rolled out extensively over the next few years - and really, as long as it made the daily commute a bit less of a fucking nightmare, most people seemed okay with that. The very few fringe lunatics opposed to progress of this shape would end up filtering out to the countryside eventually, where they wouldn't bother much of anyone.

"Excuse me, I'm ah, I'm here about the job posting for Hunters-"
