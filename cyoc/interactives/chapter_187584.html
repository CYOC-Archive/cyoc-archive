    "Well then I guess the only question is who leads," Takeo stated. The siblings all nodded, sloqly but surely turning to Kari. "Why are you all looking at me?"

    "Well its not me either," Chisai said. "I'm just a blacksmith. I don't know the first thing about leading our people."

    "Me!"

    "But I'm not like our folks," Kiko stammered. "I'm no warrior. Hell, I'm not even a boy or the eldest! I go completely against tradition. How can I be the best option?"

    "I hate to agree with Fukushu but he's right," Chisai shrugged. "Face it you kinda already run things day to day."

    "And I think dad would want someone who wasn't so traditional leading our people forward," Kari added.

    Kiko was at a loss for words. She had never fathomed being the successor to her father's rule. She wasn't married, had no children, she wasn't even a nine tail like her siblings. Kiko was the free spirit and had acted naturally on her own impulses. She never truely thought any of the ideas she had proposed would be accepted by the clan. "I don't know what to say."

    "Not sure what needs to be said," Takeo shrugged. "I mean let's face it Kiko, you maybe a bit unorthodox but you definitely know what you're doing. Look how much better things have gotten with the ideas you helped usher in."

    Kiko fidgeted slightly, feeling every bit the child she now resembled. "Dont worry Little Kiko," Fukushu smirked. "I'm sure you'll GROW into the role."

    "I swear if I have to hear one more short joke from you brother my first act as leader will be throwing you in jail!"

    Fukushu simply laughed softly, gazing down at his sister. Kiko took a deep breath, looking at her siblings before nodding. "Guess I'll take the job," she said. "Just as soon as we grow up again."
