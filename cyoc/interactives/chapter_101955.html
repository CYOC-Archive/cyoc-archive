    Dancer: Leaps and Bounds

    Jen stood waiting for her body to run, but instead, her body twirled and spun around, twice, then started leaping toward the beach. Jen let out a small scream as her body covered the block within a minute, jumping over anyone she could have traded with, and finally landing on the beach. It did one last twirl, leaped twice toward the water and landed in the sea, took a bow and Jen gained control of her body. Trying not to cry or scream, she walked in the uncomfortable ballet shoes until she managed to get out of the city. She had expected to see people either running or gawking, but no one batted an eye, like it was an every day thing to see.

    It was dusk, and Jen managed to find a quite park, just outside a suburb, it was lite well enough that Jen could now take stock of her new large body. The first thing Jen did was to take off the clown gloves, when she did she breathed a big sigh of relief to find that her hands matched the body, dainty and feminine, instead of the clown's head. She took the tutu off, and remove the ballet shoes, startled a little by the fact that she could lift her leg up right next to her head, her new body more flexible than her own had been before this trip. Glancing around to see if anyone was near, she decided to take off the leotard as well, just so she could get the hosiery off of her legs. She found herself to be almost perfectly proportional to her old body, all be it about three times larger in scale, and that she was actually wearing a strapless bra and panties underneath. She slipped the leotard back on and walked over to a park bench. Without thinking about it she sat down on the bench, only realizing it might be a bad idea after she had nearly reached it and it was too late to stop the action. Pleasantly she found her huge rump could fit a park bench, even a sofa, all be it a little uncomfortable, her field of view was high but not too high, and her body agile enough to move around like her original, if not a little clumsily at the moment because of it's new owner.

    "I guess I can live with this. I just wish my head matched." Jen said. "Well I've got to get back home somehow, I sleep on it and figure it out tomorrow."

    Jen lay back, obliterating the park bench underneath her.

    "Shit... I gotta be more careful with this thing."

    She lay staring at the stars and finally falling asleep.
