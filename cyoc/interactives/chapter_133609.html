Sure, he got made fun of for it, but Tyler knew that his devotion would pay off when he was in heaven and all his former classmates burned in hell.

    Just as Tyler managed to finish placing the lock and bar over the church doors (it was the only protection they could afford), an object came and flew straight into the wooden door, lodging itself into the crack it made. Upon closer inspection, it appeared to be a lamp of some sort. Tyler recognized it from one of the children's books his mother had read to him as a child before his father had admonished them for not reading the word of God. Tyler's father believed that every waking moment should be in worship of the Lord, so children's books were not allowed.

Everything about him was in the right place. The only off-putting thing about the materialized man were his eyes, a sharp golden color that made people immediately look away due to their intensity.

It sounded so enticing, however, Tyler's father had always told him that if it was too good to be true, it probably was, and this definitely seemed too fantastic. Not to mention, the man's entrance reeked of magic and devil work.

Usually people jumped at the opportunity to get all their desires fulfilled; it was how they were so easily manipulated. The genie began reading the situation. Looking around the brunette's room, he was able to quickly deduce that this man was not like his past masters. Based on the 'empowering quotes' that definitely weren't based in this era and the idols that adorned the room, the brunette clearly seemed faithful to something that was keeping him strong. It was child's play to recognize the faith in religion that this man had.

    "I can assure that I am not one of 'Lucifer's minions' as you say. I'm nothing but a servant to your will, Master."

    "Then I wish you'd stop and go away," Tyler ordered. Luckily for both them, the genie was easily able to reword the wish in both of their favors. Time reversed and Tyler stated his new wish. "I wish you'd stop MY DISBELIEF and NEVER go away."

    "Well, I can grant the first part of your wish, but the second disobeys my 24 hour rule, so I cannot grant it," the genie compromised, acting as if he hadn't altered the pastor-in-training's wish. It was nothing he hadn't done before. The genie's eyes glowed for a split second as the wish came into effect. In response, Tyler's eyes widened as he realized that this was in fact a real genie.

    "You're... You're real?" Tyler exclaimed. The brunette was now able to believe the genie, however, he knew nothing about the genie's +3 ability. That was a closely guarded secret that no being other than himself was allowed to know.

    "Precisely Master. I'm here to serve you," the genie stated.

    Tyler became even more fearful at that response. Tyler believed the genie wanted to serve him, but that didn't change the fact that he could still be one of the devil's servants. "How do I know I can trust you?"

    The genie rolled his eyes. "Make a wish and find out."

    Tyler thought hard for a moment. One wish couldn't hurt, especially if it meant he could use it to find correct the genie's possible intentions, right? "Alright, I wish that it is impossible for you to lie."

    Child's play, the genie thought. "I wish that it is impossible for you to lie WHILE STANDING THERE."

    Tyler recognized the flaw in his wish but figured he could see the genie's movement in case the muscled man wanted to lie. "Ok, since that's settled. Are you in any way aligned with Satan or have any ill intentions towards me?"

    "Whatever you wish Master," the genie answered.
