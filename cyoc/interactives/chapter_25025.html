    Shit, Josh thought, was we he going to do? He had to get out of there, that much was clear! Then he realized that, even if he did escape, he had no idea where he would go. If he managed to get home, his family would see a donkey and not their son!

    "Having fun?" a voice asked, it was the man from before.

    Opening his eyes, Josh turned his head to see the man escorting a horse. Josh brayed at the man in a desperate attempt to beg for his humanity. It didn't work.

    "This will make you feel better," the man said.

    Walking the horse up to Josh's right side, the man untied Josh before taking several steps backwards.

    "Go ahead, what are you waiting for?"

He most definitely wasn't attracted to her! Josh didn't need long to realize that, yes, he *was* compatible with her! With horror, Josh realized that not only had the man told him that they bred mules here but that in his current form, Josh was in position to do just that!

    "I know what you're thinking. You think you're better then this, that you're still human inside! Give it up, you're a jackass now. Human inhibition means nothing to you," the man said. He walked right up to Josh and looked into Josh's long face. "You only have one rule now; be a jackass. Even if you fight it and escape, where will you go? I think your mate wants something from you. Would you let down such a sweet thing?"

Something about her scent awakened a need from within him. Josh felt his donkey hood grow. Instinct and desire flooded his mind. Inhibition vanished from inside of him as though the mare's scent had plucked it away. All that mattered to Josh now was what was between his legs.

He knew he was becoming the stereotype but he didn't care. All that mattered was satisfying that desire. Yes, this was what he wanted out of life he realized, this was what mattered, nothing else! Josh brayed and put everything into this as though it were the sole reason for his existence. As far as his hormone crazed mind was concerned, it was.

This was beyond anything Josh imagined were possible. Maybe the magic of the island made it better then a natural donkey could ever have in an attempt to lure Josh. He didn't care, then and there, it felt incredible and that was all that concerned him.

    No, Josh thought with a shake of his head, this wasn't who he was. But, if he wasn't going to give in, Josh wondered what his next step was to be.
