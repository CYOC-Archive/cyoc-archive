    Nice Legs You Have There.

    added by Stark A month ago A Mythological Insect

    Erin was an arachne: built much like a centaur, but with the lower body of a spider. She appeared to be human from the waist up, but from the waist down she was a big, fuzzy, black-furred tarantula. Her human waist grew out of the spot where a spider's cephalo-thorax would normally be located, albeit bent at a right angle so that Erin's torso stood upright relative to her spider-abdomen.

    The area on the front of her spider-body just below her waist was a smooth, chitinous plate with no outwardly visible features, neither those of a human crotch, nor a spider's jaws and pedipalps. Simply put, Erin had what appeared to be a completely human torso growing straight out of the front of a great spider's abdomen and eight hairy legs.

    There were some white markings that dappled the otherwise black fur of her bulbous abdomen. Her human half was without question very beautiful, although her skin was fair to the point of pale. Her short hair was black, the same as the fuzzy hairs all over her spider-body. She wore a plain red t-shirt (stretched tight over her generous bust) and bright red lipstick, and the overall effect made her appear more than a little "goth." Although her spider-body was huge, she didn't stand any taller than John.

    "I hope you like your steak rare," she said, holding up a platter of choice cuts. "I hate to ruin a good T-bone by overdoing it."

    John tried to speak, but he found his voice catching in his throat. Finally, he managed to choke out, "Sp-sp-spuh-spider. Big spider. I mean tarantula. I mean drider. You're a drider!"

    "Hey!" said Erin indignantly. "I'm an *arachne*, buster! Do I look like I'm part elf to you?" She emphasized the point by turning her head sideways and running a finger along the top of her decidedly rounded and human-looking, not-at-all-pointy ears. Then she froze up, only just then seeming to realize what she was saying. "Wait a minute." Erin looked for a moment as if she were going to be sick, and she said in a very small, meek voice, "You can see my—my—"

    "Spider-half?" said John. "Yeah, it's pretty hard to miss."

    "Oh. Oh, wow." Erin seemed to shrink away from John just then, and she skittered over to the dinner table so she could set down the meat-platter; then she leaned over the table and used her arms to hold herself up. "This must be such a shock to you." From her tone, John could tell that she expected him to flee screaming out into the streets.

    "You could say that," said John. Then he shrugged and added, "But . . . I like to think I'm open minded. I read a lot, and I'm a gamer, so I like sci-fi and fantasy stuff; that's probably why I'm not totally freaked out right now." He paused. "I *should* be totally freaked out right now, shouldn't I?"

    Erin smiled weakly. "That *is* how most guys normally react when I finally show them the real me. But I've never sprung the fact that I'm a—a—a big, creepy-crawly *bug monster*—on a guy during the first date."

    "I don't think you're a monster," said John, walking over to the dinner table and sitting down. "But now you do have me curious. How do you hide being a dri—I mean, an arachne? And how did you become one?" He lowered his voice down to a tense whisper and says, "You weren't cursed by the Chaotic Evil spider-goddess Lolth, were you?"

    Erin rolled her eyes. "Again, that's driders."

    John grinned cheekily. "I know. I'm just pulling your le—I mean, just messing around." (He winced internally.) "But I'm not gonna go running away screaming, just because you're not what I expected."

    "Then . . . you're still interested in our date?" asked Erin. Her voice was still meek, as if she feared rejection, and there was no disguising the hint of hope in it.

    John thought about how disastrous his last few dates had been, and how much nicer and more normal Erin seemed, even in spite of the fact that she had the lower half of a tarantula. "More than ever," he admitted.

    "All right," said Erin. "Let's have dinner, and I'll tell you anything you want to know."
