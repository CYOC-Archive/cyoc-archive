    Heading out as cheerleaders

    "Alright girls lets move out" Kayla exclaims, this way of speaking was quickly becoming natural, not that he really noticed, instead just being glad it looked like they weren't getting caught anytime soon.

    Getting up Kayla adjusts his skirt, not noticing the skin under it has gone completely. Heading out into the main area they pass by at least a dozen cheerleaders, all gathered around chatting. Most not really paying the three newcomers much mind. A few greet them, to which they easy respond back to. All in all they blend in perfectly, almost too perfectly.

    Finally getting out they enter the halls.

    "Great now we can finally stop acting like cheerleaders...." Kyle mutters under his breath before being nudged by the now much more slender arms of Kannon, who then whispers "We're like, in a crowded hall Kayla. We can't drop the act until we get somewhere private, god, you can be like such a ditz sometimes girl"

    Kyle was a tad unsettled by how much of a girl Kannon was sounding, but brushed passed that, they did need to stay in-character.

    "Uh.. girls, a few people are staring already" Tina said "Which is too be expected cause we're totes hawt but we still do something" he quickly adds on, sounding more feminine by the word.

    Turning to Kami, who quickly unzips the bag and pops out a phone and yells "Selfie time girls!" causing all three to pose in shockingly girlish manners. Kayla gets a look at all three of them in the screen of the phone just as the picture is taken, is Tina wearing makeup? In fact all three of them seem to be wearing a bit of makeup. They didn't put any of that on, he thinks.

    'Well of course, what kind of girl would go out without at least a bit of lip gloss' thinks Kayla, who almost realises the strangeness of thinking that internally when the flash goes off, distracting them all.

    Rubbing his eyes he gets back to walking, now seeing that their girly selfie taking resulted in any suspicious onlookers to move on. Now that he was thinking about it, he didn't remember Kami... Kannon having a phone.

    "Like, where'd you get that phone from anyways Kami" Kayla asks with genuinely curiosity. To his surprised, Kami just looks at him weirdly, as if the question was unfounded.

    "From my dad, DUH!" He says.

    "Wait... isn't your dad a factory worker or something..." Kayla says silently enough so that only Kami could hear. He swears that Kannon's dad worked in the local factories, definitely not enough money to buy their dau- son such an expensive phone. Kami looks at him even more weirdly.

    "Uh no? My Dad owns the factory Kayla. You know that. It how we met remember? Your dad become his business partner like years ago" Kami explains matter-of-factually.

    Kayla blinks "My dad? But he's a... uh..." He wants to say grocer but something catches his tongue, allowing Kami to finish his sentence "A stock investor. God, did you hit your head during practice Kayla?"

    Kayla... Kyle thinks back, blurry images of meeting Kami at a country club while their fathers discussed business comes to mind. The memory is blurry, but it almost remembers wearing a dress in it. Which is because he's a boy, just pretending to be a girl.

    His thoughts are interrupted by Tina jumping in "What are you too talking about" Kayla excepts Kami to talk about how weird he was acting, but instead the moment Tina jumps in Kami's face loses all its previous confusion and springs into an instant grin "We were just talking about the new phones our fathers brought us" he says holding up the expensive phone he used to take the picture moments ago.

    Thinking back on the conversation they had just now, Kayla wonders what Kami is talking about. Where's that confusion over Kayla not knowing what their fathers did. Wait... why wouldn't he know what their fathers did? They got them these sweet new phones because of it.

    Scratching his wig, which almost feels attached to his head now, Kayla takes out his new phone as well from his purse. Wait.. purse? He didn't have a purse... he thinks.

    Before he can think too much on his sudden purse, Tina speaks up "Ugh, I wish my parents would buy me stuff like that more often, my dad just owns some supermarket franchise, totally lame compared to yours"

    They all idlily chat about their new stuff their parents have brought them, before Kayla realises the crowds are dying down "Okay girls, just a bit longer and we can get back to being boys" he whispers, to which his two friends look oddly confused at for a moment before having a look of mild realisation and nodding.
