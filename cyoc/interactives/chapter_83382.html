    Gunther figured his best bet was to play it cool. From the beginning, he'd been designing brain architecture with extra emphasis on promoting the baser instincts. To him, it seemed if you were going to become an animal, you should be a creature of almost pure instinct, doing what came natural without care for the consequences, living in the eternal now. The others, of course, disagreed, at least until recently. He'd finally had a chance to deploy a few designs on a trial basis, and from what he understood, those who had been given his experimental modifications had resulted in some of the absolute happiest, most well-adjusted people in the park. Of course, he understood the controversy involved. It would be one thing to perform these trial runs on a proper volunteer basis, but those asking for them to be implemented had been very secretive about the whole affair. He had of course, taken good measures to hide his tracks. The type-2 deer models were public knowledge. While he of course purged all the specifics there, it would look suspicious if he hadn't. The type-2 husky trial run was even easier to hide. That one had actually falsified her own records, and been flagged accordingly. There were a few others he could hide as routine corruption from bad drive sectors, a legitimate issue, and the reason original scans were generally backed up. Nothing would come of that if they left the park in their altered forms, which seemed to be the likely case for everyone involved. He felt a bit of guilt over all of this, but he truly had been pressured quite seriously to take such measures, and felt he had at least balanced the scales of karma. A few of those who were accepting the money for these changes had requested he design "emergency escape" forms for them, off all the records, should this sort of attention fall on them, and he'd keyed them in when everyone started to panic. He'd felt though that anyone advocating for his designs should reap their benefits, so any who did manage to slip past the police and go on the run would soon be giving up their greedy ways, favoring a much much simpler life.

    When the skunk from the FBI called him down for questioning, he was incredibly forthright. He happily named names of those he felt were doing things for the wrong reasons, particularly regarding that deer business, and the shady plans for foreign prisoners. He was less enthused about discussing the offsite research facility which had apparently quite recently become a matter of public knowledge. Not that he had much he was able to divulge in that regard. He had never been to the facility, learned it's location, or even been given a clear understanding of what went on there. All he knew is that he had passed along some of his more theoretical designs for further study. Non-mammalian concepts in particular, where he was afraid changes might be too radical, both physically and mentally. Some methods for altering sexuality he'd been developing, as the park had an alarming number of cases where one partner's sex change had caused abrupt ends to what were formally happy relationships. As far as he had been told, trials were to be conducted, using lesser primates at first, and eventually brave human volunteers who had signed the appropriate wavers.
