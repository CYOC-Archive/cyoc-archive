    You wake up in the morning, your mind foggy as the cold begins to lift. Your exhibit is dark, and you begin slithering around your home. You slither up the branches, hang upside down for a little while (but it is quite uncomfortable). You hear people talking in the back, and as you are hungry, you go to the back to see if they are going to feed you.
    "She's awake" said one man.
    "Give her something to eat" Yessssss you think. You're starved. They throw you another young antelope, and you strike it, and swallow it whole just as you did yesterday. You rest by your lamp, and people begin coming in to see you. You realize that the best way to go about your day is to completely ignore them, and so you do just that. Your meal gives you enough energy for the rest of the day. Most of the day, you lay by your lamp, sunning yourself. Your thoughts seem to have change though, instead of thinking of things from your human life, like movies, books, the news, you seem to be thinking about how nice the antelope tasted this morning, how nice it would be if you could live normally out of captivity. You slither around, and into the trees. Up there you feel more secluded, but you miss the heat of your lamp. Finally you see your light switch off.
