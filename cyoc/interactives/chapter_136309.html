    While the majority of Animalia Ambassadoria was covering the Animalia Summer Events, there were some scenes where they talked about events regarding Animalia in general.

    ---

    "For once, both the heterosexual members in congress and the xenophobic members managed to pull off something beneficial to Animalia." said Abby, after JC and Pauline were introduced to their audience. That news was spliced in with Stewie's famous "Say Whaaat?!" clip. Malice proceeded to explain.

    "When word reached congress, (who were originally working on a new bill to control the Virus, and along with it Animalia, with intention of slowing the spread of Regressive Hypersexuality), that the Virus could, erroneously, be a cure for homosexuality, there were those who were intending to use it as a cure, but both of those arguing both for and against control of the virus managed to lock the bill in gridlock long enough for the pro-Animalia members of congress to kill the bill."

    ---

    After some more coverage of the Animalia Summer Events, there was another thing done.

    "Before we cover some more news," said Sarah, "Here's a new clip of the new TV show coming from Japan that's one of those that mix CGI with live action actors, and also was inspired by the GGAC."

    At that, there was a showing of a scene that showed the GGAC doing battle with summoned demons in a cyberpunk universe, similar to an Oriental/Eastern version of The Matrix. One of the funnier scenes was where Peter gave a lethal "Martial Arts Atomic Wedgie" to two demons at once and when they were dispelled, he was left with a pair of underwear in each hand. What made it even funnier was Peter's comment on it: "Boy am I glad that some demons don't fight dirty."

    ---

    After some more coverage, there was some other news.

    "Apparently some people in the Military want to know some more about the Island where the Asian Animalia Colony is going to be located." said JC, reading one of the posts on the website. "What they want to know is why is the island not recorded on modern maps while it does appear on ancient maps."

    "According to the records at the time," said Daniel, before he and Mandy went off on their own colony tour for their honeymoon, "While the island is capable of sustaining an agricultural, and some modern tourism, town, it just wasn't tactically significant to those of WWII-era Imperial Japan."

    "The maps, when compared to each other, also show that the island is of equal distance from China and Japan to not be affected by the fallout from both the Hiroshima and Nagasaki nuclear bombs, but also close enough to be easily reachable by ferry from both nations.

    ---

    After some more laughs, including the shot of one Rottweiler Animalian getting headbutted by a sheep during the Mister and Miss Farmhand competition, and some other announcements, Animalia was able to close the show before having to do some shots of the events, including SAMAS, UFC-A, A-BC, and the Bare-As-You-Dare competition for the next episode.
