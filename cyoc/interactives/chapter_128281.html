    Kellie looked at the two small girls. Her heart ached terribly for what she had done, but she knew that it was the right thing to do. She couldn't help, however, feeling as if she had lost so much just to get to this point, and she didn't want to lose her whole life, her childhood, teen years or whatever else. It felt now like her life had been in fast forward for some time now, and she was only just becoming aware of it with the ring.

    "I know exactly what I am going to do girls. " She said at last.

    "Wait," Katie said. This was her chance now or never, to tell her mother and former lover the truth. Before she could though, Kellie made her wish.

    "I wish that you two would become unborn, your vital essence and what makes you you stored inside me for safe keeping."

    In that instant, both girls vanished, leaving a pile of their clothes on the floor. Kellie took in a deep breath and touched her belly. "That was step one my little ones....now step two. I cannot remember either of you, not as you were, and I cannot lose my life and time or ask my own parents to do the same."

    Kellie took a deep breath. "I wish to regress until I am an ordinary six year old girl, and that my parents would regress until they were the proper age to have me. I further wish to have all other wishes and changes before this time in regards to our ages undone, so i can have a fresh start and grow up as an ordinary girl, and that my babies would be born healthy when the time is right with no memory of their past lives or this ring.

    Kellie felt the ring warm as the world began to warp around her. Even as she felt herself shrinking and memories floating away, she smiled to herself. The ring, barely clinging to her finger dropped onto the floor. A moment later the six year old girl blinked as she looked around. She was in an empty house now, and her parents were just outside talking to a realor. Giggling, the girl spun around in her sundress, delighted at the warm light that danced through the windows. She didn't even notice as her feet kicked a shiny ring out the door and into the planter.

    A moment later her two parents came inside. "Well, Kiddo, what do you think?" Her father asked.

    "Do you like it princess?" her mother asked.

    Kellie nodded, and ran to her parents. She was happy, very and truely happy, as if an entire lifetime of happiness denied her was alive in that moment. So ended the adventures of the ring with Kellie, Lisa, Katie and their friends...but who would be the next person to find the ring?
