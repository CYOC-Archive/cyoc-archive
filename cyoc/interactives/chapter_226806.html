Those decorations flowed and hummed with magic as Alisariah’s pen flowed over the page.

In an afternoon, she was passed down six centuries worth of experience and practice with the brush and pen, her graceful fingers could manipulate paint and ink with a steadiness and dexterity she couldn’t have dreamed until now of replicating. Most importantly, the magic now beginning to gust over the world answered her beckoned call. Not only was she drawing, she was enchanting, weaving her magic into her latest piece.

    It was one she’d already been working on for some time, one she’d thought a few days ago she was done and satisfied with. A portrait, one of her favorite subjects, depicting a proud and warlike Wood Elf, olive skinned with dark brown hair which tumbled wildly down one shoulder. Her exposed midriff displayed a toned core and emerald tattoos, interrupted by well-healed battle scars. Her hand rested at ease on the string of a curved shortbow as she surveyed a verdant landscape of evergreens which stretched all the way to the roots of snow capped mountains. Alisariah could not help but hum in pride and desire, as she knew that very soon her art was about to become reality, sooner than she realized.

    Wanda Hadley was already making her way down the hall to Alice’s appartement. She’d been counting the days until it was time for this pickup. She’d already been highly impressed with the digital scans of the commission she’d ordered from Alice, worth every dollar. Now she was about to get her hands on the physical version, the hand-illustrated portrait of her Elven Fighter character, Astania. That warrior had been a companion through two years of highs and lows, astounding triumphs and hilarious failures on the tabletop. Now that campaign was finished, her story was over, this portrait serving as the final closure to something that had become a part of her.

    Alisariah’s ears perked up to attention as she heard a knock at her door. “Be with you in just a moment!” She called out to her guest. She waved her hand and wrapped her whole flat in a glamour that masked it in its original appearance, herself included. She rolled her eyes as she looked down at her old self, before banishing the tic and putting on human mannerisms as she opened the door.

    “Oh hi! Wanda, right? I- I have your commission ready, it’s just over here on my table. I hope it’s up to what you expected?”

    “Way, way better than that! Look at this! It’s like I’m standing right there with her! You did so good.” Wanda gushed, partly in sincere excitement, partly to try to put the nervous girl at ease. She hugged the painting and spun on the spot with it. She looked down at it once more, she swore she could imagine a gust of wind tussling her hair when she did.
