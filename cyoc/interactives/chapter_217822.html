From there, they could go on to university, and Juliet had made sure to see whether or not the girls had interest in that. Some of this was a bit disappointing, but the guides that were there to meet them also explained that once Songkran started on April 13, the school day would be shorter and there would be no homework due until the Monday after the "holiday." It might then allow Juliet's students to interact with some of the local children and learn about their lives and culture on the island.

    And their time on Heiya Island did provide answers for various questions for Juliet's students, such as the fact that Songkran came to Heiya Island not because the people of Japan actually celebrated the holiday, but because some of the people that had been rescued from various criminal smuggling rings and either didn't know their families or had no surviving family were from Thailand and managed to remember the celebration there and wished to continue it. Having a small Buddhist temple on the island, a lot of that was no problem, and various elements that were typical in Thailand were incorporated into Heiya Island's routine, though it gained a life of its own as many of those who were not originally from Thailand wanted to expand on certain concepts as part of a sort of "spring renewal" that seemed to involve a lot of water balloon fights in certain areas, among other things.

    The thing that set things up was on April 15, the last day. Things had gone well, and some of her students had picked up a few basic words and phrases of Japanese and Chinese. It was limited to "hi, my name is..." and the like, but it worked and it allowed for everyone to learn a lot more about Animalia's colonies, and thankfully, the "fan girls" didn't bother Juan too much as he helped Juliet with chaperone related duties as part of his own education, which Juliet was to take notes on to provide as recommendations and observations to those running the education training program that Juan was enrolled in to teach at French schools.     "A family specialty, madam," the man gave a rather toothy smile, "guaranteed to loosen things up a bit."

    "Loosen things up," Juliet said, her voice slurring a bit and leading her to get up as her mind didn't feel completely there and she began to stagger out.

    The last thing she heard was Gigi barking at the restaurant's server for getting Juliet drunk and with Olympe joining in, while Juan was hurriedly trying to settle things and keeping the other students present under control. Juliet really wasn't paying attention and simply staggered out of the restaurant and found herself ambling along, and swaying a bit side to side as she tried to avoid bumping into people that were around. Thankfully, no one appeared to have any cameras, as if anything like this made the internet, she could probably kiss her teaching career goodbye.

    There was a little bit of activity going on that she could hear, and thankfully no one decided to throw a water balloon at her. But her mind was presently quite hazy and all she really wanted was a place where she could just lay down and rest. Her head didn't hurt, but it was clear that she was in no real condition to do much of anything as the alcohol in her system was impeding various motor functions and she sort of staggered and wasn't even fully aware that she was headed toward the north shore of the island, where a "wet tee-shirt contest" was one of the things that had been added to Songkran on Heiya Island. Given the cooler and more temperate temperatures, the rational part of Juliet's mind would question why they do that, when the articles on the holiday's tradition had nothing to do with nudity or anything close to it. The part of her that wanted to lie down, also wasn't entirely sure why she'd headed in that direction. She supposed some sand would make for a soft bed, but other than that, her mind wasn't too focused...

    That made Juliet blink, as she wasn't in a swimsuit and neither was she in a tee shirt. Even if the main educational part of the trip was over, she was still essentially fulfilling an educational function, and thus she wore a blue button-down blouse and a dark blue skirt. She wasn't even wearing anything that would typically be used in these contests. And while her understanding of oriental languages was somewhat weak and she and her students had largely had to rely on translators, she did know her own name. And she knew she had not entered into some contest that would only end her teaching career, let alone win it.

    "What?" Juliet spoke, slowly, and noticing the slur in her voice and she would privately curse herself later for butchering her own language, "I did not enter any contest."

When they found some that spoke English, some things worked out well, but that wasn't always perfect, and Juliet wasn't in a state to see as to whether or not the other Fox Animalian spoke English. It made her wonder if something like that had happened to Juliet, or a family member in the past. The fact that Juliet was aiming the attack on another silver phase Red Fox Animalian added to her own sense of confusion. The two Fox Animalians looked fairly similar, with the obvious differences in what they were wearing, but Josephine still noted that there were other differences. Juliet had brown hair while the other fox had black hair, and had it cut in a different style, and well shorter than Juliet's style. There were also differences in the eyes in that this other Fox Animalian had more Japanese eyes than what one would expect from a person of European ancestry.

Stuck with overbearing parents who were all very traditional... and by that a traditional Japanese family. No fun... no anime. No manga. No looking at all the other places in the world. Wine... parties, pastry... the can can... it all seemed fun, and I wanted a piece of that, but never could. Which lead to me being depressed, and so I ended up eating... and... that lead to me gaining weight."

Though as I got my degree, and ultimately made decent money designing ads for various companies in Japan... I was soon busy with other things. Deadlines, schedules, and everything else... So I needed something new... a new name. So, I went into the various things that I'd read about from the tourist books that I found interesting... along with literature. I liked "Romeo and Juliet," though it's a shame that both main characters died..."

And it'd fuel the hope for time to one day see France... though, I never expected to run into anyone with this name who teaches kids to keep them out of the army."

If the girls I've taught... or the young men I'm teaching now one day CHOOSE to join the military, that is their choice. And your choice of name could well ruin that because when people find your name in these records... or in captions, even on "adult" sections for Animalia's website, they will assume it was ME and I will pay the price for your desire to have fun."
