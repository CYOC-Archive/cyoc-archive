    be taken care of by their loved ones, and seen in 'modern' times as a genuine illness. In

    treated no better than animals."

    Glass Marie felt a headache coming on. She felt as if the past as continuing to change as
    things balanced themselves out, with people catching up to things at different rates. All in
    all, it was just giving Glass Marie one big headache!

    Jesen was in his office, but so concentrated on his long neglected task of keeping an eye
    on the pack's holdings, that he never once looked outside the window and never once saw
    the dance of chaos that he, the teens, and nearly everyone who wasn't looking after the
    injured or listening to Glass Marie's story was now engaged in.

    Zelipe stayed with Amy in her small scale Shinto shrine.

    The dance outside had become a full scale spontaneous production by those who now had
    the dance of chaos engraved in their bones and those who were entranced into joining in
    as they looked at the hypnotic movements, several times more powerful than the Omega's
    dance.

    Even the mourning cubs for the death of their mothers were pulled into the dance,
    becoming one with it, become whole with it. It was almost a pity one had a video camera to
    record any of it. The practical sea of naked furry brightly colored bodies moved like an
    ocean with waves and tides.

    And at the center of it all, Amethyst remained... her heart pounding, her arms and legs on
    fire to where she wondered why they didn't burst into flames. Her lungs gasping in and out
    for air.

    This went on for hours. To the point where some of the pack and pride wondered where
    some of the other were, the ones who wandered off joined the dance.

    Amethyst knew this was beautiful, she knew it was good, she knew the dance that never
    repeated itself in precisely the same way was transcendent... finally the six foot tall plus
    lupman's legs gave out from under her and she fell to the ground with a thud. Gasping for
    breath on her hands and knees before those gave out too.

    The dance stopped like a plug had been pulled, some crash into each other, others stopped
    right where they stood like statues, yet others stood around looking about as if they had
    just woken up from a dream.

    Garnet was the first one to reach Amethyst with concern for the eldest gem wolf... true to
    the curse Garnet was sure was placed on her as revenge towards her absent parents,
    Amethyst cost that moment to collapse totally and Garnet was underneath her pillowy
    breasts with Amethyst breath blowing on her fur. Garnet sighed, 'Oh whatever... at least it's
    comfy...'

    1685 Winter

    Kathrine panted, using the spear as a cane, her muscles burned, and every time she
    thought she had reached her limit or maybe was making a little headway, Armando either
    pushed her harder or slowly made the training more intense. It was somewhat oppressing
    on her sense of accomplishment. But improving she was... slowly but surely she was. She
    just needed to... needed to... catch her breath and maybe... lay down for just a little bit...

    "Cat, monkey."

    It was about as ugly a slur as calling a lupman a wolf. And Armando used them to prod
    Kathrine into getting back on her feet whenever Kathrine felt now as a good time for a little
    good sleep where she stood.

    Kathrine thought of the cover story used to make sure that her life as a would be nun and
    the side kick to a trained killer for the church didn't contradict each other.

    "I need to ask," Kathrine said, "Who IS precisely looking after the pups your Omegas had?"

    Armando said, "The Omegas of course, and when of course they're busy turning the
    smaller wheel of the pack, we had whoever happened to be on hand do it. ... Worthless
    Omegas, breaking tradition, having pups, what a waste."

    Glass Marie said, "This was sixteen-eighty-five, what did you think they were like? And
    remember this wasn't -your- pack, it wasn't even from the same country!

    "Draco was NOT HAPPY to hear he'd have to shift his entire plan to getting them legally
    across the sea by three years! And that would leave Marie only about two years before she
    knew the lines of fate reached the point of no return and the world the witches wished to
    create would inevitable become reality. But, as much as Marie loathed to admit it, she had
    given her word. So she used what time she had productively, learning anything she could
    about how magic actually work, and how it -didn't- work. And of course... taking care of
    business for the Inquisition on under the table affairs.

    "I could have been... immortal..." The alchemist gasped out, his blood, as much blood as
    quicksilver it leaked from his body. His fully grown Homunculus was impaled to the wall
    bleeding white seamen.

    "And all that time I thought it was going to be another blood sucker..." Marie thought,
    looking at the ruined lab. And the quicksilver poisoned corpses of the alchemist's test
    subjects... some willing, some not, all promised immortality of it had worked. "Idiots...
    God is the only path to eternal life." Marie said simply before gathering some items to
    submit to the inquisition and left.

    That christmas, Marie and Kathrine exchanged gifts on New Years. At the same time Marie
    was wondering if she should tell Draco she was 'Marius.'

    The three witches had finally physically recovered from the rune fiasco. The magical

    plans from the ground up, but at a much faster pace than before...
