She was buff for a girl, but still very attractive, with hazel eyes and a few freckles scattered across the bridge of her nose.     "I mean, if you want to, but thanks to Amir, we're not doing bad. Even if I get less hours at the shop. Nobody wants a girl to work on their cars." Chris grumbled. 'Amir' must be Sahar's fake (or not so fake) Dad.

    "But you're the best!" Elena hadn't expected this. Her brother...err...sister, was a mechanic and a really good one, having learned from their Dad. Elena had watched them (and even helped a little) for years, even if she was rather averse to engine grease.

    "I know that, and you know that, and my boss knows that, but the customers don't. It's this dumb cute face. Maybe I should take up boxing, lose a few teeth, then I'd have a better chance?"

    Elena frowned. "Don't you dare, Sis! I like you the way you are." Which, given the circumstances, was somewhat ironic, to be sure.

    Chris walked up and ruffled Elena's hair. "Aw, I love you too, Sis. Anyways, I'm going to take a shower, you two have fun."

    "Actually," Sahar interjected, "I'm just giving Elena a ride. I was thinking to make you dinner when I return, in appreciation for you opening up your home to me."

    "Wow, really Sahar? You don't have to...but I won't turn down a home cooked meal!" Chris brightened.

    Sahar bowed. "It would be my pleasure. I will return shortly."

    "Always so formal, Sahar. Ok then, see you when you get back."

It looked a little cleaner- Chris was great at keeping the car running, but sometimes slacked on detailing. The car freshener she had given him, which before had been permanently exiled to the glove box, was now hanging from the rearview mirror (it showed a purple haired girl, Mai Sakurajima, wearing a Playboy bunny-esque outfit), and purple seat covers.

    "Yes, but she's probably better that way, don't you think?" Sahar got into the car as well, the seat adjusted for her long legs. "I don't actually know where I am going, so you'll have to tell me." She turned the key in the ignition, and the engine roared to life. "Something tells me your Sister is my mechanic as well."
