Some of this had been done by Horizon before, but so far as Fabina knew, much of that was also to see how well the herm gene would work with an uplifted animal and often came with additional paperwork. In most cases, when animals were uplifted, they stayed within the gender that they had.

    "That's a bit of a story in its own right," Paula gave a shrug.

    "No... I think with these birds, they came from a private collector of exotic animals," Paula answered, "though... he either didn't pay to the appropriate licenses, never sought them, or just flat tried to hide that he had them. From what I was told and when... the birds had been confiscated and were in California's state custody. But the official sanctuaries were either full or over-tasked with other animals. To a degree, I'm not even sure what led to their being taken into state custody or what happened to the owner. For all I know... the guy died of old age and the birds were left without care, and a neighbor reported in odd "noises" and stuff like that. I really don't know."

    "I take it that they called Horizon?" Fabina wondered.

From what shi'd experienced in hir own becoming of a herm Peafowl Animalian, the transformation could get many things started that could be "weird" to those unprepared. And there were plenty of things that had come from the stories on Animalia's founding where animals when they were uplifted might not fully aware of everything at first, and some of that was taken to the extreme when Heather Wilcox-Green became the first Dinotopian.

"At the same time... there could be the potential for one or more of this group that may wish to be your mate. Some may be thinking about that now."

Some of our herm Animalians have met and found love before their partner became an Animalian. But as I said... Animalians can be surprisingly adaptive to things. I think the first herm Dinotopian was an uplifted bird and took to hir condition quite easily. So, these peahens wouldn't necessarily have a problem."

    Paula breathed a sigh of relief and then thought for a moment. Shi'd had platonic friendships with the others that had joined hir and been especially good friends with Santaga and Amarina. However, while many of the others had had similar issues to Paula, there had never been any real romantic interest in them on Paula's part and things were rather platonic in their opinion of hir. The time with both Santaga and Amarina was fun, but that rapidly became a case of "friends with benefits."     "Some of Horizon's curiosity is more in what would happen with species that are known to have color differences between male and female," Judy admitted, "which is present in a lot of bird species... not all, but a lot of them. Thus, in the course of the uplifting, we're curious as to whether or not these peahens would gain the male's bright blue coloring that you have, or would they retain their more mundane coloration and only gain the long tailfeathers and... reproductive equipment... Testing on feathers that have been preened hasn’t revealed anything more than what many other tests with the Animalia Virus have already confirmed. It can't outright create something out of shed fur or feathers... or revive the dead."

    “Don’t worry, we aren’t,” Judy commented a small chuckle.

    Paula breathed a sigh of relief at that, "Okay then... then what will you need from me? Some of my milk or something?"

    "We actually have some of the formula used for your conversion, and we can use that to add do some water," Judy spoke, "which would work better with them right now. We've found that while non-mammal based Animalians will need and will drink milk from their mothers once they've been born or hatched, while they're still mundane animals... they don't and may not drink from the milk because of the scents to it."

    Paula nodded. The small group of peahens were mundane birds and they looked to hir on occasion, but the looks instinctively told Paula there would be the need to dance and display to hold that attention. Things that were instinctively part of how courtship between the males and females among peafowl worked. And while the idea of having a mate was a nice one and was there, Paula wasn't going to completely do things on instinct, and Animalians also saw the need for a sort of "barrier" between mundane animal and Animalian.

Shi ended up leaving the lab room to let Judy carry out her work and having some sense of an idea that it would be best that the peahens would deserve some privacy while they were uplifted. From there she watched as a couple of other Horizon workers came in carrying a large water tub, which likely had the herm variation of the Animalia Virus for birds that Paula carried. A few moments later they came out with the wire fencing that had been keeping the peafowl penned up. Judy was the last to come out and she shut and locked the door as she did so. She held up a thumb's up to Paula.

    "Not really," Judy answered, "they may not all drink at once, but they will all drink and that will lead to the biggest part of the year for them."

Paula closely approached the door and turned hir head to it, mostly to see if the newly uplifted Peafowl Animalians would have enough recognition to note that while many things in Horizon had been "soundproofed," things weren't entirely perfect.

Gaining those feathers, though, had also meant for a lot of work to keep them in good order and undamaged. The completeness Paula felt at being what shi was now was enough to make that work "worth it." Shi could only hope that these new Peafowl Animalians came out well and accepted their own changes.

That would give some indication that these three had all been female before their uplifting. They had also grown the long train of tail feathers, brightly colored and with the distinctive eyespots. However, going down their chests and torsos, there were more signs of their original gender. Non-Mammalian Animalians have also gained them and have had reason to use them to feed their own children as well... or at least the milk."

The penis and tail feathers... they're extra. When I became an Animalian... it was to be female. The herm treatment is the best Horizon's been able to manage."

    "Yes," Paula answered, "but I've liked the male's flashy colors and wanted that to... It would be good to dance with and provide some sense of entertainment... which is often aimed more at those that don't understand why mundane Peafowl are the way they are..."
