She quickly looks in the rearview mirror to see the three children already kissing and trying to fondle each other.

    “None that you can wear,” Spencer sticking out her tongue; “Let’s take these to my room Eric and Frieda.”

    “Are Eric and Frieda staying for dinner,” Evan calmly asks as he sighs watching the Seahawks quarterback getting sacked.
    “Not tonight Mr. Lawson,” Eric pulling himself away from Spencer; “Mom is waiting and Frieda needs to be dropped off as well.”
    “Okay,” Evan smiling; “I guess you three will have a lot to do this Tuesday were-panther gathering.”
    “You don’t mind that it’s on a school night Mr. Lawson,” Frieda asking.
    “Well it’s not like you were the one to plan it," Evan commenting; "It is a bit short notice but I always figured Spencer would of joined the werewolf pack I’m a part of.”
    “Yes but then I couldn’t bring Eric or Frieda along,” Spencer answering.
    “Alright say your good byes,” Evan still smiling; “and see if you can scrounge up some coffee for your grandfather.” Evan looking at Mike who has had one too many, “I don’t want him passing out before telling us the woman he met.”

    After saying good bye and promising to Skype later on; Eric and Frieda left Spencer and hopped back into Martha’s car. Before the two fourteen year old were-panthers could begin making out; Martha coughs to let them know she is still there.
    “Frieda can you please give me directions to your home?”
    “Oh I’m not heading home just yet,” Frieda answering; “I need to talk to my uncle.”
    Martha looks at the red headed girl as she starts kissing Eric she coughs again; “Still need directions.”

    ******

    Minutes later Martha was pulling up to a driveway of a single level house; seeing no car in the driveway she looks at Frieda; “It’s doesn’t look like your uncle is home. Perhaps I should take you home instead.”

    Frieda doesn’t answer she is on her phone texting.
    ‘Father, back in Moon Lake from shopping at the big box store; uncle Lance needs to talk to me about something. I think it has to do with elder council business. Will be having supper over at his place; I’ll have him take me home later. I have lots to talk to you about. Don’t worry it’s not bad news.’
    Frieda hits send and looks up at Martha; “Sorry, I was sending my father a text letting him know where I am. And it’s okay if my uncle isn’t home. I have a key to let myself in.”
    “Alright,” Martha still looking worried; “Eric and I will help bring your purchases inside.”

    All three get out of the car to grab Frieda’s bags form Awareness and the Big Box Store and head inside after Frieda unlocks the front door. A distinct smell of were-panther and were-tigress can be detected from the house as all three enter.
    “I guess my uncle had company last night or this morning,” Frieda commenting; “Good for him,” she smiles.
    “I still think I should take you home,” Martha unsure of leaving a young teenage girl in a strange house.
    “If it makes you feel any better,” Frieda trying to reassure Martha Anderson; “You can wait.” Looking at the time and seeing it’s almost 8pm; “Uncle Lance should be back any time now. He had some elder council business to deal with.”

    Martha still feeling worried was mollified at this and agreed it was best to stick around. Frieda grinning quickly removes her clothes and shifts into her fursona; a grey fur were-panther with russet highlights and feline eyes just as green as her human looking eyes. Eric seeing that the sun has set, also shucks off his clothes and quickly assumes his white were-panther fursona. Also sporting green feline eyes; both look at Martha and she sees their eyes while green, sparkle as if magic is about to happen. She grins ever so slightly, as Frieda leads Eric to the spare bedroom and can clearly hear the two giggle. With nothing better to do she takes a seat at the kitchen table and plays a game on her mobile phone; the beeps and other sounds coming from her game are the only noise to fill the house, save the yowling and purring coming from Eric and Frieda as they enjoy each others company.
