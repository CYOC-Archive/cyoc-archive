    Lia was a blue-and-yellow macaw. She was a "teen" in parrot years, and owned by a 18-
    year-old boy named Jim. Jim always took very good care of Lia, and knew he could tell her
    anything. He had no girlfriend, so he talked to her often.

    One day, he gave her water to drink, not knowing it was infected with the virus, and she
    drank some of it.

    When he left the room to go do something, she felt herself growing, so she flew out of her
    cage fast, and she grew into a very attractive parrot-woman, with long feather-hair, big
    wings, and a perfectly shaped hips and breasts. Her body was covered in feathers, so
    nothing could be seen. She could now make human facial expressions with her beak.

    Perhaps the biggest change was her brain - as a parrot, she was already smarter than most
    birds, but now that she was an anthro, that intelligence was translated over to make her
    smarter than most humans, so she was super-intelligent. She smiled in approval of her
    new self, and flew to where Jim was to surprise him.

    Jim turned, and saw her. "Who are you?" he asked "And how'd you get in here?"

    "It's me, Jim." she replied in a voice that sounded like a mix between parrot and teen girl
    "I'm Lia, your beloved pet. That water you gave me to drink turned me into this beautiful
    creature! Don't you think I'm beautiful, Jim?"

    "Uhh, yes." he replied, a bit nervous.

    She was about to hug him, but then stopped.

    "I'm sorry Jim, may I take a shower first?" she asked "I don't want to embrace you if I'm not
    sanitary."

    "Sure." he replied "Can you do it yourself?"

    "Of course." she replied "I've watched you do it, so I believe I'm more than capable."

    After she showered, and dried herself, she came back to him, hugged him with her wings,
    and since she couldn't kiss him, she gently nuzzled the side of his face with her beak.

    "Do you think you could buy mango-scented shampoo for me next time?" she asked.

    "Okay." he said "Now what?"

    "Why don't I help you with your math homework?" she suggested.

    "You don't have to." he said.

    "I know," she replied "but I want to."

    So after she helped him, she prepared a dinner for them of fruit and nuts. She cut all the
    fruit with a knife and a board using her feet while flapping in the air, and she carried all the
    food over to the table flying with her feet carrying it there. When she sat down, she used
    her feet to hold the fork and knife. Jim noticed Lia was very graceful in everything she did.

    "How did you learn to prepare fruit like that?" Jim asked.

    "I watched the Food Network." she replied "I've always wanted to do what I saw on TV, and
    now I can. If you drink the same water I drank, you'll become a parrot-man, and we could
    be so happy - just the two of us. What do you say, Jim?"
