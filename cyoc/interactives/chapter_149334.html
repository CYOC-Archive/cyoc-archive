    "Please turn me back," begs Shannon, "I'll do anything for you."
    "Oh so. Now you're begging?" Replies Brodie.
    "Please. I don't like being this small. I feel too vulnerable."
    "You don't like it do you?"
    "Why would I?"
    "I don't know," says Brodie, "Maybe it's because you made me turn my friends into children, and one of them into a doll."
    "Please. I'll forever be in your debt."
    "Hmm. Maybe," says Brodie, "On one condition."
    "Yeah?"
    "You become my pet/ slave."
    "Fine. Just please make me my original size again."

    Suddenly, without warning. A bird flies in through the open 'windows' of the car park and grabs Shannon in its claws. It flies back out of the car park, Shannon screaming his head off.
    "Wow," says Brodie, "Was not expecting that."
    "Ok. Don't shrink anyone," says Luke.
    "Yeah," says Braiden with his 10 year old voice, "I don't want to become part of the food cycle."
    "You want to see if you can turn Maggie back?" Asks Callum, still as a 10 year old too.
    "Oh yeah. Definitely," replies Brodie.

    Luke drops the Maggie doll onto the concrete floor. He pulls out the phone and unlocks it. He finds the app and opens it. He aims the camera at Maggie and selects the restore button. As message appears and Luke clicks confirm. There is a bright flash, and the sound of lighting hitting the ground. Within a second, this sound and brightness is gone. As his eyes adjust to the lighting, Luke sees Maggie standing there. She's her proper age and height. She looks releaved. "Thank God," she says, "I'm human again."
    "Ahh," screams Luke dropping the phone, making it shatter into tiny pieces on the ground, "That fucker electrocuted me."
    "You alright," asks Maggie concerned.
    "Yeah. It was only a small zap."
    "Great," says Callum like a somewhat angry child, "Me and Braiden are stuck like this."
    "Hey," says Maggie, "At least you two are pretty cute." Callum's face goes red with embarrassment.

    Brodie's phone vibrates. He reads it and says, "Right. That transformative phone is toast. No chance of repair."
    "Ugh," sighs Callum, "Dodgy technology."
    "But," continues Brodie, "Before it fucked up. It altered reality so Callum and Braiden are 10 year olds."
    "What?" Cries Callum.
    "Hold on," says Brodie, "You and Braiden are now part of one of our families."
    "You mean. I'm a little brother to one of you guys," asks Callum.
    "Yep," replies Brodie.
    "Who?"
