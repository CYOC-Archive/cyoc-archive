    You looked into a mirror.
    27 years old, 5'11, short brown hair and an you guess an average built. Fairly good shape. You wonder if he

    And why asking if someone is a man or a woman? What's up with that.

    You walk to the dressing rooms.
    There are several dozens small cabins in which people get dresses and undressed.

    Right before you want to step out of the stall you notice something carved into the wood. Of course vandalism
    is everywhere and to be honest, it is fun to carve your name in these kind of stalls but this message seems
    out of place.

    It says: Run, save yourself now you still can.

    'Okay,' you mumble to yourself. 'Apparently there are lunatics everywhere.'
    You shrug the thoughts about the message off and decide not to let yourself be interrupted from having a
    relaxed, great day at the pool.
    'Where were we? Oh yes, the part where I go swimming.' You start walking to what looks like the entrance but
    right before you can enter there is a man in uniform who stops you.

    'Excuse me sir?' He says
    You stop walking.
    'Have you taken your shower yet?'
    'What shower?' You ask.
    'It's policy sir. You have to take a shower before heading to the pools, that way we can guarantee that our
    pools stay clean and pure at all times.'
    'Policy?' You're getting a little bit annoyed by the weird policies this swimming pool has but after a deep
    breath you look up again.
    The man nods.
    He reminds me of someone you know, you can't put my finger on it but he seems familiar.

    You are getting brought back to the normal world when the man gestures towards some other entrance. Sounds of
    people showering are coming from there.
    You decide just to go along with the man and follow him to the showers.
    'Which one would you prefer?' He asks.
    You give him a questioning look.
    'Oh I'm sorry, I didn't know you were new here.'

    You nod. 'Well, I am, can you please hurry this up a bit and just tell me what to do so I can go swimming
    without any supervision?'
    The man doesn't looks moved in the slightest.
    'You have six different showers and thus six different rooms.' he says.

    What do you do now?
