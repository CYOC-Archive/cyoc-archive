    Julie thought of her daughter Sabrina. They used to be so close, but as Sabrina got older, she felt she was losing her little girl. Sabrina wanted to party and have fun, reveling in her independence. Julie wished that Sabrina would thing through her actions, and be a bit more cautious. There was no need to go out every night or drink like a fiend. Then there was the crowd Sabrina was hanging out with at college. Most of the girls were superficial party girls who were coasting through school, and the guys she kept bringing home had looks, but no substance. There was no way any of those men would be able to love and support Sabrina.

    Looking at the remote she noticed a setting labeled "Me". The remote was previously set to "People" for her previous test. Julie hoped that if she tried the function on Sabrina her daughter would think more like she did.

    Julie finished her jog, still debating using the remote on Sabrina as she went to the fridge to get a cold water bottle. When she had tried the remote on those two girls they had changed drastically, but Sabrina wasn't that different from her, was she?

    "Hey mom, how was your jog? I'm heading out soon to meet Heather and the girls." Julie heard Sabrina say. Julie turned to speak with her daughter, but was shocked by the younger woman's attire. Sabrina had too much make-up on, and her light blonde hair was in an up do. She was wearing a tiny shirt that was streched tightly against her cleavage, and didn't cover much else. Her shapely legs, and butt were hugged by a similarly small and tight set of hot pants. To accessorize Sabrina had a thin black choker and a couple bracelets.

    "You're going out like that!?" Julie asked incredulously.

    "Mom, this is pretty tame. I know a lot of girls who wear less than this out. It's just how fashion is. Besides, I bought these clothes, so what's the big deal?" Sabrina tried calming her mother. She was a little sick of these kind of reactions. She was 20 yeas old, and more than capable of making her way in the world. Maybe she didn't exactly know what she wanted to do yet, but she still had plenty of time to figure that out.

    Julie stared at Sabrina for a moment, imagining all of the possible scenarios for how the night could go wrong. 'What if somebody puts something in her drink? What if her friends leave her? What if she ends up pregnant?' Julie thought. She looked down at the remote again with resolve. If Sabrina just thought a little more like her, she wouldn't have any problems. Julie set the command and fired at Sabrina.

    At first it seemed like things had worked perfectly as Sabrina's clothes changed to something still appropriate for going out, but more conservative, and more mature in taste. Julie smiled at her success, but faltered as she noticed more changes happening. First Sabrina's hair started getting longer, darkening a little as it grew, a few strands of gray even finding their way in. Sabrina's features changed as she lost the innocent look of a young girl, and gained the experienced face of an older woman. Sabrina's trim tummy pooched out a little as she acquired a flabby potbelly. Her hips widened considerably, and her butt drooped a but lower. Sabrina's perky breasts grew fuller, before getting a bit longer, sagging a little. Soon Julie was seeing a version of her daughter that rivaled herself in age.

    "So come on Julie, let's go have some fun tonight." Sabrina shocked Julie out of her stupor. Julie was surprised by how Sabrina's voice was a bit lower and more sultry, but also laced with authority.

    "Wha-, what?" Julie stammered.

    "Come on it's girls night. We don't hang out nearly as much as we used to. You've been my best friend since elementary school, and I hate seeing you less than my bitchy boss." Sabrina said.

    "We aren't family?" Julie asked, curiosity and fear entering her mind.

    "I told you, I'm not really interested in your brother. You know you're still like the sister I never had." Sabrina sighed. Sometimes she wished they were sisters or something, but she just wasn't interested in being anything more than an honorary member of the Ryan family.
