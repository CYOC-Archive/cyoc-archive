    Many of the frightened girls-turned-donkey brayed in protest. Addison couldn't understand what they were trying to say, but their meaning was perfectly clear.

    Her companion started braying as well, betraying her fear.

    But to her surprise, her companion obeyed, staring at her in surprise. "I understood that," She said after a few seconds. "Are you talking?"

    "I'm Layla, by the way," She introduced herself.

    "I'm Addison."

    "I'd say nice to meet you, but it doesn't seem right," Layla snorted, lashing her tail.

    "I understand," said Addison. "How did they do this? Why turn us all into donkeys?"

    "For money," Layla answered, as if it should be obvious. "My dad is always saying that he needs donkeys for our farm. We're in high demand."

    Addison considered this, remembering a donkey on her dad's boss's farm that had died from being overworked.
    “But why only females?” Addison asked again.

    “My dad also says that female donkeys are more docile and loyal than the males, they live more and have practically the same strength and dexterity as male ones. Also the females can breed mules and produce donkey milk. The donkey milk is expensive and very appreciated. We are a good business”. Layla said with some pride.
