Jimmy knows he is going to have to lug the bed as well as the desk to the basement soon; the desk being used as a makeshift home office as he balances the books for the restaurant as well as managing the household finances. Having a knack for numbers, a gift he got from his late mother Victoria, Jimmy was able to get a degree in finance during the prior COVID lock-downs during 2020 to 2022. A year later he becomes the anthro stag-deer fury he is now. Staring at the two female tigress furies; one a mother who has protective grip around her daughter, Jimmy carefully wakes up Denise. Letting her know that they have returned and that Michael has her car outside parked on Edward Heartpaw’s driveway.

I don’t need to regal you of it,” Denise glaring at deer fury who was smiling wryly back at her; “I’m sure your mate filled you in.”

    When Alison was preparing the soup base, Roxy and Jimmy were shouting off items to add to the stock. Ingredients such as carrots, celery and mushrooms made the cut. However some of the more odd requests, such as marshmallows, chocolate chips and popcorn were vetoed by both Brittany and Denise. Also in the soup was barley, potatoes, onions as well as some peas. Radishes was another ingredient that the cubs requested and despite Brittany and Denise's earlier vetoes; that particular vegetable made the cut. The soup base consisted of garlic, canned chicken and canned ham, tomato paste, basil, parsley and other assorted herbs and spices. Along with the soup was fresh made garlic bread topped with cheese. This added aroma made everyone's stomach rumble as they gladly took a bowl and a slice of the bread and eagerly dug into the inviting concoction.

    “This could be a borderline stew,” Michael savaging his bowl; using the garlic bread to sop up the remaining juices as he contemplates licking the bowl clean.
    “And I thought I was hungry,” Jimmy S.R watching his father spend less time eating compared to sitting down; “would you like a spoon to eat it with?”
    “Funny,” the elder deer fury retorting; “hard work made us all hungry.”
    “The cold didn’t help much,” Edward chiming in as he does his best to savor the bowl as it warms his paws as he holds it; “it generally never gets this cold on the west side of the rocky mountains.”
    “Why is that,” Jimmy J.R looking up at his grandfather; his bowl of soup only half eaten as he watches everyone else devour theirs with gusto.
    “It all has to do with the mountains blocking the arctic air that usually comes down from Canada. Instead we typically get rain. Lots of it as we are close to the Pacific Ocean.”
    “But now and then,” Jimmy’s father adding as he puts down his empty bowl of soup; “mother nature throws a curve ball and lets us know who is in charge.”
    “This is one of those curve balls,” Jake speaking up; “and a right nasty one at that.”
    “Good news,” Brittany looking at her phone before putting it away; “no snowfall is forecasted for the next few days. It will be ten degrees warmer tomorrow than it currently is today, so you can play outside for a few hours before you start to freeze.”
    “Can we go sledding again daddy,” Roxy looking at her father; her bowl of soup almost empty as she looks excited for the fact of playing in the snow again.
    “Of course,” Michael smiling the looking at Denise; “and mom will join us.”

    Roxy looked excited at the fact that mom would join them in some snow fun and she soon gobbled up the rest of her soup. Alison adding some pickles in her bowl gets the usual groans from the men and a chuckle or two from Brittany and Denise; those that finished their soup quickly got up to get themselves another bowl. Being told to save some as Alison wants to send some over to her mother and her brother Adam; an anthro cougar cub who came down with a cold thanks to his energetic playtime in the snow the day prior.
    “Dusk and Dawn might like a bowl,” Brittany finishing her first helping; “and mom would like some.”
    “We still have plenty of leftover chili,” Lucas Soulfang putting his now empty bowl down; “I was thinking of sending some of that home with you and Jake.”
    “Just none of that spontaneous combustion hot sauce of yours,” Jake chuckling; “that stuff is nasty on the tongue.”
