    "What's wrong Eric?" Spencer asked mid way through their late night snack. Sitting crossed legged on the floor of Frieda's bedroom, the three teenagers were enjoying the leftovers Frieda had managed to find in her fridge. Normally after a vigorous session of love making the trio would go out hunting, but with the Were Alert in affect, cold spaghetti and meatballs would have to do.

    "Lost my appetite." Eric mutters. With nothing but a low rumbling in his stomach to distract him, Eric's mind kept drifting back to the events earlier in the night.

    He knew that what he did was in self defense, and had he not that man probably would have seen him in his fursona, or worse killed the three kids Eric was trying to protect. But that didn't stop Eric from feeling like crap. Scum or not, Eric killed a man. And while he didn't think he was in any danger of throwing up again, he certainly didn't feel up to eating more.

    "How can you do it?" Eric asked Spencer, locking eyes with her.

    "Kill someone?" Spenser assumed. Unlike Eric, she felt little to no qualms about the man she's killed earlier. Frieda remained silent as Spencer spoke, as she too was curious how her mate was so at ease with her actions. Frieda imagined that her response would be similar to Eric's. Though not as extreme, given that Eric was still Eric. Were or not, he was still that same awkward bookworm deep down. And very rarely do bookworms do anything violent, let alone murder someone.

    "I can't explain it. Maybe it's because my dad was a SEAL, not to mention most of his side of the family, but it I just don't react that way. I've never actually had to use lethal force before, but I grew up knowing that there are times when it's appropriate. Like if your family is threatened, or you find yourself in danger." Spencer explains.

    "Eric, I know you feel guilty, and I doubt there's anything I can really say to get you to feel otherwise, but just know you did the right thing."

    "Spencer's right, you're not the bad guy here Eric. If you hadn't acted you'd be dead, and so would the kids. You're a hero as far as we're concerned." Frieda added.

    Eric nodded, sighing briefly. They were right. And yet still Eric didn't ever want to raise his claws again. Not even for hunting, which he loved so much since becoming a were. Every time he looked at his paws he saw his fist colliding with that thugs chest, hearing the sickening crunch of his bones, and watching the life drain out of him.

    The girls tried to comfort Eric again, rubbing their bodies against his. But this time it didn't work. Eric was close to tears again, wishing he could erase the horrible memories from his mind. They just kept coming back, no matter how hard he tried to focus on something else.
