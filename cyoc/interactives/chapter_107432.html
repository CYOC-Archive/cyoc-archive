The female was a grey tabby with blue eyes named Jasmine.

    “Fine doctor,” Frances answers; “what can I do for you?”
    “Actually, I’m calling to refer a patient to you;” Abigail responding. “Do you have a pen and paper handy?”
    “Just a moment,” Frances responds as she walks out of the kitchen; passing by her adoptive son Ben as she heads to the small table by the front door. “Okay Doctor Robison, I’m ready with the details.”

    “A bi-polar were,” Frances looking ashen; “Does he know the risks for not taking the proper medication?”
    “Yes Frances,” Abigail answering; “I believe doctor Tora Tachibana explained that to him yesterday as he was visiting his wife in the hospital. He was shown the secured ward in the sub-basement; the feral ward. I think that convinced him to agree to being tested; what with his erratic behavior of late.”
    “And what is the patient’s name,” France quickly writing down some notes; “As well as the medication you started him on.”
    “Are you sitting down,” Abigail asking; “I’m referring Benton Knight. I already have him prescribed the usual medication to treat Bi-polar, but with each person different we will both need to see what works best and what doesn’t.”

    Frances not wanting anyone to hear what she is about to say rushes to her car for some privacy; “you got to be kidding. The man who tried to have my licensed revoked and Samantha removed from her job just because we are lesbians. That same Benton Knight?”
    “The very same,” Abigail answers; “also while I found this strange when I was about to give him a list of therapist to see, he asked if any of them also did anger management therapy.” Abigail continuing, “you were the only one on the list that had the qualifications and knew what could happen to a were if they left their bi-polar disorder untreated.”

    Frances was thinking for a bit; with her schedule the way it is she would have to rearrange her current patients to accommodate the new emergency. ‘What do I tell Martin and Melissa Stovall that I might not be able to see them for a few weeks? What do I tell the other patients?’
    “So much for a Christmas break,” Frances sighs; “I take it the elder council will want Benton to be a priority case?”
    “I would assume so,” Abigail wondering; “but since this revelation only happened this morning, I doubt all are aware of the matter yet. Benton’s brother Lance was present and from what I heard he is sitting on the elder council as the were-panther representative until Benton’s daughter is old enough to take the seat.”
    “So I should expect a scroll with the elder council’s seal on it in a few days,” Frances sighing; “You know how protective I am in regards to doctor patient confidentiality.”
    “So am I,” Abigail answering; “but if said patient is a risk to exposing existence of weres to the whole world, we have a duty to inform the council.”
    “Well, have his file sent to my office on Monday so I can review it;” Frances sighing. “I’ll contact him to set up an appointment soon after.”
    “Okay Frances,” Abigail responding; “and thank you.”

    Hanging up; Frances is sitting in her car thinking ‘how can this week get any worse?’

    Minutes later Frances re-enters the house to see Samantha with her purse and holding two active kittens.
    “Problem,” Samantha seeing the long look on her life partners face.
    “Just a new patient referral,” Frances sighing.
    “I thought you weren’t accepting any new patients until the New Year,” Samantha asking.
