    Harry was still fuming over all this "Lone Wolf" nonsense as he waited for the management to send down their liaison. Most of the park's staff
    had abandoned humanity shortly after the first wave of departures had tested the waters, but every time they sent someone down to speak with
    the huskies, it was always a human. He couldn't entirely fault them for relying on a little subconscious canine loyalty when Naomi was running
    the show, hers being a rather... forceful personality as it were. Now that he was the one attending these meetings though, the premise
    bothered him a fair bit. As the door across from him opened, he was also briefly shocked at just how strange they looked. Bald all over, like
    some horrible skin disease had made all their hair fall out.

    "What's this about granting 'VIP' status to to a bunch of rowdy punks? You realize we were already short staffed before Naomi left. The last
    thing we need is some sort of boneheaded 'special exemption' policy getting introduced the same day you flood the park with a bunch of self-
    entitled jackasses."

    "The fact that we are understaffed," said the human, with a look that stole enough of the fire from Harry's indignation for him to take a seat, "is
    the reason we are being so accommodating to this group."

    "So what? You figure they're going to be self-policing? Because I have news for you..."

    "No. I figure the donation they made to the company will keep this park fully funded for quite some time. This place runs up quite a few
    expenses you realize. Apart from paying the staff, maintaining the grounds, and keeping the guests well-fed, there's a host of other concerns.
    Developing new species templates, working out all the little problems they introduce. Training the medical staff to deal with all the subtle
    variations and potential drug interactions. There's a whole team of lawyers on the payroll now, just to ensure basic rights for those who leave
    the park in their new forms, not to mention all the lobbyists needed to keep the government from shutting the whole place down. This is still
    very much a growing company, and we can't afford to pass on this kind of generous offer."

    Harry sighed. Root of all evil indeed. "OK... and just what sort of 'special treatment' are they asking for in return? Bringing beer kegs and hot
    wings out into the forest for them?"

    "No, nothing that would damage the park's aesthetics. Apparently, they plan to hold some sort of formalized tournament, with 'official
    recognition of the titles prizes and punishments' for those involved, or some such nonsense. It's all about some sort of fraternity or country
    club or some such. Just inform your subordinates they're not to interfere with whatever silly little hazing rituals they have unless there's some
    form of life-threatening injury, make sure a medical team is on standby when they hold their little 'tournament,' and afterwards we are to
    'respect the new chain of command' they set up for themselves. Past that, just try and show a little more lenience with any minor infractions.
    From the sound of it, they're planning to make this a yearly tradition, and we could all benefit from that sort of dependable revenue."

    "This is a huge mistake. It may be before your time, but the last time something like this went on, someone was nearly killed, and we do not
    have the staffing to manage this sort of thing on top of regular patrols. I don't know if you heard, but-"

    "Again." The forceful tone snapped Harry to attention. "Hiring additional security staff is the top priority for allocating these new funds.
    Openings have already been posted, and with any luck you'll be seeing a sizable batch of new trainees by the end of the week. Now unless
    there were any further concerns, I suggest you pass this information along to the others, get some sleep, and do your best to make sure our
    latest guests have an enjoyable stay."

    Harry had quite a number of 'further concerns' in mind, but new better to voice them when clearly the matter was not up for debate. Instead, he
    simply said "I may know a person or two who would be willing to come back on board."
