    "Oh? What?"
    Then Samantha told Frances about the visit from Andrew Curtis and the revelation that Martin Stovall would be stepping down as Mayor of Moon Lake to become the new were-merman representative on the Elders' Council when Curtis retired.
    "REALLY?" was all Frances could say. "When?"
    "Right now I only have Curtis' word on all of this, since I've had little chance to talk to Martin this week because of Julie's legal matters," began Samantha. "Best I can figure out, Martin will step down after the first of the year at the latest, but doesn't take over for Curtis until after any outstanding matters the Council still has with Benton Knight are resolved."
    "Without breaking doctor/patient confidentiality, Benton has to prove 30 days of consistent mental stability before the Council will allow him to resume traveling outside of Moon Lake's town limits, so unless there's more going on between him and the Council than I'm aware of, I expect our mayor will probably be joining the Elders by the end of January. But does that mean..."
    "Yep. I'll be Mayor Pro-Tem until the regular election next November, unless I decide to run for the office myself."
    "So, should I congratulate you or..."
    "I don't know," admitted Samantha, holding Frances closer. "I'm happy as I am as Town Chancellor, and I always ran as an Independent for the position. I never wanted Martin's job. Not sure I do now."
    "Well, you've filled in for him before when Martin was unavailable," pointed out Frances.
    "Some days were easy. Others a pain in the ass. All that in fighting between the two parties vying for power. People always wanting to curry political favor. I don't see how Martin ever dealt with it all."
    "Well, you told me the only weakness Martin Stovall ever had in office was never being able to keep it in his pants, and you don't have to worry about that. You've got me," said Frances, snuggling up against her wife.
    "I know, but whether or not I decide to run for the office, there's still the Benton Knights of the world to deal with," pointed out Samantha.
    "The man will still have his rough edges, but he is trying to become a better person. Yet right now, who knows about this besides us, Curtis, his family, and the Stovalls?" wondered Frances.
    "The higher echelon of the Elders' Council for sure. I'm going to wait until it's closer to actually happening to tell Ben. Beyond that?" and Samantha's only answer to her own question was a shoulder shrug.
    "Well, we've had some trying times of late, especially dealing with my family," began Frances, moving to stare deeply into her wife's beautiful feline eyes, "but we've always been there for each other and we always will, Madame Mayor," she said, before kissing Samantha again.
