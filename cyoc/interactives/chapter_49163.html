    Mad Science

    Victor participates in a study to control athlete's foot- Uncontrollable Results

    added 13 years ago BM S O

    Simply put stem cell research gone awry.

    Victor H. our college guinea pig has signed up to earn class credit
    and cash by testing a new athlete's foot treatment. The treatment
    involves a biogen using human stem cells with the idea that the
    subject's foot will generate its own immunity to fungus. His right
    foot receives treatment with the stem cell product. His left foot is
    treated with traditional fungicide. Victor undergoes daily treatment
    for 6 weeks. At the end of six weeks he is to continue showering
    barefoot at the campus gym and at his dorm. The product will be a
    success if his right foot remains athlete's foot fungus free.

    After three weeks, Victor notices a slight swelling of his right foot,
    but the researchers dismiss this as a harmless side effect. When in
    week six the stem cell treatment is halted. The cells of Victor's
    right foot go wild producing their own rapidly dividing cells. The
    main effect is that Victor is now nicknamed Big Foot by his dorm
    mates. By the end of the seventh week, he's find it difficult to walk
    and finds that he is forced to hop around on his giant foot. The
    researchers had not anticipated any problems so they timed the seventh
    and eighth weeks to fall during winter break.

    The highly reactive stem cells are targeted to be foot cells and they
    begin to infect Victor's normal cells. By the end of the eighth week
    most of his body has been absorbed into his now humongous foot.

    On the morning of the ninth week, classes resume and the researchers
    find a giant foot waiting for them in their lab. At first they think
    it's a plastic joke, but are soon proved wrong. One of the researchers
    mistakenly examines Victor the foot with his bare hands. Inside of the
    week he is well on his way to morphing into a foot creature as well.

    Mutant adaptive qualities of the manfoot is that it retains it's
    sentience the brain tissue is apparently unaffected, but the muscle
    and other tissues are. The big foot is able to absorb oxygen that it
    needs from the atmosphere. and similarly is able to suck up water and
    nutrients through its sole. A night watchman's dog foolishly attacks
    Victor who stomps it, and then absorbs the crushed dog as nutrients.
    The night watchman draws his taser. In the morning, beside the giant
    bloody footprint only his watch, wallet, flashlight taser, buttons and
    zipper are found.
