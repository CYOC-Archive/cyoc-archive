    "First thing's first. Let's see if I can bring some spark back into this marriage. Let's see.. take a hair from each person, twist them together and burn in a candle's flame while speaking the following words.." Angela stopped reading aloud. Of course it wouldn't work, but no sense reading magic words out loud before the spell. But there was a problem, Jacob has started to go bald young and kept his head completely shaved, but maybe she could find another solution.

    She got up from the kitchen table and went upstairs to the bathroom. First she checked the trimmer Jacob used to keep his beard neat, removing the head as she'd seen him do a hundred times to clean it out, but all the hairs were cut to tiny little bits. Maybe it would do in a pinch. She looked around the bathroom, trying to find something and then she saw it.

    "Jacob.. I thought we agreed you wouldn't use the soap without a washcloth, but for once I'll forgive you." She wrinkled her nose slightly as she pulled the curly black hair off of the bar of soap in the shower. It was the problem with liking furry men, and it had taken her years to break him of the habit, but she guessed he still got lazy once in a while. She didn't think the spell had specified head hair either, here was hoping.

    Angela returned to the kitchen and found one of her scented candles and a lighter to take over to the table. "Sorry, Gran, gotta use what I have on hand." She intoned as she lit the candle then plucked a hair from her head with a small wince, and using her thumb and forefinger twirled her blond stand together with the coarse black hair from the bathroom.

    She gave the words she was supposed to say a few quiet reads before thrusting the hairs into the candle flame and saying them aloud. The smell of burning hair was hardly pleasant, but the cedar scent of the candle covered the worst of it. However as she spoke the words the candle flame went from yellow to blue to bright white, then winked out. A second later the yellow flame was back and dancing on the wick as if nothing happened. And it seemed nothing had happened. Other than the little trick with the candle, nothing.

    "Of course, what did you expect, actual magic? Grow up, Angela." She closed the book and carried it upstairs to keep safe in her closet. She then got changed to go and do the grocery shopping, having wasted enough of the day browsing through that book. Though almost as a test, she pulled out her phone and texted Jacob a quick 'Thinking of you love', maybe he would respond, who knew how that spell would work if it did?

    She thought about it the whole time she was shopping. Would it just make them more affectionate? Or was it something more active, like they'd become irresistible to each other? Would it be another honeymoon phase all over again? She just couldn't stop the thoughts circling her head. The only other thought to strike her was that maybe she needed to exercise more, sitting around the house seemed to be catching up with her as her clothes felt a little tight on her. She didn't even really notice when she plucked something off the top shelf when she normally needed to ask for help.

    She unloaded her register at the cart, still in her daydream as she watched the groceries getting scanned and bagged. "Nice tattoo." The words made Angela blink a few times.

    "What?"

    "I like your tattoo." The cashier said again between scans, motioning at Angela's arm. Sure enough she looked down and her forearm was decorated with an intricate and well-designed flame tattoo. Angela felt a sinking in her stomach. She must have done something wrong. The spell was changing her, but if it was going for soulmates it was off. Jacob made no secret he didn't like tattoos, there had been a big fight in the house earlier this year when Andy had asked to get one for his birthday.

    "Th.. thanks." She managed to stammer out, before paying and getting out to the car. She loaded up her groceries and got into the driver's seat. She had to reach down and adjust the seat, scooting it back a few inches as she was scrunched up over the steering wheel in the spot she normally kept it. She said a short prayer before lowering the sun visor and peering at herself in the small mirror. It was hard to get a good look but some things were definitely off. First, her blond hair was streaked with strands of fire red, and it had retreated a few inches, once brushing her shoulders she now sported more of a bob hairstyle, ending at her jaw. Jacob loved long blond hair. She also looked younger, most of the lines and creases in her face had faded away, she looked closer to thirty than her actual forty, the mirror was no good for a close look though. She shut the visor, took a few calming breaths and started driving home. The spell had worked, but unless she seriously mistook her husband's tastes or they changed, she had done something wrong.
