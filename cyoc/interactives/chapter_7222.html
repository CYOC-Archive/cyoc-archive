    "Yeah, make Chris rabbit-sized..."

    Sid put his arms on his hips and strode back to the table, glowering with a semi-perplexed expression on his face down at the diminutive Tyler. "Very funny. Put me back..."

    Tyler shook his head and laughed. "Come on, Sid... Look at it this way: it's fun. It's possibly the only good thing to come of this game so far! Besides, I'll change you back before my power runs out..."

    "You always did like big men," quipped Chris, putting his cell phone away in his pocket again. Reaching down, and trying not to stare up at the big predator to his left, Chris picked up the dice. "I think, though, it's my turn, now..."

    The dice clattered to the board with all of them standing, now. They watched as the black dots spun and danced their way across the board, finally coming to a halt with a "4" and a "6".

    "Not bad," said Chris to himself. "Another big roll... At this rate, I'll be there in no time!"

    All of them watched as his lapine pawn slid along the spaces, heading for its inevitable destination. Slowly, it came to a halt and Chris felt his heart start to race.

    A black space.

    He'd stopped on the only color yet unmarked.

    He swallowed hard and looked down to see what would happen.

    However, unlike the previous times, each and every black square did not light up with scrolling text. This time, just the single square he was one became populated with words. In plain and simple English, without a rhyme, they said:
    LOSE A TURN
    Do not roll the dice on your next turn.
    "Does ... does that mean, my turn's over?" he eventually asked, looking at the others.

    "I guess so, Chris," Sid rumbled, looking down at the smaller rabbit, curiously. "I think you -well- lucked out?"

    Chris nodded, mostly to himself, and sighed, relieved. "Ok, maybe we'll make it through this after all..." he said, to himself.

    Tyler nodded and leaned against the table, relieved.

    Silence filled the room. Finally, Sid spoke up, looking at the others, furtively. "Well, that's it for now. If you guys will excuse me, I need to go to my room and -uh- handle a few things..." He looked at the Incubus and -after a moment- shook his head. "I...I'll be back later..."

    With that, he walked past Chris towards his room. However, just behind him, the huge lion paused and turned. Looking down at the tall-eared form of his friend, he sighed. Leaning in, he hugged him and kissed Chris on the top of his head. "Your clothes have changed, hon; nice to have you back..."

    With that, and with a shocked look on Chris' face, the massive lion strode into his bedroom and shut the door.

    The first of the temporary changes had worn off ... Chris was no longer famous, wealthy or hated.
