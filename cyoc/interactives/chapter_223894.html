    Jason couldn't help himself. He had to know what it was like to be a customer at that store! He altered the settings so that the user of the app was always aware of changes, but no one else was. Then, he made himself the son of a farmer. After all, he didn't want the responsibility of running a farm himself.

    When he submitted the change, Jason suddenly found himself in the store, wearing only a diaper. His dad was next to him, an obvious diaper bulge around his waist under his overalls. Jason couldn't wait. He had to try it out. He dropped to a squat and began to grunt, pushing a massive load into his diaper.

    "I just don't believe that they actually improved the previous design," Jason's dad said, looking at a package of diapers. He looked at his son. "I was wondering when you were going to go. You didn't help me fertilize the fields this morning, after all." Jason finished with a loud sigh of relief. His dad shook his head. "Well, time to change you."

    Jason's dad brought him over to a bench and laid him down. In full view of anyone walking past the store, he removed Jason's diaper, putting it in a special bag. He then wiped Jason clean and taped a fresh diaper on. Jason promptly peed. At the hissing sound and visible yellowing of the diaper, Jason's dad chuckled.

    "Kids. Gotta dirty as many diapers as possible."

    "Just means more fertilizer, right?" Jason said. His dad chuckled again.

    "True enough. Come on, let's get going. We've got some work to do at home." Jason quickly used the app to give himself a pair of overalls like his dad, then followed him to the register. Once their supplies were purchased, they both left. As they walked, Jason could hear his dad grunting softly, but didn't realize what he was doing until his diaper began to sag under his overalls.

    "More manure, huh, dad?" Jason teased. His dad ruffled his hair.

    "Yup. Let's go spread it on the fields."

    Jason followed his dad, but discretely pulled up the app on his phone. He could continue, or go back to his normal life.
