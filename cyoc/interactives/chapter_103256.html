    "I can understand your dilemma. It's like having an unlimited expense account at a toy store, but being told you can only buy one thing, and needing that choice to last the rest of your life."
    "Must be different for naturally born weres, huh?" asked Claire.
    "I guess. Truthfully, never really thought about it before," admitted Ryan. "Werewolf is what I am."
    "I see. And if I do become something other than lupine, what happens to our kids?"
    "Well, were-science created this medicine that most weres simply refer to as the were birth control pill, because otherwise the world would have been overrun by weres generations ago. Besides a highly active libido, both men and women are highly fertile too, but our doctors haven't been quite as successful targeting the men for treatment, while they've managed to refine the ladies down to individual species."
    "I see," said Claire, not mentioning the fact that while she didn't think that was quite fair, that Ryan was blushing again.
    "Anyway, a woman stays on the pill until she's ready to be pregnant. Then she just stops taking it for a day or so, and the rest proceeds naturally. If you're asking me about the science stuff, genetics ain't my strong suit, but unless the couple luck out and produce a natural hybrid somehow, which I've never heard of*, boys tend to favor their fathers while the girls take after their mothers."
    "Okay. Now let's get back to our research," said Claire, cuddling next to Ryan while finding their place in the guide book.
