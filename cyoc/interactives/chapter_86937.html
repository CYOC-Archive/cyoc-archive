His parents may not like it, but if he decides to move, they can't stop him."

    "And so you plan to send them to me?" Selene asked. "To me and Elizabeth?"

    "Only you when we made the decision," Sally spoke, "we made the decision before head-sister Florencia transformed your husband, based on what you told us, and thus before Elizabeth decided to switch sisterhoods."

    "If she is okay with it, I am okay with it," Selene answered.

    "Emily?" he asked, as if expecting one of you to be his girlfriend. He did look rather curious. Sally floated nearby to let your sisterhood interact with him.

    "I was a man when a different head-sister transformed me," you explained, "we're kind of in the process of working to get my male self restored."

    "That would be impossible," Selene warned, "there are no male sirens. Any man turned into a siren would become female, as you can see."

    The teen nodded, "I was told that. I'd also figured maybe to find away we can all live together... share in that beauty rather than seeing each other as monsters."

    You found yourself giving a soft smile. The teen did clearly love mermaids and their world. It wasn't a personal love, but it was a kind of love all the same. And in a way you sympathized with him. You loved Selene and figured that once you were human male again you'd want the world to be such that everyone could live together.

It radiated from your hips and went through the rest of your body, though this tingle felt tight around your heart which beat stronger the more you felt it. As you felt it, your eyes widened. You were feeding, the boy's academic love of mermaids and their world and the desire to find a way for them all to live together, which ran with you hopes for the future with Selene and Elizabeth had formed a cycle of platonic love or love between friends. It wasn't the same as romantic love, the love between lovers, but it was love nonetheless, and you felt yourself growing stronger because of it.

    "Do you feel it?" you asked to Selene, your face looking visibly relieved.

    "Yes, you've made a friend," Selene smiled back, "and he is a good boy. You and he will be able to do much when your a man again. I just know it."

    "Uh... thanks?" the teen said slowly, somewhat lost on what was going on.

    "You're welcome young man," Selene said to him and pulled herself out of the water to sit on the rock beside him, "the world could use more men like you and your girlfriend will be very fortunate to have you as her boyfriend."

    The teen nodded, and as you watched you could almost feel the boys love for the girl, Emily, if her name was the name he addressed to the four of you. He then glanced over at your wife's womb.

    "You... you... you're pregnant," he stuttered.

    "Yes, yes I am," Selene answered, "we can get pregnant just like human women do. Despite having this tail where you have your legs... sirens are genetically compatible with humans... though our magic makes any and all children daughters..."

    "And no male sirens," the teen commented.

    Selene didn't immediately answer.

    "And you said Michelle is the father?" the teen asked, "or..."

    "Yes, before a rival of mine transformed him from Michael to Michelle, Michael conceived my child... my daughter, and love Michael very much and he loved me very much as a man and still loves me as a siren," Selene spoke, "but as a man, his love for me fed me and I didn't need to feed on anyone and that mutual link between us strengthened him and kept him looking young. You and your girlfriend will enjoy that link as well and will be happy together. She won't need to kill anyone to feed and you'd be with the one you love."

    "Yes, your councilor told me that," the teen nodded. "So... do you lay eggs like fish do? Or...?"

    "We give birth like any mammal," Selene answered, "and if things go well... you'll be there when I give birth."

    "I will?" the teen asked.

    It was at this time that Sally came forward.

    "Your girlfriend, Emily doesn't want to stay with my sisterhood, she doesn't want to be reminded of the attack on her, she would prefer to remain with her folks, who live in the same town as Selene, Elizabeth, and Michelle/Michael," Sally explained, "the present plan is to let her stay with Selene's sisterhood."

    "You would be more than welcome to come with her to be with us as well," Selene answered, "we can set things up for what you and your girlfriend want to do with regard to college and we'd keep you together."

    "And we'd be a big happy family!" Elizabeth said with a smile, "three sisters, two brothers, and a baby! Wouldn't that be fun with all of us together?"

    "I suppose it would," the boy answered, "but I'd want that to be Emily's choice."

    "And we're now ready to take you to her," Sally commented, "We'll stay at the surface and Michelle/Michael and Elizabeth will be more than willing to help if you have trouble swimming."
