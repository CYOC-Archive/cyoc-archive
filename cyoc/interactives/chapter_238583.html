    “What? Panicked about what? Panicked about… me getting away?” That didn’t make sense to Colin at all, adrenaline was still rushing through his veins, his heart was still pounding. He opened up her phone again, flicking to her Yiffr app, trying to make sense of what happened.

    “I told you I’d never hurt you! I mean – that one time I just wasn’t thinking, but I… I promise to think about it all the time from now on and-” She trailed off, Colin hearing a thump from the other side of the door as her head pressed against it, like she was listening to something intently. Through the sound of both their breathing, Alex’s phone made little rumbles and buzzes as Colin looked through the app, coming across a big red button at the bottom of her page the read “Revert Changes”.

    “NOUUR!” NONONO! NO! PLEASE!” Alex screamed from the other side of the door like she was getting electrocuted. She screamed like she knew exactly what her brother was looking at now – as if she knew exactly what was going to do.

    “Please! PLLLEAASEE! DON’T CHANGE ME BACK!” Her voice soared in a desperate plea. “I just don’t know what’s fucking going on, okay!? Just please! I’m so close to being a real girl! Please don’t make me go back yet!”

    Her voice dampened to a shuddering cry as she listened for a response, but Colin didn’t speak, he just starred at the button on the screen. All he had to do was press it and it all would go back to normal. It was right there.

    Alex’s body still pushed, tensing on the other side of the door as if she was preparing to be de-lion-ified at any moment.

    “I just don’t know… what’s going on with me!” She repeated, her brother feeling her trembling from the other side of the door. “It’s too much for me to think about all at once and I don’t know why I wanted to lick you so bad! I don’t even know if lion brains are hooked up right to think about all the stuff that’s going on in my head! Please, just wait.”

    Colin shrunk away from the door, taking a step back as he finally responded. “I mean… Okay… that part about things being hard to make sense of… makes sense…” He huffed out, a little tremble running down his spine as he prepared to defy his big sister. “But you’re big and scary now and… you’re acting wild.”

    “I-I know.” Came a sullen voice panting from the door, Colin hearing the lioness’s paws gliding down the wood, claws cutting against the door until she apparently was back down on all fours. “I really don’t mean it though. I don’t want to hurt you. I’m just not used to being this way yet! It feels so… good… like you wouldn’t believe…”

    “Alex…” her brother shuddered out a sigh, trying to inject a dose of reality. “Mom’s not going to accept a lion in the house. You’re going to need to change back sooner or later.”

    “I know… It’s just… I need to go all the way before I change back!” Alex begged, nudging her head against the other side of the door. “I’ll do anything, anything you want. I just want to be a girl… I just want to be Nadia…”

    “Alex, I told you. You’re a girl to me.” Colin reassured her in a sullen tone, “You don’t need to be Nadia.”

    That didn’t seem to help. She seemed to be sobbing, huffing in trembling gasps of air. Colin felt a tinge of sorrow wrinkle down his spine. She really wanted to be that lioness from the movie so bad, she must have wanted it for a long time, maybe since they were little kids. It would have been so much easier if she’d only just turned into something less ferocious and dangerous, but no. She NEEDED to be Nadia.

    What should he do?
