    The next memory started with everyone around Isaac in a state of panic. Either they were casting spells or cowering in fear, and some were even fleeing from the room. Whatever was happening at that time, it gave Isaac a feeling of dread as his hands gone cold.

    ...

    “Hope! Run back to your room!” a voice called out for her. Hope only turned to see one of Crawnav’s assistants, who was casting spells left and right, mixing words and syllables from incantations with warnings for Hope.

    “Where’s Daddy?!” Hope asked them.

    “I don’t know! But you need to go to your room now!” he kept casting warding spells. The thing which the wizards were fighting against or repelling came from the hallway going down, so Hope couldn’t see what made everyone panic. And soon, she felt like panicking as well.

    “But I need to know where Daddy is!” she pleaded, almost on the verge of crying with everyone else who cowered in the corners.

    “Ghh… get out now! I’ll seal this room to prevent the corruption from spreading!”

    Hope wasn’t getting any answers, but she finally acquiesced as she ran to her room to hide under her bed. She shut the door behind her, the warding runes working their magic as she couldn’t feel the strange magic in the air. However, she could only hear music coming from her music box and the sounds of her toys. Yet, she was crying profusely and wishing for her father’s comfort.

    Time didn’t seem to pass in the shut room. Hope tried to listen through the door but the magicaly sealed door was sound proof. Everything was silent. It was like nothing happened. Her room was jus as peaceful as when she left. But she knew something terrible was happening right now. But something inside of her was telling her to NOT open the door. So she locked it instead. Hope, with her mind numb, has went to bed. It took hours but the 10 year old girl finally fell into a restless sleep.

    The next day, or rather, when Hope woke up, her room was exactly as it was the last time she woke up. Except that Pipi wasn't with her anymore...

    Taking only an old wooden staff that her father used to use, she peeked outside her room only to see— no, feel something had gone terribly wrong.
    The walls were covered in purple fibers that almost looked like veins. They spread everywhere save for the warded door, which was why Hope was able to open the door without difficulty. The floor was covered in the grotesque fibers as well, and it… pulsated as Hope shivered from the sight of it. Part of her tells her to NOT go outside. Maybe she’ll just wait until her father comes back…

    The days after that event were always filled with anxiety and worry for Hope. There was enough food for her to go for weeks, but she worried on what she’ll do when there was no more food anymore. As she sat in the room for hours, she wondered if her father would come back home, but she was sure that he would never leave her side. Everyday, she prayed to all the deities she knew to bring her father back, safe and sound. Everyday, she tried to save whatever food was left, trying to eat smaller portions per day.

    However, the day came. She had run out of food, the chest containing their preserved goods and treats devoid of anything. Hope felt hungry as she needed to eat, but the black fibers outside her room were still there. Worse, they discolored the ground and walls as it all looked like stone. It was almost as if the fibers were eating the walls and floors.

    ‘I need to find food… and Daddy as well,’ she thought to herself as she took the staff again and a satchel to gather food while venturing out the corrupted caverns. She took a deep breath as she stepped down on the corrupted fiber outside her room.

    She gasped in surprise as she touched the ground outside her room.

    …

    The memory only stopped there, and Isaac was left confused to what was the memory about.

    Isaac tried to piece together the events. The wizards were fighting off… something. Hope couldn’t see it, and he couldn’t as well. From Grevialus’ letter, he knew that Crawnav worked on some teleportation spell, but it required going into another dimension, one which was a world similar, if not Hell itself. It was cut off as well, so he must’ve written it during the event… but where did Crawnav go? He must’ve had time to go to his study room from the letter there when Isaac found the ring. Did he flee or did he perish?

    Isaac had a feeling that Calmach didn’t have a direct connection to the corruption, but a small part of Isaac tells him that Calmach's invention of magic must’ve led to the spread of corruption. The Ring of Purity must’ve been created to fight off the corruption, though if that was the event when the corruption spread all throughout the caverns, then how did Crawnav forge the Ring of Purity? If the Ring’s purpose is to prevent corruption, then surely they must’ve had a problem like the one he saw in Hope’s memory. So, the question is “When did that memory occur?”. The Dark Knights used corruption for their own gain; then, the Paladins succeeded them and fought against it, so corruption must’ve existed for a long time.

    “I see you’re trying to figure out this puzzle that plagues your mind,” Angela said as she observed Isaac’s hypothesis. “The creation of the Ring must be perplexing for you to understand, but all of that should be explained in the next few memories."

    "How many memories are you planing on showing me? My friends needs me."

    Angela simply smiled. "Not too much longer. Time flows differently in the dreamscape then in the real world. The flow of time here is very random. A few minutes here can be a full day in the real world. But the exact opposite can happen as well. A whole week could pass here and a minute would pass in the real world. It all depends on your perception."

    "Id's still rather wake up as soon as possible. But answer my questions first? What happened to Hope?” Isaac only asked. Angela didn’t answer.

    “Hope was left in the room but had to leave due to hunger. Did she…?”

    “No, she didn’t die,” Angela quickly answered, sensing Isaac’s thoughts.

    “But where’s she now?”

    “I think it’d be best to leave that piece of information vague for now,” she said. To his chagrin, Isaac’s questions were left unanswered. The caprice of a goddess was quite annoying.

    “I don’t think it’s caprice,” Angela replied to his thoughts. “I’d rather keep some facts from you because it would impede you on your mission.”

    "You mean my mission to purge the material plane of corruption?" Isaac asked.

    "Yes, but you also have duties as the deity of purity. Let me ask you this, would you willingly give up your life as a human? Would you give up your identity to become a comepletly different person?" Angela seems to be passionate about her questions.

    "Of course not. Giving up who you are is the same as killing yourself. That's why I prefer to be male. I wouldn't want to lose my identity as a man. I'm a man at heart." Isaac proclaimed.

    Angela looked sad. "But what if you had no choice? What if you were changed against your will?"

    "So as long as "I" am still around, I would fight for my identity to the bitter end. You remember when Azaviel tried to take me over? I held on to keep from losing myself. I won't bend over to other people's wills and give in to temptation." Isaac said proudly.

    Angela seems to agree with Isaac. "Very well. I shall show you the rest of the memories I want to show you. But be prepared for any twists and turns you might see."

    Before Isaac could respond, he was engulfed by another memory.
