    Jacobson scurried away from the group quickly out distancing the others as they completed their changes. The muscular grey wolf opted to seize the most strategic location of the town. Nestled above the town on a small hill was the police station. From there her team could cut off outside communication and all emergency personnel...

    Lt. Shawn Peterson was the desk officer for the night. Pulling night duty always sucked especially in a small town because nothing EVER happened. After saying good night to Nancy the PD's hard working secretary Shawn started the nightly ritual of locking all the doors and window. With that out of the way Lt. Peterson walked to the arms room to do the daily count...

    Nancy's mind was on work, family and dinner not on the road. While fumbling with her 97 Taurus's radio she failed to notice the grey blur cross the road towards the police station...

    "Serial number 15689-127.... Check" Shawn placed the service 9mm back into the arms rack. "Only halfway done there's gotta be a better way to do this." This never used to be necessary but ever since that dumb ass Matt drew his weapon to go hunting daily weapons checks were mandated by the chief. Shawn thinks to himself...

    Licking her black nose Jacobson thinks of the best way to get inside the police department. It's a small building and all the windows and doors are locked. No sky lights nor basement windows so the easy obvious ways are out. Looks like I get to do it the old fashioned way Jacobson thinks to herself as she punches through a side window. She quickly jumps through into a common looking office. Lights are off and she pauses for a minute to listen for any response. Hearing none she proceeds to cut all the phone lines with her razor sharp claws. Judging from the lone car out front there should only be one to two police officers in this station. No sense in taking any unnecessary risks in alerting the town to her teams presence...

    The sound of breaking glass quickly awakes Lt. Peterson from his slight doze. That was odd just checked the building and nothing was broken a few minutes ago. Probably just a stupid bat or something flying into a window. But I need a break so I mite as well check it out. Shawn thinks to himself as he rises from the desk in the arms room...

    Twitching her ears towards the sound of foot steps Jacobson quickly finishes cutting the remaining phone line. This station is totally isolated, now to change the watch. The werewolf almost smiles bearing her sharp canines as she takes her position hiding behind the door…

    Shawn’s adrenalin starts to kick up as he hears a rustle coming from the office. That sounds bigger than a bat he thinks to himself and he unlatches the safety latch on his holster. He quietly approaches the old wooden door to the office. Erring on the side of caution Shawn gives the door a swift kick in. that’s when everything seemed to go to hell. After the door swung open a muffled yelp came from behind the door. Shawn took a few steps backwards quickly as the door splintered in half…

    Furious the werewolf curses herself for underestimating this human and committing a rookie mistake. Have to move fast now before the humans are alarmed. Jacobson punches the door with both fists in the center of the door and rips outward sending both halves spiraling into the walls with a deafening crash. Dropping to all fours she rounds the corner extremely pissed at being surprised by this human, he as she quickly determines will be her first convert from this dimension…

    Shawn had only enough time to half draw his service sidearm before he had to dodge the flying pieces of wood that used to be the office door. The Lt. almost froze as he saw it. It looked like a gigantic wolf at least twice as big as a normal one and defiantly a lot more pissed off. Without thinking Shawn fires as he continues to raise his weapon. His first shot is low striking the marbled floor and sending fragments everywhere. His second strikes in the wolf’s right front elbow joint. The remaining 7 shots imbed themselves into the wolf’s skidding body. Quickly reloading the Lt. steps back a few paces as the wolf is obviously alive. Even more surprising is right before his eyes the bullet wounds seem to be closing up and the bleeding has entirely stopped…
