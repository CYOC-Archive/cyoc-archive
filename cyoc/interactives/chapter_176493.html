    The portal between the two worlds seemed to both long and short at the same time as Karen and Kat went through it. Princess Amara had explained how the transformation would work before opening the portal to the merpeople's world and some of it seemed rather interesting and odd, in a way. That they would essentially join with another creature and yet as the two of them merged, their human mind would dominate the creature that they were joined to. From there, the "mind" that the creature had before that would die off as they became mermaids.

    Karen was smart enough to know that that wasn't quite like how things had gone in many of the movies she remembered growing up with or taking Taylor, Angelia, and Rose to see, but then real life was often different in various ways from stories. And at present, Karen really didn't care about the old stories of mermaids or the magic with them. All she wanted was to see Taylor again, and if that meant becoming a mermaid, herself, she didn't care what pain she'd have to go through or how long it would take, or even what she'd give up to do so. Karen and Kat walked through it with ease, though Karen found herself steadying Kat a bit, probably given the alcohol that Kat had taken in. Magical portals didn't cure one of being "buzzed." As they went through, Karen noted that the pathway seemed lengthy in a swirl of bluish energy with a current of electricity that went along with it. They went through it, and while it looked to the eye like a lengthy portal, they actually made it through to the other side of the portal in the matter of seconds. They emerged into the air on a tropical looking island and on the edge of a small lake at the edge of a mountain.

    "This looks like the place the mermaid said what comes next would begin," Karen commented as they stepped and and found the area on the edge of the lake having rather soft sand.

    Karen looked out over the lake and noted its dimensions and its near crystal clear color. The sun was setting in the west, but for the moment it was just light enough that they could look into the water and notice that it was clear, noting a white sandy bottom. This well all the way up to the mountain where she thought she saw an arch in the rock as though the pool lead into a cave or some other underground structure. It was odd, but with her memories of losing her husband, she couldn't lose her daughter and thus didn't care. She helped Kat toward the edge of the lake and sat the other young woman down. From there she sat down beside her and began undoing her shoes and socks. To some extent they were already wet from when they'd been swept into the water to meet with Princess Amara, but at the same time Karen also knew that this specialized and "holy" fish that would join with them couldn't "digest" the materials in shoes well.

Karen let that be for the moment, as Kat was likely still under the effect of the alcohol. From what Princess Amara had said to them, Karen thought the director of the facility that handled the transformation of humans to merpeople would come to greet them. Yet, no one had come out.

    "Don't you think there should be someone to greet us?" Karen asked carefully as she stood up feeling the warm and soft sand rub on the soles of her feet now that she had her shoes and socks off. "I mean... we were invited to join their society and thus we know about them. So it shouldn't be like they have to hide themselves from us..."

    "Who knows? Don't care!" Kat answered and moved more into the lake's water and eventually dove in while calling, "I'm coming Amy!"

    Karen was hesitant for a moment, but knowing that this was part of her move to stay close to her daughter, she made her choice and walked forward to step into the lake. Karen didn't enter the lake as quickly as Kat did, but she did so with her own determination to join her daughter. She could look out to see Kat almost dancing as she walked out and got to the point where she had to swim and tread water. It let her splash about for a few moments, and Karen wanted to feel that was just a young college graduate's excitement of seeing her lover again in the future. It least that was Karen's hope. With the clear water, even in the low light of dusk, she could movement of the "holy animal" to the merpeople. It was about the same length as a human, but had some build that was different from most normal fish. It's head was very wide, and Karen thought she could see the mouth was lined with teeth and it moved quickly up to where Kat was in the water. It was enough for the "mother" in Karen to call out to Kat, even though Princess Amara had told them that the process of transformation from human to merperson essentially started with being bitten by the "holy animal" before it and and the human merged to become the new merperson.

Kat thought she saw a look of panic, but wasn't fully sure on what as she kicked her legs slowly as part of the process of treading water. It wasn't until she got a nip at her foot that she became more aware and she looked down to see that one of the "holy animal" had caught her right foot in its mouth and she could see the outline of its lower jaw bones.
