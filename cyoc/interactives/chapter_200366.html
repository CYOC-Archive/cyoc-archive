Sure being a dog meant you couldn't do your normal activities, but you were sure you could come up with something fun to do. 'Hell this entire thing wasn't going to be a punishment at all, more like a vacation from myself.' You cursed your luck as you drifted off to sleep. You dreamed of crawling through a narrow tunnel on your hands and knees. It might be a sewage pipe it was wet and smelly enough. Your mom glanced down, and held out a piece of sausage. You tried to snap at it, but your mom pulled away and said, "No, this is for good dogs. This is for Stella. Your breakfast is over there." Your mom pointed at a bowl of dry dog food and a bowl of water. "I'll save a piece for you if you do well at obedience school." Your mom gave Stella a piece of hot sausage as she put the rest into the refrigerator. Cursing to yourself, you wonder to the bowl where you reluctantly had a few mouthfuls of the disgusting smelling sorry excuse for food.

The three of you made it to the school and you were quickly remembered that Stella was a smaller breed of dog. You had been around your mom and Belle for the last few hours and them being so much large than you didn't bother you that much, but being dwarfed next to your current peers was a bit intimidating. The class started off well, the teacher gave some sort of speech to the owners, you didn't pay much attention. Then some basic commands started and you were the instant star of the class hitting every command on the spot. You did it all, sitting, shaking hands, rolling over. The owners were all complimenting your sister for your good work, including some hotshot college freshman who was spending a little too much time talking to. You made your way over to them to break up the conversation, but the college boy's dog, a muscular greyhound stood between you. The two of you looked at each other for a beat, but the greyhound made the first move breaking his gaze and going around to your hind end and putting his cold nose right up on you. In a panic you barked, well, more like yipped at the greyhound who countered your yip with what felt like an earth rattling bark that was more like a roar to you.

Belle said sternly, "You just wait until your mom hears about this!"

You looked up to Belle shaking your head, this did not sound like any weekend getaway you wanted to be involved with.
