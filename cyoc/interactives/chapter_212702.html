    Dan was trying to finish his wish to return everyone to normal when they heard the basement door bang open. "Bill, are you still here??" It was Bill's 5-year-old little sister, Molly. Usually Bill would let her come downstairs to play with him and his friends for a while when she got home from school, but now was definitely not the time. Bill yelled as loud as he could with her little lungs to keep his sister from coming down but it wasn't enough. Molly, not hearing an answer, went down to investigate.

    Everyone was instantly startled and panicked. Unable to tear themselves from each other's hands, they flew as best they could towards a shelf very close to the basement ceiling and hid behind a dusty box, however, on the way they ended up dropping the necklace on the floor, right in front of a curious Molly who had just come down.

    "What is this?..." to Molly the necklace just looked like an accessory like the ones her mom used, she looked up to try to see where it had come from but, since the basement ceiling was very high and it wasn't very well lit, saw nothing. She took the necklace and thinking that maybe her brother was playing a prank on her, she kept looking for him. She searched under the table, behind several boxes and even in a dusty old closet they had but she didn't find her brother. Disappointed at not having her afternoon of games today, she sat down on one of the chairs by the table and said "I wish my brother was here..." and sighed.

    On the other hand, on the shelf, everyone was waiting for what could happen since Molly took the necklace. As soon as Molly finished saying her wish, Bill began to feel an intense force pulling him off the shelf. He tried to grab hold of him and the others tried to pull him back, but they finally failed and Bill (with everyone else behind him) went flying back towards the table, right in front of Molly.

    They fell and rolled noisily on the table, making Molly immediately turn to see them, in front of the 5-year-old girl were 4 real fairies, a little later she was able to recognize that the green-haired fairy was actually her own brother! "Bill! Is that you??" Said the girl getting a little closer. "How did you become a fairy??"

    "Molly! Please, just drop the necklace and go upstairs, I'll explain everything later!" Bill said quickly that he was in front of the other three boys, but since his hands were still attached, his back was to Molly.

    "I won't leave until you tell me since when you were a fairy and why didn't you tell me sooner!!" Molly said somewhat annoyed but equally fascinated that the fairies were real.

    "No, Molly, it's not what it seems, please listen to me and I'll explain later, this is just a curse!"

    "Curse? How can being a fairy be a curse?? And why are you acting so weird? You don't even turn to look at me" Molly was frankly frustrated by the situation, which made the fairy boys scared, what a frustrated toddler can say always exceed expectations.

    "Molly, please calm down, I swear I'll explain everything, but now is not the time, just let go of the collar" Bill was trying his best to calm Molly down but, again, it wasn't enough.

    "I wish you could trust me and tell me now!!" Molly cried on the verge of tears.

    "It was the necklace! It was the necklace, okay? When you grab it and make a wish it comes true, certain things happened and my friends and I ended up like this." The words were almost forced out of Bill's mouth.

    Then, Molly's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Is that true? You swear to me??" Then Molly turned back to her necklace.

    "Yes! That's right! And if you don't plan on going upstairs at all, help us get back to normal, please!"

    "What should I wish then?" Molly asked, not sure what to say, to which the boys start making suggestions.
