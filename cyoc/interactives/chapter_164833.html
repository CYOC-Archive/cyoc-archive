    "...what a nightmare... what a great nightmare" Nina Lowers cried as land drew further and further away and with it more and more of her dreams and future.

    "should have thought of that beforehand... long before putting everything on that worthless bet missy" her mother firmly countered while retaining her eye on Nina "least even though I personally think that you don't deserve it even though somehow that poor lady who's family tree you permanently damaged somehow saw otherwise". Nina would have said more but what else was left to say now.

    Still... of all those to hit this huge run of bad luck it just had to be her. after all, all she had been doing back in February was running an errand that involved her driving into town in the car her grandfather had brought her, used of course but good quality and affordable... but right at this crossing with a green traffic light for her some arrogant speedster in a flashy brand new convertible, most likely drunk behind his wheel, rammed right into her and sent her skidding into a corner...

    ...a corner that tragically had a young mother and her preschool aged son that got caught in the crash, mother had survived albeit lost an arm but her son was killed. Nina told the god to honest truth to the police at the hospital after the crash but the next day they returned to arrest her for dangerous driving and ignoring traffic... only reason she wasn't going down for the child's death was due to the mother refusing to have Nina as a scapegoat. Still as a result of the crash Nina had to take her exams in the detention centre, her car written off, being classified as a insurance risk behind the wheel and now to perhaps salvage as much university criteria as possible had to spend her summer as a staff member to a summer camp.

    "well here we are" Nina's mother announced as the boat they had been riding reached the campgrounds. "see you-" Nina started. "not a chance young lady. judge insisted you remained monitored and that means we don't part until we meet concealer Evans" Nina's mother interrupted. "gezz thanks" Nina mumbled in response as the pair headed onward.

    "oh... little old to be escorted are you?" this kind looking African man in the camp's t-shirt remarked upon seeing Nina and her mother while giving Nina a look down. at 17 Nina was not like most girls, keeping her raven hair messy and in a low ponytail, currently dressed in a black shirt over a green sleeves jacket, baggy jeans and white sneakers with her things being carried in her green backpack and black suitcase. "you concealer Evans?" Nina asked back. "that I am" the man agreed. "Nina Lowers. I believe i'm expected" Nina answered. "ah yes. our new court appointed staff member" Evans responded. "alright. I know he was told everything" Nina's mother accepted "now keep in mind this is your only chance young lady to assure neither of my kids become official criminals". "yes mum" Nina assured.

    "so you have a sibling. older or younger?" Evans asked once Nina's mother had left. "14...and now mum's prime focus at university" Nina sadly answered. her brother Luke was more athlete and prankster then brains with his main focus being professional volleyball. yet since the trail her mother had committed everything to making him look far brighter and mature then he really was.

    "so what exactly am I doing for you for the summer?" Nina then asked as Evans showed her to the main office. "aren't you going to ask why this is called merfolk camp?" Evans returned.
