    The cat led Shedra into a different room, that had little in it aside from a crib and a box of toys.
    "I'll keep you in a different room from the kittens, because you're..." She saw the dragon woman look at her with a dumb look on her face.
    "...a big girl! Yes, you're a big girl, so you get your own room!" She realized how patronizing she sounded, but decided it was fine,
    considering the fact that the dragon woman didn't seem to be smart enough to understand her.
    The young cat opened the side of the crib and had Shedra crawl inside, before closing it again and handing her a few cat toys.
    "Play with those while i'm gone, i'll be back once i've found some diapers in your size."
    She looked and the tall woman that barely fit inside the crib. "...and maybe a bigger bed."
    The cat left the room with a sigh.
    Shedra played with the toys for a few more minutes, before stealthily looking around the room.
    The shocks really had fried her brain, but only for a short while, her serum quickly let her return to normal.
    She thought that she would be able to take advantage of the situation, if they believed that she'd been turned into a drooling idiot,
    and even went as far as to soil herself to make them let their guard down. And it had worked.
    Or atleast she initially thought it did. 'How important are their babies to them for the nursery to be like this?'
    She had noticed that the entire nursery was secured like Fort Knox.
    Shedra could tell that there were extremely durable metall plates behind the wallpaper, the doors were made of thick steel with magnetic locks and there were several cameras in every room. She had entirely underestimated how much the WildKats cared about their children.
    'It would have probably been easier to escape if i had just pretended to lose my memories.'
    She didn't forget to slobber all over one of the toys while thinking about her escape plan. 'I have to make sure they don't get suspicious.'
    She dropped the toy and started chewing on one of the wooden bars of the crib. 'I will have to find a way to see more of the nursery.'
    There was little she could do for now, so she forced herself to fall asleep.
