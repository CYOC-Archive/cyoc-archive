    Much of the Lunar New Year was something that really played more to those of Asian ancestry, and primarily from Japan and China. For the members of Animalia Ambassadoria, this celebration was very much a new thing that came about after Animalia formed and gained some means of contact between the people of Lyre and the outside world. This soon included some other parts of the celebration that soon gained some attraction to some of those that were in Animalia for the Lunar New Year Celebration and recognized some of the Animalia Ambassadoria members that were present.

    "This would be a lot more fun if it wasn't so cold at night," JC said to Sophie and Roland as he and Pauline were with the two Pterodactylus Dinotopians and enjoying some of the fireworks that were set off to welcome the "new year."

    "Well... it's hard to see fireworks in the day," Sophie managed a shrug, "and in this part of the country... we really can't expect spring weather until closer to mid-March. We're far enough north that we will not only get winter weather, but it will stick with us for a while."

    Roland slowly nodded and the four of them all shivered a little bit while bundled up in heavy winter jackets. JC and Pauline both periodically scratched as the heavy jackets put a lot of pressure on their feathers and that likely made them itch a little. However, none of them could really avoid the issue as in the Jurassic Period, when Pterodactylus lived in Germany or the early Cretaceous when Microraptor lived in China global temperatures were much warmer, and the specific climates the two species lived in were much warmer. Some Dinosaurs may have lived in colder temperatures and saw snow on a regular basis during certain portions of the year, but Microraptor wasn't one of them, and among Pterosaurs, neither was Pterodactylus.

    "And they do may for a spectacular show and display," came a new voice from behind them as another set of fireworks went off overhead in brilliant colors and flashing lights. They then turned to see a lone Red Crowned Crane Animalian, Maho Yurikazi, standing there politely. Like them she was in a warm winter coat, but she seemed to better tolerate some of the late winter cold a bit better.

    "They do... but before I was uplifted to become a Microraptor Dinotopian, I was mostly inside in a well heated room," JC spoke with Pauline nodding in agreement.

    "Microraptor..." Maho slowly mused.

    "A small Theropod from China," Sophie explained, "Though from a different part from China than Velociraptor."

    "They would be related though... with "raptor" in the name?" Maho wondered.

    "Distantly," Sophie answered, "both would be considered Dromaeosaurs but Microraptor is smaller and more of a tree dweller while Velociraptor was probably slightly larger and more ground dwelling... and lacking Microraptor's four wings."

    "But they don't do much for warmth," Pauline commented.

    "You'll have to be strong," Maho actually laughed, "given the stories of Dinosaur and Pterosaur bones being found and labelled as "dragon bones" in traditional Chinese culture... you four might find yourselves in some of the contests connected with Animalia's celebrations for the Chinese New Year."

    The four gave a small shrug with Roland glancing to Sophie and then to JC. Some of the "dragon bones" reference he understood, but he really wasn't good enough with the Chinese Zodiac to know what "year" it was without some outside guide.

    Abby and Samson went along to follow the Mister and Miss Zodiac 2019 Competition, partially to be there for some of the pigs that had been part of the Brown Farm in the initial outbreak. It wasn't something as big as other things that had been done on the Brown farm, but just as with other things, the Animalia Virus had led to many changes, and both Abby and Samson had heard from some of the uplifted pigs on how they were a bit disheartened at learning on how so many humans seemed to think they were filthy and lazy animals.
