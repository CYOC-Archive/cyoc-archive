The rest home Kasai had been staying at was a fair distance away, so Curt had asked Meredith to leave before the meeting had ended, hoping she could avoid rush hour traffic and be back to pick him up by the time it was over.

    Unfortunately, he found himself standing in the parking lot with no sign of the mouse-maid in sight. After pacing around for a few minutes, his cell phone rang.

    “Hello?”

    “Hello Master, Meredith speaking.”

    “Ah, I was wondering where you were.”

    “Well, I managed to claim Kasai’s things with no issue, Master. Unfortunately, there was an accident on the highway that’s held up traffic to nearly a standstill.”

    Curt sighed. “I figured something like that must have happened.” He couldn't prepare for everything.

    “I apologize that I will not be able to pick you up for some time.”

    “Don’t worry about it, it’s not your fault,” Curt said quickly. After seeing how far Meredith had been willing to follow orders this morning, he didn’t want her to start feeling guilty about stuff beyond her control.

    “If you do not wish to wait for me, Master, Susan’s bus, going to Cornell County, is scheduled to make a stop near Green Ridge Park just a few blocks away, in about fifteen minutes. I’m certain she’d be happy to see you, and she stops very near your home shortly before her route ends.”

    Susan? Curt didn’t remember any Susans. Must have been someone Anna or Skyla changed. Putting the thought aside, Curt spoke again. “Good idea. Thank you. I’ll take the bus, and you just stay safe and come home.”

    “Of course, Master. Again, apologies for not being able to help you myself.”

    “Again, don’t worry about it. I’ll talk to you later. Bye.”

    “Goodbye, Master.”

    Curt headed down to the bus stop, idly looking over the toy-like ray gun again. He 'holstered' it in his pants pocket again as he approached the bus stop, the clear booth’s bench occupied only by a teenage boy. He quietly took a seat at the other end of the bench, and waited.

    The boy glanced at him for a moment, then spoke. “Hey, aren’t you that Evanson guy?”

    Curt was a bit surprised to hear a complete stranger say his name, but compared to the weirdness of yesterday, it wasn’t that unusual. “Oh, yeah, that’s me. How’d you know my name?”

    “You’re the guy that won the huge lottery payout a few years back and hired a bunch of maids. A lot of people know your name.”

    “Lottery? I don’t even remember entering the lottery. But, I do have a lot of maids, yes.”

    “Must have heard the rumors wrong, then. Still… I heard you liked dragons a lot, right? You’ve even got a statue of one. And a couple of your maids were dragons too.”

    Statue? Did the teen mean that 3D printed figure of a dragoness he had commissioned? How would he know about that? Had he left it by the window by mistake?

    “Well, yes… I like reptiles of all stripes, but I’ve got a soft spot for dragons.”

    “Us?”

    “Yes, us! I might look like a human on the outside, but I am an Otherkin! Within my heart is the soul of an ancient dragon god!”

Anna may have already made a pair of dragons, but Curt figured the world could use a few more. Plus he hadn’t been there to see those two change. Than again, he didn’t want to force a kid to work as a maid for him. But on the other this teen would outright want the change! And while he might also be enabling the teen’s delusions of grandeur, he figured that once the mental changes kicked in, he would be able to help him (well, it would be her, by then) tone down a bit.

But he’d already said ‘yes’ now. He’d have to at least give it a shot.

That being said, Janet’s cooking skills were truly something else; her use of spice and seasoning made even the simple meal taste like something out of a high-class restaurant.

He didn’t think of himself as antisocial, but something about having that many people in the house at once made him feel a bit weary. He never had been one for big parties. Nevertheless, he did have a good time, his rushing back into a relationship with Danielle notwithstanding, but he was ready for a rest.
