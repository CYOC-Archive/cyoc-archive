    When Ty got home from school, he was surprised to find June already home. "Hello, Ty," she said.

    "Hi, mom," Ty replied "I didn't expect you to be home already."

    "I'm ahead of schedule on my accounting work," June said, "thanks to you, and I didn't have any client meetings scheduled, so I decided to use the time to catch up on some housework."

    "Oh, that's nice," said Ty. "I hope you can also spend some time just relaxing, or doing something fun."

    "Well, I had an idea about that," June said. "Would you be willing to transform me again?"

    "You want to be an app again now?" Ty asked.

    "No, a different transformation," she said as she led him into the laundry room. The clothes washer was running, and he could see that the clothes dryer had clothes in it, but the door was open. "I want to try something that would let me, umm... enjoy... some of my housework more."

    Ty was puzzled. "What do you mean?"

    "I want you to transform me into the clothes dryer," June said, "and be sure to make it so that I enjoy drying the clothes."

    "Um, Mom," Ty said. "I'd be glad to do that for you. But won't Dad be upset if I transform you again without telling him?"

    "I thought of that," June said, "and I certainly don't want Mark to be upset with either of us, so I called him, and we talked it over. He's not super enthusiastic about it, but he said that if you're sure you can transform me, and then transform me back again safely, that it's OK."

    "Oh," said Ty. "In that case, sure. I'm know I can do that without hurting you. But you'll have even less free will than as an app. You'll be aware of what you're doing, and enjoy it, but you won't be able to make any choices at all. If you're absolutely certain you want this, I'll do it. How do you want it to work?"

    "Great!" said June. She put a fabric softener sheet into the dryer, closed the door, and set the knob to automatic, 'more dry'. "Everyhing is ready. It will probably take a little over an hour. Just turn me into the dryer, and press my 'start' button."
