    "So... high school football player eh?" Julie asked making awkward small talk with the
    tabby.

    "Yes..." The tabby said with a large tone of bitterness, "Some reserve player I didn't even
    know the name of apparently wanted my spot, I thought the Game was a bunch of kitty
    litter so I ended up with both. Apparently the guy, -who I am very sure I barely spoken to
    before!- Though Daring me to just quite the team or Transforming me into a skinny
    clumsy guy was somehow -too nice-! I swear the guy had to be on too much or not
    enough medication! So he dared me to write a suicide note and never ever speak to or in
    any form whatever so ever communicate with any of my family or friends again, then
    Transformed into a house cat leaving me with the ability to speak just for spite... he also
    told me precisely how I'd die of old age within two decades now being a house cat!!!"

    "Sorry," Madam Serenity said, "Trevor doesn't get to share his story that often and... it can
    be a lot for him to carry."

    "i don't want your pity!"

    "He's also scared that if he manages to make a friend he'll stop being able to communicate
    to that person due to his curse."

    "I said stop it!"

    Julie looked in horror as she realized the full scope of Trevor's curse.

    "What if you got hold of the game again for someone to Transform you into a human
    being?"

    "Wouldn't work. He included IN the transform itself that I'd die of old age within twenty
    years no matter what species I was at the time or whatever dare or transform was given
    me! So even if I was made human again I'd succumb to premature aging."

    "What did he do with you?"

    "He dumped me in a pet store when the owner wasn't looking after putting a muzzle on
    my mouth so I couldn't say the game back to him. ... I was in there for a year at least, the
    place didn't have a calendar or holiday decorations so I don't know."

    "He's as smart as ever but dates confuse him sometimes as part of his curse it seems so he
    can't know how long he has left."

    "I said shut up!" He said actually with a couple tears in his eyes, loathing them as they
    came. "At any rate, some girl came in, chatting with me at random tra la la, like she knew I
    could understand her. When the owner wasn't look she took off the muzzle and lifted me
    out of the pen. First time in a year at least I could speak again! I didn't hesitate and asked
    her the game. She ended up choosing transform. She didn't think the curse was real and
    that I was just a regular talking cat. Yes, that's what she said. I gave her cat ears, tail and a
    few minor things as a proof of how real the game was. But instead of asking me back the
    game, she screamed and ran away... I took the chance to high tail it... I tried going back to
    my family but... I just couldn't... so I wandered about for a bit and found myself with
    Madam a few weeks ago."

    Serenity said, "I can sense -when- the game is invoked but I can't sense where." She
    answered Julie's question before she could ask it. "He help me finally find this one little
    boy got turned into a mouse with a human life-span but had gone crazy from it
    happening, lucky he had smarts enough to type out a message to his family on a
    processor when I showed him to them... whoever cursed the boy however make sure to
    the lad harmlessly dared the curse back to the asker. It was likely the person who gave
    the game to who transformed Trevor."

    Julie wondered what kind of crazy town her family had moved to.

    Julie also wondered how much of Trevor's story was true and how much was point of view
    of a jock who didn't realize how much he hurt others, but at the same time she couldn't
    deny the talking cat in front of her.

    When they got to the house via Julie's car, the house looking perfectly mundane, they
    encountered their first road block. Front door was locked. there was also a 'For Sale' by the
    real estate the house by default returned to in absence of the owners.

    However, Trevor happened to find that the door next to the kitchen was sealed with far
    more mundane measures with the door having been removed. Trevor crawled in, undid the
    lock on the front door on the inside and two women stepped inside.

    "Ugh!" Trevor said covering his nose with one paw, "Whoever was here sprayed this place
    good! They decidedly didn't want whoever came looking to be able to SMELL what
    happened here in any shape or form. My nose can't pick anything but a bunch of
    deodorant and anti-scent sprays! It's that stuff that negates a smell rather than just
    covers it up...my nose is useless!"

    The two women looked at each other and sighed, seems they had some detective work
    ahead of them.

    Of course, it was the next day after Frank had called Julie and he had not come home, that
    was when she had called the police, and then later called the FBI, and it hadn't been until
    the NEXT day -after- that she had finally exhausted all her option and had gone to the
    bizarre woman she had assumed was some kind of fortune teller. Only then and only then
    had she gone to Madam Serenity. So effectively, Julie had not gone to Madam Serenity for
    three whole days! One day waiting for Frank to come home to talk about it, one day talking
    to the police and getting nowhere, and another day talking to the FBI getting nowhere.
    THEN coming to see Madam Serenity (it would have been a little silly for her to go see a
    'fortune teller' a on the same day her husband vanished when it was still before the time
    he was supposed to come home!)

    Now that we've seen this preview of events that will occur relating to Julie, let's turn back
    to the clock to the wolf's estate, there was a lot to happen in the next few days before Julie
    began her fated search.

    The kids had moved the majority of Jennifer's stuff to the master bedroom, the majority of
    stuff in the master bedroom originally to a store room where it was divided by Jenny into
    stuff to be auctioned off, and the useless left overs (girl magainzes, eyeliner, etc) had
    gone onto the garbage. Kaylee had been all for burning all the clothes in the house in a
    bond fire, which excited the kids but Jensen figured they'd be more viable to the pack sold
    off. The items of clothing however that weren't even worth the cost to ship did indeed go
    into the fire which the wolf cub's cheered as it happened.

    Jenny had placed several items on E-Bay and by that night had sold a good deal of them
    already and had the twins with ninja like speed and stealth put them out for UPS at the
    outside of the Estate's mailbox. Not that it would have matter, IF the driver had seen them,
    he would have not found anything wrong with two naked wolf women and wouldn't have
    even felt it worth mentioning when asked for an in-depth description.

    Since the staff was not available at the time at the estate, the twin at dinner time had
    taken a large hunk of meat from the meat locker and returned with it barely cooked. Not
    that the wolves cared and sickness wasn't really an issue. Jenny made notes on how much
    meat they had, and how large the pack was likely to get before they could bring in herd
    animals and thus how much meat she'd have to order to be delivered to the house.

    The wolves tore into their meal without forks or knives, their fangs and claws working well
    enough, leaving them all bloody in the face and blood in the fangs before washing
    themselves (didn't want to completely scare the staff tomorrow). Not Bart, not Jenny, not
    Kevin, not Marcy, nor any of the adult wolves blink an eyelash or gave a second thought to
    their style of eating.

    Fran and Darla leaned against each other with satisfaction of a job well done. The twins
    thought about, 'This is a good thing. We're like a family with Master. True there's always
    feeding Master's carnal hungers and creature comforts." Both twins laughed, 'Not that we
    have even the slightest problem with THAT!' Both were excited at the idea of Master inside
    them. 'But all in all, we're all getting along pretty well... the more people that become part
    of the pack the better...' While the twins knew it was likely an impossible dream, but the
    idea of the entire town being a community of wolves who GOT ALONG was appealing to
    them somehow.

    "Bart."

    "Yes dad?"

    "Send a text message to Martha and MAKE SURE she gets it, I don't want any surprises
    tomorrow. I want her to show up before ANY of the staff tomorrow, have her get here
    before dawn if that's what it takes."

    "Of course master dad! She'll have to be dead not to get our message."

    "Good! I want her to be Transform or Dared before any of the other staff arrive, she'll be
    your Asker to transform or dare the first batch of staff. I'll oversee Martha's transform or
    Dare with Kevin's turn."

    "And I already have some unique ideas on how ta specialize some of the staff father! You'll
    love'em!"

    "Good. I'll look over them after we Transform or Dare Martha."

    After that, Kaylee finally got her Wii set up and challenged the cubs to a fighting game
    tournament. Kaylee was taken out in the first round and Kevin emerged as the victor.

    By Jensen's command, the cubs had gone to bed when told, though Jenny did spend a bit
    more looking over numbers for the estate. Meanwhile, Jensen looked at Kaylee and the
    twins.

    "This time there are no nosy neighbors to hear us."

    Kaylee and the twins all grinned as they followed their master to the master bedroom.
