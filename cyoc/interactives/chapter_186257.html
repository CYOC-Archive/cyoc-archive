    I remember that night as if it was just yesterday. It was 10:30 PM, and Gina was driving Josh's parents Toyota Highlander to my house. I was in shotgun telling Gina where to go if she was ever to forget. I didn't worry too much though, she was the only one of us who was a semi-safe driver. Personally, I had been pulled over 2 or 3 times within the first 6 months of me getting my license, whereas Gina had never been pulled over in her full year of driving so far. The only thing I didn't like about her driving is that she always kept the windows rolled down and the AC turned off. It was practically torture on hotter days, but thankfully it was nighttime and instead of the almost unbearable heat with the occasional breeze, it was very cool the whole ride there, and I was thankful for that at least.

    Hank and Amber were seated directly behind us in the second row. Amber was on her phone, her face covered in the bright, artificial light radiating from her screen. She swiped up with her finger, giggled, swiped once more, watched for a few seconds, and chortled once more before showing Hank, who laughed as well. This cycle would continue throughout the rest of the car ride.

    Josh was passed out in the back row, snoring way too loud for a normal human being. Thankfully, Take 5 was playing loudly on the speaker, so his obnoxiously loud noises were being drowned out by the steady 5/4 beat and entrancing saxophone solo. Thomas sat directly opposite of him, and stared blankly out the window, his face occasionally being brightened by the passing street lights outside. He was obviously tired, and every once in a while his eyes would start to close, but he would jolt himself back up a few seconds later.

    I had a habit of checking my phone for the time every five minutes. It drastically drained my battery life, but I didn't care. Time was my oyster, I told myself, and as long as I could keep it in check, I would always get where I wanted to go on time. I remember that I wanted to get home by 10:45. There was no logical reason, I just had a feeling that was the right time. I looked out the window and noticed that we just passed our school, which was about 7 minutes away from my house. Good. Even if we only have a minute to spare, as long as we got there before 10:45 we would be good.

    My mind was blank at that point, and I noticed that there was something resting on my lap. I looked down and instantly remembered what I had done just 15 minutes ago.

    Rules:
    1. Game is played clockwise.
    2. Each character has a resistance bar. If it reaches zero, that character is stuck in whatever form they are currently in until the game is over.
    3. If a character's HP reaches zero, they will re spawn after three turns, after which their resistance will drastically be reduced.
    4. If a character cannot play, they will be skipped and their form will be reset after three turns, with each turn decreasing their resistance as time goes on.
    5. If a character becomes pregnant in some way, they will go into labor after three turns, and will be unable to transform until then. After the birth, their resistance will be drastically lowered.
    6. If a character attempts to leave after the game starts, they will be transformed into something drastic, and their resistance will go down.
    7. Players may choose to skip any one of their turns if they wish to do so, at the cost of a small amount of resistance.
    8. New players may choose to join the game at any time, but they will be given a negative effect depending on when in the game they join.
    9. The last character standing wins. The winner will be given complete and total transformative control over themselves and the other characters for a month.
