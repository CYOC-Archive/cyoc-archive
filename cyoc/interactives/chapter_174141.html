    As I went over the syllabus, rehashing what I had known from first period, I saw a familiar face among the students. It was Billy from middle school, someone who constantly picked on me through those years. Finding myself staring at his gruff physique and matching demeanor, I realized that I felt butterflies in my stomach by instinct. However, I quickly reminded myself that I was now Ms. Jessica, which explained why he wasn't sneering or mocking me for over 10 minutes now.

A small smile crept over my face, as I held back a more menacing one that would've looked uncharacteristic on Ms. Jessica's face. "So Billy, what is the late assignment policy?" I asked with glee.

    "Huh?" Billy asked perplexingly. He was obviously not paying attention to my talk earlier, and he was going to pay for it now.

    "Young man, if you're not going to pay attention in the first day of school, then how should I expect you to listen for the whole school year. Please read the policy on the syllabus out aloud to the whole class," I reprimanded.

    Several students snickered as Billy's face turned a nice shade of pink. He straightened himself, stammered, "I'm--I'm sorry," and read the requested section. For the first time in my life, I felt prideful around Billy. He might not know it, but one of his biggest victims in middle school just gave him a good payback.

Of course, I didn't want to do too much, since I didn't want to sour Ms. Jessica's reputation. She seemed like a nice teacher, so I didn't want to bring her down for my revenge. After assigning the reading assignment for the remainder of the period, I sat back down in my chair. Not one of those hard student chairs, but Ms. Jessica's soft cushioned chair. I laid back as I relaxed my sore feet (heels are hard to stand in for a long time!), and grabbed a sip of coffee for my parched mouth. Staring at the students, it was surreal thinking that I was one of them earlier today. Sure, my student life wasn't downright pitiful (save for Billy), but being a teacher also has its perks. I was no longer directly supervised, and I get to boss around, especially Billy, who was reading attentively now. "Guess I really knocked some sense into him today. That was fun," I whispered under my breath, making sure no one heard me.
