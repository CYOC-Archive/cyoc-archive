I’m sure you realize that even though they’re immensely powerful we shouldn’t let two 6 year old kids run around unsupervised.”

These anthros were much different from her last visit though. The anthro creatures walking around the park looked demonic, fiendish with features that were truly monstrous. Before the anthro people appeared to be humanoid animal people. Their features were animalistic yes but you could still see they had been human. The ones now mixing with them though contained no humanity whatsoever. These anthros looked like creatures of legend like the Minotaur, animal features crafted onto monsters that were quite frightening.

    “Umm anyone know what happened,” Samantha asked.

If that were the case the armies they controlled would be unstoppable, it also meant that Kellie would need to train the rest of her friends in magic. As she now turned towards Baba Yaga to talk, she paused. Baba’s dress looked slightly loose around her hips, while her face appeared fresher than when they had arrived. If Kellie were to guess the witch looked closer to 26 than 30.

    “Baba look,” she yelled.

    Baba gave Kellie a confused look before glancing in a nearby mirror. “Damn,” she sighed. “It appears the hourglass is still affecting me.”

    “Wait the hourglass is affecting you too,” Jack exclaimed. Baba could only nod sheepishly. “It was an unintended side effect,” she stated. “I had hoped to avoid this..complication but unfortunately it appears the hourglass was more chaotic than I anticipated.”

    “Has your magic been affected,” Merlin asked, slightly concerned. “No,” she stated. “I still have complete control over my magic. I do worry though about what may have happened with Circe and Lilith. I find it highly improbable that they would join forces considering the mutual dislike they had for one another not long ago.”

    Kellie sighed as she glanced first at her friends then at Jack. “We better go find your mom,” she said. “Before things get even more out of hand.”

    “Agreed,” he stated. “So how do we find them?”

    Kellie now looked over the fairgrounds, smiling as she recognized one of the anthros. “I know just who to ask.”

Despite the demons monstrous appearance they seemed to be getting along rather well with Circes followers. Franklin though was practical and needed to anticipate the worst case scenarios. If the demons were to ever turn on any of them, their would be no way to stop them. Hearing someone calling his name he turned, sighing in relief. “Kellie what an unexpected but welcome surprise.”

    “Yeah good to see you too Franklin,” she smirked. “Can you tell me what in the world is going on here?”
