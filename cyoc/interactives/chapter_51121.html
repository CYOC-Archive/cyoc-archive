    The morning of "D-Day" came to the pack as all the others had been. Jensen awoke with Kaylee and the triplets around him. Each of them looking happy after a night of love making. But as he became fully awake, a strange sense of foreboding came over him. Like something was coming that wasn't going to be easy. That it would potentially be a threat to himself and his mates. As Kaylee and Julie wrapped their arms around his waist and Fran and Darla did the same for Kaylee and Julie, Jensen knew they couldn't come to harm. He would die to defend them. He loved them too much.

    It was somewhat of a strange feeling as he realized it. His past form, Jennifer Smith, never loved anybody, and largely treated most of her boyfriends as toys rather then as people. And those who made it into bed, weren't much more then conquests for Jennifer's own personal ego and ability to manipulate others.

    But it seemed the more and more he stayed Jensen, the Big Bad Wolf, the more he gained the things that his past self either faked or blatantly didn't consider. He sincerely loved his mates. They were the world to him.

    And at the same time, he was embracing the responsibilities that went along with leadership. His pack was devoted to him, and he was certain that all of them would willingly give their lives for him. And while that gave him some feast of power and dominance... it no longer tasted as sweet anymore. He couldn't send his pack members off to risk their lives without good reason. He would do only what he could to protect his pack, to prevent them from taking risks that would needlessly risk their lives. He would insure that his race lived on, even if he did not.

    This didn't mean that Jensen didn't feel confident in his pack's future. Serenity was their only known threat and if she could be silenced, or persuaded to call everything off, his family, his pack could continue with the daily business of living. They could maintain their present numbers, and only growing through the likely coming pregnancies of the females, and a normal life could begin. One where they faced now threats. Maybe in year, if Serenity accepted Julie's argument and left the manor unharmed, he could think of some way to insure his pack's long term survival... but that would take time.

    "Good morning, dear," Kaylee said to him as she awoke.

    "Good morning, Kaylee, my love," Jensen gave a weak smile to his Alpha Female.

    "The island is large, Master," Opal explained, "Large enough to support plant life and some animal life that is capable of either swimming or flying great distances. I do not know if the plant life would be conducive to the deer herd, but I suppose we could also terraform it."

    "The way you describe it is that this island is a paradise in many ways," Hope commented from a nearby area, "It's a wonder that no one has built a hotel or something on it."

I'm sure there are people that know the island is there, but since it's no where near any shipping lanes and most air traffic doesn't currently come close enough for the island to appear as anything more then a speck, no one's bothered to develop it."

    She then licked the check of the other fore-wolf. He many not have been as well built as Nick, Roger, Lawrence, and Zack were, but he was diligent and very well skilled.

    "There's a piece of higher ground on the east end of the island, away from the surf and such," Opal spoke, "I think we'll start clearing that area today to build a shelter, if you can order the wood for the project."

    Mary awoke in her hammock to the smell of bacon sizzling downstairs. It was six thirty on a Monday morning. A little later then she normally remembered waking up on a school day, but then, as a young lion girl, she had less to get ready. She quickly gathered an outfit for the day, a pair of jeans and a tee shirt which a picture of a lioness batting the nose of a male lion on it. It was something that reminded her of how things had changed for her, and she sighed at that... but then, her only other choice would be to go naked, and she wasn't confident enough to do that.

    "Good morning, sunshine," came Brian's voice.

    "Good morning, daddy," Mary said, inwardly cringing as she had used the word "dad" to address her father for a long time now, to be addressing him the way she did when she was little was both difficult and yet loving at the same time.

    "Good to see you're up," Brian told her, "Ms. Serenity will be here shortly to talk some things over with your mother and I. And remember you have school today. Do you need us to walk you to the elementary school?"

    "No, daddy," Mary sighed.

    "Have a great day with Mrs. Smythe's class," Brian told her a he departed from the doorway.

    Mary only sighed. At least I have the same teacher as when I truly was an eight year old. She followed her father downstairs to see her mother opening the door to let Madame Serenity in. Again, she had Paul and Trevor with her. She eagerly came down the stairs, hoping Serenity could convince her parents to let her be human again.

    "She's stopped," Jensen spoke as he opened his eyes, "a small home in one of the middle class neighborhoods. 874 Lee Ave."

    The gathered wolves around Jensen all blinked in surprise. All he had done was close his eyes and seem to think for a moment. Yet he knew Serenity's exact location.

    Breakfast had gone well, but had also gone quickly. Mostly because of the knowledge of the mission coming. Zack's knee had fully recovered, other then some bruising, which wasn't all that visible through his charcoal black fur. Pearl and June wished him luck and told him to come back to them safe and sound. Margot licked Lawrence's check. Megan gave Roger a very uneasy sounding "good luck", which made Roger feel concerned. Like he was losing his best friend.

    The gem colored wolves were currently gathering the tools needed for the next stage of building up their island sanctuary. And now the combat team was getting ready to go.

    "How can you do that, Master?" Roger asked in surprise.

    "I don't really know," Jensen answered, "I just know where my victims are."

    "Ah," came the near unanimous response.

    "Remember, that Serenity is to be brought here alive," Jensen spoke, "Julie wants to talk with her, and even I wouldn't mind saying a few things to her myself. Trevor, if he was the one who escaped Mr. P is, also, not to receive any injuries... and actually, I wouldn't mind talking with him either."

    "What of anyone else who is there?" Nicole asked as she stood by Nick.

    "Knock them out," Jensen instructed, "the objective is Serenity."

    "What about Zelipe or Amy?" Zack wondered, "Serenity may be immune to the game our the pack's transform powers, but I doubt the people she's visiting are. If they see us, they're bound to report us. We'll need them to join the Pack. Once they do... Glendale will largely forget about their existence."

    "Too much of a risk," Jensen shook his head, "I will not risk the Pack's pups... and I'm surprised you'd risk your own."

    "Not a risk, Master," Zack argued, "Zelipe or Amy could remain in the mirror with Alice until we've collected Serenity and restrained anyone else. Once that is done, they could transform nonthreatening and restrained prisoners."

    "It would make covering our tracks in this mission easier," Ashley commented, thinking over the idea.

    They were invading someone else's home. And since there wasn't time for Alice to write out a transform or dare for them, testing Zelipe's or Amy's powers would be the only easy way they could cover their trail.

    "Very well," Jensen sighed, "Alice, run along and get Amy. Inform her that she will be going along. And that she will be in the mirrors with you until Nicole is certain all the threats have been neutralized."

    "Yes, Father," Alice curtsied and moved quickly from the office and quickly ran until she reached the study, where the Pack's pups were once again either reading, drawing, or doing something else.

    Martha was working with Samantha on her writing, which was advancing rapidly. Alice wondered bringing them into the mirrors taught Samantha and Alice these things, or if it was slowly making them like the triplets... that one day, there would be nothing that could make Alice, Abbey, and Samantha three separate individuals. Abbey was still in the office, waiting to take the gem colored wolves to the island.

    Zelipe was laying against Mr. P's side asking about Mr. P's life as Patrick. The black and gold wolf looked somewhat uneasy as he didn't look comfortable sharing the experiences of fighting in two world wars and surviving with an eight year old, but he seemed curious nonetheless, and Mr. P did an excellent job of censoring out the the inappropriate parts.

    Kevin and Marcy were once again reading Alice in Wonderland, and it looked like they were making good progress. Bart was drawing pictures of wolf people walking around a city street, which Alice guessed was either the Pack as it was, or Bart's idea of "Wolvesdale". Amy was silently watching Jenny map out new plans for her garden. Alice noticed that her eldest half sister looked rather uncomfortable with this.

    "Excuse me, I do not mean to intrude, but my Father has requested Amy come along with us on the mission," Alice spoke as calmly as she could.

    "Jensen-sama wants me to transform someone, Alice-chan?" Amy asked.

    "Just some people that Serenity is visiting," Alice answered, "You are to wait with me within the mirrors until all the fighting is over."

    "What if they lose, Alice-chan?" Amy asked nervously.

    "I doubt that," Alice commented, "Nicole is leading the group, and every male, except for Father and the gem colored males are going. Father said there are only three there. And even if they do appear to lose, we will come back for the rest of the Pack and overwhelm them."

    "You do not mind, Martha-san?" Amy asked the pack's nanny.

    "If the Master has ordered it, you'd best go," Martha spoke, "Remain in the mirrors with Alice until Nicole says it is safe."

    "Yes, Martha-san."

    Amy then hopped down and followed Alice out.

    "Finally," Jenny commented.

    "I hope you were meaning that in reference to dealing with Madam Serenity and not against a member of your pack," Mr. P spoke firmly, interrupting his stories to Zelipe.

    "Of course," Jenny lied.

    "Don't lie to me Jennifer," Mr. P said firmly.

    "Sorry, Grandpa," Jenny whimpered, knowing she'd been caught.

    "Amy is a member of the Pack and your playmate," Mr. P scolded, "she may not be your blood sister, like Marcy, Alice, Abbey, and Samantha are, but you are still to treat her with respect and to learn to be her friend... or Martha and I will be forced to inform your mother of your behavior."

    "Yes, Grandpa," Jenny whimpered again, dreading a spanking.

    "I trust Alice has informed you of your mission?" Jensen inquired.

    "Yes, Jensen-sama," Amy bowed, "I am to remain with Alice until it is safe to bring whoever Serenity is visiting into the Pack."

    "Please, everyone hold hands," Alice instructed, "this will make things much easier to insure we all get there.

    And sure enough they were. Nicole, Ashely, and Sarah were all dressed in a middle class woman's attire from the Victorian era. Nick, Roger, and Lawrence were dressed in business suits from the late 1800s. Zack was actually wearing the dress uniform of a British army officer from around the time of Boer War. Amy, meanwhile, was dressed up as a Japanese priestess.

    "This is most amazing, Alice-chan, can we take these clothes outside the mirrors?" Amy asked.

    Alice looked down at her standard blue and white dress and then shook her head.

    "No," Alice answered, "Outside the mirror we will return to our natural nude forms."

    "So what now?" Ulysses asked.

    "Three steps," Alice smiled.
