    So not surprisingly it wasn't long before the 15 Night Mares of the
    Northern Steppes Advance Guard had managed to knock all the medievel
    Dragons senselss,including Damon Blackfire.

    The snorting Hel Mares dragged the senseless Dragons to an abandoned castle and hobbled their wings,ensuring their prisoners
    couldn't just fly away when they reawakened.

    Off the Night Mares stomped and began making treaties and deals and bargains with the surround human kings and queens,including Damon
    Blackfires human dad and his human brothers Aaron and Brendan.

    So it was that 9 Night Mares stayed by the abandoned castle
    to keep an eye on their prisoners.Alas these 9 couldn't count
    very well and hadn't noticed that 6 of the medievel Dragons were missing,including
    Damon Blackfire.

    No problem

    The leader of the Night Mares simply sent messages to their human allies
    and soon Damons dad and two brothers were merrily calling for Damon's arrest,dead or alive,be he/she in
    human or Dragon form.

    Now Damon Blackfire and his 5 companions were more interested in getting help from the Dragon King
    and had headed off to where the harmonious Dragon City of the Far Far Future
    had been as little as a day earlier.

    There was no sign of the Dragon City or of their supposed allies from the future.

    Now one of the medievel Dragons once had been a sea Captain in his human life and so noticed there was something nautical looking sticking out of the Forest of Doom and No Return.

    He summoned Damon Blackfire and the other four medievel Dragons
    and the six of them discovered a Dragon sized medievel looking sailing ship in those trees.

    Cautiously they approached the strange craft and suddenly a net dropped from the trees above and trapped them.

    Out of the trees came Fel'Hiren,one of the few Dragons from the Future
    who hadn't left with the Dragon King when the Dragon City had gone elsewhere.

    One look at the netted medievel Dragons and Fel'Hiren began to regret staying behind.
