    Not quite an apology

    added by O 4 years ago A BM TG

    "Wait so your telling me a shadow demon is screwing with my mind," Mika exclaimed, glaring at the severed head.

    Kellie nodded as she gazed back at both Lilith and Circe. "Yeah looks like it," she mumbled.

    Mika tried to shake her head, clearing a fog that wanted to settle on her mind. As she looked at her girlfriend she frowned. "Look," she sighed. "Maybe I was a bit thrown off okay. But who the fuck you think you are calling me a racist."

    Claudia furrowed her brow, her whiskers twitching on her face. "Well you goes off like that," she yelled. "I was doing this for you idiot."

    "I never asked you too change," Mika yelled. "Jesus Christ what is so hard to understand that I love you just the way you are. Calling me a racist for wanting the same old Claudia ain't gonna make me apologize."

    "See your being a racist," Claudia huffed.

    "No asshole," Mika yelled. "But I'd rather not have my girlfriend have a set of nuts bigger than my head."

    Claudia glanced down at the massive set of testicles dangling between her legs. "But you like guys and girls," Claudia snorted. "Just like me, what's wrong with having both."

    "I dont want both," Mika exclaimed. "I want you. God, if I wanted to be with a guy I'd be with a guy. I want to be with you and you screaming at me like a fucking lunatic isn't helping the situation."

    The teenage couple now sat in awkward silence as they tried to come up with something to say. "So now what," Claudia asked.

    "I don't know," Mika groaned. "I'm not exactly thrilled about this, and guess what I dont have to be. You want to be an anthro whatever that's fine. You want to be the girl I fell in love with that's fine too."

    "What do you want," Claudia asked.

    "I want my girlfriend back," Mika sighed. "The girl I shared a kiss with in study hall. The quiet girl that brightens the room everytime she walks in. Then I want to go home. I've had enough magic for one day."

    Kellie had watched the exchange with great interest. It distressed her that Mika and Claudia were fighting. She could understand both sides. Claudia wanted to try something new, Mika didn't. It in turn led to the girls first fight perhaps ever in this timeline.

    "Hey girls," Kellie said tentatively. Mika and Claudia turned, curiously looking at Kellie. "I think you both kind of made your points. Dragging this out wont help. Your both angry, your both in shock. Let's just go home."

    Mika and Claudia simply nodded, turning towards one another before walking away in silence. As they did Kellie turned once more to Circe, the goddess having a stern look on her face. "Look," she sighed, rubbing her eyes. "That freaking head is screwing with us right now. Not exactly thrilled Claudia got transformed but willing to step back for a moment."

    "So where does that leave us," Circe asked, a stern look on her face.

    "Leaves us nowhere," Kellie answered. "Your ambassador is dead so theirs no one to teach. When shes up and about will be back."

    Circe nodded as Kellie turned to leave. "One last thing," Kellie stated. "Leave my friends alone. Think we can both agree that whether they choose it or not, it leads to problems."

    "For now I will," Circe growled. "But mark my words child. You can't control them. If they wish to transform they will make the choice themselves."

    "For your sake Circe," Kellie sighed. "Let's hope that doesnt happen." Without another word Kellie turned and exited Circes castle. The peace summit for now was a failure.
