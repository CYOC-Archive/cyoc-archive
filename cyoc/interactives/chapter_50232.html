    Things were quieter in Glendale's police station then Alice had thought. She had thought that they were always busy solving big and complicated crimes, but from what she heard through the mirrors most everything seemed rather small in scale, and from what she could see most of the people were working on some sort of paperwork.

    "Best to start with the Chief of Police, as he likely knows the most," Alice thought and carefully looked out through the mirrors to make sure her coast was clear.

    For the moment the hallway was clear and she wasn't that far from the chief's office. After a few moments she saw a man come out of the chief's office and then announce something to the officers present.

    "I'm going to check the impound, see if Daryl came back for his motorcycle," Gill spoke, "Hopefully he has and he's seen Frank."

    As Alice heard a few faint motions of acknowledgment, she wondered, "mother rides a motorcycle?"

    Alice then watched as Gill left, and left his door open. She checked to see if she was clear, and when she was sure she was, she dove out of the mirror, landed as silently as glass could and moved into the office. Alice found it small and almost cramped. She partially wondered if Jensen's instructions to not disturb anything really mattered, as the place looked like a royal mess to the glass wolf. Sighing, Alice carefully approached the desk, noting the reflective surface on the computer, and that it was in no danger of loosing that reflectiveness for a good while.

    She carefully looked over the desk for anything that might be of help, but was extremely careful not to move anything. As she came closer, however, she saw a small post-it note sitting on top of a small stack of papers. The note read "To Do: Find Frank and Daryl". Underneath it was a rather official looking sheet of paper that read: "Case Number 876778943, Type: Missing Persons, Frank Butler and Daryl Lazarus, Status: Open*".

    "I wonder what the asterisk means," Alice wondered as she looked at the paper, she carefully lifted it up to see another asterisk at the bottom followed by "see file 87678943", "ah, it is instructions. I must go to the file room next"

    And with that Alice dove into the reflective light of Gill's computer screen. A moment later an almost depressed looking Gill returned to his office.

    Alice found the file room to be even smaller then the office she had just left. It wasn't much larger then a closet and was packed full of filing cabinets. She wondered why there was a mirror in the small space, but decided not to dwell on it.

    "A royal mess," Alice commented, "Most improper."

    She quickly found that the files were arranged by their case numbers and quickly began looking at the cards on the drawers to the filing cabinets until she came to one marked "80000000 to 90000000". She then opened that draw and began flipping through the files inside it till she came to the "missing persons" file for her mother and aunt.

    The first page was an official missing persons report with a basic description of the people missing and when they were reported missing. The remaining pages were written reports about what the police had uncovered so far. Glancing through it quickly, Alice noted that the police had made little if any progress in getting any lead to where "Frank and Daryl" were. Apparently people had seen their police car, but missed seeing Frank and Daryl become Fran and Darla. Alice did her best to make sure she could memorize everything from the files that would be required, so that she could make her report.
