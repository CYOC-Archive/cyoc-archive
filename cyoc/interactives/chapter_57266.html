    "Hello, you are the leading hunter?" one of the fairies asked as she fluttered up to Marie's face on her wings. Contrary to stories that Marie remembered, the fairies all looked very friendly.

    "I suppose you could say that," Marie Cruz answered.

    "And you will be trying the witches?" the fairy asked.

    "I will be defending them," Marie answered, "Linus Sure, here is the prosecutor."

    The fairy looked over to see the man with no hands seated on a couch. A couple of her sisters were fluttering over his shoulder to look the book in front of him.

    "But would that not be fair?" the fairy asked, "you fought against them."

    "In outward appearance," Marie answered, "but as an Inquisitor, a fair trial is the key to justice. I WILL insure that things are done fairly."

    "I see," the fairy answered, "My name is Titania, the Fairy Queen, my people and I have agreed to sit as a jury for the witches."

    Marie wasn't sure what to say. According to what the witches had told her, they had created the fairies.

    "And you would find them guilty if the evidence proves them to be guilty?" Marie asked.

    Titania thought for a moment, "we can. We were indebted to them for restoring our families... but once we paid out debt, we left to seek our own fortune. Would you like to meet the others?"

    That sort of struck a nerve with what the witches had said to Marie. Gomorrah had transformed Titania, Pucca, and Olga as a test of hers and Solome's powers. But one of the fairies that had been created by the witches wasn't wallowing in some sort of remembrance of who "she" was. Stopping the witches was important, but it appeared more and more likely that if they tried to reverse what the witches had done would only make things worse.

    She then looked at the host of unconscious child familiars and Witch Pride members.

    "Not at the moment," Marie answered, "there are still some setting up to do for the trial. If you can help make sure that their child familiars aren't harmed that would be good. We'll let you know when we're ready."

    Titania nodded and fluttered off. Marie was then about turn to Linus when they heard Katherine above everything.

    "GET OUT OF THERE!"

    Everyone looked up to see Ophelia and Desdemona come crawling out of Katherine's dress.

    "What were you two doing?" Titania asked them.

    "We couldn't help it," Desdemona answered weakly, "she's so pretty with all the bright colors."

    "Felmen are NOT nudists!" Katherine answered.

    "We meant no harm," Ophelia said, "You are very pretty. Boy cats must love you..."

    "Felman."

    "Felman boys must love you," Ophelia repeated.

    "I am a nun," Katherine answered, "the only "husband" I will ever have, is God."

    "Oh," Ophelia said slowly, "You are still pretty."

    The little fairy then moved to hug Katherine's ankle. Katherine only sighed at that.

    Linus then motioned to the unconscious child familiars and witch pride members. Mal'akh was once again hugging her sister, hoping that she could wake the cub up.

    "We can't dart them forever, you know," Linus then spoke, "You said we only have a month's supply of the darts. Eventually we'll run out and they're return to full strength."

    "I know," Marie nodded, "maybe part of me thinks we can reform them. Get them to give it up."

    "No magic user ever gives it up," Linus warned, "my guardian used his magic to help the people of Bavaria until he grew too old and weak to even do the small things that he had done before. And given the insanity you've said they've fallen into, even if you can convince them to be "good" a mind that doesn't follow reason or logic, even if it tries to be good, won't be able to truly function."

    Marie only sighed. A good part of her knew that Linus was right. If they did go free, they would start everything all over again, and she would have to fight them all over again. But she had to live up to her father's teachings. She couldn't just murder them out of fear.

If that path had continued, it was entirely likely that the witches would have unintentionally enslaved the world...

    "You'd best eat," Draco said to Gomorrah as he held out a spoon filled with porridge to her.

    "To give you pleasure?" Gomorrah shot back.

    As the day started, the witches were again darted, to insure that they couldn't use their magic, and they had all let Draco have it for doing such a thing to them, but the dragon largely ignored it.

    "To insure that you retain some physical strength for this trial," Draco answered, showing no favoritism either for or against them, "Marie wants this trial to be fair. Therefore you will be cared for while the trial goes on... and suicide is a sin, so do not think that any of us will stand by and let you try that."

    Gomorrah glanced to her sisters and then back to the Dragon that was feeding her. A lot of this contrasted with what they had expected from the hunters if they were to lose... which they still couldn't understand, but still... to be defended by the Dog herself. To have the dragon feeding them in a way that didn't make a mess on their clothing, and the Felman actually walking them to a bathroom during the night to insure they didn't make a mess of themselves.

    Awhile later, Marie and Linus entered what had been the witches' citadel to check on what Draco had done the night before.

    "Looks rather good," Linus commented, "for an improvised courtroom, but it still looks good."

    "I mostly had to bring in what would fit," Draco answered, "it was either that or begin dismantling the house itself, and I am no master builder."

    Marie and Linus nodded.

    "Are you ready?" Draco then asked.

    "Essentially," Marie nodded, "we are waiting for Rosa, Mal'akh, and Monica to arrive. Dr. Azrael is on his way and so is the jury."

    Golgotha turned her head slightly at the names, as did Gaal. Rosa and Monica were two that they had made good a long time ago. Part of them hoped that the two would free them and let them escape the hunters for the time being. Golgotha also recognized Mal'akh's name from Joella's last birthday party. For the moment they didn't say anything.

    "We're here!" came a young sounding voice from outside the room. The sound of footsteps followed and the witches turned to see two young Leoman females enter. To their surprise, one looked like an older version of Aimiliona, and they had sworn they'd seen her on the witches' grounds before. The Leoman seemed extremely nervous however, and was kept under a fairly firm grip by Rosa. Monica stayed close to both.

    What entered the room next, was a greater shock to the witches. It was the fairies.

    "You came back!" Gomorrah spoke as Titania lead Ophelia, Desdemona, Portia, Beatrice, Hermia and a few others flew into the room and landed on a nearby windowsill.

    "We were asked to sit as the jury for this trial," Titania answered, "for the interest of insuring that you are treated fairly, we have agreed."

    "If you believe that, you must free us NOW!" Gomorroh said firmly.

    "That would be the fair thing to do," Gaal added.

    "I am afraid we can not," Titania answered, "We have made a promise, and will keep it."

    "But you're supposed to be our FRIENDS!" Gomorrah shouted at them.

    "Possibly," said Beatrice, "but that doesn't mean you haven't made mistakes, which I would guess is what they want to either prove or disprove."

    "WE'VE MADE NO MISTAKES! WHO ARE YOU? WE DIDN'T MAKE YOU?!" Gaal roared.

    "I am Beatrice," Beatrice answered, "as for how I joined the Fae Folk, that was a gift from the other fairies to my family."

    "HOW DARE YOU PRESUME TO JUDGE US!!!!" Golgotha roared, realizing that Beatrice didn't have her mind rewritten completely the way the other fairies had.

    The one eyed witch then felt a cool breeze enter the room that gave her a slight chill. A man dressed entirely in black entered the room. Part of her wondered if she had seen him before somewhere or somehow.

    "They are the jury," the man said in an authoritative voice that even made the witches quiver a little, "I am the judge. And I assure you, what will be decided here will be fair and has the approval of a power higher then yourselves."

    Dr. Azrael then turned to Marie and Linus, "if the two of you are ready, we had best begin."

    Linus and Marie nodded. While Dr. Azrael went to the larger table and chair that had been set up for him as the judge's "platform", Linus and Marie went to the smaller tables that were provided for them. Draco followed Dr. Azrael to serve as a sort of organizer while Katherine, as per Marie's instructions, followed Linus to handle the papers and pieces of evidence that Linus felt would be useful.

    "This court is now in session," Dr. Azrael spoke, "I am Doctor Ezra Azrael presiding. Before the charges are announced, I will ask the Jury to state their names and list any possible grievances they may have for or against the charged..."
