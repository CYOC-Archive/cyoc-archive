He looked at his parents, and Ethan seemed to pick up on Luke questioning something.

    "Everything good, Luke?"

    "Oh. Just trying to figure out how you two are still conjoined when me and Suzie are wearing the suit?" The two parents looked at each other, then looked back at their kids.

    "Well," Ethan started. "We kind of just, agreed to make it permanent and that was it."

    "Yeah," Alisa added. "It was honestly kind of simple. Somehow we just... knew." Suzie look confused, but Luke seemed to get it.

    "Ok. So you both agreed and boom! Permanent partners." The parents nodded before Alisa reached for her purse.

    "That's right, now we need to head to the store, try not to get into trouble you two."

    "Yes ma'am," Luke and Suzie said in unison before looking at each other, then laughing. Ethan then chuckled a bit too before he and Alisa walked out and went to the store. Suzie then looked at her brother, seeming a bit nervous now.

    "Uh... big bro? What do we do now?" Luke smiled, shacking his head.

    "What we usually do, just together." After getting some cloths on, all Luke's since none of Suzie's would fit, the pair walked downstairs to the living room, and Luke seemed to get an idea.

    "He sis. Wanna try and play a game. Maybe that fighting game we both like." Suzie smiled, but then looked at her hand.

    "How? We only have a hand each."

    "We'll just have to work together. Come on, it'll be fun." Suzie looked at the game system, then eyed her brother.

    "Alright!" For the next hour, Luke and Suzie did their best at figuring out how to play then game now that they were conjoined, and how to adapt to each others ticks and how they moved. After an argument or two though, they seemed to hit a stride. Yes, it wasn't the same difficulty Luke was use to playing, but it still worked. Better yet Suzie seemed to be enjoying herself. After beating another computer, Luke head his hand in front of his sister, yelling "High five!" Suzie was quick to drop the controller and do her best to giver be brother a high five, giggling as she did.
