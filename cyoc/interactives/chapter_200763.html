    "I can't believe how easy that was," Marcus exclaimed to the two vampires waiting by the street.

    Donovan's minions had successfully infiltrated the Davis home and now held Tricia Davis in their arms. The toddler sleeping soundly in their arms. "This child sleeps like the dead," one of them chuckled as he gazed at the brunette child.

    "Interesting choice of words all things considered," the other remarked, a soft laugh escaping his lips. "Now put her in the car. Lord Miro is waiting." As the trio now turned, they were surprised to see a young man in his early twenties and a very stern middle aged woman.

    "Hello," the man said politely. "Can I ask you what you're doing with Tricia?"

    "Who the hell are you?"

    "Oh sorry where are my manners. My names Matt." The trio of vampires paused, giving the young man a puzzled look. Matt didn't appear like much. An average sized looking human with long brown hair. His companion, the middle aged woman, looked more intimidating. For the vampires they felt as if they were gazing at the live action version of the evil queen from Snow White.

    "Enough with the chatter Dracon," the woman huffed. "Finish these vermin off!"

    "Mom," Matt sighed. "Will you relax please. I've got this, and for the love of the Dragon God can you PLEASE call me Matt not Dracon."

    The trio of vampires were confused, whoever these two were they were certainly odd. Matt now composed himself, taking a breath as he now gazed at the trio of vampires. "Sorry about my mom," he replied sheepishly. "She has a bit of a short temper. To answer your question though, my name is Matt and THAT little girl in your arms is under my protection."

    The vampires simply scoffed, the largest of the three storming forward. "Get lost," he bellowed as he proceeded to backgand Matt.

    Instead of being thrown back by the blow, Matt stood his ground. A soft chuckle now slowly emanating from him. "Really," he grinned. "That's it. That's the best you got?"

    The vampire was confused, his face turning crimson in anger. As he looked to strike Matt again, he was stunned as the man transformed. It looked as if Matt's body seemed to shimmer, going blurry as it expanded to massive proportions. The vampires now shocked as a golden dragon now stood before them. "Pretty scary huh," Matt growled, a reptilian like growl emanating from deep within the dragons throat. "Im not the one you should be scared of though."

    As the vampires now slowly backed away, they could hear a series of low growled echoing behind them. It dawned on the vampires too late that in their shock, they hadn't noticed where the woman had gone.

    Turning they now saw the woman standing behind them. A red glow emanating from her dark eyes. The womans body now began to twist, the sound of sinew and bone snapping as she grew to monstrous proportions, a series of five heads tearing through her human form and all five heads of the dragon snarling at the three unfortunate vampires.

    "Id like to introduce you to my mom, Tiamat."

    The vampires didn't even have a chance to respond. If they hadn't been so frightened they would not have released their grasp of Tricia Davis. Instead they dropped the girl, Matt's massive claw catching the girl and cradling her gently. With Tricia now safe, Tiamat had no reason to hold back.

    Three of her heads now lurched forward, the jaws of the dragon snatching the vampires as she tore them to pieces. It happened so fast that they could barely utter a scream as Tiamet devoured them before roaring.

    "Geez mom do you have to be so loud," Matt grumbled. "You'll wake up the neighborhood."
