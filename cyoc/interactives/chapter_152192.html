It may not have been the best food or roof, but it was the best they could do, still with all their work they had never shown up at any of her art shows, never helped her with homework or took a real interest in her. It wasn’t her parents which was the bane of her life, it was her bullies, those who never let her have a moment of peace.

Taking care of hair that length required a good amount of money, in hair products and that money could have been spent on other things. Her face fell as she recalled the words her step father would say. “Sorry honey but we can’t afford that.” She would have taken a job and started to pay for her own things like she did once she started school but in high school she only had her bike to get anywhere besides school. There was a school bus for that and they lived 15 miles out of town, so getting to work would be next to impossible.

    “Nothing…” said Kim

    “Talk about it, it’s the only way.” Advised Jess “I had to deal with from becoming a fat man to a hot babe and the only way I did was to talk about it with my mentors and family.”

    “That is the issue, family.” Said Kim tears forming in her eyes. “We are dirt pour no nothings who where luckly to have enough money for food and rent every month. I always wanted long hair, but we couldn’t afford it my step father would say. Sometimes I wonder if my parents even really care about me.”

    “I am sure they do.” Said Beth as she stood up and sat down beside the younger women, and pulled her into a hug.

    “I know they do, they try their best to provide food and shelter but a kid needs more…I need then that.” Explained Kim as she began to cry as the other two women came closer forming a protective ring around her. “They never came to see my art work, I pretty much raised my younger siblings and half siblings once my oldest sister moved out when I was 12. Never got a thanks or even a day off, they didn’t seem to care about me personally they didn’t even come to my graduation or send me off to college. Once My Aunt drove me down to my college and worse of all they never helped me with….”

    She paused as she tried to fight down the flood of emotions, she had never actually let this all out before. It didn’t make sense, maybe it was the change coming over her and forcing everything out.

    “With what?” inquired Jess placing a hand on her shoulder even as she cried into Beth’s arm. Sally was a little more standoffish but she could see emotion in her eyes as well.

I was the 300 pound computer nerd who would go play D&D instead of going to prom.” Stated Jess before grinning. “It was fun showing them up during our reunion, top computer programmer married to a hot succubus and I a goddess on two legs.
