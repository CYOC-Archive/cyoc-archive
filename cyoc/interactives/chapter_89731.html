    Having turned 18 just a few weeks ago I found the thought of owning a mansion was beyond exhilarating. There appeared to be several stories, each with 7 to 8 rooms, and a basement as well as a shed, pool, and greenhouse outside on the expansive lot.

    My head was feeling foggy when I first woke up but now everything seemed to have come into focus, I recalled Mom telling me about the passing of Great Great Uncle Ted Transer several years ago. She was bitter that there was no inheritance for her and that the estate was going to great lengths (at exceptional expense) to find male decedents.

    In his later years Mom had visited with Uncle Ted several times a week, despite his failing health he always insisted on getting "out of the house" for his socializing. When he was no longer able to leave the house, Mom, being the only near relative, visited but wasn't allowed past the entry hall. She kept commenting on how strange it was but was more concerned that he knew he was cared for. His having such great wealth and seeming to have forgotten her for all the times she remembered him had hurt her.

    Looking around in the white room there was a small piece of paper labeled Heirs Attempted, below it there were hash marks that totaled 67. I tried to wrap my head around how an heir could be attempted and how 67 could fail the simple task of getting outside when a map is given. Perhaps the other heirs declined accepting responsibility? Most, if not all, are overseas. The value of the property may not be worth the effort involved with dropping everything to manage a foreign estate. Even so, not knowing for sure left an unsettling feeling.

    Looking around the only thing in the room that wasn't white was the reflection in a mirror, though it did have a white frame, it actually seemed to be a focal point, drawing your attention intentionally. Beneath the mirror was a note saying, "Remember who you are, otherwise you will have nothing to return to if you loose yourself." Looking at my reflection I pondered the message but felt that it and the mirror were oddly creepy.

    I have plain brown eyes, plain brown hair, a plain white face with spotty brown facial hair... well, it would be spotty if I wasn't clean shaven. I still have the spotty growth of a young teen so I shave it all. Once it fills in I'll let it grow out, but not till its at its best. I'm average build, maybe on the thin side. Nothing special.

    The know yourself message was more irritating than motivational. I'm going to go to community college next year for something. Nothing seems important enough for me to dedicate my life toward it. I don't want to be locked into doing the same thing every day for the rest of my life, why do people and mirrors expect me to know everything?!?!? Feeling flustered, I turned and looked around the rest of the room. White bed, white chair, white table, white dresser, white carpet, white closet, white outfit, white pillows...

    Finally getting bored of looking at white, I took a final glance at the map and stepped out of the white room in search of the exit. Looking around I saw a number of rooms, already decorated with themes and appearing to be ready for guests or entertaining. One room appeared to include a fully stocked bar, which held great excitement for me, because being 18, I am not allowed to purchase alcohol until I'm 21.

    I wanted desperately to run in and check what was stocked, but I held back. I presumed that there was someone waiting for me to walk out the front door and I had a bit to say about being drugged and drug into a strange location, inheritance or not. Drinking illegally, before officially acquiring the property, would probably make the confrontation outside that much worse... but maybe at the housewarming party...

I glanced at my dresser and fully expected to see my clothes should I open the drawer. Catching a glance of myself in the mirror I saw my usual self. I was looking devilishly handsome since I began working out in my mid 20s and had gained a few pounds. The mustache looked good and my 5 'o-clock shadow was more pronounced since it was late evening.
