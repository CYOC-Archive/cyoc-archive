    Mr. Neil remembered how he had lost his older sister. He was the same age than Stan back then, if not younger, while his sister was already a teenager. For the first time, said sister took him to her daily session of beast-people harassing, not knowing it would be the last.

    Before she could enter she beastmen shelter she had recently discovered, what looked like a cat-person, a young woman, got in her path, stopping her. This beastwoman was covered in black fur and had golden eyes, and was dressed in nothing more than a big and colorful necklace.

    Neil's sister instantly ordered her to move, calling the woman-cat ‘a soulless abomination’. At this, the two kids heard a voice DIRECTLY IN THEIR MIND, a voice that sounded as furious as than the mysterious woman looked, calling the sister a ‘waste of a soul’, before plunging her arm into the teenager's torso.

    To Neil's surprise, the cat-person extracted a sphere of light from his sister, and without making any wound.

    Then, said cat revealed herself as Bastet, before telling her story Neil. A story he would hear again a few days latter, in history lesson.

    While his sister wasn't technically dead, without her soul she was little more than a pet.

    For a long time after this, Mr. Neil stayed as far away from beast-people as he could, fearing them like the plague… Until meeting Mora, the mouse-woman who would eventually become his wife, who make him slowly realize beast-peoples weren't that different from humans.
