    Living as ConjoinVerse siblings

    An idea came through Ryan and Bradley's heads. They were in ConjoinVerse, and their ConjoinVerse counterparts were in their own world in NormVerse. Ryan and Bradley thought, if the Ryan and Bradley that were now in NormVerse were conjoined siblings, why couldn't NormVerse Ryan and Bradley be conjoined here?

    Granted, it was a stretch; the two could not be physically conjoined, but they could at least look like they were. Also, if they did look like conjoined siblings, it would cut back on stares from the residents of ConjoinVerse--remember, everyone in this universe was born with at least one conjoined sibling, so it was unheard of here to have only one head. Either way, the two decided that would disguise all traces of the fact that they were separate beings.

    In the dresser, Ryan and Bradley found a large pair of pants and socks. From the closet, their ConjoinVerse friends--conjoined counterparts of their friends back home--took one of the two-headed shirts and a pair of shoes. When Leo-Donnie-Raph-Mikey asked what Ryan and Bradley were doing, the two told the quadruplet brothers about what was called "the conjoined twin challenge" as it existed in NormVerse--since everyone in ConjoinVerse already was a conjoined siblings, such a challenge did not exist here. (However, there were conjoined siblings who longed to live merged with other conjoined siblings, and there were shirts and other clothes large enough to fulfill that desire, so this kind of "challenge" was not unheard of here.)

    Ryan stuck both of his legs through the right-hand leg of the pants, while Bradley did the same with the left-hand pants leg. Next, the two boys each put one of the giant socks over their own feet: one sock over Bradley's feet, the other over Ryan's. The boys then did the same for the shoes; when they stood up together, they looked like fraternal conjoined twins from the waist down. Next came the shirt--short sleeves and two head holes. Ryan stuck his head and right arm through the right-hand side of the shirt, and Bradley put his head and left arm through the left-hand side; the two put their other arms underneath the shirt at each other's shoulders. When the six guys looked in the mirror above the dresser, they saw two wide bodies--by our standards--one with four heads in a row, the other with two. Ryan and Bradley now looked exactly like their ConjoinVerse counterparts (ConjoinVerse Ryan and Bradley were conjoined in the same way, with Ryan on their right-hand side and Bradley on their left).

    Ryan and Bradley were impressed at how they looked, as were Leo-Donnie-Raph-Mikey. Believe it or not, the two were even able to walk properly--they had dressed as conjoined siblings several times before with the quadruplets in NormVerse, and all six had gotten used to being with someone else, making walking and other such tasks simple for all of them. Of course, pretending to be conjoined twins in another universe was a first for Ryan and Bradley, let alone a universe where all humans were conjoined siblings!

    The six guys walked out of ConjoinVerse Ryan and Bradley's bedroom, and went back downstairs. It was lunch time, and they did not want to keep the ConjoinVerse families waiting.
