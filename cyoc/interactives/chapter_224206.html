    Created in the darkest depths of the CYOC laboratory, there was a body altering ray, a ray that could alternate bodies, in even the most weirdest way possible. This ray later was upgraded and given the not-so-original name of the "body altering ray 2.0".

    The device had the form of a toy beam gun but with one shot the target could change completely. The ray had many functions that could be configured in a small screen before shooting, such as:
    - Size: change the size of the target in any scale, can be big as giants or shrink to microscopic size.
    - Age: change the age of the target, can turn as oldest they can be, or being able to de-age them like babies. If the target's age regreses too much past baby they turn into a fetus, the ray's holder would had to shoot a woman to store the fetus there, if not, the fetus will teleport to the closest womb past 10 seconds.
    - Mass: change the form and size of the body parts of the target.
    - Swap: shoot two targets to swap their minds, some parameters can be configured such as if the targets are aware they swapped or not.
    - Part-swap: shoot two targets and select the body parts you want to swap between them.
    - Merge: this function counts with two modes: the full fusion, where you shoot two targets and make them fuse into one being (you can modify the traits of the resulting fusion, like being a 50/50 of the two targets, 80/20 or even 99/1 for example). The other mode is the partial fusion, where you can entirely costumize how the targets are fused.

    Some additional notes:
    - the ray user can also use the device on themselves.
    - the ray can't kill anyone, such as making someone so giant that they can't breathe or turning someone so old that they die of natural causes for example.
    - the device was programmed and tested to be used on humans, using it on animals or other beings could end in unexpected results.

    Soon, the head scientist of the CYOC laboratory decided to teleport the ray to someone.

    Who was it, you ask?
    Well, that's for us to know, and you to find out.

    So tell me, where was the ray sent?
