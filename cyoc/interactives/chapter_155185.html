    The Sea-Maid's Secret

    added by Stark A month ago O Mythological Aquatic

    Erin winced when John spoke up. Of course he would; he was perceptive like that, and not the type to let things go. "Are you… avoiding the ocean for some reason?"

    She sat up on the gym-mat and tried to play it off. "Pfft, what? A mermaid, avoid the ocean? What kind of nonsense talk is that—"

    "The lady doth protest too much."

    Erin glared at John, lying there with his hands behind his head. Of course the bloody writer would quote Shakespeare at her. "Okay… yeah. I don't want to go swimming, and I haven't been in the sea for a while."

    "Don't you miss it?"

    "Of course I miss it! I'm a freaking mermaid! Do you have any idea what it's like, to be a mermaid, live on land, and never feel the seawater on my scales!? It's been—" her voice shrank. "It's been years."

    "Doesn't that take some sort of toll?" John asked. He was both curious and concerned. "Don't mermaids need the sea?"

    "I'm half fish, not all fish," said Erin. "I can go on breathing air for as long as I like, no magic required. As long as I keep my tail moist, there aren't any side-effects. And those magic seashells I left upstairs? They keep my scales from flaking off whenever I tailwalk anywhere."

    "That's… not exactly what I meant," said John.

    "You mean, don't I need the ocean for some kind of emotional or supernatural reason? I've been living on land for three years no without a problem, so clearly the answer is no."

    "So, why avoid it?"

    Erin hesitated. "I don't want to lie to you, John. But I also can't tell you everything, not just yet. We haven't known each other that long."

    "Is there anything you can tell me?"

    "I can tell you that it has to do with my research. And that I don't want to swim in the ocean, because someone cast a spell on my tail. If my fins touch the water, there are merfolk down there who will know immediately—and I don't want them to find me, not yet."

    John sat up, alarmed. "Are you in some kind of trouble?"

    "No, nothing like that," said Erin. "No kind of immediate danger, anyway. It has more to do with what might happen if I fail."

    She fell silent, and John pondered. "Aren't there any more hints you can give me?"

    "Maybe someday," said Erin. "But you're sweet to be so concerned." She kissed John on the cheek and then said, "Let's have dinner. Do you have any seafood around here?"

    John thought for a moment. "Um, just some frozen halibut in the freezer, and I think I have a tray of cocktail shrimp—"

    "Raw shrimp?" said Erin, her face brightening. "That's perfect!"
