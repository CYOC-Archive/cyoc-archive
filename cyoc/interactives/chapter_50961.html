    Mary sat nervously in the back seat of the car that Madame Serenity was driving. Normally she would have sat up front, but with her present smaller size, Madame Serenity told her it wasn't safe for her to be there. Paul sat quietly beside her, although Mary tried not to look at him... though this was because something screamed inside her mind that the orangutan was prey to her. And that made her shudder at what had happened to her.

    Some demon man had used the "transform or dare" curse to turn her into a humanoid lion cub, equivalent to a eight or nine year old girl, but she wasn't entirely sure. She found out that as a lion cub she could understand house cats, which was confusing to her. The lion and the house cat, while both cats were not as closely related as say wolves and dogs were. The wolf and the domestic dog both carried the genus Canis. The wolf was Canis Lupus while the domestic dog was Canis Familiaris.

    Lions and house cats however were not that closely related. The lion, the only cat to live a group, was Panthera Leo. The house cat was Felis Catus. And most of the differences came from their vocal cords. Lions could roar but couldn't purr. House cats could purr, but not roar. Because of that, if animal vocalizations were their own languages, Mary figured that lions and house cats were just distantly enough related that they wouldn't be able to understand her. But then, humanoid animals didn't exist in nature, and neither did demons, so maybe the game was making some generalization that didn't follow a scientific guess.

    Although, at the present time, Mary wished she hadn't earlier decided to be a vet when she grew up as her mind wondered over what she had become and how things were for her.

    And to top all of it off, she was being recruited by an elderly woman with a wounded tabby and an orangutan, who was also a victim of the Game to deal with a pack of wolves who had taken over the estate where her friend Ulysses worked and was using the game to enslave them to someone named Jensen. The whole thing left Mary confused.

    "And this is your house here?" Madame Serenity asked.

    Mary looked out the window at the moderately sized building in front of them.

    "Yes," Mary nodded, "Not too big, not too small. And very comfortable."

    Serenity nodded and carefully parked the car. As she parked it, Mary wrestled with her seat belt. When she finally got it off she turned to see that Paul had gotten out and opened the car door for her.

    "Thank you, I guess," Mary spoke and hoped out while Serenity collected Trevor and got out, herself.

    "You had best lead the way," Serenity told Mary, "they are your parents."

    Mary nervously nodded and began to move toward the front door. She found that despite her regressed age, she could easily reach the doorknob. This made her nervously wonder how tall she would be if she couldn't turned back and she grew up into a humanoid lioness.

    "Hello, Mom, I'm home," Mary called as she entered, "please don't be freaked but some..."

    "And there's my little cub," came her mother's voice as Mary found herself being grabbed from the side and gripped in a strong hug, "Done at the park already?"

    Mary looked, speechless toward her mother. She was relieved to see that she was still completely human, but the fact that she had referred to Mary as a cub and did nothing to notice her younger form bothered her.

    "Is something wrong, dear?" Mary's mother asked in a voice sounding so sweat it'd give an elephant diabetes.

    "Mother! LOOK!" Mary protested.

    "At what?" her mother asked, "did you mess up your fur? Did some boy pull your tail?"

    "Mother! I'm supposed to be a human, not a lion... well, a humanoid lion, but still," Mary said.

    "Mary, I know what you're supposed to be," her mother said softly and hugged her daughter tightly, "we don't know how you were born with all your hormones out of whack, but that is what happened. It doesn't matter. Your father and I love you all the same."

    Mary felt relieved that her mother still loved her, but found it strange that her mother thought her form to be completely normal. It hurt her, as she wanted her mother to be understanding and promise to help help her.

    "I'm not supposed to be like this," Mary said weakly as her mother set her down.

    "So who are your friends, Mary," her mother then spoke, "Did you meet them at the park? You know what I've told you about talking to strangers."

    "Ms..."

    "Hardy, and its Mrs." Mary's mother told the African American woman, "Mrs. Felicity Hardy."

    "My name is Madame Serenity," Serenity spoke, "this is "my" cat, Trevor, and this Paul, and we need your daughter's help. But we thought it a good idea to speak talk with you and your husband before we go any further. Mostly to explain the risks."

    Felicity Hardy gave Serenity a very nervous and parental type look before saying, "Brian is just starting the grill to make dinner. You can wait here for a few minutes."
