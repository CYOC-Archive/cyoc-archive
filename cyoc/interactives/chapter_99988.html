    Andrew looked down and saw a couple of the security guards start to shift into wolves. He silently cursed himself for having been spotted and now having to escape again. It wouldn't be as easy as it had been last time but he didn't have much of a choice. Since there were no other trees within his jumping distance he would have to jump to the ground which would mean exposing himself to getting tackled by the wolves. There was no choice however and he thought he could take two of them though if there were more wolves then he would be in trouble. Taking a breath he quickly used his hands and feet, which he could use just like hands, to climb down to the lowest branch and launch himself in the direction of what smelled like two normals. To their credit the normals held their ground and drew their guns but their reaction speed was too slow and he was able to barrel past them without much difficulty. He smiled to himself thinking that he might get away when he felt two bodies hit his back. He had expected this so he rolled with it and in doing so was able to use the wolves own momentum to throw them off of him. That didn't last long though as the wolves recovered quickly and came back at him. One of the wolves grabbed one arm and the other grabbed the other arm in an attempt to hold him. The thought that they were going to have to get serious if they wanted to detain him crossed his mind as he let out a soft bellow and using his strength brought his arms, along with the wolves holding them, to his front. The two wolves hit each other's head with an audible crack and they staggered away on unsteady feet.

    Andrew was congratulating himself when he heard someone say, "Now." Suddenly pain racked his body as electricity flowed through it from about ten different places. He couldn't do much except fall to the ground and twitch which is what he did. It wasn't long before the same person said, "Cease," and the current cut off. Andrew just lay there panting trying to regain his wits from being tased and to try and come up with a way to escape. He heard the voice speak again saying, "We need the super heavy duty restraints out here by Linna's tree. We caught someone up in it." Well it was pretty clear to Andrew at this point that he wasn't going to get away.

    Rikki looked out the window hearing the shout and then the soft bellow. "What's going on out there?" she asked mostly to herself and not expecting an answer.

    "I'm sure it's nothing for you to be concerned about," a woman said coming into the room.

    Rikki looked at her taking in her scent which she had no trouble identifying as being that of a skunk. She tilted her head and asked, "Who are you?"

    The woman smiled and said, "I'm Dr. Jacqueline Moreau and I'll be your doctor from now on."

    "Why?" Rikki asked a little confused.

    "Well since Frank and I are adopting you it's not in the best interest for me to be your doctor," Kathy said coming. "Besides most of what I do is in the lab though I do see a few patients now and then and I am available for emergencies." She looked down a bit and said, "I have some work to do but I wanted to stop in to see how you were doing."

    "I'm doing great except for this catheter," Rikki said shifting slightly.

    "Well according to your chart your vitals were stable all through the night so we can take it out and disconnect you from the monitors," Dr. Moreau said looking at a clipboard. "There are some brain scans scheduled for later, nothing to worry about though, as well as a few other tests. This morning though you have an appointment with Dr. Twist so she can evaluate your mental state." She apparently saw something because she added, "And don't start arguing. You may believe you're fine and that may be true but you've been through something no one else has. It would be irresponsible of us not to have you see Dr. Twist at least once."

    "Alright," Rikki said sounding resigned. She thought there was nothing wrong with her mentally as this was a dream come true for her though she would accept what Dr. Moreau thought was best. "When can I get out of here?"

    "I'd like to keep you at least one more night just to be on the safe side," Dr. Moreau began, "though depending on how the tests turn out you may be free to go this evening."

    "Don't worry about clothes," Kathy said coming over. "Frank and Diane will get you some after Diane gets out of school. Now I have to get to work so do as Dr. Moreau tells you." With that she gave Rikki a hug.

    "I will," Rikki promised hugging Kathy back.

    Kathy broke the hug with a bit of a smile and then left the room. As soon as she was gone Dr. Moreau said, "Alright let's get that catheter out before Dr. Twist comes in."
