    Jensen sat in the chair specially selected for him. Around here were his various business
    partners. Given the chaos Kurse had caused by turning the world upside down and then
    (mostly) reverted back... there had been predictably chaos in the market place. Stocks had
    gone down, many were selling their stock in fear that the end of the world was upon them.

    Everyone more or less that Kurse, the 'spirit' of an old and powerful curse had been the
    cause of it all, after all, she had been in the minds of billions of people around the world at
    once who caught a glimpse of her vision of a world born of total chaos.

    Some mild lunatics had found the image of that world beautiful and were now causing
    short term, and not so short term havoc and property damage, while others were forming
    cults, and some simply became anarchists even if most anarchists screamed their world
    without laws wouldn't actually be chaos (somehow). Some broke down into despair and a
    wonder if they'd ever emerge from it.

    The majority treated it like a near death experience and actually turned to their respective
    religions for help, guidance and protection. Thank God in Heaven that not a single one had
    begun to play the 'blame game' with one person blaming some other faction for causing
    the curse or bringing it down: Kurse had been crystal clear in everyone's mind that she
    loathed all religions and governments and businesses.

    Naturally most atheists chose to politely keep their opinions to themselves until the hot
    dust had settled.

    The Big Bad Wolf Pack's holders were actually quite bigger than those of the Smith family in
    the previous reality. This made them much bigger players to boot and drawing their
    attention.

    One person at the large table at the meeting room permanently rented out to the Big Bad
    Wolf Pack and it's associates was another Lupman. A semi-large male at eight feet tall, his
    fur was the color of blood and his eyes deep almost black brown. He wasn't part of
    Jensen's pack and was from Frostvile.

    A nine foot tall female leoman, and an ten foot and one incch tall male leoman also sat at
    the table having come all the way from New York. No one knew of course that earlier that
    morning they had been human beings, his wife taking great pride in her long red hair that
    had now not ever existed and though wigs were weird.

    Another was a sand colored scales female dragon. She was incredibly old, and it was a joke
    she only kept going through sheer will power. Unlike Mr. P and Mother Glen, her age
    showed, with cracks in her natural armor and missing scales. Her blue scaled grandson
    stood three steps behind her to cater to her every need. She only had one wing. A japanese
    Zero had blown it off in world war two. She had spent a decade in emotional rehab. She
    wore a custom military uniform, with of course, a purple heart medal. Her tail impatiently
    flicked behind her.

    There were also four middle aged humans, three men and one woman, the one woman
    wearing sun glasses she never took off, and she made a point of always carrying a pistol
    when coming to Glendale and wearing earphones when walking outside. Normally she did
    all her business via conference calls, not daring to set foot in this cursed city. But sadly the
    bylaws here required her to be there in person.

    Finally a small felman (even by felman standards) sat in the last chair, his bright red and
    blue fur had nearly all turned gray. An miniature imitation image of a gargoyle topped his
    cane.

    Jensen sighed when he saw all their harsh prying eyes. 'There are days I wish that I could
    just give Bart his wish and let him take care of these meetings...'

    Gillie looked at Serenity, there was only one answer she could have ever given. She wanted
    this Hell over with. And if she had to risk trusting her friend to do it, to trust some Catholic
    priests and one weird girl she hadn't met... well... they'd be no no worse off then when
    Kurse had for a short time turned the entire world upside down.

    "I do believe that the cure Madam Serenity is suggesting will work. I've known her for two
    life times, and I'd trust her with my life and those of my family."

    Chief Gillie didn't realize just how powerful those words were.

    The Mayor's eyes went wide, 'She's willing to trust her with the lives of the Pride?'

    She had done the equivalent of give a what could best be described as a 'blood oath.' She
    gave this promise on the value of her life, this gave this promise on the value of all the
    lives of the Glendale Pride, and that meant there was no questioning it.

    The Mayor spoke as if he was addressing a family member now, "I understand Gillie.... tell
    me everything that needs to happen."

    +++++++

    Death was in the air. The horses' saw something no else could: they screamed and ran. The
    ropes dropped, life began to be pulled out of the witches from around their necks... but
    with the last remains of their great magic, with the last remains of their vision and plans,
    they prevented life from leaving their bodies until their last spell, their last curse was over
    and done with.

    Omega-Winifred, Monica, and Rosa felt like they were puppets in a show unable to do
    anything or think of anything to do. Only able to watch....

    All five of the hunters had a powerful feeling of suddenly being trapped. Of being before
    two dooms and unable to chose anything but an even WORSE third option.

    "Transform or... dare...." Kathrine gasped out in fear.

    All three witches' hateful voice echoed through their minds, speaking as one, ~Chose your
    doom! Either have your body and mind and precious 'fate' laid bear for us to alter as we
    please. Or let the free will you have surrendered to law already be taken from you and
    rewritten for as many words as we can think of before we die... and speak soon... or we'll
    simply use BOTH! And to make things entertaining for our last act... let your changes or
    commands only trigger once we've cursed all five of you!~

    "THEN SIMPLE!" Father Draco called out, trying to be a hero, "I refuse to play your game! AT
    ALL! Now your curse can never activate!"

    ~HA! You don't answer? At all! We'll accept that answer! AND NOW YOU'LL RECEIVE BOTH
    CURSES!~

    Father Draco felt sick.

    ~And so you may know... to prove that you are no different from us at all... any of you still
    alive, able to speak, or interact with the World of Man or with the Moving World may pass
    on our curse... resist using it for as long as you like, you'll use it eventually... for you'll
    have no choice!~

    Marie Cruz knew what the witches would chose if she chose 'Transform' ... being a
    mundane stupid beast wasn't really all that bad a fate for her all things considered...
    maybe she deserved it. But ... it felt like cowardice... of all the things Marie Cruz
    considered herself, a coward had never been one of them.

    "Dare."

    The witches cursed, they seemed to be able to consider something, maybe Daring Marie to
    say 'Transform' instead or some other obvious cheat. But then Kathrine said,

    "Dare."

    Kathrine considered the fate of becoming a simple child again, to abandon everything... to
    just start over... but she had accepted her marriage the Lord...

    The witches seemed to suddenly change what they were going to say.

    ~You handless man, and you rock monster, we'll deal with you in a minute. But you two!
    YOU TWO! DOG OF GOD! Marie Cruz! We're Dare you, to kill the person alive who you care
    about the most!

    Pink runt leoman, felman, whatever. Fight Marie Cruz to the death at your absolute full
    potential as if you had been groomed and trained to be a killer all your life like her.~

    +++++++

    Of course all of Marie-Eliza's audience gasped at this. Even her mother.

    Of course EVERYONE screamed at this point what irredeemable monsters devoid of
    anything worthy of sympathy the witches were.

    Marie-Eliza said, "It runs deeper than that. The witches had intentionally left a loophole.
    They had instructed Kathrine to fight her absolute hardest. They hadn't given that same
    instruction to Marie Cruz... she could chose to let Kathrine kill her, and have Kathrine live
    with that guilt... or kill Kathrine, and live with that guilt."

    Marie-Eliza then continued her tragic tale. None of her audience the heart to interrupt her.
