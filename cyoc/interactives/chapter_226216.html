As she reached the end of the corridor, she glanced in a mirror on the wall before sulking.

The girl was swamped in her adult clothing, her feet wobbling in heels a couple sizes too big with the hem of her pants dragging along the ground. "I find negotiations with children to be far easier than seasoned federal agents," Lilith grinned. "Be thankful this is the worst of it Director, afterall two year olds are capable of speech." Sulking, she could do little more than nod as she and Lilith made their way towards a waiting table. Sarah now found herself shuffling towards the table, trying not to fall over as the pair sat down. As they did, Sara could feel her feet lift from her heels as she now shook her hands free of her jacket. "I think you know why I'm here Lilith."

    "Yes that business with the necromancer and the vampire," Lilith replied. "I am aware of what they did and yes they are currently residing on the grounds. However, I wish to be clear, I did not assist in freeing the necromancer nor did I authorize the vampire to harm your people."

    "You do realize I'm going to need more than just an apology," Sarah stated, trying to make her childish voice sound authoritative. "Jolie did alot of damage on that island and Jolie seriously injured four of my men." Lilith nodded, offering the child Director a soft smile meant to humor children. It only made Sarah flush in anger and embarrassment yet she needed to keep her composure.

    "I respect you Director," Lilith smiled. "Others in your situation would not have acted so measured in their statements. I applaud your character. That and I'm sure if something were to happen to you your demon hunting bodyguard and the wizards would kick in my door." Now it was Sarah's turn to smile, which came across as a kid who knew they were getting away with something. "I didn't get this far without ha ing a backup plan."

    "Duely noted young Director," Lilith smiled. "I will compensate you monetarily as well as provide medical care for your injured staff. As far the necromancer and vampire, I am in need of their services until the threat of the Normal has passed."

    "But we will receive both in the future," Sarah replied. To this Lilith paused, rubbing her chin with her one hand. "The necromancer I understand however the vampire is in need of a second chance."

    "I'm all for second chances Lilith but she did injure my people," Sarah stated. "She needs to be punished for that. If your interested how about this. A probationary sentence. If the vampire behaves herself, she can work off her sentence. Provided she shares Intel from any missions she's part of. If she doesn't, than she's in our custody when the threat passes."

    Lilith nodded, the demon queen standing as she approached the young Sarah. Taking the girls hand, the pair shook as Lilith gazed down at the preteen Sarah. "Its settled then," she said. "We shall provide monetary and medical assistance to your people in exchange for the necromancer and vampires services. In addition, the necromancer will be placed in your custody when the threat of the Normal has passed while the vampire will provide your organization with information or face criminal penalties."

    "Sounds fair," Sarah stated.

    "Always a pleasure doing business with you Director McMillan," Lilith smirked. "If you wish, you may want to enjoy an afternoon in the park, in your current youthful state."

    "Maybe another time," Sarah grinned. "I think I've had enough of the sixth grade for one day."
