Jeff then responded back as peaceful as he could. “I need to use something to test it out. Besides, I haven’t used your sock for months and I don’t know if you need it back. It’s already dry and crusty as it is.” But Carl wasn’t buying it and demanded the sock back. “You had my sock for a few years now and it’s really starting to annoy me. Turn my sock back and give it to me so that way I could clean it. Now!” Jeff sighed and said back, “I’ll give it back to you later. See I left the change to last one day, just to see if I want to keep him that way or change him back. You’ll get the sock once the day is over.” But that made Carl angrier as he marched to him demanding, “I’m not joking Jeff! Give me my sock back right now, or else!”

But after hearing the argument, between the two he didn’t like this one bit. He already enjoyed being human rather then a lifeless sock and doesn’t want to return. And even if the real Jeff wanted to keep him, he might have second thoughts and turn him back into a sock. The clone Jeff had to do something and fast before things get worse for him. That’s when he looked at the Chronivac and came up with an idea.

    Being since the Chronivac turned him from a sock to human, what if it did the exact opposite. Mainly turn a human being into an inanimate object in particular. The clone thought of this and finds that it is perfect. Looking back, he could see the two fighting over him and thought that they were distracted.
