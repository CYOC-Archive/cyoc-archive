    Stripped down to the bone

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added by TaleSpinning A year ago I O

    "Arise, my minion!" said a somewhat excited sounding Vampire
    "Now Alice she's a thinking feeling woman in there. Treat her with some respect." Said what sounded like an older man. The zombie couldn't exactly open her eyes in this situation, but the sensation seemed similar. It's just instead of her eye lids revealing light to her irises some magical light lit up her eye sockets granting her a sort of vision. She knew there was something wrong when her vision was different. Most everything was blurry, but there seemed to be some faint tendrils of colors in the air... Wait... what happened to her muscles? The Zombie was concerned as she was trying to use a brain which no longer existed to move her equally as non-existent muscles to move her body.
    "I'm not sure she knows how to move..." The Vampire who was presumably named Alice stated "Perhaps she needs to be shown how?"
    The older man said "Well that's up to you. I'm still just a guy."
    "Well then I guess I'll just take control of her for now." Alice coughed then commanded "Rise and Stand on your own two feet Minion!" to which the Zombie's felt her body tingling and then it got itself up and stood. "Utter my name!"
    The Zombie's mouth opened and then out came out a cacophonous "AlICe" as if there was very little control over the volume or even sound being uttered.
    "Do what you wish." Alice commanded.
    Having seen a lot more the Zombie noticed two interesting things. Alice seemed to have a very clear red aura around her which made it easy to see her, and some thin tendrils of that aura were coming to the Zombie. The man had a seemingly brightish aura... not really of any color but bright. Then the Zombie was able to see at least parts of her body and... Well the Zombie was sure she wasn't a zombie. maybe some definition of zombie would have her included, but while the aura surrounding herself wasn't as bright as Alice's it was bright enough to give her a good image of her bony hands and legs. Bony not in that she was very thin... Which she technically was, but Bony as in she seemingly had shed all her flesh leaving only bone. It was very concerning as was the fact that when Alice had lead the Skeleton to what she seemed to be saying was a mirror... she didn't really see anything. The whole world was a blur, but the magical strands of aura all around where so clear... As was Alice, the older man, and all the multi-colored twinkling lights underneath them all.
    "Do you have a name?" Asked a concerned Alice and the Skeleton had to think... and then the Skeleton tried thinking some more. It was clear to the Skeleton that their thinking wasn't just tied to their brain. They could understand speech and think, but maybe the removal of the brain had removed some important things... or maybe that was being in isolation for who knows how long. Whatever she had looked like or whoever she had been were effectively gone. Maybe some genius could bring up something by just judging her bones, but her old self was gone... She was just a skeleton. So she shook her head no to her master.
    "Can you see this? Do you want anything? Is there anything that you remember?" were all asked but all resulted in the Skeleton shaking her head no.
    "Are you certain that there's a person in there?" Alice asked the older man
    The older man said "Yes... whether or not they are still in one piece is another question."
    "They seem mostly blind... I'm going to go forward with my plan and go forward from there. It's no use asking what type of body she might want if she can't see the options." The older man was silent as Alice ordered "Go to sleep" To the skeleton. At which point the Skeleton's consciousness faded until...
