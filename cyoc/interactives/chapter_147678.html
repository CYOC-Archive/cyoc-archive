Though the unique X/Y chromosome of herm DNA did have an interesting way of determining an embryo's birth. Even if both parents were hermaphrodites, that didn't necessarily mean that all their children would also be hermaphrodites. For instance, the Anaconda brood contained single-sexed offspring as well as dual-sexed. Most notable were the only two-headed children among them were each a single sex. Another good example was with Louise's cubs.
