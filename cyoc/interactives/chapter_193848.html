    Isaac stayed in the Captain’s room not daring to venture anywhere as she was. She fretted about what she would do with her life. She had only known life as a pirate. Now that choice was denied to her.

    She contemplated her other choices. There was Madam’s offer to replace Annabelle, which she wasn’t that keen on. She had no desire for her new anatomy to be explored by men. The only other women Isaac knew well were the tavern wenches. She knew how they were treated by patrons. Isaac was guilty of groping the wenches as much as anybody.

They were on a boat the pirates had captured. The captain had not let the crew have their way with them and had released them unharmed. He said such women needed to be treated with respect. To be honest Isaac did not know what nuns did.

    Lastly there were what he termed honest women. She had seen what her former comrades had told him were honest women in the streets, often they had children in tow. Isaac guessed they were the mothers of the world and everyone had a mother. When he came across such women in the streets they would turn away and not meet his gaze or even cross to the other side of the road. He had learnt not to approach them. They were so timid. That life did not seem very appealing. Yet that was the life Annabelle had apparently chosen over working for Madam.

    Isaac tried to recall if she had ever met a female pirate. She had not. The captain was right in that a pirate ship was no place for a lass. And thinking of her reflection she could not deny that a lass she now was. She knew what happened to young woman found on boats they had captured. It was rare that the Captain spared them from the lusts of the crew. If they were lucky they survived to be sold to a brothel or slavery. The only exceptions were those women who convinced the captain they were worth more if ransomed such as the duchess or that time they had captured the nuns.

    Isaac eventually came out of her morbid ruminations and began to explore the Captain's room. She was grateful that the Captain had allowed her to stay here. There was a large comfortable looking bed and a desk against the wall. There was also a door leading out to a small private patio from which there was a view of the harbour. She could see the Crimson Pearl at anchor. Small boats were running to and from the vessel. Though it was too far away to recognise individual sailors. No doubt provisioning was underway. The boat was getting ready for it's next voyage - a voyage that Isaac sadly realised she would not be part of.
