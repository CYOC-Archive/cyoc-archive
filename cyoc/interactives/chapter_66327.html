    Jefferson's sheltered life had left him somewhat unaware of what the rest of the world was like. His parents controlled just about every aspect of his life. He was home schooled from the time he was able to read. He was book smart, smarter than most kids his age. Two years ago, on his 12th birthday, his parents let him go on to the family computer by himself. Unbeknownst to them he was quickly able to bypass the parental controls they had set on his log in account. He even figured out how create a secret subroutine that would delete his search history when he logged out.

    His family had just moved into area from a no name mid west town that no one ever heard about. It was a peaceful little town, very "Norman Rockwellish" by nature. It was somewhat self-isolated from the rest of the world in most of the modern ways. Much to the dismay of his parents, they had to move away from their idyllic way of life. Work was some getting harder to find close to home so they had to move to where the better paying jobs were to be found.

    At first his father was the only one working. Even with him working two dead end low paying jobs the bills never seemed to get any smaller. Eventually his mother had to get a job as well. Jefferson's parents never really felt that they could trust their son enough to let him stay home by himself while they at work. But what choice did they have. After all they couldn't very well hire a sitter for their fourteen year old son, that cost money that they didn't have.

    Jefferson was outside reading a book. There wasn't much else to do because his parents had locked the computer and television away in the family room. It was a warm day and Jefferson had fallen asleep under the tree in his front yard and didn't see the two large teens approaching.
