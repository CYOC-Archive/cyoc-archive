    As Josh and Dean leave the abandoned Airport, leaving the “plane” behind, the CEO calls his tow-crew and tells them about the plane and to transport it into one of the free hangers.

    Jeff is still in sleep-mode when the tow truck with two men arrives, the passenger leaves the car, while the driver positions the truck. The man, who left the car, looks up at the “plane”, scratching his head and a confused expression on his face, “why would anyone dump a brand new plane on an abandoned airfield,” he asks himself.

    “Who cares,” a voice says from behind him, he turns to see the driver, an older white guy standing behind him, “let's get this plane hooked up and into the hanger,” he says.

    “Don't you find it weird, Nik,” the other man, who looks as if he is in his mid 30s, says, “I mean... the plane has to belong to someone.”

    Nik rolls his eyes and lets out a frustrated groan, “listen Todd,” he says, “I don't give a rats ass, I just follow orders, and the order is to get this plane into a hanger. So stop questioning it and get it hooked up.”

    Todd throws up his hands, “fine, sorry,” he says. The young man walks up to the tow truck and starts to grab heavy thick chains, “grumpy old fart,” he mumbles as he attaches the chains to the plane's wheels. Todd checks to see if the brakes are loose, which surprisingly they are, “weird, well it is a flat surface, so it defiantly would not have rolled off,” he then walks back to the truck, “okay, she is hooked up,” he says as he gets in.

    Nik does not reply, he simply starts to slowly drive forward, both men look back and see that the “plane” is rolling smoothly, so they continue to drive. They leave the abandoned airport and drive down the road towards the back of the new airport where all of the hangers are located.

    30 minutes later Jeff the plane is standing in a huge hanger all by himself, this is when Jeff finally wakes up. Jeff feels his system boot up and he feels his senses returning, 'what a weird dream,' he thinks to himself, 'I dreamed that I transformed myself into a plane... he... I wish that was real.' The teen tries to sit up in bed, but finds that he can't move, 'hu... why... why can't I move? Wait... this is not my bed room at all,' he thinks, 'it looks more like a hanger... that means... it was not a dream, I really am a plane.'

    After the excitement and realization subsided, Jeff starts to think, 'wait... wasn't I standing between two hangers on an abandoned airport,' he asks himself, 'I can't move, but from what I can see is that I am defiantly in a hanger, a clean and modern one. How did I get here? Have I been moved while I was asleep?'

    While racking his brain to try and make sense, Jeff is suddenly hit by a realization: his clothes! His clothes were laying next to his plane-body, if he was moved, what had happened to his clothes. His phone is in his jeans and on his phone is the chronivac. If he is nowhere near his clothes, he'd be unable to transform back. He mentally kicks himself, 'why did I not think of connecting the chronivac to my plane-computer-mind? I am so stupid!'

    Jeff has no time to think of a way out of his predicament as a door next to the huge hanger doors opens and someone enters.
