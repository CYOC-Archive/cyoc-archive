    Mark, a 12 year old boy, had recently found a wad of birthday money from months ago that he had never spent. He felt downright rich and the money was now burning a hole in his pocket. So, after telling his parents he was going to the local GoodWill he ran out of the house and went the two blocks to the local store.

    Going through the aisles he looked at the toys and games people had just given away trying to decide what he wanted. Mark noticed a neat looking toy gun which he picked up and tested in the air a couple times. It was neat in that it had a bunch of switches with weird indicators but didn't seem to do anything cool like light up or make any sounds. Mark was quickly growing disinterested until he saw a brown leather cowboy like hat.

    Feeling inspired, he grabbed the hat (which was adult sized and way too big) and went to the full sized mirror making his arnryist face, he pulled the gun pretending he was a rough and tough gunslinger having a duel back in the wild west. He was not too impressed with how fierce he looked and he really wasn't intimidating at all. In the reflection he noticed that when he pulled the trigger, one of the lights on the top of the barrel came on. "Strange, it didn't do that before..." he thought to himself.

    After several minutes of growing increasingly frustrated, curious, and confused he managed to put together that the toy lights up when you point it at people. "Which is pretty cool that a toy gun could save its battery by not activating on accident." he reasoned. Trying it out he also noticed that it lights up different amounts for different people but didn't figure out why. The toy had a small tag he could read later, maybe it had instructions, he had already decided to buy it, it had one really cool feature, it probably had more.

    Approaching the register he grabbed gobs of candy and kept the hat figuring that one day it would fit him and until then, it was still pretty cool. Between the hat, toy gun, candy and tax, he only got change back from the 25 dollars he walked in with but he was happy as he walked out and opening a bag of skittles.

    None of the patrons, employees, nor Mark realized how lucky they were that all the blue and green switches happened to be down and how close everyone in the store was to having the weight their body naturally stays at increased by 5-10lbs each of the 3-5 times he tested the toy on them. The increase mass switch had been stuck in between settings and was jostled into the off position by a king size snickers bar.

    Mark walked home happily munching on candy, appreciating his birthday money... from... some relative or another.
