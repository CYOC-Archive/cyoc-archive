    A ringing sound broke the silence. “Someone’s at the door.” Tulipelle said as she leapt to the doorway, carried briefly by her wings. “Just a minute, let me get changed first!” She shouted before she flits to a dresser. Stripping off her nightgown, she rifles through a drawer. You turn your head purely out of courtesy.

    Another ringing came, and you were curious what was making that sound. Peeking your head out of the bedroom curtain you hear the ringing once more, coming from what appeared to be bluebells hanging near the doorway. “Boy, you use flowers for everything.” You remarked.

    “You have no idea.” Tulipelle said with a sly grin. She now had a flower bikini on; this one was red, though. She also threw a violet article of clothing on the bed. “Here, put this on. It’s the least revealing thing I could find that isn’t formal.” She said before rushing out of the bedroom with another ring sounding.

    “Calm down, I’m coming!” Tulipelle said as you slip off your nightgown and examine the clothing she gave you. It resembled a one-piece bathing suit made of violet flower petals.

    As you slip your feet into the clothes, you could hear the conversation in the next room.

    “Hey, Tulipelle what gives? I came by last night and you weren’t here.” Came a bubbly girl’s voice; though around here ‘girl’ is pretty much a given.

    “I was… out… doing stuff.” Tulipelle answered. “What were you doing here last night?” By now, you were dressed and stepped out of the room. Rosetta had a pink flower petal bikini to match her wings and bright red hair to her shoulders with a yellow flower bud in it.

    “Well, I came over to tell you…” Tulipelle’s friend spotted you. “Well hello, and who’s this?” She asked.

    “This is Irisette MoonGlow, I… met her last night.” Tulipelle answered.

    “Oh, I see; bumpin’ blossoms, were we?”

    “No! It… it’s not like that at all…”

    “Bumpin’… huh?” You ask, not sure what she meant.

    “You know, swappin’ nectar, flower bedding, dueling pistils.” she continued.

    None of these phrases made any sense to you, and your expression showed it.

    “She means… having sex.” Tulipelle said with a slight embarrassment.

    “Um… no, we weren’t doing any of that.” You answer. “We were… well, it’s actually a long story miss… uh…”

    “Oh, This is Rosetta GossamerPetal,” Tulipelle spoke up, “my best friend growing up.”

    “GossamerWing, in three day’s time!” She said, gesturing to the flower bud in her hair.

    “By the Goddesses, I completely missed that! Does this mean Dandel accepted!? Oh, that’s wonderful!”

    “Yup, that’s what I wanted to tell you last night.”

    Come to think of it, a number of the fairies you saw last night had flowers in their hair; and most of them were paired up. “Who’s Dandel, and what is that flower anyway?” You ask.

    “Seriously, you don’t know what an Amora Flora is? Cliodhna’s wings, it’s as if… wait, you’re a changeling, aren’t you?”

    At least that was a familiar term to you. “If you mean a human who became a fairy, then yes; but like I said, it’s a long story.”

    “Ha; I knew it” Rosetta said; “I always knew you’d end up with a changeling for a heartfriend. You always were fascinated by those humans, weren’t you?”

    “We’re just regular friends, alright!?” Tulipelle shouted, blushing redder than a cherry; (well, a normal one).

    “Okay, okay; sorry;” Rosette apologized; “but anyway; Dandel LaceWing is my heartfriend, well, amorapromised now that we exchanged Amora Floras, and soon to be flowerbound once we have our Bonding Ceremony.” Rosetta answered. Once again, her phrases go over your head.

    “Perhaps I should explain,” Tulipelle said to Rosetta. “I am the human expert here and I can use comparisons she’ll understand.” Tulipelle turns to you.

    “First, ‘heartfriend is what you’d call a ‘girlfriend’. ‘Amorapromised’ is what you’d call a ‘fiancée’. You with me so far?”

    “Yes.” You answer.

    “Okay.” Tulipelle continued. “One becomes amorapromised by being given an Amora Flora, and giving one, yourself; it can be any flower plucked from anywhere, but most go for their heartfriend’s favorites for that extra romantic flair. The Amora Flora itself is like a combination engagement ring and wedding ring. They remain closed, like Rosetta’s, until completing the Bonding Ceremony…”

    “A wedding, I take it?”

    “You’re catching on! The Amora Flora opens at the end of the ceremony, signaling the two fairies being florabound.”

    “That clear things up for ya?” Rosetta asked.

    “Yes, I understand now.” You answer.

    ”Good, ‘cause I’m inviting Tulipelle to the ceremony, and I think bringing her new just friend would make for some good exposure to our ways, seein’ as how you’ll be livin’ the fair life from now on. Speakin’ of which, how’d a human wind up like this anyway?

    You recount your story to Rosetta. She seemed amused by how you agreed to be turned from male to female, figuring most male humans would have refused and parted ways then and there; and surprised how well you took your transformation, despite fainting.

    “So, how do you like flying?” Rosetta asked.

    “I don’t know; I never tried it yet. I’m not even sure I know how to work these things.” You answer.

    “Well, don’t worry, it’ll come naturally; in fact, most changelings say their first flight is almost as exhilarating as their first time nectaring.”

    “Rosetta, does everything have to be about sex with you?” Tulipelle asked.

    Ignoring her remark, Rosetta continues. “Say, I’m headin’ over to Dandel’s right now, wanna come with? It’s the perfect opportunity for you to spread your wings and truly join us as a fairy.”
