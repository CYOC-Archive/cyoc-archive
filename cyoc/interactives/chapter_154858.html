To that end, she mostly kept the amulet to herself, and kept its powers and it purpose a closely-held secret.

Yes, many times she even searched the dry land above the island, but she never found it. (How it came to be at the shrine in the sand-dunes, out in the open and easily found, just in time for John and Alex to appear and lay claim to it, is perhaps a mystery that will forevermore go unsolved.) She reminds them that their Goddess demands that they must be maidens and not women, for to be a mermaid and to be protected from the world of men, they must remain pure, and their carnal urges must remain buried and go unquenched. But the mermaids disagree; they want to be women again, and they say so, sometimes with strong words and make Nephi very angry. She passes the relic to Alex, who also pits her will against Nephi's song; but try as she might, she cannot win. She gives in and returns the sacred amulet to the Queen.

But they also feel fear: fear that any disobedience will be met with swift punishment, enforced by the power of the Queen's enchanted siren-song. With no way to resist that magic, the mermaids are already defeated before a rebellion can even begin.

    Alex, though, does not consider open rebellion. She stands apart from the other mermaids. She has an idea—and to see it through, she needs your help.
