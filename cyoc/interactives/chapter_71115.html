    Jane brushed a few strands of her dirty blonde hair aside as she exited the store. She looked down through her

    that little shop there, a kinda new age herbal nick knack shop. Considering that most of the businesses around
    were empty, or worse crumbling slowly into rubble, that little store seemed like a tiny light. Likely to be
    swallowed up soon though, Jane mused.

    Still, she was lucky to find something. She looked down again at her purchase, counting the candles again.
    "Pleasure Island Set..." She softly read the name aloud. She had needed something for her party tonight, and
    this seemed to fit the bill. She hoped. Jane had never thrown a slumber party before, so not at seventeen,

    was for.

    Her friend, Lauren, and neighbor for over a decade and a half, was in definite need of cheering up. Lauren was
    considered by some, to be Jane's better half. Jane had dirty blonde hair, hazel eyes, glasses, B cup at best,
    and an overall plain appearance and demeanor. Lauren far from plain. She had brilliant platinum blonde hair,

    Well, they were, till Lauren's boyfriend of over four years dumped her. That was why Jane was having her

    coldly as he left for university, a whole country away. She had been in tears for nearly a week now, and had
    even missed some classes! She couldn't sit idly by and wait anymore, so she set up this party.

    And with that pleasant thought, she found herself outside her house. With a smile and a skip she entered,

    parents from earlier. They had called to tell her they'd be out until Monday, having gotten their flight
    canceled and rescheduling. Meant she would have all the extra time to clean up, since it was only Friday

    other. What would they do when they graduate...

    Her thoughts were interrupted by the reply from one friend. After a quick glance she pocketed her phone, and
    made her way to the front door. The other two would be arriving soon, so she went to collect Lauren. Being
    right next door, this wasn't much harder than walking over to her house, and knocking. She smiled warmly and
    waited, the door clicking and opening...
