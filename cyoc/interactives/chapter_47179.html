    The skunkette turned her attention to the Transportation District, deciding it might be best to plan out an escape route to someplace else before she got herself into too much trouble. Her boot-covered feet slapped against the floor as she hurried ahead to the central area of the Megaplex, noting that most of the Food Court and eating space was there in the central space. It was an outer coating to the main walls that housed the Grand Atlantic Casino, and the main walkway that connected the Blocks in a hexagonal-like circle.

    She took a right, pulling out her yellow-rimmed glasses and sliding them onto her muzzle as she looked around the entrance to the Transportation District. Over her head was a griffin to the right and a space ship to the left that looked more like one from Star Trek than Star Wars. Along the floor was black and white tiled floors and streaming colored banners up the walls that must have represent the other regions of The Kingdom.

    Stephanie noted that she was in Amarnia, an America-like Region of The Kingdom. There was a flag that had nine alternating red and white stripes with a blue square in the top left corner that had four, three, and four star upon it. It looked like the red bars represented the original nine locations inside of Amarnia and the stars counted for how many there were now after six months.

    Along the Block were more people than she'd seen in any other place in The Kingdom so far. People were lined up underneath the flags of the different regions that each had a listing for departing flights. Wether those flights be planes, space ships, carriages, taxis or busses, she couldn't tell from where she was. By the scent of things, it could have easily been all of the above.

    She saw a sign for Fantastica… a flag with a sword and shield and skull against a white and red background. Underneath the flag was a group of about twelve people all talking about someone they had lost that morning. They must have been Players because there was a Dwarf who was holding a Nintendo DS and seemed to be playing Tetris upon it. They were waiting to get a griffin flight back to Icepeak Mountain.

    Fantastica sounded like a World of Warcraft or Everquest portion of The Kingdom from what Stephanie could gather as she went by. Apparently one of their group had gone missing while looking for a wizard on a wanted poster out in Amberpine Forest. An interesting idea, to be sure. Wanted posters meant killing for money, and Stephanie made a point to go back and look into the situation later.

    She passed a large centaur and three woman, one of who smelled like a mare. She wasn't going to ask. Still, they were just outside the SimWay Subway system, a big green and yellow sign over the hallway next to them deemed it as such. It appeared to go down into the basement levels, and by what she could tell it had a map that connected it all over Vector City. It would be handy, again, if she needed to get out in a hurry. Subways didn't check passports like she assumed the aerospace segment would.

    Indeed, there were mobile platforms (QuickTrams) off to Stephanie's side that lead off to George Clinton International Airport. She bumped into a Satyr on her way through the hall and mumbled something about people always being in her way. It was only once she came to the end of the Block did she finally see a lot of human beings. She had begun to wondered where they all were.

    The left and right sides of the hall for the first two floors all lead into Teslaphuk Motors. It proclaimed automobiles of all kinds, and Stephanie could find no fault in that claim. She saw tiny electric cars, big pickup trucks, sports cars, busses, dirt bikes, motorcycles, and even a vehicle that resembled a Tesla Roadster as she walked by the front windows. A few of the vehicles were name-brand, but most of them resembled the cars from Burnout. Based on real cars, but unique.

    She smiled to herself, wondering how much security was on the shop. One of those Teslas would look really good wrapped around her like a second skin. She glanced around at all the other inhabitants and thought over her next action.
