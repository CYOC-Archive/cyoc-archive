Once the window had been fully exposed, she found herself squinting to peer into the dark room, and promptly backing up with a gasp as a pair of golden eyes opened to stare back at her.

    Sharp clicks echoed through speakers mounted near the window as something began moving, slowly approaching the better-illuminated portion of the room. A figure that became more recognizable the closer it was - after all, she'd seen the photos of Captain Stone hundreds of times in the past few weeks. It curled and twisted in what seemed like a curious manner as Siobhan looked back up into those golden eyes. They seemed to be... probing her, in a way. Intensely scanning a stranger, trying to discern all that was possible about this new addition to the habitat. She tried to stare back, only to falter as the predatory glint in Stone's gaze sent a shiver of primal fear down her spine.

    "Hasn't said a word since landing," Kelly elaborated, her unease lesser than Siobhan's yet still clearly visible. "Just stalks around her cage, staring at the scientists and guards. We've tried sending people in to collect samples from her, but..." She winced. "It got messy."

    As if in response, Stone briefly snarled, showing off rows of razor-sharp teeth. "Yeah... I can imagine." Slowly she reached up to take off her glasses, stepping closer to get a better look; the woman on the other side of the glass responded by moving closer to the window herself, still staring at Forrester. "Incredible. She must have been exposed to some kind of spaceborne organism with... well, I can't even imagine what kind of natural mechanism could be used to cause such fundamental tissue and genetic alterations... i-it must have been something engineered, maybe surgical... and the ramifications of that..."

    Kelly sighed. "It's just an alien, Forrester. Don't lose your shit."

    "Just an alien?! Just a bloody alien?! This... this completely changes our fundamental understanding of the universe, of life as we know it, of our place in-"

    "Gonna stop you right there." The older doctor held up a hand. "This isn't the USAF's first extraterrestrial rodeo. But this?" She gestured to Stone. "This is completely new. We've never cataloged anything like it before. That's why you're here - all the veterans can't make heads or tails of what's happened to her. We need fresh eyes, somebody who doesn't think they know all there is to know about alien bullshit - but I don't need someone freaking out and having an existential crisis." She sighed. "Trust me, get enough of those in an average day already..."
