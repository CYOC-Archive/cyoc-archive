    "Okay, I think that's enough dance lessons for one night," commented Martha Anderson, as she looked at the living room clock and saw it was almost 9:30. "How do you feel?" she asked Eric.
    "I think I'm starting to get the hang of this. I just hope I'm good enough to dance with Julie next weekend," replied her youngest son.
    "Don't worry son. You will be," said his father, confidently.

    "What do you mean?" asked Eric, as everyone looked at Nate with somewhat of a confused expression on their faces.
    "I don't know all their names, but there are plenty of dances where it's just basically the couple moving around together in time with the music and the rhythm of the song's beat more than actually dancing. Here, I'll show you," offered Nate, as he went back to one song from earlier on his parents' CDs that they skipped during Eric's dance lessons.

    As Micky Dolenz announced, The local rock group down the street is trying hard to learn their song to the opening bars of The Monkees' "Pleasant Valley Sunday", Nate just began dancing around in a relatively small space on the cleared floor. He moved his arms, shuffled, and jumped about for the weekend squire who just came out to mow his lawn.
    "See what I'm getting at Eric?" asked Nate, hoping he wasn't making a fool out of himself. "You're still dancing, but neither you nor your partner will actually be holding hands or even remotely touching each other, unless you decide to bump hips for some reason during a number."
    "That does kind of look like fun," replied Eric, as he began dancing around as Mrs. Gray took pride in her roses and Mr. Green was happy to have a TV in every room, although why anyone could or would watch so much television was beyond him.
    Soon even his parents were happily dancing together until the last strains of the final coda echoed through the speakers.
    "What Nate was trying to point out in his own unique way," began William Anderson, as he shut the stereo off for the night, "is that there is a wide variety of music out there. We would have saved what we just did for later, because it's pretty much the easiest to learn, but there's no way to know for sure what will be on the play list until the Halloween dance actually begins."
    "And what you get to dance to will also be influenced by all the requests whoever's there will make to the DJ on duty too," added his mother.
    "Wow. I hope I can master it all by next weekend," commented Eric.
    "Some of the great dance masters of our time will be the first to admit that they are still learning too," replied Martha, which made Eric smile. "The most important thing to learn from all of this is that you and Julie love each other so much. Otherwise, why take dance lessons?" added his mother.
    "That we do," replied Eric with a smile. Then he thanked everyone for their help before going upstairs to get ready for bed, hoping there was still time to catch Julie in chat.
