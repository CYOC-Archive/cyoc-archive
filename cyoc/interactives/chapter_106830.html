    The flash of lightning subsided, and the cloud of smoke cleared to reveal the weird little kid from around the corner Robin. His eyes are glowing red. You saved Robin's kitten just yesterday when it got trapped by a couple of mean dogs. He seemed real grateful, and promised he'd return the favor. You laughed it off, how could that twerp help you?

    "PUT HIM DOWN NOW, WIZ KID!" roared Robin.

    "Hey!" shouted Merlin raising his wand.

    Robin kept staring at Liam, but raised his hand to Merlin, and simply said, "Chill!"

    Merlin was instantly sealed in a giant block of ice.

    "Listen, witch-boy, I don't know what this guy did to you, but you can eat him if you like," Liam said taking you from his mouth and offering him to Robin.

    "Cretin!" growled Robin, "Change him back, and the others too," he added glaring at the fried vegetables on the table.

    "Right, they've been cooked. How am I supposed to change them back?" he stole a glance at his ice-blocked brother, and realized Merlin wasn't going to be any help.

    "Hey, how did you get passed all our wards, kid? An army of imps should've been all over you the moment you breached the first ward."

    Robin smiled. It was not a nice smile. At least he seemed to be on your side.

    "Stupid Wizardling! Did you think any of those wards could stop my magic?"

    "You're that Robby whatsit from around the corner, what's so special about your wicca?"

    The room got suddenly cold, as Robin's smile became a toothy grin.

    "The name isn't Robby. It's Robin- Robin Goodfellow. I'm of the fairy folk."

    "Oh, so what does being gay have to do with breaking all our defensive spells?"

    Robin's eyes got big as saucers, "You're dumber than you look. Here's a hint, Lamo, - this time you really pucked up."

    You already were on to this. This kid wasn't a kid at all. He's that fairy guy from Midsummer Nights Dream. He's the right hand mischief maker of the king and queen of the fairies. There's no such things as fairies, but there's no such things as witches and wizards. Oh, yeah, and no way anyone could turn you into a carrot....

    "A fairy, huh?" Liam said thoughtfully. He grabbed a knife off the table and jabbed it menacingly at Puck.

    "Seriously?" Robin laughed, and the blade of the knife lit up like a sparkler, and curled up in black ash as until it reached the handle.

    Liam dropped the knife.

    "But it was iron. I thought fairies were afraid of iron!"

    Robin sighed, "Well, it looks like someone needs some educating. First off, that was a real silver knife, just spelled by your mother to look like stainless steel to fool thieves no doubt. But even if it were stainless steel-"

    There is the rustle of clothing, and a woman in a grey dress enters. It's Liam and Merlin's mother. She looks at Liam, Robin and then the block of ice containing Merlin. She swallows hard. She takes a step toward Robin. Liam grins, now that fairy's going to get it, he thinks.

    She stops and curtsies tilting her head forward she says, "My lord Goodfellow, I had no idea that you were going to grace us with your personage."

    "Or you'd have used stronger wards?" Puck laughed, "They wouldn't have stopped me. He violated a mortal under my protection, and refuses to reverse his curse."

    "Liam?" his mother asked raising her eyebrows and voice in unison. She seems very fearful.

    "I turned them into vegetables and cooked them. Once they've been cooked, well- you know?" Liam shrugged.

    "Oh, well, perhaps if I check my books, I can find a spell to restore them. Liam, if I find the spell, you'll use it won't you?"

    Liam is thinking that this is code, his mother must have a spell that will put that weasely fairy in its place. He answered coyly, "Certainly, mother."

    She looked at Robin and asked, "So what did this mortal do to curry favor with your esteemed -"

    "Enuf prattle, sorceress, get ye thy tome of magicks an' be quick about it," Robin intoned in a sing-songy accent.

    "Why aren't speaking normal?"

    "Pesky waif, when the Puck is pestered, as like a boil festered, so is the wrath of the fairykind. A rhyme to reshape reality to suit the crime," he hissed.

    "I'll find the spell, dear Lord Goodfellow, no need to feel pestered or festered," she said nervously opening a kitchen cupboard that had contained the deep fryer, but now displaced a library of books. She hissed at Liam, "Fairies are pure magic, when they rhyme they cast spells, and he's one of the most powerful. Who did you turn into vegetables anyway!?! The President or -"

    "Just Zach, Callum and-"

    "Your friends?"

    "They were only human!" Liam protested.

    "Perhaps that's what you all should be," said Puck maliciously. Liam's mother drops a book, and gasps. Puck hesitates and adds, "But then you couldn't change my friend back, could you?"

    "No, no that's very right, and after Liam changes his friends back, and you thaw out Merlin, we will all be the best of friends, I am sure," Liam's mother says rifling through her spell book.

    "I'm not sure I'd be friends with someone who cooks their friends?" Puck queried in response to the wizardess' offer.

    "Ah, I think this will work!" she said bringing the open book over to table. She closes the book with her finger in it marking the place. "Now Good Lord Goodfellow, I would ask your oath before Liam undoes his curse that you will thaw out Merlin unharmed, take these mortals with you, and leave me and my household uncursed."

    Puck chuckled. "If your offspring breaks his spell, and the humans are unharmed, then I will let your Merlin thaw and be as if he had not been blocked in ice, and no curse shall I place on you or your kin, but I shall not promise that no ill will befall you for your actions, as Merlin bespoke of your beadles enforcing the laws your kind lives by. And if one can curry a fairy's favor, do you not think that the others pay have patrons of their own?"

    "Liam?" his mother said angrily glaring at him.

    "What? How was I know to that my best friend hung out with fairies? I don't know if the others did or not. I mean, I thought they were just normal guys? I didn't expect any of this. Don't get mad at me. Hey, if the beadles really were going to do something, wouldn't they already be here?" Liam asked.

    "There's a protective fairy ring around the house. An atomic bomb couldn't faze it, let alone a few dozen wizards."

    "Then how did you get in, mother?"

    "I let her in, silly?" laughed Puck. "And lady, you best educate your whelp, or I may turn him into something for cat to play with?" he smiled coldly.

    "The spell, Liam, quickly, and don't mess this up!"
