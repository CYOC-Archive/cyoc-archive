She knew what she wanted to be: an American badger. She had read plenty of books about badgers when she was young, namely 'The Wind in the Willows'. Quarry was well-aware that the American badger was different from the European badger in some ways, being a little smaller, more carnivorous, and having different coloration. But Quarry wasn't about to be picky about it.

    Quarry wasn't particularly surprised that there were badgers who had been uplifted in the original outbreak, considering the American badger lived in many habitats in the western states. She was rather pleased that there was a single male living in the Burrows. His name was Orlando and apparently, he's done quite a lot of reading since he was uplifted. He volunteered at the local library, though he was probably going to become a full employee as soon as he was finished with his online college courses. Orlando particularly liked the Redwall series, something that Quarry can definitely agree with.

    Quarry was pretty impressed with the Burrows. It was a lot like having an apartment complex underground. Quarry didn't know a lot about geology, but the way they carved the apartments out of the living rock was really impressive. Of course, they've also reinforced the caves they've made in case their digging weakens the rock too much. Such a place deserves a lot more attention and Quarry was going to do something about it as soon as she's moved down here.

    When she found Orlando's apartment, she knocked on the door. Very soon after that, he answered. Quarry was impressed with what she saw. As a badger, Orlando was not towering like the polar bears she saw, but he was still taller than her. And he was also thickly-muscled and broad at the shoulders. The black and white markings on his face were not as even as a European badger but it was still unmistakable. "You must be Quarry," he said in a deep voice.

    "Uh, yeah," said Quarry, a little overwhelmed.

    "My name is Orlando. You may come in," he said, standing to one side. Stepping inside, Quarry could see the apartment looked very comfortable. Some of the furniture has been carved from the rock, such as the kitchen area's countertops, a table in the living room area, and several bookshelves that weren't completely full, but in the process of filling up. There were plenty of electrical lights since there were no windows, which wouldn't have looked outside anyways. "It might be a bit plain for your tastes," said Orlando, "Bachelor's den."

    "It's taking a while to build up," said Orlando, "But I like those books."

    "Well, thank you. Though I suppose we ought to get to the reason you're here," said Orlando.
