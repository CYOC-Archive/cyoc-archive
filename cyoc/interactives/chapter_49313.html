    daughters to bring them into the pack, she was sure she could figure out a way to do so,

    love them even more as pack mates, but she loved her Master most of all and he came first

    "What?" Jensen asked surprised by their tone.

    "We could have trouble." The twin echoed.

    "I left my motorcycle at the police station." Darla said.

    Fran continued, "And I told the chief that I was quitting for family issues, but I told my

    Jensen groaned, was the complications brought on by Kaylee's impulsive recruiting oft he

    Fran said, "The motorcycle might have trouble since we quite the police force and Darla

    now."

    Darla continued, "And if Fran's wife calls the police station and finds the inconsistency, she
    might dig deeper."

    Jensen looked at Kaylee and growled lowly again. Kaylee didn't try to smile, only trying to
    look as small as possible before her Master.

    Jensen rubbed his temples and thought, how to fix their particular problem.

    Fran said, "The good news is that my family 'knows' I'm 'leaving tomorrow Master. She'll
    likely stay up all night waiting for me and fall asleep and wake up the next morning. She
    won't make the call until then, and by then we should have my car disposed of right?
    There isn't an APB out for it so we can move about freely with it. And the military and FBI

    lucky Master."

    Jensen sighed, he was going to have to rely on Fran and Darla's police knowledge to make
    sure the car was never found in enough pieces to figure out how it belonged to, and like

    "And the motor cycle?" Jensen asked.

    "They'll of course call my apartment and maybe my cell phone when I don't come back for

    Jensen sighed. He just hoped his twins were right.

    "There is one more thing." The twins echoed.

    Jensen groaned.

    "The person who made the original call to the police that there loud and wild animals in
    the house." They spoke as one. "His name was Mitch Hurteng. he lives right next door
    Master."

    Jensen asked, "So he never saw us?"

    The twins shook their heads, "No. He only said he -heard-something-, he had no idea
    what."

    Jensen thought, until Kaylee's and his own parents were transformed, Fran was the only
    one of them who had the power of the game. And Jensen didn't know what complications
    could pop up and had hoped to keep Fran's turn in reserve in case things went to hooey
    fast.

    Jensen took out the phone book, there was the chance that Hurteng (who was regarded a
    little on the stuff and rigid side anyway by his neighbors) had caller ID, but if he did,
    hopefully Hurteng wouldn't realize it wasn't from the police. Though he need not have
    worried, Kaylee's family was very private about her phone number and had their number
    restricted.

    Jensen found Mitch Hurteng's number and handed the one to Fran who dialed up his
    number. She spoke in a calm, collected, professional voice using proper police verbal
    protocol when speaking to him.

    "Hello?" Mitch Hurteng's voice answered, the rest of the wolfens doing their best to stay
    quiet, and the Dare kept Kaylee's parents from screaming out 'Help!'

    "Hello, this is the police, we'd like to follow you up on a report you made earlier today..."

    Mitch had claimed he had not heard anything new, but did ask what the police had found
    out having seen their squad card drive to and from the house. Fran calmly informed him
    that he had heard two newly bought exotic dogs and the owners had been informed of the
    disturbance their pets had caused and would take proper steps in the future. Kaylee all in
    all was very glad that her parents and Mr. Hurteng had that tall hedge fence between their
    two houses.

    Giving Hurteng a number to call if he heard or learned of anything else, (which was really
    Fran's phone number, though both twins knew they'd have to cancel their cellphone
    subscriptions in the next couple of days to slice off another means they could be tracked
    by), Fran bid him fair well and hung up.

    Darla clapped, "You were fantastic sister!"

    Fran smiled back, "I was fantastic wasn't I sister?" The twins hugged each other their
    movements like two sides of a mirror.

    "You were able to help keep the pack secret without having to waste your turn at the
    game!"

    "Yes sister! We were able to hold onto my turn, as master said it's a singular use unless
    the curse is used on us again, so we must avoid using it when we don't have to." Hearing
    this, for the first time ever, Jensen was actually -glade- Kaylee had recruited the twins.

    They turned towards Jensen, "Isn't that right master?" They asked happily.

    "Yes, it is."

    Jensen marched over to Kaylee and began to gently stroke her fur, letting her know that all
    was forgiven. Kaylee drank in the feeling of her master and lord's paws upon her body, the
    flow of his pads against her fur a sweet sensation unto itself.

    Now Jensen just hoped any other neighbors near Kyle's house didn't bring them bad luck,
    wait...

    'How did I know where Kyle's house was?' Jensen wondered. High school senior Jennifer
    Smith hadn't had a clue who freshman Kyle Perkins was, only that he had said that Jennifer
    and her friends had pranked him last week: he hadn't been anything special when it
    happened, just another guy with no looks, no grace who existed only to be toyed with and
    taunted by girls of Jennifer's stock.

    'Well, the Big Bad Wolf always knows where their victim lives, the three little pigs, Red

    to show up and vivisect me or I'm going to get cooked alive after I try to break into
    someone's brick house by coming in through the chimney.' Jensen actually shuddered at
    the thought.

    "Are you okay Master Jensen?" Kaylee asked concerned when he had stopped petting her.

    "I'm just a little worried that now that the Big Bad Wolf that I'm gonna end up on the
    business end of a woodcutter's axe." Jensen confessed to his mate.

    Kaylee put her arms around his and leaned a little closer to him, "Don't worry Master, my
    Transform was that you only became the next fictional character you read, not that you'd
    be forced to act out what happens to them at the end."

    Jensen breathed a sigh of relief. "Thank you Kaylee."

    Kaylee smiled with her muzzle saying, "I live only for you Master."

    "We're done." Said Kaylee's parents in unison.

    "Drive the cars into the garbage so we don't have to go outside to be seen." Jensen said.

    "Actually," Kaylee said looking as sheepish as a wolf could rubbing the back on her head,
    "Our garbage is kinda full of junk spanning back three generations."

    Jensen sighed, 'Of course.' Jensen gave the couple the instructions for bringing themselves
    and the wolves to the estate, who nodded robotically thought Kaylee's father said in a low
    voice (as so only the wolves could hear it and so as not to get anyone else's attention at
    all), "Bastard, you killed my son."

    "He killed me first." Jensen said darkly.

    Kaylee's mother made a silent prayer as they marched to the cars, Fran telling them how
    the keys were in the pile of ripped genes in the back seat.

    Fran and Darla looked at each other and had a close hug, memorizing the feel of each
    other. True it was just for a little while, but they also hoped nothing went wrong that could
    keep their twin away from them.

    Kaylee and Jensen also hugged. "Now remember Kaylee, Fran still has the game. We ended
    up not having to waste it on the man next door, but DON'T do anything reckless! Only use
    the game along the way if we're in a situation we'll have to fight our way out of or will be
    exposed otherwise. THINK before you act."

    "Yes Master Jensen." Kaylee bowed.

    With that, the wolfens piled into the waiting cars, Darla and Jensen in Fran's Bronco piled
    in with Kaylee's father, who said nothing but kept his eyes on the road.

    Kaylee and Fran climbed into the Buick where Kaylee's mother awaited, "Well mom! Let's
    get going" Kaylee said excited. Kaylee laid down since she was too tall for the car roof like
    the others using the seat belt as best she could (as with the rest of the wolfen), she didn't
    want the car stopped for them not using their seat belts. She also remembered at the lady
    minute to use the little wolf lady's room.

    The two cars drove out of the driveway, Kaylee remembering to lock the front door at the
    very last minute, she gave the house she'd likely never see again one last look as the
    family and pack drove away, she had grown up in that house, as had her parents and
    grandparents. However, her master's family home would be much more practical for the
    pack to live in as it's size increased and she would never ask her master to short change
    his plans for her connivence.

    As the two cars drove across town, people at stop lights did give the wolfens looks, but
    no more than you'd give the bald man with tattoos on his head in the car next to you.
    Though some looks did linger in appearance at the she wolf's tail covered butts, no one
    was really overwhelmingly alarmed or freaked out as you would seeing a nudist riding
    along.

    Jensen gave Kaylee's father precise instructions to Jennifer's home, with the Buick
    following dutifully behind. Kaylee prattled on to her mother, wondering if her mom would
    become wolf cub prodigy like Jensen's parent would be soon enough or made into a fellow
    she-wolf to add to the master's females. Kaylee told her mother how she was okay really
    with either since both had perks. Her mother gripped the steering wheel ever so tightly.

    "Don't worry," Fran said to the woman, "It'll all be for the best you'll see."

    The woman didn't respond to the blond blue eyed she-wolf beast riding along with her.

    "You're likely thinking of some way to cause trouble once you've fulfilled your Dare by
    changed the masters parents, but I was a cop until this morning, so I know about
    suspects looking for any opportunity they can spot to get away so sorry, my sister and I
    will be watching you very carefully. And be right there when the Dare is done." Said both
    twins to their respective driver a few seconds apart.

    Both twins glanced at each other through the windshield and back windows of the cars
    like any sisters would. Jensen would be glade when the ride was over, this car was just way
    too small for his taste.

    It seemed that bad luck was taking an afternoon break as they two cars drove through the
    town without running into any meaningful trouble besides the tradition short traffic clog.
    While it was like an execution march, the couple was beginning to hope for an end to the
    trip too as the lingering smell of the wolves slowly filled this nostrils.

    Too long and too short a time later, the cars parked perfectly out of view and not having
    been trailed or followed, the wolfens literally climbed out, as the parents reluctantly did
    the same the Dare not allowing them to simply stay inside the vehicles nor did The Dare
    permit them to run away.

    Darla and Fran instantly shared a short hug as did Jensen and Kaylee. Jensen then hugged
    the twins who instantly and eagerly returned his embrace.

    "Welcome home ladies." Jensen said with a grin.

    All three lady wolfens loved the look of their new home and the massive spaces of plant
    life behind it.
