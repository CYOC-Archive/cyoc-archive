    Harry had put off this "meeting" with management for as long as he could, and now he was kicking himself for not making better use of the time this latest crisis had bought him. He had strongly considered sharing his findings with that skunk from the FBI, but he most certainly would have the entire park shut down completely, and he was still holding out hope it wouldn't come to that.

    "Hello there Harry." Was this the same manager he'd met with last time? It was embarrassing how hard a time he had telling 'anthro-apes' apart sometimes. "It has been brought to our attention that you've hired a private investigator to look into some bookkeeping issues within the company. That's a bit outside your jurisdiction, isn't it?"

    Well then. No point in trying to play dumb. "Not when it puts the safety and basic human rights of visitors at risk, no. Within the last week we've had four near-fatalities, three clearly the result of someone failing to handle a proper background check, the other from park-goers participating in a clearly dangerous violent activity we were specifically banned from stepping in on. Not to mention countless cases of harassment, and I'm sure the worst of it is still to come."

    "Well," the human said, with obvious sarcasm, "we feel that level of dedication is worthy of a promotion. Effective immediately, you are to be transfered to our new offsite research facility. I believe you'll actually be working with your former superior interestingly enough. I'm sure you two will have plenty to catch up on. And don't worry, we have a new head of security all set to take things over."

    From the tone, it was clear this 'transfer' wasn't optional, and at this point Harry didn't want to even think about what would be in store for him. Obviously the door he'd come through was locked, and he did not recognize these new huskies who were entering from behind the ape...

He was catching himself now and then fantasizing about meeting back up with her on the outside. A writer's life was rather lonely, and she was apparently even a fan. Having someone around the house to keep him company, and help with proofreading... he'd long slipped past the point when his publishers had the courage to push for edits, which no writer really wants. He remembered though, eventually, that Terry on the outside was presumably far far different than the woman he was currently snuggled up against. The only way he could keep this on the outside would be if they stayed a pair of rabbits... which, he realized to his surprise, he was not entirely opposed to. The nervousness wouldn't be much of an issue. He rarely left his home in the first place. He definitely earned enough to support someone else. Even if he couldn't, his house was far past roomy enough as it was. He could easily trade down to some cozy little out of the way cottage... Terry stirred in his arms. Of course, it would become so very hard to get any actual writing done.
