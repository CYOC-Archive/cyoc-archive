    Back to a Blue Blazer

    You flip back the covers, then stare in dismay at the yellowish damp spots on your briefs and the sheets. "Bollix."
    At least this is not new to you, even if it has been some years since you had an accident. You strip off the bedclothes and get out of your undershorts, and put the lot in a plastic bag to be washed later. The boy in the mirror is still a surprise when you go in to get cleaned up - twelve is way younger looking than 16. After a wash up and taking care of business - it almost felt like you would fall through the toilet seat - you get dressed for school.

    As soon as you open the closet, you groan again. Several neat blue blazers with gray slacks hang in row, with long-sleeve white shirts and some identical ties in your school colors. Secondary School. The first few years require students to wear uniforms. You'd forgotten about that. Juniors and Seniors could dress casual with permission from parents, which you had enjoyed a lot. Still, this was nothing you haven't done before. You get dressed and stare at your reflection. The uniform makes you look even younger, but you'll blend in with all the others. Actually, the more you look at yourself, the less you mind the uniform. It's almost as if you never stopped wearing it.

    Grabbing a bowl of cereal for breakfast, you get a quick kiss on the head from your Mum, who hands you the Nutella sandwich and an apple in brown bag. Then you start for your bicycle, only to stop and remember that you take the bus to Secondary School. There are a group of kids already waiting at the stop when you trudge over, and several greet you by name. A skinny ginger boy with freckles and big teeth grins. "Hi, Sam! You get the report done for Mr. Wilkerson?"

    Ronnie. He is your best friend since first grade, though you just now 'remembered' that. You know you have seen him around the neighborhood, but that was back when you were 16 and he was just some anonymous kid. Even as you approach, your mind is full of new memories - the two of you are practically inseparable, way closer even than you and your older brother. The report? Again, you 'remember' a class assignment to write a report on one of the Chronicles of Narnia books. Yes. You did it. "I wrote about Voyage of the Dawn Treader."

    Ronnie chuckles. "Hope we didn't write too much alike - I did that one too." He raises a clenched hand for a fist bump. "Go, Eustace!"

    You automatically bump fists and grin as well. The two of you had both decided that you liked the obnoxious cousin character from the story, and both felt he'd gotten cheated when Aslan changed him back to a human after he'd become a dragon.

    The bus arrives, and the two of you find seats. You are surprised how easy it is to fall into discussions about TV shows, super heroes, Narnia, and monsters and such. Were you this much of a geek when you were 12? Sports were all you thought about as a Junior, but while you still seemed to have an interest in soccer, it seems you and Ronnie share a much deeper love of fantasy stories and movies.

    More differences pop up as the school day progresses. You have a whole new group of friends, almost all boys, which is to be expected. But classes are a lot different. Math and science had been a struggle the first time, but you find that they are easy. You appear to be highly regarded by your teachers as a bright and capable student, though you also get some not-so-nice remarks from the 'jocks' about screwing up the curve for everyone, and being a show-off.

    By the end of the school day, it becomes pretty clear that this version of you is a lot smarter and more successful academically. On the flip side, you are not on any of the sports teams, and are part of the 'geek squad' - the boys you are friends with are mostly into computers, electronics, math, science, and playing fantasy games instead of athletics. Being unpopular with the 'cool' students bothers you a little, but University looks like a real opportunity. From what a few of your teachers have said, you might even graduate early!

    On the ride home, Ronnie reminds you that he is coming over later to play Warcrafters. You wonder if you want to continue this new life as a 12 year-old, or find the jellybeans and go back to your old self. Or maybe try something even more different?
