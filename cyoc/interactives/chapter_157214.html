    Exploring a Fantasy City

    added by Wild Fantasy 5 years ago A Mythological

    Once Raiel found a path through the enchanted forest, all he had to do was follow it to to a main road, and from there to a town. He saw signs on the path, but he couldn't read the signs at all. It was some weird language. Maybe once he tasted a human, he would automatically learn to read as an inherited skill from the race.

    Most people probably wouldn't take kindly to seeing a wild cat prowling around their town, so Raiel made himself into a harmless cute kitten before entering. Wow, that last transformation made him tired for some reason. He shook the feeling off. It had worked, and that was all that mattered.

    It was a large town. Maybe better described as a city. There were all kinds of humanoid races wondering around it it. Humans seemed to be the most common creature, but there were also elves, dwarves, halflings, gnomes, dragon-kin, half-orcs and more.

    Raiel explored and found taverns and inns. Stables filled with horses and other strange domesticated creatures. An adventurer's guild filled with a diverse cast of people from simple leather armor and cheep swords too magical war axes and enchanted armor. An illusive mage tower surrounded by illusions with robed figures of all ages going in and out carrying scrolls, books, staffs and wands. A somewhat hidden shady area filled with rogues, thieves and assassins, sharing secret whispers. A holy temple with people dressed in white welding divine light. Everything you'd expect from a fantasy world was here. Raiel was so excited. He wanted to try it all. Learn it all. But where to start?

    People of all races stopped to fawn over the cute kitten in the road. It was really embarrassing, but actually felt really good too to be pet and scratched behind the ear and all that. Licking their hands with his catlike sandpapery tongue gave him their taste as well. So many new forms to transform into. Yet he'd wait a bit before becoming anyone of them. He didn't want anyone knowing he was a shapeshifter. He didn't know their culture yet. For all he knew, shapeshifters were feared and attacked on the spot. Or maybe they'd never seen a creature like him before and would imprison him for some wizard's or alchemist's experiments.

    Raiel found an alcove somewhere out of the way where he could transform discreetly. He chose a form and changed. This transformation took even more out of him than the previous one. Like he was using up his reserve of transformations. He might need to rest before being able to do it again. He didn't think he could even turn back into the goo at this point. For the next little while he was stuck as this shape. He stretched and examined the new form he'd be stuck in for at least a day now.
