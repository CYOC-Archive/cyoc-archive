    Go out with Tony

    Huh, now this was interesting. It seems that in becoming a girl, your relationship with Tony has changed and he now has possible romantic feelings for you. After some thinking you decide that while you may have been a guy before (not that anyone knows that now save for yourself), friend-zoning Tony when he asks you on a date when you are now a girl might be a bit harsh. Who knows? Going on a date with your best friend as a girl might not be so bad, plus you could always turn back afterwards.

    “Sure I would love to go out with you.” You answer Tony in your now feminine voice. “When do you want to go out?” You ask on the phone.

    “Would this evening work for you with dinner after the movie?” Tony asked.

    “That shouldn’t be a problem; I have nothing planned for tonight.” You answer.

    “Great, I will be at your house to pick you up in a few hours, see you then Erica.” Tony said with enthusiasm.

    “Okay then bye Tony.” You answer and hang up.

    This evening then, which means you now have a couple of hours to get ready for your date with Tony. Technically you are still straight, but seeing as you are now a girl in body and to the world you might as well play the part until you decide to change back. You now have some time to kill before your date, so you decide to play some games on your computer. You start to get ready an hour before Tony arrives.

    You now feel a strange sense of urgency, your date is an hour away yet you have this urge to get ready earlier than you would normally. You surmise that turning yourself into a girl made more changes to you than just your body. You first check what clothes you have, opening the closet you find that along with your shirts, which are now more feminine, you now have some dresses and skirts.

    Before you decide on what to wear, you decide to clean up a bit in your bathroom. Upon entering, you find your bathroom feminized with women’s shampoos and conditioners along with some makeup which you now “know” how to apply. After your shower, which took longer than when you were a guy, and a relatively light application of makeup you go back to your closet to decide what to wear.

    It took you a while to find what to wear. After mulling it over a bit, you decide to give in to your new femininity a bit and choose a conservative knee length white dress with short sleeves to wear to your date. You also find a beige jacket to wear over top of your dress and for shoes you choose a pair of flats. After you put everything on you take a look at yourself in a full body mirror and you find that you dress up rather well as a girl.

    You spend several minutes admiring yourself in the mirror until you get a text on your phone. Tony has arrived to pick you up. You grab your handbag and make your way downstairs. Your little brother Aaron notices you.

    “You’re going on a date with Tony?” He asks.

    “Why yes I am.” You answer. “How did you guess?” You asked.

    “You wouldn’t be all dressed up otherwise, and he is the only boy you seem interested in.” He answers. “Have fun at your date.” He says.

    “I will.” You answer.

    You then head out the door and see Tony outside with his car ready to go. Tony was fairly handsome at about 6 ft tall and had black hair with blue eyes. He was wearing jeans and a polo shirt. As you come closer looking up to meet his eyes, you notice how much taller he seems to you since you went from 5’9” as a guy to 5’6” as a girl. You are still straight but you cannot help but notice that Tony looked rather attractive. You stop that line of thinking briefly and realize that becoming a girl means having to deal with female hormones. You then take notice that Tony’s eyes have been scanning you up and down.

    “Hey Erica, you’re looking good tonight.” He says which makes you blush a bit and feel strangely pleased. Damn female hormones.

    “Thanks, you too Tony.” You reply back shyly.

    “Well then, shall we go?” Tony says has he takes your hand into his, which was now bigger than yours. His hand feels strangely warm, but not exactly in a bad way.

    “Okay.” You answer as Tony guides you to the passenger side of his car and opens the door for you to get in. As Tony begins to drive, you have the feeling that this date might not be so bad after all.
