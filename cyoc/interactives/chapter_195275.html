    It took every ounce of Ty's formidable restraint to hold back from blurting out he wanted to experiment on Eileen. The idea of having a willing woman to change as he pleased and indulge in his innermost desires was utterly delightful. Somehow, he managed to hold it in, letting out a deep breath and reminding himself that his adult crush was showing him incredible understanding and patience in this moment. He didn't want to ruin it.

    Instead, he smiled wryly. He chuckled nervously. Eileen giggled brightly in response and nodded.

    "I d-don't really know!" Ty admitted, shrugging.

    "Goodness," Eileen laughed, lowering the gravitas at the table, "I suppose not! I don't know what I'd want to do with limitless power."

    Ty considered for a moment and finally brightened. "Maybe we should start small."

    "Yes, small!" Eileen nodded eagerly. "Just a little change. Just to see what happens. That seems perfectly fine."

    Something small. I wonder what you'd look like as a barbie doll, Ty smirked.

    FLASH! Eileen had vanished in a wink. Ty gasped in terror as he realized his mistake almost instantly. In horror, he leaned over the table and gasped.

    In her seat, just twelve inches tall, was a shocked Eileen. She had been reduced to a doll. She looked up at the table in disbelief. Her expression turned to first confusion and then changed to pale realization.

    "That..." Eileen squeaked, startled at her high pitched voice, "was not quite what I had in mind, darling.?"

    "Dear God!" Ty rushed from his seat to Eileen's side, staring down at the utterly tiny mom spread out in the seat of her huge chair. His hands fell over his mouth in horror and he gasped. "I'm so sorry, Eileen! I'm so so sorry! I'll change you back!"

    "Wait a second, now!" Eileen stood up slowly, almost laughing hysterically. She looked up and turned slowly in her chair, examining the room with utter mirth and astonishment. She realized something queer and burst out laughing, shaking a playful finger at Ty. "You... you did start small, didn't you?"

    "I guess I did." Ty looked down and realized Eileen was taking being shrunken in stride. "I'm sorry, I had... kind of a weird thought, and..."

    "Mmmm." The woman nodded, considering. "I see." She looked down at herself and began laughing again in fresh amusement.

    "Y-You're okay?"

    "Quite all right!" Eileen nodded, looking up to the table top and reaching up for it. "A little small, but no worse for wear."

    Ty watched in amazement as his now quite little crush hopped up and managed to grab the top of the table. With squirming and shaking legs, she managed to mount the table top, pull herself up ungracefully, and triumphantly stood on the edge of the table. She looked down with little wide eyes.

    "Quite a drop," she commented.

    "How are you okay with this?" Ty deadpanned.

    "Hmm?" Eileen looked up smiling, face bright with joy. "Well, it's not every day you become the size of a toy!"

    Ty giggled, Eileen giggled, and he watched her explore the table, stepping on the lace covering, the giant pitcher of lemonade standing taller than she did, measuring herself next to her glass. She laughed again and bounced excitedly. "How incredibly strange! I... I must be like Alice! Or... or... perhaps a doll that's come to life!"

    Ty leaned over the table excitedly, struggling to stop blushing, and smiled warmly as 12 inch Eileen approached slowly, eyes wide with wonder.

    "You're so big!" She whispered, reaching a hand forward gently. Ty extended his own and they touched palms. Eileen gasped in shock, seeing just how little her hand was against his.

    Ty gulped as the doll mom next door backed away a little, suddenly aware of just how helpless she was. She laughed nervously. "I-I'm... I'm quite a bit less than when we started, aren't I?"

    "Are you scared?" Ty quickly blinked. "Do you want to change back now?"

    "A little."

    "Well, I can do that easily. Um... we should set you down. You know, so... you don't... uh, wreck your kitchen table."

    "Oh!" Eileen realized, giggling nervously again, hand over her mouth. "Oh, I guess so! You're so bright, Ty!"

    Or, Ty blushed harder, I could... not change you back.
