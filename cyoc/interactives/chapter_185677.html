    Lee sits in silent, he watches Maddison reading, he then notices his sister's eyes starting to become wide and an surprised expression appears on her face. “What does it say, sis,” the boy asks.

    The six year old girl looks at her brother, “I don't understand what some of the words mean,” she says, “but apparently this thing is supposedly able to transform people, animals and objects.”

    Lee raises an eyebrow, “really,” he asks, “like what?”

    “Well, there are a few tabs available, some are locked and need to be unlocked. Some will unlock when you update this... thing,” Maddison says. She closes the manual and gasps, “hey, now our names are on the screen,” she says.

    “Maybe the thing scanned us or we were already in the system?”

    Lee knows the look on his sister's face, “let me guess,” he says, “you want to test it out on me?”

    Maddison nods, “I promise to undo the change,” she says, “don't tell me you aren't as curious as I am?”

    Lee thinks for a while, “I am very curious,” he says, “if this thing really can change people...,” he looks over at the door, which continues to rattle from Maxine banging on it, then looks back at his sister, a smirk on his face, “this weekend won't be so boring after all.”
