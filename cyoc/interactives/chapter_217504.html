    Masaru grins back, happy that you accepted his invitation, and the two of you walk over to his home that was just a couple of blocks away. As you walk together, you admire the tanuki's thick brown fur that shimmers in the moonlight, how perfectly the markings look on his face, and his body that looks like a nice furry pillow, all while he looks back at you.

    When you arrive at Masaru's house, you can't help but notice how different it is from the rest of the houses in the neighborhood. However, the house next door looks familiar, and you remember it as one of your friend’s houses. His home was as if it were a home in Japan that was copied and pasted into an American town. The front yard was a traditional Japanese garden, complete with a small pond and a few statues of animals. The house itself had a more Japanese-inspired look, with shoji screens and tatami mats visible from the outside. The inside of the house was just as impressive as the outside. There were Japanese art pieces displayed on the walls and several bonsai trees arranged on shelves. The two of you walk into the house and were greeted by his parents, who were both tanukis themselves. Masaru was talking to his parents in Japanese, as he introduced them to you, and they welcomed you in as if you were a longtime friend of his.

    As you head up to his room, you look around, admiring the decor. You notice a picture on his wall of Masaru standing next to a smiling badger, on what appears to be from his prom night. It is the same badger from your memories, the one that was supposed to be you. Your heart races as you realize that the badger in the picture was, and this tanuki was supposed to be your boyfriend you had back in high school but you split up for some reason you just couldn’t put a claw on.

    "Masaru, who's that in the picture?" you ask curiously, pointing to the photo.

    Masaru looks over and smiles. "Oh, that was my best friend from school. We've known each other since we were kids."

    Your heart skips a beat. "That looks a lot like me."

    Masaru's eyes widen in surprise. "No way, is that you?"

    "I had no idea, Benji. I'm so sorry we lost touch."

    You shake your head. "It's not your fault, mate. I don't even remember much from who I was before. All I know is that… that’s me in that picture."
