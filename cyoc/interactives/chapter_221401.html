    In early spring a train derailed right outside a small isolated town in middle America. The some of the contents of the train leaked out from the wreck and into the town’s water supply before the train company could come and clean up their mess. Most of what leaked were industrial oils and unrefined compounds that the company insisted were nontoxic and nonlethal. What the company didn’t tell the media was that one of the spilled train cars had belonged to a pharmaceutical company that was transporting proprietary and experimental medical materials. After a week of bad media about worries of tainted water and safety issues the governor of the state came to the town to calm the worries. She had even brought her young son with her to demonstrate the safety of the town’s water.

    Unknown to everyone was that while the town’s water filtration systems had been able clean out the industrial waste from the water it hadn’t removed the experimental virus that the pharmaceutical company had been transporting. The industrial waste had mutated the virus far beyond its initial purposes and had permeated the entire system. Before the Governor and her son left the town, both of them along with the rest of the town were infected with the virus.
