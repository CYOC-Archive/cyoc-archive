    Ohh...perhaps being truthful wasn't such a good idea

    added 13 years ago O

    "Truthfully?" Gabe said to you, "You already knew about us, even if
    you didn't have the proof. You told me what you'd found without a hint
    of a joke in your voice."

    He paused, slowly walking closer to you. "That's why I have to bite
    you, Stephanie...you can't tell anyone about us."

    You realize you're in quite a bit of danger. You realize you were
    foolish to tell him what you'd found. You're out here by
    yourself...you have nobody to run to, and it's hard to fathom how you
    could get out of this.

    "You're going to bite me?" you ask him, undeniably scared. "But...but
    what if I just destroy the casting?"

    He shook his head. "I can't trust you, Stephanie. We both know that
    even if you did destroy all the evidence on your computer as well..it
    wouldn't prevent you from telling other people that you were right. It
    wouldn't stop you from looking for more. You think you're the first
    human to discover us? We've been dealing with this sort of thing for
    eons."

    "Fuck..." you mutter.

    "Look, it's just a bite; I'm not going to kill you. You're going to
    love being a werewolf. You never get sick, you're faster, stronger,
    and have elevated senses, even in your human shape. You'll live until
    you're probably at least 100, and you'll look like you're in your late
    forties at THAT point. There's really no downside, Stephanie."

    You take a step back toward your front door. You're not sure you want
    him to get any closer than he already is to you.

    "Stephanie," Gabe says calmly, "This can go as easy or as hard as you
    want to make it. Believe me, I don't want to hurt you...."

    "But you want to bite me. Call me cynical, but that sounds like it
    would hurt." You keep backing up toward your door. It's only about a
    four foot dash to it. The cabin's pretty solid, but there's a lot of
    glass, large windows that he could easily leap through. You do have
    some mace inside, along with a taser. You begin to debate the merits
    of running inside quite seriously...something in there might work well
    enough as a deterrent to give you an escape route.

    "The bite will hurt," Gabe admitted. "I can't lie to you about
    that...but it's a better alternative than killing you, isn't it?
    Because that's your only other choice, Stephanie. I can't allow you to
    tell anyone else."

    What do you do now?
