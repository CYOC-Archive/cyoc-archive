    Isaac's decision

    added 2 years ago AR Toddler

    "I don't need someone else to help me. I can handle this mission myself," Sarah replied to Gerald a bit annoyed.

    "I'm sorry Sarah, but if you want to continue this mission, you have to choose an agent to work alongside you."

    "But I don't need another agent to help me with this. Maybe I'm a 2-year-old toddler now, but I'm still the same old agent. So don't treat me like a baby." Sarah responded aggressively to Gerald.

    Seeing the way Sarah responded to Gerald, Isaac knew instantly what was going to happen. So he quickly left the office and went to find something to calm this situation.

    "Sarah, if you are not willing to cooperate in this, I will be forced to withdraw you from this mission and I will have to call someone else to take your place. And you will have to stay in the agency's daycare until this mission is over."

    Before Sarah could throw a tantrum, Isaac had finally returned to the office, and next to him was Millie, one of the people who worked at the agency's daycare.

    "Sorry to interrupt this conversation, but I think little Sarah needs a little break right now. Millie, could you please take her?"

    "No problem. Come on little one, I'm going to take you to the daycare so you can take your nap, ok?" Millie told Sarah as she proceeded to carry her into her arms.

    "No, but Isaac, what are you ..." Before she could say anything else, Millie popped a pacifier into Sarah's mouth, which caused her to instinctively begin to suck it very calmly.

    When Millie had finally left the office along with Sarah, Gerald asked Isaac a little confused.

    "Hey Isaac, what's going on?"

    "I'm sorry about what just happened, but I think Sarah really needs a little break for now. Kids the same age as Sarah tend to throw a lot of tantrums when they're not given what they want."

    "Well, you're right about that. But I have never expected that kind of behavior from Sarah."

    "Yes but remember Sarah is now a 2 year old toddler. It is normal for her to act like this."

    "Yes, you're right. The truth is, I don't know what to do now. If I should let Sarah continue on this mission or if we better call someone else to take her place and have Sarah stay in the agency's daycare until the mission is finally over. What do you suggest, Isaac? "

    "Well, in my point of view, the best thing would be if ....."
