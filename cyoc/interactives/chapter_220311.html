As they entered, a couple guys at the bar glanced over at them and went back to their drinks. Karaoke had already started and a man in his mid-forties was belting out Bruce Springsteen's "Born to Run"...poorly. They decided to head to the bar and take two stools around the corner at the end which gave them a clear view of the rest of the bar and allowed them to turn and see the people singing without having to turn around 180 degrees.

    Katie added, "And I'll have a Midori sour."

    Danny looked at Joan. "Great, I'll need to see your ID."

    Joan shrugged his shoulders. "Maybe. I'll pace myself." He looked at Danny. "I don't think Danny recognized me at all."

    "No, definitely not. Why would he? Worst case, he might believe you're your own sister or something. See? I told you that you had nothing to worry about. Have fun! Enjoy seeing what life is like from the other side."

    Joan nodded her head toward the two guys halfway down the bar who looked at them when they walked in. "Speaking of 'the other side', those two guys over there won't stop looking at us." Joan tried not to make eye contact.

I Feel Like a Woman!". Katie turned in their slips to get in the singer queue.     "...and I'm Jason," spoke the other guy.

    "Hey guys, I'm Katie and this is my friend Joan."

    "So, you girls gonna sing tonight?" asked Jason.

    Katie nodded. "Yeah, that's why we came. Not going to spoil the surprise and tell you what we're singing though."

    "Oh, of course," said Stan with a chuckle. "I don't suppose either of you would want to do a duet with me?"

    Katie decided to be a little more tactful. "Yeah, I think we're going to stick with solo songs for now. Thanks though."

    "Well, can we at least get you a refill on your drinks?" asked Jason.

    Joan looked at her Long Island iced tea which she had hardly touched yet. "I already have a drink."

    "I'll get you another," offered Jason. "You'll need another eventually."

    Joan tried to object, but Jason was already trying to flag down Danny when Stan changed the topic. "So are you two from around here?"

    "Yes," replied Katie. "I'm actually here fairly often."

    "Yeah," Joan added. "She comes here with her boyfriend!"

    Stan nodded. "Oh. Cool. He's not here tonight though, is he?"

    Joan was staring daggers at Stan. "Yeah. He is." Joan really hoped Stan would take a hint. Katie glanced over at Joan and winked. Joan flashed a smile in return.

    Jason put his arm on the edge of the bar beside Joan. "How about you? Is your boyfriend here?"

It's been a pleasure to make your acquaintance. We look forward to hearing your songs." With that, he walked back to his seat across the bar and Stan followed.

    "I'm glad we bumped into each other after college and decided to catch up over dinner." Katie reached over and touched Joan's hand. "If you hadn't gone to the grocery store that day, who knows how things would have played out."

I Feel Like a Woman!" and started off somewhat reserved, but slowly got into it. By the end of the song, he was not only singing but doing a whole dance with lots of body movement. From the karaoke area, Joan could see Stan and Jason applauding as well. Joan returned to his seat.
