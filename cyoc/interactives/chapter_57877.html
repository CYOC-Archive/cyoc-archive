    "You wished to speak with me?" Martha asked as she looked down to Mal'akh.

    "Yes..." Mal'akh answered slowly, realizing that some of it was nostalgia. She picked up somethings from what Joy had told her that reminded her of the the members of the pack that she had interacted with.

    Martha remained silent.

    "I was speaking with Joy last night and I was trying to find out how she and her sister ended up mutating as they had..."

    "That was my fault," Martha said slowly, "I admit it... though at the time..."

    "Yes, I know," Mal'akh said quickly, "Joy explained that it was an accident. It largely made me curious..."

    "My condition has been genetic," Martha explained, "thankfully since my husbands and sister-wives' mutation was a result of the absorption of magic, my daughters do not have the mutated genes that I do... though not that I haven't overheard them whispering from time to time about wanting to be like myself and my mates..."

    "They want to be mutated?" Mal'akh gasped.

    "I guess they feel like they are left out... unlike their parents," Martha sighed, "they do not fully understand genetics and the problems that the Game has brought on Glendale."

    "Oh," Mal'akh said slowly, "I'm sorry..."

    "It is not your fault," Martha answered, "and even if it was, as an Omega, it is not my place to "punish you" outside of an order from Alphas Jensen or Kaylee."

    "You remind me a lot of a pair of Lupmen I knew in my time," Mal'akh commented, "a pair of silver twins. One was named Winifred... I'm sorry but I can not remember the name of the other twin..."

    "I would hope that is a complement," Martha answered.

    "It is nothing bad, I assure you," Mal'akh answered, "nothing bad."

    Martha then nodded, as another group slowly made their way into the room.

    "Good Morning, Kaylee," Fran said politely as she moved about with the day shift, double checking to make sure Julie's good luck totem was safe.

    Kaylee hugged her sister-wife.

    "Good morning again, Fran," Kaylee nodded, "Jensen will be busy with paperwork and a few meetings today. So, I will take the updates on the pack and the pride's status. Remember that we will likely need to keep Mother Glen informed on how her Pride is doing."

    "And the pups?" Fran looked down to Bart, Jenny, Kevin, and Mary, who stood between their mother and their aunt, with Kevin petting one of Fran's thighs.

    "Here to see their aunts and uncles," Kaylee nodded, "and will be on their BEST behavior."

    "Yes, mom," the four pups answered like little soldiers.

    And while their mother began to converse with Fran, Bart, Jenny, Kevin, and Marcy set about their mission to see what Martha was up to. They did pay their relatives by blood and law a brief hello and received a short pat on he head from each. Zack inquired about what Zelipe and Amy were up to. They told him that Zelipe was watching television with the other pups and Amy was "growing up", which left the charcoal black male puzzled, but Jensen and Kaylee's pups didn't know anything more then that.

    Once they had finished their "cover" mission, they made their way to the silver furred mutant she wolf at the other end of the medical wing.

    "Forgive me if this is rude, but do you know how your family line's mutation came about?" Mal'akh asked.

    "What brings about such curiosity in an Omega?"

    "Well... until I arrived here in this time, your pack had no mutant wolves," Mal'akh said slowly, "the only "mutant" I knew of was a lioness that served the witches named Twilight."

    "I remind you of a Leoman lioness?" Martha asked.

    "In some respects, the mutated form, the solid color eyes... though whether or not she had a third eye on each of her heads is a little fuzzy, but the solid color is definitely there. I think you even have her eye color," Mal'akh said slowly, "again, I'm sorry if this seems rude."

    "It is alright," Martha commented, "I suppose nostalgia will do that. From what I've heard this is a whole new world for you."

    Mal'akh nodded.

    "What are you up to Miss Martha?" came a small voice from behind the silver Lupman making both Martha and Mal'akh look in that direction.

    They turned to see that it was Bart, Jenny, Kevin, Marcy looking up at her and Mal'akh, and looking very curious.

    "I thought I'd left you with the others," Martha answered.

    "We wondered what you were up to," Marcy spoke, "that you might leave..."

    "I would never leave," Martha told the pups, collecting the youngest girl in a hug and holding her up.

    "And who are these four?" Mal'akh asked, noticing the males' resemblance to Francis, right down to the violet colored eyes. Only one was black.

    "These are Alpha Jensen and Alpha Kaylee's pups," Martha spoke, "the oldest male is Bartholomew, but prefers Bart. The oldest female... and the only one of Alpha Jensen's children to have his fur color... is Jenny, the younger male is Kevin, and this is Marcy... and they should all be where I left them."

    "It's alright," Mal'akh answered, "their faces jog a few memories that I have as well."

    "Good memories?" Marcy asked from Martha's arms.

    Not wanting to frighten a child with tales of the witches, Mal'akh nodded.

    "Your brothers resemble a male pup that I knew back in my time," Mal'akh told Marcy.

    "Isn't it now your time?" Marcy asked, not knowing that her wording had a different meaning then what she meant.

    "Ms. Mal'akh is one of the Pride that the game had caught and Ms. Glen freed her," Martha explained.

    "I'm sorry," Marcy said almost without thinking.

    "It is good that she freed you then," Jenny stepped forward.

    "Yes," Mal'akh answered, "I will owe Penny Glen quite a lot when I heal up."

    "I can check with the Pack's rosters if you are looking for the fate of friends you might have had among the pack," Martha offered.

    "Not right now," Mal'akh sighed, "some of this might have been nostalgia on my part. I didn't mean to take you away from your duties."
