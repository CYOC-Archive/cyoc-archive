    Josh the Motorcycle

    added by Anonymous A year ago BM I Vehicle Robot

    After the race, Andrew was cooling down when a man approached him. He looked to be in his late twenties, with a medium build. His eyes were hidden behind a pair of square-rimmed glasses and his hair was a messy mop of brown curls. His skin was pale, probably from spending most of his time indoors, working with computers and machines.

    "Andrew?" The man asked, his voice carrying a nervous note.

    "That's me," Andrew replied, looking at the man curiously. He recognized him as Josh, one of the mechanics who always seemed to be hunched over some gadget or another.

    "May I talk to you in private for a moment?" Josh asked, his gaze never leaving Andrew's.

    "Sure," Andrew agreed, leading Josh to his trailer.

    Once inside the privacy of the trailer, Josh seemed to gather his wits about him. He took a deep breath before speaking up.

    "I have something... odd to confess," he began, looking at Andrew hesitantly.

    Andrew nodded, encouraging him to go on.

    "I've always been fascinated by robots," Josh said. "In fact, you could say it's sort of my... fetish."

    Andrew was taken aback for a moment but quickly regained composure. "You're interested in becoming like me?" he asked, pointing at himself.

    Josh nodded earnestly. "I want to know more about you and how it feels like when transforming into a machine."

    Andrew considered this for a moment before replying. "I understand your fascination," he said slowly. "And I think there's something that may help."

    His digital memory supplied him with information about a two-player mode that existed in the game - another watch that could trigger transformations independently. Remembering where he had kept it safe, Andrew opened one of the drawers in his trailer and pulled out another watch, still in its original packaging.

    He handed it over to Josh who held it with reverence. But before Josh put it on, he had one more request.

    "Can I see you transform back into your human form? The one with the leather jacket and sky-blue jeans?" Josh asked timidly.

    Andrew smiled at this request. "Alright," he said agreeably and activated the reverse transformation on his watch.

    Like moves in a choreographed dance, first his helmet retracted itself into the collar around his neck; His face slowly morphed back from the rubbery mask to its original warm fleshiness; His hair returned back from its metallic sheen to normal brunette locks; His mechanical body began losing its metallic armored look as it turned back into flesh and bones;

    His chest hardened into well-toned muscles under his black leather jacket; His arms transformed from mechanized muscular limbs into human arms, clad in leather sleeves; His metallic legs morphed back into human legs wrapped in sky-blue jeans; And finally, as if bowing out after an elaborate performance, the boots softened out into casual sneakers.

    After a few minutes of intricate transformation scenes playing out right before Josh's eyes, Andrew stood there in all his human glory - looking every bit like an average man wearing a leather jacket and sky-blue jeans.

    Josh's hands trembled with anticipation as he watched Andrew transform back into his human form. He slowly took the watch out of its packaging, the plastic crinkling in his hands. His heart pounded in his chest as he put the watch on, booting it up. It was a simple interface, and he easily found the "TRANSFORMATION" option.

    He paused for a moment, taking a deep breath, then tapped on the icon. The transformation began almost immediately. His body tensed up as he felt a tingling sensation spread from the wristwatch through his body. His hands and feet started changing first, morphing into large, sturdy wheels.

    His body started to reshape itself, his chest and abdomen stretching out and hardening, forming the sleek body of a motorcycle. His arms and legs fused together, forming the frame and the suspension system that held the wheels.

    Suddenly, he felt a strange sensation in his torso as his internal organs began to transform. He could feel his heart morphing into an engine, his lungs turning into an air intake and exhaust system, his stomach into a fuel tank. His senses were overwhelmed with new information as he could now feel the ground beneath his wheels and the wind flowing over his bodywork.

    He panicked as he started leaning to one side, his wheels not able to provide balance to his upright form. Just as he was about to tip over, Andrew sprang into action, holding him upright. Josh could feel the kickstand forming underneath him, providing the much-needed stability.

    With his physical transformation complete, it was now time for his mind to change. He could feel his consciousness slipping away as his brain began to transform into a computer. It was like falling asleep, one moment he was aware, and the next, he blacked out.

    He didn't know how long he was out, but when he came back online, he felt completely different. His senses slowly returned, one by one, giving him a new perspective of the world. His vision was replaced by multiple sensors providing real-time data about his surroundings. His hearing was replaced by a radio scanner, picking up every frequency in the vicinity.

    Josh was now a Superbike. His body was sleek and aerodynamic, covered in glossy paint that shimmered in the sunlight. His engine was a marvel of engineering, capable of producing immense power. His wheels were large and sturdy, ready to tackle any terrain. He had a built-in computer with a screen on his dashboard that could display text, allowing him to communicate.

    Despite the drastic change, he was at peace. He displayed a message on his screen for Andrew to read: "I'm fine. I like it." Andrew was a bit embarrassed, having assumed that Josh would transform into a humanoid robot like him, but he was relieved to see that Josh was okay with his new form.

    As Andrew surveyed Josh's sleek, motorbike form, he realized the newfound issue. "Well, Josh," Andrew said, taking a few steps back to fully appreciate the motorcycle's impressive size, "I think you might be a little too big to fit through the door."

    He took a seat on Josh's saddle, his hand gliding over the smooth metallic surfaces of the transformed mechanic. A playful smirk tugged on his lips as he teased, "Comfortable seat you've got here."

    A message popped up on Josh's screen, stating with a hint of humor, "Aww, I was looking forward to you riding me."

    Both Andrew and Josh shared a digital chuckle, the laughter echoing lightly within the trailer. But the mirth soon faded, replaced by a wave of panic from Josh.

    "I can't turn back," Josh's screen displayed, the short sentence carrying a wave of worry. A spike of adrenaline shot through his circuits. This was new, unexpected, and terrifyingly exciting.

    Andrew was quick to react, flicking through the options on Josh's computer. Sure enough, he found the option to revert the transformation. "I could transform you back," Andrew drawled, stretching out lazily, "But I'm feeling a bit tired." He yawned for added effect, a wicked glint in his eye as he continued his teasing.

    Josh’s screen blinked quickly, displaying a desperate message, "Please, Andrew!"

    Finally, Andrew ended his teasing and hit the button to revert Josh's transformation. In a sequence that felt like a rewind of his previous transformation, Josh's body began to morph back to its human self.

    His engine shrank back into a heart, his sensors turned back into eyes, ears, and skin. The metallic body of the motorcycle softened and reshaped, turning back into human flesh and clothes. His wheels receded, turning back into hands and feet. Finally, his computerized brain transformed back into a human brain, and Josh was back in his human form, panting and sweating but safe and sound.

    Andrew clapped Josh lightly on the shoulder, chuckling. "Quite an adventure, huh?"

    Josh could only nod, still panting heavily. But there was a glint in his eye that spoke of something more - a wild thrill of having lived his most profound fantasy.
