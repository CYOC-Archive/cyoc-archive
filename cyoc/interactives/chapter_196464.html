    added A year ago AP

    You look at the sketch of "Peyton Daniels", a fictional nursing home patient who could possibly become real thanks to the nanites. You have to admit you are kind of curious about being an old person. However, your first concern is your safety. The cartel's hitmen wouldn't expect you to turn yourself into some who was both old and helpless, would they? Everyone wants to be stronger than they are and stay young forever, right? You decide to speak up.

    "Um, some of these identities are much younger than I am, so I assume the nanites can reverse aging. If I chose to make myself a lot older, I wouldn't have to stay that way forever, would I?" You don't exactly want to skip middle age and sacrifice most of your life expectancy prematurely, even to escape El Zapato.

    "Not unless you want to," the agent answers matter-of-factly. "You will be changed back to your current age and appearance in order to testify at the trial. After that, you can choose the same identity or a new identity, and we will revisit you periodically in case you change your mind."

    "How easy do you think it will be for the cartel to find me if I go into a nursing home?"

    "Not easy at all. First, we would choose a nursing home that our agency has top-secret connections with. No one there will no who you really are, or which patients are in witness protection, but the leadership will be aware that security is a high priority for their facility. Not just anyone can visit the patients. Most importantly, you will have a radically different name and appearance from your current self. It would be very hard for anyone outside our bureau (or even within our bureau) to identify both the right nursing home and the right patient, and they would need convincing fake identities of their own just to get inside."

    "That's exactly what I wanted to hear," you say to her. "This is the identity I want to live as until the trial."

    You hand her the description of Peyton Daniels. She reads over it and smiles.

    "Somehow I thought this might be your choice. Most people wouldn't even consider this option, but you're different somehow. Please step behind this curtain and strip. A wheel chair is already provided for you because you won't be able to walk for much longer. I'll program the nanites, and a nurse will be with you shortly in order to perform the injection."

    You step in and take off your clothes. You hear beeping from the other side of the curtain. Then you hear talking; someone else has entered the room. The agent and someone else seem to be talking in code. You can't really understand much of what they are saying, but you assume it's about you. Then someone in a healthcare outfit comes in, performs a quick examination, and gives you the injection. Only the nurse is going to be behind the curtain with you while this happens. It's a very private procedure, but someone has to observe you for medical purposes in case something goes wrong. You hold your breath, but the nurse advises you to take deep breaths as the transformation begins.
