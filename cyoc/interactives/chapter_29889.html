    "What? But I don't want to make a new character, or learn a new system," John complained. "It'll take up half the night."

    "Don't worry, you'll learn this very quickly. And I'll do most of the system stuff for you anyway. As for characters, just think of a concept, and I'll help you all build them up."

    "The game will be set in my setting, as usual. You'll start off in the city of Khandri, gathered together by the Guild Master of the local Thieves Guild, Harneth, to do some work for him. So no extremely lawful characters. And I'd appreciate it if you didn't make backstabbing evil guys either. Or joke characters," As he said that, he looked at John.

    "Since you're going to work for the Guild, your characters will either be members or owe Harneth a big favour. You can make some pretty powerful characters, but don't go over the top. Best swordsman or thief or whatever in the area is allright, in the entire country is too much."

    "So, any ideas?"

    Christine was the first to answer.

    "I want to be a young elven ranger. A great shot with the bow, and pretty good with my sword as well. She's a little naive about civilised people, and fit better in with the wild animals and the tribes. I'll name her...Ialina. She's here because Harneth saved her from the gallows of the corrupt government."

    "Okay, that sounds good. Anyone else? John?"

    "I'm a noble's son, Dargan. Charismatic, well-schooled and somewhat haughty, with a talent for magic. I probably also know how to use a sword pretty well, as my fathers men-at-arms taught me this when I was young. I have a debt, I mean, like, money, because I've gambled too much."

    "I think I'll be the party thief," Brian said. "A female Wild One Grey Elf." Grey elves were a race Ty had invented for his setting. As the name suggested, their skin was a bluish-grey tone, and they lived in a rigid and well-ordered society. Wild Ones were the ones that didn't fit in, touched by Luck and Chaos, and were outcasts because of it.

    "Nimble and charismatic, Ichalia's one of the Guilds top thieves, and have been recruited to this mission because of that."

    "You're playing a girl?" John shot in, sounding almost disgusted.

    "Yeah, I thought I'd try it for once. Why not?"

    "If he's going to be a girl, I want to play a guy," Julia chimed in.

    Julia was Brians twin sister. The two would always do something like this.

    "I'll be a Wild One, too! In fact, I think I'll be her older brother. He's a fighter-rogue, stylish and maybe a bit crazy. He's a member of the Guild, too, and lives as a Highwayman in the forests around Khandri."

    Brian looked over at her, and for a moment they feared that an argument would break out. But Brian smiled.

    "Hey, that's actually quite cool, sis."

    Only Tim remained. He often tended to be the leader of the group, and today was no exception.

    "I'll be a Guild Enforcer, one of the high-ups, trusted by Harneth. Someone needs to keep an eye on the hired swords, not to mention keeping the Wild Ones in check. With their impulse control, no matter how good they are, they tend to make trouble."

    "Allright, guys, then we'll begin." Ty concentrated for a moment, and willed his friends, or rather, his friends characters, into the world he had created.
