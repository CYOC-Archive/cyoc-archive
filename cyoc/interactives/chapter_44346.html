    After digging around in the bag for a few seconds, Kurt pulled out an old talisman. Juna remarked, "Wolf paw?" and the boy nodded. "My family history is that of wolves. My family has always been part of the wolf pack for several generations." He blushed, showing it out to the other three.

    It was made of some kind of brass or bronze, the steel bent to form a wolf's paw. Covering the talisman was wolfs fur, real wolfs fur, unlike Juna's cheap mask. The shark claws also stuck out of each individual digit on the talisman. Cassie replied, "Good. Hopefully you'll learn a thing or two about your clansmen while you're here." Kurt nodded silently, moving to the empty bunk-bed. "Am I top or..." Cassie cut him off quickly "bottom."

    Juna and Rose unpacked quickly, both had brought very little. Clothes and food are provided by the school, as well as reading material and other essentials. Rose only brought along a few books that are her personal favorites; she was reading one of them on the train-ride over before encountering Juna. Juna herself only brought a few extra clothes, and a teddy bear she affectionately called Bruno. Cassie it seemed only had spare uniforms in her closet, nothing to remind her of home or comfort.

    Kurt however brought an excess of everything. In his back he brought several sets of clothing, books, family pictures, extra bedsheets and even a video game console... he sighed. "No television?" He merely left it in his book bag and stashed it back under his bed. Cassie looked down from the top bunk at the boy, "You'll come to live without it here. Nature is the ultimate form of escape, not TV and video games." Kurt shrugged, and looked over at his other two dorm-mates.

    Both Juna and Rose had finished unpacking, and were merely talking back and forth about one of Rose's books. Juna wasn't an avid reader, but she had always made an attempt to read at least some books to stay familiar with what was popular. Cassie hopped off the bed and stood at the door. Around her neck she sported a metal whistle alongside her pendant. She blew into it hard and yelled. "ALRIGHT! IN LINE!" Juna and Rose hopped up Juna taking the front and Rose falling in behind. Kurt was still fumbling inside his backpack, checking every pocket. "IN LINE STUDENT Number 2848!" Cassie hated using the numerical assigning, but it would do better to make him mad than to give him leeway. She didn't need to get the other two cadets in the mood to be lackadaisic as well. Kurt stumbled and fell in line behind Rose, however awkwardly close.
