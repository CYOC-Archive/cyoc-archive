    Harpinder and the Snake Demon Woman

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added by dragontransformation 3 months ago A I O

    So Harpinder and Mohan took a stroll along the river
    and noticed the excitement from gathering crowds as workman
    and divers pulled a big slimy and dripping wet big metal
    box out of the river muck.
    It was a centuries old bronze coal fired oven and was in
    amazingly good shape considering how long it must have lain
    on the bottom of the river.
    Harpinder knew that something about this ancient oven
    struck him as odd,something about it that was jogging a
    lesson from Cultural Studies out of his memory.

    Ah yes,a book report he had done on a book titled
    Tales From the Punjab (1916),a book of childrens folktales
    from the Punjab.
    While Harpinder tried to dredge out of his memory the
    connection between that book and this bronze oven Mohan
    mentioned to him that if you looked at the ancient relic at a
    certain angle you could see the etched images of writhing serpents encircling the sides of the oven.
    Ah,now Harpinder remembered the relevant folktale
    (Somebody) and the Snake-Woman
    In the folktale (Somebody marries a mysterious beautiful woman.She is reluctant to tell her new husband where she
    comes from but seems a devoted enough wife.Except at night
    when he wakes up in bed and his wife isn't there at his side.
    He keeps quiet but one night pretends to be sleeping and when his wife silently slips out of bed he quietly gets up and tiptoes
    after her.She walks down the hallway,seemingly getting smaller
    and vanishing from his sight.He then rushes to the nearby
    window and observes the exit from that hallway.A long
    serpent is slithering out of the hallway and into the foliage
    surrounding their house.
    He is horrified.
    The next day he feigns a excuse to go on a trip and heads
    for the nearest wise man.The wiseman confirms that
    (Somebody) had married a demon snake woman and
    advises (Somebody) to trick his new wife to enter a oven,then
    lock the oven door,turn on the coals and ignore her
    protests and banging till all is quiet.The wiseman tells
    (Somebody) he will find in the oven a lump of gold,the heart
    of a demon snake woman,and to toss the cursed thing into the
    river.Which is exactly what (Somebody) did when he returned
    to his house.End of Marriage to a demon snake woman.

    Well it looked like the excited crowd,workmen and divers
    clustered around the ancient oven had similar ideas about it
    containing gold and Harpinder and Mohan watched as the
    workers tried to force open the ovens door,all to no avail.
    After a hour of this the two guys got bored and went home again.
    It was as Harpinder was emptying the coins from his pockets
    in his bedroom that he heard the loud "CLUNKKKK" as a shiny
    yellow object fell to the floor in front of him.

    As he looked on in mostly astonishment a mist writhed around
    the lump of gold and began to take on a form.
    Within a minute there crouched on the floor was a naked
    oriental woman,She just lay there a bit stunned as she got her
    bearings.
    You don't get tricked into entering a oven by your husband,get heated up good and then spend centuries at the bottom of a
    river and come out with everything firing on all cylinders....
