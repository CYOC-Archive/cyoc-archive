    Everyone arrived at the Parallel World Travel Agency with their character sheets and back stories. Matt was finalizing the plans with the travel agent, who needed to make sure the world they were going to would fit all of their characters exactly, and that had the group together. The agent assured them that in a multiverse of infinite possibilities, they would find it. It just would take a little bit.

    Dave shook his head. "I can't believe you are turning into a girl man. You sure about this?"

    Nick shrugged. "The best part about becoming our characters is that we can experience something new. What's the point of that if we don't experiment a bit."

    "That's the spirit! " Emily held up her hand up. "Gender benders unite!" Nick smiled as he gave her a high five. "Besides he isn't just turning into a girl, but a tiefling. Being something other than human is going to be cool."

    "That I get." David agreed. "Its why I picked being an elven Paladin. I also get Nick being a tiefling. The crap in game Lore gives them sucks, but being an underdog can be fun in roll play. Less fun when you have to experience for real though. Especially for you Emily. Being a Gnoll won't be easy."

    Emily smiled. "Hey, being a well hung hyena man will have issues but as long as I get to experience all of the perks as well I'm game." She winked at Nick. "I can help you experience some of the perks of being a woman as well."

    Nick rolled his eyes. Emily could be flirtatious when playing a character but he knew she was never interested in doing anything for real. He doubted the offer was series.

    Jill, who was short, flat, and somewhat plain was playing as a human rogue. "Well I think you overlooked one thing. You are trying to jump in with both feet first but that will make future trips less special. Personally I'm just starting with a body I wish I had. I'm going to warm up to the more exotic choices."

    Emily looked at her. "Which is why you stuck with both human and female? No interesting changes?"

    I wouldn't say 6 feet tall, double D breast aren't interesting. Besides, as Nick said I did do some experimenting. Instead of being straight, I wrote my character as bisexual."

    David looked at her. "Can it work like that? Just change our sexualities by picking our new bodies?"

    "I'm about to find out." Jill said.

    Matt returned at that moment. "We are ready to go, but we are entering the new world one at a time. I'm going first. See you on the other side." With that he went through a door.

    The agent waited a bit before calling out a name. "Elyon, the elven Paladin, you are next."

    With that David left. Next the agent called out another name. "Lydia the human rogue."

    Jill stood up. "Hey Nick, we should have a girl's night once on the otherside." She looked at Emily. "I'd invite you Em, but you're about to loose the qualifications to join us."

    Emily smiled. "That's fine, I understand. Show Nick a good time and maybe he can convince the others to do an all girl team next time."

    Jill smiled. "I love the sound of that. This group sometimes has too much testosterone." Jill went through the door the others had entered.

    Finally the agent called the next party member. Rlaws the Gnoll Fighter. "

    Emily stood up. "Speaking of testosterone, I'm going to go get mine now. I'll leave my estrogen here. Feel free to use it." She went through the doors leaving Nick with the knowledge that the cute girl was about to be a monstrous man.

    Finally it was his turn. "Julia the tiefling cleric." Nick stood up and braced himself. He had made a character very different from himself. He was about to go from 5'10 to 5'5, with a breast size of 34DD. Julia had long golden hair, fiery red skin, and a tail that ended with a tuff of hair similar to a lions.

    While he didn't say anything like Jill, he too was going from straight to bi. He wasn't sure how the story would go but he want to keep his options open.

Seeing the red skin was odd, as well as feeling a tail swish behind her. Speaking of the her part, while it was strange to have a new appendage, Nick couldn't help but feel another appendage in the front was missing.
