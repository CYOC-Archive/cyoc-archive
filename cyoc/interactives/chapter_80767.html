    Emily the goat girl's summer.

    added 10 years ago A O

    "I think that's all for today," Emily replied.

    "Good," the woman said, "now if you follow me, we can settle up the payments at the counter."

    The two quickly went to the counter where Emily payed for her new necklace, which was only about $60, and
    thanked the proprietor for the necklace, and her new goat form.

    About two weeks later, Emily arrived at her Cousin Carrie's farm where she worked in the summer. During dinner
    on the first day Emily showed Carrie and her husband her new goat girl form, which freaked out them out at
    first, but was able to explain things before it got out of hand, and agreed to keep this to themselves.

    Emily's time in the summer went by without much incident. While Emily spent some time with other animals in
    her human form, she spent most of her days working with the goats in her goat girl form. As expected she
    enjoyed working with the goats a lot more than she did before she got her necklace. While in her goat form she
    wore a little less clothing, wearing only a skirt and a tanktop (plus undergarments). Since all she could do
    was bleat while in her goat form she was able to talk to the goats that she took care of; a lot of them seemed
    nice, but since their minds aren't as complex as a human's, conversations were not common and didn't last long
    either, but Emily didn't mind since her mind wasn't as complex either at the time. Once in a while she would
    have a snack while working, and her primary food while in her goat form was the grass around her, which seemed
    to be her favorite food while in that form due to her new anatomy. Sometime at night, Emily would go outside
    in her goat form, wearing nothing but the soft white fur on her body, and sleep with the goats under the
    starry night, only to return to the house in the morning before returning to her human form.

    Emily felt a little sad when it was time to go home at the end of summer, but she had to continue studies at
    college, and she can't drop out with her junior year coming up, all she could do was hope that she could come
    by the farm next summer, which is what she thought as she drove off the farm and returned home to the city.
