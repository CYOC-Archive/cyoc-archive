    Ty's Power

    A splitting image

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added 12 years ago BM

    T.J. had mixed memories. All of his memories were merged versions of Ty and Jeremy's. Ty scrapped his knee when he was 6 years old but at that same time Jeremy fell off his bike. The resulting memory turned out to be T.J. had scrapped his knee while falling off his bike.

    "Well, I feel lonely." T.J. said, talking to himself. He thought about what he would do with his power. He found a way to solve his loneliness problems. Suddenly, his face started to split in two, branching outwards. First, his nose started to split and he had two noses then his eyes grew farther apart. An eye formed in the middle of his splitting head and he had temporary triple vision. His mouth started to grow and eventually, grew skin separating them. His neck split and carried the extra head he grew with it. He now appeared like he had a conjoined twin.

    "Hey, T.J. I guess I'm T.J also, then?" His new head spoke.

    "Sure. Why not?" T.J. said.

    "Do you think three heads are in order?" His right head spoke.

    "Why?" The left head said, questionably.

    "Well, we were made from two boys so them plus you equals 3." The right head said.

    "Okay. I make some sense. We'll all be identical though." The left head said.

    "Of course." The right said. Suddenly, his heads started to grow farther apart and when their necks touched they started fusing back together.

    "Dude, what are you doing?" The left head said.

    "I'm not doing this." The right said. His heads continued to fuse back into a single one but then they started to split again, except a face grew from each side of the single head. Soon enough, he had three identical heads sitting on slightly widened shoulders.

    "Hey, T.J." He said with all three of his heads. He realized, despite of him having three heads, he still had one mind. He did develop some what of a triple personality disorder.

    "I think I developed a triple personality disorder." He said, yet again in unison with his three heads, still not used to the feeling.
