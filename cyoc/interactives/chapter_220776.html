    Enoch forms a wolf pack

    Enoch couldn’t believe what he heard from the angel with blue wings and sapphire like eyes. This former human who was once an Iron Knight needed his help in formulating and executing a jail break; in the heart of the Inquisitorial headquarters no less. If Enoch were still a demon, he would normally jumped at the opportunity to do this; but he was no longer the black furred wolftaur demon with red skin torso and yellow eyes with snake like pupils. He was now an angel. An angelic wolftaur with tanned skin, white furred lower wolf half with dove like wings folded along his sides. His eyes solid blue and crackling with power.

    “The last time I tried to break some out of jail or prison was back in 1943,” Enoch being serious; “it did not end well and the person who I was trying to rescue ended up getting gassed to death. It was in Auschwitz,” Enoch adding; “I’m certain some of you are well aware of the crazy shit going on around that time in Europe.”
    Everyone next to Tarfeil was silent; even the Cherub that Enoch rescued the other day look sad, it somehow knew that Enoch was referring to someone he loved.
    “No one wants to see anyone die,” Inari; the kitsune angel speaking. “But Azure is innocent and doesn’t deserve to be locked away without evidence to back her supposed guilt. You rescued her along with Tarfeil here,” Inari motioning to the green skinned angel with wings looking like tree branches in full bloom. “Help us free her, please.”

    Enoch took a moment to think before getting up; standing on his four legs as he stares at everyone.
    “Can I have some time to think,” he asks; “alone?”
    “I suppose,” Inari sighing; “whatever answer you provide, we’ll accept it.”
    Enoch nods as he leaves the room that Tarfeil is in and steps into the hall; he sighs as the cherub quickly follows and lands on his shoulder.
    “No matter what I do,” Enoch looking at the bird; “you are going to report to Jehovah regardless right?”
    The cherub nods as Enoch sighs and heads to an adjacent room; seeing that it is empty he heads inside and sits down on the floor. Closing his eyes he clears his head to think.

    ********************************

    “Your mind is troubled,” a voice coming through the blackness as Enoch enters into a meditative state; a technique he learned during his many travels on earth.
    “I was hoping you would show up,” Enoch opening his eyes to see he is sitting in the Shir dessert; the exact place he became an angel as he rescued those the Heka and Qesem turned into chaos thralls; “but are you going to answer my questions or keep giving me riddles?”
    “Ask your questions,” the voice speaking; Enoch seeing a large amorphous being of light approaching him.
    “Am I slated to face Tiamat?”
    “Eventually,” the being of light speaking; “but you need time to prepare first.”
    “Will I be able to help my family currently fighting a civil war in hell?”
    “That is up to you. You do have free will and access to untapped power.”
    “These powers I have been granted,” Enoch sighing; “I don’t know where to begin in using them effectively.”
    “That is why you are here,” The light speaking; “to learn.”
    “I sense chaos here in heaven,” Enoch looking at the light before him,” but I have no clue as to who is manipulating it. I am certain that Beelzebub is alive, but I suspect he is not working alone.”
    “Then ask for help,” the light responding; “you spent all your time as a lone wolf, perhaps it’s time to start running with a pack.”

    *************************************

    It was an hour that passed before Enoch returned to the group that was with Tarfeil; he looked thoughtful but he had a sly smirk on his face. Like he just came up with a plan.
    “I don’t know about you all,” Tarfeil speaking; “but I detect Enoch is about to flip the script.”
    “You know me well,” Enoch grinning; “despite our short time in Tokyo. I take it you all managed to talk amongst yourselves while I thought things over?”
    “In a fashion,” Nami grinning; her mouth exposing some wicked sharp teeth that would be better suited on a demon then on an angel; “Tarfeil now knows that I was once Himari and that she wants me to live with her.”
    “Inari here,” Cromwell smiling; “just listened to my life story without passing judgement.”
    “That is for god to decide,” Inari commenting; “but they do say confession is good for the soul.”

    “Then this is what we are going to do,” Enoch concentrating as he summons a blue sphere of energy in front of him. “each of you will be given a small portion of this power. It will help you see chaos energy as I see it.” Enoch separating the energy and sending it to each angel as well as the cherub still on his shoulder.
    “Whoever put up a chaos bubble that keeps the cherubs from the library and inquisitor headquarters,” Enoch speaking as each angel shudders and their eyes turning briefly blue before returning to normal; “is the same fuck twat that summoned an chaos shade to attack Tarfeil yesterday.”
    “What are we to do,” Cromwell examining his surroundings with fresh eyes; eyes that now see strands of energy streaming to and fro.

    “Map out exactly were the strands of energy that are powering the chaos bubble,” Enoch answering; “locate the source which should be a sitar, but don’t destroy it just yet. We need to take all the objects in question at the same time to ensure the bubble that affects the cherubs stays down. You my friend,” Enoch looking at the cherub; “I need you to get some of your friends to keep an eye out in case these four run into trouble. Be it an inquisitor agent or another shade. Have them sound an alarm that both I and Jehovah will be forced to take notice. Can you do that?”
    The cherub happily chirps and nods its head.

    “Cromwell, your desire to commit a jail break will have to wait for the moment,” Enoch speaking; “I sort of promised my uncle that I would not start shit unless I’m forced to defend myself. And change into a different robe,” Enoch looking at Cromwell as he wore the red robe of an inquisitor; “as of today you are no longer and Inquisitor and no longer under the command of Kusheil.”
    “Then what are we,” Tarfeil getting out of bed to stretch; the power that Enoch gave her making her very energetic.
    “As of now,” Enoch smiling; “you are all part of my wolf pack.”
