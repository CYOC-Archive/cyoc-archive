Wyatt was beaten yet Dukes bloodless was telling him to rip the teen to pieces. This power, as intoxicating as it was, was turning the teenager into a monster.

    "Wyatt," Duke said in a low voice, his throat was tight, close to tears but trying to maintain his composure. "Listen to me Wyatt. We both know our father will fall this evening. His lust for power will be his undoing."

    "You don't know that," Wyatt wheezed.

    "I do," Duke replied. "And so do you." Wyatt now glared at his brother, unsure of what Duke was about to say.

    "After tonight Wyatt things will be different," he said. "I will leave the castle and you will become the leader of the vampire nation."

    "Perhaps," Wyatt grimaced. "And when I'm ready, I'll come for you Duke and your wizard friends."

    "No," Duke growled. "You won't! And you never will."

    "Why?"

    "Because brother we are going to come to an understanding," Duke stated. "After tonight, you shall lead the vampires. I will not stop you. Do what you will to grow stronger."

    "Whats the catch," Wyatt asked, still trying to free himself from his brothers iron grasp. Duke now narrowed his eyes, his pupils dilating inside his magenta eyes.

    "The wizards are off limits," he growled. "Them, their children, their children's children, and every generation after that. If you ever make a move against my friends again Wyatt, I will return and I will not rest until I have watched each and every vampire slowly burn in the morning sun. Don't screw this up Wyatt, you know that I can and will do it. Now, do we have a deal?"

    Wyatt glared at his brother, a scowl forming on his face before nodding. Duke wasn't kidding and Wyatt had little doubt that after what he had witnessed Duke would and could make good on his threat. The damphir now released him, Wyatt gasping for breath as Kyra now limped to his side, the two teenage vampires glowering at the damphir as he turned to leave.

    Sasha was stunned, unnerved by Dukes brutality and strength. As she watched Duke approach, she could see that despite the feral and angry look on his face, Duke was terrified at what he had done.

    The damphirs hands were visibly trembling, a thousand yard stare plastered on the teens face. As Sasha now cautiously reached her hand towards Dukes cheek, he turned away, a melancholy look in his face.

    "monster," he whispered.

"You're not a monster Duke," she whispered. "You never were. A monster would have killed him, you showed compassion. You spared him and protected me. You're a good man Duke, a compassionate, loving man."
