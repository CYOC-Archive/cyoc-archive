    1689 August 10th 10:17 AM

    The fairies from their observation spot saw the four smoking holes in the ring of American
    Boxing Trees that surrounded Glendale like a noose. The American Boxing Trees were, of
    course, unable to uproot themselves, and thus couldn't close the gap, nor could they
    pursue the ones who had slain their fellows.

    Maria and her group, for their part, knew they could not waste the bombs, the effort, or
    the precious time to destroy every single boxing tree around Glendale. After all, they were
    not the hunters targets, the witches were.

    As Monica, Rosa, Mal'Akh soon after, and the Silver Twin, did their best to herd the
    entranced and mind fog addled towns folk into their homes, and out of the way to leave
    the hunters with one less danger to dance and one less ring of innocents to kill through to
    get to the true enemy, the five hunters came from their four different directions towards
    the witches house... it was hardly at the center of town, having been selected for it's out of
    the way location, so the hunters had different lengths of travel to get to it.

    "At least all the colors are nice." Kathrine said looking at the rainbow colored grass,
    flowers, and various plants and domesticated animals. The houses were rather bright too...
    in short they were the kinds of colors that Felman as a cultural rule loved. "There no way
    I'm so much as touching one of those giants pears though... they'd turn me into a frog or
    something I'm sure of it... the flowers sure have nice scents though... " It felt very weird to
    hear so much about this town for months and finally setting foot in it only to find it devoid
    of people with only some odd animals looking at her funny.

    "Are these animals the people?" Kathrine asked with dread but she saw children looking
    fearfully through a window. Kathrine by reaction alone waved at them, they threw a rock at
    her that her experience with the spear deflected with ease.

    She saw scared leomans in the windows looking at her who looked colored like felmans,
    many resembling the 'greatest of the great' cast of having fur of every color, Kathrine felt
    humbled by being near them even if she knew no leoman cub could ever logically have fur
    that color. Right?

    'These flower sure give off a strong scent.' Kathrine thought feeling slightly dizzy from the
    summer heat.

    North, Draco burned everything in his path. Taking this as the logical measure to insure
    that the witches thought an army was coming their way and making as much noise as
    possible to add to this. Brightly colored grass and plants burned not spreading as fast or
    far as the hungry dragon fire should have. He left a trail of grayish rainbow colored ashes
    in his way.

    Dragons could not breath fire infinitely, hardly, but they were not limited to breathing fire
    once a day like some misconceptions believed. All the same, the fire burned the flowers
    around him and the pollen the same any anything else.

    The fighting trees no longer an issue, Linus march like a solider at war towards were
    Mal'Akh's information instruct him the witches grounds were. He of course avoided the
    rings of mushroom. He knew that 'fairy rings' were supposed to be just fairy tales... but
    given what he had heard of the witches, he couldn't put it past them to make these fairy
    rings very much effective. His eyes on the rings he ignored the rainbow colored flowers at
    his feet and their scent slowly getting stronger and stronger.

    Gargoyle and Inquisitor came through Glendale from the south, going through the wild
    flowers and grass the covered nearly everything. They didn't even look at the fruits and
    didn't come within five feet of the fairy rings.

    After crossing into the Glendale town limits, Vatican noticed Marie Cruz's steps became
    smaller and slower. For a fearful second he wondered if she was getting younger (as sweet
    as that fantasy was, Vatican had a duty to serve). But no, Marie looked much the same,
    except her long cloak and jacket were marked with the pollen from the flowers.

    Vatican of course didn't smell a thing, he was living stone, he had no sense of smell or
    taste, these alien sensations were barely comprehensible to him, only vaguely comparable
    by thinking about how different rocks and stones were composed differently. His nostrils
    themselves were purely for show and went nowhere.

    He noticed Marie's eye was dilated to where it looked like a doll's. Her mouth hung open
    slightly.

    "Mutt? Papa? ... " Marie whispered at thin air.

    "Marie?" She didn't seem to hear him.

    Marie stopped dead in her tracks, and feel to her knees.

    "You're alive! I'm so happy! Is this paradise? I have so much to tell you!"

    "MARIE!" Vatican snarled and shook her. She blinked her eye, but she remained somewhere

    "YOUR FATHER IS DEAD MARIE! SO IS MUTT! ZOEY IS BACK AT THE VATICAN! THE TURK IS
    DEAD! THOSE CHILDREN ARE NEVER EVER COMING BACK! MARIE LISTEN TO ME!"

    Marie, like a puppet, slowly and awkwardly covered her eye, and with her other hand slowly

    "The... THE MASKED CROW AGAIN! Why are you here? Are you.. why are... that can't be
    but... but it's... I'm.. please... can I see you without your mask just once?... My Lord
    You're-!!!.... Vatican?"

    "Yes Marie?" He hoped.

    "I don't think what I'm seeing with one of my eyes is real... I have to trust my other eye
    until I feel I can trust the other one... please... can you lead me?"

    "Yes Marie... I will... " He also reached inside the cloak, tore off a piece of Marie's clothing
    and covered her nose and face and slowly led her out of the field of dangerous flowers. His
