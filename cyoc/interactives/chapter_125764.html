    What the Herd has been up to

    added by Anonymous 5 years ago I O

    Now Saggitarius had no notion that King Midas had kidnapped his Herd,and thought the evil doer was Issac himself.

    Clearly kidnapping the entire Herd was against the rules of the Duel,and
    since Issac had broken the rules,a increasingly angered Saggitarius
    figured this was now a Duel to the Death
    and he dived down and retrieved his great great grandpa's
    boxed set of
    Dwarvish Wind Tubes (think finely made and balanced 18th dueling pistols)

    from the now vanished Herd's
    Secret Armoury,called up two Seconds and a Dueling Referee from a neighbouring herd
    and returned to where
    Issac was still flopping about.

    Issac tried to get a explanation out of Saggitarius about
    what was going on but the Saggitarius would have nothing more to do with
    Issac,but dashed off to a nearby hilltop and cleaned his hooves
    whilst the Seconds and the Duel Referee
    explained to Issac that the
    Duel for Domination
    had just turned into
    a life or death,winner take all ( meaningless since the prize,the Herd,was gone)
    and explained what a nasty thing a Dwarvish Wind Tube
    could be if its barbed projectile got you in a duel.

    Meanwhile somewhere else the females in the Herd watched in horror as Saggitarius's adopted unicorn son

    Agwe ( awe in norse) charged King Midas.

    The females knew of course that anything King Midas touched turned to gold,which amongst other things explained why
    the cursed King went about in a outfit made of finely beaten gold.

    If the impetus neighing young unicorn somehow got touched by Midas

    it was lights out for the unicorn.

    However King Midas had a few decades of living with the Midas Touch and swifty
    uncoiled a whip and flicked it along the nose of the youth,who squealed in pain and swiftly ran back
    into the Herd.

    King Midas shook his masked head at the youth
    disapprovingly and got down to business about
    rewriting the future,

    He knew the pegasi got around at 300 miles per hour,a speed impossible for
    any other species of winged horse
    and dragon.

    Clearly the pegasi were in ca hoots
    with the Titan of Time himself

    Kronos!!!

    Since King Midas planned to alter the pre-ordained future
    a pegasi who could manipulate time was a useful
    tool in King Midas's plan to avenge his godchild's death.
