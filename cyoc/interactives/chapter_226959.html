She had always been in love with Egypt, it was the one place on Earth she wanted to visit more then anywhere else in the world. She had a tattoo of an Egyptian Ankh on the back of her right shoulder. It drove her parents crazy having a pagan symbol on their first born daughter’s body was unholy.

    Sammatha held the book in her hands as the rest of the books symbolizing the different schools of witchcraft and magic in general faded away.

    She thought that would be it, but it wasn’t.

    As she was about to open the book a shimmering doorway of golden energy appeared by her bed. This would have been scary for anyone else but Sammatha felt a warmness coming from the simple oaken door. She was about to open it but the moment she touched the handle there was a blinding flash of light and she found herself standing in a long stone chamber.

Torch light illuminated the room but seamed to stop past the second row of columns to either side of her. Massive stone statues stood between each pillar except for the single row before her, a path she knew she had to walk.

Some of the statues which she had not known before she now knew to be minor Egyptian gods and goddess. The columns began to be covered in hieroglyphics; she had no idea what they said earlier but now she could read them as if she had been using them all her life.

    As she moved down the line of statues they began to get grander and grander, starting out only being 10ft tall where she started to now being 30 or 40 ft tall with much more details. What used to be plain simple yet beautiful statues became ones inlayed with gold, silver, jewels and painted to an unbelievable detailed way.
