    The Zoo

    You come to the Zoo looking for a Job

    You are just an ordinary person, you are not rich or poor, you don't have many friends, you live by yourself, are independent, and all those other things. You are in desperate need of money, and you see a flyer for a job as a Zoo Worker, The Zoo will pay you 24 Dollars an hour, if you do a good job. You really need this job and so you go to get a job as a Zoo Worker.

    You fill out the application for the job and you hand it in. Then you get an Interview with the Owner of the Zoo. They ask you why you want this job. You say that ever since you were a young kid, you were always fascinated with animals, and you want to work alongside them. You also really need some money to help you survive some bills. The Zoo Owner considers your words, and they say they will call you if they decide to hire you.

    Several days go by and you still haven't heard anything from the Zoo Owner on the phone give you a call that says that they have hired you. You are about to lose hope when all of a sudden you hear the phone ring. The Zoo Owner congratulates you and tells you that you get the job, you get to be a Zoo Worker and that you start tomorrow, you can't believe it. You are so happy that you finally get the job.

    The next day, you go to the Zoo and the Zoo Owner has you sign a contract that discusses your working hours, wages and what animals you have to take care of. You have to make sure that none of the animals die of hunger or poor health or accidents, and you have to make sure that none of the animals escape the Zoo too. Luckily however you don't have to clean up their urine or pee, or feces or poop, some other poor sap has to do that. You sign the contract, and it looks like there is a little fine print at the bottom that says if you screw this up, you can consider your Human Life over. The Zoo Owner gives you a group of Animals to look over.
