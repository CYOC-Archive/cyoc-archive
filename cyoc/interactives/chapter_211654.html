    The main core of Christmas is tied with Christianity. However, the more general meaning of Christmas, giving others gifts and spending time with family, has become cross-cultural over the years. That was why Christmas is celebrated in places where Christianity isn't the dominant religion. On Heiya Island, the main festivity was the Dongzhi Festival, which is the winter solstice festival for most of Eastern Asia. 'Dongzhi' literally means 'winter's arrival', marking the turning point of the shortest day where the day starts to get longer. Dongzhi is about honoring the ancestors as well as eating with living relatives, not unlike other winter solstice celebrations in other parts of the world. Though while the Dongzhi Festival was taking place, other people on Heiya Island were preparing for Christmas.
