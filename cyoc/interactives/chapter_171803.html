    “Like that’s ever stopped anyone before,” Amanda threw her hair back with a flourish, “We could have far more fun then you by yourself.”

    “Sorry, relationship is strictly monogamous at the moment.” Maggie deadpanned.

    Now Amanda just looked annoyed, “Look, just because your mommy and daddy got away with taking money like pigs, doesn’t-”

    “Shut,” Maggie’s jaw tightened, “Look, this has nothing to do with my parents. I love Taylor and I want to be with her. You literarily have nothing to offer to make yourself better in comparison.”

    Amanda sneered, “Dunno what you see in Taylor, she’s basic.”

    “To you, maybe. But don’t speak about my girlfriend like that, girly! A centillion of you wouldn’t even be worth a nail clipping of Taylor.”

    Amanda threw her hands up and stomping away, “Fine, but you’ll see that we would make a way better couple, Woods!”

    “That is…childish…”

    “Yeah, somehow she thinks she’s in the right and will come out victorious in the end.”

    Maggie snorted, “Like that would ever happen. I know our story is…the weirdest, but in no way in hell would I ever want to get with anyone else if we were to break up for a good reason. I meant what I said: there is nothing anyone could offer to even compare.”

    “I know…but if this is but a taste of what comes…”
