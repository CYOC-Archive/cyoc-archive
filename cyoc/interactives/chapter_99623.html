    There was pause in the brushing before Melissa said, "I sense a 'but' coming, though." Then she resumed cleaning her teeth.
    "The emotional side of things. Sometimes I think Julie sees the romance more than anything else."
    Martin heard his wife spit before she replied. "Julie is only 14. Of course she's going to see that. Between the books she reads in her personal time and our shining example of how a happy romantic couple should be..."
    "Well, I certainly don't want to discourage that. And she does seem happy with Eric Anderson."
    "I think they're both trying to feel their way on this. From the one private chat I had with his mother when I dropped off his book bag yesterday, Eric's a very intelligent kid too, but this is foreign territory for both of them," observed Melissa, as she began flossing her teeth.

    "Sometimes as a parent, I want to do everything I can to help Julie grow and succeed. Yet there are other times when I just want that cute little child who fell asleep in my arms every night as I read to her to never grow up," admitted Martin.
    "I think every parent feels that way. I know I do," agreed Melissa from the bathroom.
    "From what I've seen, they do make a good couple though," conceded Martin. "They're both smart, and they compliment each others' strengths and weaknesses so well. Together, they balance the head and the heart. Her over emotionalizing things at times augmenting his overthinking things at times and vice versa."
    "Like us. It's uncanny. Especially whenever Julie calls herself and Eric us in reverse."
    "I know." Then Martin paused before adding, "I've never told you this before, because you're... touchy about the ten year gap between our first meeting and our wedding night as it is. But some of those nights I went without you, I pined so miserably about being alone..."

    "Like I told Eric. Love at first sight is a genuine, proven scientific fact for were-mers. The moment I first saw you, I knew you were the one. Intellectually, I was willing to wait as long as it took, but emotionally..."
    Silence was the only response from the bathroom.

    "Honey? Are you all right?" he asked, as Martin started to get out of bed.
    "I'm okay," sobbed his wife. "Just give me a moment," she sniffled.
    "I didn't mean to upset you."
    "You didn't. That was a wonderful compliment. That your love for me runs so deep..."
    "It still does."
    There was another pause, before Melissa changed the subject by asking, "You still worried about the pill issue?"
