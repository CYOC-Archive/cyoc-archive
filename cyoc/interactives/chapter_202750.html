    Ethan shook his head. A deep rumble of a growl vibrated in his throat. "You know you don't have to go if you don't want to," he was trying to back out.

    "No, it's fine. Afterall you're not a werewolf."

    The low growl continued as he led me into the forest on the backside of the campus. It wasn't huge, but it was enough to have a small hiking trail and student could see some foxes or weasels running around if they were really lucky. Otherwise, it was just used by some of the ecology students to study in a more natural environment. The park was meant to be closed after dark, but the only time security ever went over there was if it was reported. And that was only when the frat guys got too much into their hazing and were causing problems.

    "Still not a werewolf?"

    He continued to shake his head as he walked ahead of me. There was a large rock next to a tree that he sat down on and he kicked off his shoes and pulled off his socks. "Don't be weird about it ok?" Ethan glared at me. I nodded. He removed his shirt and tossed it to the side.

    "Fine," Ethan said. His voice had even more grit that usual. "You wanted to know if I was a werewolf. Here's your answer." A loud cracking sound came from his direction. It almost sounded like a bone breaking. He let out a heavy grunt as his back spasmed. Every muscle on his body started to flex. All the way down to his fingertips, they strained themselves. His nails turned dark. Then grew longer out of his fingertips. They quickly sharpened into claws. Claws that could easily tear into a tree of any kind of flesh.

    His hands looked like they doubled in size. Paw pads formed on the palm of his hands while black fur ran up the back. The forest of hair only grew denser and darker as it covered up any of his skin. It ran all the way up his biceps and over his shoulders. Blood was pumping through him at an alarming rate. Wherever the fur touched, his muscles only grew. Already being bigger than you're average guy, Ethan just started to grow bigger, resembling more the size of a bear than a human. His back stretched, growing longer as it did so.

His tail bone pushed further out of his backside. Just a little at first then long enough to be a massive tail. Long stands of fur quickly grew out of the added appendage. All the while his feet were completely reshaping into paws. The bones in his toes quickly reshaped themselves, growing taller and wider to help support his new weight. With each pump of blood they only grew bigger and bigger. More animalistic like claws grew out of where his nails had been. But when Ethan tried to stand back up, his foot lengthened to where his weight was pressed only on the front of his foot instead of the whole thing.

    Once he saw the moon, he reeled his head back and let out a howl. His nose quickly turned black and extended out of his face into a long snout. Massive fangs and sharp teeth filled his mouth. The towering posture he had only grew even more as he stood at least a foot and a half taller than he had. Somehow he looked to almost have doubled his weight as he dropped back down onto all fours. The sharp nails dug into the dirt. His attention turned towards me, snarling. Slowly inching closer and closer as his deep threatening growl exited his throat.

He fell into the hug as I pet his back. His tail started to wag a bit faster as his growl lowered to almost nothing.
