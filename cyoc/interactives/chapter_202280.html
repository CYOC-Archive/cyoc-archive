    Transform or Dare?

    Jenny's first case starts

    "Hey hey, I clearly remember you sister was naked and drunk at a private property and she is also accused of trespassing, right?" Kyle maniacly laughed. "I'm so sorry, hope you can win this case. I will call again once I see papers whenever you win or loose. Good luck."

    As the phone call closed. Jenny hit his bed as hard as she could and begun to read all the papers of the case. It was tight. All the evidence pointing out how guilty she was. Camera footage, voice clips and fingerprints everywhere. And if even anybody asks he about something it become the truth even if it isn't. What can she do to win this.

    "I can win this, right?" Jenny said to herself. "Like, I. Can. Win. This. Right?"

    Jenny didn't felt any different and rushed out of the room to notice that there was nobody in the house beside her. She begun to think. She remembered that her mother moved away and gave Jenny her house. Jenny tried to scroll her phone and saw a lot of names she couldn't recognize. She was too scared to leave the building and felt that if she even had even smallest of small talk. She might turn everything worse.

    She took a breather. Walked back to her room and begun to think like an adult lawyer.

    "I'm an adult now. Lawyer. I think like I've been in position so long that I can defend people. I was chosen since my sister asked me and I was given permission since nobody else wanted to defend her. Which is odd, but I take it. It seems this Kyle guy wants to mess up with me, but seems like he made a simple mistake." Jenny thought to herself out loud.

    She opened her phone and called on the first name she found on her phone.

    "Hello, Jenny here." Jenny greeted.

    "Umm. Hi. Did you have any further questions?" The voice asked.

    Jenny waited for a while and noticed that this kind of question was fine to ask since it was not a statement, but a question.

    "Mhmm, yes. Could you repeat something for me?" Jenny smiled and wanted to see if something worked out.

    The voice on the phone agreed.

    "You are head taller than me, right?" Jenny stated.

    The voice reluctantly repeated what Jenny had asked them and waited for a minute then abruptly closed the phone and blocked the number as nothing happened.

    It seemed that the person had to come up with the question. The question itself couldn't be leading. Or that the question couldn't be a overall statement or general question which could be asked from anybody. Jenny also figured that if it wasn't a yes or no question, no transformation wouldn't occur since it would be spirit of the Truth and Dare -game. These were logical conclusions which Jenny could work with. She made an assumption that if she was forced to ask the question, the transformation will occur, but if she could avoid something like that being asked. She would avoid the transformation.

    She looked at the clock and noticed that she had enough to time to drive to the court before they would start the questioning. She took the keys and hopped into her car.
