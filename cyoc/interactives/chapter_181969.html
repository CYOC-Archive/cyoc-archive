    "Hey, Jade." He said, leaning in on his knees and kissing her on the cheek. She smiled, at least, she did her best to. She didn't really feel like smiling, since her humanity was lost, and instead was a massive bovine.

    Mark stood up. "Feeling tired?" he then asked. Jade tilted her head as if to shrug. Mark sat down and laid his back onto her furry stomach. He tried to be careful, but intentionally squiggled on her soft fur.

    Jade Chuckled. "What are you doing?" she said.

    Mark stretched his arms atop his head, like he was reaching for something high upon a shelf, or to get his teachers attention to answer a question. Mark sighed in relief. "Your fur is really soft, you know." He said.

    Jade smiled. At least Mark was there to cope through her hardships right now. "I love you, Mark." she says, leaning her down on the floor, beginning to drift to sleep. Jade yawned.

    "You going to sleep already?" He said, rubbing her back."It's only 6 'o' clock!"

    Jade shook her ears. "I don't know." she said. "Just feel like taking a little nap."

    "Ah, alright." Mark replied. "One second." he says, getting up from his original position on her belly. Her body jiggled as Mark's weight is no longer there. He got out of the barn for a few minutes. When he came back, he brought in unused pillows from their closet and a blanket that they used earlier.

    He placed the pillows beneath her head and the blanket above her back. "Feel better?" he asked.

    "A little bit, yeah." Jade replied. She shook her head once more, her legs adjusting into a finer position. "Though I don't think sleeping like this will be any more comfortable than it already is." she said.

    "Oh, okay then." He said. Mark pat her back once more. "Goodnight hon, see you tomorrow." It was almost like tucking a child to bed, rather it's his wife turned cow. Who would've thought.

    "Goodnight Mark." Jade said.

    Mark left the barn and closed it gently, as he returned to the house. Jade slept surprisingly comfortably that night. She hadn't had a sleep like that in years, which refreshed her mental state a bit. She was slowly starting to adjust to the life of being a cow. Though she may not know, many more hardships and embarrassments will come, but luckily for her, Mark would always be there for her, by her side.
