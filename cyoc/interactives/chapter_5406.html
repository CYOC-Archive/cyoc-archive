    "Okay where is it then? This is Feather Street and this is where it is suppose to be. Where is it? Now I always have trouble finding it," Sarah muttered.

    "Why is that?" You ask.

    "Because, the person who owns it made charmed the building so that only wiccans of age who were in need could see it. I am not yet of age but according to Wiccan law, Women are suppose to be of age when they... well. When they turn around eleven," She says with a blush.

    You nod and are uncertain by what she means but you think nothing of it.

    Before she could say anything more you noticed a shop that ressembled an old house but above it read the 'Orchard's Carry'. At first one would not really notice anything strange about it until you looked a second time and saw plants growing around it and you could swear a tree stood in the very center of the old strange house. You kind of stammered at this and wondered if anyone really lived in this old house or worked in it for that matter but the sign was the strangest thing of all. Everything looked old and grown out, but the sign looked fresh and new, like it had just been put up an hour ago.

    "Sarah," You pointed to the house and she gasped.

    "That's it!" She hugged and kissed you before dashing into the street and towards the weird building.

    You followed hurridly and was just barely making your way up the steps when you heard the bell ring and Sarah slip inside. You rushed to the door and hurried in as well and followed Sarah with just as much apprehension to the place as you had to her house.

    The place was very dusty. Inside looked like a cross between an old toy store and a pawn shop but some of the items on the shelves were strange. True some items were toys; dolls, cards, and small sculptures of animal. Some items that were also seen where candles, broomsticks, and crystal ball. A full library lay to the right containing many books of differnt sizes and shapes. At the back lay a strange grocery full of many bottles of miscellaneous items and several plants. The counter of the storekeeper lay to the right of the door right next to it. A door that lead to the side of the house was opened and it's view dissapeared further inside the house. You had guessed the store owner had just been past there and Sarah called for him.

    "Mr. Owens, Carry Owen?"
