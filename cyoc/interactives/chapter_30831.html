Hannah would have to make it a point to tell them both.

    “Hannah!” Lateisha snapped her hooves. “Do you always have to do that?”

    “Oh.” Hannah REALLY didn’t want to discuss that, “Nothing important.” She thought quick on how to change the subject. “Don’t you think it weird how we’re talking about bucks and does as if we’re always been them or that’s how males and females have always been split?”

    “No, not really.” Su Yin said, “Since we’ve effectively become our own culture inside the park. With our own rules, nuisances and dos and don’t that aren’t directly effected by the outside world. Yes, we’re all just passing through, but since we don’t leave or enter at the same time, we pass on cultural habits we’ve learned from others onto new arrivals before we leave ourselves. Not really that different from any other culture, no matter on how small a scale it is. The sentient mind seeks orders in its life.”

    “Oh, sorry!” Jenny smiled guiltily rubbing the back of her head, “Just trying to lighten the mood.”

    “So ladies,” Hannah said, “We were talking about bucks and us?”

They didn’t have them on the outside of the park, but losing them now, while they were their current species, seriously felt the same like losing an arm or a leg.

    “Forget your six pointers and talk to me.”

    “Huh? Oh yeah! The husky, I think her name was Katie, “ A dog named Katie, oh brother, “commented that the management were considering offering a program for some people to become permanent members of the park, but with some very nasty restrictions in the works.”

    Did he have to keep trailing off like that? “Which where?”

    “Oh, really creepy stuff, legally severing all ties with the outside world, all of it, signing a legal contract that says you won’t try to develop the land by building yourself a log cabin or a veggie garden, and that you’ll no longer be on the Huskies’ ‘rescue if seriously injured list’ and of course your original human DNA pattern is erased from the archive, for good.”

    “In other words, live here with the fantasy of it totally stripped away. Who’d agree to something like –that-?!”

    “I don’t know, hippies who still think nature is all rosebuds and butterflies? Someone who doesn’t think they’re serious? A criminal whose looking for a permanent hiding place?”

    “Naw, not that last one, that would be the same as trading one prison for another.”

    “You really think of this place as a prison? Last time I checked you’ve been living it up.”

    “What makes a prison a prison is any place you can never ever leave.”
