    used for driving. He adjusted the mirrors, getting a glimpse of himself at one point looking like he had been
    middle aged for a while now. He felt a twinge of despair, hoping it'll all be over soon.

    He pulled out of the car park and joined the highway. About a third of the way into their journey, they'd
    ended up in one of the road's infamous traffic jams. He punched the steering wheel in anger. Of all the times
    to get a traffic jam it had to be the day he was rapidly aging to death! They'd once sat in traffic for 4
    hours before. He remembered Mark got out and jogged the rest of the way, leaving him to wait it out by

    head was throbbing from the influx of memories competing with the perception of his changes. If they didn't
    get this sorted soon, he might do something he'd regret. Anything to get peace of mind. Mark was worried as

    time they got home, he'd probably be Simon's age. He glanced over at his uncle. He had grown even heftier now,

    beard was rivalling his own now, and he was combing his fingers through it in frustration, both at the traffic
    jam and his aging predicament. It was completely grey and starting to verge on white. He had a pair of glasses
    perched on his nose, originally to help him see better while driving, but now they were a permanent pair,
    rarely ever leaving his face.

    Mark really wished he could help Danny right now, but the only person who knew about the Chronivac, now that
    he'd made Jay unaware, was his dad. At least he assumed he knew about it. It was in his bedroom after all. Yet

    turned to look at his nephew and gave him a smile he hoped looked reassuring but ended up obscured by the hair
    covering his face. They started to move again, only to stop after a yard. It was going to be a while.
