    “What?” John looked around. So far, all he had pulled from the suitcase was toddler clothing. There was even a diaper. “You’re joking, right?”

    “No. I’m not.” Robert’s voice became ominous. “Go ahead and put on the clothes.”

    “They’re way too small!”

    “Oh, they’ll fit,” Robert said. John stared at him, stupefied. “Fine, I’ll help you out.” Robert snapped his fingers. To John’s horror, his body began to move on its own. Robert picked him up and carried him over to the bathroom mirror. “And I think you look much cuter than before.” John stared. In the mirror was Robert, holding a toddler boy.

    “Why- how-” John squeaked out. Robert chuckled. He set John down and began to pack up his suitcase.

    “Don’t steal from a wizard, kid. You’re lucky I packed my nephew’s extra clothes, and not my sister’s. Or my dog’s. You’d be in a much stickier situation.” Robert stood up. “All right. Come along, Bobby.”

That is, if I decide you’re worthy of returning to normal.” Robert took John’s hand. “And since you wanted to be Robert Jones today, Robert Jones you’ll be. My son Robert Jones II, whom everyone calls Bobby.”

    “Yes. And I’ll tell them the truth. They’ll understand.” Robert looked at his watch. “Shoot, we really have to get going.”
