    While there was plenty to learn about during school, school clubs also were part of WSA, as they were in other schools. From Mock Trial to Debate Club, there were those that were also present at WSA. What made WSA clubs different was that there were more physical clubs, even those that were normally considered "only for nerds".

    ---

    One such "club" was the GGAC, which was often surprising for those who often derided it as a "nerds' social club". After the DEA had seized the property that the illegal underground marijuana farm, it was determined that the house was suffering from a one-two punch of both dry rot and a beetle infestation. Because of that, it was basically decided to gut and "purge" the old building and build a newer building over the entrance to the underground Marijuana farm. Because of the preference of some of the members of the previous incarnation of the GGAC for playing the Eberron Campaign Setting rather than Greyhawk Campaign Setting, it was decided to create the building as half pseudo-late medieval/early renaissance inn, half Contemporary Era nightclub. This helped blunt the reputation about the GGAC as the next evolution of the "nerds' social club".

    What was also done was convert the marijuana farm into an easily adaptable underground dungeon. Because the farm was 45 feet high by 35 feet wide by 750 feet long, it was easy to install modular walls that were easy to move and position to create common underground dungeon rooms. There were also was easily movable props for when one adventure needed an "abandoned evil shrine" one adventure then a "giant's castle" the next.

    While most of the people were a little "irritated" about Jerry including sexual roleplaying, there were some new members who weren't as "straight laced" as the other members. For example, there were two girls who were looking for a place to party for a weekend who wanted somewhere new. While initially unwilling because one of them "didn't want her possible dance mates fluent in Klingon", it was interesting enough to get them to join up for "weekend sessions".

    There were also some guys from WSA who were joining as a kind of apology towards the founding members. These guys were actually more surprised to the more physical aspects of the GGAC. There were also some who appreciated the chance for "seduction" rolls, some of them more than others.

    ---

    Another club was the Foreign Affairs Club. While it wasn't either a typical "Foreign Affairs Office" or "school club", there was actually a lot of things going on at that club. Part of it was from foreign exchange students, who were either part of Project Phoenix, or were human and didn't undergo conversion.

    The majority of the Foreign Affairs Club's project was planning cultural festivals. While some of them were easily planned, such as the Chinese New Year celebrations, there were also some that were a bit more difficult to understand and/or plan. But there were other activities that were part of the Foreign Affair Clubs, such as creating posters that showed how people in other countries greet each other, for example.

    ---

    The most surprising of all the school clubs was the Fight Club. While it wasn't anything like the underground fighting rings that most people think of when they think of the words Fight Club, there were still things that made it amusing enough to make people want to join (including the "First Rule about Fight Club"). It really was a place where teens and young adults learn how to box/fight/spar/etc. properly without flailing your arms like windmills. It also taught the difference between real wrestling moves and "professional" wrestling moves.
