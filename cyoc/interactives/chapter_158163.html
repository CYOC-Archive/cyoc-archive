He and Eileen had made love, he had changed Eileen’s size and age, and humbled his foster mom June. Aside from playing around with Eileen some more, what else was there to do? Ty’s prayers though seemed to be answered as the doorbell now began to ring.

    “Whose that,” Eileen asked. Ty shrugged, approaching the door he now answered it, seeing three middle aged women who did not look thrilled to see him.

    “Hello Mrs. Franklin,” Ty said politely to an auburn haired woman. “Ty where is your mother,” Mrs Franklin frowned. Ty motioned upstairs, causing the three women to glance upstairs before looking back at Ty. “Well Ty,” Mrs. Franklin asked, raging an eyebrow. “Are you going to invite us in?”

    “Sorry,” Ty said quietly, quickly stepping aside as the three women now entered his home. As they did they noticed Eileen standing on the stairs. “Ty is that a friend of Dustin’s,” a brunette woman asked. “No Mrs. Jensen,” Ty said. “That’s my girlfriend Eileen.”

    Eileen stepped forward, extending her hand as she smiled politely. Mrs, Jensen gave the girl a curious look as she looked her up and down. “Young lady where are your shoes,” she asked. Turning to Ty once more she gave the teen a reproachful look. “Ty I wouldn’t associate with a girl that dresses like this,” she said. “It shows poor parenting. Dressing in a dress like that with no shoes or bra for that manner.” Mrs. Jensen now shook her head, attempting to shame Eileen for her outfit.

    “Diana,” a raven haired woman sighed. “The girl is most likely the products of one of those hippy families. She probably does t know much better. Now come on, let’s find June and get this meeting underway.”

    “You mean you’re ready to start drinking Victoria,” Diana sneered, gazing back at the raven haired woman. Victoria waved off her friend, turning towards Ty once more. “Ty dear could you get your mother please,” she asked. “After which you and your little friend can run off and do whatever it is you wish.”

    “Of course,” Ty said quickly. “Before I do can I offer you anything to drink?”

    “Melissa and I will take water,” Diana said haughtily gesturing towards the auburn haired woman. “Victoria on the other hand will most likely want wine but will wait til your mother arrives.” Ty nodded, taking Eileen’s hand as he quickly went into the kitchen. As the two arrived he could see the look of rage on Eileen’s face.

    “Who the fuck are those bitches,” Eileen seethed, glaring back towards the foyer.

    “Diana Jensen, Melissa Franklin, And Victoria Connors,” Ty sighed. “Their finds of my step mom. Apparently they were all cheerleaders back in high school.”

    “They’re unbelievably rude,” Eileen exclaimed. “Do they always act like this?”

    “Pretty much,” Ty shrugged. “They were June’s best friends back in high school. They get together like this all the time.”

    “And they always treat you like your a servant,” Eileen asked.

    Ty nodded. “Unfortunately Yeah. Every time they come over June uses me to serve them drinks or bring food out to them. It’s annoying, sometimes I wish I could just get back at them somehow.”

    Ty and Eileen paused as they now looked at one another. Ty had forgotten for a moment he now had the power to get back at June’s friends. The only question now was what to do to them?
