    It was a normal day at the 20 Fitness Gym when the folks showed up with the so titled workout drink Muscle Tech-X. The tables they reserved were manned by a pair of young bodybuilders who they claimed were beneficiaries of the use of Muscle Tech-X, as well as their boss Kelly Crauss, the corporate representative, an athletic thirty something red-headed young woman who had a position in the corporation responsible's supplement division. Of the three, the first bodybuilder, Hans Scheier, was a charming and handsome German boy with crewcut blond hair, a huge build, and just enough of an accent to make him seem alluring.

    The second, Tyson York, was a black bodybuilder, slightly less huge than Hans but much more toned - whereas Hans might win the "size monster" awards, Tyson had him beat when it came to aesthetics. Tyson's skin was light caramel colored and he styled his hair in cornrows. His hazel eyes and handsome features left him with multiple women interested in him back home and in his corporate work with Muscle Tech-X's firm.

    The advertisements they displayed, and the bodies Hans and Tyson demonstrated, were for Muscle Tech-X, a new supplement - specifically a workout drink - that supposedly had the power to grant immediate and drastic results via some chemical mined from an old village associated with the Greek Amazon stories. Yet despite the eminent power and capacities of the workout drink, people were barred from having any more than a single cup of it.

    "Security measures", that was what Kelly said before turning someone away who had had their fill already.

    Supposedly, that was all that was needed.

    And when the regular gym goers saw David Harlan, a washed up former football player, go from wash up to rippling with muscles anew, maybe that should've been all that was needed to show the efficacy of the drug. But once people have something, they inevitably want more of it. And it seemed Muscle Tech-X innately had some allure that made people curious about another dose.

    The truth was far more concerning.

    The more cups of the drink someone consumed, the more their body would change. While they would indeed pick up yet more muscle growth and size, some even growing taller by a foot or more, in testing they found that once someone drank past a certain threshold, other changes began setting in. Strangely, the drink seemed to turn men into women as they drank past that threshold. While their minds would remain largely unchanged, there would be some interesting and bizarre slight changes to how they conducted themselves.

    And if anything, when women were exposed to it past that threshold, they became even more fierce and dangerous. Charged with sexuality and ready to fight at the drop of a hat.

    The drink induced a warrior mentality, easier to launch into a fight.

    Perhaps this substance mined out there was the source of the legendary Amazon spirit that so bewitched and intrigued the Greeks. But whatever the case, unleashing this substance in the context of a gym? It was certainly bold - but reckless. Only a matter of time, really, until someone got hooked a little too much on the drink after trying it for the first time.

    Who is it to start his exposure to Muscle Tech-X? And what happens with him or her?
