    Aden changes his mind

    Previous chapter
    Branch Full view
    added 12 years ago A

    Jaffar said, "I guess you're ready to change back. Here's your stuff."

    Aden said, "Hold off on that. This has been a change as the box promised. It's been kind of
    fun. Besides, you really should have a pet. My main reason for not letting you have a pet
    was that I didn't think you were old enough to take care of it but this provides a way I can
    teach you responsibility. This might take some explaining to Guy or maybe we can keep
    this our secret. I'll be the Dad when I have to be and be your dog otherwise."

    "Really, you mean it?" Jaffar asked.

    "You'll have to put food and water for me and take me for walks and you'll have to clean up
    after me." Aden said, "I'll growl at you if you slip up on your responsibility. But maybe we
    can play frisbee. I have heard of a competition coming up for frisbee catching dogs and it
    would be fun to win that."

    Jaffar wrapped his arms around his dad's neck, "Oh thank you, thank you."

    Aden said, "Now put a leash on Luke and I and let's go home."

    ----------------------------------------------------------------
    Luke also decided to stay a dog for a while. This wasn't because Luke wanted to be a dog.
    He thought it would be strange to show up human after two years and he didn't feel ready
    to face the awkwardness of explaining things. So for now, he wanted time to think things
    through. In the meantime, he played frisbee with Aden and the boys.

    One day, Aden was playing in the front yard and he got carried away. He ran off and a
    dogcatcher caught him. Aden pretended to be a dog so he could keep his secret but he
    was glad that he had insisted on the purchase of a tag with phone number. But when Jaffar
    came to pick Aden up, the dogcatcher insisted, "It's against the law to let your dog run
    without a license. I could fine you."

    Jaffar pleaded and the dogcatcher said, "Well, I could license him right now."

    So the dogcatcher began the process. This pound had a small veterinary bay and so Aden
    was up on the table. The dogcatcher felt Aden's balls and said, "You haven't neutered him
    either. I'll have to neuter him."

    Aden whimpered and looked at Jaffar. Jaffar asked, "Do you have to neuter him?"

    The dogcatcher said, "He'll be a lot of trouble if he's not neutered. Are you planning on
    breeding him?"

    Aden said, "I hadn't thought about it."

    The dogcatcher said, "I could skip neutering him but it would cost you extra and you'd
    have to keep a stricter hold on him to prevent him from running loose."

    Aden got his license and his vaccinations before the dogcatcher released him. On the way
    home, Aden said, "Thanks son. You're really taking responsibility for your pet now. You've
    saved my balls. Did you remember to bring the pooper scooper?"

    "I forgot." Aden said.

    "Then how are you going to clean up after me?" Aden growled. Then he began licking his
    balls.

    "Please don't do that in front of me." Aden complained.

    "I'm a dog." Aden said, "This is what dogs do."
