    The man chuckled and said "The same way I have this monochromatic time bubble. We both are of a similar kind. The world bends to our whims no matter what those whims have been. Also do not think it's just the two of us who have this power. There are usually a dozen of us at a time... and it looks like you've already gotten into your second life Tyler."

    The man sighed then said "Either way, Alice, I was here to just say stop messing with reality in such a big way from now on. No time loops without the rest of our consent as well. Those of us who have these powers get annoyed with time loops, but since you are new we decided to give you some time to test things out."

    "So why can't I just wish you out of existence right now along with the others?"

    laughing the man said "It's possible, but it takes time. We have immunities against each other and we have to really concentrate to change another one of us, or do something as drastic as wiping one of us out of existence. In the mean time the person you are attacking could just summon something to crush you while your attention is on the attack. Now i'm not saying you can't do something like use your power to rule the world, heck i'm basically the guy running the Illuminati, but try not to completely destroy the fabric of reality or society with these wishes. If you want to do some really big things then I'd suggest talking to a few of us first so you don't tick off the rest of us, it's hard for one of us to wipe you out but easy for all of us to. Don't worry about communication just wish to know any of us and you'll know where or who we are. Now then I suggest you get some sleep, wouldn't want to make things look weird do you sweety?" the man was about to walk out and then clapped his hand and a card materialized in front of you and he said "I usually don't give random strangers one of these cards, but feel free to give it to any mortal you want. It's made of a special material that we somehow can't manipulate or recreate. Now if you'll excuse me i'll be late for a meeting I was doing 500 times before realizing you wasn't going to stop this, and now time will move regularly when the clock strikes midnight."
