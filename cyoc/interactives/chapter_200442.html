    While Lucy was busy changing, her mother was busy running from rack to rack grabbing anything that she thought was going look great on her daughter. She took a minute to pause and think back on her life.

    Recca (short for Rebecca) was the middle child of five and the only girl. With both older and younger boys to look after, she didn’t have much time to herself. It didn’t help that her mother was in the drug game for way too long and her dad was basically nonexistent. Between dodging the punches and kicks from her older brothers and trying to wrestle her younger brothers, the only thing she had time to do was sleep. She didn’t need to workout through her youth, she had to lug the entire family around. She was mature, she was strong, and she was growing up fast. Her puberty was probably the first time she felt like she could finally be a normal girl. With one of her brothers in college and her younger brothers finally separated from her, she had a little more time to question herself, and find out who exactly she was. Her brothers never really cared for pink, frilly dresses and girly clothes, her mother didn’t even dress feminine to keep her alias while in the streets. Her first time even seeing a “real girl” was during a school event where everyone had to dress up. She saw her, her first crush, a blonde chubby-cheeked girl in a flowing pink dress that looked absolutely stunning. She tried walking up to her, but she couldn’t build up enough courage to get close. Ever since then, she’s been so closed and shut off from most people.

    So how did she get married and have kids?

    Well, in college, her roommates invited her to a party. She was already on the fence about going since she was still figuring out whether or not she was into women, but then she saw another potential crush. A brunette cutie with glasses that was seemingly out-of-place at a college party. Long story short, their dad was a goofy female at first, made Recca laugh, they dated, and eventually got married before they graduated. After graduating, Recca decided to help her break the curse so they could truly be happy. She came to terms with the person, not the body and their love grew stronger. She and her now husband moved into a house and immediately got busy making a family. Despite all that, her lingering desire to be with a girl remained, but it never damaged her marriage. She now had the opportunity to live vicariously through her daughter, and she was not going to let this opportunity go to waste. With a demonic gleam in her eye and a heavy stack of clothes in hand, she marched straight into Lucy’s changing room and surprised her with…

    What is she holding?
