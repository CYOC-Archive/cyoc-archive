    Get out of those clothes

    added 7 years ago A O

    "All right, Matthew, see this empty terrarium? Do you want to tell the class why it is empty?" teacher scolded.

    "I did something or," he was having trouble because he honestly didn't know the reason the tank was empty or what was supposed to be inside it. From the look on the teacher's face, he changed his answer, "Or something I didn't do?" you say helplessly.

    "Tsk, tsk, I tell you young man you're too immature. But at least you recalled our discussion from yesterday and came prepared with a wet suit. You could have just brought it with you in a bag, but wearing it saves time."

    You look confused, as dim light dawns. "Oh, I was supposed to take care of something alive in the terrarium. I forgot to feed it?" you say helplessly. And uh," you stumble with the words.

    "And Matthew left the cover off the terrarium when he took it home Friday, and his cat killed our frog."

    "Aaawww!" went out the collective wail.

    "But Matthew has agreed for his punishment to replace our frog. Haven't you, Matthew?"

    "Uh, Yeah, uh, sure. That's why I wore the wetsuit?"

    Now take off your clothes, Matthew," the teacher ordered, and added after thinking about Matthew's likely behavior, "Except for the wet suit."

    "I thought he wore it because he was going surfing?" whispered one student.

    As he stripped down to rubber, one of his classmates commented, "He does sort of look a bit like a frog."

    Another said, "He's took big for the terrarium."

    Matthew smiled, he felt like he was getting away with something. His teacher had wanted to humiliate him by having wear only a wet suit in front of the class. It didn't hide much of his physique, so it was almost like being naked, but he wore the wet suit to the beach almost every day- even school days if the waves were breaking. That was part of his problem. He beamed. There was no way he'd fit in the terrarium.

    "Now he's, but we can do something about it, can't we children?"

    Matthew hated it when the teacher called them children. They were young adults. He grit his teeth. Now she was going to due that funky voodoo wiccan junk again.

    "Yes, Ma'am," they answered.

    Matthew rolled his eyes.

    The students gathered around him forming an irregular circle holding hands, and chanting.

    Now he remembered why he usually ditched this class: all the weirdness.

    He started to feel a bit warm, and sweaty inside the wet suit.

    Matthew's stomach rumbled. He really should have eaten breakfast, he thought.

    A fly was buzzing around the room. Matthew wasn't paying attention to his classmates or his teacher, just watching the fly. He sure was hungry, and that incessant buzzing was annoying him. Suddenly the fly flew 18 inches in front of him, and he opened his mouth. His tongue shot out an impossible 2 feet, and caught the fly in midair, before it retracted into his mouth. Matthew's jaw clamped down with a satisfying crunch.

    "I skipped breakfast," Matthew explained. Then it hit him that his tongue was now two feet long and he had just eaten a fly. And his classmates were still chanting in gibberish. He looked down at his tight wet suit. It was shiny and damp, and greenish yellow on his belly and more of an olive green elsewhere. Damn, it wasn't a wet suit. It was his skin. He looked down at his big webbed feet. He sort of felt pleased. It would make surfing easier. He could swim faster with these flippers. Then he noticed his classmates were getting taller, and he felt something pushing out his backside.

    "What's that?" Nathan asked pointing at the tail protruding from Matthew's back.

    "Keep chanting, I will give the play by play," the teacher explained. "You see Matthew was an adolescent human male, now he is becoming an adolescent male frog. Notice how he's already lost 3/5ths of his former mass. I'd say he weighed less than a hundred pounds, and as an adolescent frog, he should be less than a pound. Keep chanting, he'll never fit in the terrarium if you slack off on the chanting."

    "Ribbit?" you croak.

    You're not sure now that you shouldn't have taken the suspension or expulsion instead. At last the chanting stops. You look up from the floor, your legs have shrunk to baby size,but you can crawl on your hands pulling your big ass tail behind you. You need water badly. You are breathing shallowly.

    "Alright, Nathan, pick up Matthew and put him in his tank. We'll let him acclimate a bit, and do our science module this afternoon."

    "Shouldn't we feed him? He's only had one fly all day," noted Annie.

    "Alright, Nathan put him in the tank, and Annie, get the container of bugs marked Tadpole Chow out of the minifridge."
