    Of course, the idea of transferring a soul into a body it hadn't been born into let alone had
    meant to be in the first place, had not been looked well upon by... well, -many- religions
    (many who loathed to admit they agreed with others).

    Ironically, it was he gargoyles themselves that were abhorrent to do this, since the new
    gargoyles kept their original memories, personalities, and loyalties: many 'natural'
    gargoyles consider these 'false gargoyles' to be 'impure' and a threat to the gargoyle
    consistency that the living stone creatures took so much pride in. And saw the
    introductions of creatures who were not directly cloned from a previous gargoyle as
    horrendously unnatural. So the ritual of transferring souls into a gargoyle body had been
    a -very- great taboo among gargoyles, being about as welcome as pedophiles were
    among humans.

    When the terrible purge of the Knight Templar had begun as a pretense to cease the
    Templar's assets, some had actually come to the stone creatures imploring membership...
    They were now safely hidden among the statuary of the Vatican, centuries later, the secret
    line of defense of the heart of an entire faith.

    'Though personally,' Marie Cruz thought in the late sixteen hundreds, standing what
    would be reorganized one day into the Holy Office staring out at the silent ever watchful
    guardians, 'I wonder if they even still remember being humans after all this time...'

    Marie was relatively good at what she did, if you didn't count her glaring handicap of
    missing an eye. The blond inquesitor didn't mind, she had often been able to see things
    with that lost eye that others couldn't.

    And of course, there was the small fact she was a woman: and thus was both officially --
    unoffical--, below the radar, and considered totally -expendable.-

    The Inquisition had been established in name to root out people who were taking the
    message and creed of their theology and twisting it to mean what they wished it to mean,
    but in practice seemed to only be directed towards people with attractive holdings that
    the king then took into his own hands.

    She also could sense that the Inquisition in it's current incarnation was coming to it's
    close.

    "The Inquisition and it's courts do not recognize, nor humor the superstitious belief in the
    existence of witchcraft or sorcerery!" The Council of the Suprema had told her to her face
    on a Tuesday afternoon (since that was normally when they were closed and not in
    session), "And we will not grant resources to such a ridiculous fantastic quest!"

    Marie Cruz had seen that one coming. her other eye had shown her. It was hard to
    convince the Council of the Suprema when the corpse of what had torn out her eye had
    turned to dust when the sunlight hit it.

    Marie Cruz did have one advantage in that she wasn't married, which meant her own
    resources didn't belong to a husband instead of her. So if she was lucky she'd be able to
    catch a boat to the new world and to the small town of Glendale. The Council of the
    Suprema couldn't accuse her of abandoning her post if she didn't officially exist.

    'If I'm to kill three witches set on turning a small town into a hole of insane creatures that
    will not even be called human... I'm going to need some friends.'

    She had five years. Five short years to find allies and to travel to a small town named
    Glendale in the new world.

    She began to wheel out of her room, then slowly stepped out of the chair should should
    never have been able to walk out of again, and took a few steps.

    Fur as white as snow covered her entire body, her white hair becoming just fur leaving her
    bald as her round ears moved to the top of her head. Her eyes a brownish red. Her hands
    and feet became thick paws identical. Her face twisted into a muzzle as her teeth became
    dedicated for tearing apart meant. Look near identical to any other lioness.

    A plum tail snaked out of her dress even as it dissolved around her like the wheel chair
    behind her. Naked except for her fur. The lioness walked down the hall hunching over and
    waving at the other residents who waved back.

    Then her hips and shoulders rearranged, and she calmly got on all fours, her body suited
    for the task perfectly. Tribal tattoos appeared on her 'shoulders' and hind quarters. And
    she grew, becoming twice the size of any mundane lioness. There was no grayness in her
    fur. And her body was built to last.

    "Excuse me." She said calmly to two senior citizens playing cards who were in her way.
    They quickly but calmly got of the way for her.

    "Good day Mother Glen." Said the nurse at the front counter nodding at the beast walk
    right past her.

    "Good day dearie." Replied Mother Glen.

    No one screamed or ran or called animal control as Mother Glen left the nursing home on
    her own. She was after all the eldest of the Pride like Mr. P was the eldest of the Pack.
