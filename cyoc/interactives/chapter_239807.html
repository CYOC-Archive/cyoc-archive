    Curiosity killed the...raccoon?

    Jennifer shook her head, she must have been dreaming still she thought as she squeezed her eyes shut.

    After a few moments Jennifer slowly peaked out to see if anything at changed, nothing obviously did. Sighing Jennifer looked at her closet for clothes. While she didn't feel naked with her fur, given her mom's reaction, people still wore clothes. All of her outfits had changed, most of her clothes seemed loose and airy. She had a few heavier bits of clothing but it seemed only for the winter months. Jennifer held out a pair of panties in front of her, just below the waistband in the back there was a lining hole meant to fit her tail, the same design were on all her pants as well, except for the skirts and dresses. The biggest change was to her socks and shoes, in short, she only had two pairs of shoes, both seemed meant for snow. Her socks were all toeless, either as stirrup thigh socks or cut crew socks. Even her leggings and stockings had the design. Looking at her paws Jennifer could see how she couldn't exactly wear normal shoes.

    Jennifer decided on a dark blouse, a skirt and thigh socks, hoping to be a bit more modest. She could understand why fur would cause more skimpy clothing. Her fur pulled and shifted against her clothes, it was uncomfortable, no way anyone could wear anything tight she guessed.

    Grabbing her bag Jennifer left her room as she went to grab breakfast. A pair of boiled eggs and a cup of blueberries. Jennifer wondered wear the eggs came from, she guessed maybe like the news the herbivores and fish were still animals and not people, or maybe there's some farm out there of chicken people. Jennifer giggled at the thought as she ate.

    Jennifer winced as she accidently bit her tongue. She wasn't used to her new mouth, it felt completely alien to her. Running her tongue over her teeth served to show just how different a snout was to a humans mouth. Smacking her lips Jennifer finished breakfast, then hugged her mom as she left for the school-bus. Jennifer was curious, even a little excited to see how the school had changed, it was a new world, it felt like an adventure.

    The bus pulled up infront of Jennifer, as the door swung open she noticed the driver, yesterday he was a burly man, now a burly bear. Jennifer slowly stepped up the bus, using the rail to pull herself up, still unconfident with her feet. She could feel the ribbed rubber of the bus floor on her paws, though muffled through her pads.

    The bus was filled with a menagerie of animals, it didn't seem the town had a dominate species, lions, leopards, different breeds of dog and cats, ferrets and bears, all in varying colors from white, brown or black, a few foxes added a splash of color from the natural sauve pallet.

    As the bus arrived at the school, Jennifer watched out a window of the changes to the world, people carried out their lives as if nothing had changed at all. Jennifer stepped down off the bus, a line of students entering the the building. The sound of wings caused Jennifer to look up surprised. Above her a few bird people swooped down to land near the door before entering the building. She was amazing at the range of different body shapes, some people were really tall, others like Jennifer quite short. Jennifer stared at a nearby kestrel girl, her arms were instead giant wings, she didn't have any hands. Curious to see how she could open the door Jennifer noticed that the school doors had two handles, one for hands, the other below that anyone could use their feet to open it, the bird girl extended her talon as she pulled the door.

    Jennifer entered the school, the crowd caushing everyone to push together into the hallway. Jennifer could smell so much better now, and the blend of everyone's animalistic scents filled her head. She blushed as she noticed how she must of looked, her nose pointed upwards as she sniffed around.

    The bell began to ring as the crowds began to thin out, everyone going to their respectful classes. Following along Jennifer entered her first class.
