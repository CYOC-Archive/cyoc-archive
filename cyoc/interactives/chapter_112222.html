    The lone wolf and his pack

    "Well, " said Nate, blushing and a little embarrassed, " the truth is I was just planning on running around the woods, maybe doing a little hunting."

    "You were gonna kill some cute little animals? Like deer and rabbits?" asked Alice, peering at him with sad puppy dog eyes.

    Nate blushed even brighter.

    "Nah, just chase them around a little," he lied.

    In truth, he had planned on killing them, testing his new strength and claws by tearing them apart and devouring them. They were just animals, after all. And even humans ate them, despite how cute they looked. But he didn't know how to explain the circle of life to his little sister, especially when she looked at him with those innocent doe-like eyes.

    "Whatever, that's fine, " said Mandy dismissively as she grabbed her keys. "Let's go."

    "What?" asked Nate with some confusion.

    "Did I stutter? I said 'Let's go.' If you are going to do your wolf shit we need to leave now. We don't have much time," said Mandy impatiently.

    "We?" asked Nate, still confused. Then suddenly it clicked.

    "No way! You guys can't come. I'm a lone wolf. You will ruin all my fun, " whined Nate.

    Alice giggled.

    "You sound just like a dog when you whine like that," she said happily.

    Mandy ignored her and focused her wrath on Nate, rebuking his protest.

    "If you think I am going to let you go out of the house like that unsupervised you are crazy. You might get shot or even killed. Or what if someone catches you and locks you up? How would we explain it to everyone when you changed back the next day? I promised Mom and Dad I was going to watch over you this week and I mean to keep my word. If you want to go out tonight you have to take us with you, " said Mandy.

    Nate stood up the his full height and glared down at Mandy menacinigly. "And what if I refuse? How are you going to stop me?"

    Mandy's gaze never wavered and she stared back at Nate without fear. Suddenly her hand shot forward and she gripped his exposed testicles, squeezing them sharply.

    "Don't take that tone with me. I am still the one in charge and don't you forget it. I am older and wiser. And I have handled much bigger and tougher guys than you. So unless you want to spend the night in your room nursing some sore nuts, " said Mandy, squeezing his testicles tighter for emphasis and causing him to moan in pain, " you better do what I say."

    "Fine, fine, you guys can come. Just let go!" said Nate with exasperation.

    Mandy released her tight grip and Nate sighed with relief. "Good. I am glad you are being reasonable. Now if you behave and follow my instructions we can all have a good time tonight."

    Alice had been watching the tense exchange with rapt attention, waiting for the victor to emerge. Now that it was over, she was ready to celebrate.

    "Yay! Now we can all have some fun! Bite me Nate so I can be a werewolf, too! I want to run around the woods and chase animals with you!" she said, holding up her forearm to Nate.

    Mandy quickly pushed her back away from him.

    "Now, now. None of that. No one is biting anyone tonight, " said Mandy a little nervously. Nate was reasonable, but a feral young Alice might be more difficult to control.

    Nate sighed. "It doesn't work like that anyway. My bite isn't infectious. You have to drink the potion to change and I used it all up."

    "Oh pooh, " said Alice as she pouted.

    "Does she really have to come with us?" asked Nate with exasperation.

    "Hey!" shouted Alice with indignation. "That's not nice. You can't leave me."

    "She's right, " said Mandy. "I can't leave her home alone. She is too young."

    Nate sighed, his face crestfallen. His whole night was ruined. He was going to be stuck with his sisters all night. What fun could that be? He was supposed to be the lone wolf, running wild and free. Now he was stuck with a pack, a pack comprised solely of his annoying sisters.

    Mandy recognized the look on his face and tried to comfort him.

    "Relax, Nate. We aren't going to rain on your parade. I'll let you do what you wanted to do - running around the woods or whatever. But I just want to keep an eye on you, to help you keep out of trouble. I worry because sometimes it doesn't seem like you think things through. I mean, how did you plan on getting to the woods? Were you just going to run through the neighborhood like that? You are certainly too big to drive. And even if you could, a werewolf driving a car would draw an awful lot of attention. You should be thanking me for offering to drive you."

    Nate suddenly realized he hadn't really thought about that. He was too excited about his transformation to do much planning at all. Maybe she was right. But he couldn't admit that to his smug sister.

    "Whatever. Let's just go," said Nate sullenly, thinking maybe he could lose them when he got to the woods.

    "Yay!" screamed Alice at the top of her lungs as she ran out the door. "Camping trip!"

    Mandy chased after her. "Not just yet, young lady. First we need to pack some things. Like your coat. It's cold out there and I don't want you getting sick."

    Nate ran one hairy palm down his face and grumbled, fearing that this adventure was not going to be as much fun as he imagined.
