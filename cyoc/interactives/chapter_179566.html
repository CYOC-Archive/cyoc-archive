    “You’re worried about your cousin testing positive, huh?” Antonio supposed, “Come on, there’s nothing to worry about, Ryan. You should know that by now.”

    “What if it happens though?” Ryan asked, “What if she can’t walk anymore? What if she loses her mind to bouncing forever? She could end up too big to function, and you directed this thing so I know you know it could happen!”

    “That’s only one in about a hundred cases where they get that big,” Antonio reasoned, “And so what if she loses the ability to walk? Most bouncerbutts hate walking anyway.”

    “Walking no fun as boingy, teehee,” Francesca giggled, “I glad I no gotta do that anymore! Bouncy way better way to travel!”

    “What about that detective we interviewed though, Fran?” Ryan asked, “She’s a bouncerbutt just like you and she can walk on her own two feet just fine.”

    Ryan stood up, saying, “Well at least she could talk like a normal person and not like some kind of-“

    “You no say it!” Francesca warned, “You say it then you just as bad as the bullies at the station!”

    “Would you two just calm down?” Antonio snapped, “This is exactly the kind of thing that made me want to make this documentary in the first place. Bouncerbabble is nothing to be ashamed about, Fran. It’s just how your brain works while you’re on your bouncy heinie, right?”

    “I no help it,” Francesca shook her head, “and it no from lack of trying. My mouth just no wanna talk properly like everyone else. I hate that no one take me seriously just because of my stupid speech impediment, but I no wanna stop bouncy. Bouncerbutts who no bounce make me sad. I no able to stand by myself and I okay with that, you okay with that, Mama and Papa okay with that… but the rest of the world see me as a freak.” Her giddy demeanor fell away as tears began to well up in her eyes. Bouncerbutts are always so happy and giggly most of the time, and oftentimes people forget that they can feel any other emotions. Sometimes of course, a bouncerbutt can feel sad, angry, scared, disgusted, and even some combination of emotions. This is because bouncerbutts, behind the veneer of airheaded giggling and the space hopper posteriors, they’re only human.

    “Most of us bouncerbutts literally no can walk anymore,” Francesca continued, “and most of the ones who can choose to listen to their booties and not the hateful masses who think boingy is something to be ashamed of! We no help who we are, or what our bodies need, but those… pendejos who run the world think that something wrong with us! They think we sick with a disease… that we are a disease. I NO DISEASE! Antonio simply slowly stood up, and gave his dear older sister a hug. “Está bien, hermana. Todo saldrá bien,” he said as he stroked her back, “Everything’s gonna be okay.”

    “Man,” Ryan said finally, “I… didn’t know you felt that way.”

    “This oppression needs to end,” Antonio stated, “My documentary is just the start. Hopefully, someone will watch it and be moved to end this plague of ignorance. Just because they’re different should be no excuse. I can’t see the people I love be treated so horribly anymore.”
