    There have been murmurs and rumors about a bizarre website found among the Dark Web of the internet, a strange oddity that appears to appeal to a particular group or fandom; certain deviants. They say the website allows its user to peruse and explore some very kinky and disturbing beastly transformations upon "willing" or "unwilling" participants. Other than hearing about watching videos or "livestreams" of these participants turning into various animals based on your decisions, you don't know much more than that. Perhaps you didn't seek it out right away because out of safety from diving into the nightmare of the dark web. Maybe you didn't have it in you to watch people transformed against their will. Maybe you figured a site like that can't be THAT impressive, most likely just a archive of random amateur home-made videos you could probably fine on some random porn site, costume and make-up effects probably from cheap emporiums and Halloween outlets.

    But something about tonight...something finally peeked your interest. Theme's like this; watching people turn into various animals and were-creatures have been a secret kink of yours. You have pretty much devoured a treasure trove of transformation media over the years, for good or bad, only to have finally hit the bottom of the barrel. And it's becoming hit or miss on when or what new types of art, story, or video's will be posted that meets your particular desires.

    Something stirs, reminding you of this website. You start searching almost madly for it, though you think it will be a worthless effort seeing as none of these rumors ever mentioned the website by name.

    But; not only 10 minutes in your search, this one name catches your eye and before you realize it, before you can even use your virus scanner to double check this isn't a scam or Trojan infestation, you've clicked it.

    The site your found is called “Choose Their Metamorphoses.” It advertises itself exactly as you've heard, the front page's background littered with screenshots of various individuals undergoing horrifying, or erotic, transformations into various different animals and creatures. And staged as some images appear to be...the costume designs (or digital manipulations) are actually incredibly decent...and attractive.

    You click pass the Summary and History sections, eager to check out the variety of themes they claim to have. And there is a long list, popular among them being mammals like wolves and tigers and foxes. But even less popular animals are available, ranging from assortments of birds and lizards, even insects. It even has options for fantastic and mythological creatures too, like Griffins and Trolls and; of course, Dragons. There is no way the people and actors behind this site can cater to this astounding selection and have all the acting and cosmetics and effects pay-off, can they?

    As you browse through the list, the website pings and suddenly flashes a pop-up on your screen. Some sort of Disclaimer...or warning? It tells you that; while every participant in our CYOA will be kept as safe from real harm as possible, the site does not guarantee the safety of those around said participants. It also suggestions that; while players are encouraged to chose YOUR favorite choices, players consider the consequences of them before pursuing. There is also something about "side-effects vary based on the result of your choices" but the site doesn't make that very clear exactly what those are.

    Reluctant or confident, or just morbidly curious; you click on the pop-up's selection of understanding the disclaimer. And before you are allowed to browse the list once more, the site pings and pulls up another pop-up with a simple inquiry.

    What do you do now?
