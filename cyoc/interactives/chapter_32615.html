In addition to this, the last transformation it does becomes permanent and no amount of magic or magical items can undo it. Now you're stuck that way..."

    "It's alright, Catherine, just lay down for your nap and I'll take care of everything, don't you worry your precious, little head," said Karen pulling her brother closer in a tight hug. "You can even take it here in my room."

    'A nap does sound like a good idea,' thought Dylan as his sister wiped away his tears.

Next came telling her father what happened, but surprisingly, he was just fine with the idea since Dylan was more of a trouble-maker than he cared to admit. The only issue he had with the transformation was who was going to keep an eye on their new six-year-old child, but Karen hastily assured him that she'd be more than happy to keep an eye on her little sister.


    Waking up, Dylan momentarily forgot about what happened as he rolled off his sister's bed and it wasn't until he looked down at his tiny feet that no longer hit the floor that he remembered what happened. Instantly he began to cry again, causing Karen to run across the hall to her room.

    "What's wrong, Catherine?" she asked with a bewildered but amused look across her face.

    "What do you think is the problem?" he said angrily.

    "Oh, I know," responded Karen snapping her fingers and grabbing hold of his tiny hand, pulling him back into his room.

    "What are they doing here?" asked Dylan seeing Karen's two best friends standing in his room and going through four large bags filled with little girl's outfits and two large bags filled with room decorations and three cans of light pastel pink paint.

    "Your big sister invited us over to help her with all the preparations you're going to need," said Megan while Karen and Hannah chuckled.
