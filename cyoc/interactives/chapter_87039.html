    2. Take these ingredients to Lake Moor, fill a flask with water from the lake, and mix all the ingredients together.
    3. Take the flask to the stone altar near the lake, chant the phrase "Open the walls and let the power come through", and drink the concoction.
    4. Enter the water and embrace your change."

    Cassia thought the letter was nonsense, and decided a good night's sleep would help her feel better. Yet, Cassia could not sleep, not even one minute. She got up and looked at herself in the mirror. She was growing to be an attractive, young woman but what did that matter in this land? Beauty didn't win wars, it didn't end conflicts, and it didn't make men less cruel and thoughtless. Father would either match him to some greedy, spoiled prince who she hated, or keep him all to himself. Either way, she would have no power to do anything, other than to watch Kasergrov fall into irreparable ruin. Cassia got angry. She refused to watch her beloved homeland fall because her father was a spoiled child. She had to do do something, to try.

    At that point, Cassia decided to follow the letter's instructions. Even if it didn't work out, it would allow her to focus her mind on something other than her father. Cassia, sneaking past the guards, went into the mage's den and got everything but the wireweed. She briskly went out into her garden, and retrieved two stalks of the weed. She then decided to make the trek to the lake with the ingredients, a flask, and a bowl.

    As Cassia walked, she started to get nervous. What would happen to her? Would she die? Would she remain human? Would she become an animal? A man? Cassia had no idea what to expect, and the short hike to the lake itself didn't help matters. Cassia took the bowl, and mashed the ingredients together. She then filled the flask, poured the ingredients in, and walked to the massive stone altar. It had been there for centuries, and no one in the kingdom had any idea who built it or for what purpose. Cassia supposed she would find out.

    Before she stepped on to the altar, Cassia took one last look at her reflection. She wasn't sure what would happen, but she knew for certain it would be the last time she would ever be a naive, young princess. Cassia nervously walked onto the altar. Struggling to get the words out, Cassia uttered the phrase "Open the walls and let the power come through." She then stood there for a minute, pondering all that was to come. Her life was about to change forever, and she had no way to prepare. But, with all the courage she had, Cassia drank the liquid mix, nasty as it was, and walked to the water.
