    1675, the woods outside the praying town that would merge into Glendale. Griselle who
    would become Gomorrah was alone in the forest, enjoying her alone time as she always
    did. She and her sisters had become famous for their generosity in the praying town by
    now. And has a reputation in Glendale for being 'a little strange' but nothing that would
    draw negative attention to them, they were careful to avoid that. They needed the children
    of Glendale to trust them after all.

    Sadly they were still struggling to find a perfect way of avoiding adulthood. Their parents
    and both grandparents had never thought to teach their girls a thing about alchemy, and
    thus none of the girls had a clue that alchemists for centuries had been trying to perfect
    precisely what the girls were trying to do, nearly all met with failure, most of the rest met
    with death, the precious one maybe two though out all of human history that did
    succeed... they wisely kept to themselves.

    Gita was now taking classes for Confirmation with Goldwyn close behind. She wandered
    without giving it one thought today, she had her power, she had nothing to fear from some
    predator or scared prey (the little girl not giving a thought that most predators as a rule
    weren't noticed by their prey until the moment their fangs were in their meal's neck).

    Griselle then saw it, a sharp shooter alone, testing out a new gun of his... she saw him fire
    at a crow. She felt it, FELT IT get hit! She knew it was just an animal. She knew it had
    nothing to do with her. But that crow... she knew it, SHE KNEW IT!

    Griselle tapped into her magic, via the infinite chaos... the sharp shooters gun fell to the
    ground, not going off since it only had one shot in it. His clothes fell into themselves. What
    emerged from his tent of clothes a second later was a close proximity to a fairy-tales
    looking fairy. Naked, with a pair of bright purple, impossible butterfly wings, looking to be
    the age of a toddler, the size of a doll, and now female, and hair that was bright pink and
    a pair of antenna from her forehead. The newly created fairy looked around with solid gold
    eyes with no white or centers, feeling very confused.

    Feeling VERY DIZZY Grissle wandered over to where the crow was, bleeding. The world
    turned twins and out of focus as Grissle's sudden and reckless use of magic left her nearly
    drunk as a side effect.

    She fell to her knees next to the baby crow, looking like it had been abandoned by it's
    family. (Or, more likely if Grissle had known nature as well as her father Running-Bear,
    tossed out of it's nest by it's own nest mates with their mother watching on to see which
    one was more worth her time to raise). Grissle figured the crow's other family had been
    killed by hunters like the fairy had been a second ago.

    She touched the crow, and saw it was still breathing, and bleeding. His breath was shallow.
    It looked at her with eyes that knew.

    "I... I know you... you are... you are... G... you're the one I was waiting for..."

    "You're the crow I saw! I know you are! From my dream! So .. so long ago.. BUT I
    REMEMBER! It is you!" Grissle took the crow and brought it to her chest. And once again
    tapped in the infinite chaos, healing him, a task she hadn't used all that often, usually just
    turning people into creatures, animals, or objects rather than repairing their broken
    bodies. But she forced the infinite chaos through her, and into the crow.

    "You're lucky... yes, Lucky Crow, that is what I'll call you." None of the girls had figured out
    their special friends real names yet. But as the chaos flowed through her and into the bird...
    it hit her, like a blow to the head it hit her... As the crow tittered on life, and she touched it
    on that edge of the cliff between life and death.

    "You're name... your name.... it is Solome!"

    And it was like fate itself was tied them together with a string at the hearts. And both felt
    everything the other felt, and could feel everything the other could feel.

    "Yes... my mistress... it is Solome..." The male crow said.

    "But.. you're a boy..."

    "Yes mistress."

    "I didn't know girl witches could have boy familiars."

    "There is no rule against it my master..." Said Lucky/Solome as he tested his repaired
    muscles in his master's hands.

    Meanwhile, the fairy that had been the hunter that had shoot Lucky flew next to the girl,
    "Er, excuse me, I don't mean to interrupt a witch and their familiar but... I think I was in
    the middle of testing something someone I really love gave me as a gift.. but I can't
    remember what it is now... I can't seem to remember my name either... and... I think this
    place is called Glendale, or the place near it is... but I can't seem to remember if I live here
    or not... can you help me?"
