I mean, from what I'd heard, this place had been sitting here abandoned for at least a couple of years, if not longer. I mean, you did a nice job fixing those boats and the dock up, but they've been sitting here a long time."

    There's another moment of silence as you turn the beer can over in your hands. "I don't know. George hasn't heard from him in a long time, either. I think they're worried, but George said that he would just go off and disappear sometimes. So this isn't too unusual."

    Rani sighs. "Well, if you're not worried..."

    A sharp whistle cuts the afternoon air. You both turn to see Colby and a slightly singed George standing next to a roaring campfire. George starts waving for the two of you to come over. "Fire's ready! Let's eat!"



    The four of you have a traditional campfire dinner of hot dogs and s'mores. At George's behest, you manage to eat an entire package of hot dogs on your own. Colby merely shakes his head as you snarf down eight of the dogs, buns and all. Rani finds the whole thing very amusing. You finish off your meal with at least four s'mores, indulging in your father's sweet tooth.

    Soon, the sun starts to dip low in the horizon, setting the sky above the emerald lake ablaze into a show of oranges, pinks, and reds. The stars begin to come out, like tiny holes pin-pricked into the sheet of the sky. The air is abuzz with the droning of frogs, insects, and the crackling of your fire's dying embers.

    Colby and Rani push one of the repaired boats out onto the lake. They paddle about as you and George stay behind, on your backs, half naked, looking up at the darkening sky.

    George scratches his hairy face with an equally hairy hand. "Not long now, eh?"

The best part was it was all temporary. In a few weeks, you would be turning back into yourself again and be at home. "Yeah, I know."

    Then you see it. "Well, I'll be damned."

    George grins and chuckles. "Well, hell, if we had bet on this I'd be winning right now."

    You sigh and shake your head. George nudges you with a large, furry arm. "What?"

    You shrug. "It's like you said, George. We can't stay here forever."
