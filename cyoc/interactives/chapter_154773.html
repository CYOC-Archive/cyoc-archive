    The Great Barrier of Mermaid Island

    You dive under the surface of the lagoon, with the lovely pink-tailed mermaid Yasmin holding your hand. As you swim through the water together, your tail-fins undulating up and down, shafts of sunlight cut through the waves and reflect off of your glittering scales, green from you and pink from her. She's really beautiful, and she isn't shy about touching you, or cuddling up close to get your attention and point out some new and interesting underwater feature or example of sea-life. You're pretty sure that you're blushing now, and Yasmin is definitely giving you flirtatious smiles and other signals, like playing with her hair.

    You keep up a good pace behind Alex and Sally, and it's the same for them: the red-orange and golden-yellow of the two mermaids' tails makes them look like quite the attractive, tropical pair. At one point, you see Sally boldly reach out and brush Alex's hair out of the way so that her chest is more fully exposed. Oh, yeah, you're sure of it: Sally is definitely flirting too.

    Eventually, the four of you come to a spot in the ocean where a row of white shells and rocks forms a line in the sandy seabed, and extends off in either direction as far as you can see. "This is it," says Sally to Alex and to you. "The border-line. Some of the other girls have had centuries to make sure they're marking the right spot, and we have all the time we could ever need to keep the markers maintained."

    "It's an almost perfect circle around the island," adds Yasmin. "Approximately seven nautical miles from the center of the volcano, as near as any of us can figure."

    "What's it for?" you ask.

    "It's to tell us where the Barrier is," says Sally. "Never, ever try to swim across the barrier."

    "Fish and birds can come and go as they please," says Yasmin. "But we mermaids can't."

    Alex gets a shiver from their deadly-serious tone. "What happens if you try?"

    "It ain't pretty," says Sally. "Just trust us on this one."

    "No," says Yasmin. "It's better if I show them." She then swims down to the sea-floor, very careful to stay on the inside of the markers, and sifts through the sand until she comes away with a sea-shell. She cracks the shell in half and uses the sharp edge to cut several locks of hair from her head. Then, steeling her courage, she drifts up to the row of markers and casts the handful of hair over the line, quickly drawing her hand back immediately after.

    You and Alex watch in fascination and horror as the mermaid's hair floats across the line of marker-stones, CATCHES FIRE under water, and burns away to ash.

    "Oh," says Alex.

    "Yeah," says Sally. "So we stay near the fuckin' island."

    "Duly noted," you say.

    "But, wait," says Yasmin. She looks at you for a moment, clearly deep in thought; and then she holds the shell up to your head and asks, "May I?"

    "What, my hair?" You ask. (Yasmin nods.) "Okay."

    She cuts away some of your hair and tries the same thing again, letting it drift across the Barrier. And, as before, the strands are summarily immolated the moment they pass over the stones. "Damn," says Yasmin, annoyed and disappointed. "I had thought, maybe, because you're a merman—"

    "You thought that because I was already breaking one rule, maybe I'd break this one too," you reason.

    "Yeah," says Yasmin sadly.

    "It's okay, girl," says Sally. "It was a good thought. But if we all gotta be stuck here for eternity, and least we're stuck with each other."

    "So," says Yasmin, regaining her composure. (You get the feeling that if you'd all been above water, there would be tears in her eyes right now.) "The rules. You've just seen Rule Number One: never cross the Barrier. We can't leave the island, so don't try."

    "Rule Number Two," says Sally. "All the mermaids agreed long ago, we're happier if we just accept our fate and try to live our lives here as best we can. So before any new girls—or guys—can be accepted into the fold, we have a little ritual. You gotta tell us what year it is on the outside, and how you died, so you can put it behind you and forget about your old life."

    You and Alex look at each other. What do you do? You're certain that whatever you decide, Alex will play along. Your cousin was always good at that, improvising and playing your partner-in-mischief. But do you tell the truth about how you became mer-people and arrived on the island, or do you make something up?
