This then meant that over the summer as Juliet's Chateaux and Vineyard area were set up to be France's Animalian colony, this meant that a part of the Chateaux was to be set aside as a makeshift hospital and conversion area. It took up a few rooms and included one bathroom that was then made "separate" from many of the other rooms, but it was something that ultimately worked well enough.

    "You're certain this will all work?" Juliet asked to one of the scientists and doctors that had come along.

    "Yes, and to a degree, necessary... at least in the short term," the doctor answered, "while Animalians have many Human features, they also, obviously, have many animal features and at times can be vulnerable to many of the things one would expect to find in veterinary medicine rather than just in Human medicine.     "And France's legal requirements?" Juliet asked.

    "We're reading through them as best we can, and we thank you for your help in the translations," the doctor answered, "we can then amend our policies to work with French Law here just as we work to comply with US Law in Dinotopia Aquatica, Dino City, and Animalia. And in time, with our own training program and offers, we can then spread that expertise to local physicians here in France so that your Chateaux won't be the sole focus of all activities related to the colony. You'd thus be better integrated with the community."

    Juliet slowly nodded at the time. She might have her own private worries, particularly given that the government had wanted something like the Meat Market constructed and she'd had to sacrifice one of her Vineyard's old wine cellars to that enterprise, but there were other things that were of concern and things that couldn't be avoided. So far, the people from Horizon had proven themselves respectful and trustworthy and for the moment, she had no reason not to trust the Horizon staff.
