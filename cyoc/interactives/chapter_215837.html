But Jeff is someone who never gives up, he hopes that Ken becomes reckless and make a mistake, that is when Jeff will seek help… at least that is what Jeff is hoping for.

    After greeting his friends, Ken leads them inside the “house” and into the living room where the others are waiting.

    After everyone greeted each other, Ken leads them into the dining hall where he had set up a big table, loaded with all the goods he prepared. “Please,” he says, “help yourselves, there is plenty more in the kitchen,” and smiles when the guests start tucking in.

    “Man,” Ken’s brother says, “this tastes amazing!”

    “I agree,” Ken’s mother says, “I didn’t know you were this good of a cook!”

    Ken grins, “oh, I am full of surprises, mom,” he says. The teen is about to join everyone, when the door bell suddenly rings, “I wonder who that could be,” he mumbles, he then turns to the others, “be right back,” and heads to open the door.

    When Ken opens the door, he sees a group of 6 kids between 10 and 14 and some grown ups, “yes,” Ken asks.

    “Sorry for disturbing,” a man says, “we are your new neighbors and just wanted to come over and say “hi”,” he says.

    Ken smiles, “it is nice meeting you,” he says, “I am just throwing a little party, you are welcome to join”.

    The group looks at each other, “thanks,” a woman says, “but we really do not want to intrude”.

    “Besides,” a man adds, “we saw the people arriving and noticed that they were… well… not as wealthy looking”.

    Ken understands, he chuckles, “oh, you think they’ll have a problem if some rich folks suddenly join?”

    “Yeah,” they all say.

    Ken chuckles, “don’t worry,” he says, “they are all cool. Your kids can have some fun with my brother who is 10 and his friend who is 12. I promise, they have no issues with rich kids, plus, I have an entertainment room in the basement”.

    The kids looks at Ken, “b-but we have no costumes,” a boy with brown hair says.

    Ken looks at the boy, “doesn’t matter,” he says, “you can still have fun, even without a costume,” he smiles.

    The kids eyes light up, they look up at their parents, “pleeeeeease?!”

    After a while of silence the grown ups shrug, “we can at least give it a try,” a man says.

    “Great,” Ken says, smiling, “please, come in,” he then leads the group into the dining hall. Ken gets everyone’s attention by clearing his throat, “guys, these are neighbors of mine, who came by to welcome me into the neighborhood”.

    “Hello,” they all say awkwardly.

    Jeff’s and Ken’s parents, friends and siblings all smile, “a pleasure to meet you,” they say, “please, join us,” Ken’s dad says.

    The group sits down, “please,” Ken says, “help yourselves to some food,” the kids waste no time and start piling their plates.

    “Boys,” one of the men says, “behave yourselves!”

    “S-sorry,” the boys say, “it… it just smells so damn good!”

    Everyone chuckles, “hehe, looks like every kid is the same,” Ken’s mother says, “no matter if rich or not: can’t wait to tuck into something delicious!”

    After a while the grown up start to talk about grown up stuff, so it doesn’t come as a surprise that Ken, his friends and the kids all start to get bored.

    Ken clears his throat, “sorry to interrupt,” he says, “I just wanted to suggest that we youngsters head down into the entertainment room, while you grown ups discuss… well… grown up stuff”.

    The grown ups all chuckles, “sure, have fun boys,” they say.

    So Ken leads the group of teens and kids down into the basement, smirking inwardly, ‘hehe… they will not be the same once I am done with them, and with a little reality alteration… most of the parents will leave without their kids,’ he thinks to himself.
