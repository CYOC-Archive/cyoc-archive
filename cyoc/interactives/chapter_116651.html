    Jack walked towards one of the many different hallways, this one in particular seemed to be directed towards a medical center. He could see a couple makeshift gurneys against the walls, along with a few other makeshift areas. Not seeing much else he continued down the hallway, until he came across a boarded up room.

    *Quarantine*

    He cautiously approached the door. There was no noise that could be heard from this side of the door, so Jack hesitantly raised one hand and knocked. At first there was nothing, but then the door rattled and a voice cried out from inside.

    "Hello! Hello! Is anyone out there? Please you have to help us, we locked ourselves in here to escape the satyrs." It was a boys voice, probably someone in their mid-teens.

    Jack was apprehensive, after all why would the door be boarded up if they locked themselves inside? Still, if there was someone inside there, he couldn't very well leave them to starve. Right?

    "Why is the door boarded up then?" He asked, airing on the side of caution.

    The boy responded through the door. "Some of the police officers did that before they left, it was the only thing besides the measly lock keeping the Satyrs out. Please you've got to help us!" The boy sounded urgent.

    Seeing no more reasons to stall, Jack gave his answer. "Alright then, give me a moment I need to find something to open the door with."

    The ground was bare, and there was nothing nearby that could be used. So, he branched out, looking inside the other rooms that were part of the medical center. Eventually he found a turned over satchel, covered in some dried fluid. Not thinking about what it could be, Jack reached down and looked inside. Inside he found a small pocket knife, it was rusting but still useable.

    Turning around Jack went ahead and returned to the door. "One moment, I'll have you guys out of there soon!" He only hoped they heard him, because he got no response.

    Using the pocket knife and his strength, Jack pried the nails out of the boards. Thankfully, they were a rush job and the nails only loosely remained in their spots to begin with, so, they came out with ease. After carefully pulling down each and every board, eventually Jack stood before the unblocked door.
