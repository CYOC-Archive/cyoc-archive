    “…don’t see why you’re so hung up on Eric. He’s like the weirdest guy I’ve met. Brian’s been asking how you’re doing, why don’y you go back to seeing him?” Dana Sanders asked.

    “Because I’m still grounded until January. My mom won’t let me out of her sight. And meanwhile Dad’s got his hands full with some work issues. I don’t want to end up sushi because I snuck out for one night with him.” Julie sniffed.

    “So should I tell him you said hi…?”

    “Sure Dana. And while you’re at it, tell Brian that every night before I go to bed I kiss a life sized cardboard cutout of him.”

When she looked up and saw Eric was still alone in his corner, obviously bent out of shape. Against her better judgement she decided to join him. She could feel every pair of eyes in the room following her. And what’s worse, she knew Julie’s were just about burning a hole in the middle of her back.

    “Don’t think too much of it. Julie’s just being… Julie.” she said.

Julie was the first person I really connected with after moving here. But if she’s only after me for, you know, then I don’t want her in the same room as me let alone sitting at the same table.” Eric said in one long drawn out breath.

    Before Frieda could offer any sort of advice, the bel rang. Eric gave her a quick thank you before darting into the sea of faces. Frieda was about to merge with the oncoming traffic as well when someone yanked her into the girls’ bathroom. And when Frieda turned around, she found herself face to face with a visibly annoyed Julie.
