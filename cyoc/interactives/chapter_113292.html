    A Month Later

    Colin walked into the enormous glass rotating doors of the new mall. The new mall had just opened and was reckoned to be having opening day prices that were way cut. He looked around, taking in the view from the glass skylight 3 floors above. The mall was probably the biggest building in the city so far. Colin walked up to a map and looked for a food court. The old mall had had a fire recently, so Colin's only experiment with crowded people had been with the little boy selling cookies. The new mall, in Colin's opinion, was finished at the perfect time.
    Colin wandered around, eventually getting to the new food court. It was immense, and it was surrounded by a lot of shops. Most of them were clothes stores, but others were selling video games, phones, or jewelry. Colin, with his endless supply of money, was in heaven.
    Colin surveyed the people in the food court. So far, he was one of the only people there, so he would have to play around with people later. Right now, he would go shopping.
    One of the reasons Colin was still the same, a 14-year old, was because he was unsure if he could change back. Colin hadn't known if the changes he made were permanent, and he didn't want to find out on himself. So he remained in high school, a year younger than his sister. Suddenly, a voice shook Colin from his thoughts.
    "Hello, magician, sir," a young voice said from behind Colin's back. Colin spun around and saw the boy from the previous food court standing there. He was back to normal, even the mischievous smile.
    "You turned back!" Colin exclaimed.
    The boy nodded. "Of course. But... I kinda want to be a girl permanently."
    "What do you mean?" Colin asked.
    "Well, I decided I liked being a girl better. I also kinda want to be a bit older this time, too. I'll do something in exchange for you, too."
    "Okay..." Colin considered this. Then he had an idea. "How about this. I make it so we are both 16, and then you can pick out clothes from the store over there. You will turn into a girl to fit those clothes. But you will have to be my girlfriend."
    "Okay!" the boy exclaimed, seemingly overjoyed.
    Colin, decision made for him, said, "Are we both 16?"
    Time seemed to stop as everything in the room froze, said "Yes," and resumed what they were doing. Then Colin felt himself getting taller, gradually stopping. Colin looked at his reflection in the recently polished table, and he saw himself. He looked basically the same, just a tiny bit older. The boy standing next to him also looked like an older version of himself.
    "Well, let's get to it! I want to be a girl!" He then took off into the nearest clothes store, while Colin muttered under his breath, "Is he going to turn into a girl when he puts on girl clothes, and is she going to be my girlfriend?"

    A few hours later, Colin was walking out of the mall with the boy in tow. The boy, whose name was Nathan, said that he wanted Colin to wait to get home until he saw the new girl, whose name was Natalie. For some reason, Nathan had been able to turn back into a boy when he put on his own clothes, so Colin hadn't even seen a glimpse of the girl who was to be his girlfriend.
    Eventually, they arrived at Colin's house and walked into Colin's room. Nathan walked into the bathroom and closed the door. A few minutes later, Nathan walked out.
    When Colin's bathroom door opened, a girl who looked a lot like Nathan had walked out. She had the same color hair, the same green eyes, and a feminized version of the smile. But that was where the similarities ended.
    While Nathan had seemed relatively tall, Natalie was about average height. She was thin, had big breasts and wide hips. She was basically Colin's perfect girl.
    "For some reason, I somehow knew that you would like this," she said.
