    soon... or maybe not... events with Kurse had left them with mixed emotions of the place...

    herself present for them.

    Kaylee's pups had. But Amy had a maturity beyond her years that was scary sometimes

    Mal'Akh meanwhile approached the Pack Manor, and found it neglected, smelling more like
    wolf than normal, and seemingly a mad house with broken windows and half eaten kills
    laying about.

    And then there were the pups running about like they were kings of the world, many
    looking downright dirty, other looks worse.

    Mal'Akh shivered when one tried to bite her tail put pulled it out of the way in time and the
    blond pup scurried off.

    Mal'Akh hesitantly stepped inside the entry way that looked like a disaster area of a child's
    play area, but so did every inch of the manor. "Hello?... Can someone help?... Please?"

    "Who is that?" Came a duo of voices. Walking perfectly in sync, speaking perfectly in sync,
    two silver lupman females walked into the living room and looked down at the leoman that
    had entered their home. "Who are you?"

    "My... my name is Mal'Akh, and, I--, I need help."

    "So do we." They said again together perfectly shrugging like mirror images of each other
    and gestured around the house and the pups running wild. A couple odd colored pups
    bumping heads getting the smaller one from playing with sharp objects.

    "I... I can see that..." Mal'Akh admitted.

    The Northern American Boxing Trees (considering themselves far superior the Mexican
    Fighting Tree) only had time to wonder what the fireball in the sky was until flames rained
    down from the sky onto them, the Boxing Trees mimed screaming in pain waving their
    branches madly, wondering why the witches had cursed them to be able to feel pain! Many
    wished they could uproot themselves to run away from the flaming fire breathing lizard
    monster, others in a desperate attempt to save themselves threw dirt on themselves only
    for more fire to come down on them.

    The Western Fighting Trees 'looked' confused at the strange pink and blue furred, clothed,
    short, and scrawny Leoman who ran at them holding a jar full of water, which she threw,
    and the water turned into flames as the jar broke and made contact with air and
    themselves. "I'm sorry! But I must pass!"

    The Eastern Fighting Trees knew at once they were doomed as the dragon flames ate
    through their bark like it was nothing as the human with the useless gun came to them and
    threw the jar of fire-water that broke on contact. So they did their duty the last,
    determined to grab the human take him into flaming death with them or break his
    branches and leave him to burn among them.

    The Southern Fighting Trees 'heard' the agony of their fellows and dropped their playing
    cards. Their saw immediately who would have won if they had called the other's clever
    bluff before the fireballs in a jar hit them.

    Marie meanwhile calculated the risk vs gain of running through the burning trees or letting
    the fire do the work first.
