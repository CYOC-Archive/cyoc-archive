    Sarah had just left with the maze game. Their annual family game night was tonight so she was in charge of getting a new one. when she saw the maze game she thought it was perfect. it only had the maze on the cover. the box was made of some type of fake animal hide.

    She arrived at her house to see her 2 daughters and son setting up. 17-year-old Nate was busily setting up the snacks. her 14-year-old daughters were helping him. "hey mom, got the game?" Lisa said. "yep it's called the maze," she said while she pulled it out of her bag.

    "cool leather box," Molly said as she felt the lid. they set the game on the table and opened it up. inside was an old portable computer. "well the box didn't say how old it was. we should see if it works. if not then I can return it in the morning" Sarah said.

    her husband was overseas treating people. this left her with time to do these games. pressing the power button the screen lit up. in green text, it explained the game. "so it's like a retro build it your self game thing?" Nate said. Sarah set the parameters.

    Parameters:
    The first to the exit wins.
    set mode: easy
    puzzles: on
    traps: on
    changes: Default
    maze room style: house interior

    after the rules were made she hit begin. in a bright flash, the game vanished. the 2 story house shifted. the dining room table grew and split into 4 identical tables. the chandelier grew to be touching the floor as the room expanded.

    its fixtures stretched into fence-like structures that reached the wall. the windows on the left and bottom of the room became doorways. Sarah was shocked at what she saw. "kids can you hear me" she tried to yell through the fence. she got a few muffled replies.

    "I can't understand you. look ill try to find a way to undo this" she said before turning to the new doorway. what had been an open entrance to the living room was now a dark red door. 'OK I set the exit as the front door. all I have to do is get there' she thought. opening the door she found it was not the living room it was...
