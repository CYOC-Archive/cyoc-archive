    Now the thing was that although a week after Michelles
    vanishing her parents had contacted the authorities
    the search was conducted in the wrong places.

    Michelle had told her mom and dad that she was going to
    be spending the long weekend camping at
    Blackbeards Beach and Campgrounds,within the small coastal
    town of Blackbeard.

    She'd gone there all right that Friday afternoon,but without telling anybody else had gone to the small
    Blackbeard Archives to see if there was any interesting
    local history.
    And there in the old newspaper collection from a century earlier
    the World War One era
    she'd found a few articles on a government run research facility
    about 15 to 20 miles further up the coast only accessible by
    walking a inland railbed from the 19th century or by beaching a
    supply boat at the isolated beach at the bottom of the
    tall cliffs on which the facility was perched.

    It appeared from the short paragraph in the 1917 Blackbeard Chronicle
    that there'd been a emergency when the supply ship had wrecked up the coast
    in a gale and the research staff had used the abandoned rail
    line to summon help.

    So Michelle had then sifted through the archives collection of
    yellowed nineteenth century maps,traced the location of the
    rail line
    the likely location of the old research facility near it and
    a few mines and mining camps near it.

    Likely now all crumbled to sawdust and cracked sagging
    old ruins.
    She'd slept in her car that Friday night and come extra early
    Saturday morning ,with her tent,bedroll and camping supplies
    on her back,found the starting point of the rail line and began the daylong hike up the coast,all without leaving a note or
    informing her parents of what she was up to.

    So as far as anyone knew,she had arrived in Blackbeard and vanished without a trace,except for her car parked at the fast food parking lot.

    Michelle had finally arrived at the old
    research facility late in the day at the end of the rail line.
    She'd briefly explored it but had found it to be of little
    interest since after a century there really wasn't any traces or relics left there.

    Just a few old greenhouse ruins with a few ceramic pots labelled
    DRAGONSWART SEEDLINGS to give a hint what the scientists
    from a century earlier had been up to.

    So she'd carefully made her way down the rusty swaying stairs
    along the cliff and had noted the several open gaping storage chambers along the cliff .
    The docks along the beach had long ago been washed away in
    a storm but with her binoculars Michelle had
    finally spotted a few rusty plates up the beach that might once
    have been the wrecked supply ship.

    The rest is history.Michelle set up camp in one of the roomier
    supply chambers,gone skinnydipping in its pool and gotten herself infected with something,transformed
    into a dragoness and flow off,leaving her abandoned tent,bed roll,clothing and other camping supplies in that Cliffside
    storage chamber.

    Since no one knew she was there in the first place,they'd
    assumed her vanishing had happened 15 to 20 miles south of
    where it actually did.

    Days passed,weeks passed.......
