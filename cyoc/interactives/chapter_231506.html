Gosh, the kindergartner--not even a Kindergartner, given his birthday was in November--before him in the mirror looked so cute and sweet--a sight he wouldn't have been able to observe in himself when he was originally 5. Now he was only a few years older than his daughter. He was more a big brother than a father to Tracey now.     "Oh, well, I'll let you get to it. Call me if you need any help," assured Mommy before shutting the door.

    Will let a sigh of relief go that he hadn't known he'd been holding.

    It was harder to dress oneself at age five than he'd remembered--but Will managed and soon left the bathroom to see a man his child brain was telling him was "Daddy" but his adult memories were recalling him as "Benny" and "little bro". Apparently the computer had made his former little brother Ben his new father. Ben was 23 and perpetually late for everything and anything--as his mad rush to button his dress shirt was proving right now.

    "Don't be too loud, Ben, or you'll wake up Tracey and Janet too early, and then that'll knock them off their schedule for the whole day," chided Paula as she, now dressed, passed him to exit the master bedroom.

    "Pop a bagel in the toaster for me, honey, I'm not gonna have time for much else--not with the traffic I'm going to hit."

    "Will do!" called out Paula as she left the room.

    Ben by this point had finished buttoning his dress shirt and was now tucking it in before walking over to grab a tie at random.

    "Daddy... can I tie your tie?" asked Will, both to appease his child brain to get Daddy's attention, and his adult mind that remembered Ben never tied a tie well under pressure.

    "Aww, Billy, now's not the time--" Ben even used the childhood nickname he'd called Will by when they had been kids.

    "Please?" asked Will, conscious to give Ben puppy dog eyes as he asked.

    It worked better than Will had thought it would.

    "Oh... all right. Five minutes won't make much of a difference," humored Ben as he sat on the ground and hung the tie loosely about his neck. Will, carefully recalling his adult memories began to tie the tie.

    "And where did we learn to tie a tie, Billy?" asked Ben good-naturedly.

    "Mommy showed me... she said I'd need to know how to one day" said Will.

    "Well, apparently she did a really good job, considering my ties aren't all in sailor's knots."

    "Wow... you did an especially nice tie job, Billy," admired Ben as he stood and walked over to the entrance of the master suite bathroom to see the tie job in the mirror.
