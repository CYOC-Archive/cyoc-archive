    Katie was surprised, but not really stunned by the panther morph's request. And figured
    that many other female anthros might be asking the same thing soon enough. Including
    the stupid impulsive ones who wouldn't think about what would happen after those nine
    months were up. Katie wondered what would happen in those nine months. Logically
    speaking, such off spring would be legally USA citizens, no questions asked. But the real
    fact would be that the cubs that would be born, while humanoid, wouldn't have a human
    template to be transformed to. Transformation Park USA had never made one to Katie's
    knowledge, only used the information of the person -before- they were changed to make
    them human again when their stay was over.

    It often creeped Katie that the only thing between her and a lifetime stuck with a tail and
    fur was a major hard drive crash. Heck, that was the only thing between everyone
    transformed and such a life. Katie couldn't understand why anyone would want to spend a
    lifetime like this. Being human was hard enough. Of course the Management promised
    that there was a back up archive of everyone's human template in case of a fatal system
    failure, but Katie often worried about what the Management -wasn't- telling their
    employees and customers.

    "wake up dear." Keith heard the voice of an angel and woke up with one looking down at
    him. Keith couldn't say he wasn't enjoying every second of this, but he did wish that there
    was a way to gauge their 'progress.'

    "One of the huskies showed up and offered us a pregnancy test." Speak of the devil.

    Keith sat up, "Well that's good news." Dang he felt sore in some places.

    "Good," Said apparently said husky. She was a looker, but like any different species, Keith
    found he couldn't find himself attracted to her. "In that case, no time like the present.
    Sooner done, the sooner taken care of."

    "Right now?" Keith asked.

    "It's not like you have other engagements right?" The husky laughed. "I'm Katie by the
    way."

    Jackie couldn't stop herself. "Strange name for a dog."

    "I wasn't born one."

    True, the anthros as a whole, made up their own events, keeping themselves entertained,
    they knew as humans they'd be bored with such a life, but being hyper evolved animals at
    the moment, their threshold of boredom had been greatly increased. It was almost if, even
    sublimely, the change got ride of, at least PART of that very human aspect 'beyond
    survival.' It was still there, but not as influential.

    Keith stretched, "Okay."

    Katie didn't bother to try and ask Keith to stay, knowing how that wouldn't go anywhere.

    "Please follow me please." Katie led the couple.

    "We aren't going to use... whatever it is you people use to get from here to there?"

    "Nope. We do have to have some secret." Katie said keeping up a cheerful face for the sake
    of the two park guests. She knew better than to bellyache to the two of them. "We've also
    offered free examinations for your, mate, sir, since there might be unknown dangers to
    the pregnancy if one occurs.
    It's not like any of your species has ever been pregnant before." Indeed, Keith and Jackie's
    species hadn't existed a few years ago.

    Katie sold it well, as it was her job to. Besides, she honestly considered Murphy's Law in
    full effect here, and the more precautions were taken with these pregnancy as they were
    now beginning to occur, the better for everyone.

    "By the way, we need to make a stop along the way." Katie said, "We have to pick up your
    fellow."

    "My what?" Jackie asked.

    "Our confirmed, X chromosome added with child anthro woman."

    Oh right. Leticia. Who was now rivaling Beauty Queen Hannah as the most popular doe in
    the park. Jackie found it incredible. Wondering how the most girly girls in the park were
    turning out to be former males, and the most manly men were former females. Jackie
    didn't know what to make of it. Katie just figured fate had a really weird sense of humor.

    The authority that Katie represented enabled her and her wards to path though the buck
    unchallenged. They found Jeremy and Leticia together near what had been used as the
    arena, though Leticia hadn't sat in her 'throne' since then. Though it might be particle for
    her to do so once she reached a certain stage.

    Leticia had greatly calmed down from her first shock, and the interviews with the news
    hounds had proven to actually help, talking about it calmed her down. Jeremy of course
    helped greatly too.

    While she had no obligation or need to, Katie put on an air of respect when address the
    herd leader and his female. While they were here in the park only at the grace of the
    Management, they had effectively calmed the problem with the bucks, so Katie felt they
    had earned it.

    "So for the sake of your fawn, we're wondering if you'd let us examine you for any signs of
    possible complications."

    Leticia looked concerned, "complications?"

    "Don't worry, we just don't know the first thing about an anthro deer being pregnant, so
    it's best to learn what we can to help you later on. The panthers here want to see if they've
    managed to grow anything from their labors and will be with you."

    While having the panthers there made them unease actually, the deer logically agreed to
    the Management's request voiced through Katie.

    Like seeing their king and queen off, the herd looked on and stood at attention as Katie
    now led the two pairs to the entrance, she could have lead them though the husky's way of
    getting around at any time she wanted, but the park preferred she not reveal how that
    secret worked.

    The disguised building opened up, and Katie lead them in, it wasn't into one of the TF
    rooms the anthros found. Two more huskies were waiting for them. The room itself felt
    strange to the anthro now, enclosing, unnatural, alien, not alive like the park itself.

    Lead through a door without a handle or knob that slid open on it's own, apparently by
    remote, the anthros were stunned by the strange creatures waiting for them. They were
    furless except for their heads, their faces were squashed flat, and their ears were on the
    sides of their heads, not to mention the odd extra layers of skin over them, they couldn't
    even tell if they were male or female.

    Katie looked at their eyes as they remembered with a thunderclap what a human being
    looked like, she had heard of it before, but had never seen it. It was interesting to see.

    "Hello." Said the first man in the white coat, his square glasses up against his nose. "I am
    Dr. Number One. And this is Dr. Number Two. Please don't ask, I know it sounds stupid, it
    was in my contract. I'll tell you my real name if you bother to ask because the huskies
    never report it anyway."

    The four anthros just nodded.
