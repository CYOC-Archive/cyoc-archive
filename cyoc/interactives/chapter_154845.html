    A Merman Never Gives Up

    The next morning, you wake up on the beach. Alex is still there, sleeping, a few feet away from you. Normally affectionate (maybe more affection than a cousin should be), last night she kept her distance. Her bikini-top and pirate-jewels are in a small pile on the sand next to her, and she seems to be sleeping peacefully. So you decide to take it upon yourself to swim out and catch the two of you some breakfast before starting your day.

    You come back to the beach half an hour later dragging with you some huge, slow-moving, colorful tropical fish to kill and butcher. Alex is already awake when you arrive: she's been sitting on the sand, hugging her tail to her chest like a pair of legs bent at the knee, staring out at the waves and thinking.

    You've been doing some thinking too, and you say to Alex, "I'm not ready to give up yet."

    "What do you mean?"

    "Well, think about it. All these mermaids, trapped her, transformed with magic. The Queen knows something about it that she isn't telling anyone, and I'll just bet that that if we can figure out what it is, we can beat her and escape. Help everyone escape."

    Alex is skeptical. "You want to do the Scooby thing?"

    "I always did want to be a hero. Will you help me?"

    Alex nods. Over breakfast, the two of you discuss your plans. "One of us should try and get close to the Queen," Alex suggests. "You know, try and *pump* her for information." (She accompanies the word "pump" with a suggestive thrust of her fish-hips.)

    "Well, she's expecting us to spend egg-day all alone with her and her cronies," you point out. "In the meanwhile . . . I don't know. I'm just getting a weird vibe, like there's something we've been missing."

    "A vibe?" asks Alex. "It's been a while since you had one of those."

    "Yeah." In your younger days, as kids together, you always had a good head for premonitions and predicting danger. Alex knew well to trust your gut feelings when it came to making mischief while staying out of danger, dodging blame, and generally getting away clean with any shenanigans you cared to pull.

    "What're you thinking?" asks Alex. She scoots her fish-butt across the sand and sits closer too you; she's intrigued now.

    "Something the Queen said. About how this island is consecrated ground. And about that 'holy relic' of hers that she lost. I don't know why, but I'm just feeling like . . . they're connected somehow. Call it a hunch."

    "All right, so . . . let's search the island," Alex suggests. "We haven't really had a reason to do that yet, but maybe now we should see if there's anything inland."

    "And if that doesn't turn up anything, we can always dig around the beaches for buried treasure," you say with a chuckle.

    "God, I hope not," says Alex. "This island isn't very big, but it's got a metric fuck-ton of beach to it."

    Once breakfast is done, you and Alex decide where to look first.

    What do you do now?
