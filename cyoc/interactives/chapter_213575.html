After some vaguely answered questions from Holly to the elders about why she was traveling at that time of the night with her two young children, they were allowed to stay until morning when the nearest tire shop would open.

    Holly didn't know what to make of David when he gave her his phone number the next morning after David Senior towed the car to the garage. This wasn't a pick-up at the time, but simply a friendly gesture. It didn't seem to him like she had many people she could depend on, and while he made no promises, he'd try and help her if he could.

    Not long afterwards—a few weeks—Tommy got into a fight on the school bus and wouldn't be allowed back onto it. That was when Tommy and eventually Alice began spending occasional time with the David as he took them to and from school and, every once and a while, activities. Holly paid for the gas and food, but their originally one-off interaction eventually expanded into friendship, trusted friendship, and then whatever David was now...

    Tommy got how it worked. His mom had failed to go through with a divorce around the time they'd first met David. She'd gotten cold feet, or her husband lured her back in, one. David wasn't sure. This current time seemed different, though. Holly was pushing forward even though it seemed like she was worried her and the kids were in danger. But there was to be no sex, because that would be adultery...

    The boy understood, you see, because two years ago, David stepped over a line and answered a few questions about sex that as a non-parent he probably shouldn't have. But once that pussycat is out of the bag, you can't really put it back. David may not have had sex until he was in high school, but he'd been taught way too early by his father, Junior's boozer friends, and some movies one shouldn't be watching with youngsters present...

He suspected her anger was left over from her Catholic upbringing. Somewhere in the fight, David pointed out that not being educated about reproduction is how someone ends up having unprotected children too early. Holly got even more heated on the topic and David didn't understand why. He'd thought she was older than she was... and he was talking about his own parent's, not her personal life experience.

He wasn't to do that with Alice, and he should definitely tell Holly whenever the kids had questions like that so either she could answer them herself, or be present when he tried.

Not everyone has religious people protesting and having their sex education canceled like happened with Taylor... He ended up learning from Cinemax on his parent's illegal cable almost as much as he ended up learning from David.
