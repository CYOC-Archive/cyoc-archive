    "Well I've got to get Sam a bed so I I'm going to buy one," says Maggie sounding like it's normal.
    "Guess Jake needs one two," replies Braiden also sounding like it's normal.
    "Maybe we can also get them some chew toys or something. They're always so cute when playing fetch," says Maggie.
    "That they are," replies Braiden, "Especially Callum's little butt when he chases after stuff."
    "Very," replies Maggie. Callum's face turns red with embarrassment. He's also terrified, Braiden seems to think this is normal.

    They arrive at the pet store. A sign reads, 'Human pets must remain outside.' There is a large empty cage outside. Callum begins to feel a sense of dread, he's going to be locked in a cage. Vulnerable to the world who sees him as nothing more than a trained animal.

    Callum is lead into the cage by Braiden. He is praying that this is a dream. Braiden shuts the door and locks it. The cage covers a large area but has a low height, about a mitre high at most. It's shared and there are other pets in there too. Some of them are styled like poodles. Pink fake fur and pink dog collars. Callum can't help but feel extremely sorry for the poodle humans, especially the guys.

    Callum crawls over to Brodie. He takes a deep breath and says, "This is weird?"
    "What is?" Asks Brodie oblivious to his recent past.
    "Um. The way people dress us up."
    "Yeah. It's pretty funny though. Hate to be them though."
    "You know what else is funny?" Says Callum.
    "What?" Replies Brodie.
    "The fact that we're pets."
    "Not really."
    "Seriously. There was a phone that altered the world to be this way?"
    "Are you okay?" Asks Brodie concerned.
    "Positive," replies Callum.
    "Okay. But what you're saying is pretty far fetched."
    "How?"
    "We've been together for your whole life and so have our owners. Heck, I remember when mum gave birth to you. They weren't sure if you would make it. You looked too weak and frail. Now look at you, you wouldn't think that you're only 7. You look and act at least double that age."
    "I'm 7?" Says Callum confused.
    "Yeah. Still a pup. Even that other guy is mid teens at least."
    "Uhm okay," says Callum. His mind going a million miles a minute.

    Callum suddenly feels something touching his 'tail'. He jumps around and sees Jake with his tongue out. "Um. Hi," says Callum.
    "Hi," replies Jake enthusiastically.
    "How you doing?"
    "Great. Can't believe your master accepted me into his pack."
    "Um yeah. He's really nice," replies Callum creeped out.
    "And I also get a little sibling to teach."
    "Um?"
    "You. You look like you're trying to be something you're not."
    "What do you mean?"
    "You look so serious. Loosen up dude. Being a pet is far better than not."
    "How do you know?"
    "Because I volunteered to become one. Life was turning to shit and I knew it would only get worse."
    "OK, I guess," says Callum still confused.
    "I'm guessing you're gay?"
    "What?" Says Callum shocked.
    "You haven't been neutered. I have because I'm straight."
    "Why?"
    "They say it's a population control thing."
    "Oh okay."
    "What's your name by the way?"
    "Callum."
    "Nice. I think I'll call you Cal for short."
    "Okay."

    Just then Maggie and Braiden return. Each of them are carrying a bag. Jake crawls up to Braiden first who clips the leash on. Callum does the same. Brodie and Sam do the same for Maggie too.

    Before too long. They are travelling down the footpath. Callum begins to wonder if Jake was right. 'Who actually wants to live a human life when he could live the simple life of a pet?'
