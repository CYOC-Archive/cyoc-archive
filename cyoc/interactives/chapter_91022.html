    Emma was complaining about the English assignment given to them earlier in the day to Jacqui, Andy looked them both up and down. Emma was a fairly athletic girl, and you could tell by looking at her, she was 5'6, with toned legs and stomach, perky 32B boobs, shoulder length sandy light brown/blonde hair and blue eyes to top it off. Jacqui on the other hand was 5'7, with neck length black hair, dark green eyes, 32C boobs and a slender build that often made her look graceful as she walked. Andy had also know Jacqui since they were both 8 and had a crush on her for almost as long.

    To his left was his best friend Sam, Sam was the same height as Andy, coming in at 5'8, but unlike Andy, Sam had broad shoulders and a strong build, not that Andy looked particularly weak, he just had an average build when compared to Sam. Sam had short dark brown styled hair that worked marvelously with his hazel eyes and strong face. Andy himself had brown eyes and medium length very dark brown hair that was often mistaken for being black, but in the sun showed up as a brown.

    Andy snapped back to reality as Emma was interpreted by Sam.

    "But we had only just handed the last one in, I don-" Emma was saying until she was cut off.

    "Hey guys, have you ever seen that store before?" Sam said, oblivious of Emma's complaints. Jacqui and Andy both looked over to where Sam was pointing.

    "I don't think so, wanna check it out?" I suggested.

    "Sure, why not?" Jacqui said as she took the lead into the store.

    There was a sign hanging over the door, "One stop shop for entertaining magic". Inside there was a prominent display for a new board game 'Beautiful You', with a 30% off sticker on it.

    "Hey! We haven't actually played some board games in a while have we!? We should give it a go." Emma exclaimed.

    Andy took a look at the price sticker on the box, "It is pretty cheap, let's give it a go, not like we have anything else to do at this point" he said, handing the box to Jacqui to inspect.

    "What is it about?" Jacqui inquired.

    Seemingly appearing out of nowhere, the shop assistant, a older man, with a twinkle in his eye, said "Well, it is a magic board game, just like the sign above the door says. It shows just how much you do and don't know about each other. I'll tell you what, since you are my first customers, you can have that one for free, just be sure to tell everyone about this place."

    Jacqui sprouted one of her sunny grins. "Thanks! We'll be sure to let people know!" Everyone else chipped in with their thanks and the group turned and left to play.
