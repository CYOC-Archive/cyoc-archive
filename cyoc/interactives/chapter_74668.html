    "There is nothing wrong with being one of us, Michelle," Elizabeth told you as the two of you walked along, "you are beautiful, you are loving, you are as magical as I am. And once your wife joins us, we can live for a very long time out at sea and we can love one another."

    You glanced over to Elizabeth and gave a slight sigh. She looked like a sort of big sister/best friend that was trying to care for you, and the more you looked to her, the stronger those feelings became. It felt sort of like you were falling in love with her, which also made you feel bad. While Elizabeth was beautiful, you felt your wife was beautiful also, and the more these feelings toward Elizabeth grew, the more it felt like you were cheating on your wife. And that was something you really didn't want.

    Strangely, a part of your mind didn't feel that way about having sex with the two men and draining them. That part of your mind thought of them as nothing more than food. Generally, it didn't help you out very much, as you were still trying to come to grips with what you had just done. It also sent a shudder down your spine.

    "Is their magic affecting me mentally too?" you thought to yourself, "Is the twenty four hours simply the time I have before my mind is completely a mermaid's and I see men as nothing more then food?"

    You wanted to ask Elizabeth about this, but found yourself too embarrassed to ask.

    "And look at it this way, Michelle," Elizabeth continued as you both walked along, "we don't need to feed every day. You'll probably be good for a few days. Though, you may have to help your wife feed shortly after she joins us."

    You didn't want show this to Elizabeth, but that made you inwardly shudder a bit as well. You had stumbled onto the mermaids by accident. Thankfully they found you "worthy" and transformed you, instead of killing you outright, but when you voiced that you wanted to return to your wife... your human wife, they had told you that the only way they could allow you to return to your wife would be to find her, take her back to the beach, and transform her into a mermaid. If you did so, not only would you need to adjust to "eating" men every few days, but so would your wife. It was not something you liked, and made you feel all the more guilty at going along with it as you and Elizabeth walked along. The only reason that you had agreed to it was because you didn't want to die. If you didn't return to the beach with your wife and transform her, the deal with the mermaid's "leader," Florencia, was that while you'd become male again, you'd be drained of your energy to protect the mermaid's existence and their home.

    Elizabeth was tasked to do that job if you failed, and she had said she would carry out the deal if you couldn't. She looked like she didn't want to do it, it was clear that she had accepted it. You still didn't want to die, and Elizabeth had been the friendliest toward you. The more you looked to her, the more you saw her as a sister, and you couldn't bring yourself to doing anything that would disappoint her.

    "It'll be okay, Michelle," Elizabeth said to you, as you began to make your way into a nicer part of town, in fact the very neighborhood where you and your wife lived.

    You glanced to see the looks on some of the people's faces that saw the two of you. You didn't know what they were thinking, but you were certain they didn't think much of you. You only walked along, hugging yourself and with Elizabeth having one arm around you in a fairly protective manner.

    "We're coming up on my house now," you said to her slowly, and pointed toward the structure.

    "We're making good time," Elizabeth said with a sisterly smile, "Now, all we'll have to do is convince your wife to come back to the beach with us. We have tonight and most of tomorrow."

    You only sighed as you reached out to open the door.
