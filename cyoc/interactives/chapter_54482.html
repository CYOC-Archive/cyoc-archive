    The girl started humming the wedding march, and chuckled. "Today's the
    big day, gal. We're moving you into the breeding pen with Titus.
    He's a championship pig. Won six blue ribbons. After he studs you
    three or four litters, we'll probably barbecue him.

    "Oink!" squealed Dan stepping backwards a couple of feet.

    "Oh, wedding day jitters, I understand, sowie. Titus is one big boar.
    In more ways than one, if you get my meaning. Yesterday he did three
    other sows, and each time it was wham, bam, thank you ma'am. Now get
    over to the gate piggy. It's time to meet your mate," the farm girl
    said. As she climbed into the pen to guide Dan, the crow flew into
    her face. It clawed at her, and she tripped and banged her head on the
    fence post.

    "She's dead?" oinked Dan in alarm.

    "No," said the crow.

    Dan stared at him.

    "She's still breathing," the crow continued. I expect she'll be okay
    when she comes to. I don't think my little scratches will even scar
    her."

    "You can speak?"

    "So can you?"

    "I oink - at least that's - but wait you can understand me?"

    "Yeah, I'm under the same type of transformational spell. Common
    magical influence allows you to hear me and me to hear you. Or rather
    understand each other, if that weren't the case I'd hear grunting and
    you'd hear cawing."

    "My sister's a witch. Did she do this to you?"

    "No, and no. She probably told you she was a witch to make it easier
    to scare you and mislead you. You ever watch any original Star Trek
    episodes?" The pig nodded. The crow continued, "Remember the one
    where aliens made themselves human and created a Halloween Haunted
    Castle world? Bones was a zombie, the Enterprise was sealed in an
    acrylic block?"

    "Uh, sort of. I think it was on video at Rick's Halloween party last
    year. The effects were hokey."

    "Yeah, well, that one was based on real aliens. I should know I am
    one of them."

    The crow changed into a naked man in his mid 60's. "Actually, your
    sister is one too. I was an intern and I kind of slipped the writer
    the ideas. They changed it a bit. See my species can't use our
    powers to make us human. We're parasites that need human or other
    hosts to live. Uh, remember the movie The Hidden from the 80's. That
    was me too. See I'm the cop, or good cop, here to stop and capture,
    the being inhabiting Kat. So we both have the ability to occupy
    humans, and the technology to change humans into other things. It's
    all a game to her. She went rogue over 4 decades ago, and I've been
    on her trail ever since. With your help I think I can -" he paused,
    "Okay, now what? Why are you staring at me?"

    "So my sister isn't a witch. She's a human possessed by an alien
    parasite with tech that let's her appear to be a witch? And you're one
    too?"

    "Yes."

    "Uh, can you change me back?"

    "That's the plan. First, we need to get you back to my ship."

    "You're not going to incinerate her to get the parasite are you?" Dan
    asked. He set his teeth. He'd remain a sow forever to keep his sister
    from being killed.

    "I can't even kill the parasite. I have to bring her back alive," the
    man sighed, "I really wasted a lot of time with the movies back then.
    I was fascinated with the whole concept. Our world doesn't have
    anything even close. Do you recall the film Dark Crystal?"

    "Yeah, muppet dungeons and dragons?"

    "Sort of. The evil Skekses controlled the world with the Dark Crystal.
    They were feathered lizards or scaly birds. Their counterpoint were
    the good peaceloving Mystics. They were more like hairy tortoises.
    When the crystal was repaired, the shard returned, then the urSkeks
    are reformed by the fusing of the Skekses and the Mystics to their
    true form. Well, she -the parasite in your sister - is my feminine
    side. My evil half if you like. So if she dies, I die. So I have to
    capture her."

    "Does that mean if you die, she dies?" Dan asked thoughtfully.
