    Prophetic Dream? And Missing Person

    added by loony28 12 years ago I

    "I think we should make camp elsewhere," Nausikaa said with a little smile.

    "I agree," Semele said looking around.

    "Well Dike it looks like we'll be camping someplace else," I said nodding. I turned and
    started walking with my sisters following me.

    We walked for ten minutes when we came upon a clearing and we began setting up
    camp. It didn't take us long to set up the tent and dig a fire pit so within an hour we
    had a fire going and food cooking. I still felt like there was something strange here and
    looking up at the sky again it didn't look like the moon had moved. Maybe I was
    getting a little paranoid but I didn't like that. As soon as we finished eating I looked at
    my sisters and said, "We should get some sleep. I'll take the first watch."

    "The first watch?" Semele asked looking confused.

    "One of us stays awake for a set time while the others sleep so we're not taken by
    surprise by a monster or someone," I explained. "When your watch is over you wake
    the next person up and go to sleep yourself."

    "We don't need to do that," Nausikaa said.

    "Remember that we're not safe here," I said shaking my head. "If we're all asleep a
    monster could come and kill us all with no warning. This way we'll have at least some
    warning if a monster comes. Now get in the tent and get some sleep. I'll wake you up
    in three hours Nausikaa then you'll wake Dike up three hours later and finally Dike will
    wake Semele up after her three hour watch. Semele will wake us all up after her three
    hours. This will give each of us a total of nine hours sleep." With a little grumbling my
    sisters went into the tent to sleep. Nothing happened on my watch except for the moon
    remaining where it had been. At least we had a watch so we knew what time it was. It
    was with some relief that I woke Nausikaa up for her watch since the quiet was starting
    to make me drowsy. I could only hope that my sisters would be able to stay awake.
    After changing into a nightgown I slipped into my sleeping bag and was soon asleep.

    My dream started with me and my sisters on a train. The door to our compartment
    opened revealing a young girl standing there with a look of surprise on her face. "I'm
    sorry but there seems to be some sort of mistake," she finally said. "I was assigned to
    this compartment but it looks like it's already full."

    "What's your name?" I asked the girl.

    "Helen King," the girl said.

    "Well hello Helen," I said with a smile. "You're in the right place. We were told that
    we'd have someone else with us for the night. I'm Lisa Plunkett and these are my
    sisters Nancy Boone, Bai Kwan, and Sara Slade." I pointed to Nausikaa, Dike, and
    Semele in turn.

    "You don't have the same last name and Bai's name sounds oriental," Helen said.

    "Well Bai's mom is Chinese," I said. "We're actually half sisters. Our dad only knew our
    mom for one night each and left them with us. Our moms never knew what our dad's
    last name was so we took or stepdad's last names."

    The scene grew dark and then we were in some sort of great hall with floating candles.
    Somehow I knew that this was Salem's Witch Academy though there were wizards
    here too. I looked around to see Nausikaa and Dike sitting at a table. There was a
    stern looking guy holding a pointed hat behind a stool. Looking at a piece of
    parchment he called out, "Katrina McCusker." A girl walked forward looking nervous
    and sat on the stool. When I saw her my pearl vibrated against my skin and I knew
    she had been transformed.

    As the hat is lowered onto Katrina's head the dream shifts again. I was now standing
    in an office facing a stern looking woman. I was standing with Katrina, Helen, who
    looked nervous and a boy that looked really smug. I knew that he wasn't in our house
    and that there was a little animosity between the two houses. "There's no reason to be
    roaming around the school at night without permission and being in the restricted
    section of the library," the woman said. "The restricted section is dangerous enough to
    the teachers let alone first years. All four of you will have a weeks worth of detention
    and your houses will lose one hundred points for each of you."

    The dream dissolved and I became aware of Semele shaking me and saying, "Wake
    up Anthea."

    "What time is it?" I asked opening my eyes.

    "Well it's seven but it's still dark outside and the moon is still overhead," Semele said.

    I sighed and got up. I had figured that it would still be dark but it was disappointing to
    know that I was right. Once we were all dressed we left the tent only to find ourselves
    in a bedroom. The bed didn't look like it had been slept in but there were girl's clothes
    on the floor. Then I noticed there was something on the bed. Approaching it Nausikaa
    asked, "Is that what I think it is?"

    I picked the medallion up and nodding said, "I believe it is. This has to be the
    Medallion of Zulo. At least it won't transform us, I hope."

    Just then we heard a man's voice ask, "Who are you?"

    I dropped the medallion back on the bed and quickly turn around tapping my bracelet
    as I do. The silver disk grew into a shield and straps wrapped around my arm and the
    palm of my hand. Out of the corner of my I saw that Nausikaa had drawn her sword,
    Dike had drawn her dagger, and Semele had drawn her bow and arrow. I reached for
    my sword as I completed my turn and saw a man and woman standing in the door.
    Both of them looked frightened and before my sisters could do anything else I called
    out, "Hold," and took my hand off my sword. I then tapped my shield and it shrank
    back down to my bracelet.

    "Who are you girls and what did you do with my daughter?" the woman asked.

    "We have done nothing with your daughter," I said keeping my voice non-
    confrontational. "As to who we are I am Lisa Plunkett and these are my sisters."

    "Why are you here?" the man asked.

    "To be truthful I don't know why we're here," I said sighing. "We were in a tent and
    when we left we were surprised to find ourselves here. Perhaps we're here to help find
    your daughter. Could you tell us what happened?"

    "We may think about it if you put those weapons away," the man said. "You girls
    shouldn't playing with them anyway."

    "I assure you we are not playing with weapons," I said and then nodded to my sisters.
    They reluctantly put their weapons away and the man motioned for us to follow him. I
    grabbed the medallion and my bag and followed after them.

    They led us to the kitchen and the woman said, "Sit down and I'll tell you." After we
    had sat down she continued, "She went to her friends house yesterday to stay the
    night with her friend. She woke up this morning to find my daughter gone which isn't
    like her. Poor Aimee is beside herself with worry and wondering if she did something
    wrong. What happened to her? Did she run away?"

    "I may have an idea," I said then showed them the medallion. "Have either of you seen
    this before?" When they shook their heads I continued, "It may be that your daughter
    has been transformed and she left out of fear."

    "You can't be serious," the man said. "My daughter couldn't have been transformed
    because it's impossible."

    "You saw my bracelet become a shield then a bracelet again," I said looking the man
    square in the eyes.

    The man looked away and said, "Well yes I did and I can't explain it."

    "It's magic," I said sighing. "This medallion is also magic and has the power to
    transform someone when they touch it to an article of clothing. Did either of you see
    any clothes that didn't belong to your daughter?"

    "Well I was hanging up some of her clothes when I noticed an Army jacket in her
    closet," the woman said. "I thought that she may have a boyfriend and he had given it
    to her."

    I thought for a bit then said, "It may be possible that your daughter came home for
    something last night. That something may have been in one of the pockets of the
    jacket but when she reached into one pocket she touched the medallion and it
    transformed her. The clothes she was wearing probably didn't fit so she took them off
    and seeing that she had been transformed she pulled the jacket and maybe some
    pajamas on then went to find the guy who gave her the jacket hoping he would have
    some answers."

    "So what do we do?" the woman asked. "We can't call the police and tell them that
    our daughter was transformed. They wouldn't believe us."

    "That's true," I said nodding. "You can call them and report your daughter missing.
    They won't find her if she has been transformed but it's all you can do. We can look for
    her and reverse the transformation."

    "How can you do that?" the man asked.

    "With these pearls," I said showing them my necklace. "We each have one and they
    can reverse transformations. They can also sense someone who has been
    transformed."

    "You girls shouldn't go out by yourselves," the man said shaking his head.

    "We'll be alright," I said smiling. "We have some power from our father." I concentrated
    on the water in the glass before me and felt the tug as it began to swirl around. Soon
    there was a tube of water swirling up out of the glass. I let it go back down and settle
    before I said, "Our father is Poseidon, god of the sea."

    "Well you girls will at least stay to eat?" the woman asked.
