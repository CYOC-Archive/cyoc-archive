    Codename: ‘The Eddy Eggplant Show’

    Description: E-650, ‘The Eddy Eggplant Show’, is a children’s television show appearing on channels throughout North America.

    E-650 is a variety show featuring live-action skits, and some animated segment. Live-action segments are hosted by Eddy Eggplant, an eggplant mascot played by an unknown actor in a felt suit. He is accompanied by Silly Sally, a pink-dressed ‘neighbor girl’ played by a different young woman in each episode. They take part in various activities in Eddy Eggplant’s house, the backyard, and the nearby woods, all of which are artificial sets.

    Other characters include (but are not limited to):
    • Dingleberry Dan, a wild west cowboy who would often get into slapsticks, while trying to be helpful.
    • Lovey Bunny, a puppet rabbit who often appears in skits with Silly Sally. In some episodes, she appears in her own segment, called ‘Lovey Bunny Playtime!’, in which she demonstrates kid activities or tells stories. Occasionally, she also starts in her own animated shorts called 'Lovey Bunny Magic!', as a magical bunny girl.
    • Wendy the Witch, a witch who’s often exasperated by other characters trespassing on her home, or stealing her magical items.
    • Rascal Red, a fox-eared man in a beany hat, and whisker face paint. He would often make trouble for the rest of the cast, in many cases tying up Silly Sally, with the other cast having to save her.
    • Lil’ Cupid, an animated winged cupid boy who stars in his own animated shorts, called "Lil’ Cupid Adventures". In each short, Lil’ Cupid stops various mythical creatures from causing trouble, while also helping characters out. He has magic arrows which can transform people, or make them fall in love.

    E-650 does not appear on programming slots of any official TV channels, and is considered an unauthorized broadcast. In all sightings, the show would replace a 30- minute program originally scheduled for 5:00pm, with no knowledge from the channel’s management. Runtime is a total of 24-minutes, with commercial breaks totaling 6 minutes (no anomalies have been found in these ad breaks).

    The show broadcasts on an irregular basis, often picking random days to air episodes. It is most often shown on local satellite channels with family programming, along with public access networks. There have been scant reports of E-650 appearing on popular cable networks, including [REDACTED] and [REDACTED].

    More unusually, the show would only appear on TV sets in households with children under 13 years old. Additionally, the E-650 has memory-affecting effects on both children and adults, which obscure the true nature of E-650.

    Due to this, public knowledge of E-650 is limited. The majority of known E-650 witnesses were found in online communities focused on transformation fetishes.

    The first known witness report of E-650 was a Usenet post from October 2nd, 1992, on the newsgroup “alt.sex.transformation”. The post was made by user MrHorseCock, later identified as then-18 year old Michael Sawyer of North Carolina.
