    “Goddammit!”

    Ezekiel was going to play D&D with his friends tonight at 7:30, but he had somehow misplaced his set of Dice. It was given to him as a present when he went off to college, but now he had lost it. Not wanting to have to use his friends’ dice like a mooch, he decided to go buy some more dice from the nearby gaming store: Level 7 Games.

    Ezekiel walked into the store with a sort of anger that you only feel once you have been so emotionally drained that you can’t be sad. Wanting to make this journey quick and cheap, he decided to get some bargain dice.

    He looked through your standard collection of bargain bin acrylic dice, but then, he saw an extremely beautiful set of crystalline dice for 5 bucks. Enamored by the cheapness and the beauty of the dice, he bought them without a second thought

    Soon, Zeke went to his friend Leo’s apartment, who was acting as the DM for the session. His friends arrived promptly. Zeke’s character was a Dragonborn Paladin. His friends Zoe, Aiden, and Francis played a Dwarf Cleric, a Yuan-Ti Rogue, and a Human Sorcerer, respectively.

    Funnily enough, everyone except for Zeke had left their dice at home. Willing to share in order to prevent the mess of everyone having to grab the dice with gusto, Zeke shared his dice and offered up an app which functioned as dice roller.

    Finally, the game started, with the players en route to a major city. Suddenly, using his dice roller, the DM sent the party into an ambush by goblins, and the players were asked to roll initiative.

    Allured by the beauty of the crystalline dice, the players decided to use its d20 to roll initiative. This would have been an uneventful session, if the die hadn’t been enchanted by a real wizard to transform whoever had rolled the die into something else depending on what the die landed on.

    And the first player who rolled the die was...
