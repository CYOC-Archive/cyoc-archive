    After a while, Jeff gets an idea, his brother, who is 15, a year younger than Jeff, has just received his full licensed driver's permit. However, Kyle hasn’t yet gotten a car, caused by their parents currently unable to afford even a used car, what if Jeff would transform himself into his brother’s car? The thought somehow excites Jeff, yes, he’d be unable to move on his own, but his curiosity is just to strong, “HEY KYLE, COME HERE FOR A SEC,” he calls out.

    Only a minute later, the door to his room opens and his brother steps into the room, “what’s up Jeff,” he asks. Kyle looks a lot like Jeff, both have athletic bodies, brown eyes and black hair, the only difference is that Kyle has blond highlights in his hair, both of them have a fair skin tone.

    “Close the door, and I’ll explain,” Jeff says.

    After explaining his idea to his brother, and after Jeff demonstrating the power of the program, by transforming himself into a 10-year-old and back, Kyle has an excited expression, “gee, bro, that would mean a lot to me,” he says, “I really could use a car to get around in”.

    Jeff smiles, “thought you’d like it,” he says, “do you have a car in mind that you want me to become?”

    Kyle nods, “I do, though, before I tell you, I do have one question”.

    “Which would be?”

    “Well, my teacher asked if anyone would have a car for a school project,” Kyle says, “you know, to learn about cars, how to take them apart, put them back together, upgrading them and so on”.

    Jeff looks concerned, “I dunno, Kyle,” he says, “I mean… what if something goes wrong, causing me to be stuck as a car? I really don’t want that to happen!”

    “Oh come on, Jeff,” Kyle says, noticeably excited, “I promise I won’t let anything bad happen to you. I doubt that you’ll be unable to transform back, but if it should turn out that way, I promise I’ll find a way to transform you back!”

    Jeff thinks for a while, he then sighs, “okay, I guess it’ll be fine,” he says.

    “Sweet,” Kyle says.

    Jeff chuckles, “so, what car shall I become,” he asks, “oh, and I’ll set it so I’ll be able to communicate over the radio, however: I won’t be able to communicate while a CD is running or when you or someone has his or her phone hooked up to my system and playing their playlist or charging their phone”.

    Kyle nods, “understood, Jeff,” he says, “well… the car I want you to be is... .”
